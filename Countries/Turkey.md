**Turkey**,[^1] officially the **Republic of Türkiye**,[^2] is a country mainly located in [Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolia "Anatolia") in [West Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Asia "West Asia"), with a smaller part called [East Thrace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Thrace "East Thrace") in [Southeast Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Europe "Southeast Europe"). It borders the [Black Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea "Black Sea") to the north; [Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)"), [Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenia "Armenia"), [Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan "Azerbaijan"), and [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran") to the east; [Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq "Iraq"), [Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syria "Syria"), and the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea") to the south; and the [Aegean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_Sea "Aegean Sea"), [Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greece "Greece"), and [Bulgaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria "Bulgaria") to the west. Turkey is home to over 86 million people;[^14] most are ethnic [Turks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_people "Turkish people"), while [Kurds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kurds_in_Turkey "Kurds in Turkey") are the [largest ethnic minority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minorities_in_Turkey "Minorities in Turkey").[^18] Officially [a secular state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secularism_in_Turkey "Secularism in Turkey"), Turkey has [a Muslim-majority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_in_Turkey "Islam in Turkey") population. [Ankara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ankara "Ankara") is Turkey's capital and [second-largest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_cities_and_towns_in_Turkey "List of largest cities and towns in Turkey") city. [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul") is its largest city and economic center. Other major cities include [İzmir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%B0zmir "İzmir"), [Bursa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bursa "Bursa"), and [Antalya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antalya "Antalya").

First inhabited by modern humans during the [Late Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Paleolithic "Late Paleolithic"),[^19] present-day Turkey was home to [various ancient peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_ancient_peoples_of_Anatolia "List of ancient peoples of Anatolia").[^20] The [Hattians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hattians "Hattians") were assimilated by the [Hittites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hittites "Hittites") and other [Anatolian peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolian_peoples "Anatolian peoples").[^21] [Classical Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Anatolia "Classical Anatolia") transitioned into cultural [Hellenization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenization "Hellenization") after [Alexander the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_the_Great "Alexander the Great") 's conquests, and later [Romanization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_of_Anatolia "Romanization of Anatolia") during the [Roman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire") and [Byzantine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine "Byzantine") eras.[^22] The [Seljuk Turks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seljuk_Empire "Seljuk Empire") began migrating into Anatolia in the 11th century, starting the [Turkification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkification "Turkification") process.[^23] The Seljuk [Sultanate of Rum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate_of_Rum "Sultanate of Rum") ruled Anatolia until the [Mongol invasion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_invasions_of_Anatolia "Mongol invasions of Anatolia") in 1243, when it disintegrated into [Turkish principalities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolian_beyliks "Anatolian beyliks").[^24] Beginning in 1299, the [Ottomans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") united the principalities and [expanded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rise_of_the_Ottoman_Empire "Rise of the Ottoman Empire"). [Mehmed II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mehmed_II "Mehmed II") conquered [Constantinople (modern-day Istanbul) in 1453](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_Constantinople "Fall of Constantinople"). During the reigns of [Selim I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selim_I "Selim I") and [Suleiman the Magnificent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suleiman_the_Magnificent "Suleiman the Magnificent"), the Ottoman Empire became a [global power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_power "Global power").[^25] From 1789 onwards, the empire saw major changes, [reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzimat "Tanzimat"), centralization, and [rising nationalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rise_of_nationalism_in_the_Ottoman_Empire "Rise of nationalism in the Ottoman Empire") while [its territory declined](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decline_and_modernization_of_the_Ottoman_Empire "Decline and modernization of the Ottoman Empire").[^26]

In the 19th and early 20th centuries, [persecution of Muslims during the Ottoman contraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Muslims_during_the_Ottoman_contraction "Persecution of Muslims during the Ottoman contraction") and [in the Russian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Muslims#Russian_Empire "Persecution of Muslims") resulted in large-scale loss of life and [mass migration into modern-day Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhacir "Muhacir") from the [Balkans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkans "Balkans"), [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus "Caucasus"), and [Crimea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_Khanate "Crimean Khanate").[^27] Under the control of the [Three Pashas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Pashas "Three Pashas"), the Ottoman Empire [entered World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_entry_into_World_War_I "Ottoman entry into World War I") in 1914, during which the Ottoman government committed [genocides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Ottoman_genocides "Late Ottoman genocides") against its [Armenian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_genocide "Armenian genocide"), [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_genocide "Greek genocide"), and [Assyrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assyrian_genocide "Assyrian genocide") subjects.[^28] [^29] [^30] Following Ottoman defeat, the [Turkish War of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_War_of_Independence "Turkish War of Independence") resulted in the [abolition of the sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abolition_of_the_Ottoman_sultanate "Abolition of the Ottoman sultanate") and the signing of the [Treaty of Lausanne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Lausanne "Treaty of Lausanne"). Turkey emerged as a more homogenous [nation state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation_state "Nation state").[^31] The Republic [was proclaimed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_Republic_of_Turkey "Proclamation of the Republic of Turkey") on 29 October 1923, modelled on [the reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atat%C3%BCrk%27s_reforms "Atatürk's reforms") initiated by its founder and first president, [Mustafa Kemal Atatürk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mustafa_Kemal_Atat%C3%BCrk "Mustafa Kemal Atatürk"). Turkey [remained neutral during most of World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey_during_World_War_II "Turkey during World War II"), but was involved in the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War"). Several military interventions interfered with the transition to a multi-party system.

Turkey is an [upper-middle-income](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_national_income "Gross national income") and [emerging country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emerging_country "Emerging country"); [its economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Turkey "Economy of Turkey") is the world's [16th-largest by nominal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\) "List of countries by GDP (nominal)") and [11th-largest by PPP-adjusted GDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(PPP\) "List of countries by GDP (PPP)"). As the [15th-largest electricity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_electricity_production "List of countries by electricity production") producer in the world, Turkey aims to become a hub for regional energy transportation. It is a [unitary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitary_state "Unitary state") presidential [republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_system "Presidential system"). Turkey is a founding member of the [OECD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OECD "OECD"), [G20](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G20 "G20"), and [Organization of Turkic States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organization_of_Turkic_States "Organization of Turkic States"). With a geopolitically significant location, Turkey is a [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO") member and has [its second-largest military personnel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Member_states_of_NATO#Military_personnel "Member states of NATO"). It may be recognized as an [emerging](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emerging_power "Emerging power"), a [middle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_power "Middle power"), and a [regional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_power "Regional power") power. As [an EU candidate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accession_of_Turkey_to_the_European_Union "Accession of Turkey to the European Union"), Turkey is part of the [EU Customs Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EU_Customs_Union "EU Customs Union").

Turkey has coastal plains, [a high central plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_of_Turkey "Geography of Turkey"), and various mountain ranges with rising elevation eastwards. [Turkey's climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_of_Turkey "Climate of Turkey") is diverse, ranging from [Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_climate "Mediterranean climate") and other [temperate climates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperate_climates "Temperate climates") to [semi-arid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-arid "Semi-arid") and [continental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_climate "Continental climate") types. Home to three [biodiversity hotspots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity_hotspot "Biodiversity hotspot"), Turkey is prone to [frequent earthquakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_earthquakes_in_Turkey "List of earthquakes in Turkey") and is [highly vulnerable to climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_in_Turkey "Climate change in Turkey").[^32] Turkey has [a universal healthcare system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_care_in_Turkey "Health care in Turkey"), growing [access to education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education_in_Turkey "Education in Turkey"), and increasing levels of [innovativeness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Innovation_Index "Global Innovation Index").[^33] It is a leading [TV content](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_television_drama "Turkish television drama") exporter.[^34] With numerous UNESCO [World Heritage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_World_Heritage_Sites_in_Turkey "List of World Heritage Sites in Turkey") sites and [intangible cultural heritage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intangible_cultural_heritage "Intangible cultural heritage") inscriptions, and [a rich and diverse cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_cuisine "Turkish cuisine"), Turkey is the [fourth most visited country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Tourism_rankings "World Tourism rankings") in the world.

## Etymology

*Turchia*, meaning "the land of the Turks", had begun to be used in European texts for [Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolia "Anatolia") by the end of the 12th century.[^35] [^36] [^37] As a word in [Turkic languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkic_languages "Turkic languages"), *Turk* may mean "strong, strength, ripe" or "flourishing, in full strength".[^38] It may also mean ripe as in for a fruit or "in the prime of life, young, and vigorous" for a person.[^39] As an [ethnonym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnonym "Ethnonym"), the etymology is still unknown.[^40] In addition to usage in languages such as Chinese in the 6th century,[^37] the earliest mention of *Turk* (𐱅𐰇𐰺𐰜, *türü̲k̲*; or 𐱅𐰇𐰼𐰚, *türk/tẄrk*) in Turkic languages comes from the [Second Turkic Khaganate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Turkic_Khaganate "Second Turkic Khaganate").[^41]

In [Byzantine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine "Byzantine") sources in the 10th century, the name *[Tourkia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourkia_\(disambiguation\) "Tourkia (disambiguation)")* was used for defining two medieval states: [Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Principality_of_Hungary "Principality of Hungary") (*Western Tourkia*); and [Khazaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourkia_\(Khazaria\) "Tourkia (Khazaria)") (*Eastern Tourkia*).[^42] [^43] The [Mamluk Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mamluk_Sultanate "Mamluk Sultanate"), with its ruling elite of Turkic origin, was called the "State of the Turks" (*Dawlat at-Turk*, or *Dawlat al-Atrāk*, or *Dawlat-at-Turkiyya*).[^44] Turkestan, also meaning the "land of the Turks", was used for a historic region in [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia").[^45]

[Middle English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_English "Middle English") usage of *Turkye* or *Turkeye* is found in *[The Book of the Duchess](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Book_of_the_Duchess "The Book of the Duchess")* (written in 1369–1372) to refer to Anatolia or the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire").[^46] The modern spelling *Turkey* dates back to at least 1719.[^47] The [bird called turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey_\(bird\) "Turkey (bird)") was named as such due to trade of [guineafowl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guineafowl "Guineafowl") from Turkey to England.[^37] The name *Turkey* has been used in international treaties referring to the Ottoman Empire.[^48] The [Government of the Grand National Assembly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_the_Grand_National_Assembly "Government of the Grand National Assembly") adopted the name *Türkiye Devleti* ("State of Turkey").[^49] With the [Treaty of Alexandropol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Alexandropol "Treaty of Alexandropol") in 1920, the name *Türkiye* entered international documents for the first time. In the treaty signed with [Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Afghanistan "Emirate of Afghanistan") in 1921, the expression *Devlet-i Âliyye-i Türkiyye* ("Sublime Turkish State") was used, likened to the Ottoman Empire's name.[^50]

Upon the [proclamation of the republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_Republic_of_Turkey "Proclamation of the Republic of Turkey") on 29 October 1923, the name *Türkiye Cumhuriyeti* (توركيه جمهوريتى) was adopted. In December 2021, President [Recep Tayyip Erdoğan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan "Recep Tayyip Erdoğan") called for expanded official usage of *Türkiye*, saying that *Türkiye* "represents and expresses the culture, civilization, and values of the Turkish nation in the best way".[^51] In May 2022, the Turkish government requested the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations") and other international organizations to use *Türkiye* officially in English; the UN agreed.[^52] [^53] [^54] Until 2022, its official English name remained the *Republic of Turkey*. Though not official, the term *Turkish Republic* is sometimes used in descriptive, historic, or academic contexts.

## History

### Prehistory and ancient history

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d5/G%C3%B6bekli_Tepe%2C_Urfa.jpg/250px-G%C3%B6bekli_Tepe%2C_Urfa.jpg)

Some henges at Göbekli Tepe were erected as far back as 9600 BC, predating those of Stonehenge by over seven millennia. 55

Present-day Turkey has been inhabited by [modern humans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modern_humans "Modern humans") since the [late Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Paleolithic "Late Paleolithic") period and contains some of the world's oldest [Neolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic "Neolithic") sites.[^56] [^57] [Göbekli Tepe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%B6bekli_Tepe "Göbekli Tepe") is close to 12,000 years old.[^56] Parts of [Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolia "Anatolia") include the [Fertile Crescent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fertile_Crescent "Fertile Crescent"), an [origin of agriculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origin_of_agriculture "Origin of agriculture").[^58] Other important Anatolian Neolithic sites include [Çatalhöyük](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%87atalh%C3%B6y%C3%BCk "Çatalhöyük") and [Alaca Höyük](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alaca_H%C3%B6y%C3%BCk "Alaca Höyük").[^59] Neolithic Anatolian farmers differed genetically from farmers in [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran") and [Jordan Valley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan_Valley "Jordan Valley").[^60] These early Anatolian farmers also [migrated into Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_European_Farmers "Early European Farmers"), starting around 9,000 years ago.[^60] [^61] [^62] [Troy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Troy "Troy") 's earliest layers go back to around 4500 BC.[^59]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Empire_of_the_Hitties.png/250px-Empire_of_the_Hitties.png)

Map of the Hittite Empire at its greatest extent, with Hittite rule c. 1300 BC

Anatolia's historical records start with [clay tablets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clay_tablets "Clay tablets") from approximately around 2000 BC that were found in modern-day [Kültepe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%BCltepe "Kültepe").[^63] These tablets belonged to an [Assyrian trade colony](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karum_\(trade_post\) "Karum (trade post)").[^63] The languages in Anatolia at that time included Hattian, Hurrian, [Hittite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hittite_language "Hittite language"), [Luwian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luwian "Luwian"), and [Palaic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palaic "Palaic").[^64] [Hattian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hattic_language "Hattic language") was a language indigenous to Anatolia, with no known modern-day connections.[^64] [^65] [Hurrian language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hurrian_language "Hurrian language") was used in northern [Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syria "Syria").[^64] Hittite, Palaic, and Luwian languages were "the oldest written [Indo-European languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-European_languages "Indo-European languages") ",[^66] forming the [Anatolian sub-group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolian_languages "Anatolian languages").[^67] [^3]

[Hattian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hattians "Hattians") rulers were gradually replaced by [Hittite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hittites "Hittites") rulers.[^63] The Hittite kingdom was a large kingdom in Central Anatolia, with its capital of [Hattusa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hattusa "Hattusa").[^63] It co-existed in Anatolia with [Palaians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pala_\(Anatolia\) "Pala (Anatolia)") and [Luwians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luwians "Luwians"), approximately between 1700 and 1200 BC.[^63] As the Hittite kingdom was disintegrating, further waves of Indo-European peoples migrated from southeastern Europe, which was followed by warfare.[^71] The [Thracians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thracians "Thracians") were also present in modern-day [Turkish Thrace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_Thrace "Turkish Thrace").[^72] It is not known if the [Trojan War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trojan_War "Trojan War") is based on historical events.[^73] [Troy's Late Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Bronze_Age_Troy "Late Bronze Age Troy") layers matches most with *[Iliad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iliad "Iliad")* 's story.[^74]

### Early classical antiquity

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/The_Lycian_Way_-_2014.10_-_panoramio.jpg/250px-The_Lycian_Way_-_2014.10_-_panoramio.jpg)

The Lycian Way is a 760 kilometers (470 mi) long hiking path in Southwestern Turkey. 75

Around 750 BC, [Phrygia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phrygia "Phrygia") had been established, with its two centers in [Gordium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gordium "Gordium") and modern-day [Kayseri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kayseri "Kayseri").[^76] [Phrygians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phrygians "Phrygians") spoke an Indo-European language, which was closer to [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language "Greek language") than Anatolian languages.[^67] Phrygians shared Anatolia with [Neo-Hittites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neo-Hittites "Neo-Hittites") and [Urartu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urartu "Urartu"). Luwian-speakers were probably the majority in various Anatolian Neo-Hittite states.[^77] Urartians spoke a non-Indo-European language and their capital was around [Lake Van](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Van "Lake Van").[^78] [^76] Urartu and Phrygia fell in seventh century BC.[^76] [^79] They were replaced by [Carians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carians "Carians"), [Lycians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lycians "Lycians") and [Lydians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lydians "Lydians").[^79] These three cultures "can be considered a reassertion of the ancient, indigenous culture of the Hattian cities of Anatolia".[^79]

In the late [Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age "Bronze Age"), there were three [^80] or four [^81] Greek-speaking settlements in Anatolia, including [Miletus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miletus "Miletus").[^80] [^81] Around 1000 BC, [Greeks started migrating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron_Age_Greek_migrations "Iron Age Greek migrations") to the west coast of Anatolia.[^76] These eastern Greek settlements played a vital role in shaping the Archaic Greek civilization;[^76] [^82] important [cities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polis "Polis") included [Miletus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miletus "Miletus"), [Ephesus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ephesus "Ephesus"), [Halicarnassus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halicarnassus "Halicarnassus"), [Smyrna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smyrna "Smyrna") (now [İzmir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%B0zmir "İzmir")) and [Byzantium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantium "Byzantium") (now [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul")).[^83] These settlements were grouped as [Aeolis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aeolis "Aeolis"), [Ionia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionia "Ionia"), and [Doris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doric_hexapolis "Doric hexapolis"), after the specific Greek groups that settled them.[^84] [^85] Further Greek colonization in Anatolia was led by Miletus and [Megara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megara "Megara") in 750–480 BC.[^86]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/Ephesus_Celsus_Library_Fa%C3%A7ade.jpg/250px-Ephesus_Celsus_Library_Fa%C3%A7ade.jpg)

The Library of Celsus in Ephesus was built by the Romans in 114–117. 87

[Cyrus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyrus_the_Great "Cyrus the Great") attacked eastern Anatolia in 547 BC, and [Achaemenid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire") eventually [expanded into western Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Sardis_\(547_BC\) "Siege of Sardis (547 BC)").[^79] In the east, the [Armenian province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satrapy_of_Armenia "Satrapy of Armenia") was part of the Achaemenid Empire.[^76] Following the [Greco-Persian Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greco-Persian_Wars "Greco-Persian Wars"), the Greek city-states of the Anatolian Aegean coast regained independence, but most of the interior stayed part of the Achaemenid Empire.[^79] Two of the [Seven Wonders of the Ancient World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seven_Wonders_of_the_Ancient_World "Seven Wonders of the Ancient World"), the [Temple of Artemis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temple_of_Artemis "Temple of Artemis") in Ephesus, and the [Mausoleum of Halicarnassus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mausoleum_of_Halicarnassus "Mausoleum of Halicarnassus"), were located in Anatolia.[^88]

Following the victories of Alexander in [334 BC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Granicus "Battle of the Granicus") and [333 BC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Issus "Battle of Issus"), the Achaemenid Empire collapsed.[^79] This led to increasing cultural homogeneity and [Hellenization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenization "Hellenization") of Anatolia,[^89] [^90] [^91] which met resistance in some places.[^92] Following Alexander's death, the [Seleucids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seleucids "Seleucids") ruled large parts of Anatolia, while native Anatolian states emerged in the Marmara and Black Sea areas. In eastern Anatolia, [the kingdom of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_\(antiquity\) "Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)") appeared.[^93] In third century BC, [Celts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celts "Celts") invaded central Anatolia and continued as a major ethnic group in the area for around 200 years. They were known as the [Galatians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galatians_\(people\) "Galatians (people)").[^94]

### Rome and Byzantine Empire

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9b/Justinian555AD.png/250px-Justinian555AD.png)

The Eastern Roman (Byzantine) Empire in 555 under Justinian the Great, at its greatest extent

When [Pergamon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Pergamon "Kingdom of Pergamon") requested assistance in its conflict with the Seleucids, [Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic "Roman Republic") intervened in Anatolia in the second century BC. Without an heir, Pergamum's king left the kingdom to Rome, which was annexed as [province of Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Province_of_Asia "Province of Asia"). Roman influence grew in Anatolia afterwards.[^95] Following [Asiatic Vespers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asiatic_Vespers "Asiatic Vespers") massacre, and [Mithridatic Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mithridatic_Wars "Mithridatic Wars") with [Pontus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Pontus "Kingdom of Pontus"), Rome emerged victorious. Around the 1st century BC, [Rome expanded into parts of Pontus and Bithynia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_province "Roman province"), while turning rest of Anatolian states into Roman satellites.[^96] Several [conflicts with Parthians ensued](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman%E2%80%93Parthian_Wars "Roman–Parthian Wars"), with peace and wars alternating.[^97]

According to [Acts of the Apostles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acts_of_the_Apostles "Acts of the Apostles"), early Christian Church had significant growth in Anatolia because of [St Paul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St_Paul "St Paul") 's efforts. Letters from St. Paul in Anatolia comprise the [oldest Christian literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_Christianity "Early Christianity").[^98] Under Roman authority, [ecumenical councils](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecumenical_councils "Ecumenical councils") such as [Council of Nicaea (Iznik)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Council_of_Nicaea "First Council of Nicaea") in 325 served as a guide for developing "orthodox expressions of basic Christian teachings".[^98]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0b/Aya_Sophia_%287144824757%29.jpg/250px-Aya_Sophia_%287144824757%29.jpg)

The Hagia Sophia in Constantinople (now Istanbul ) was built by the Eastern Roman emperor Justinian the Great in 532–537. 99

The Byzantine Empire, also referred to as the Eastern Roman Empire, was the continuation of the [Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire") centered in [Constantinople](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constantinople "Constantinople") during [Late Antiquity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Antiquity "Late Antiquity") and the [Middle Ages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Ages "Middle Ages"). The eastern half of the Empire survived the conditions that caused the [fall of the West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_the_Western_Roman_Empire "Fall of the Western Roman Empire") in the 5th century AD, and continued to exist until the [fall of Constantinople](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_Constantinople "Fall of Constantinople") to the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") in 1453. During most of its existence, the empire remained the most powerful economic, cultural, and military force in the [Mediterranean world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_world "Mediterranean world"). The term *Byzantine Empire* was only coined following the empire's demise; its citizens referred to the polity as the "Roman Empire" and to themselves as *Romans*. Due to the imperial seat's move from Rome to [Byzantium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantium "Byzantium"), the [adoption of Christianity as the state religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity_as_the_Roman_state_religion "Christianity as the Roman state religion"), and the predominance of [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval_Greek "Medieval Greek") instead of [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "Latin"), modern historians continue to make a distinction between the earlier *Roman Empire* and the later *Byzantine Empire*.

In the early Byzantine Empire period, the Anatolian coastal areas were Greek speaking. In addition to natives, interior Anatolia had diverse groups such as [Goths](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goths "Goths"), [Celts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celts "Celts"), [Persians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persians "Persians") and [Jews](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_the_Byzantine_Empire "History of the Jews in the Byzantine Empire"). Interior Anatolia had been "heavily Hellenized".[^100] [Anatolian languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolian_languages "Anatolian languages") eventually became extinct after [Hellenization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenization "Hellenization") of Anatolia.[^101]

To the west, Anatolia was under the independent rule of [Suleiman ibn Qutalmish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suleiman_ibn_Qutalmish "Suleiman ibn Qutalmish") as the [Sultanate of Rum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate_of_Rum "Sultanate of Rum").

According to historians and linguists, the [Proto-Turkic language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Turkic_language "Proto-Turkic language") originated in Central-East Asia.[^102] Initially, Proto- [Turkic speakers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkic_languages "Turkic languages") were potentially both hunter-gatherers and farmers; they later became [nomadic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nomadic "Nomadic") [pastoralists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pastoralists "Pastoralists").[^102] Early and medieval [Turkic groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkic_peoples "Turkic peoples") exhibited a wide range of both East Asian and West-Eurasian physical appearances and genetic origins, in part through long-term contact with neighboring peoples such as [Iranic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranic "Iranic"), [Mongolic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolic_peoples "Mongolic peoples"), [Tocharian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tocharians "Tocharians"), [Uralic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uralic_people "Uralic people"), and [Yeniseian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yeniseian_people "Yeniseian people") peoples.[^103] During the 9th and 10th centuries CE, [the Oghuz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oghuz_Turks "Oghuz Turks") were a Turkic group that lived in the [Caspian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontic%E2%80%93Caspian_steppe "Pontic–Caspian steppe") and [Aral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aral_Sea "Aral Sea") steppes.[^104] Partly due to pressure from the [Kipchaks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kipchaks "Kipchaks"), the Oghuz migrated into [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_Plateau "Iranian Plateau") and [Transoxiana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transoxiana "Transoxiana").[^104] They mixed with Iranic-speaking groups in the area and converted to [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam").[^104] Oghuz Turks were also known as Turkmen or [Turkoman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkoman_\(ethnonym\) "Turkoman (ethnonym)").[^105] [^104]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/Beylicats_d%25u2019Anatolie_vers_1330-en.svg/500px-Beylicats_d%25u2019Anatolie_vers_1330-en.svg.png)

A map of independent Turkish principalities in Anatolia during the 14th century

The ruling family of [the Seljuks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seljuq_dynasty "Seljuq dynasty") originated from the *[Kınık](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C4%B1n%C4%B1k_\(tribe\) "Kınık (tribe)")* branch of the Oghuz Turks.[^106] In 1040, the Seljuks defeated the [Ghaznavids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghaznavids "Ghaznavids") at the [Battle of Dandanaqan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Dandanaqan "Battle of Dandanaqan") and established the [Seljuk Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seljuk_Empire "Seljuk Empire") in [Greater Khorasan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greater_Khorasan "Greater Khorasan").[^107] [Baghdad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baghdad "Baghdad"), the [Abbasid Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbasid_Caliphate "Abbasid Caliphate") 's capital and center of [the Islamic world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Golden_Age "Islamic Golden Age"), was taken by Seljuks in 1055.[^105] Given the role [Khurasani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khurasani "Khurasani") traditions played in art, culture, and political traditions in the empire, the Seljuk period is described as a mixture of " [Turkish, Persian and Islamic influences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turko-Persian_tradition "Turko-Persian tradition") ".[^108] In the latter half of the 11th century, the Seljuk Turks began penetrating into [medieval Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval_Armenia "Medieval Armenia") and Anatolia.[^105] At the time, Anatolia was a diverse and largely Greek-speaking region after previously being [Hellenized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenized "Hellenized").[^109] [^110] [^100]

The Seljuk Turks defeated the Byzantines at the [Battle of Manzikert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Manzikert "Battle of Manzikert") in 1071, and later established [the Seljuk Sultanate of Rum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate_of_Rum "Sultanate of Rum").[^111] During this period, there were also [Turkish principalities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolian_beyliks "Anatolian beyliks") such as [Danishmendids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danishmendids "Danishmendids").[^112] Seljuk arrival started the [Turkification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkification "Turkification") process in Anatolia;[^110] [^72] there were Turkic/Turkish migrations, intermarriages, and conversions into Islam.[^113] [^114] The shift took several centuries and happened gradually.[^115] [^116] Members of [Islamic mysticism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mysticism#Islamic_mysticism "Mysticism") orders, such as [Mevlevi Order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mevlevi_Order "Mevlevi Order"), played a role in the [Islamization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamization "Islamization") of the diverse people of Anatolia.[^117] [^118] Seljuk expansion was one of the reasons for the [Crusades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crusades "Crusades").[^119] In 13th century, there was a second significant wave of Turkic migration, as people fled [Mongol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_Empire "Mongol Empire") expansion.[^120] [^121] Seljuk sultanate was defeated by the Mongols at the [Battle of Köse Dağ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_K%C3%B6se_Da%C4%9F "Battle of Köse Dağ") in 1243 and disappeared by the beginning of the 14th century. It was replaced by various Turkish principalities.[^24] [^122]

### Ottoman Empire

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/OttomanEmpireMain.png/250px-OttomanEmpireMain.png)

The Ottoman Empire at its greatest European extent, in 1683, during the Battle of Vienna

Based around [Söğüt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%B6%C4%9F%C3%BCt "Söğüt"), [Ottoman Beylik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Beylik "Ottoman Beylik") was founded by [Osman I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Osman_I "Osman I") in the early 14th century.[^123] According to Ottoman chroniclers, Osman descended from the *[Kayı](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kay%C4%B1_\(tribe\) "Kayı (tribe)")* tribe of the [Oghuz Turks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oghuz_Turks "Oghuz Turks").[^124] Ottomans started annexing the nearby Turkish beyliks (principalities) in Anatolia and expanded into the [Balkans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkans "Balkans").[^125] [Mehmed II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mehmed_II "Mehmed II") completed Ottoman conquest of the Byzantine Empire by [capturing its capital, Constantinople](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_Constantinople "Fall of Constantinople"), on 29 May 1453.[^126] [Selim I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selim_I "Selim I") united Anatolia under Ottoman rule.[^127] Turkification continued as Ottomans mixed with various indigenous people in Anatolia and the Balkans.[^124]

The Ottoman Empire was a global power during the reigns of [Selim I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selim_I "Selim I") and [Suleiman the Magnificent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suleiman_the_Magnificent "Suleiman the Magnificent").[^127] [^128] In the 16th and 17th centuries, [Sephardic Jews](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_Jews_in_the_Ottoman_Empire "History of the Jews in the Ottoman Empire") moved into Ottoman Empire following their [expulsion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reconquista#Conversions_and_expulsions "Reconquista") from Spain.[^129] From the second half of the 18th century onwards, the [Ottoman Empire began to decline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decline_of_the_Ottoman_Empire "Decline of the Ottoman Empire"). The [Tanzimat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanzimat "Tanzimat") reforms, initiated by [Mahmud II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahmud_II "Mahmud II") in 1839, aimed to modernize the Ottoman state in line with the progress that had been made in Western Europe. The [Ottoman constitution of 1876](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_constitution_of_1876 "Ottoman constitution of 1876") was the first among Muslim states, but was [short-lived](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Constitutional_Era "First Constitutional Era").[^130] [^131]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/56/View_of_Topkap%C4%B1_Palace_from_the_Galata_Tower%2C_Istanbul%2C_Turkey_001_cropped.jpg/250px-View_of_Topkap%C4%B1_Palace_from_the_Galata_Tower%2C_Istanbul%2C_Turkey_001_cropped.jpg) ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Dolmabah%C3%A7e_Palace%2C_Istanbul_cropped.jpg/250px-Dolmabah%C3%A7e_Palace%2C_Istanbul_cropped.jpg)

[Topkapı Palace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topkap%C4%B1_Palace "Topkapı Palace") and [Dolmabahçe Palace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dolmabah%C3%A7e_Palace "Dolmabahçe Palace") were the primary residences of the [Ottoman sultans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_sultans "Ottoman sultans") in [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul") between 1465 and 1856 [^132] and 1856 to 1922,[^133] respectively.

As the empire gradually shrank in size, military power and wealth; especially after the [Ottoman economic crisis and default](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Eastern_Crisis "Great Eastern Crisis") in 1875 [^134] which led to uprisings in the Balkan provinces that culminated in the [Russo-Turkish War (1877–1878)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_\(1877%E2%80%931878\) "Russo-Turkish War (1877–1878)"). The decline of the Ottoman Empire led to a [rise in nationalist sentiment among its various subject peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rise_of_nationalism_under_the_Ottoman_Empire "Rise of nationalism under the Ottoman Empire"), leading to increased ethnic tensions which occasionally burst into violence, such as the [Hamidian massacres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamidian_massacres "Hamidian massacres") of [Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenians_in_the_Ottoman_Empire "Armenians in the Ottoman Empire"), which claimed up to 300,000 lives.[^135] Ottoman territories in Europe ([Rumelia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rumelia "Rumelia")) were lost in the [First Balkan War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Balkan_War "First Balkan War") (1912–1913).[^136] Ottomans managed to recover some territory in Europe, such as [Edirne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edirne "Edirne"), in the [Second Balkan War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Balkan_War "Second Balkan War") (1913).

In the 19th and early 20th centuries, [persecution of Muslims during the Ottoman contraction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Muslims_during_the_Ottoman_contraction "Persecution of Muslims during the Ottoman contraction") and [in the Russian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Muslims#Russian_Empire "Persecution of Muslims") resulted in estimated 5 million deaths,[^137] [^138] with the casualties including Turks.[^138] Five to seven or seven to nine million [refugees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhacir "Muhacir") migrated into modern-day Turkey from the [Balkans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkans "Balkans"), [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus "Caucasus"), [Crimea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_Khanate "Crimean Khanate"), and [Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean "Mediterranean") islands,[^139] shifting the center of the Ottoman Empire to Anatolia.[^140] In addition to a small number of Jews, the refugees were overwhelmingly Muslim; they were both Turkish and non-Turkish people, such as [Circassians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circassians "Circassians") and [Crimean Tatars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_Tatars "Crimean Tatars").[^141] [^142] [Paul Mojzes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Mojzes "Paul Mojzes") has called the Balkan Wars an "unrecognized genocide", where multiple sides were both victims and perpetrators.[^143] Circassian refugees included the survivors of the [Circassian genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circassian_genocide "Circassian genocide").[^144]

Following the [1913 coup d'état](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1913_Ottoman_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "1913 Ottoman coup d'état"), the [Three Pashas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Pashas "Three Pashas") took control of the Ottoman government. The Ottoman Empire entered [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I") on the side of the [Central Powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Powers "Central Powers") and was ultimately defeated.[^145] During the war, the empire's Armenian subjects were [deported to Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temporary_Law_of_Deportation "Temporary Law of Deportation") as part of the [Armenian genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_genocide "Armenian genocide"). As a result, an estimated 600,000 [^146] to more than 1 million,[^146] or up to 1.5 million [^147] [^148] [^149] [Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenians_in_the_Ottoman_Empire "Armenians in the Ottoman Empire") were killed. The Turkish government has [refused to acknowledge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_genocide_denial "Armenian genocide denial") [^150] [^151] the events as genocide and states that Armenians were only ["relocated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_transfer "Population transfer") " from the eastern war zone.[^152] [Genocidal campaigns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Ottoman_genocides "Late Ottoman genocides") were also committed against the empire's other minority groups such as the [Assyrians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assyrian_genocide "Assyrian genocide") and [Greeks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_genocide "Greek genocide").[^153] [^154] [^155] Following the [Armistice of Mudros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armistice_of_Mudros "Armistice of Mudros") in 1918, the victorious [Allied Powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I "Allies of World War I") sought the [partition of the Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_the_Ottoman_Empire "Partition of the Ottoman Empire") through the 1920 [Treaty of Sèvres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_S%C3%A8vres "Treaty of Sèvres").[^156]

### Republic of Türkiye

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/91/Atat%C3%BCrk_%C5%9Fapkas%C4%B1yla_selam_verirken.jpg/250px-Atat%C3%BCrk_%C5%9Fapkas%C4%B1yla_selam_verirken.jpg)

Mustafa Kemal Atatürk, the founder and the first President of the Turkish Republic

The [occupation of Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupation_of_Istanbul "Occupation of Istanbul") (1918) and [İzmir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupation_of_Smyrna "Occupation of Smyrna") (1919) by the [Allies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I "Allies of World War I") in the aftermath of World War I initiated the [Turkish National Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_National_Movement "Turkish National Movement"). Under the leadership of [Mustafa Kemal Pasha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mustafa_Kemal_Pasha "Mustafa Kemal Pasha"), a military commander who had distinguished himself during the [Battle of Gallipoli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Gallipoli "Battle of Gallipoli"), the [Turkish War of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_War_of_Independence "Turkish War of Independence") (1919–1923) was waged with the aim of revoking the terms of the [Treaty of Sèvres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_S%C3%A8vres "Treaty of Sèvres") (1920).[^157]

The [Turkish Provisional Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_Provisional_Government "Turkish Provisional Government") in [Ankara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ankara "Ankara"), which had declared itself the legitimate government of the country on [23 April 1920](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_National_Assembly_of_Turkey "Grand National Assembly of Turkey"), started to formalize the legal transition from the old Ottoman into the new Republican political system. The Ankara Government engaged in armed and diplomatic struggle. In 1921–1923, the Armenian, Greek, French, and British armies had been expelled.[^158] [^159] [^160] [^161] The military advance and diplomatic success of the Ankara Government resulted in the signing of the [Armistice of Mudanya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armistice_of_Mudanya "Armistice of Mudanya") on 11 October 1922. On 1 November 1922, the Turkish Parliament in Ankara formally abolished the Sultanate, thus ending 623 years of [monarchical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchical "Monarchical") Ottoman rule.

The [Treaty of Lausanne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Lausanne "Treaty of Lausanne") of 24 July 1923, which superseded the Treaty of Sèvres,[^156] [^157] led to the international recognition of the sovereignty of the new Turkish state as the successor state of the Ottoman Empire. On 4 October 1923, the Allied occupation of Turkey ended with the withdrawal of the last Allied troops from [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupation_of_Istanbul "Occupation of Istanbul"). The Turkish Republic [was officially proclaimed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_Republic_of_Turkey "Proclamation of the Republic of Turkey") on 29 October 1923 in Ankara, the country's new capital.[^162] The [Lausanne Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convention_Concerning_the_Exchange_of_Greek_and_Turkish_Populations "Convention Concerning the Exchange of Greek and Turkish Populations") stipulated a [population exchange between Greece and Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_exchange_between_Greece_and_Turkey "Population exchange between Greece and Turkey").[^163]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b3/Ankara_asv2021-10_img04_An%C4%B1tkabir.jpg/250px-Ankara_asv2021-10_img04_An%C4%B1tkabir.jpg)

Anıtkabir in Ankara was completed in 1953 to become the mausoleum of Mustafa Kemal Atatürk.

Mustafa Kemal became the republic's first [president](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Turkey "President of Turkey") and introduced [many reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atat%C3%BCrk%27s_reforms "Atatürk's reforms"). The reforms aimed to transform the old [religion-based and multi-communal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Millet_\(Ottoman_Empire\) "Millet (Ottoman Empire)") Ottoman monarchy into a Turkish [nation state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation_state "Nation state") that would be governed as a [parliamentary republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_republic "Parliamentary republic") under a [secular constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_Constitution_of_1924 "Turkish Constitution of 1924").[^164] Women gained the right to vote nationally in 1934.[^165] With the [Surname Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surname_Law "Surname Law"), the [Turkish Parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_Parliament "Turkish Parliament") bestowed upon Kemal the honorific surname "Atatürk" (*Father Turk*).[^157] Atatürk's reforms caused discontent in some [Kurdish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kurds "Kurds") and [Zaza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zazas "Zazas") tribes leading to the [Sheikh Said rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheikh_Said_rebellion "Sheikh Said rebellion") in 1925 [^166] and the [Dersim rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dersim_rebellion "Dersim rebellion") in 1937.[^167]

[İsmet İnönü](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%B0smet_%C4%B0n%C3%B6n%C3%BC "İsmet İnönü") became the country's second president following Atatürk's death in 1938. In 1939, the [Republic of Hatay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Hatay "Republic of Hatay") voted in favor of joining Turkey with a referendum. Turkey [remained neutral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Cairo_Conference "Second Cairo Conference") during almost all of [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"),[^168] but entered the war on the side of the [Allies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II "Allies of World War II") on [23 February 1945](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_declaration_of_war_on_Germany_and_Japan "Turkish declaration of war on Germany and Japan").[^169] Later that year, Turkey became a [charter member](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Charter "United Nations Charter") of the United Nations.[^170] In 1950 Turkey became a member of the [Council of Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Europe "Council of Europe"). After fighting as part of the [UN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UN "UN") forces in the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War"), Turkey joined [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO") in 1952, becoming a bulwark against Soviet expansion into the [Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Basin "Mediterranean Basin").

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a8/Visit_of_Tansu_%C3%87iller%2C_Turkish_Prime_Minister%2C_to_the_EC_6.jpg/250px-Visit_of_Tansu_%C3%87iller%2C_Turkish_Prime_Minister%2C_to_the_EC_6.jpg)

Tansu Çiller, Turkey's first female prime minister, attends a European Commission meeting in January 1994.

Military coups or memorandums, which happened in [1960](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1960_Turkish_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "1960 Turkish coup d'état"), [1971](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1971_Turkish_military_memorandum "1971 Turkish military memorandum"), [1980](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1980_Turkish_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "1980 Turkish coup d'état"), and [1997](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1997_Turkish_military_memorandum "1997 Turkish military memorandum"), complicated Turkey's transition to a democratic [multiparty system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-party_period_of_the_Republic_of_Turkey "Multi-party period of the Republic of Turkey").[^171] [^172] Between 1960 and the end of the 20th century, the prominent leaders in Turkish politics who achieved multiple election victories were [Süleyman Demirel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%BCleyman_Demirel "Süleyman Demirel"), [Bülent Ecevit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B%C3%BClent_Ecevit "Bülent Ecevit") and [Turgut Özal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turgut_%C3%96zal "Turgut Özal"). PKK started a "campaign of terrorist attacks on civilian and military targets" in the 1980s.[^173] It is designated as a [terrorist organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrorist_organization "Terrorist organization") by Turkey,[^174] the United States,[^175] and the European Union.[^176] [Tansu Çiller](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tansu_%C3%87iller "Tansu Çiller") became the first female prime minister of Turkey in 1993. Turkey applied for full membership of the [EEC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EEC "EEC") in 1987, joined the [European Union Customs Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union_Customs_Union "European Union Customs Union") in 1995 and started [accession negotiations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accession_of_Turkey_to_the_European_Union "Accession of Turkey to the European Union") with the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") in 2005.[^177] [^178] Customs Union had an important impact on the Turkish manufacturing sector.[^179] [^180]

In 2014, prime minister Recep Tayyip Erdoğan won Turkey's first direct [presidential election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Turkish_presidential_election "2014 Turkish presidential election").[^181] On 15 July 2016, an [unsuccessful coup attempt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Turkish_coup_attempt "2016 Turkish coup attempt") tried to oust the government.[^182] According to the Turkish government, there are 13,251 arrested or convicted people in jail as of 2024, related to the 2016 coup attempt.[^183] [^184] With a [referendum in 2017](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Turkish_constitutional_referendum "2017 Turkish constitutional referendum"), the parliamentary republic was replaced by an [executive presidential system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_president "Executive president"). The office of the prime minister was abolished, and its powers and duties were transferred to the president. On the referendum day, while the voting was still underway, the [Supreme Electoral Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_Election_Council_\(Turkey\) "Supreme Election Council (Turkey)") lifted a rule that required each ballot to have an official stamp.[^185] The opposition parties claimed that as many as 2.5 million [ballots without a stamp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Turkish_constitutional_referendum#Unstamped_ballots "2017 Turkish constitutional referendum") were accepted as valid.[^185] In 2025 the PKK declared a ceasefire.[^186]

## Geography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/db/Turkey_topo.jpg/250px-Turkey_topo.jpg)

Topographic map of Turkey

Turkey covers an area of 783,562 square kilometres (302,535 square miles).[^187] With [Turkish straits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_straits "Turkish straits") and [Sea of Marmara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara "Sea of Marmara") in between, Turkey bridges [Western Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Asia "Western Asia") and [Southeastern Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeastern_Europe "Southeastern Europe").[^188] Turkey's Asian side covers 97% of its surface, and is often called [Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolia "Anatolia").[^189] Another definition of Anatolia's eastern boundary is an imprecise line from the [Black Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea "Black Sea") to [Gulf of Iskenderun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Iskenderun "Gulf of Iskenderun").[^190] [Eastern Thrace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Thrace "Eastern Thrace"), Turkey's European side, includes around 15% of the population and covers 3% of the surface area.[^191] The country is encircled by seas on three sides: the [Aegean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_Sea "Aegean Sea") to the west, the Black Sea to the north and the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea") to the south.[^192] Turkey is bordered by Georgia, Armenia, Azerbaijan and Iran to the east.[^192] To the south, it's bordered by Syria and Iraq.[^193] To the north, its Thracian area is bordered by Greece and Bulgaria.[^192]

Turkey is divided into " [seven major regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographical_regions_of_Turkey "Geographical regions of Turkey") ": [Marmara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marmara_Region "Marmara Region"), [Aegean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_Region "Aegean Region"), [Central Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Anatolia "Central Anatolia"), [Black Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea_Region "Black Sea Region"), [Eastern Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Anatolia "Eastern Anatolia"), [Southeastern Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeastern_Anatolia "Southeastern Anatolia") and the [Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Region,_Turkey "Mediterranean Region, Turkey").[^192] As a general trend, the inland [Anatolian Plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolian_Plateau "Anatolian Plateau") becomes increasingly rugged as it progresses eastward.[^194] Mountain ranges include [Köroğlu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%B6ro%C4%9Flu_Mountains "Köroğlu Mountains") and [Pontic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontic_Mountains "Pontic Mountains") mountain ranges to the north, and the [Taurus Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taurus_Mountains "Taurus Mountains") to the south. The [Lakes Region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_Lakes_Region "Turkish Lakes Region") contains some of the largest lakes in Turkey such as [Lake Beyşehir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Bey%C5%9Fehir "Lake Beyşehir") and [Lake Eğirdir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_E%C4%9Firdir "Lake Eğirdir").

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/T%C3%BCrkiye_tectonic_map.jpg/250px-T%C3%BCrkiye_tectonic_map.jpg)

Tectonic map of Turkey. 195 Straight lines and lines with triangles denote different types of faults, such as North Anatolian Fault and East Anatolian Fault.

Geographers have used the eastern Anatolian plateau, Iranian plateau, and [Armenian plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_plateau "Armenian plateau") terms to refer to the mountainous area around where [Arabian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Plate "Arabian Plate") and [Eurasian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasian_Plate "Eurasian Plate") tectonic plates merge. The eastern Anatolian plateau and Armenian plateau definitions largely overlap.[^196] The [Eastern Anatolia region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Anatolia_region "Eastern Anatolia region") contains [Mount Ararat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Ararat "Mount Ararat"), Turkey's highest point at 5,137 metres (16,854 feet),[^197] and [Lake Van](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Van "Lake Van"), the largest lake in the country.[^198] Eastern Turkey is home to the sources of rivers such as the [Euphrates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euphrates "Euphrates"), [Tigris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tigris "Tigris") and [Aras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aras_River "Aras River"). The [Southeastern Anatolia Region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeastern_Anatolia_Region "Southeastern Anatolia Region") includes the northern plains of [Upper Mesopotamia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_Mesopotamia "Upper Mesopotamia").

[Earthquakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_earthquakes_in_Turkey "List of earthquakes in Turkey") happen frequently in Turkey.[^199] Almost the entire population lives in areas with varying seismic risk levels, with around 70% in highest or second-highest seismic areas.[^200] [^201] [Anatolian plate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolian_plate "Anatolian plate") is bordered by [North Anatolian Fault](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Anatolian_Fault "North Anatolian Fault") zone to the north; [East Anatolian Fault](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Anatolian_Fault "East Anatolian Fault") zone and Bitlis–Zagros collision zone to the east; [Hellenic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenic_subduction_zone "Hellenic subduction zone") and Cyprus subduction zones to the south; and [Aegean extensional zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_Sea_Plate "Aegean Sea Plate") to the west.[^202] After [1999 İzmit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1999_%C4%B0zmit_earthquake "1999 İzmit earthquake") and [1999 Düzce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1999_D%C3%BCzce_earthquake "1999 Düzce earthquake") earthquakes, North Anatolian Fault zone activity "is considered to be one of the most dangerous natural hazards in Turkey".[^203] [2023 Turkey–Syria earthquakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Turkey%E2%80%93Syria_earthquakes "2023 Turkey–Syria earthquakes") were the deadliest in contemporary Turkish history.[^204] Turkey is sometimes unfavorably compared to [Chile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chile "Chile"), a country with a similar [developmental level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_Human_Development_Index "List of countries by Human Development Index") that is more successful with [earthquake preparedness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earthquake_preparedness "Earthquake preparedness").[^205] [^206] [^207]

### Biodiversity

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Tulip_Festival_Emirgan_2018.jpg/250px-Tulip_Festival_Emirgan_2018.jpg)

Tulip is a national flower of Turkey. Emirgan Park in Istanbul hosts Tulip Festival each year.

Turkey has rich biodiversity,[^208] [^209] [^210] and includes three [biodiversity hotspots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity_hotspot "Biodiversity hotspot") [^209] [^210] out of the 36 in the world.[^210] These are the [Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Basin "Mediterranean Basin"), [Irano-Anatolian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irano-Anatolian "Irano-Anatolian"), and [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus "Caucasus") hotspots.[^210] [Central](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Anatolian_steppe "Central Anatolian steppe") or [Eastern Anatolian steppe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Anatolian_montane_steppe "Eastern Anatolian montane steppe") ecosystems contain wild animal and plant species whose genetic variation have important implications for the food industry, agriculture, and medicine. [Other ecosystems in Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_ecoregions_in_Turkey "List of ecoregions in Turkey") include [Mediterranean plant types](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_forests,_woodlands,_and_scrub "Mediterranean forests, woodlands, and scrub"), [temperate deciduous forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperate_deciduous_forest "Temperate deciduous forest"), and [Alpine tundra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpine_tundra "Alpine tundra").[^211] An extensive number of species of plants and animals exist in Turkey.[^211] These include around 197 [mammal species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_mammals_of_Turkey "List of mammals of Turkey"), 420 [bird species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_birds_of_Turkey "List of birds of Turkey"), 820 marine and freshwater fish species, 130 [reptile species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_reptiles_of_Turkey "List of reptiles of Turkey"), and 10,150 vascular [plant species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora_of_Turkey "Flora of Turkey").[^212] Some of these are [endangered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endangered "Endangered") or [critically endangered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Critically_endangered "Critically endangered").[^211] A lot of the animal and plant species are [endemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endemic "Endemic").[^210]

Landscapes in Turkey face a variety of threats and risks such as [desertification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desertification "Desertification"), [soil erosion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_erosion "Soil erosion"), declining [groundwater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Groundwater "Groundwater") levels, and [salinization of soil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_salinity "Soil salinity"). 27% of Turkey is protected, which includes conservation areas, forests, [national parks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_national_parks_of_Turkey "List of national parks of Turkey"), biosphere reserves, [natural monuments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_natural_monuments_of_Turkey "List of natural monuments of Turkey"), and historical sites.[^213] There are 50 national parks;[^214] most visited in 2022 were [Marmaris National Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marmaris_National_Park "Marmaris National Park") and [Beydağları Coastal National Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beyda%C4%9Flar%C4%B1_Coastal_National_Park "Beydağları Coastal National Park").[^215] As of 2020, [Turkey's forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forests_in_Turkey "Forests in Turkey") covered 29% of Turkey's surface area.[^216] Turkey's forests include globally largest concentrations of [Cedrus libani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cedrus_libani "Cedrus libani") and [Mediterranean cypress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_cypress "Mediterranean cypress") trees, while [Turkey's wetlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wetlands_of_Turkey "Wetlands of Turkey") are significant for [migratory birds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Migratory_birds "Migratory birds").[^211]

### Climate

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/df/Temperature_and_Precipitation_in_Turkey.png/500px-Temperature_and_Precipitation_in_Turkey.png) ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/K%C3%B6ppen_types_of_Turkey.jpg/500px-K%C3%B6ppen_types_of_Turkey.jpg)

Clockwise from top left image: Yearly total precipitation, yearly average temperatures, and Köppen climate types in Turkey [^217]

Turkey's climate is highly varied due to rising overall elevation eastwards and the mountains that run alongside the coasts.[^188] Warm summers, "cool to cold" winters, and year-round wet conditions are found in the northern coasts. Rainless and hot summers, and wet, cool to mild winters describe the western and southern coasts of the country.[^218] Inland areas feature more severe winters, sharper temperature swings,[^218] and relatively low precipitation.[^219]

According to the [Köppen climate classification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification"), Turkey has 14 distinct climate zones.[^217] The [Mediterranean climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_climate "Mediterranean climate") is found in the Mediterranean, Aegean, Marmara, and Southeastern Anatolia regions of the country. In the Black Sea area, [humid subtropical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humid_subtropical "Humid subtropical") and [oceanic climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceanic_climate "Oceanic climate") types are usually found. Large swaths of Central and Eastern Anatolia are classified into [semi-arid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-arid "Semi-arid") or [continental climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_climate "Continental climate") types.[^220] [^221]

Turkey is highly [vulnerable to climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vulnerable_to_climate_change "Vulnerable to climate change"),[^222] [^223] due to socioeconomic, climatic, and geographic factors.[^222] This applies to nine out of ten climate vulnerability dimensions, such as "average annual risk to wellbeing".[^222] [OECD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OECD "OECD") median is two out of ten.[^222] Inclusive and swift growth is needed for decreasing vulnerability.[^224] Turkey aims to achieve [net zero emissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Net_zero_emissions "Net zero emissions") by 2053.[^225] [^226] Accomplishing climate goals would require large investments, but would benefit the economy due to less illness and death from [Turkey's air pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_pollution_in_Turkey "Air pollution in Turkey"), and less fuel imports.[^227]
