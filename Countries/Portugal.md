**Portugal**,[^3] officially the **Portuguese Republic**,[^4] is a country in [Southwestern Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southwestern_Europe "Southwestern Europe"). It is a unitary republic comprising [mainland Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_Portugal "Continental Portugal"), located on the southwestern of the [Iberian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula "Iberian Peninsula") and [bordered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal-Spain_border "Portugal-Spain border") by Spain to the north and east, and the archipelagos of [Madeira](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madeira "Madeira") and the [Azores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azores "Azores") in the Atlantic Ocean. The country has [a population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographics_of_Portugal "Demographics of Portugal") of over 10.7 million, and Lisbon, its capital, is the largest city. Portugal's internal waters and territorial sea together account for two-fifths of its territory, and its [exclusive economic zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone_of_Portugal "Exclusive economic zone of Portugal") is one of Europe's largest, while its terrain contains a diverse range of landscapes and regional climates.

The western Iberian Peninsula has been inhabited since [prehistory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prehistoric_Iberia "Prehistoric Iberia"), with the earliest signs of settlement dating to between 5500 BC and 5300 BC. Portugal was established as a county of the Kingdom of León in 868, and [formally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifestis_Probatum "Manifestis Probatum") as a kingdom in 1179 as a result of the *[Reconquista](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal_in_the_Reconquista "Portugal in the Reconquista")* against the Muslims, who had occupied the Iberian Peninsula [since 711](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Guadalete "Battle of Guadalete"). During the [Age of Discovery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Discovery "Age of Discovery"), the kingdom made several advancements in nautical science and maritime exploration to discover new territories and sea routes, which led to the establishment of the [Portuguese Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Empire "Portuguese Empire"). The kingdom [became a republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/5_October_1910_revolution "5 October 1910 revolution") in 1910 and was a dictatorship [from 1926](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/28_May_1926_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "28 May 1926 coup d'état") until [the dictatorship's overthrow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnation_Revolution "Carnation Revolution") in 1974 enabled the full establishment of democracy in 1976.

Portugal is a semi-presidential constitutional unitary republic and multi-party representative democracy with four separate [sovereignty bodies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereignty_body_\(Portugal\) "Sovereignty body (Portugal)"): president, government, parliament, and judiciary. It has a unicameral national legislature known as the [Assembly of the Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assembly_of_the_Republic_\(Portugal\) "Assembly of the Republic (Portugal)"). Portugal is divided into [two autonomous regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomous_Regions_of_Portugal "Autonomous Regions of Portugal") and [seven intermunicipal communities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intermunicipal_communities_of_Portugal "Intermunicipal communities of Portugal") while it remains highly centralised.

A developed country, Portugal has an advanced economy that chiefly relies upon services, industry, and tourism. Shaped by the various civilisations that have inhabited its territory, Portugal developed a [culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Portugal "Culture of Portugal") with a worldwide influence that allowed [Portuguese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language "Portuguese language") to become the world's [fifth-most spoken native language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_languages_by_number_of_native_speakers "List of languages by number of native speakers") with more than 250 million native speakers. With a stable foreign policy shaped by its colonial and diplomatic history and location, Portugal is a member of [multiple international organisations and forums](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_organization_membership_of_Portugal "International organization membership of Portugal").

## Etymology

The word *Portugal* derives from [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "Latin") *[Portus Cale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portus_Cale "Portus Cale")*, meaning 'port of *Cale* ', itself the origin of [Porto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porto "Porto"), while *Cale* was a town on the [Douro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douro "Douro"). The commonly accepted explanation regarding the etymology of Cale is that it is an [ethnonym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnonym "Ethnonym") derived from the Celtic [Gallaeci](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gallaeci "Gallaeci") peoples, who occupied the north-west of the Iberian Peninsula.[^26] [^27]

Around 136 BC, the [Romans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Rome "Ancient Rome"), during the [Second Punic War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Punic_War "Second Punic War"), conquered Cale from the [Carthaginians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punic_people "Punic people") and renamed it *Portus Cale*.[^28] [^29] During the [Middle Ages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Ages "Middle Ages"), the region around Portus Cale became known by the [Visigoths](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visigoths "Visigoths") as *Portucale* and by the [Suebi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suebi "Suebi") as *Parochiale*.[^29] The name *Portucale* evolved into *Portugale*; by the 11th and 12th centuries *Portugal* was referred to the region between the Douro and [Minho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minho_River "Minho River") rivers.[^30]

## History

### Prehistory

The region has been inhabited by humans since approximately 400,000 years ago.[^5] [Neanderthals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neanderthal "Neanderthal") roamed the southwestern Iberian peninsula until 37,000 years ago, and a Neanderthal tooth has been found at the Nova da Columbeira Cave in [Estremadura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Estremadura_Province_\(1936%E2%80%9376\) "Estremadura Province (1936–76)").[^32] *[Homo sapiens sapiens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_sapiens_sapiens "Homo sapiens sapiens")* arrived in what is now Portugal around 35,000 years ago and spread rapidly.[^33] The onset of the [Neolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic_Europe "Neolithic Europe") in the area dates to between 5500 BC and 5300 BC.[^34] In southern Portugal, [Iron Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron_Age_Europe "Iron Age Europe") inscribed [stele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stele "Stele") have been found, representing the earliest known evidence of writing on the Iberian Peninsula.[^35] Pre-Celtic tribes inhabited Portugal and the [Lusitanians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusitanians "Lusitanians") occupied central, inland regions.[^36] [Celts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celts "Celts") inhabited most of the remaining territory of mainland Portugal, leaving lasting traces in the language and culture such as contributing to the formation of Portuguese values and identity, such as *[saudade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudade "Saudade")*.[^37] [^38]

### Antiquity and early Middle Ages

![Ruins of the Roman Temple of Évora with tall Corinthian columns standing on a raised platform made of rocks in a sunlit town square, set against a bright blue sky, with a medieval tower and white buildings in the background.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Roman_Temple_Evora_Portugal_02.jpg/250px-Roman_Temple_Evora_Portugal_02.jpg)

The Roman Temple of Évora, built in the 1st century

The Mediterranean coast was well known to the [Carthaginians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Carthage "Ancient Carthage"), whose close relations with Phoenician-Punic settlements likely gave them considerable political and economic influence there from the 6th or 5th century BC. After Carthage's defeat by Rome in the [First Punic War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Punic_War "First Punic War"), it sought new western territories, culminating in the [Barcid conquest of the Iberian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barcid_conquest_of_Hispania "Barcid conquest of Hispania") in 237 BC.[^39]

Romans first [invaded the Iberian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Punic_War "Second Punic War") in 218 BC.[^40] The Carthaginians were expelled from the peninsula in 206 BC after the [Battle of Ilipa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Ilipa "Battle of Ilipa").[^41] [^42] Within 200 years, almost the entire Iberian Peninsula had been annexed by the [Roman Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic "Roman Republic"), facing [resistance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusitanian_Wars "Lusitanian Wars") from local tribes, such as the Lusitanians led by [Viriathus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viriathus "Viriathus").[^43]

In 409, with the decline of the [Western Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Roman_Empire "Western Roman Empire"), the Iberian Peninsula was invaded by [Germanic tribes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germanic_tribes "Germanic tribes").[^44] Western Iberia was integrated into the [Suebian Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suebian_Kingdom "Suebian Kingdom"), with its capital in [Braga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Braga "Braga").[^45] The Visigoths defeated the Suebi and ruled the peninsula until the early 8th century.[^46] [^47]

In 711, the Iberian Peninsula was [invaded from the south](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_conquest_of_the_Iberian_Peninsula "Muslim conquest of the Iberian Peninsula") by the [Umayyad Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate "Umayyad Caliphate"), which expanded rapidly and by 716 most of the peninsula was part of [al-Andalus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Andalus "Al-Andalus").[^48] The Umayyads ruled al-Andalus until it fragmented into [small states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taifa "Taifa"), with the caliphate being abolished in 1031,[^48] and most of what is now Portugal falling under the [Taifa of Badajoz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taifa_of_Badajoz "Taifa of Badajoz"). These were conquered by the [Almoravids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almoravids "Almoravids"), who were in turn conquered by the [Almohads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almohads "Almohads").[^49] [^50] Northern incursions also occurred during this period, with [Viking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viking "Viking") raids recorded from 844 into the 11th century.[^51]

### Reconquista and independence

![Statue of Vímara Peres](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6b/Estatua_de_Vimara_Peres%2C_Oporto%2C_Portugal%2C_2012-05-09%2C_DD_01.JPG/250px-Estatua_de_Vimara_Peres%2C_Oporto%2C_Portugal%2C_2012-05-09%2C_DD_01.JPG)

Vímara Peres

The *[Reconquista](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reconquista "Reconquista")* was a series of military campaigns by northern Iberian Christian polities against the Muslim-ruled [al-Andalus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Andalus "Al-Andalus").[^52] It began with the defeat of Umayyad forces at the [Battle of Covadonga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Covadonga "Battle of Covadonga") by [legendary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legend "Legend") [Pelagius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pelagius_of_Asturias "Pelagius of Asturias"), a Visigothic nobleman.[^53] [^54] In 868, the region between the Minho and the Douro rivers was secured from the [Moors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moors "Moors") and constituted as the [County of Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/County_of_Portugal "County of Portugal") under [Vímara Peres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/V%C3%ADmara_Peres "Vímara Peres").[^55] The first county of Portugal lasted until it was abolished following the [Battle of Pedroso](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Pedroso "Battle of Pedroso"), in 1071.[^56] In 1096, [Alfonso VI of León](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfonso_VI_of_Le%C3%B3n "Alfonso VI of León") refounded the county and bestowed it on [Henry of Burgundy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry,_Count_of_Portugal "Henry, Count of Portugal"), who married Alfonso's [illegitimate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legitimacy_\(family_law\) "Legitimacy (family law)") daughter, [Teresa of León](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theresa,_Countess_of_Portugal "Theresa, Countess of Portugal").[^57]

![Medieval miniature of King Afonso I](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/D._Afonso_Henriques_-_Compendio_de_cr%C3%B3nicas_de_reyes_%28Biblioteca_Nacional_de_Espa%C3%B1a%29.png/250px-D._Afonso_Henriques_-_Compendio_de_cr%C3%B3nicas_de_reyes_%28Biblioteca_Nacional_de_Espa%C3%B1a%29.png)

King Afonso I

A dispute erupted after the death of Count Henry in 1112, culminating in the [Battle of São Mamede](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_S%C3%A3o_Mamede "Battle of São Mamede") in 1128 whereby [Afonso Henriques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afonso_I_of_Portugal "Afonso I of Portugal") defeated the forces of his mother [Countess Teresa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teresa,_Countess_of_Portugal "Teresa, Countess of Portugal") and her lover [Fernando Pérez de Traba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fernando_P%C3%A9rez_de_Traba "Fernando Pérez de Traba"), establishing himself as sole leader of the [county](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/County_of_Portugal "County of Portugal").[^58] In 1139 he won the [Battle of Ourique](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Ourique "Battle of Ourique") and took the title of [King](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_of_Portugal "King of Portugal").[^59] Afonso's claim is documented as having been recognized by [Alfonso VII of León](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alfonso_VII_of_Le%C3%B3n "Alfonso VII of León") at the [Conference of Zamora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conference_of_Zamora "Conference of Zamora") in 1143, and by [Pope Alexander III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope_Alexander_III "Pope Alexander III") in 1179 through the [papal bull](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papal_bull "Papal bull") *[Manifestis Probatum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manifestis_Probatum "Manifestis Probatum")*.[^60] [^61] [^62]

With the support of north-European [crusaders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crusades "Crusades") and Christian [military orders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_order_\(religious_society\) "Military order (religious society)"), Afonso Henriques and his successors continued pushing south until the [capture of Algarve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_conquest_of_the_Algarve "Portuguese conquest of the Algarve"), which was concluded in 1249.[^63] [^64] With minor readjustments, Portugal's borders have largely remained the same since the signing of the [Treaty of Alcanizes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Alca%C3%B1ices "Treaty of Alcañices") in 1297, making it one of the oldest established international borders in Europe.[^65] Between 1348 and 1349 Portugal was devastated by the [Black Death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Death "Black Death"), as was the rest of Europe.[^66] In 1373, during the reign of [King Ferdinand I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_I_of_Portugal "Ferdinand I of Portugal") and in the midst of the [Fernandine Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fernandine_Wars "Fernandine Wars"), Portugal made an [alliance with England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Portuguese_Alliance "Anglo-Portuguese Alliance"), which would be later strengthened by the signing of the [Treaty of Windsor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Windsor_\(1386\) "Treaty of Windsor (1386)"), making it the oldest standing alliance in the world.[^67] [^68]

### Age of Discovery

![Painting showing 10 Portuguese vessels near a fortified walled town with a tower rising above the harbor where several vessels lie at anchor, wooded hills stretch into the background, and a distant island appears offshore, with colors shifting from brown-green in the foreground to blue in the distance.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/98/Portuguese_Carracks_off_a_Rocky_Coast_RMG_BHC0705_%281%29.png/250px-Portuguese_Carracks_off_a_Rocky_Coast_RMG_BHC0705_%281%29.png)

The Portuguese Carracks Off a Rocky Coast painting displays Portuguese vessels of the Age of Discovery during the 16th century.

In 1383, [John I of Castile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_I_of_Castile "John I of Castile") and [Beatrice of Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beatrice_of_Portugal "Beatrice of Portugal"), the only surviving legitimate child of Ferdinand I of Portugal, claimed the throne of Portugal.[^69] [John of Aviz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_I_of_Portugal "John I of Portugal") led a revolt against him and defeated the Castilians in the [Battle of Aljubarrota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Aljubarrota "Battle of Aljubarrota"),[^70] [^71] [^72] with the [House of Aviz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Aviz "House of Aviz") becoming the ruling house that led Portugal to prominence in European politics and culture.[^73] [^69] [^74] Portugal acquired its first colonies by [conquering Ceuta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_conquest_of_Ceuta "Portuguese conquest of Ceuta") in 1415.[^69] The country led the Age of Discovery under the sponsorship of [Prince Henry the Navigator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_Henry_the_Navigator "Prince Henry the Navigator"),[^75] [^76] and made several advancements in [nautical science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_nautical_science "Portuguese nautical science").[^77] [^6]

Portugal was the first and the last colonial power in the era of modern European [colonialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colonialism "Colonialism"),[^7] and by the mid-16th century it controlled a network of possessions stretching from Lisbon to [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanban_trade "Nanban trade") and [Timor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Timor "Portuguese Timor") through [Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colonial_Brazil "Colonial Brazil").[^82] Throughout the 15th and 16th centuries, [Portuguese explorers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_exploration_of_Africa#Portuguese "European exploration of Africa") sailed the coast of Africa, establishing trading posts [for commodities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_history_of_Portugal#Expansion_of_the_Portuguese_empire_\(15th_and_16th_centuries\) "Economic history of Portugal") especially [gold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold "Gold") and [slaves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavery_in_Portugal "Slavery in Portugal"), explored the [Indian Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean "Indian Ocean") and eastern Asia, established trade routes in most of southern Asia taxing most trade criss-crossing the Indian Ocean.[^83] [^84] [^85] The 1494 [Treaty of Tordesillas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Tordesillas "Treaty of Tordesillas") divided newly encountered non-European territories between Portugal and Spain along a [meridian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meridian_\(geography\) "Meridian (geography)") west of [Cape Verde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_of_Cape_Verde "Geography of Cape Verde"), while the 1529 [Treaty of Zaragoza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Zaragoza "Treaty of Zaragoza") extended this partition to the Pacific Ocean.[^86] In 1498, [Vasco da Gama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vasco_da_Gama "Vasco da Gama") reached India by sea, and two years later [Pedro Álvares Cabral landed in Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Discovery_of_Brazil "Discovery of Brazil") and claimed it for Portugal.[^87] [^88] Between 1519 and 1522, [Ferdinand Magellan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_Magellan "Ferdinand Magellan") organised a Spanish [expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magellan_expedition "Magellan expedition") to the [East Indies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Indies "East Indies") that completed the first [circumnavigation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Circumnavigation "Circumnavigation") of the globe.[^89] During the 15th century, Portugal established the earliest transatlantic slave-trade circuits and, by the mid-19th century, had become the longest-active and second-largest participant in the [Atlantic slave trade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade "Atlantic slave trade").[^90]

### Iberian Union and Restoration

![Engraving of the coronation of John IV of Portugal](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Coroa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_de_D._Jo%C3%A3o_IV.png/250px-Coroa%C3%A7%C3%A3o_de_D._Jo%C3%A3o_IV.png)

The coronation of King John IV in Lisbon

The 1578 death of [King Sebastian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sebastian,_King_of_Portugal "Sebastian, King of Portugal") in [battle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Alc%C3%A1cer_Quibir "Battle of Alcácer Quibir") and the subsequent 1580 death of his great-uncle, [King Henry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry,_King_of_Portugal "Henry, King of Portugal"), left the Portuguese throne without a direct heir, precipitating a [dynastic crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_succession_crisis_of_1580 "Portuguese succession crisis of 1580").[^91] [Philip II of Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain "Philip II of Spain") claimed the throne on the grounds that his mother, [Isabella of Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isabella_of_Portugal "Isabella of Portugal"), was a Portuguese princess, and in 1580 he was crowned king of Portugal,[^91] forming a [personal union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberian_Union "Iberian Union") of the two kingdoms,[^8] depriving Portugal of an independent foreign policy and leading to its involvement in the [Eighty Years' War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eighty_Years%27_War "Eighty Years' War").[^91] The [Dutch-Portuguese War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch-Portuguese_War "Dutch-Portuguese War") accelerated the decline of Portugal's Indian Ocean trade monopoly between 1595 and 1663.[^93] [^94]

On 15 December 1640, [John, Duke of Braganza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_IV_of_Portugal "John IV of Portugal"), was proclaimed king following an uprising led by [disgruntled nobles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forty_Conspirators "Forty Conspirators") on [1 December](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Restoration_of_the_Independence_\(Portugal\)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Restoration of the Independence (Portugal) (page does not exist)"), ending the Iberian Union under the [House of Habsburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Habsburg "House of Habsburg"), and beginning the rule of the [House of Braganza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Braganza "House of Braganza").[^95] Following its defeat in the [Portuguese Restoration War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Restoration_War "Portuguese Restoration War"), Spain [recognised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Lisbon_\(1668\) "Treaty of Lisbon (1668)") [Afonso VI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afonso_VI_of_Portugal "Afonso VI of Portugal") as king of Portugal.[^96] [^97] During the reign of [John V](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_V_of_Portugal "John V of Portugal"), the large influx of Brazilian gold into the royal treasury, chiefly through the [royal fifth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_fifth "Royal fifth"), produced a [resource curse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resource_curse "Resource curse") that weakened industry and cereal production, created a [gold rush](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold_rush "Gold rush") from Portugal to Brazil, and led to prolonged economic stagnation, permanent reduction of income growth, and the interruption of the institutional and economic progress achieved in the 17th century.[^98]

After the depletion of gold revenues, Portugal was left with a weak industrial base and backward institutional and educational structures, a condition exacerbated by [Sebastião José de Carvalho e Melo, 1st Marquis of Pombal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A3o_Jos%C3%A9_de_Carvalho_e_Melo,_1st_Marquis_of_Pombal "Sebastião José de Carvalho e Melo, 1st Marquis of Pombal"), who emerged as the country's *[de facto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De_facto "De facto")* ruler in the aftermath of the [1755 earthquake and tsunami](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1755_Lisbon_earthquake "1755 Lisbon earthquake"), a [natural disaster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_megathrust_earthquakes "List of megathrust earthquakes") that shook the kingdom and recognized as one of the worst to ever occur. Pombal's failed industrialisation efforts, education policies that weakened human-capital formation, and [expulsion of the Jesuits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suppression_of_the_Society_of_Jesus "Suppression of the Society of Jesus"), reducing student numbers and contributing to Portugal's decline in literacy. Pombal's instrumentalisation of the [Inquisition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Inquisition "Portuguese Inquisition") for political purposes, helping to entrench an extractive system that outlasted his rule. The nature of the existing political institutions contributed to Portugal's sustained divergence from Western Europe despite comparable [family values](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Family_values "Family values") and high [state capacity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_capacity "State capacity"), with partial recovery beginning around the mid-20th century.[^98] [^99]

### Constitutional monarchy

![A large historical painting shows a crowded parliamentary chamber in the middle of a heated debate during the Constituent Cortes of 1820. Dozens of formally dressed men gesture, argue, and rise from their seats beneath towering crystal chandeliers and a richly decorated ceiling, while officials sit at a central desk below a grand canopy and throne.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/78/Sess%C3%A3o_das_Cortes_de_Lisboa.jpg/250px-Sess%C3%A3o_das_Cortes_de_Lisboa.jpg)

The Constituent Cortes of 1820 approved the first constitution of Portugal.

In 1807, Portugal refused [Napoleon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleon "Napoleon") 's demand to join the [Continental System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_System "Continental System") of embargo against the United Kingdom; [an invasion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invasion_of_Portugal_\(1807\) "Invasion of Portugal (1807)") led by French [General Junot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Andoche_Junot "Jean-Andoche Junot") followed, and Lisbon was captured in 1807.[^100] [^101] During the [Napoleonic invasions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_invasions_of_Portugal "French invasions of Portugal"), the [Portuguese royal family transferred](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transfer_of_the_Portuguese_court_to_Brazil "Transfer of the Portuguese court to Brazil") the court to [Rio de Janeiro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rio_de_Janeiro "Rio de Janeiro"), in Brazil, making it the capital between 1808 and 1821.[^102] British intervention in the [Peninsular War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsular_War "Peninsular War") helped support Portuguese independence, and all French troops were expelled by 1812.[^103]

In 1820, an [uprising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberal_Revolution_of_1820 "Liberal Revolution of 1820") in [Porto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porto "Porto") sought to establish a constitution for Portugal and led to the return of King [John VI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_VI_of_Portugal "John VI of Portugal") and his court to mainland Portugal in 1821.[^104] Although the [1822 Constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Constitution_of_1822 "Portuguese Constitution of 1822") was adopted, the process was marked by compromise and efforts at consensus, which allowed absolutist forces to regain strength, culminating in [Miguelist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguelist "Miguelist") counter-revolts against liberalism, prompting Prince [Miguel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguel_I_of_Portugal "Miguel I of Portugal") 's exile.[^104] [^105] After John VI's death in 1826, his eldest son, [Pedro I of Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedro_I_of_Brazil "Pedro I of Brazil"), briefly reigned as Pedro IV of Portugal, granted the [1826 Charter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_Charter_of_1826 "Constitutional Charter of 1826"), and abdicated in favour of his minor daughter, [Maria da Glória](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maria_da_Gl%C3%B3ria "Maria da Glória"), on the condition that she marry Miguel, who was appointed regent.[^106] Although Miguel's return was under the condition of swearing allegiance to the Charter, he soon dismantled liberal institutions and was proclaimed king in 1828, leading to the [Liberal Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberal_Wars "Liberal Wars"), after which Pedro forced Miguel's abdication in favour of Maria and his exile causing absolutist ideology to wane.[^106] Under the constitutional monarchy, the country faced economic crises, political instability, and several coups d'état.[^107] At the same time it expanded its colonies in Africa, but this culminated in the [1890 British Ultimatum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1890_British_Ultimatum "1890 British Ultimatum"), which thwarted Portugal's imperialist ambitions and represented a devastating blow to the monarchy.[^108]

### First Republic and Estado Novo

![Black-and-white photograph of José Relvas proclaiming the Republic together with several formally dressed men leaning over the balcony railing of Lisbon City Hall and looking down onto a square surrounded with buildings.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Proclama%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Rep%C3%BAblica_Portuguesa.jpg/250px-Proclama%C3%A7%C3%A3o_Rep%C3%BAblica_Portuguesa.jpg)

José Relvas proclaiming the republic on 5 October 1910, at Lisbon City Hall

In 1908, [King Carlos I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlos_I_of_Portugal "Carlos I of Portugal") and his son and heir, [Luís Filipe, Prince Royal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lu%C3%ADs_Filipe,_Prince_Royal_of_Portugal "Luís Filipe, Prince Royal of Portugal"), [were assassinated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lisbon_Regicide "Lisbon Regicide") by [republican](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Republican_Party "Portuguese Republican Party") sympathizers.[^109] In 1910, the monarchy was [replaced](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/5_October_1910_revolution "5 October 1910 revolution") with a republic.[^109] During [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I"), Portugal [fought for the Allies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal_during_World_War_I "Portugal during World War I").[^110] However, the war increased political instability and deepened the economic crisis creating further chaos and unrest during the [First Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Portuguese_Republic "First Portuguese Republic").[^111] [^112] These conditions led to the [overthrow of the Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/28_May_1926_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "28 May 1926 coup d'état") in 1926 and the establishment of the *[Ditadura Nacional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ditadura_Nacional "Ditadura Nacional")*, which [eventually evolved](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1933_Portuguese_constitutional_referendum "1933 Portuguese constitutional referendum") into the right-wing dictatorship of the *[Estado Novo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Estado_Novo_\(Portugal\) "Estado Novo (Portugal)")*, under [António de Oliveira Salazar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Oliveira_Salazar "António de Oliveira Salazar"), in 1933.[^113] [^114]

During the *Estado Novo*, Portugal [remained neutral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal_during_World_War_II "Portugal during World War II") in [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), after which the postwar decline of colonial legitimacy and the growth of African independence movements increasingly challenged Portuguese rule overseas.[^115] [^116] The regime introduced reforms intended to strengthen ties between the [metropole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropole "Metropole") and the [colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overseas_province "Overseas province"), yet the [empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Empire "Portuguese Empire") was no unified customs and economic area.[^117] [Indigenous peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indigenous_peoples "Indigenous peoples") already [second-class citizens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second-class_citizen "Second-class citizen"), saw limited improvements in living conditions further fuelled nationalist movements, ultimately leading to the [Portuguese Colonial War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Colonial_War "Portuguese Colonial War") from 1961 to 1974.[^118] [^119]

### Return to democracy

![Black-and-white nighttime photograph of several armoured military vehicles lined up in Rossio Square, with soldiers standing beside them near the Column of Pedro IV and bright neon signs glowing in the background.](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Ondanks_een_verbod_wilden_leden_van_de_Portugese_Mao%C3%AFstische_beweging_MRPP_op_31%2C_Bestanddeelnr_927-7289.jpg/250px-Ondanks_een_verbod_wilden_leden_van_de_Portugese_Mao%C3%AFstische_beweging_MRPP_op_31%2C_Bestanddeelnr_927-7289.jpg)

Armoured vehicles in Lisbon's Rossio Square, in 1975

On 25 April 1974, the Carnation Revolution overthrew the *Estado Novo* regime, initiating the [transition to democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_transition_to_democracy "Portuguese transition to democracy") and also the dissolution of the Portuguese empire, from the independence of the [African colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese-speaking_African_countries "Portuguese-speaking African countries") to the [Handover of Macau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Handover_of_Macau "Handover of Macau") in 1999.[^120] [^119] [^121] During the [Ongoing Revolutionary Process](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ongoing_Revolutionary_Process "Ongoing Revolutionary Process") (PREC), Portugal was governed by a succession of unstable provisional governments amid significant social and political tension, until the failed [coup of 25 November 1975](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coup_of_25_November_1975 "Coup of 25 November 1975") cleared the way for the approval of a new constitution and the holding of [new elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1976_Portuguese_legislative_election "1976 Portuguese legislative election").[^120]

After the transition to democracy, Portugal set about to remove the early aspects of [socialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialism "Socialism") from the revolution period and move towards [neoliberalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neoliberalism "Neoliberalism").[^122] In 1986, Portugal joined the [European Economic Community](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Economic_Community "European Economic Community") (EEC), which led to a considerable growth of its economy.[^123] The [2010–2014 Portuguese financial crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2010%E2%80%932014_Portuguese_financial_crisis "2010–2014 Portuguese financial crisis") resulted in an international bailout and intense [austerity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austerity "Austerity") policies, causing lasting social impacts.[^124] [^125]

## Geography

![Refer to caption](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Portugal_topographic_map-en.svg/250px-Portugal_topographic_map-en.svg.png)

A topographic map of Portugal

Portugal's territory comprises mainland Portugal and the Azores and Madeira archipelagos.[^17] The mainland, commonly referred to as Continental Portugal, is located in the southwest of the Iberian Peninsula in [Southwestern Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southwestern_Europe "Southwestern Europe"), while Madeira and the Azores lie in the [Atlantic Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean "Atlantic Ocean").[^17] [^126] The country's land area is 92,225 km <sup>2</sup> (35,608 sq mi), although [Portuguese law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_law "Portuguese law") defines the country's size as 156,597 km <sup>2</sup> (60,462 sq mi) which includes about 64,000 km <sup>2</sup> (24,711 sq mi) of ocean waters.[^19] [^17] [^9] [Portugal's exclusive economic zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone_of_Portugal "Exclusive economic zone of Portugal") extends 1,727,408 km <sup>2</sup> (666,956 mi <sup>2</sup>), making it the largest in the European Union.[^127] [^128] The country's highest point is the summit of [Mount Pico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Pico "Mount Pico"), located on [Pico Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pico_Island "Pico Island") in the Azores, which rises to an elevation of 2,351 m (7,713 ft) above sea level.[^129] [^10] Despite the country's size, Portugal exhibits a diverse array of [geomorphological](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geomorphological "Geomorphological") landscapes as a result of its long geological evolution, shaped by two [Wilson cycles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilson_Cycle "Wilson Cycle"), its position relative to [plate boundaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plate_tectonics "Plate tectonics"), and climatic variability that produced distinct [morphoclimatic zones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morphoclimatic_zones "Morphoclimatic zones").[^131]

Continental Portugal is over three-quarters forests and the remaining is agricultural. The Portuguese mainland is predominantly low-altitude, with over 70% of the territory lying below 400 m (1,300 ft) and less than 12% rising above 700 m (2,300 ft) of elevation. Its geography is structured by the [Tagus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tagus "Tagus") River, which enters from Spain and flows into the [Tagus Estuary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tagus_Estuary_Natural_Reserve "Tagus Estuary Natural Reserve"), as 95% of areas exceeding 400 m (1,300 ft) are situated to the north of the river while the regions south of the Tagus, encompassing [Alentejo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alentejo "Alentejo") and the [Algarve](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algarve "Algarve"), have 62% of the lands below 200 m (660 ft). The territory north of the Tagus is marked by mountains and plateaus incised by river valleys, whereas the south is distinguished by rolling plains.[^132] [^133]

The Madeira archipelago comprises the islands of [Madeira](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madeira_Island "Madeira Island") and [Porto Santo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porto_Santo "Porto Santo"), together with the [Desertas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desertas_Islands "Desertas Islands") and [Savage Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Savage_Islands "Savage Islands"), all of which are of volcanic origin. Approximately one third of Madeira Island lies above 1,000 metres (3,300 ft) in elevation, and its landscape is characterised by a dense network of deep valleys with slopes rising several hundred metres, as well as streams that originate in the island's centre and diverge towards the coast, sustained by abundant rainfall. The steep gradient and very high rainfall is a cause of [flash floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flash_floods "Flash floods") with high [sediment transport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sediment_transport "Sediment transport").[^134]

The Azores is an archipelago composed of nine volcanic islands which, from west to east, are [Flores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flores_Island_\(Azores\) "Flores Island (Azores)"), [Corvo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corvo_Island "Corvo Island"), [Faial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faial_Island "Faial Island"), [Pico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pico_Island "Pico Island"), [São Jorge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Jorge_Island "São Jorge Island"), [Graciosa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graciosa_Island "Graciosa Island"), [Terceira](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terceira_Island "Terceira Island"), [São Miguel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A3o_Miguel_Island "São Miguel Island"), and [Santa Maria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santa_Maria_Island "Santa Maria Island"). The islands retain much of their volcanic landforms, most visibly in volcanic cones and in the lakes that shape the scenery of the islands especially São Miguel, Flores, Terceira, Pico, and Corvo. Although rainfall is abundant, most streams in the Azores are temporary, and the predominance of agricultural land use further accentuates the torrential nature of the river regime during periods of intense precipitation.[^135] [^132]

### Climate

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_PRT_1991%E2%80%932020_%28labeled%29.svg/1280px-Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_PRT_1991%E2%80%932020_%28labeled%29.svg.png) ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_PRT_1991%E2%80%932020_%28labeled%29.svg/960px-Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_PRT_1991%E2%80%932020_%28labeled%29.svg.png) ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_PRT_1991%E2%80%932020_%28labeled%29.svg/500px-Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_PRT_1991%E2%80%932020_%28labeled%29.svg.png) ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/55/Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_PRT_1991%E2%80%932020_%28labeled%29.svg/960px-Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_PRT_1991%E2%80%932020_%28labeled%29.svg.png)

The [Köppen climate types](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification") of Portugal

The climate of Portugal is mainly [Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_climate "Mediterranean climate"). While the mainland and Madeira show a Mediterranean climate, the Azores mainly have a [temperate oceanic climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceanic_climate "Oceanic climate"). Portugal has a diverse variety of regional climates for a country of its size.[^136]

The Iberian Peninsula is located at the southern margin of the temperate zone and at the northern margin of the subtropical [high-pressure zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-pressure_area "High-pressure area"). Additionally, Portugal's climate is influenced by the seasonal latitudinal shift of the [jet stream](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jet_stream "Jet stream"), which directly impacts the trajectory of a [polar front](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polar_front "Polar front"). Typically in the winter, the jet stream moves southwards and Portugal comes under the influence of the polar front, producing colder temperatures. When the polar front moves northward, Portugal comes under the influence of the stable atmospheric conditions that bring milder weather during the summer.[^136]

The high altitude zones of the Azorean islands have a temperate maritime climate while the western Azores have a [humid subtropical climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate "Humid subtropical climate"). In parts of [Beja](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beja_District "Beja District") and on [Porto Santo Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porto_Santo_Island "Porto Santo Island") the climate is [semi-arid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-arid_climate "Semi-arid climate"). In the Savage Islands the climate is that of a [hot desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hot_desert_climate "Hot desert climate"). Portugal is one of the warmest countries in Europe: the average temperature in mainland Portugal varies from 10–12 °C (50.0–53.6 °F) in the mountainous interior north to 17–19 °C (62.6–66.2 °F) in the south and on the [Guadiana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guadiana "Guadiana") [river basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/River_basin "River basin"), with variations from the highlands to the lowlands.[^137]

[Wildfires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildfire "Wildfire") remain a serious challenge in Portugal, with nearly 30% of those recorded in 2024 having an undetermined origin and arson accounting for almost five-sixths of the total burned area.[^138] Between 2014 and 2024, Portugal recorded an annual average of 12,496 fires and 112,455 ha (434 sq mi) burned, making it the European Union country with both the highest average number of wildfires and the greatest average burned area over that period.[^139] [Climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_in_Portugal "Climate change in Portugal") is projected to raise average temperatures in Portugal by as much as 4 °C (39.2 °F) by 2100 relative to the 1970–2010 average, with likely consequences for the country's energy supply, particularly by diminishing electricity generation capacity.[^140] [^141]

### Biodiversity

![A wide view of a forested mountain valley in the Peneda-Gerês National Park under grey clouds, with rocky slopes on both sides, a stream running through the centre, and a small stone bridge partly hidden among the trees](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Miradouro_da_Cascata_do_Arado.jpg/250px-Miradouro_da_Cascata_do_Arado.jpg)

The Peneda-Gerês National Park in Northern Portugal is the only national park in Portugal. 142

Portugal is located on the [Mediterranean basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_basin "Mediterranean basin"), the 3rd most diverse hotspot of flora in the world.[^143] It is home to 6 terrestrial ecoregions - [Azores temperate mixed forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azores_temperate_mixed_forests "Azores temperate mixed forests"), [Cantabrian mixed forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantabrian_mixed_forests "Cantabrian mixed forests"), [Madeira evergreen forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madeira_evergreen_forests "Madeira evergreen forests"), [Iberian sclerophyllous and semi-deciduous forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberian_sclerophyllous_and_semi-deciduous_forests "Iberian sclerophyllous and semi-deciduous forests"), [Northwest Iberian montane forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northwest_Iberian_montane_forests "Northwest Iberian montane forests"), and [Southwest Iberian Mediterranean sclerophyllous and mixed forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southwest_Iberian_Mediterranean_sclerophyllous_and_mixed_forests "Southwest Iberian Mediterranean sclerophyllous and mixed forests").[^144] Almost a quarter of its land area is included in the [Natura 2000](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natura_2000 "Natura 2000") network.[^145] [Eucalyptus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eucalyptus "Eucalyptus"), [cork oak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cork_oak "Cork oak"), and [maritime pine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_pine "Maritime pine") together make up 71% of the total forested area of continental Portugal.[^146] The Portuguese west coast is part of the four major Eastern Boundary [Upwelling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upwelling "Upwelling") Systems of the ocean.[^147] Portugal has 73% of the [freshwater fish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freshwater_fish "Freshwater fish") species endemic to the Iberian Peninsula, the highest proportion for any region in Europe.[^148] Among Portugal's [protected areas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protected_areas_of_Portugal "Protected areas of Portugal") are the [Southwest Alentejo and Vicentine Coast Natural Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southwest_Alentejo_and_Vicentine_Coast_Natural_Park "Southwest Alentejo and Vicentine Coast Natural Park"), one of Europe's last remaining stretches of wild coastline, and [Montesinho Natural Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montesinho_Natural_Park "Montesinho Natural Park"), which preserves one of the few thriving Mediterranean mountain landscapes and is an example of successful coexistence between human communities and nature.[^149] [^150]

Geographical and climatic conditions facilitate the introduction of exotic species that later turn out to be invasive and destructive to the native habitats. Around 20% of the total number of extant species in continental Portugal are [exotic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Introduced_species "Introduced species").[^151] Portugal is the 2nd country in Europe with the highest number of threatened animal and plant species.[^152] The country as a whole is an important stopover for migratory bird species.[^153] [^154] [^155] The mammalian species of Portugal such as deer, [Iberian ibex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberian_ibex "Iberian ibex"), [wild boar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wild_boar "Wild boar"), [red fox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_fox "Red fox"), [Iberian wolf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberian_wolf "Iberian wolf"), and [Iberian lynx](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberian_lynx "Iberian lynx") were once widespread throughout the country, but intense hunting, habitat degradation, and growing pressure from agriculture and livestock largely reduced their populations in the 19th and 20th centuries.[^156] Others species, such as the [Portuguese ibex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_ibex "Portuguese ibex"), became extinct, but some mammalian species have been re-expanding their native range.[^157]
