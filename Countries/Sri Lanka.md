**Sri Lanka**,[^1] officially the **Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka**, formerly known as **Ceylon**,[^2] is an [island country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Island_country "Island country") in [South Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia "South Asia"). It is located in the [Indian Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Ocean "Indian Ocean"), southwest of the [Bay of Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal "Bay of Bengal"), and is separated from [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India") by the [Gulf of Mannar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Mannar "Gulf of Mannar") and the [Palk Strait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palk_Strait "Palk Strait"). Sri Lanka shares a [maritime border](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_border "Maritime border") with the [Maldives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maldives "Maldives") to the southwest and India to the northwest, and lies across the Bay of Bengal from [Bangladesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh "Bangladesh") and [Myanmar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar "Myanmar") to the northeast and the [Andaman and Nicobar Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andaman_and_Nicobar_Islands "Andaman and Nicobar Islands") of India to the east. Its capital is [Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Jayawardenepura_Kotte "Sri Jayawardenepura Kotte"), while [Colombo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colombo "Colombo") is its [largest city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Sri_Lanka "List of cities in Sri Lanka") and the political, financial and cultural centre. Sri Lanka's [population is 22 million](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographics_of_Sri_Lanka "Demographics of Sri Lanka"); with the [Sinhalese people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhalese_people "Sinhalese people"), who speak the [Sinhala language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhala_language "Sinhala language"), forming the vast majority—while [Tamil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamil_language "Tamil language") is spoken by the [large Tamil minority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Tamils "Sri Lankan Tamils"). Other long-established ethnic groups include the [Moors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Moors "Sri Lankan Moors"), [Indian Tamils](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Tamils_of_Sri_Lanka "Indian Tamils of Sri Lanka"), [Burghers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burgher_people "Burgher people"), [Malays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Malays "Sri Lankan Malays"), [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_people_in_Sri_Lanka "Chinese people in Sri Lanka"), and [Vedda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vedda "Vedda").[^18]

The island has a documented history of over 3,000 years, with evidence of [prehistoric human settlement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_human_migrations "Early human migrations") dating back 125,000 years.[^19] Sri Lanka has been given [various names](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Names_of_Sri_Lanka "Names of Sri Lanka") throughout its long history, with Ceylon most notably being used prior to its independence in 1948.[^20] The earliest known [Buddhist writings in the island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism_in_Sri_Lanka "Buddhism in Sri Lanka"), known collectively as the [Pali Canon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pali_Canon "Pali Canon"), date back to [29 BCE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fourth_Therav%C4%81da_Council "Fourth Theravāda Council").[^21] [^22] Sri Lanka, owing to its strategic geographical location, played a role as a major trading hub, and was well known to explorers across the world as early as the [Anuradhapura period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anuradhapura_period "Anuradhapura period"). The [Portuguese Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Empire "Portuguese Empire") established [a colony](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Ceylon "Portuguese Ceylon") in the sixteenth century, during a period of [political upheaval](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crisis_of_the_Sixteenth_Century "Crisis of the Sixteenth Century") in the [Kingdom of Kotte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kotte "Kingdom of Kotte") where it also faced attacks from neighbouring [kingdoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhala_kingdom "Sinhala kingdom") of [Kandy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kandy "Kingdom of Kandy") and [Sitawaka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sitawaka "Kingdom of Sitawaka"). Following the [Sinhalese–Portuguese War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhalese%E2%80%93Portuguese_War "Sinhalese–Portuguese War"), the [Dutch colonial empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_colonial_empire "Dutch colonial empire") controlled the [coastal areas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_Ceylon "Dutch Ceylon"). By the early 19th century, the [British Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire "British Empire") established a [colony on the island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Ceylon "British Ceylon"), which lasted until 1948. The early 20th century saw the rise of nationalist movements and [increasing calls for independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_independence_movement "Sri Lankan independence movement"). In 1948, Sri Lanka gained independence as the [Dominion of Ceylon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion_of_Ceylon "Dominion of Ceylon"), eventually becoming a [republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic "Republic") in 1972. Sri Lanka's recent history has been marred by a [prolonged civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_civil_war "Sri Lankan civil war") between the Tamil separatist militant organisation of [Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberation_Tigers_of_Tamil_Eelam "Liberation Tigers of Tamil Eelam") and the [Sri Lankan Armed Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Armed_Forces "Sri Lankan Armed Forces"), which lasted from 1983 to 2009, and ended with the [defeat of the separatists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eelam_War_IV "Eelam War IV").[^23]

In the 21st century, Sri Lanka has emerged as a [developing country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country "Developing country"), with a strategically important geographical location in the Indian Ocean, with its deep harbours giving it significant geopolitical leverage as a major trading post from the ancient [Maritime Silk Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_Silk_Road "Maritime Silk Road").[^24] [^25] [^26] It has the [highest level of human development in South Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_Human_Development_Index "List of countries by Human Development Index") and the [second-highest GDP per capita](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\)_per_capita "List of countries by GDP (nominal) per capita") in the region. Sri Lanka has a long history of international engagement and cooperation, and is a member of several international organisations—including [SAARC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asian_Association_for_Regional_Cooperation "South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation"), the [G77](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_of_77 "Group of 77"), the [Non-Aligned Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement "Non-Aligned Movement") and the [Commonwealth of Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations "Commonwealth of Nations").

## Toponymy

In antiquity, Sri Lanka was known to travellers by a variety of names. According to the *[Mahāvaṃsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mah%C4%81va%E1%B9%83sa "Mahāvaṃsa")*, the legendary [Prince Vijaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_Vijaya "Prince Vijaya") named the island [Tambapaṇṇĩ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Tambapanni "Kingdom of Tambapanni") (" [copper-red](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copper_\(color\) "Copper (color)") hands" or "copper-red earth"), because his followers' hands were reddened by the [red soil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_soil "Red soil") of the area where he landed.[^27] [^28] In [Hindu mythology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindu_mythology "Hindu mythology"), the term [*Lankā*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanka "Lanka") ("Island") appears but it is unknown whether it refers to the island. The Tamil term [Eelam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eelam "Eelam") ([Tamil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamil_language "Tamil language"): ஈழம், romanised: *īḻam*) was used to designate the whole island in [Sangam literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sangam_literature "Sangam literature").[^29] [^30] The island was known [under Chola rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chola_conquest_of_Anuradhapura "Chola conquest of Anuradhapura") as *Mummudi Cholamandalam* ("realm of the [three crowned](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Crowned_Kings "Three Crowned Kings") Cholas").[^31]

[Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "Ancient Greek") geographers called it *[Taprobanā](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taprobana "Taprobana")* ([Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language "Ancient Greek language"): Ταπροβανᾶ) or *Taprobanē* (Ταπροβανῆ) [^32] from the word *Tambapanni*. The Arabs and Persians referred to it as *Sarandīb* (the origin of the word " [serendipity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serendipity "Serendipity") ") from [Sanskrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanskrit "Sanskrit") *Siṃhaladvīpaḥ*.[^33] [^34] *Ceilão*, the name given to Sri Lanka by the Portuguese when they arrived in 1505,[^35] was transliterated into English as *Ceylon*.[^36] As a British [crown colony](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_colony "Crown colony"), the island was known as Ceylon; it achieved independence as the [Dominion of Ceylon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion_of_Ceylon "Dominion of Ceylon") in 1948.

The country is now known in Sinhala as **Śrī Laṅkā** ([Sinhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhala_language "Sinhala language"): ශ්රී ලංකා) and in Tamil as **Ilaṅkai** ([Tamil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamil_language "Tamil language"): இலங்கை, IPA: [\[iˈlaŋɡaɪ\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Tamil "Help:IPA/Tamil")). In 1972, its formal name was changed to "Free, Sovereign and Independent Republic of Sri Lanka". Later, on 7 September 1978, it was changed to the "Democratic Socialist Republic of Sri Lanka".[^37] [^38] As the name Ceylon still appears in the names of a number of organisations, the Sri Lankan government announced in 2011 a plan to rename all those over which it has authority.[^39]

## History

### Prehistory

The pre-history of Sri Lanka goes back 125,000 years and possibly even as far back as 500,000 years.[^40] The era spans the [Palaeolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palaeolithic "Palaeolithic"), [Mesolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesolithic "Mesolithic"), and early [Iron Ages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iron_Age "Iron Age"). Among the [Palaeolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palaeolithic "Palaeolithic") human settlements discovered in Sri Lanka, [Pahiyangala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fa_Hien_Cave "Fa Hien Cave") (37,000 BP), named after the [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_people "Chinese people") traveller [monk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhikkhu "Bhikkhu") [Faxian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faxian "Faxian");[^41] [Batadombalena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batadombalena "Batadombalena") (28,500 BP);[^42] and [Belilena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belilena "Belilena") (12,000 BP) are the most important. In these caves, archaeologists have found the remains of [anatomically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatomy "Anatomy") modern [humans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humans "Humans") which they have named [Balangoda Man](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balangoda_Man "Balangoda Man"), and other evidence [^43] suggesting that they may have engaged in [agriculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture "Agriculture") and kept domestic dogs for driving game.[^44]

The earliest inhabitants of Sri Lanka were likely ancestors of the [Vedda people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vedda_people "Vedda people"),[^45] an indigenous people numbering approximately 2,500 living in modern-day Sri Lanka.

During the protohistoric period (1000–500 BCE) Sri Lanka was culturally united with southern India,[^46] and shared the same megalithic burials, [pottery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_and_red_ware_culture "Black and red ware culture"), iron technology, farming techniques and [megalithic graffiti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megalithic_graffiti_symbols "Megalithic graffiti symbols").[^47] [^48] This cultural complex spread from southern India along with Dravidian clans such as the [Velir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Velir "Velir"), prior to the migration of [Prakrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prakrit "Prakrit") speakers.[^49] [^50] [^47]

One of the first written references to the island is found in the Indian [epic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epic_poetry "Epic poetry") [Ramayana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ramayana "Ramayana"), which provides details of a kingdom named *[Lanka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanka "Lanka")* that was created by the divine sculptor [Vishvakarma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vishwakarma "Vishwakarma") for [Kubera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kubera "Kubera"), the God of Wealth.[^51] It is said that Kubera was overthrown by his [rakshasa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rakshasa "Rakshasa") stepbrother, [Ravana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ravana "Ravana").[^52]

### Ancient history

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Ptolemy%27s_Taprobana.jpg/250px-Ptolemy%27s_Taprobana.jpg)

Ptolemy's world map of Ceylon, first century CE, in a 1535 publication

According to the *[Mahāvaṃsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mah%C4%81va%E1%B9%83sa "Mahāvaṃsa")*, a [Pāḷi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pali "Pali") chronicle written in the 5th century CE, the original inhabitants of Sri Lanka are said to be the [Yakshas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yaksha "Yaksha") and [Nagas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naga_people_\(Lanka\) "Naga people (Lanka)"). Sinhalese history traditionally starts in 543 BCE with the arrival of [Prince Vijaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_Vijaya "Prince Vijaya"), a semi-legendary prince who sailed with 700 followers to Sri Lanka, after being expelled from [Vanga kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanga_kingdom "Vanga kingdom") (present-day [Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal "Bengal")).[^53] He established the [Kingdom of Tambapanni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Tambapanni "Kingdom of Tambapanni"), near modern-day [Mannar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mannar,_Sri_Lanka "Mannar, Sri Lanka"). Vijaya (Singha) is the first of the approximately [189 monarchs of Sri Lanka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Sri_Lankan_monarchs "List of Sri Lankan monarchs") described in chronicles such as the *[Dīpavaṃsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%C4%ABpava%E1%B9%83sa "Dīpavaṃsa")*, *Mahāvaṃsa*, *[Cūḷavaṃsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C5%AB%E1%B8%B7ava%E1%B9%83sa "Cūḷavaṃsa")*, and *Rājāvaliya*.[^54]

Once [Prakrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prakrit "Prakrit") speakers had attained dominance on the island, the *[Mahāvaṃsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mah%C4%81va%E1%B9%83sa "Mahāvaṃsa")* further recounts the later migration of royal brides and service castes from the Tamil [Pandya kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pandya_kingdom "Pandya kingdom") to the [Anuradhapura kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anuradhapura_kingdom "Anuradhapura kingdom") in the early historic period.[^55]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/23/Buda_de_Avukana_-_01.jpg/330px-Buda_de_Avukana_-_01.jpg)

The Avukana Buddha statue, a 12-metre-tall (39 ft) standing Buddha statue from the reign of Dhatusena of Anuradhapura, 5th century

The [Anuradhapura period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anuradhapura_period "Anuradhapura period") (377 BCE – 1017 CE) began with the establishment of the [Anuradhapura kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anuradhapura_kingdom "Anuradhapura kingdom") in 380 BCE during the reign of [Pandukabhaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pandukabhaya "Pandukabhaya"). Thereafter, Anuradhapura served as the capital city of the country for nearly 1,400 years.[^56] Ancient Sri Lankans excelled at building certain types of [structures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_constructions_of_Sri_Lanka "Ancient constructions of Sri Lanka") such as [tanks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_irrigation_network "Sri Lankan irrigation network"), [dagobas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stupa "Stupa") and palaces.[^57] Society underwent a major transformation during the reign of [Devanampiya Tissa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devanampiya_Tissa "Devanampiya Tissa"), with the arrival of Buddhism from India. In 250 BCE,[^58] [Mahinda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahinda_\(Buddhist_monk\) "Mahinda (Buddhist monk)"), a [bhikkhu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhikkhu "Bhikkhu") and the son of the [Mauryan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurya_Empire "Maurya Empire") Emperor [Ashoka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashoka "Ashoka") arrived in [Mihintale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mihintale "Mihintale") carrying the message of Buddhism.[^59] His mission won over the monarch, who embraced the faith and propagated it throughout the [Sinhalese population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhalese_people "Sinhalese people").[^60]

Succeeding kingdoms of Sri Lanka would maintain many [Buddhist schools](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schools_of_Buddhism "Schools of Buddhism") and monasteries and support the propagation of Buddhism into other countries in Southeast Asia. Sri Lankan Bhikkhus studied in India's famous ancient Buddhist University of [Nalanda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nalanda "Nalanda"), which was destroyed by [Bakhtiyar Khalji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Bakhtiyar_Khalji "Muhammad Bakhtiyar Khalji"). It is probable that many of the scriptures from Nalanda are preserved in Sri Lanka's many monasteries and that the written form of the [Tripiṭaka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tripi%E1%B9%ADaka "Tripiṭaka"), including Sinhalese Buddhist literature, were part of the University of Nalanda.[^61] In 245 BCE, [bhikkhunī](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhikkhun%C4%AB "Bhikkhunī") [Sanghamitta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanghamitta "Sanghamitta") arrived with the [Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jaya_Sri_Maha_Bodhi "Jaya Sri Maha Bodhi") tree, which is considered to be a sapling from the historical [Bodhi Tree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bodhi_Tree "Bodhi Tree") under which [Gautama Buddha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gautama_Buddha "Gautama Buddha") became enlightened.[^62] It is considered the oldest human-planted tree (with a continuous historical record) in the world. ([Bodhivaṃsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bodhiva%E1%B9%83sa "Bodhivaṃsa")) [^63]

Sri Lanka experienced the first of many foreign invasions during the reign of [Suratissa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suratissa_of_Anuradhapura "Suratissa of Anuradhapura"), who was defeated by two horse traders named [Sena and Guttika](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sena_and_Guttika "Sena and Guttika") from South India.[^60] The next invasion came immediately in 205 BCE by a [Chola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chola "Chola") named [Elara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elara_\(monarch\) "Elara (monarch)"), who overthrew [Asela](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asela_of_Anuradhapura "Asela of Anuradhapura") and ruled the country for 44 years. [Dutugamunu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutugamunu "Dutugamunu"), the eldest son of the southern regional sub-king, [Kavan Tissa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kavan_Tissa,_Prince_of_Ruhuna "Kavan Tissa, Prince of Ruhuna"), defeated Elara in the [Battle of Vijithapura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Vijithapura "Battle of Vijithapura"). During its two and a half millennia of existence, the [Sinhala kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhala_kingdom "Sinhala kingdom") was invaded at least eight times by neighbouring South Indian dynasties such as the [Chola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chola_dynasty "Chola dynasty"), [Pandya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pandya_dynasty "Pandya dynasty"), and [Pallava](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pallava_dynasty "Pallava dynasty"). There also were incursions by the kingdoms of [Kalinga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalinga_\(region\) "Kalinga (region)") (modern [Odisha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odisha "Odisha")) and from the [Malay Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_Peninsula "Malay Peninsula") as well.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c7/Sigiriya.jpg/250px-Sigiriya.jpg)

The Sigiriya ("Lion Rock"), a rock fortress and city, built by King Kashyapa (477–495 CE) as a new more defensible capital. It was also used as a Buddhist monastery after the capital was moved back to Anuradhapura.

The [Fourth Theravāda Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fourth_Therav%C4%81da_Council "Fourth Theravāda Council") was held at the [Anuradhapura Maha Viharaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anuradhapura_Maha_Viharaya "Anuradhapura Maha Viharaya") in Sri Lanka under the patronage of [Valagamba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valagamba "Valagamba") in 25 BCE. The council was held in response to a year in which the harvests in Sri Lanka were particularly poor and many [Buddhist monks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhikkhu "Bhikkhu") subsequently died of starvation. Because the [Pali Canon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pali_Canon "Pali Canon") was at that time [oral literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oral_literature "Oral literature") maintained in several recensions by *dhammabhāṇaka* s ([dharma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dharma "Dharma") reciters), the surviving monks recognised the danger of not writing it down so that even if some of the monks whose duty it was to study and remember parts of the Canon for later generations died, the teachings would not be lost.[^64] After the council, [palm-leaf manuscripts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palm-leaf_manuscript "Palm-leaf manuscript") containing the completed Canon were taken to other countries such as [Burma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burma "Burma"), [Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand "Thailand"), [Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambodia "Cambodia") and [Laos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laos "Laos").

Sri Lanka was the first Asian country known to have a female ruler: [Anula of Anuradhapura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anula_of_Anuradhapura "Anula of Anuradhapura") (r. 47–42 BCE).[^65] Sri Lankan monarchs undertook some remarkable construction projects such as [Sigiriya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sigiriya "Sigiriya"), the so-called "Fortress in the Sky", built during the reign of [Kashyapa I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kashyapa_I "Kashyapa I"), who ruled between 477 and 495. The Sigiriya rock fortress is surrounded by an extensive network of ramparts and moats. Inside this protective enclosure were gardens, ponds, pavilions, palaces and other structures.[^66] [^67]

In 993 CE, the invasion of [Chola emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chola_dynasty "Chola dynasty") [Rajaraja I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajaraja_I "Rajaraja I") forced the then Sinhalese ruler [Mahinda V](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahinda_V "Mahinda V") to flee to the southern part of Sri Lanka. Taking advantage of this situation, [Rajendra I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajendra_I "Rajendra I"), son of Rajaraja I, launched a large invasion in 1017. Mahinda V was captured and taken to India, and the Cholas [sacked the city of Anuradhapura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chola_conquest_of_Anuradhapura "Chola conquest of Anuradhapura") causing the fall of [Anuradhapura kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anuradhapura_kingdom "Anuradhapura kingdom"). Subsequently, they moved the capital to [Polonnaruwa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polonnaruwa "Polonnaruwa").[^68]

### Post-classical period

Following a 17-year-long campaign, [Vijayabahu I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vijayabahu_I_of_Polonnaruwa "Vijayabahu I of Polonnaruwa") successfully drove the Chola out of Sri Lanka in 1070, reuniting the country for the first time in over a century.[^69] [^70] Upon his request, ordained monks were sent from [Burma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burma "Burma") to Sri Lanka to re-establish Buddhism, which had almost disappeared from the country during the Chola reign.[^71] During the mediaeval period, Sri Lanka was divided into three sub-territories, namely, [Ruhunu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Principality_of_Ruhuna "Principality of Ruhuna"), Pihiti and [Maya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_Rata "Maya Rata").[^72]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c6/Gal_Viharaya_02.jpg/250px-Gal_Viharaya_02.jpg)

The seated image of Gal Vihara in Polonnaruwa, 12th century, which depicts the dhyana mudra, shows signs of Mahayana influence.

Sri Lanka's [irrigation system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_irrigation_network "Sri Lankan irrigation network") was extensively expanded during the reign of [Parākramabāhu the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parakramabahu_I_of_Polonnaruwa "Parakramabahu I of Polonnaruwa") (1153–1186).[^73] This period is considered as a time when Sri Lanka was at the height of its power.[^74] [^75] He built 1,470 reservoirs – the highest number by any ruler in Sri Lanka's history – repaired 165 dams, 3,910 canals, 163 major reservoirs, and 2,376 mini-reservoirs.[^76] His most famous construction is the [Parakrama Samudra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parakrama_Samudra "Parakrama Samudra"),[^77] the largest irrigation project of mediaeval Sri Lanka. Parākramabāhu's reign is memorable for two major campaigns – in the south of India as part of a Pandyan war of succession, and a punitive strike against the kings of Ramanna ([Burma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar "Myanmar")) for various perceived insults to Sri Lanka.[^78]

After his demise, Sri Lanka gradually decayed in power. In 1215, [Kalinga Magha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalinga_Magha "Kalinga Magha"), an invader with uncertain origins, identified as the founder of the Jaffna kingdom, invaded and captured the [Kingdom of Polonnaruwa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Polonnaruwa "Kingdom of Polonnaruwa"). He sailed from [Kalinga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalinga_\(historical_region\) "Kalinga (historical region)") [^76] 690 nautical miles on 100 large ships with a 24,000 strong army. Unlike previous invaders, he [looted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Looting "Looting"), ransacked and destroyed everything in the ancient [Anuradhapura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anuradhapura_kingdom "Anuradhapura kingdom") and [Polonnaruwa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Polonnaruwa "Kingdom of Polonnaruwa") Kingdoms beyond recovery.[^79] His priorities in ruling were to extract as much as possible from the land and overturn as many of the traditions of [Rajarata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajarata "Rajarata") as possible. His reign saw the massive migration of native [Sinhalese people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhalese_people "Sinhalese people") to the south and west of Sri Lanka, and into the mountainous interior, in a bid to escape his power.[^80] [^81]

Sri Lanka never really recovered from the effects of Kalinga Magha's invasion. King Vijayabâhu III, who led the resistance, brought the kingdom to [Dambadeniya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Dambadeniya "Kingdom of Dambadeniya"). The north, in the meanwhile, eventually evolved into the [Jaffna kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jaffna_kingdom "Jaffna kingdom").[^80] [^81] The Jaffna kingdom never came under the rule of any kingdom of the south except on one occasion; in 1450, following the conquest led by king [Parâkramabâhu VI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parakramabahu_VI_of_Kotte "Parakramabahu VI of Kotte") 's adopted son, [Prince Sapumal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhuvanaikabahu_VI_of_Kotte "Bhuvanaikabahu VI of Kotte").[^82] He ruled the North from 1450 to 1467 CE.[^83]

The next three centuries starting from 1215 were marked by kaleidoscopically shifting collections of capitals in south and central Sri Lanka, including Dambadeniya, [Yapahuwa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yapahuwa "Yapahuwa"), [Gampola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gampola "Gampola"), [Raigama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raigama "Raigama"), [Kotte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kotte "Kingdom of Kotte"),[^84] [Sitawaka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Sitawaka "Kingdom of Sitawaka"), and finally, [Kandy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kandy "Kingdom of Kandy"). In 1247, the Malay kingdom of [Tambralinga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tambralinga "Tambralinga") which was a vassal of [Sri Vijaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Vijaya "Sri Vijaya") led by their king [Chandrabhanu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chandrabhanu "Chandrabhanu") [^85] briefly invaded Sri Lanka from [Insular Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insular_Southeast_Asia "Insular Southeast Asia"). They were then expelled by the South Indian Pandyan dynasty.[^86] However, this temporary invasion reinforced the steady flow of the presence of various [Austronesian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_people "Austronesian people") merchant ethnic groups, from [Sumatrans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_Malays "Sri Lankan Malays") (Indonesia) to [Lucoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines%E2%80%93Sri_Lanka_relations "Philippines–Sri Lanka relations") (Philippines) into Sri Lanka which occurred since 200 BCE.[^87] Chinese admiral [Zheng He](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zheng_He "Zheng He") and his naval expeditionary force landed at Galle, Sri Lanka in 1409 and got into [battle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming-Kotte_War "Ming-Kotte War") with the local king [Vira Alakesvara of Gampola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vira_Alakesvara_of_Gampola "Vira Alakesvara of Gampola"). Zheng He captured King Vira Alakesvara and later released him.[^88] [^89] [^90] [^91] Zheng He erected the [Galle Trilingual Inscription](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galle_Trilingual_Inscription "Galle Trilingual Inscription"), a stone tablet at [Galle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galle "Galle") written in three [languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Languages "Languages") ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"), [Tamil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamil_language "Tamil language"), and [Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_language "Persian language")), to commemorate his visit.[^92] [^93] The [stele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stele "Stele") was discovered by S. H. Thomlin at Galle in 1911 and is now preserved in the [Colombo National Museum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Museum_of_Colombo "National Museum of Colombo").

### Early modern period

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/16/SpilbergenVimala.jpg/250px-SpilbergenVimala.jpg)

A 17th-century engraving of Dutch explorer Joris van Spilbergen meeting with King Vimaladharmasuriya in 1602

![A 1595 map of Sri Lanka created by Dutch cartographer Petrus Plancius](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Laor_Sri_Lanka_Map_%28Cropped%29.jpg/250px-Laor_Sri_Lanka_Map_%28Cropped%29.jpg)

A 1595 map of Sri Lanka created by Dutch cartographer Petrus Plancius

The early modern period of Sri Lanka begins with the arrival of Portuguese soldier and explorer [Lourenço de Almeida](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louren%C3%A7o_de_Almeida "Lourenço de Almeida"), the son of [Francisco de Almeida](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francisco_de_Almeida "Francisco de Almeida"), in 1505.[^94] In 1517, the Portuguese built a fort at the port city of [Colombo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colombo "Colombo") and gradually extended their control over the coastal areas. In 1592, after decades of intermittent warfare with the Portuguese, [Vimaladharmasuriya I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vimaladharmasuriya_I "Vimaladharmasuriya I") moved his kingdom to the inland city of [Kandy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kandy "Kingdom of Kandy"), a location he thought more secure from attack.[^95] In 1619, succumbing to attacks by the [Portuguese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Empire "Portuguese Empire"), the independent existence of the [Jaffna kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jaffna_kingdom "Jaffna kingdom") came to an end.[^96]

During the reign of the [Rajasinha II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajasinha_II "Rajasinha II"), Dutch explorers arrived on the island. In 1638, the king signed a [treaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kandyan_Treaty_of_1638 "Kandyan Treaty of 1638") with the [Dutch East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company "Dutch East India Company") to get rid of the Portuguese who ruled most of the coastal areas.[^97] The following [Dutch–Portuguese War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch%E2%80%93Portuguese_War "Dutch–Portuguese War") resulted in a Dutch victory, with Colombo falling into Dutch hands by 1656. The Dutch remained in the areas they had captured, thereby violating the treaty they had signed in 1638. The [Burgher people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burgher_people "Burgher people"), a distinct ethnic group, emerged as a result of intermingling between the Dutch and native Sri Lankans in this period.[^98]

The Kingdom of Kandy was the last independent monarchy of Sri Lanka.[^99] In 1595, Vimaladharmasurya brought the sacred [Tooth Relic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Relic_of_the_tooth_of_the_Buddha "Relic of the tooth of the Buddha") —the traditional symbol of royal and religious authority amongst the [Sinhalese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhalese_people "Sinhalese people") —to Kandy and built the [Temple of the Tooth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temple_of_the_Tooth "Temple of the Tooth").[^99] In spite of on-going intermittent warfare with Europeans, the kingdom survived. Later, a crisis of succession emerged in Kandy upon king [Vira Narendra Sinha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vira_Narendra_Sinha "Vira Narendra Sinha") 's death in 1739. He was married to a [Telugu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telugu_language "Telugu language") -speaking [Nayakkar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nayaks_of_Madurai "Nayaks of Madurai") princess from South India ([Madurai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madurai "Madurai")) and was childless by her.[^99]

Eventually, with the support of [bhikkhu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhikkhu "Bhikkhu") Weliwita Sarankara and ignoring the right of *"Unambuwe Bandara"*, the crown passed to the brother of one of Narendrasinha's princesses, overlooking Narendrasinha's own son by a Sinhalese [concubine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concubinage "Concubinage").[^100] The new king was crowned [Sri Vijaya Rajasinha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Vijaya_Rajasinha "Sri Vijaya Rajasinha") later that year. Kings of the Nayakkar dynasty launched several attacks on Dutch controlled areas, which proved to be unsuccessful.[^101]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Sri_Vikrama_Rajasinha.jpg/250px-Sri_Vikrama_Rajasinha.jpg)

Sri Vikrama Rajasinha of Kandy, the last ruling native Sri Lankan monarch

During the [French Revolutionary Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars "French Revolutionary Wars"), fearing that French control of the Netherlands might deliver Sri Lanka to the French, the [British Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire "British Empire") occupied the coastal areas of the island (which they called the colony of [British Ceylon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Ceylon "British Ceylon")) with little difficulty in 1796.[^102] Two years later, in 1798, [Sri Rajadhi Rajasinha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Rajadhi_Rajasinha "Sri Rajadhi Rajasinha"), third of the four Nayakkar kings of Sri Lanka, died of a fever. Following his death, a nephew of Rajadhi Rajasinha, eighteen-year-old Kannasamy, was crowned.[^103] The young king, now named [Sri Vikrama Rajasinha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Vikrama_Rajasinha "Sri Vikrama Rajasinha"), faced a [British](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire "British Empire") invasion in 1803 but successfully retaliated. The First Kandyan War ended in a stalemate.[^103]

By then the entire coastal area was under the [British East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_East_India_Company "British East India Company") as a result of the [Treaty of Amiens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Amiens "Treaty of Amiens"). On 14 February 1815, [Kandy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kandy "Kingdom of Kandy") was occupied by the British in the [Second Kandyan War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Kandyan_War "Second Kandyan War"), ending Sri Lanka's independence.[^103] Sri Vikrama Rajasinha, the last native monarch of Sri Lanka, was exiled to India.[^104] The [Kandyan Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kandyan_Convention "Kandyan Convention") formally ceded the entire country to the British Empire. Attempts by Sri Lankan noblemen to undermine British power in 1818 during the [Uva Rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Rebellion_of_1817%E2%80%931818 "Great Rebellion of 1817–1818") were thwarted by [Governor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governors_of_British_Ceylon "Governors of British Ceylon") [Robert Brownrigg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Brownrigg "Robert Brownrigg").[^105]

The beginning of the modern period of Sri Lanka is marked by the [Colebrooke–Cameron reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colebrooke%E2%80%93Cameron_Commission "Colebrooke–Cameron Commission") of 1833.[^106] They introduced a [utilitarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utilitarianism "Utilitarianism") and [liberal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberalism "Liberalism") political culture to the country based on the [rule of law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rule_of_law "Rule of law") and amalgamated the Kandyan and maritime provinces as a single unit of government.[^106] An [executive council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_Council_of_Ceylon "Executive Council of Ceylon") and a [legislative council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legislative_Council_of_Ceylon "Legislative Council of Ceylon") were established, later becoming the foundation of a representative legislature. By this time, experiments with [coffee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coffee "Coffee") plantations were largely successful.[^107]

Soon, coffee became the primary commodity export of Sri Lanka. Falling coffee prices as a result of the [depression of 1847](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panic_of_1847 "Panic of 1847") stalled economic development and prompted the governor to introduce a series of taxes on firearms, dogs, shops, boats, etc., and to reintroduce a form of *rajakariya*, requiring six days free labour on roads or payment of a cash equivalent.[^107] These harsh measures antagonised the locals, and [another rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Matale_rebellion "Matale rebellion") broke out in 1848.[^108] A devastating leaf disease, *[Hemileia vastatrix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hemileia_vastatrix "Hemileia vastatrix")*, struck the coffee plantations in 1869, destroying the entire industry within fifteen years.[^109] The British quickly found a replacement: abandoning coffee, they began cultivating tea instead. [Tea production in Sri Lanka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tea_production_in_Sri_Lanka "Tea production in Sri Lanka") thrived in the following decades. Large-scale rubber plantations began in the early 20th century.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/e/e0/Kandyan_Chiefs_in_1905.JPG/500px-Kandyan_Chiefs_in_1905.JPG)

British appointed Kandyan chief headmen in 1905.

By the end of the 19th century, a new educated [social class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_class "Social class") transcending race and [caste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caste "Caste") arose through British attempts to staff the [Ceylon Civil Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceylon_Civil_Service "Ceylon Civil Service") and the legal, educational, engineering, and medical professions with natives.[^110] New leaders represented the various ethnic groups of the population in the [Ceylon Legislative Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legislative_Council_of_Ceylon "Legislative Council of Ceylon") on a communal basis. Buddhist and Hindu revivalists reacted against [Christian missionary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_missionary "Christian missionary") activities.[^111] [^112] The first two decades in the 20th century are noted by the unique harmony among Sinhalese and [Tamil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamils "Tamils") political leadership, which has since been lost.[^113]

The [1906 malaria outbreak in Ceylon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1906_malaria_outbreak_in_Ceylon "1906 malaria outbreak in Ceylon") actually started in the early 1900s, but the first case was documented in 1906.

In 1919, major Sinhalese and Tamil political organisations united to form the Ceylon National Congress, under the leadership of [Ponnambalam Arunachalam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ponnambalam_Arunachalam "Ponnambalam Arunachalam"),[^114] pressing colonial masters for more constitutional reforms. But without massive popular support, and with the governor's encouragement for "communal representation" by creating a "Colombo seat" that dangled between Sinhalese and Tamils, the Congress lost momentum towards the mid-1920s.[^115]

The [Donoughmore reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donoughmore_Constitution "Donoughmore Constitution") of 1931 repudiated the communal representation and introduced [universal adult franchise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Universal_suffrage "Universal suffrage") (the franchise stood at 4% before the reforms). This step was strongly criticised by the Tamil political leadership, who realised that they would be reduced to a minority in the newly created [State Council of Ceylon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Council_of_Ceylon "State Council of Ceylon"), which succeeded the legislative council.[^116] [^117] In 1937, Tamil leader [G. G. Ponnambalam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G._G._Ponnambalam "G. G. Ponnambalam") demanded a 50–50 representation (50% for the Sinhalese and 50% for other ethnic groups) in the State Council. However, this demand was not met by the [Soulbury reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soulbury_Commission "Soulbury Commission") of 1944–45.

### Contemporary history

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/SL_Independence.jpg/250px-SL_Independence.jpg)

The formal ceremony marking the start of self-rule, with the opening of the first parliament at Independence Square

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/J._R._Jayawardene%2C_President_of_the_Democratic_Socialist_Republic_of_Sri_Lanka_on_June_20%2C_1984.jpg/250px-J._R._Jayawardene%2C_President_of_the_Democratic_Socialist_Republic_of_Sri_Lanka_on_June_20%2C_1984.jpg)

J. R. Jayewardene was the most influential political figure of Sri Lanka in the 1970s and 1980s.

The Soulbury constitution ushered in [dominion status](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion_of_Ceylon "Dominion of Ceylon"), with independence proclaimed on 4 February 1948.[^118] [D. S. Senanayake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Don_Stephen_Senanayake "Don Stephen Senanayake") became the first [Prime Minister of Ceylon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Sri_Lanka "Prime Minister of Sri Lanka").[^119] Prominent [Tamil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tamils "Tamils") leaders including Ponnambalam and [Arunachalam Mahadeva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arunachalam_Mahadeva "Arunachalam Mahadeva") joined his cabinet.[^116] [^120] The [British Royal Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Navy "Royal Navy") remained stationed at [Trincomalee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trincomalee "Trincomalee") until 1956. A countrywide [popular demonstration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1953_Ceylonese_Hartal "1953 Ceylonese Hartal") against withdrawal of the rice rations resulted in the resignation of prime minister [Dudley Senanayake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dudley_Senanayake "Dudley Senanayake").[^121]

[S. W. R. D. Bandaranaike](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S._W._R._D._Bandaranaike "S. W. R. D. Bandaranaike") was elected prime minister in 1956. His three-year rule had a profound influence through his self-proclaimed role of "defender of the besieged Sinhalese culture".[^122] He introduced the controversial [Sinhala Only Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhala_Only_Act "Sinhala Only Act"), recognising [Sinhala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinhala_language "Sinhala language") as the only official language of the government. Although partially reversed in 1958, the bill posed a grave concern for the Tamil community, which perceived in it a threat to their language and culture.[^123] [^124] [^125]

The [Federal Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illankai_Tamil_Arasu_Kachchi "Illankai Tamil Arasu Kachchi") (FP) launched a movement of non-violent resistance ([satyagraha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satyagraha "Satyagraha")) against the bill, which prompted Bandaranaike to reach an agreement ([Bandaranaike–Chelvanayakam Pact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bandaranaike%E2%80%93Chelvanayakam_Pact "Bandaranaike–Chelvanayakam Pact")) with [S. J. V. Chelvanayakam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S._J._V._Chelvanayakam "S. J. V. Chelvanayakam"), leader of the FP, to resolve the looming ethnic conflict.[^126] The pact proved ineffective in the face of ongoing protests by opposition and the Buddhist clergy. The bill, together with various government [colonisation schemes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lankan_state-sponsored_colonisation_schemes "Sri Lankan state-sponsored colonisation schemes"), contributed much towards the political rancour between Sinhalese and Tamil political leaders.[^127] Bandaranaike was [assassinated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S._W._R._D._Bandaranaike#Assassination "S. W. R. D. Bandaranaike") by an extremist Buddhist monk in 1959.[^128]
