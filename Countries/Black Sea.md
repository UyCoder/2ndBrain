The **Black Sea** is a [marginal sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marginal_sea "Marginal sea") located between [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "Europe") and [Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "Asia"), east of the [Balkans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkans "Balkans"), south of the [East European Plain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_European_Plain "East European Plain"), west of the [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus "Caucasus"), and north of [Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolia "Anatolia"). It is bordered by [Bulgaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgaria "Bulgaria"), [Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)"), [Romania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romania "Romania"), [Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia "Russia"), [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey"), and [Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ukraine "Ukraine"). The Black Sea is [supplied by](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inflow_\(hydrology\) "Inflow (hydrology)") major rivers, principally the [Danube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danube "Danube"), [Dnieper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dnieper "Dnieper") and [Dniester](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dniester "Dniester"). Consequently, while six countries have a coastline on the sea, its [drainage basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drainage_basin "Drainage basin") includes parts of 24 countries in Europe.[^2]

The Black Sea, not including the [Sea of Azov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Azov "Sea of Azov"), covers 436,400 km <sup>2</sup> (168,500 sq mi),[^3] has a maximum depth of 2,212 m (7,257 ft),[^4] and a volume of 547,000 km <sup>3</sup> (131,000 cu mi).[^5] Most of its coasts ascend rapidly. These rises are the [Pontic Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontic_Mountains "Pontic Mountains") to the south, the southwest-facing peninsulas, the [Caucasus Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus_Mountains "Caucasus Mountains") to the east, and the [Crimean Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_Mountains "Crimean Mountains") to the mid-north. In the west, the coast is generally small [floodplains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Floodplain "Floodplain") below foothills such as the [Strandzha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strandzha "Strandzha"); [Cape Emine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Emine "Cape Emine"), a dwindling of the east end of the [Balkan Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkan_Mountains "Balkan Mountains"); and the [Dobruja Plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dobruja_Plateau "Dobruja Plateau") considerably farther north. The longest east–west extent is about 1,175 km (730 mi).[^6] Important cities along the coast include (clockwise from the [Bosporus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosporus "Bosporus")) the northern suburbs of [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul"), [Burgas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burgas "Burgas"), [Varna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varna,_Bulgaria "Varna, Bulgaria"), [Constanța](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constan%C8%9Ba "Constanța"), [Odesa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Odesa "Odesa"), [Yevpatoria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yevpatoria "Yevpatoria"), [Sevastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol "Sevastopol"), [Yalta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yalta "Yalta"), [Kerch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerch "Kerch"), [Novorossiysk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Novorossiysk "Novorossiysk"), [Sochi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sochi "Sochi"), [Poti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poti "Poti"), [Batumi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batumi "Batumi"), [Rize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rize "Rize"), [Trabzon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trabzon "Trabzon"), [Ordu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordu "Ordu"), [Samsun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsun "Samsun") and [Zonguldak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zonguldak "Zonguldak").[^7]

The Black Sea has a positive [water balance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_balance "Water balance"), with an annual net outflow of 300 km <sup>3</sup> (72 cu mi) per year through the [Bosporus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosporus "Bosporus") and the [Dardanelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dardanelles "Dardanelles") into the [Aegean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_Sea "Aegean Sea").[^8] While the net flow of water through the Bosporus and Dardanelles (known collectively as the [Turkish Straits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_Straits "Turkish Straits")) is out of the Black Sea, water generally flows in both directions simultaneously: Denser, more [saline water](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saline_water "Saline water") from the Aegean flows into the Black Sea underneath the less dense, fresher water that flows out of the Black Sea. This creates a significant and permanent layer of deep water that does not drain or mix and is therefore [anoxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anoxic_waters "Anoxic waters"). This anoxic layer is responsible for the preservation of [ancient shipwrecks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Black_Sea_shipwrecks "Ancient Black Sea shipwrecks") which have been found in the Black Sea, which ultimately drains into the [Atlantic Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean "Atlantic Ocean"), via the Turkish Straits and the Aegean Sea into the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea"), and from it to the Atlantic proper through the [Strait of Gibraltar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Gibraltar "Strait of Gibraltar"). The Bosporus strait connects it to the small [Sea of Marmara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara "Sea of Marmara") which in turn is connected to the Aegean Sea via the strait of the Dardanelles. To the north, the Black Sea is connected to the Sea of Azov by the [Kerch Strait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerch_Strait "Kerch Strait").

The water level has varied significantly over geological time. Due to these variations in the water level in the basin, the surrounding [shelf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_shelf "Continental shelf") and associated aprons have sometimes been dry land. At certain critical water levels, connections with surrounding water bodies can become established. It is through the most active of these connective routes, the Turkish Straits, that the Black Sea joins the [World Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_ocean "World ocean"). During geological periods when this hydrological link was not present, the Black Sea was an [endorheic basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endorheic_basin "Endorheic basin"), operating independently of the global ocean system (similar to the [Caspian Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caspian_Sea "Caspian Sea") today). Currently, the Black Sea water level is relatively high; thus, water is being exchanged with the Mediterranean. The [Black Sea undersea river](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea_undersea_river "Black Sea undersea river") is a current of particularly saline water flowing through the [Bosporus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosporus "Bosporus") Strait and along the [seabed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seabed "Seabed") of the Black Sea, the first of its kind discovered.[^7]

### Historical names and etymology

The earliest known name of the Black Sea is the Sea of Zalpa, so called by both the [Hattians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hattians "Hattians") [^11] and their conquerors, the [Hittites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hittites "Hittites"). The Hattic city of [Zalpa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zalpuwa "Zalpuwa") was "situated probably at or near the estuary of the Marrassantiya River, the modern [Kızıl Irmak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C4%B1z%C4%B1l%C4%B1rmak_River "Kızılırmak River"), on the Black Sea coast." [^12]

The principal [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language "Greek language") name *Póntos Áxeinos* is generally accepted to be a rendering of the [Iranian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_languages "Iranian languages") word \* *axšaina-* ("dark colored").[^10] Ancient Greek voyagers adopted the name as *Á-xe(i)nos*, identified with the Greek word *áxeinos* (inhospitable).[^10] The name Πόντος Ἄξεινος **Póntos Áxeinos** (Inhospitable Sea), first attested in [Pindar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pindar "Pindar") (c. 475 BC), was considered an ill omen and was euphemized to its opposite, Εὔξεινος Πόντος **Eúxeinos Póntos** (Hospitable Sea), also first attested in Pindar. This became the commonly used designation in Greek, although in mythological contexts the "true" name *Póntos Áxeinos* remained favored.[^10]

[Strabo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strabo "Strabo") 's *[Geographica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographica "Geographica")* (1.2.10) reports that in antiquity, the Black Sea was often simply called "the Sea" (ὁ πόντος **ho Pontos**).[^13] He thought that the sea was called the "Inhospitable Sea" Πόντος Ἄξεινος **Póntos Áxeinos** by the inhabitants of the [Pontus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontus_\(region\) "Pontus (region)") region of the southern shoreline before Greek colonization due to its difficult navigation and hostile barbarian natives (7.3.6), and that the name was changed to "hospitable" after the [Milesians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milesians_\(Greek\) "Milesians (Greek)") [colonized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_colonisation "Greek colonisation") the region, bringing it into the Greek world.[^14]

Popular supposition derives "Black Sea" from the dark color of the water or climatic conditions. Some scholars understand the name to be derived from a system of Turkic [color symbolism representing the cardinal directions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardinal_direction#Cultural_variations "Cardinal direction"), with black or dark for north, red for south, white for west, and green or light blue for east.[^10] Hence, "Black Sea" meant "Northern Sea". According to this scheme, the name could only have originated with a people living between the northern (black) and southern [(red) seas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea "Red Sea"): this points to the [Achaemenids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire") (550–330 BC).[^10] This interpretation, however, has been contested.[^15]

In the [Greater Bundahishn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bundahishn "Bundahishn"), a [Middle Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Persian "Middle Persian") [Zoroastrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoroastrianism "Zoroastrianism") scripture, the Black Sea is called *Siyābun*.[^16] In the tenth-century Persian geography book *[Hudud al-'Alam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hudud_al-%27Alam "Hudud al-'Alam")*, the Black Sea is called *Georgian Sea* (*daryā-yi Gurz*).[^17] *[The Georgian Chronicles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Georgian_Chronicles "The Georgian Chronicles")* use the name *zğua sperisa* ზღუა სპერისა (Sea of Speri) after the [Kartvelian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kartvelian_peoples "Kartvelian peoples") tribe of Speris or [Saspers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saspers "Saspers").[^18] Other modern names such as *Chyornoye more* and *Karadeniz* (both meaning Black Sea) originated during the 13th century.[^10] A 1570 map *Asiae Nova Descriptio* from [Abraham Ortelius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abraham_Ortelius "Abraham Ortelius") 's *[Theatrum Orbis Terrarum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theatrum_Orbis_Terrarum "Theatrum Orbis Terrarum")* labels the sea *Mar Maggior* (Great Sea), compare Latin *Mare major*.[^19]

English writers of the 18th century often used *Euxine Sea* ([/ ˈ j uː k s ɪ n /](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English") or [/ ˈ j uː k ˌ s aɪ n /](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English")).[^20] During the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire"), it was called either *Bahr-i Siyah* ([Perso-Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perso-Arabic "Perso-Arabic")) or *Karadeniz* ([Ottoman Turkish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Turkish "Ottoman Turkish")), both meaning "Black Sea".[^21]

## Geography

The [International Hydrographic Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Hydrographic_Organization "International Hydrographic Organization") defines the limits of the Black Sea as follows:[^22]

> *On the Southwest.* The Northeastern limit of the [Sea of Marmara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara "Sea of Marmara") \[A line joining [Cape Rumili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Rumili "Cape Rumili") with [Cape Anatoli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Anatoli "Cape Anatoli") (41°13'N)\]. *In the [Kertch Strait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerch_Strait "Kerch Strait").* A line joining Cape Takil and Cape Panaghia (45°02'N).

The area surrounding the Black Sea is commonly referred to as the *Black Sea Region*. Its northern part lies within the *[Chernozem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chernozem "Chernozem") belt* (black soil belt) which goes from eastern Croatia ([Slavonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavonia "Slavonia")), along the [Danube](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danube "Danube") (northern Serbia, northern Bulgaria ([Danubian Plain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danubian_Plain_\(Bulgaria\) "Danubian Plain (Bulgaria)")) and southern Romania ([Wallachian Plain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wallachian_Plain "Wallachian Plain")) to northeast Ukraine and further across the [Central Black Earth Region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Black_Earth_Region "Central Black Earth Region") and southern Russia into [Siberia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siberia "Siberia").[^23]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/Olimp_Romania.jpg/250px-Olimp_Romania.jpg)

Coast of the Black Sea at Olimp

The [littoral zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Littoral_zone "Littoral zone") of the Black Sea is often referred to as the **Pontic littoral** or **Pontic zone**.[^24]

The largest [bays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay "Bay") of the Black Sea are [Karkinit Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karkinit_Bay "Karkinit Bay") in Ukraine; the [Gulf of Burgas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Burgas "Gulf of Burgas") in Bulgaria; [Dnieprovska Gulf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dnieper-Bug_Estuary "Dnieper-Bug Estuary") and [Dniestrovsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dniester_Estuary "Dniester Estuary") [Liman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liman_\(landform\) "Liman (landform)"), both in Ukraine; and [Sinop Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamsilos_Bay "Hamsilos Bay") and [Samsun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsun "Samsun") Bay, both in Turkey.[^25]

### Climate

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Ice_on_the_Gulf_of_Odessa.jpg/250px-Ice_on_the_Gulf_of_Odessa.jpg)

Ice on the Gulf of Odesa

Short-term climatic variation in the Black Sea region is significantly influenced by the operation of the [North Atlantic oscillation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Atlantic_oscillation "North Atlantic oscillation"), the climatic mechanisms resulting from the interaction between the north Atlantic and mid-latitude air masses.[^33] While the exact mechanisms causing the North Atlantic Oscillation remain unclear,[^34] it is thought the climate conditions established in western Europe mediate the heat and precipitation fluxes reaching Central Europe and Eurasia, regulating the formation of winter [cyclones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclone "Cyclone"), which are largely responsible for regional [precipitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precipitation "Precipitation") inputs [^35] and influence Mediterranean [sea surface temperatures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_surface_temperature "Sea surface temperature") (SSTs).[^36]

The relative strength of these systems also limits the amount of cold air arriving from northern regions during winter.[^37] Other influencing factors include the regional [topography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topography "Topography"), as depressions and storm systems arriving from the [Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean "Mediterranean") are funneled through the low land around the Bosporus, with the [Pontic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontic_Mountains "Pontic Mountains") and [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus_Mountains "Caucasus Mountains") mountain ranges acting as waveguides, limiting the speed and paths of cyclones passing through the region.[^38]

## Geology and bathymetry

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/%D0%A1%D1%83%D0%B4%D0%B0%D0%BA.jpg/250px-%D0%A1%D1%83%D0%B4%D0%B0%D0%BA.jpg)

The bay of Sudak, Crimea

The Black Sea is divided into two depositional basins—the Western Black Sea and Eastern Black Sea—separated by the Mid-Black Sea High, which includes the Andrusov Ridge, Tetyaev High, and Archangelsky High, extending south from the [Crimean Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimea "Crimea"). The basin includes two distinct relict [back-arc basins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Back-arc_basin "Back-arc basin") which were initiated by the splitting of an [Albian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albian "Albian") [volcanic arc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanic_arc "Volcanic arc") and the [subduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subduction "Subduction") of both the [Paleo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleo-Tethys_Ocean "Paleo-Tethys Ocean") - and Neo- [Tethys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tethys_Ocean "Tethys Ocean") oceans, but the timings of these events remain uncertain. Arc volcanism and extension occurred as the Neo-Tethys Ocean subducted under the southern margin of [Laurasia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laurasia "Laurasia") during the [Mesozoic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesozoic "Mesozoic"). Uplift and compressional deformation took place as the Neotethys continued to close. Seismic surveys indicate that rifting began in the Western Black Sea in the [Barremian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barremian "Barremian") and [Aptian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aptian "Aptian") followed by the formation of oceanic crust 20 million years later in the [Santonian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santonian "Santonian").[^39] [^40] [^41] Since its initiation, compressional tectonic environments led to [subsidence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subsidence "Subsidence") in the basin, interspersed with extensional phases resulting in large-scale [volcanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanism "Volcanism") and numerous [orogenies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orogeny "Orogeny"), causing the uplift of the [Greater Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greater_Caucasus "Greater Caucasus"), [Pontides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontides "Pontides"), southern Crimean Peninsula and [Balkanides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkan "Balkan") mountain ranges.[^42]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Yavuz-Sultan-Selim-Br%C3%BCcke.jpg/250px-Yavuz-Sultan-Selim-Br%C3%BCcke.jpg)

The Yavuz Sultan Selim Bridge in Istanbul, Turkey, crosses the Bosporus strait near its entrance to the Black Sea. Connecting Europe and Asia, it is one of the tallest suspension bridges in the world.

During the [Messinian salinity crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Messinian_salinity_crisis "Messinian salinity crisis") in the neighboring [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea"), water levels fell but without drying up the sea.[^43] The collision between the [Eurasian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasian_Plate "Eurasian Plate") and [African](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Plate "African Plate") plates and the westward escape of the [Anatolian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolian_Plate "Anatolian Plate") block along the [North Anatolian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Anatolian_Fault "North Anatolian Fault") and [East Anatolian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Anatolian_Fault "East Anatolian Fault") faults dictates the current tectonic regime,[^42] which features enhanced subsidence in the Black Sea basin and significant volcanic activity in the Anatolian region.[^44] These geological mechanisms, in the long term, have caused the periodic isolations of the Black Sea from the rest of the global ocean system.

The large [shelf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_shelf "Continental shelf") to the north of the basin is up to 190 km (120 mi) wide and features a shallow apron with gradients between 1:40 and 1:1000. The southern edge around Turkey and the eastern edge around [Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)"), however, are typified by a narrow shelf that rarely exceeds 20 km (12 mi) in width and a steep apron that is typically 1:40 gradient with numerous submarine canyons and channel extensions. The [Euxine abyssal plain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euxine_abyssal_plain "Euxine abyssal plain") in the center of the Black Sea reaches a maximum depth of 2,212 metres (7,257.22 feet) just south of [Yalta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yalta "Yalta") on the [Crimean Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimea "Crimea").[^45]

### Chronostratigraphy

The Paleo- [Euxinian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euxinia "Euxinia") is described by the accumulation of [eolian silt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aeolian_processes "Aeolian processes") deposits (related to the [Riss glaciation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riss_glaciation "Riss glaciation")) and the lowering of sea levels ([MIS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_isotope_stages "Marine isotope stages") 6, 8 and 10). The Karangat [marine transgression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_transgression "Marine transgression") occurred during the [Eemian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eemian "Eemian") [Interglacial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interglacial "Interglacial") (MIS 5e). This may have been the highest sea levels reached in the late [Pleistocene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pleistocene "Pleistocene"). Based on this some scholars have suggested that the [Crimean Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_Peninsula "Crimean Peninsula") was isolated from the mainland by a shallow strait during the Eemian Interglacial.[^46]

The Neoeuxinian transgression began with an inflow of waters from the [Caspian Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caspian_Sea "Caspian Sea"). Neoeuxinian deposits are found in the Black Sea below −20 m (−66 ft) water depth in three layers. The upper layers correspond with the peak of the Khvalinian transgression, on the shelf shallow-water sands and [coquina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coquina "Coquina") mixed with silty sands and brackish-water fauna, and inside the Black Sea Depression [hydrotroilite](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hydrotroilite&action=edit&redlink=1 "Hydrotroilite (page does not exist)") silts. The middle layers on the shelf are sands with brackish-water mollusc shells. Of continental origin, the lower level on the shelf is mostly [alluvial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alluvial "Alluvial") sands with pebbles, mixed with less common [lacustrine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lacustrine "Lacustrine") silts and freshwater [mollusc shells](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mollusc_shell "Mollusc shell"). Inside the Black Sea Depression they are [terrigenous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrigenous_sediment "Terrigenous sediment") non-carbonate [silts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silt "Silt"), and at the foot of the [continental slope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_slope "Continental slope") [turbidite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turbidite "Turbidite") sediments.[^47]

## Hydrology

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/The_Danube_Spills_into_the_Black_Sea.jpg/500px-The_Danube_Spills_into_the_Black_Sea.jpg)

This SeaWiFS view reveals the currents on the sea's surface.

The Black Sea is the world's largest body of water with a [meromictic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meromictic "Meromictic") basin.[^48] The deep waters do not mix with the upper layers of water that receive oxygen from the atmosphere. As a result, over 90% of the deeper Black Sea volume is [anoxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anoxic_waters "Anoxic waters") water.[^49] The Black Sea's circulation patterns are primarily controlled by basin topography and [fluvial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fluvial "Fluvial") inputs, which result in a strongly stratified vertical structure. Because of the extreme stratification, it is classified as a [salt wedge estuary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Estuary#Salt_wedge "Estuary").

Inflow from the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea") through the [Dardanelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dardanelles "Dardanelles") and [Bosporus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosporus "Bosporus") has a higher salinity and density than the outflow, creating the classic estuarine circulation. This means that the inflow of dense water from the Mediterranean occurs at the bottom of the basin while the outflow of fresher Black Sea surface-water into the [Sea of Marmara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara "Sea of Marmara") occurs near the surface. The outflow is 16,000 cubic metres per second (570,000 cubic feet per second) or around 500 cubic kilometres per year (120 cubic miles per year), and the inflow is 11,000 m <sup>3</sup> /s (390,000 cu ft/s) or around 350 km <sup>3</sup> /a (84 cu mi/a).[^50]

The following water budget can be estimated:

The southern sill of the Bosporus is located at 36.5 m (120 ft) below present sea level (deepest spot of the shallowest cross-section in the Bosporus, located in front of [Dolmabahçe Palace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dolmabah%C3%A7e_Palace "Dolmabahçe Palace")) and has a wet section of around 38,000 m <sup>2</sup> (410,000 sq ft).[^50] Inflow and outflow current speeds are averaged around 0.3 to 0.4 m/s (1.0 to 1.3 ft/s), but much higher speeds are found locally, inducing significant turbulence and vertical shear. This allows for turbulent mixing of the two layers.[^53] Surface water leaves the Black Sea with a salinity of 17 [practical salinity units](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salinity#Seawater "Salinity") (PSU) and reaches the Mediterranean with a salinity of 34 PSU. Likewise, an inflow of the Mediterranean with salinity 38.5 PSU experiences a decrease to about 34 PSU.[^53]

Mean surface circulation is [cyclonic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyclonic_rotation "Cyclonic rotation"); waters around the perimeter of the Black Sea circulate in a basin-wide shelfbreak [gyre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gyre "Gyre") known as the Rim Current. The Rim Current has a maximum velocity of about 50–100 cm/s (20–39 in/s). Within this feature, two smaller cyclonic gyres operate, occupying the eastern and western sectors of the basin.[^53] The Eastern and Western Gyres are well-organized systems in the winter but dissipate into a series of interconnected [eddies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eddy_\(fluid_dynamics\) "Eddy (fluid dynamics)") in the summer and autumn. Mesoscale activity in the peripheral flow becomes more pronounced during these warmer seasons and is subject to interannual variability.

Outside of the Rim Current, numerous quasi-permanent coastal eddies are formed as a result of [upwelling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upwelling "Upwelling") around the coastal apron and "wind curl" mechanisms. The intra-annual strength of these features is controlled by seasonal atmospheric and fluvial variations. During the spring, the Batumi eddy forms in the southeastern corner of the sea.[^54]

Beneath the surface waters—from about 50 to 100 metres (160 to 330 ft)—there exists a [halocline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halocline "Halocline") that stops at the Cold Intermediate Layer (CIL). This layer is composed of cool, salty surface waters, which are the result of localized atmospheric cooling and decreased fluvial input during the winter months. It is the remnant of the winter surface mixed layer.[^53] The base of the CIL is marked by a major [pycnocline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pycnocline "Pycnocline") at about 100–200 metres (330–660 ft), and this density disparity is the major mechanism for isolation of the deep water.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/OrduCoast.jpg/250px-OrduCoast.jpg)

Black Sea coast in Ordu, Turkey

Below the pycnocline is the Deep Water mass, where salinity increases to 22.3 PSU and temperatures rise to around 8.9 °C (48.0 °F).[^53] The hydrochemical environment shifts from oxygenated to anoxic, as bacterial decomposition of sunken biomass utilizes all of the free oxygen. Weak [geothermal heating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geothermal_heating "Geothermal heating") and long [residence time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Residence_time "Residence time") create a very thick convective bottom layer.[^54]

The [Black Sea undersea river](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea_undersea_river "Black Sea undersea river") is a current of particularly saline water flowing through the Bosporus Strait and along the [seabed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seabed "Seabed") of the Black Sea. The discovery of the river, announced on 1 August 2010, was made by scientists at the [University of Leeds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Leeds "University of Leeds") and is the first of its kind to be identified.[^55] The undersea river stems from salty water spilling through the Bosporus Strait from the Mediterranean Sea into the Black Sea, where the water has a lower salt content.[^55]

## Hydrochemistry

Because of the anoxic water at depth, organic matter, including [anthropogenic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_impact_on_the_environment "Human impact on the environment") artifacts such as boat hulls, are well preserved. During periods of high surface productivity, short-lived [algal blooms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algal_bloom "Algal bloom") form organic rich layers known as [sapropels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sapropel "Sapropel"). Scientists have reported an annual [phytoplankton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phytoplankton "Phytoplankton") bloom that can be seen in many NASA images of the region.[^56] As a result of these characteristics the Black Sea has gained interest from the field of [marine archaeology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_archaeology "Marine archaeology"), as ancient shipwrecks in excellent states of preservation have been discovered, such as the [Byzantine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine "Byzantine") wreck [Sinop D](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinop_D "Sinop D"), located in the anoxic layer off the coast of [Sinop, Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinop,_Turkey "Sinop, Turkey").

Modelling shows that, in the event of an [asteroid impact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asteroid_impact "Asteroid impact") on the Black Sea, the release of [hydrogen sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrogen_sulfide "Hydrogen sulfide") clouds would pose a threat to health—and perhaps even life—for people living on the Black Sea coast.[^57]

There have been isolated reports of flares on the Black Sea occurring during thunderstorms, possibly caused by lightning igniting combustible gas seeping up from the sea depths.[^58]

## Ecology

### Marine

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/POTI.JPG/250px-POTI.JPG)

The port of Poti, Georgia

The Black Sea supports an active and dynamic marine ecosystem, dominated by species suited to the [brackish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brackish "Brackish"), nutrient-rich, conditions. As with all marine food webs, the Black Sea features a range of [trophic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trophic_level "Trophic level") groups, with [autotrophic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autotroph "Autotroph") algae, including [diatoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diatom "Diatom") and [dinoflagellates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dinoflagellate "Dinoflagellate"), acting as primary producers. The fluvial systems draining Eurasia and central Europe introduce large volumes of sediment and dissolved nutrients into the Black Sea, but the distribution of these nutrients is controlled by the degree of physiochemical stratification, which is, in turn, dictated by seasonal physiographic development.[^59]

During winter, strong winds promote convective overturning and upwelling of nutrients, while high summer temperatures result in marked vertical stratification and a warm, shallow mixed layer.[^60] Day length and [insolation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insolation "Insolation") intensity also control the extent of the [photic zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photic_zone "Photic zone"). Subsurface productivity is limited by nutrient availability, as the anoxic bottom waters act as a sink for reduced [nitrate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrate "Nitrate"), in the form of [ammonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonia "Ammonia"). The [benthic zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benthic_zone "Benthic zone") also plays an important role in Black Sea nutrient cycling, as chemosynthetic organisms and anoxic geochemical pathways recycle nutrients which can be upwelled to the photic zone, enhancing productivity.[^61]

In total, the Black Sea's biodiversity contains around one-third of the Mediterranean's and is experiencing natural and artificial invasions or "Mediterranizations".[^62] [^63]

#### Phytoplankton

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/Black_Sea_Nasa_May_25_2004.jpg/500px-Black_Sea_Nasa_May_25_2004.jpg)

Phytoplankton blooms and plumes of sediment form the bright blue swirls that ring the Black Sea in this 2004 image.

The main phytoplankton groups present in the Black Sea are [dinoflagellates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dinoflagellate "Dinoflagellate"), [diatoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diatom "Diatom"), [coccolithophores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coccolithophore "Coccolithophore") and [cyanobacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanobacteria "Cyanobacteria"). Generally, the annual cycle of phytoplankton development comprises significant diatom and dinoflagellate-dominated spring production, followed by a weaker mixed assemblage of community development below the seasonal thermocline during summer months, and surface-intensified autumn production.[^60] [^64] This pattern of productivity is augmented by an *[Emiliania huxleyi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emiliania_huxleyi "Emiliania huxleyi")* bloom during the late spring and summer months.

- [Dinoflagellates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dinoflagellate "Dinoflagellate")

Annual dinoflagellate distribution is defined by an extended bloom period in subsurface waters during the late spring and summer. In November, subsurface plankton production is combined with surface production, due to vertical mixing of water masses and nutrients such as [nitrite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nitrite "Nitrite").[^59] The major bloom-forming dinoflagellate species in the Black Sea is *[Gymnodinium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gymnodinium "Gymnodinium")* sp.[^65] Estimates of dinoflagellate diversity in the Black Sea range from 193 [^66] to 267 species.[^67] This level of species richness is relatively low in comparison to the Mediterranean Sea, which is attributable to the brackish conditions, low water transparency and presence of [anoxic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anoxic_waters "Anoxic waters") bottom waters. It is also possible that the low winter temperatures below 4 °C (39 °F) of the Black Sea prevent [thermophilous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermophilous "Thermophilous") species from becoming established. The relatively high organic matter content of Black Sea surface water favors the development of [heterotrophic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heterotroph "Heterotroph") (an organism that uses organic carbon for growth) and mixotrophic dinoflagellates species (able to exploit different trophic pathways), relative to autotrophs. Despite its unique hydrographic setting, there are no confirmed [endemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endemism "Endemism") dinoflagellate species in the Black Sea.[^67]

- [Diatoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diatom "Diatom")

The Black Sea is populated by many species of the marine diatom, which commonly exist as colonies of [unicellular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicellular "Unicellular"), non-motile auto- and heterotrophic [algae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algae "Algae"). The life-cycle of most diatoms can be described as 'boom and bust' and the Black Sea is no exception, with diatom blooms occurring in surface waters throughout the year, most reliably during March.[^59] In simple terms, the phase of rapid population growth in diatoms is caused by the in-wash of [silicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silicon "Silicon") -bearing terrestrial sediments, and when the supply of silicon is exhausted, the diatoms begin to sink out of the photic zone and produce resting [cysts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyst "Cyst"). Additional factors such as predation by zooplankton and [ammonium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ammonium "Ammonium") -based regenerated production also have a role to play in the annual diatom cycle.[^59] [^60] Typically, *Proboscia alata* blooms during spring and *Pseudosolenia calcar-avis* blooms during the autumn.[^65]

- [Coccolithophores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coccolithophore "Coccolithophore")

Coccolithophores are a type of [motile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Motility "Motility"), [autotrophic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autotrophic "Autotrophic") [phytoplankton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phytoplankton "Phytoplankton") that produce CaCO <sub>3</sub> plates, known as [coccoliths](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coccolith "Coccolith"), as part of their life cycle. In the Black Sea, the main period of coccolithophore growth occurs after the bulk of the dinoflagellate growth has taken place. In May, the dinoflagellates move below the seasonal [thermocline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thermocline "Thermocline") into deeper waters, where more nutrients are available. This permits coccolithophores to utilize the nutrients in the upper waters, and by the end of May, with favorable light and temperature conditions, growth rates reach their highest. The major bloom-forming species is *[Emiliania huxleyi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emiliania_huxleyi "Emiliania huxleyi")*, which is also responsible for the release of [dimethyl sulfide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dimethyl_sulfide "Dimethyl sulfide") into the atmosphere. Overall, coccolithophore diversity is low in the Black Sea, and although recent sediments are dominated by *E. huxleyi* and *Braarudosphaera bigelowii*, [Holocene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holocene "Holocene") sediments have been shown to also contain Helicopondosphaera and Discolithina species.

- [Cyanobacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyanobacteria "Cyanobacteria")

Cyanobacteria are a phylum of [picoplanktonic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Picoplankton "Picoplankton") (plankton ranging in size from 0.2 to 2.0 [μm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micrometre "Micrometre")) [bacteria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bacteria "Bacteria") that obtain their energy via [photosynthesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photosynthesis "Photosynthesis"), and are present throughout the world's oceans. They exhibit a range of morphologies, including filamentous colonies and [biofilms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biofilm "Biofilm"). In the Black Sea, several species are present, and as an example, *Synechococcus* spp. can be found throughout the photic zone, although concentration decreases with increasing depth. Other factors which exert an influence on distribution include nutrient availability, predation, and salinity.[^68]

#### Animal species

- [Zebra mussel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zebra_mussel "Zebra mussel")

The Black Sea along with the [Caspian Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caspian_Sea "Caspian Sea") is part of the zebra mussel's native range. The mussel has been accidentally introduced around the world and become an [invasive species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invasive_species "Invasive species") where it has been introduced.

- [Common carp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_carp "Common carp")

The common carp's native range extends to the Black Sea along with the Caspian Sea and [Aral Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aral_Sea "Aral Sea"). Like the zebra mussel, the common carp is an invasive species when introduced to other habitats.

- [Round goby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Round_goby "Round goby")

Another native fish that is also found in the Caspian Sea. It preys upon zebra mussels. Like the mussels and common carp, it has become invasive when introduced to other environments, like the [Great Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Lakes "Great Lakes") in North America.

- ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Dolphins_swim_near_ferry_in_port_of_Batumi_150505-A-PU919-7446.jpg/330px-Dolphins_swim_near_ferry_in_port_of_Batumi_150505-A-PU919-7446.jpg)
	Common dolphins porpoising with a ferry at Batumi port
	Marine mammals and marine [megafauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megafauna "Megafauna")

Marine mammals present within the basin include subspecies of two species of dolphin ([common](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_dolphin "Common dolphin") [^69] and [bottlenose](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea_bottlenose_dolphin "Black Sea bottlenose dolphin") [^70]) and the [harbour porpoise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harbour_porpoise "Harbour porpoise"),[^71] although all of these are endangered due to pressures and impacts by human activities. All three species have been classified as distinct subspecies from those in the Mediterranean and the Atlantic and are endemic to the Black and [Azov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Azov "Sea of Azov") seas, and are more active during nights in the Turkish Straits.[^72] However, construction of the [Crimean Bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_Bridge "Crimean Bridge") has caused increases in nutrients and planktons in the waters, attracting large numbers of fish and more than 1,000 bottlenose dolphins.[^73] However, others claim that construction may cause devastating damages to the ecosystem, including dolphins.[^74]

[Mediterranean monk seals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_monk_seal "Mediterranean monk seal"), now a vulnerable species, were historically abundant in the Black Sea, and are regarded to have become extinct from the basin in 1997.[^75] Monk seals were present at [Snake Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snake_Island_\(Black_Sea\) "Snake Island (Black Sea)"), near the [Danube Delta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danube_Delta "Danube Delta"), until the 1950s, and several locations such as the [Danube Plavni Nature Reserve](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Danube_Plavni_Nature_Reserve&action=edit&redlink=1 "Danube Plavni Nature Reserve (page does not exist)") and [Doğankent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Do%C4%9Fankent "Doğankent") were the last of the seals' [hauling-out](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hauling-out "Hauling-out") sites post-1990.[^76] Very few animals still thrive in the [Sea of Marmara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Marmara "Sea of Marmara").[^77]

Ongoing Mediterranizations may or may not boost cetacean diversity in the Turkish Straits [^72] and hence in the Black and Azov basins.

Various species of [pinnipeds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinniped "Pinniped"), [sea otter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_otter "Sea otter"), and [beluga whale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beluga_whale "Beluga whale") [^78] [^79] were introduced into the Black Sea by mankind and later escaped either by accidental or purported causes. Of these, [grey seals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grey_seal "Grey seal") [^80] and beluga whales [^78] have been recorded with successful, long-term occurrences.

[Great white sharks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_white_shark "Great white shark") are known to reach into the Sea of Marmara and Bosporus Strait and [basking sharks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basking_shark "Basking shark") into the Dardanelles, although it is unclear whether or not these sharks may reach into the Black and Azov basins.[^81] [^82]

#### Ecological effects of pollution

Since the 1960s, rapid industrial expansion along the Black Sea coastline and the construction of a major dam on the Danube have significantly increased annual variability in the N:P:Si ratio in the basin. Coastal areas, accordingly, have seen an increase in the frequency of monospecific phytoplankton blooms, with diatom-bloom frequency increasing by a factor of 2.5 and non-diatom bloom frequency increasing by a factor of 6. The non-diatoms, such as the prymnesiophytes *Emiliania huxleyi* (coccolithophore), *Chromulina* sp., and the Euglenophyte *Eutreptia lanowii*, can out-compete diatom species because of the limited availability of silicon, a necessary constituent of diatom frustules.[^83] As a consequence of these blooms, benthic macrophyte populations were deprived of light, while anoxia caused mass mortality in marine animals.[^84] [^85]

Overfishing during the 1970s further compounded the decline in macrophytes, while the invasive ctenophore *Mnemiopsis* reduced the biomass of [copepods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Copepod "Copepod") and other zooplankton in the late 1980s. Additionally, an alien species—the [warty comb jelly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warty_comb_jelly "Warty comb jelly") (*Mnemiopsis leidyi*)—established itself in the basin, exploding from a few individuals to an estimated biomass of one billion metric tons.[^86]

Pollution-reduction and regulation efforts led to a partial recovery of the Black Sea ecosystem during the 1990s, and an EU monitoring exercise, 'EROS21', revealed decreased nitrogen and phosphorus values relative to the 1989 peak.[^87] Recently, scientists have noted signs of ecological recovery, in part due to the construction of new [sewage-treatment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sewage_treatment "Sewage treatment") plants in Slovakia, Hungary, Romania, and Bulgaria in connection with those countries' membership of the European Union. *Mnemiopsis leidyi* populations have been checked with the arrival of another alien species that feeds on them.[^88] However other sources say that there was ecological decline in the early 21st century.[^89]

## History

### Mediterranean connection during the Holocene

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Istanbul_and_Bosporus_big.jpg/250px-Istanbul_and_Bosporus_big.jpg)

The Bosporus, taken from the International Space Station

The Black Sea is connected to the [World Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Ocean "World Ocean") by a chain of two shallow [straits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait "Strait"), the [Dardanelles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dardanelles "Dardanelles") and the [Bosporus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosporus "Bosporus"). The Dardanelles is 55 m (180 ft) deep, and the Bosporus is as shallow as 36 m (118 ft). By comparison, at the height of the [last ice age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Last_Glacial_Period "Last Glacial Period"), sea levels were more than 100 m (330 ft) lower than they are now.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Dardanelles_map2.png/500px-Dardanelles_map2.png)

Map of the Dardanelles

There is evidence that water levels in the Black Sea were considerably lower at some point during the post-glacial period. Some researchers theorize that the Black Sea had been a landlocked freshwater lake (at least in upper layers) during the last glaciation and for some time after.

In the aftermath of the last glacial period, water levels in the Black Sea and the [Aegean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_Sea "Aegean Sea") rose independently until they were high enough to exchange water. The exact timeline of this development is still subject to debate. One possibility is that the Black Sea filled first, with excess freshwater flowing over the Bosporus sill and eventually into the Mediterranean Sea. There are also catastrophic scenarios, such as the " [Black Sea deluge hypothesis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea_deluge_hypothesis "Black Sea deluge hypothesis") " put forward by [William Ryan](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=William_Ryan_\(geologist\)&action=edit&redlink=1 "William Ryan (geologist) (page does not exist)"), [Walter Pitman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Walter_C._Pitman_III "Walter C. Pitman III") and [Petko Dimitrov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petko_Dimitrov "Petko Dimitrov").

#### Deluge hypothesis

The **Black Sea deluge** is a hypothesized catastrophic rise in the level of the Black Sea c. 5600 BC due to waters from the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea") breaching a sill in the [Bosporus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosporus "Bosporus") Strait. The hypothesis was headlined when *[The New York Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_New_York_Times "The New York Times")* published it in December 1996, shortly before it was published in an [academic journal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academic_journal "Academic journal").[^90] While it is agreed that the sequence of events described did occur, there is debate over the suddenness, dating, and magnitude of the events. Relevant to the hypothesis is that its description has led some to connect this catastrophe with prehistoric [flood myths](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flood_myth "Flood myth").[^91] [^92]

### Archaeology

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Aivazovsky_-_Black_Sea_Fleet_in_the_Bay_of_Theodosia.jpg/330px-Aivazovsky_-_Black_Sea_Fleet_in_the_Bay_of_Theodosia.jpg)

Ivan Aivazovsky. Black Sea Fleet in the Bay of Theodosia, just before the Crimean War

The Black Sea was sailed by [Hittites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hittites "Hittites"), [Carians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carians "Carians"), [Colchians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colchis "Colchis"), [Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenians "Armenians"), [Thracians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thracians "Thracians"), [Greeks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greece "Ancient Greece"), [Persians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire"), [Cimmerians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cimmerians "Cimmerians"), [Scythians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scythians "Scythians"), [Romans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Rome "Ancient Rome"), [Byzantines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Greeks "Byzantine Greeks"), [Goths](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goths "Goths"), [Huns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huns "Huns"), [Avars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pannonian_Avars "Pannonian Avars"), [Slavs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavs "Slavs"), [Varangians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Varangians "Varangians"), [Crusaders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crusaders "Crusaders"), [Venetians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Venice "Republic of Venice"), [Genoese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa "Republic of Genoa"), [Georgians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colchis "Colchis"), [Bulgarians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bulgarians "Bulgarians"), [Tatars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tatars "Tatars") and [Ottomans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire").

The concentration of [historical powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Historical_powers "Historical powers"), combined with the preservative qualities of the deep [anoxic waters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anoxic_waters "Anoxic waters") of the Black Sea, has attracted increased interest from [marine archaeologists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_archaeology "Maritime archaeology") who have begun to discover a large number of [ancient ships and organic remains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Black_Sea_shipwrecks "Ancient Black Sea shipwrecks") in a high state of preservation.

### Recorded history

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Diego-homem-black-sea-ancient-map-1559.jpg/500px-Diego-homem-black-sea-ancient-map-1559.jpg)

A 16th-century map of the Black Sea by Diogo Homem

The Black Sea was a busy waterway on the crossroads of the ancient world: the [Balkans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balkans "Balkans") to the west, the Eurasian [steppes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steppe "Steppe") to the north, the [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus "Caucasus") and [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia") to the east, [Asia Minor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia_Minor "Asia Minor") and [Mesopotamia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesopotamia "Mesopotamia") to the south, and [Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greece "Greece") to the southwest.

The land at the eastern end of the Black Sea, [Colchis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colchis "Colchis") (in present-day [Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)")), marked for the [ancient Greeks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greece "Ancient Greece") the edge of the known world.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Pontos_Euxeinos.gif/500px-Pontos_Euxeinos.gif)

Greek colonies (8th–3rd century BC) of the Black Sea (Euxine, or "hospitable" sea)

The [Pontic–Caspian steppe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontic%E2%80%93Caspian_steppe "Pontic–Caspian steppe") to the north of the Black Sea is seen by several researchers as the pre-historic [original homeland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_homeland "Proto-Indo-European homeland") (*[Urheimat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urheimat "Urheimat")*) of the speakers of the [Proto-Indo-European language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-European_language "Proto-Indo-European language") (PIE).[^93] [^94] [^95] [^96]

Greek presence in the Black Sea began at least as early as the 9th century BC with [colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_colonisation "Greek colonisation") scattered along the Black Sea's southern coast, attracting traders and colonists due to the grain grown in the Black Sea hinterland.[^97] [^98] By 500 BC, permanent Greek communities existed all around the Black Sea, and a lucrative trade network connected the entirety of the Black Sea to the wider Mediterranean. While Greek colonies generally maintained very close cultural ties to their founding [polis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polis "Polis"), Greek colonies in the Black Sea began to develop their own *Black Sea Greek* culture, known today as [Pontic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pontic_Greeks "Pontic Greeks"). The coastal communities of Black Sea Greeks remained a prominent part of the [Greek world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_diaspora "Greek diaspora") for centuries,[^99] and the realms of [Mithridates of Pontus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mithridates_VI_Eupator "Mithridates VI Eupator"), [Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire") and [Constantinople](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire "Byzantine Empire") spanned the Black Sea to include [Crimean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimea "Crimea") territories.

The Black Sea became a virtual [Ottoman Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Navy "Ottoman Navy") lake within five years of the [Republic of Genoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa "Republic of Genoa") losing control of the Crimean Peninsula in 1479, after which the only Western merchant vessels to sail its waters were those of [Venice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venice "Venice") 's old rival [Ragusa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Ragusa "Republic of Ragusa"). The Black Sea became a [trade route of slaves between Crimea and Ottoman Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_slave_trade "Crimean slave trade") via the [Crimean–Nogai slave raids in Eastern Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean%E2%80%93Nogai_slave_raids_in_Eastern_Europe "Crimean–Nogai slave raids in Eastern Europe").[^100]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/dd/BattleOfSinop_2.jpg/250px-BattleOfSinop_2.jpg)

The destruction of the Ottoman fleet in Battle of Sinop

[Imperial Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_Russia "Imperial Russia") became a significant Black Sea power in the late-18th century,[^101] occupying the littoral of [Novorossiya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Novorossiya "Novorossiya") in 1764 and [of Crimea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annexation_of_the_Crimean_Khanate_by_the_Russian_Empire "Annexation of the Crimean Khanate by the Russian Empire") in 1783. Ottoman restrictions on Black Sea navigation were challenged by the [Black Sea Fleet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea_Fleet "Black Sea Fleet") (founded in 1783) of the [Imperial Russian Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Navy "Imperial Russian Navy"), and the Ottomans relaxed export controls after the outbreak in 1789 of the [French Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Revolution "French Revolution").[^102] [^103] [^104] [^105]

### Modern history

The [Crimean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimean_War "Crimean War"), fought between 1853 and 1856, saw naval engagements between the French and British allies and the forces of [Nicholas I of Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicholas_I_of_Russia "Nicholas I of Russia"). On 2 March 1855, after the death of Nicholas I, [Alexander II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_II_of_Russia "Alexander II of Russia") became Tsar. On 15 January 1856, the new tsar took Russia out of the war on the very unfavorable terms of the [Treaty of Paris (1856)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_\(1856\) "Treaty of Paris (1856)"), which included the loss of a naval fleet on the Black Sea, and the provision that the Black Sea was to be a [demilitarized zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demilitarized_zone "Demilitarized zone") similar to a contemporaneous region of the [Baltic Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltic_Sea "Baltic Sea").

#### World Wars

The Black Sea was a significant naval theatre of [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_warfare_of_World_War_I#Black_Sea "Naval warfare of World War I") (1914–1918) and saw both naval and land battles between 1941 and 1945 during [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Front_\(World_War_II\) "Eastern Front (World War II)"). For example, [Sevastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol "Sevastopol") was obliterated by the German Wehrmacht, successfully assisted by [Schwerer Gustav](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schwerer_Gustav "Schwerer Gustav") in the [Siege of Sevastopol (1941–1942)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Sevastopol_\(1941%E2%80%931942\) "Siege of Sevastopol (1941–1942)"). The Soviet naval base was one of the strongest fortifications in the world. Its site, on a deeply eroded, bare limestone promontory at the southwestern tip of the Crimea, made an approach by land forces exceedingly difficult. The high-level cliffs overlooking [Severnaya Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Severnaya_Bay&action=edit&redlink=1 "Severnaya Bay (page does not exist)") protected the anchorage, making an amphibious landing just as dangerous. The Soviet Navy had built upon these natural defenses by modernizing the port and installing heavy [coastal batteries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coastal_batteries "Coastal batteries") consisting of 180mm and 305mm re-purposed battleship guns which were capable of firing inland as well as out to sea. The artillery emplacements were protected by reinforced concrete fortifications and 9.8-inch thick armored turrets.

#### 21st century

In the [2022 Snake Island campaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Snake_Island_campaign "2022 Snake Island campaign"), following the [2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine "2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine"), [Snake Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snake_Island_\(Ukraine\) "Snake Island (Ukraine)") was a source of contention. On 24 February 2022, two Russian navy warships attacked and captured Snake Island.[^106] It was subsequently bombarded heavily by Ukraine.[^107] On 30 June 2022, Ukraine announced that it had driven Russian forces off the island.[^108]

On 14 April 2022, the flagship of the [Black Sea Fleet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea_Fleet "Black Sea Fleet"), [Russian cruiser Moskva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_cruiser_Moskva "Russian cruiser Moskva") was sunk by Ukrainian missiles.[^109]

As early as 29 April 2022 submarines of the Black Sea Fleet were used by Russia to bombard Ukrainian cities with [Kalibr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalibr "Kalibr") [SLCMs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SLCM "SLCM").[^110] [^111] The Kalibr missile was so successful that on 10 March 2023 Defense Minister [Sergey Shoigu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sergey_Shoigu "Sergey Shoigu") announced plans to broaden the type of ship which carried it, to include the [corvette](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corvette "Corvette") [Steregushchiy](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Steregushchiy&action=edit&redlink=1 "Steregushchiy (page does not exist)") and the nuclear-powered cruiser [Admiral Nakhimov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_battlecruiser_Admiral_Nakhimov "Russian battlecruiser Admiral Nakhimov").[^112]

On the morning of 14 March 2023, a Russian [Su-27](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Su-27 "Su-27") fighter jet intercepted and damaged an American [MQ-9 Reaper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MQ-9_Reaper "MQ-9 Reaper") drone, causing the latter [to crash into the Black Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Black_Sea_drone_incident "2023 Black Sea drone incident"). At 13:20 on 5 May 2023 a Russian [Su-35](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Su-35 "Su-35") fighter jet intercepted and threatened the safety of a Polish [L-140 Turbolet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L-140_Turbolet "L-140 Turbolet") on a "routine [Frontex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frontex "Frontex") patrol mission.. and performed 'aggressive and dangerous' manoeuvres".[^113] The incident, which occurred "in international airspace over the Black Sea about 60km" east of Romanian airspace,[^114] "caused the crew of five Polish border guards to lose control of the plane and lose altitude." [^115]

As of January 2025, neither Ukraine nor Russia control the Black Sea, making it contested, claims [Estonian Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Estonian_Navy "Estonian Navy") Commander Ivo Värk. "The entire Black Sea can currently be seen as a contested maritime area where both sides have some room for action,". This room is greater near the coasts, where both sides benefit from air defense systems, sea mines, and various land-based weaponry. Ships only move out for specific operations to prevent excessive risks. He also noted that both parties operate gas drilling platforms equipped with surveillance devices to monitor the situation, but that those platforms often change hands.[^116] [^117]

Amasra, Turkey, is located on a small island in the Black Sea.

The Black Sea plays an integral part in the connection between Asia and Europe.[^118] In addition to sea ports and fishing, key activities include hydrocarbons exploration for oil and natural gas, and tourism.

According to [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO"), the Black Sea is a strategic corridor that provides [smuggling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smuggling "Smuggling") channels for moving legal and illegal goods including drugs, radioactive materials, and counterfeit goods that can be used to finance terrorism.[^119]

### Navigation

According to an [International Transport Workers' Federation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Transport_Workers%27_Federation "International Transport Workers' Federation") 2013 study, there were at least 30 operating merchant [seaports](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seaport "Seaport") in the Black Sea (including at least 12 in [Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ukraine "Ukraine")).[^120] There were also around 2,400 commercial vessels operating in the Black Sea.[^120]

### Fishing

The Turkish commercial fishing fleet catches around 300,000 tons of [anchovies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_anchovy "European anchovy") per year. The fishery is carried out mainly in winter, and the highest portion of the stock is caught in November and December.[^121]

### Hydrocarbon exploration

The Black Sea contains oil and natural gas resources but exploration in the sea is incomplete. As of 2017, 20 wells are in place. Throughout much of its existence, the Black Sea has had significant oil and gas-forming potential because of significant inflows of sediment and nutrient-rich waters. However, this varies geographically. For example, prospects are poorer off the coast of Bulgaria because of the large influx of sediment from the Danube which obscured sunlight and diluted organic-rich sediments. Many of the discoveries to date have taken place offshore of Romania in the Western Black Sea and only a few discoveries have been made in the Eastern Black Sea.

During the [Eocene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eocene "Eocene"), the [Paratethys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paratethys "Paratethys") Sea was partially isolated and sea levels fell. During this time sand shed off the rising Balkanide, Pontide and Caucasus mountains trapped organic material in the Maykop Suite of rocks through the [Oligocene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oligocene "Oligocene") and early [Miocene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miocene "Miocene"). Natural gas appears in rocks deposited in the Miocene and Pliocene by the paleo-Dnieper and paleo-Dniester rivers, or in deep-water Oligocene-age rocks. Serious exploration began in 1999 with two deep-water wells, Limanköy-1 and Limanköy-2, drilled in Turkish waters. Next, the HPX (Hopa)-1 deepwater well targeted late Miocene sandstone units in Achara-Trialeti fold belt (also known as the Gurian fold belt) along the Georgia-Turkey maritime border. Although geologists inferred that these rocks might have hydrocarbons that migrated from the Maykop Suite, the well was unsuccessful. No more drilling happened for five years after the HPX-1 well. In 2010, Sinop-1 targeted carbonate reservoirs potentially charged from the nearby Maykop Suite on the Andrusov Ridge, but the well-struck only Cretaceous volcanic rocks. Yassihöyük-1 encountered similar problems.

Other Turkish wells, Sürmene-1 and Sile-1 drilled in the Eastern Black Sea in 2011 and 2015 respectively tested four-way closures above Cretaceous volcanoes, with no results in either case. A different Turkish well, Kastamonu-1 drilled in 2011 did successfully find thermogenic gas in Pliocene and Miocene shale-cored anticlines in the Western Black Sea. A year later in 2012, Romania drilled Domino-1 which struck gas prompting the drilling of other wells in the Neptun Deep. In 2016, the Bulgarian well Polshkov-1 targeted Maykop Suite sandstones in the Polshkov High and Russia is in the process of drilling Jurassic carbonates on the Shatsky Ridge as of 2018.[^122]

In August 2020, [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") found 320 billion cubic metres (11 trillion cubic feet) of natural gas in the biggest ever discovery in the Black Sea, and hoped to begin production in the [Sakarya Gas Field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sakarya_Gas_Field "Sakarya Gas Field") by 2023. The sector is near where [Romania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romania "Romania") has also found gas reserves.[^123]

### Modern military use

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/36/USS_Yorktown_collision.jpg/250px-USS_Yorktown_collision.jpg)

Soviet frigate Bezzavetny (right) bumping the USS Yorktown during the 1988 Black Sea bumping incident

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/72/Boat_U170_Skadovsk_2012_G1.jpg/250px-Boat_U170_Skadovsk_2012_G1.jpg)

Ukrainian Navy artillery boat U170 in the Bay of Sevastopol

The [1936 Montreux Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montreux_Convention_Regarding_the_Regime_of_the_Straits "Montreux Convention Regarding the Regime of the Straits") provides for free passage of civilian ships between the international waters of the Black and the Mediterranean seas. However, a single country (Turkey) has complete control over the straits connecting the two seas. Military ships are categorized separately from civilian vessels and can pass through the straits only if the ship belongs to a Black Sea country. Other military ships have the right to pass through the straits if they are not in a war against Turkey and if they stay in the Black Sea basin for a limited time. The 1982 amendments to the [Montreux Convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montreux_Convention_Regarding_the_Regime_of_the_Straits "Montreux Convention Regarding the Regime of the Straits") allow Turkey to close the straits at its discretion in both war and peacetime.[^129]

The Montreux Convention governs the passage of vessels between the Black, Mediterranean, and Aegean seas and the presence of military vessels belonging to non- [littoral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Littoral "Littoral") states in the Black Sea waters.[^130]

The Russian [Black Sea Fleet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea_Fleet "Black Sea Fleet") has its official primary headquarters and facilities in the city of [Sevastopol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol "Sevastopol") ([Sevastopol Naval Base](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sevastopol_Naval_Base "Sevastopol Naval Base")).[^131]

The Soviet hospital ship [*Armenia*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_hospital_ship_Armenia "Soviet hospital ship Armenia") was sunk on 7 November 1941 by German aircraft while evacuating civilians and wounded soldiers from [Crimea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crimea "Crimea"). It has been estimated that approximately 5,000 to 7,000 people were killed during the sinking, making it one of the deadliest [maritime disasters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_maritime_disasters_in_the_20th_century "List of maritime disasters in the 20th century") in history. There were only eight survivors.[^132]

In December 2018, the [Kerch Strait incident](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kerch_Strait_incident "Kerch Strait incident") occurred, in which the Russian navy and coast guard took control of three Ukrainian vessels as the ships were trying to transit from the Black Sea into the [Sea of Azov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Azov "Sea of Azov").[^133]

In April 2022, following the [2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine "2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine"), the [Russian cruiser Moskva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_cruiser_Moskva "Russian cruiser Moskva") was sunk in the western Black Sea by sea-skimming [R-360 Neptune](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/R-360_Neptune "R-360 Neptune") missiles of the [Armed Forces of Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armed_Forces_of_Ukraine "Armed Forces of Ukraine") [^134] while the Russians claimed that an onboard fire had caused munitions to explode and damage the ship extensively.[^135] She was the largest ship to be lost in naval combat in Europe since [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II").[^136]
