**Azerbaijan**,[^1] officially the **Republic of Azerbaijan**,[^2] is a [transcontinental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boundaries_between_the_continents "Boundaries between the continents") [landlocked country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked_country "Landlocked country") at the boundary of Western Asia and Eastern Europe.[^3] It is a part of the [South Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Caucasus "South Caucasus") region and is bounded by the [Caspian Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caspian_Sea "Caspian Sea") to the east, Russia's republic of [Dagestan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dagestan "Dagestan") to the north, [Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)") to the northwest, [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey"), via the exclave of [Nakhchivan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nakhchivan_Autonomous_Republic "Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic"), and [Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenia "Armenia") to the west, and [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran") to the south. [Baku](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baku "Baku") is the capital and largest city.

The territory of what is now Azerbaijan was ruled first by [Caucasian Albania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasian_Albania "Caucasian Albania") and later by various [Persian empires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_empires "Persian empires"). Until the 19th century, it remained part of [Qajar Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qajar_Iran "Qajar Iran"), but the Russo-Persian wars of [1804–1813](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Persian_War_\(1804%E2%80%931813\) "Russo-Persian War (1804–1813)") and [1826–1828](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Persian_War_\(1826%E2%80%931828\) "Russo-Persian War (1826–1828)") forced the Qajar Empire to cede its Caucasian territories to the [Russian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire"); the treaties of [Gulistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Gulistan "Treaty of Gulistan") in 1813 and [Turkmenchay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Turkmenchay "Treaty of Turkmenchay") in 1828 defined the border between Russia and Iran.[^17] [^18] The region north of the [Aras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aras_\(river\) "Aras (river)") was part of Iran until it was conquered by Russia in the 19th century,[^19] [^20] where it was administered as part of the [Caucasus Viceroyalty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus_Viceroyalty_\(1801%E2%80%931917\) "Caucasus Viceroyalty (1801–1917)").

By the late 19th century, an [Azerbaijani national identity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijani_national_identity "Azerbaijani national identity") emerged, and the [Azerbaijan Democratic Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan_Democratic_Republic "Azerbaijan Democratic Republic") proclaimed its independence from the [Transcaucasian Democratic Federative Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transcaucasian_Democratic_Federative_Republic "Transcaucasian Democratic Federative Republic") in 1918, a year after the Russian Empire [collapsed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Revolution "Russian Revolution"), and became the first [secular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secularity "Secularity") democratic [Muslim-majority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_world "Muslim world") state in the world. In 1920, the country was conquered and incorporated into the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union") as the [Azerbaijan SSR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Azerbaijan Soviet Socialist Republic"). The modern Republic of Azerbaijan proclaimed its independence on 30 August 1991,[^21] [^22] shortly before the [dissolution of the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union "Dissolution of the Soviet Union"). In September 1991, the ethnic [Armenian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenians "Armenians") majority of the [Nagorno-Karabakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_Autonomous_Oblast "Nagorno-Karabakh Autonomous Oblast") region formed the self-proclaimed [Republic of Artsakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Artsakh "Republic of Artsakh"), which became *de facto* independent with the end of the [First Nagorno-Karabakh War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Nagorno-Karabakh_War "First Nagorno-Karabakh War") in 1994, although the region and [seven surrounding districts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian-occupied_territories_surrounding_Nagorno-Karabakh "Armenian-occupied territories surrounding Nagorno-Karabakh") remained internationally recognized as part of Azerbaijan.[^23] [^24] [^25] [^26] Following the [Second Nagorno-Karabakh War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Nagorno-Karabakh_War "Second Nagorno-Karabakh War") in 2020, the seven districts and parts of Nagorno-Karabakh were returned to Azerbaijani control.[^27] An [Azerbaijani offensive in 2023](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Azerbaijani_offensive_in_Nagorno-Karabakh "2023 Azerbaijani offensive in Nagorno-Karabakh") ended the Republic of Artsakh and resulted in the [flight of Nagorno-Karabakh Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expulsion_of_Nagorno-Karabakh_Armenians "Expulsion of Nagorno-Karabakh Armenians").

Azerbaijan is a [unitary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitary_state "Unitary state") [semi-presidential](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-presidential_system "Semi-presidential system") republic.[^9] It is one of six independent [Turkic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkic_peoples "Turkic peoples") states and an active member of the [Organization of Turkic States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organization_of_Turkic_States "Organization of Turkic States") and the [TÜRKSOY](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Organization_of_Turkic_Culture "International Organization of Turkic Culture") community. Azerbaijan has diplomatic relations with 182 countries and holds membership in 38 international organizations,[^28] including the United Nations, the [Council of Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Europe "Council of Europe"), the [Non-Aligned Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement "Non-Aligned Movement"), the [Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe (OSCE)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organization_for_Security_and_Co-operation_in_Europe "Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe"), and the [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO") [PfP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partnership_for_Peace "Partnership for Peace") program. It is one of the founding members of [GUAM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GUAM_Organization_for_Democracy_and_Economic_Development "GUAM Organization for Democracy and Economic Development"), the [Commonwealth of Independent States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States "Commonwealth of Independent States"),[^29] and the [OPCW](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_for_the_Prohibition_of_Chemical_Weapons "Organisation for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons"). Azerbaijan is an observer state of the [World Trade Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Trade_Organization "World Trade Organization").

The vast majority of the country's population (97%) is Muslim.[^30] [^31] The [Constitution of Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Azerbaijan "Constitution of Azerbaijan") does not declare an official religion, and all major political forces in the country are secular. Azerbaijan is a [developing country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country "Developing country") and ranks 81st on the [Human Development Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index "Human Development Index"). The ruling [New Azerbaijan Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Azerbaijan_Party "New Azerbaijan Party"), in power since 1993, has been accused of authoritarianism under presidents [Heydar Aliyev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heydar_Aliyev "Heydar Aliyev") and his son [Ilham Aliyev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilham_Aliyev "Ilham Aliyev"). The ruling [Aliyev family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aliyev_family "Aliyev family") has been criticized on Azerbaijan's [human rights record](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_Azerbaijan "Human rights in Azerbaijan"), including [media restrictions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Media_freedom_in_Azerbaijan "Media freedom in Azerbaijan") and repression of its [Shia Muslim population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_in_Azerbaijan "Islam in Azerbaijan").[^32]

## Etymology

The term *Azerbaijan* derives from *[Atropates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atropates "Atropates")*,[^33] [^34] a [Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persians "Persians") [^35] [^36] [satrap](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satrap "Satrap") under the [Achaemenid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire") who was reinstated as the satrap of [Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medes "Medes") under [Alexander the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_the_Great "Alexander the Great").[^37] [^38] The original etymology of this name is thought to have its roots in the once-dominant [Zoroastrianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoroastrianism "Zoroastrianism"). In the [Avesta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avesta "Avesta") 's *Frawardin [Yasht](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yasht "Yasht")* ("Hymn to the Guardian Angels"), there is a mention of *âterepâtahe ashaonô fravashîm ýazamaide*, which translates from [Avestan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avestan "Avestan") as "we worship the *[fravashi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fravashi "Fravashi")* of the holy [Atropatene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atropatene "Atropatene") ".[^39] The name "Atropates" is the Greek transliteration of an [Old Iranian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_languages "Iranian languages"), probably [Median](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Median_language "Median language"), compounded name with the meaning "Protected by the (Holy) Fire" or "The Land of the (Holy) Fire".[^40] The Greek name was mentioned by [Diodorus Siculus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diodorus_Siculus "Diodorus Siculus") and [Strabo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strabo "Strabo"). Over the span of millennia, the name evolved to *Āturpātākān* ([Middle Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Persian "Middle Persian")), then to *Ādharbādhagān*, *Ādhorbāygān*, *Āzarbāydjān* (New Persian) and present-day *Azerbaijan*.[^41]

The name *Azerbaijan* was first adopted by the government of [Musavat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musavat "Musavat") in 1918 [^42] after the [collapse of the Russian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Revolution "Russian Revolution"), when the independent [Azerbaijan Democratic Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan_Democratic_Republic "Azerbaijan Democratic Republic") was established. Until then, the designation had been used exclusively to identify the [adjacent region of contemporary northwestern Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan_\(Iran\) "Azerbaijan (Iran)"),[^43] [^44] [^45] [^46] while the area of the Azerbaijan Democratic Republic was formerly referred to as *[Arran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arran_\(Caucasus\) "Arran (Caucasus)")* and *[Shirvan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shirvan "Shirvan")*.[^47] On that basis Iran protested the newly adopted country name.[^48]

During Soviet rule, the country was also spelled in Latin from the [Russian transliteration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_transliteration "Russian transliteration") as **Azerbaydzhan** ([Russian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language"): Азербайджа́н).[^49] The country's name was also spelled in [Cyrillic script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyrillic_script "Cyrillic script") from 1940 to 1991 as Азәрбајҹан.

## History

### Antiquity

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Ancient_Azerbaijan_4.jpg/250px-Ancient_Azerbaijan_4.jpg)

Petroglyphs in Gobustan National Park dating back to the 10th millennium BC indicating a thriving culture

The earliest evidence of human settlement in the territory of Azerbaijan dates back to the late [Stone Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stone_Age "Stone Age") and is related to the [Guruchay culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guruchay_culture "Guruchay culture") of [Azykh Cave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azykh_Cave "Azykh Cave").[^50] Early settlements included the [Scythians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scythia "Scythia") during the 9th century BC.[^40] Following the Scythians, Iranian [Medes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medes "Medes") came to dominate the area to the south of the [Aras river](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aras_\(river\) "Aras (river)").[^38] The Medes forged a vast empire between 900 and 700 BC, which was integrated into the [Achaemenid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire") around 550 BC.[^51] The area was conquered by the Achaemenids leading to the spread of [Zoroastrianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoroastrianism "Zoroastrianism").[^52]

### From the Sasanid period to the Safavid period

The [Maiden Tower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maiden_Tower_\(Baku\) "Maiden Tower (Baku)") and the [Palace of the Shirvanshahs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palace_of_the_Shirvanshahs "Palace of the Shirvanshahs") in the [Old City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_City_\(Baku\) "Old City (Baku)") of Baku are a UNESCO [World Heritage Site](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Heritage_Site "World Heritage Site"), built in the 11th–12th centuries.

Palace of the Shirvanshahs

The [Sasanian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sasanian_Empire "Sasanian Empire") turned [Caucasian Albania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasian_Albania "Caucasian Albania") into a [vassal state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vassal_state "Vassal state") in 252, while King [Urnayr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urnayr "Urnayr") officially adopted Christianity as the state religion in the 4th century.[^53] Despite Sassanid rule, Caucasian Albania remained an entity in the region until the 9th century, while fully subordinate to Sassanid Iran, and retained its monarchy. Despite being one of the chief vassals of the Sasanian emperor, the Albanian king had only a semblance of authority, and the Sasanian [marzban](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marzban "Marzban") (military governor) held most civil, religious, and military authority.[^54]

In the first half of the 7th century, Caucasian Albania, as a vassal of the Sasanians, came under nominal Muslim rule with the [Muslim conquest of Persia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_conquest_of_Persia "Muslim conquest of Persia"). The [Umayyad Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate "Umayyad Caliphate") repulsed both the Sasanians and [Byzantines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire "Byzantine Empire") from the [South Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Caucasus "South Caucasus") and turned Caucasian Albania into a vassal state after Christian resistance led by King [Juansher](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juansher "Juansher") was suppressed in 667. The power vacuum left by the decline of the [Abbasid Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbasid_Caliphate "Abbasid Caliphate") was filled by numerous local dynasties such as the [Sallarids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sallarid_dynasty "Sallarid dynasty"), [Sajids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sajid_dynasty "Sajid dynasty"), and [Shaddadids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shaddadids "Shaddadids"). At the beginning of the 11th century, the territory was gradually seized by the waves of migrating [Oghuz Turks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oghuz_Turks "Oghuz Turks") from Central Asia, who adopted a [Turkoman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkoman_\(ethnonym\) "Turkoman (ethnonym)") [ethnonym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnonym "Ethnonym") at the time.[^55] The first of these Turkic dynasties established was the [Seljuk Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seljuk_Empire "Seljuk Empire"), which entered the area by 1067.[^56]

The pre-Turkic population spoke several [Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-European_languages "Indo-European languages") and Caucasian languages, among them [Armenian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_language "Armenian language") [^57] [^58] [^59] [^60] [^61] and an [Iranian language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_languages "Iranian languages"), [Old Azeri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Azeri "Old Azeri"), which was gradually replaced by a [Turkic language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkic_languages "Turkic languages"), the early precursor of the [Azerbaijani language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijani_language "Azerbaijani language") of today.[^62] Some linguists have also stated that the [Tati dialects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tat_language_\(Caucasus\) "Tat language (Caucasus)") of [Iranian Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan_\(Iran\) "Azerbaijan (Iran)") and the Republic of Azerbaijan, like those spoken by the [Tats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tat_people_\(Caucasus\) "Tat people (Caucasus)"), are descended from Old Azeri.[^63] [^64] Locally, the possessions of the subsequent Seljuk Empire were ruled by [Eldiguzids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eldiguzids "Eldiguzids"), technically vassals of the Seljuk sultans, but sometimes *de facto* rulers themselves. Under the Seljuks, local poets such as [Nizami Ganjavi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nizami_Ganjavi "Nizami Ganjavi") and [Khaqani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khaqani "Khaqani") gave rise to a blossoming of [Persian literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_literature "Persian literature") in the region.[^65] [^66]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Map_Safavid_persia.png/250px-Map_Safavid_persia.png)

Having ended the rule of the Shirvanshahs in 1538, Tahmasp I established Shirvan (in present-day Azerbaijan) as an administrative unit of the Safavid Iran.

[Shirvanshahs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shirvanshahs "Shirvanshahs"), the local dynasty of Arabic origin that was later Persianized, became a vassal state of [Timurid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timurid_Empire "Timurid Empire") of [Timur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timur "Timur") and assisted him in his war with the ruler of the [Golden Horde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Horde "Golden Horde") [Tokhtamysh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tokhtamysh "Tokhtamysh"). Following Timur's death, two independent and rival Turkoman states emerged: [Qara Qoyunlu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qara_Qoyunlu "Qara Qoyunlu") and [Aq Qoyunlu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aq_Qoyunlu "Aq Qoyunlu"). The Shirvanshahs returned, maintaining for numerous centuries to come a high degree of autonomy as local rulers and vassals as they had done since 861. In 1501, the [Safavid dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Safavid_dynasty "Safavid dynasty") of Iran subdued the Shirvanshahs and gained its possessions. In the course of the next century, the Safavids [converted the formerly Sunni population to Shia Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Safavid_conversion_of_Iran_to_Shia_Islam "Safavid conversion of Iran to Shia Islam"),[^67] [^68] [^69] as they did with the population in what is modern-day Iran.[^70] The Safavids allowed the Shirvanshahs to remain in power under Safavid suzerainty until 1538, when Safavid King [Tahmasp I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tahmasp_I "Tahmasp I") completely deposed them and made the area into the Safavid [province of Shirvan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Safavid_Shirvan "Safavid Shirvan"). The Sunni Ottomans briefly managed to occupy present-day Azerbaijan as a result of the [Ottoman–Safavid War of 1578–1590](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Safavid_War_\(1578%E2%80%931590\) "Ottoman–Safavid War (1578–1590)"); by the early 17th century, they were ousted by Safavid Iranian ruler [Abbas I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbas_I_of_Persia "Abbas I of Persia"). In the wake of the demise of the Safavid dynasty, [Baku](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baku "Baku") and its environs were briefly occupied by the Russians as a consequence of the [Russo-Persian War of 1722–1723](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Persian_War_\(1722%E2%80%931723\) "Russo-Persian War (1722–1723)"). Remainder of present Azerbaijan was [occupied by the Ottomans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_Shah#First_Ottoman_campaign_and_the_reconquest_of_the_Caucasus "Nader Shah") from 1722 to 1736.[^71] Despite brief intermissions such as these by Safavid Iran's neighboring rivals, the land remained under Iranian rule from the earliest advent of the Safavids up to the course of the 19th century.[^72] [^73]

### Modern history

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/12/Political_map_of_the_eastern_part_of_the_Southern_Caucasus_between_1795%E2%80%931801.svg/330px-Political_map_of_the_eastern_part_of_the_Southern_Caucasus_between_1795%E2%80%931801.svg.png)

Political map of the eastern part of the South Caucasus between 1795 and 1801

After the Safavids, the area was ruled by the Iranian [Afsharid dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afsharid_dynasty "Afsharid dynasty"). After the death of [Nader Shah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nader_Shah "Nader Shah") in 1747, many of his former subjects capitalized on the eruption of instability. [Numerous khanates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanates_of_the_Caucasus "Khanates of the Caucasus") with various forms of autonomy [^74] [^75] [^76] [^77] [^78] emerged. The rulers of these khanates were directly related to the ruling dynasties of Iran and were vassals and [subjects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commoners "Commoners") of the Iranian shah.[^79] The khanates exercised control over their affairs via international trade routes between Central Asia and the West.[^80]

Thereafter, the area was under the successive rule of the Iranian [Zands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zand_dynasty "Zand dynasty") and [Qajars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qajar_dynasty "Qajar dynasty").[^81] From the late 18th century, [Imperial Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire") switched to a more aggressive geo-political stance towards Iran and the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire").[^82] Russia actively tried to gain possession of the Caucasus region which was, for the most part, in the hands of Iran.[^83] In 1804, the Russians [invaded and sacked the Iranian town of Ganja](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Ganja_\(1804\) "Siege of Ganja (1804)"), sparking the [Russo-Persian War of 1804–1813](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Persian_War_\(1804%E2%80%9313\) "Russo-Persian War (1804–13)").[^84] The militarily superior Russians ended the war with a victory.[^85] Following Qajar Iran's loss, it was forced to concede suzerainty over most of the khanates, along with Georgia and Dagestan to the Russian Empire, per the [Treaty of Gulistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Gulistan "Treaty of Gulistan").[^86]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/%D0%92%D0%B7%D1%8F%D1%82%D0%B8%D0%B8_%D1%88%D1%82%D1%83%D1%80%D0%BC%D0%BE%D0%BC_%D0%BA%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BF%D0%BE%D1%81%D1%82%D0%B8_%D0%93%D1%8F%D0%BD%D0%B4%D0%B6%D0%B8.jpg/250px-%D0%92%D0%B7%D1%8F%D1%82%D0%B8%D0%B8_%D1%88%D1%82%D1%83%D1%80%D0%BC%D0%BE%D0%BC_%D0%BA%D1%80%D0%B5%D0%BF%D0%BE%D1%81%D1%82%D0%B8_%D0%93%D1%8F%D0%BD%D0%B4%D0%B6%D0%B8.jpg)

The siege of Ganja Fortress in 1804 during the Russo-Persian War of 1804–1813

The area to the north of the Aras River was Iranian territory until Russia occupied it in the 19th century.[^19] [^87] [^88] [^89] [^90] [^91] About a decade later, in violation of the Gulistan treaty, the Russians invaded Iran's [Erivan Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erivan_Khanate "Erivan Khanate").[^92] [^93] This sparked the final bout of hostilities between the two, the [Russo-Persian War of 1826–1828](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Persian_War_\(1826-1828\) "Russo-Persian War (1826-1828)"). The resulting [Treaty of Turkmenchay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Turkmenchay "Treaty of Turkmenchay") forced Qajar Iran to cede sovereignty over the Erivan Khanate, the [Nakhchivan Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nakhchivan_Khanate "Nakhchivan Khanate") and the remainder of the [Talysh Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Talysh_Khanate "Talysh Khanate").[^86] After the incorporation of all Caucasian territories from Iran into Russia, the border between the two was set at the Aras River.[^94]

Despite the Russian conquest, throughout the entire 19th century, preoccupation with [Iranian culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Iran "Culture of Iran"), [literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literature_of_Iran "Literature of Iran"), and language remained widespread among Shia and Sunni intellectuals in the Russian-held cities of Baku, Ganja and Tiflis ([Tbilisi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tbilisi "Tbilisi"), now Georgia).[^95] Within the same century, in post-Iranian Russian-held East Caucasia, an Azerbaijani national identity emerged at the end of the 19th century.[^96] As a result of the Russian conquest, the [Azerbaijanis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijanis "Azerbaijanis") are nowadays parted between two nations: Iran and Azerbaijan.[^97]

After the collapse of the Russian Empire during [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I"), the short-lived [Transcaucasian Democratic Federative Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transcaucasian_Democratic_Federative_Republic "Transcaucasian Democratic Federative Republic") was declared, constituting the present-day republics of Azerbaijan, Georgia, and Armenia. It was followed by the [March Days](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/March_Days "March Days") massacres [^98] [^99] that took place between 30 March and 2 April 1918 in Baku and adjacent areas of the [Baku Governorate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baku_Governorate "Baku Governorate").[^100] When the republic dissolved in May 1918, the leading [Musavat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musavat "Musavat") party declared independence as the [Azerbaijan Democratic Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan_Democratic_Republic "Azerbaijan Democratic Republic") (ADR), adopting the name of "Azerbaijan", a name that prior to the proclamation of the ADR was solely used to refer to the adjacent [northwestern region of contemporary Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_Azerbaijan "Iranian Azerbaijan").[^43] [^44] [^45] The ADR was the first modern [parliamentary republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_republic "Parliamentary republic") in the Muslim world.[^19] [^101] [^102] Among the important accomplishments of the Parliament was the extension of suffrage to women, making ADR the first Muslim nation to grant women equal political rights with men.[^101] [Baku State University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baku_State_University "Baku State University"), first modern university founded in the Muslim East, was established during this period.[^101]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/Map_of_the_Azerbaijan_Democratic_Republic_2_%281919%29.gif/330px-Map_of_the_Azerbaijan_Democratic_Republic_2_%281919%29.gif)

Map presented by the delegation of Azerbaijan in the 1919 Paris Peace Conference

Independent Azerbaijan lasted only 23 months until the [Bolshevik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bolshevik "Bolshevik") [11th Soviet Red Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/11th_Army_\(RSFSR\) "11th Army (RSFSR)") invaded it, establishing the [Azerbaijan Soviet Socialist Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Azerbaijan Soviet Socialist Republic") on 28 April 1920. Although the bulk of the newly formed Azerbaijani army was engaged in putting down an Armenian revolt that had broken out in [Karabakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karabakh "Karabakh"), Azerbaijanis did not surrender their brief independence of 1918–20 quickly or easily. As many as 20,000 Azerbaijani soldiers died resisting what was effectively a Russian reconquest.[^103] Within the ensuing early Soviet period, the Azerbaijani national identity was forged.[^96]

On 13 October 1921, the Soviet republics of Russia, Armenia, Azerbaijan, and Georgia signed an agreement with Turkey known as the [Treaty of Kars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Kars "Treaty of Kars"). The previously independent [Republic of Aras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Aras "Republic of Aras") would also become the [Nakhchivan Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nakhchivan_Autonomous_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Nakhchivan Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic") within the Azerbaijan SSR by the treaty of Kars.[^19] [^104] On the other hand, Armenia was awarded the region of [Zangezur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syunik_Province "Syunik Province") and Turkey agreed to return [Gyumri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gyumri "Gyumri") (then known as Alexandropol).[^105]

During [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), Azerbaijan played a crucial role in the strategic energy policy of the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union"), with 80 percent of the Soviet Union's oil on the [Eastern Front](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Front_\(World_War_II\) "Eastern Front (World War II)") being supplied by Baku. By decree of the [Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_the_Soviet_Union "Supreme Soviet of the Soviet Union") in February 1942, the commitment of more than 500 workers and employees of the oil industry of Azerbaijan were awarded orders and medals. [Operation Edelweiss](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Edelweiss "Operation Edelweiss") carried out by the German [Wehrmacht](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wehrmacht "Wehrmacht") targeted Baku because of its importance as the energy (petroleum) dynamo of the USSR.[^19] A fifth of all Azerbaijanis fought in the Second World War from 1941 to 1945. Approximately 681,000 people (with over 100,000 women) went to the front, while the total population of Azerbaijan was 3.4 million at the time.[^106] Some 250,000 people from Azerbaijan were killed on the front. More than 130 Azerbaijanis were named [heroes of the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hero_of_the_Soviet_Union "Hero of the Soviet Union"). Azerbaijani Major-General [Azi Aslanov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azi_Aslanov "Azi Aslanov") was twice awarded the Hero of the Soviet Union.[^107]

### Independence

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8c/RedArmy_Paratroops_Baku_1990.jpg/250px-RedArmy_Paratroops_Baku_1990.jpg)

Soviet Airborne Forces trooper during the Black January tragedy in 1990

Following the politics of *[glasnost](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glasnost "Glasnost")* initiated by [Mikhail Gorbachev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev "Mikhail Gorbachev"), civil unrest and ethnic strife grew in various regions of the Soviet Union, including [Nagorno-Karabakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_Autonomous_Oblast "Nagorno-Karabakh Autonomous Oblast"),[^108] an autonomous region of the Azerbaijan SSR. The disturbances in Azerbaijan, in response to Moscow's indifference to an already heated conflict, resulted in calls for independence and secession, which culminated in the [Black January](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_January "Black January") events in Baku.[^109] Later in 1990, the [Supreme Council of the Azerbaijan SSR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_Soviet_of_Azerbaijan_SSR "Supreme Soviet of Azerbaijan SSR") dropped the words "Soviet Socialist" from the title, adopted the "Declaration of Sovereignty of the Azerbaijan Republic" and restored the flag of the Azerbaijan Democratic Republic as the state flag.[^110] As a consequence of the failed [1991 Soviet coup attempt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1991_Soviet_coup_attempt "1991 Soviet coup attempt") in Moscow, the Supreme Council of Azerbaijan adopted a Declaration of Independence on 18 October 1991 which was affirmed by a nationwide referendum in December, while the Soviet Union officially ceased to exist on 26 December.[^110] The country celebrates its [Day of Restoration of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Day_of_Restoration_of_Independence_\(Azerbaijan\) "Day of Restoration of Independence (Azerbaijan)") on 18 October.[^111]

The early years of independence were overshadowed by the [First Nagorno-Karabakh war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Nagorno-Karabakh_war "First Nagorno-Karabakh war") with the ethnic Armenian majority of Nagorno-Karabakh backed by Armenia.[^112] By the end of the hostilities in 1994, Armenians controlled 14–16 percent of Azerbaijani territory, including Nagorno-Karabakh.[^31] [^113] During the war many atrocities and pogroms by both sides were committed including the massacres at [Malibeyli, Gushchular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capture_of_Gushchular_and_Malibeyli "Capture of Gushchular and Malibeyli") and [Garadaghly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capture_of_Garadaghly "Capture of Garadaghly") and the [Khojaly massacre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khojaly_massacre "Khojaly massacre"), along with the [Baku pogrom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baku_pogrom "Baku pogrom"), the [Maraga massacre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maraga_massacre "Maraga massacre") and the [Kirovabad pogrom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kirovabad_pogrom "Kirovabad pogrom").[^114] [^115] Furthermore, an estimated 30,000 people were killed and more than a million people were displaced (more than 800,000 Azerbaijanis and 300,000 Armenians).[^116] Four [United Nations Security Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council "United Nations Security Council") resolutions ([822](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_822 "United Nations Security Council Resolution 822"), [853](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_853 "United Nations Security Council Resolution 853"), [874](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_874 "United Nations Security Council Resolution 874"), and [884](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_884 "United Nations Security Council Resolution 884")) demand for "the immediate withdrawal of all Armenian forces from all occupied territories of Azerbaijan." [^117] Many Russians and Armenians fled Azerbaijan as refugees during the 1990s.[^118] According to the 1970 census, there were 510,000 ethnic [Russians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_diaspora "Russian diaspora") and 484,000 Armenians in Azerbaijan.[^119]

### Aliyev family rule, 1993–present

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/21/Nagorno-Karabakh_conflict_map_%28pre-2020%29.png/500px-Nagorno-Karabakh_conflict_map_%28pre-2020%29.png)

Military situation in the Nagorno-Karabakh region prior to the Second Nagorno-Karabakh War

In 1993, democratically elected President [Abulfaz Elchibey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abulfaz_Elchibey "Abulfaz Elchibey") was overthrown by a military insurrection led by Colonel [Surat Huseynov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surat_Huseynov "Surat Huseynov"), which resulted in the rise to power of the former leader of Soviet Azerbaijan, [Heydar Aliyev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heydar_Aliyev "Heydar Aliyev"). In 1994, Huseynov, by that time the prime minister, attempted another military coup against Heydar Aliyev, but he was arrested and charged with treason.[^120] In 1995 [another coup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1995_Azerbaijani_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt "1995 Azerbaijani coup d'état attempt") was attempted against Aliyev, this time by the commander of the Russian [OMON](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OMON "OMON") special police unit, [Rovshan Javadov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rovshan_Javadov "Rovshan Javadov"). The coup was averted, resulting in the death of Javadov and disbanding of Azerbaijan's OMON units.[^121] [^122] At the same time, the country was tainted by rampant corruption in the governing bureaucracy.[^123] In October 1998, Aliyev was re-elected for a second term.

[Ilham Aliyev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilham_Aliyev "Ilham Aliyev"), Heydar Aliyev's son, became chairman of the [New Azerbaijan Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Azerbaijan_Party "New Azerbaijan Party") as well as [President of Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Azerbaijan "President of Azerbaijan") when his father died in 2003. He was [re-elected to a third term](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijani_presidential_election,_2013 "Azerbaijani presidential election, 2013") as president in October 2013.[^124] In April 2018, Aliyev secured his fourth consecutive term in the [election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Azerbaijani_presidential_election "2018 Azerbaijani presidential election") that was boycotted by the main opposition parties as fraudulent.[^125] On 27 September 2020, clashes in the unresolved [Nagorno-Karabakh conflict](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_conflict "Nagorno-Karabakh conflict") resumed along the [Nagorno-Karabakh Line of Contact](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_Line_of_Contact "Nagorno-Karabakh Line of Contact"). Both the armed forces of Azerbaijan and Armenia reported military and civilian casualties.[^126] The [Nagorno-Karabakh ceasefire agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Nagorno-Karabakh_ceasefire_agreement "2020 Nagorno-Karabakh ceasefire agreement") and the end of the six-week [war between Azerbaijan and Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Nagorno-Karabakh_War "Second Nagorno-Karabakh War") was widely celebrated in Azerbaijan, as they made significant territorial gains.[^127] Despite the much improved economy,[^128] particularly with the exploitation of the [Azeri–Chirag–Guneshli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azeri%E2%80%93Chirag%E2%80%93Gunashli "Azeri–Chirag–Gunashli") oil field and [Shah Deniz gas field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shah_Deniz_gas_field "Shah Deniz gas field"), the Aliyev family rule has been criticized with election fraud,[^129] high levels of economic inequality [^130] and [domestic corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Azerbaijan "Corruption in Azerbaijan").[^131] In September 2023, Azerbaijan [launched an offensive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Azerbaijani_offensive_in_Nagorno-Karabakh "2023 Azerbaijani offensive in Nagorno-Karabakh") against the breakaway [Republic of Artsakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Artsakh "Republic of Artsakh") in Nagorno-Karabakh that resulted in the dissolution and reintegration of Artsakh on 1 January 2024 and the [expulsion of nearly all ethnic Armenians from the region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expulsion_of_Nagorno-Karabakh_Armenians "Expulsion of Nagorno-Karabakh Armenians").[^132] [^133] [^134]

On 8 August 2025, Azerbaijani President [Ilham Aliyev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilham_Aliyev "Ilham Aliyev") and Armenian Prime Minister [Nikol Pashinyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikol_Pashinyan "Nikol Pashinyan") signed a joint declaration at the [White House](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_House "White House"),[^135] committing to a [peace deal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenia%E2%80%93Azerbaijan_peace_agreement "Armenia–Azerbaijan peace agreement") facilitated by the [Trump administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_presidency_of_Donald_Trump "Second presidency of Donald Trump") that would end nearly four decades of conflict between the two countries.[^136]

## Geography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Koppen-Geiger_Map_AZE_present.svg/500px-Koppen-Geiger_Map_AZE_present.svg.png)

Köppen-Geiger climate classification map for Azerbaijan 137

Azerbaijan is located in the [South Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Caucasus "South Caucasus") region of [Eurasia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasia "Eurasia"), straddling [West Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Asia "West Asia") and [Eastern Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Europe "Eastern Europe"). It lies between latitudes [38°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/38th_parallel_north "38th parallel north") and [42° N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/42nd_parallel_north "42nd parallel north"), and longitudes [44°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/44th_meridian_east "44th meridian east") and [51° E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/51st_meridian_east "51st meridian east"). The perimeter of Azerbaijan's land borders is 2,648 km (1,645 mi), of which 1,007 km (626 mi) are with Armenia, 756 km (470 mi) with Iran, 480 kilometres with Georgia, 390 km (242 mi) with Russia and 15 km (9 mi) with Turkey.[^138] The coastline stretches for 800 km (497 mi), and the length of the widest area of the Azerbaijani section of the [Caspian Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caspian_Sea "Caspian Sea") is 456 km (283 mi).[^138] The country has a landlocked [exclave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclave_and_exclave "Enclave and exclave"), the [Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nakhchivan_Autonomous_Republic "Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic").[^139]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Budug_Azerbaijan16.jpg/250px-Budug_Azerbaijan16.jpg)

Caucasus Mountains in northern Azerbaijan

Three physical features dominate Azerbaijan: the Caspian Sea, whose shoreline forms a natural boundary to the east; the [Greater Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greater_Caucasus "Greater Caucasus") mountain range to the north; and the extensive flatlands at the country's centre. There are three mountain ranges: the Greater and [Lesser Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lesser_Caucasus "Lesser Caucasus"), and the [Talysh Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Talysh_Mountains "Talysh Mountains"), together covering approximately 40% of the country.[^140] The highest peak is [Mount Bazardüzü](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Bazard%C3%BCz%C3%BC "Mount Bazardüzü") 4,466 m (14,652 ft), while the lowest point lies in the Caspian Sea −28 m (−92 ft). Nearly half of all the [mud volcanoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mud_volcano "Mud volcano") on Earth are concentrated in Azerbaijan, [these volcanoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mud_volcanoes_in_Azerbaijan "Mud volcanoes in Azerbaijan") were among nominees for the [New 7 Wonders of Nature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_7_Wonders_of_Nature "New 7 Wonders of Nature").[^141]

The main water sources are surface waters. Only 24 of the 8,350 stream are greater than 100 km (62 mi) in length.[^140] All the streams drain into the Caspian Sea.[^140] The largest lake is [Sarysu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Sarysu "Lake Sarysu") at 67 km <sup>2</sup> (26 sq mi), and the longest river is [Kur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kura_\(Caspian_Sea\) "Kura (Caspian Sea)") at 1,515 km (941 mi), which is [transboundary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transboundary_river "Transboundary river") with Armenia. Azerbaijan has several islands along the Caspian sea, mostly located in the [Baku Archipelago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baku_Archipelago "Baku Archipelago").

Since independence in 1991, the government has taken measures to preserve the [environment of Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environment_of_Azerbaijan "Environment of Azerbaijan"). National protection of the environment accelerated after 2001 when the state budget increased through revenues provided by the [Baku–Tbilisi–Ceyhan pipeline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baku%E2%80%93Tbilisi%E2%80%93Ceyhan_pipeline "Baku–Tbilisi–Ceyhan pipeline"). Within four years, protected areas doubled and now make up eight percent of the country's territory. Since 2001 the government has set up seven large reserves and almost doubled the sector of the budget earmarked for environmental protection.[^142]

### Landscape

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Bazarduzu_detail.JPG/250px-Bazarduzu_detail.JPG)

Mount Bazarduzu, the highest peak of Azerbaijan, as seen from Mount Shahdagh

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Azerbajiani_landscape_-_Another_version.jpg/250px-Azerbajiani_landscape_-_Another_version.jpg)

The landscape of Khinalug valley

Azerbaijan is home to a wide variety of landscapes. Over half of the land consists of mountain ridges, crests, highlands, and plateaus which rise up to levels of 400–1,000 meters (including the middle and lower lowlands), in some places (Talis, Jeyranchol-Ajinohur and Langabiz-Alat foreranges) up to 100–120 meters, and others from 0–50 meters and up ([Qobustan, Absheron](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qobustan,_Absheron "Qobustan, Absheron")). The rest of Azerbaijan's terrain consists of plains and lowlands. Elevations within the Caucasus region vary from about −28 meters at the Caspian Sea shoreline up to 4,466 meters ([Bazardüzü](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Bazard%C3%BCz%C3%BC "Mount Bazardüzü") peak).[^143] [^144]

The climate is influenced by cold arctic [air masses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_mass "Air mass") of Scandinavian [anticyclone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anticyclone "Anticyclone"), temperate air masses of [Siberian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siberia "Siberia") anticyclone, and Central Asian anticyclone.[^145] [^146] [^147] Azerbaijan's landscape affects the ways air masses enter the country.[^145] The Greater Caucasus protects the country from direct influences of cold air masses coming from the north. That leads to the formation of [subtropical climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subtropics "Subtropics") on most foothills and plains of the country. Meanwhile, plains and foothills are characterized by high [solar radiation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunlight "Sunlight") rates.[^148] [^149]

Nine out of eleven existing [climate zones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification") are present in Azerbaijan.[^150] Both the absolute minimum temperatures (−33 °C or −27.4 °F ) and the absolute maximum temperatures were observed in [Julfa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Julfa_District "Julfa District") and [Ordubad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ordubad_District "Ordubad District") —regions of [Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nakhchivan_Autonomous_Republic "Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic").[^150] The maximum annual precipitation falls in [Lankaran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lankaran "Lankaran") (1,600 to 1,800 mm or 63 to 71 in) and the minimum in Absheron (200 to 350 mm or 7.9 to 13.8 in).[^150] [^151] [^152]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/Murov_mountain_in_Azerbaijan-Caucasus3.jpg/250px-Murov_mountain_in_Azerbaijan-Caucasus3.jpg)

Murovdag is the highest mountain range in the Lesser Caucasus.

Rivers and lakes form the principal part of the water systems of Azerbaijan; they were formed over a long geological timeframe and changed significantly throughout that period. This is evidenced by remnants of ancient rivers found throughout the country. The water systems are continually changing under the influence of natural forces and human-introduced industrial activities. Canals and ponds are a part of Azerbaijan's water systems. In terms of water supply, Azerbaijan is below the average in the world with approximately 100,000 cubic metres (3,531,467 cubic feet) per year of water per square kilometre.[^150] All big [water reservoirs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reservoir "Reservoir") are built on Kur. The hydrography of Azerbaijan belongs to the Caspian Sea basin.

The [Kura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kura_\(South_Caucasus_river\) "Kura (South Caucasus river)") and [Aras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aras_\(river\) "Aras (river)") are the major rivers in Azerbaijan. They run through the [Kura-Aras lowland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kura-Aras_lowland "Kura-Aras lowland"). The rivers that directly flow into the Caspian Sea originate mainly from the north-eastern slope of the Major Caucasus and Talysh Mountains and run along the Samur–Devechi and Lankaran lowlands.[^153] [^154]

[Yanar Dag](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yanar_Dagh "Yanar Dagh"), translated as "burning mountain", is a natural gas fire which blazes continuously on a hillside on the [Absheron Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absheron_Peninsula "Absheron Peninsula") on the Caspian Sea near Baku, which itself is known as the "land of fire." Flames jet out into the air from a thin, porous sandstone layer. It is a tourist attraction to visitors to the Baku area.[^155]

### Biodiversity

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Stamps_of_Azerbaijan%2C_2006-750.jpg/250px-Stamps_of_Azerbaijan%2C_2006-750.jpg)

The Karabakh horse is the national animal of Azerbaijan.

The first reports on the richness and diversity of animal life in Azerbaijan can be found in travel notes of Eastern travellers. Animal carvings on architectural monuments, ancient rocks, and stones survived up to the present times. The first information on flora and fauna of Azerbaijan was collected during the visits of naturalists to Azerbaijan in the 17th century.[^140]

There are 106 species of mammals, 97 species of fish, 363 species of birds, 10 species of amphibians, and 52 species of reptiles which have been recorded and classified in Azerbaijan.[^140] The national animal of Azerbaijan is the [Karabakh horse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karabakh_horse "Karabakh horse"), a mountain-steppe racing and riding horse endemic to Azerbaijan. The Karabakh horse has a reputation for its good temper, speed, elegance, and intelligence. It is one of the oldest breeds, with ancestry dating to the ancient world, but today the horse is an endangered species.[^156]

Azerbaijan's flora consists of more than 4,500 species of higher plants. Due to the unique climate in Azerbaijan, the flora is much richer in the number of species than the flora of the other republics of the South Caucasus. Sixty-six percent of the species growing in the whole [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus "Caucasus") can be found in Azerbaijan.[^157] The country lies within four ecoregions: [Caspian Hyrcanian mixed forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caspian_Hyrcanian_mixed_forests "Caspian Hyrcanian mixed forests"), [Caucasus mixed forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus_mixed_forests "Caucasus mixed forests"), [Eastern Anatolian montane steppe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Anatolian_montane_steppe "Eastern Anatolian montane steppe"), and [Azerbaijan shrub desert and steppe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan_shrub_desert_and_steppe "Azerbaijan shrub desert and steppe").[^158]

Azerbaijan had a 2018 [Forest Landscape Integrity Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest_Landscape_Integrity_Index "Forest Landscape Integrity Index") mean score of 6.55/10, ranking it 72nd globally out of 172 countries.[^159] [Forest cover](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest_cover "Forest cover") is around 14.% of the total land area, equivalent to 1,131,770 hectares (ha) of forest in 2020, up from 944,740 hectares (ha) in 1990. In 2020, naturally regenerating forest covered 826,200 hectares (ha) and planted forest covered 305,570 hectares (ha). Of the naturally regenerating forest 0% was reported to be [primary forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_forest "Primary forest") (consisting of native tree species with no clearly visible indications of human activity) and around 33% of the forest area was found within protected areas. In 2015, 100% of the forest area was reported to be under [public ownership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_ownership "State ownership"), 0% [private ownership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Private_property "Private property") and 0% with ownership listed as other or unknown.[^160] [^161]
