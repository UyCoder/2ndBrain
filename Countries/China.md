**China**,[^8] officially the **People's Republic of China** (**PRC**),[^9] [^29] is a country in [East Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Asia "East Asia"). It is the [second-most populous country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population_\(United_Nations\) "List of countries by population (United Nations)") after India, with [a population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_of_China "Population of China") exceeding 1.4 billion (17% of the world's population), across an area of 9.6 million square kilometers (3,700,000 sq mi), making it the [third-largest country by area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_area "List of countries and dependencies by area").[^10] It is divided into 33 [province-level divisions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Province-level_divisions_of_China "Province-level divisions of China"), including two [special administrative regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_administrative_regions_of_China "Special administrative regions of China"). [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") is the capital, while [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai") is the [most populous city by urban area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_China_by_population "List of cities in China by population"). Its geography features the vast [Central Plain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhongyuan "Zhongyuan"), major rivers such as the [Yangtze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yangtze "Yangtze") and [Yellow River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_River "Yellow River"), deserts, [subtropical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subtropical "Subtropical") and temperate forests, and [mountain ranges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountain_range "Mountain range") such as the [Himalayas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Himalayas "Himalayas").

The first humans in China arrived during the [Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleolithic "Paleolithic"). By the 2nd millennium BCE, [dynastic states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynasties_in_Chinese_history "Dynasties in Chinese history") had emerged. The 1st millennium BCE saw political turmoil and cultural growth. In 221 BCE, China was unified under the [Qin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_dynasty "Qin dynasty") and the succeeding [Han dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty"), ushering in two millennia of imperial rule across periods of unity and division. Its achievements include [widespread cultural influence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinosphere "Sinosphere"), the [Silk Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silk_Road "Silk Road"), and the invention of [gunpowder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invention_of_gunpowder "Invention of gunpowder"), [paper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_paper#Paper_in_China "History of paper"), [printing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Printing "Printing"), and the [compass](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compass "Compass"). After increased Western political, economic, and philosophical influence, the [1911 Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1911_Revolution "1911 Revolution") overthrew the empire and established the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") (ROC). The [Warlord Era](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warlord_Era "Warlord Era") and [Chinese Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War "Chinese Civil War") followed, interrupted by [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Japan "Empire of Japan") 's [invasion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War "Second Sino-Japanese War"). This ended in a Chinese victory in 1945. In 1949, the [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") (CCP) [proclaimed the People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Proclamation of the People's Republic of China") and forced the [ROC's retreat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retreat_of_the_government_of_the_Republic_of_China_to_Taiwan "Retreat of the government of the Republic of China to Taiwan") to [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan"). [Both sides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two_Chinas "Two Chinas") claim political legitimacy. CCP attempts to advance [communism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communism "Communism") faltered through famine and political turmoil. The [reform and opening up](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reform_and_opening_up "Reform and opening up") that began in 1978 moved China towards a [market economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist_market_economy "Socialist market economy"), spurring economic growth.

The PRC is a [unitary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitary_state "Unitary state") state with the CCP as its [sole ruling party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One-party_state "One-party state"). It is one of the five [permanent members](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Permanent_members_of_the_United_Nations_Security_Council "Permanent members of the United Nations Security Council") of the [UN Security Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UN_Security_Council "UN Security Council") and a member of [many multilateral organizations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_organization_membership_of_China "International organization membership of China"). China is the world's largest [manufacturer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manufacturing "Manufacturing") and makes up about one-fifth of the global economy. It is the [second largest economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\) "List of countries by GDP (nominal)") on Earth. International organizations [rank China poorly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_rankings_of_China "International rankings of China") in measures of [democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy_in_China "Democracy in China") and [human rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_China "Human rights in China"). Since the 2020s, it has been described as a [superpower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superpower "Superpower") due to its large military, [nuclear stockpile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuclear_weapons_of_China "Nuclear weapons of China"), and influence in geopolitics, [science and technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_and_technology_in_China "Science and technology in China"), [manufacturing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industry_in_China "Industry in China"), [economics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_China "Economy of China") and [culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_culture "Chinese culture").

[Chinese art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_art "Chinese art") and culture has influenced much of Asia. [Chinese characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_characters "Chinese characters") are among the oldest writing systems on Earth, with a [literary tradition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_literature "Chinese literature") dating back millennia to the [Chinese classics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_classics "Chinese classics"). China is the birthplace of [Confucianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucianism "Confucianism") and [Daoism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daoism "Daoism"), which form the traditional [three teachings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_teachings "Three teachings") of [Chinese philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_philosophy "Chinese philosophy") and [folk religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_folk_religion "Chinese folk religion") alongside [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism"). [Chinese cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_cuisine "Chinese cuisine") is diverse and highly regional, with rice as a staple in the south and wheat in the north. It has over [60 World Heritage Sites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_World_Heritage_Sites_in_China "List of World Heritage Sites in China"), including the [Great Wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Wall_of_China "Great Wall of China") and [Grand Canal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Canal_\(China\) "Grand Canal (China)"). The [Han](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_Chinese "Han Chinese"), mostly speakers of [Sinitic languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinitic_languages "Sinitic languages"), are China's dominant ethnicity, although it is home to [55 recognized minorities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_ethnic_groups_in_China "List of ethnic groups in China"), including the [Hui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hui_people "Hui people"), [Mongols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongols "Mongols"), [Tibetans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibetans "Tibetans"), [Uyghurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uyghurs "Uyghurs"), and [Zhuang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhuang_people "Zhuang people").

## Etymology

The word "China" has been used in English since the 16th century; however, it was not used by the Chinese themselves during this period. Its origin has been traced through [Portuguese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_language "Portuguese language"), [Malay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_language "Malay language"), and [Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_language "Persian language") back to the [Sanskrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanskrit "Sanskrit") word *Cīna* (चिन), used in [ancient India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_India "Ancient India").[^31] *Cīna* was first used in early [Hindu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism "Hinduism") scripture from the 3rd century BCE to 4th century CE, including the *[Mahabharata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahabharata "Mahabharata")* and the *[Laws of Manu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manusmriti "Manusmriti")*. In 1655, the missionary [Martino Martini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martino_Martini "Martino Martini") suggested that the word China is derived ultimately from the name of the [Qin dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_dynasty "Qin dynasty") (221–206 BCE) or the prior state of [Qin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_\(state\) "Qin (state)").[^32] This remains a common etymology, although Indian sources precedes the dynasty.[^32] [^33] Another possible source is the ancient [Guizhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guizhou "Guizhou") polity of [Yelang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yelang "Yelang"), known as *ʐina* in [Loloish languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loloish_languages "Loloish languages").[^32]

The official name of the modern state is the "People's Republic of China" (中华人民共和国; *Zhōnghuá Rénmín Gònghéguó*).[^29] The shorter form of this name is 中国; *Zhōngguó*, from *zhōng* ('central' or 'middle') and *guó* ('state'), a term first used for the [demesne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_land "Crown land") of the [Western Zhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Zhou "Western Zhou") dynasty.[^11] [^34] [^35] The names of ruling imperial dynasties were typically used to refer to the region and state, with *Zhongguo* vague and uncommon. In the 1800s, *Zhongguo* was officially adopted as the name of the country by the [Qing dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty").[^35] China is sometimes referred to as [mainland China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_China "Mainland China") or "the Mainland" when distinguishing it from the [Republic of China on Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") or the [PRC's Special Administrative Regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_administrative_regions_of_China "Special administrative regions of China").[^36]

## History

### Prehistory

Evidence of early humans such as *[Homo erectus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_erectus "Homo erectus")* in China dates to the [Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleolithic "Paleolithic"), around 1.7 million years ago, with unconfirmed sites as old as 2 million years ago. Modern *[Homo sapiens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_sapiens "Homo sapiens")* are attested from around 50,000 years ago.[^37] Following the end of the [Last Glacial Period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Last_Glacial_Period "Last Glacial Period"), in about 8,000 BCE, pottery-making [Neolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic "Neolithic") cultures emerged. By the 6000s BCE, sedentary agricultural societies had spread across the lower [Yellow River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_River "Yellow River") basin.[^38] [^39] These societies became increasingly complex, urbanized, and stratified,[^40] but many experienced a population collapse in the late 2000s BCE for unclear reasons.[^41]

### Ancient China

![A set of bronze ritual vessels arranged in a museum display](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cd/MET_DP219959.jpg/250px-MET_DP219959.jpg)

A set of Western Zhou -era ritual bronzes, c. 1000 BCE

After 2000 BCE, a [Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age "Bronze Age") culture emerged in the [Central Plain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhongyuan "Zhongyuan"), dubbed the [Erlitou culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erlitou_culture "Erlitou culture") after its [main site](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erlitou "Erlitou"). Erlitou has been controversially identified with the [Xia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xia_dynasty "Xia dynasty"), the traditionally-accepted first [dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynasties_of_China "Dynasties of China"), but no written records exist to confirm this.[^42] [^43] [^44] The [Shang dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shang_dynasty "Shang dynasty") purportedly succeeded the Xia around 1600 BCE, with its early stages tentatively identified with an expansionist state known archaeologically as the [Erligang culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erligang_culture "Erligang culture").[^45] The historicity of the [Late Shang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Shang "Late Shang") is attested through divinational writings in the [oracle bone script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oracle_bone_script "Oracle bone script"). These are the earliest known form of writing in China and the ancestor of modern [Chinese characters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_characters "Chinese characters").[^46] [^47]

The Shang were overthrown by the [Zhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhou_dynasty "Zhou dynasty") c. 1046 BCE. The Zhou ruled over a vast and loose confederation of vassal states across central China gradually weakened by regional lords.[^48] Centralized authority finally collapsed in 771 BCE, giving way to constant regional warfare. During the [Eastern Zhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Zhou "Eastern Zhou"), a multitude of small aristocratic [Spring and Autumn period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spring_and_Autumn_period "Spring and Autumn period") polities evolved into seven territorial [Warring States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warring_States_period "Warring States period") over the following centuries.[^49] [^50] Literary and philosophical developments of this period include the emergence of various schools of thought, such as [Confucianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucianism "Confucianism"), [Daoism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daoism "Daoism"), [Mohism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohism "Mohism"), and [Legalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legalism_\(Chinese_philosophy\) "Legalism (Chinese philosophy)"), alongside [Chinese classics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_classics "Chinese classics") like the *[Analects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Analects "Analects")* and the *[Tao Te Ching](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tao_Te_Ching "Tao Te Ching")*.[^49] [^51]

### Imperial China

#### Classical period

![A group of the Terracotta Warriors standing in an archaeological dig site](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9a/Terracotta_Army_%286143564816%29.jpg/250px-Terracotta_Army_%286143564816%29.jpg)

Soldier statues of the Terracotta Army, buried alongside Qin Shi Huang c. 210 BCE

In the 220s BCE, [Qin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_\(state\) "Qin (state)") rapidly conquered the other warring states. In 221, its ruler [Qin Shi Huang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_Shi_Huang "Qin Shi Huang") proclaimed himself the first [emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emperor_of_China "Emperor of China") (皇帝; *Huángdì*) and founded the [Qin dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_dynasty "Qin dynasty").[^52] [^53] He led an [autocratic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autocracy "Autocracy") Legalist state organized in a system of [commanderies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commandery_\(China\) "Commandery (China)") and [counties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/County_\(China\) "County (China)"). The dynasty lasted only fifteen years, falling soon after his death.[^54] [^55]

Following [widespread revolts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chu%E2%80%93Han_Contention "Chu–Han Contention"), the [Han dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty") emerged to rule China between 206 BCE and 220 CE.[^12] [^56] The Han gradually reinstated centralized control, legitimizing their rule through Confucian scholarship.[^57] [^58] Military expeditions against the [Xiongnu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xiongnu "Xiongnu"), a confederation of nomadic [steppe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasian_Steppe "Eurasian Steppe") tribes frequently in conflict with the dynasty, expanded Han influence into parts of [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia") and helped to establish the [Silk Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silk_Road "Silk Road"), allowing for trade connections between China and western Eurasia. Contemporaneously, merchants established [maritime trade routes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_Silk_Road "Maritime Silk Road") linking China, [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia"), and [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India").[^57] [^59]

The Han faced widespread uprisings and the emergence of local warlords in the 100s CE.[^60] By 220, the empire was split into the [Three Kingdoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Kingdoms "Three Kingdoms"). These were briefly united by the [Jin dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jin_dynasty_\(266%E2%80%93420\) "Jin dynasty (266–420)") in 280, which fell into civil war. [Sinicized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinicization "Sinicization") formerly nomadic peoples who had settled in Northern China, such as the Xiongnu, rebelled and [founded new dynasties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sixteen_Kingdoms "Sixteen Kingdoms").[^61] [^62] These coalesced into the [Northern and Southern dynasties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_and_Southern_dynasties "Northern and Southern dynasties") in the 400s.[^63] During these conflicts, [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism") was introduced to China via the Silk Road.[^64] [^65]

#### Medieval period

After centuries of warfare, China was reunited under the [Sui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sui_dynasty "Sui dynasty") in 589,[^66] who constructed the [Grand Canal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Canal_\(China\) "Grand Canal (China)") to link Northern and Southern China.[^67] The Sui collapsed in the 610s, and were succeeded by the [Tang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_dynasty "Tang dynasty"). The Tang dynasty centralized the state and dispatched military expeditions to pacify the surrounding regions. It promoted [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Buddhism "Chinese Buddhism"), but through expanding international trade built a heavily cosmopolitan society centered on its capital of [Chang'an](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chang%27an "Chang'an").[^68] It reformed the [civil service examinations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_examination "Imperial examination") and oversaw a flourishing of art, poetry, architecture, and scholarship.[^69] [^70] [^71] The 755–763 [An Lushan rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/An_Lushan_rebellion "An Lushan rebellion") weakened the Tang, which gradually fragmented before [collapsing completely](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five_Dynasties_and_Ten_Kingdoms_period "Five Dynasties and Ten Kingdoms period") in 907.[^72] [^73]

Section from the Song -era landscape painting, Along the River During the Qingming Festival, early 1100s

The [Song dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Song_dynasty "Song dynasty") rose to power in 960. It faced military crises, unable to subdue its Sinicized non-Han neighbors, the [Khitan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khitan_people "Khitan people") -led [Liao dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liao_dynasty "Liao dynasty") and the [Tangut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangut_people "Tangut people") -led [Western Xia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Xia "Western Xia"). The consistent focus on defense allowed for a heavily centralized state and military, which made the first military application of [gunpowder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gunpowder "Gunpowder").[^74] [^75] The proliferation of [printing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Printing "Printing") technology allowed books to become widely available,[^76] while the elite class of [scholar-officials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scholar-official "Scholar-official") grew increasingly powerful.[^77] Production, population, and trade expanded massively, alongside innovations such as industrial [metallurgy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metallurgy "Metallurgy") and [hydraulic machinery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydraulic_machinery "Hydraulic machinery").[^78] The Song capital [Kaifeng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaifeng "Kaifeng") was overrun by the [Jurchen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jurchen_people "Jurchen people") -led [Jin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jin_dynasty_\(1115%E2%80%931234\) "Jin dynasty (1115–1234)") in 1127, forcing the Song to retreat to Southern China. During this period, the revivalist philosophical movement of [Neo-Confucianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neo-Confucianism "Neo-Confucianism") emerged.[^79]

The Great Wall of China, first unified by Emperor Qin Shi Huang by connecting earlier Warring States fortifications. Most of the surviving structure dates to the Ming dynasty.

#### Late imperial period

In 1206, [Genghis Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genghis_Khan "Genghis Khan") united the nomadic [Mongols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongols "Mongols") to the north under the [Mongol Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_Empire "Mongol Empire"). Over the following fifty years, they [conquered the Western Xia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_conquest_of_Western_Xia "Mongol conquest of Western Xia") and [the Jin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_conquest_of_the_Jin_dynasty "Mongol conquest of the Jin dynasty"), in addition to [their other conquests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_conquests "Mongol conquests") as far west as Europe. [The empire split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Division_of_the_Mongol_Empire "Division of the Mongol Empire") into separate khanates. In 1271, the Mongol leader [Kublai Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kublai_Khan "Kublai Khan") established the [Yuan dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuan_dynasty "Yuan dynasty") and [subjugated the Song](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_conquest_of_the_Song_dynasty "Mongol conquest of the Song dynasty") by 1279.[^80] [^81] The Yuan maintained a Mongol elite culture, but Chinese culture remained largely unchanged.[^82]

In the 1340s, central China was devastated by disease, famine, and mass floods along the Yellow River. [Various rebellions erupted](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Turban_Rebellions "Red Turban Rebellions"), leading to a peasant leader declaring himself the [Hongwu Emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hongwu_Emperor "Hongwu Emperor") of the [Ming dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty") in 1368.[^83] [^84] The Ming built a new capital at [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing"), with the [Forbidden City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbidden_City "Forbidden City") as its imperial palace, although the lower Yangtze remained the wealthiest region. Trade grew, and the [European colonization of the Americas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_colonization_of_the_Americas "European colonization of the Americas") brought new crops and a massive influx of silver to China.[^85] The population doubled, and a growing publishing industry began producing works in [vernacular Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Written_vernacular_Chinese "Written vernacular Chinese"). including the [Four Classic Novels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classic_Chinese_Novels "Classic Chinese Novels").[^86]

Tax evasion became common among the rich during the 1500s, resulting in greater rent and tax burdens on the poor. The bankrupt and factionalized government was unable to contain peasant rebellions. The [Manchu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchu_people "Manchu people") to the north declared the [Qing dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty") in 1636 and [conquered the Ming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transition_from_Ming_to_Qing "Transition from Ming to Qing"),[^87] killing their last claimant emperor in 1662 and conquering a [Ming rump state on Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Tungning "Kingdom of Tungning") in 1683.[^88] From the late 1600s to the end of the 1700s, the [High Qing era](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_Qing_era "High Qing era") saw economic growth and territorial expansion westward, including the occupation of [Tibet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibet "Tibet") and much of Central Asia.[^89] [^90] [^91]

![An oil painting of European and Chinese ships docked at a trading port](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/View_of_Canton_factories.jpg/250px-View_of_Canton_factories.jpg)

1805 painting of the European factories in Guangzhou (Canton).

European powers fought various wars against the Qing dynasty during the 19th century. These began with the United Kingdom's [First Opium War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Opium_War "First Opium War") in 1839–1842,[^92] [^93] which resulted in the first of the " [unequal treaties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unequal_treaties "Unequal treaties") " imposed on the dynasty, which opened [treaty ports](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_ports "Treaty ports"), allowed Christian missionary activity, and loosened trade restrictions.[^94] China faced an economic crisis and internal unrest, and uprisings such as the 1850–1864 [Taiping Rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiping_Rebellion "Taiping Rebellion") resulted in the deaths of millions.[^95] [^96] Reformist factions of the Qing state responded with the [Self-Strengthening Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-Strengthening_Movement "Self-Strengthening Movement"), seeking to adopt western weapons and technologies, but this had little impact for much of the empire.[^97]

The [Qing's defeat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Sino-Japanese_War "First Sino-Japanese War") against the [Empire of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Japan "Empire of Japan") in 1895 resulted in the loss of [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") and the growth of both reformist and revolutionary political movements. An [imperial push for reform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hundred_Days%27_Reform "Hundred Days' Reform") in 1898 was ended after an internal coup, while the anti-foreign [Boxer Rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion "Boxer Rebellion") was defeated by a coalition of foreign powers in 1901. The Qing state again [advanced reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Qing_reforms "Late Qing reforms"), but growing revolutionary and anti-Manchu sentiment culminated in the [1911 Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1911_Revolution "1911 Revolution"). A coalition of revolutionaries overthrew the Qing and declared the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") in 1912.[^98] [^99]

### Republic of China

![A black and white postcard showing the Shanghai Bund, with streetcars and European-style buildings](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Shanghai_Bund_seen_from_the_French_Concession.jpg/250px-Shanghai_Bund_seen_from_the_French_Concession.jpg)

The Shanghai Bund, c. 1934

President [Yuan Shikai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuan_Shikai "Yuan Shikai") crushed his main opposition, the [Kuomintang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuomintang "Kuomintang"), and ruled China as a dictator until his death in 1916, following an abortive attempt to restore the monarchy. During the succeeding [Warlord Era](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warlord_Era "Warlord Era"), feuding regional warlords and governors took power across China, while Tibet and [Mongolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolia "Mongolia") declared independence. The nominal republican government had little control outside Beijing. During this period, [New Culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Culture_Movement "New Culture Movement") intellectuals and students rebelled against traditional society. A crackdown on a student protest in 1919 led to the [May Fourth Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/May_Fourth_Movement "May Fourth Movement") and further agitation by intellectuals for a cultural and political upheaval, with many embracing Western political ideas such as [communism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communism "Communism").[^100] [^101]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/1945_Mao_and_Chiang.jpg/250px-1945_Mao_and_Chiang.jpg)

Mao Zedong and Chiang Kai-shek toasting together in 1945 following the end of World War II

In the mid-1920s, the Kuomintang allied with the nascent [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") (CCP) and launched the [Northern Expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Expedition "Northern Expedition") to reunify China. In 1927, the Kuomintang violent purged the CCP and gained the allegiance of the northern warlords, establishing a new government at [Nanjing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanjing "Nanjing").[^102] The CCP was driven into the countryside and repressed, before [regrouping in the northwest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yan%27an_Soviet "Yan'an Soviet").[^103] [^104]

Japan [occupied Manchuria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_Manchuria "Japanese invasion of Manchuria") in 1931, before launching an [invasion of the rest of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War "Second Sino-Japanese War") in 1937. A renewed coalition between the CCP and Kuomintang fought Japan in what became a theater of [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), as Japanese forces committed numerous [war atrocities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_war_crimes "Japanese war crimes") against the civilian population and occupied most of China's major cities.[^105] [^106] [^107] After the [surrender of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surrender_of_Japan "Surrender of Japan") in 1945, China became a founding member of the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations") and regained control over Manchuria and Taiwan. The [civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War "Chinese Civil War") between the CCP and the Kuomintang resumed the following year.[^108]

### People's Republic of China

After a string of military victories, CCP chairman [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong "Mao Zedong") formally [proclaimed the People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Proclamation of the People's Republic of China") (PRC) in 1949. The Kuomintang government [retreated to Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retreat_of_the_government_of_the_Republic_of_China_to_Taiwan "Retreat of the government of the Republic of China to Taiwan") and continued to claim legitimacy.[^109] The following year, the PRC began to [occupy and annex Tibet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annexation_of_Tibet_by_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Annexation of Tibet by the People's Republic of China").[^110] The PRC, a " [people's democratic dictatorship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_democratic_dictatorship "People's democratic dictatorship") " under CCP control, enacted many reforms: it restructured the economy under state control, promoted literacy and women's equality, expanded heavy industry, and promoted land collectivization through the [Land Reform Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_Reform_Movement "Land Reform Movement"), which saw state-tolerated violence against landlords by the peasantry and the death of upwards of a million people.[^111] [^112] [^113]

![A group of pedestrians and cyclists by a large poster with Chinese political writing, alongside a portrait of Mao Zedong and a Chinese flag](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Big-_character_Posters_in_Peking_1976_%2825673509518%29.jpg/250px-Big-_character_Posters_in_Peking_1976_%2825673509518%29.jpg)

A big-character poster and portrait of Mao Zedong in Beijing during the Cultural Revolution, 1976

In 1958, Mao launched the [Great Leap Forward](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Leap_Forward "Great Leap Forward"), a mass industrialization project which resulted in the [Great Chinese Famine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Chinese_Famine "Great Chinese Famine") and around 30 million deaths. As the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War") deepened, the PRC [grew politically isolated from its former ally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Soviet_split "Sino-Soviet split"), the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union"), as well as the [Western Bloc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Bloc "Western Bloc"). China [detonated its first atomic bomb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Project_596 "Project 596") in 1964.[^114] [^115] Attempting to reassert control of the CCP after the Great Leap, Mao and his allies launched the [Cultural Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultural_Revolution "Cultural Revolution") in 1966, sparking a decade of political violence, crackdowns on perceived counterrevolutionaries, and social upheaval that lasted until [Mao's death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_and_state_funeral_of_Mao_Zedong "Death and state funeral of Mao Zedong") in 1976.[^116] [^117]

[Deng Xiaoping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping "Deng Xiaoping") served as [paramount leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paramount_leader "Paramount leader") from 1978 to 1989. The PRC's [reform and opening up](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reform_and_opening_up "Reform and opening up") during the 1980s and 1990s saw economic liberalization towards a " [socialist market economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist_market_economy "Socialist market economy")," rapid economic growth, friendlier relations with the West, and crackdowns on political dissidence in events such as the [1989 Tiananmen Square massacre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1989_Tiananmen_Square_protests_and_massacre "1989 Tiananmen Square protests and massacre").[^118] China shifted away from an agricultural economy and rapidly urbanized, although this led to stark [economic inequality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_inequality_in_China "Economic inequality in China") between urban and rural areas, as well as environmental deterioration.[^119] Since the 1990s, the [free market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_market "Free market") has become the PRC's main economic driver.[^120] Paramount leader [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping"), in power since 2012, has overseen the centralization of political power and the expansion of Chinese economic influence through the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative").[^121]

## Geography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/83/Koppen-Geiger_Map_CHN_present.svg/500px-Koppen-Geiger_Map_CHN_present.svg.png)

Köppen-Geiger climate classification map for mainland China 122

China's geography is highly varied, featuring a [dry and mountainous west](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_China "Western China"), alongside both mountains and wide river valleys in the east. Chinese civilization was traditionally centered in the lowlands around its two largest rivers: the [Yangtze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yangtze "Yangtze") and the [Yellow River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_River "Yellow River"), both originating on the [Tibetan Plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibetan_Plateau "Tibetan Plateau"). This temperate region is bordered by forest and [steppe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steppe "Steppe") to the north, alongside the mountainous and subtropical [south](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_and_southern_China "Northern and southern China").[^123]

Large mountain ranges in the west, including the [Himalayas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Himalayas "Himalayas") and [Tian Shan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tian_Shan "Tian Shan"), separate China from [South](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia "South Asia") and [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia"). The west features both the [Turpan Depression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turpan_Depression "Turpan Depression"), among the deepest points on land, and the high Tibetan Plateau.[^124] The world's highest point, [Mount Everest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Everest "Mount Everest") (8,848 metres (29,029 ft)), lies on the border with [Nepal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nepal "Nepal").[^125]

Its climate is mainly dominated by [dry seasons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dry_season "Dry season") and wet [monsoons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsoons "Monsoons"), which lead to pronounced temperature differences between winter and summer. In the winter, northern winds coming from high-latitude areas are cold and dry; in summer, southern winds from coastal areas at lower latitudes are warm and moist.[^126] Its northwestern deserts receive as little as 50 mm (2.0 in) of rainfall annually, while much of southern China exceeds 1,000 mm (39 in).[^127] Much of eastern China is well-suited for agriculture, with two or three crops able to be harvested per year. The south is dominated by [rice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rice "Rice") farming, while the north grows crops such as [wheat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wheat "Wheat") and [maize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maize "Maize").[^128] In 2021, 12% of global permanent meadows and pastures belonged to China, as well as 8% of global cropland.[^129]

### Environment

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8d/Giant_Panda_Eating.jpg/250px-Giant_Panda_Eating.jpg)

A giant panda, one of China's most famous symbols, at the Chengdu Panda Base in Sichuan

China is one of 17 [megadiverse countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megadiverse_countries "Megadiverse countries"),[^130] lying in two of the world's major [biogeographic realms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biogeographic_realm "Biogeographic realm"): the [Palearctic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palearctic_realm "Palearctic realm") and the [Indomalayan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indomalayan_realm "Indomalayan realm"). By one measure, China has over 34,687 species of animals and vascular plants, making it the third-most biodiverse country in the world, after [Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil "Brazil") and [Colombia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colombia "Colombia").[^131] The far north contains [taiga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiga "Taiga"), which to the south transitions into [deciduous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deciduous "Deciduous") forest. South of the Yangtze, mixed conifer and deciduous forest is common, with tropical and subtropical forests farther south.[^132]

At least 840 of China's [animal species are threatened, vulnerable or in danger of local extinction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_endangered_and_protected_species_of_China "List of endangered and protected species of China"), due mainly to human activity such as habitat destruction, pollution and poaching.[^133] Endangered wildlife is legally protected. As of 2019, China has over 2,750 [nature reserves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protected_areas_of_China "Protected areas of China"), covering 15% of China's total land area.[^134] According to government surveys, [China's forest coverage](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Forestry_in_China&action=edit&redlink=1 "Forestry in China (page does not exist)") grew from 10% of the overall territory in 1949 to 25% in 2024.[^135]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/ThreeGorgesDam-China2009.jpg/250px-ThreeGorgesDam-China2009.jpg)

The Three Gorges Dam is the largest hydroelectric dam in the world.

In the early 21st century, China has suffered from [environmental deterioration and pollution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_issues_in_China "Environmental issues in China") due to rapid industrialization.[^136] [^137] Environmental regulations are fairly stringent, although poorly enforced and often disregarded.[^138] The [expansion of China's deserts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desertification "Desertification"), particularly the [Gobi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gobi_Desert "Gobi Desert"), has posed a persistent threat.[^139] Water quality, [erosion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erosion "Erosion"), and [pollution control](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pollution_in_China "Pollution in China") have become important issues in China's foreign relations. Melting [glaciers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glaciers "Glaciers") in the Himalayas could potentially lead to [water shortages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_shortage "Water shortage") for hundreds of millions of people.[^140]

China has the second-highest death toll from air pollution, with approximately 1 million deaths as of 2016.[^141] [^142] Although China ranks as the highest [CO <sub>2</sub> emitting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_carbon_dioxide_emissions "List of countries by carbon dioxide emissions") country, it only emits 8 tons of [CO <sub>2</sub> per capita](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_carbon_dioxide_emissions_per_capita "List of countries by carbon dioxide emissions per capita"), significantly lower than many other developed countries.[^143] [Greenhouse gas emissions by China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas_emissions_by_China "Greenhouse gas emissions by China") are the [world's largest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_greenhouse_gas_emissions "List of countries by greenhouse gas emissions").[^143] The PRC has prioritized clamping down on pollution,[^144] announcing its aims to reach its peak emissions levels before 2030, and achieve [carbon neutrality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_neutrality "Carbon neutrality") by 2060.[^145]

China is the world's leading investor in [renewable energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renewable_energy "Renewable energy") and [its commercialization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renewable_energy_commercialization "Renewable energy commercialization"), with [$](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/US$ "US$") 546 billion invested in 2022.[^146] Long heavily relying on non-renewable energy sources such as coal, China's adaptation of [renewable energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renewable_energy_in_China "Renewable energy in China") has increased significantly in recent years.[^147] In 2025, 54.4% of China's electricity came from [coal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coal_in_China "Coal in China"), while 42% came from clean energy sources.[^148] Despite its emphasis on renewables, China remains deeply connected to global oil markets.[^149] [^150]

### Political geography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/China_administrative.png/250px-China_administrative.png)

Map depicting territorial disputes between the PRC and neighboring states.

China is the [second-largest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_area "List of countries and dependencies by area") country by land area after [Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia "Russia"), and the third- or fourth-largest by total area.[^13] Total area figures range from 9,572,900 km <sup>2</sup> (3,696,100 sq mi) [^30] to 9,596,961 km <sup>2</sup> (3,705,407 sq mi).[^22] [^21] China [borders 14 nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borders_of_China "Borders of China"), with a combined land border length of 22,117 km (13,743 mi). Its [coastline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coastline_of_China "Coastline of China") spans approximately 14,500 km (9,000 mi).[^21]

The PRC has engaged in 23 border disputes since 1949, of which six are unsettled.[^151] It actively disputes several portions of its Himalayan border with India, including the [Aksai Chin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aksai_Chin "Aksai Chin") in [Kashmir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kashmir "Kashmir") and most of the Indian state of [Arunachal Pradesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arunachal_Pradesh "Arunachal Pradesh"). It maintains maritime disputes with with Japan [over the Senkaku/Diaoyu Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senkaku_Islands_dispute "Senkaku Islands dispute") and with various countries over the [South China Sea Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea_Islands "South China Sea Islands"), including the [Spratly Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spratly_Islands "Spratly Islands") and [Paracel Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paracel_Islands "Paracel Islands").[^152]
