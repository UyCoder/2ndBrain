**Egypt**,[^5] officially the **Arab Republic of Egypt**, is a country spanning the northeast corner of Africa and southwest corner of Asia via the [Sinai Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinai_Peninsula "Sinai Peninsula"). It is bordered by the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea") to [the north](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_coast_of_Egypt "Northern coast of Egypt"), [Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine "Palestine") and [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel "Israel") to [the northeast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt%E2%80%93Israel_barrier "Egypt–Israel barrier"), the [Red Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea "Red Sea") to the east, [Sudan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudan "Sudan") and the [Sahara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahara "Sahara") to [the south](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt%E2%80%93Sudan_border "Egypt–Sudan border"), and [Libya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya "Libya") to [the west](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt%E2%80%93Libya_border "Egypt–Libya border"). The [Gulf of Aqaba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Aqaba "Gulf of Aqaba") in the northeast separates Egypt from [Jordan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan "Jordan") and [Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia"). [Cairo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairo "Cairo") is the capital, [largest city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_and_towns_in_Egypt "List of cities and towns in Egypt"), and leading cultural centre, while [Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexandria "Alexandria") is the second-largest city and an important hub of industry and tourism.[^28] With over 107 million inhabitants, Egypt is [the most populous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Arab_League_countries_by_population "List of Arab League countries by population") country in the [Arab world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_world "Arab world"), [third-most populous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_African_countries_by_population "List of African countries by population") country in Africa, and [15th-most populated in the world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_population "List of countries and dependencies by population").

Egypt has one of the longest histories of any country, tracing its heritage along the [Nile Delta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile_Delta "Nile Delta") back to the 6th–4th millennia BCE. Considered a [cradle of civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cradle_of_civilisation "Cradle of civilisation"), [Ancient Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egypt "Ancient Egypt") saw some of the earliest developments of writing, agriculture, urbanisation, organised religion and central government.[^29] Egypt was an early and important [centre of Christianity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity_in_Egypt "Christianity in Egypt"), later [adopting Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamization_of_Egypt "Islamization of Egypt") from the seventh century onwards. [Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexandria "Alexandria"), Egypt's former capital and currently second largest city, was a hub of global knowledge through its [Library](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Library_of_Alexandria "Library of Alexandria"). Cairo became the capital of the [Fatimid Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatimid_Caliphate "Fatimid Caliphate") in the tenth century and of the subsequent [Mamluk Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mamluk_Sultanate "Mamluk Sultanate") in the 13th century. Egypt then became part of the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") in 1517, until its local ruler [Muhammad Ali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ali_of_Egypt "Muhammad Ali of Egypt") established modern Egypt as an autonomous [Khedivate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khedivate_of_Egypt "Khedivate of Egypt") in 1867. The country was then [occupied](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Egypt_under_the_British "History of Egypt under the British") by the [British Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire "British Empire") along with [Sudan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Sudan "British Sudan") and gained independence in 1922 as [a monarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ali_dynasty "Muhammad Ali dynasty").

Following the [1952 revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1952_Egyptian_revolution "1952 Egyptian revolution"), Egypt declared itself a [republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Egypt_\(1953%E2%80%9358\) "Republic of Egypt (1953–58)"). Between 1958 and 1961, Egypt [merged with Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Syrian_Republic "Second Syrian Republic") to form the [United Arab Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Republic "United Arab Republic"). Egypt fought [several armed conflicts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab%E2%80%93Israeli_conflict "Arab–Israeli conflict") with Israel in [1948](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1948_Arab%E2%80%93Israeli_War "1948 Arab–Israeli War"), [1956](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suez_Crisis "Suez Crisis"), [1967](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six-Day_War "Six-Day War") and [1973](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yom_Kippur_War "Yom Kippur War"), and [occupied](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupation_of_the_Gaza_Strip_by_the_United_Arab_Republic "Occupation of the Gaza Strip by the United Arab Republic") the [Gaza Strip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_Strip "Gaza Strip") intermittently until 1967. In 1978, Egypt signed the [Camp David Accords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camp_David_Accords "Camp David Accords"), which [recognised Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt%E2%80%93Israel_peace_treaty "Egypt–Israel peace treaty") in exchange for the latter's withdrawal from the occupied Sinai. After the [Arab Spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Spring "Arab Spring"), which led to the [2011 Egyptian revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011_Egyptian_revolution "2011 Egyptian revolution") and overthrow of [Hosni Mubarak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hosni_Mubarak "Hosni Mubarak"), the country faced a [protracted period of political unrest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_Crisis_\(2011%E2%80%932014\) "Egyptian Crisis (2011–2014)"); its first [democratic election in 2012](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2012_Egyptian_presidential_election "2012 Egyptian presidential election") resulted in the short-lived, [Muslim Brotherhood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_Brotherhood "Muslim Brotherhood") -aligned government of [Mohamed Morsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohamed_Morsi "Mohamed Morsi"), which was [overthrown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_Egyptian_coup_d%27etat "2013 Egyptian coup d'etat") by the military after [mass protests in 2013](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/June_2013_Egyptian_protests "June 2013 Egyptian protests"). The current government is a [semi-presidential republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-presidential_republic "Semi-presidential republic") led by [Abdel Fattah el-Sisi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdel_Fattah_el-Sisi "Abdel Fattah el-Sisi"), who was [elected in 2014](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Egyptian_presidential_election "2014 Egyptian presidential election") but is widely regarded as [authoritarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authoritarian "Authoritarian").[^30]

Egypt is a [developing country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country "Developing country") with the [second-largest economy in Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_African_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\) "List of African countries by GDP (nominal)"). It is considered to be a [regional power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_power "Regional power") in the Middle East, North Africa and the [Muslim world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_world "Muslim world"), and a [middle power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_power "Middle power") worldwide. [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam") is the [official religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_religion "State religion") and [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic "Arabic") its official language. Egypt is a founding member of the United Nations, the [Non-Aligned Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement "Non-Aligned Movement"), the [Arab League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League "Arab League"), the [African Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union "African Union"), [Organisation of Islamic Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation "Organisation of Islamic Cooperation"), [World Youth Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Youth_Forum "World Youth Forum"), and a member of [BRICS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BRICS "BRICS").[^7] [^31] [^32]

## Names

[Ancient Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egypt "Ancient Egypt") had several names; one of them was ([𓆎](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Special:Search/%F0%93%86%8E "wikt:Special:Search/𓆎") [𓅓](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Special:Search/%F0%93%85%93 "wikt:Special:Search/𓅓") [𓏏](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Special:Search/%F0%93%8F%8F "wikt:Special:Search/𓏏") 𓊖) *km.t*, which is formed from [𓆎 𓅓](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/km#Egyptian "wiktionary:km"), meaning black.[^33] This likely refers to the [fertile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_fertility "Soil fertility") black soils of the [Nile floodplains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile_flood "Nile flood"), distinct from the *deshret* ⟨ *dšṛt* ⟩, or "red land" of the [desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desert "Desert").[^34] [^35] This name is commonly vocalised as *Kemet* \[kɛmɛt\], but was pronounced differently in Ancient Egyptian.[^36] Scholars reconstruct its [Old Egyptian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Egyptian "Old Egyptian") pronunciation as \[ˈkuːmat\],[^37] its [Middle Egyptian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Egyptian "Middle Egyptian") pronunciation as \[ˈkuːmaʔ\], and its [Late Egyptian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Egyptian "Late Egyptian") pronunication as \[ˈkeːmə\].[^38] The name is realised as **K(h)ēmə** ([Bohairic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coptic_language#Dialects "Coptic language"): ⲭⲏⲙⲓ, [Sahidic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coptic_language#Dialects "Coptic language"): ⲕⲏⲙⲉ) in [Egyptian Coptic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coptic_language "Coptic language"), and appeared in [Early Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_language "Greek language") as Χημία (**Khēmía**).[^39] [^40] Another name was ⟨ *tꜣ-mry* ⟩ "land of the riverbank".[^41]

The names of [Upper and Lower Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_and_Lower_Egypt "Upper and Lower Egypt") were *Ta-Sheme'aw* (⟨ *tꜣ-šmꜥw* ⟩) " [sedgeland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sedge "Sedge") " and *Ta-Mehew* (⟨ *tꜣ mḥw* ⟩) "northland", respectively. They were also collectively called "tꜣwy" (tāwy), meaning [The Two Lands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_and_Lower_Egypt#Structure "Upper and Lower Egypt"), referring to both Lower Egypt and Upper Egypt after unification.[^42]

The English name "Egypt" is derived from the [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "Ancient Greek") " *Aígyptos* " (" Αἴγυπτος "), via [Middle French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_French "Middle French") "Egypte" and [Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "Latin") " *Aegyptus* ". It is reflected in early Greek [Linear B](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linear_B "Linear B") tablets as "a-ku-pi-ti-yo".[^43] The [ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greece "Ancient Greece") [geographer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographer "Geographer") [Strabo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strabo "Strabo") provided a [folk etymology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Folk_etymology "Folk etymology") stating that " *Αἴγυπτος* " (Aigýptios) had originally evolved as a [compound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compound_\(linguistics\) "Compound (linguistics)") from " *Aἰγαίου ὑπτίως* " (*Aegaeou huptiōs*), meaning " [Below the Aegean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_Sea "Aegean Sea") ".[^44] Another tradition claims that it was named after the legendary king [Aegyptus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegyptus "Aegyptus").[^45] The actual derivation is thought to be from *[ḥwt-kꜣ-ptḥ](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E1%B8%A5wt-k%EA%9C%A3-pt%E1%B8%A5 "wikt:ḥwt-kꜣ-ptḥ")* (*Hutkaptah*, [Late Egyptian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Egyptian "Late Egyptian") pronunciation [\[ħəjˌkojpəˈtaħ\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA "Help:IPA")), " [Temple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_temple "Egyptian temple") of the [*Ka*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_conception_of_the_soul#Ka_\(vital_essence\) "Ancient Egyptian conception of the soul") of [Ptah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptah "Ptah")," a term for the city of [Memphis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memphis,_Egypt "Memphis, Egypt").[^46] [^47]

Greco-Roman writers report that Egypt was sometimes called Aeria (Ἀερία).[^48] [^45] [^49] [^50] [Stephanus of Byzantium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephanus_of_Byzantium "Stephanus of Byzantium") derives the name from aer (ἀήρ, "air"), explaining that Egypt was a "misty land". From this name, the ethnic form Aerioi (Ἀέριοι) is also recorded for its inhabitants.[^49] [Thomas George Tucker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thomas_George_Tucker "Thomas George Tucker") suggested that Aeria could derive not only from "misty" or "hazy air", but also be understood in the sense of "far-off" or "dimly seen".[^50]

" *Miṣr* " (Arabic pronunciation: [\[misˤɾ\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Arabic "Help:IPA/Arabic"); " مِصر ") is the [Classical Quranic Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Arabic "Classical Arabic") and modern official name of Egypt, while " *Maṣr* " (Egyptian Arabic pronunciation: [\[mɑsˤɾ\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Egyptian_Arabic "Help:IPA/Egyptian Arabic"); مَصر) is the local pronunciation in [Egyptian Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_Arabic "Egyptian Arabic").[^51] The current name of Egypt, Misr/Misir/Misru, stems from the [Ancient](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Semitic-speaking_peoples "Ancient Semitic-speaking peoples") [Semitic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semitic_languages "Semitic languages") name for it. The term originally connoted " [Civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civilization "Civilization") " or " [Metropolis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolis "Metropolis") ".[^52] [Classical Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Arabic "Classical Arabic") **Miṣr** (Egyptian Arabic **Maṣr**) is directly cognate with the [Biblical Hebrew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biblical_Hebrew_language "Biblical Hebrew language") *Miṣráyīm* (מִצְרַיִם / מִצְרָיִם), meaning "the two straits", a reference to the predynastic separation of [Upper and Lower Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_and_Lower_Egypt "Upper and Lower Egypt"). Also mentioned in several Semitic languages as *Mesru*, *Misir* and *Masar*.[^52] The oldest attestation of this name for Egypt is the [Akkadian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akkadian_language "Akkadian language") "mi-iṣ-ru" ("miṣru") [^53] [^54] related to *miṣru/miṣirru/miṣaru*, meaning "border" or "frontier".[^55] The [Neo-Assyrian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neo-Assyrian_Empire "Neo-Assyrian Empire") used the derived term , *Mu-ṣur*.[^56] In Egyptian colloquial speech since the 21st century, the English name "Egypt" has been used to refer to wealthy Egyptians, while the Arabic name "Misr/Masr" has been used to refer to poor Egyptians.[^57]

## History

### Prehistoric Egypt

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Predynastic_collage.png/250px-Predynastic_collage.png)

Clockwise: a Badarian mortuary figurine, a Naqada jar, a statuette of the goddess Bat, the Four dogs palette Gebel el-Arak Knife, and a diorite vase.

Evidence of [rock carvings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_carvings "Rock carvings") along the [Nile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nile "Nile") and in surrounding oases indicates early habitation. In the [10th millennium BCE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/10th_millennium_BCE "10th millennium BCE"), a culture of [hunter-gatherers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hunter-gatherer "Hunter-gatherer") and [fishers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fishing "Fishing") was replaced by a [grain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cereal "Cereal") -grinding culture. Climate changes or [overgrazing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overgrazing "Overgrazing") around 8000 [BCE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Before_common_era "Before common era") began to desiccate the pastoral lands of Egypt, forming the [Sahara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sahara "Sahara"). Early [tribal peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tribal_people "Tribal people") migrated to the Nile River where they developed a settled agricultural [economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_system "Economic system") and more centralised society.[^58]

By about 6000 BCE, a [Neolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic "Neolithic") culture took root in the Nile Valley.[^59] During the Neolithic era, several predynastic cultures developed independently in [Upper and Lower Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_and_Lower_Egypt "Upper and Lower Egypt"). The [Badarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Badarian "Badarian") culture and the successor [Naqada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naqada "Naqada") series are generally regarded as precursors to [dynastic Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egypt "Ancient Egypt"). The earliest known Lower Egyptian site, Merimda, predates the Badarian by about seven hundred years. Contemporaneous Lower Egyptian communities coexisted with their southern counterparts for more than two thousand years, remaining culturally distinct, but maintaining frequent contact through trade. The earliest known evidence of [Egyptian hieroglyphic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_hieroglyphs "Egyptian hieroglyphs") inscriptions appeared during the predynastic period on Naqada III pottery vessels, dated to about 3200 BCE.[^60]

### Ancient Egypt (3150 BCE–305 BCE)

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/af/All_Gizah_Pyramids.jpg/250px-All_Gizah_Pyramids.jpg)

The Giza Necropolis is the oldest of the ancient Wonders and the only one still in existence.

Around c. 3150 BCE, King [Menes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Menes "Menes") unified Egypt, establishing a succession of [dynasties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Egyptian_dynasties "List of Egyptian dynasties") that ruled for three millennia. [Egyptian civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Egypt "Culture of Egypt") thrived with distinctive achievements in [religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_religion "Ancient Egyptian religion"), [art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Art_of_Ancient_Egypt "Art of Ancient Egypt"), and [writing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_language "Egyptian language"). The [Old Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Kingdom "Old Kingdom") (c. 2700–2200 BCE) saw the construction of the [pyramids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_pyramids "Egyptian pyramids"), including those at [Giza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giza_Necropolis "Giza Necropolis"). A brief [interregnum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Intermediate_Period_of_Egypt "First Intermediate Period of Egypt") followed, succeeded by the [Middle Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Kingdom_of_Egypt "Middle Kingdom of Egypt") (c. 2040 BCE), a phase of renewed stability and prosperity under rulers such as [Amenemhat III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amenemhat_III "Amenemhat III").[^61]

After the [Second Intermediate Period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Intermediate_Period_of_Egypt "Second Intermediate Period of Egypt") and the [Hyksos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyksos "Hyksos") occupation, Egypt was reunified by [Ahmose I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmose_I "Ahmose I"), founder of the [Eighteenth Dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eighteenth_dynasty_of_Egypt "Eighteenth dynasty of Egypt") and the [New Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Kingdom_of_Egypt "New Kingdom of Egypt") (c. 1550–1070 BCE). This era marked Egypt's height as a major power in the region, extending influence into [Nubia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nubia "Nubia") and the [Levant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levant "Levant"). It produced many of Egypt's most renowned [Pharaohs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharaoh "Pharaoh"), [Hatshepsut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hatshepsut "Hatshepsut"), [Thutmose III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thutmose_III "Thutmose III"), [Akhenaten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akhenaten "Akhenaten"), [Tutankhamun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tutankhamun "Tutankhamun"), and [Ramesses II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ramesses_II "Ramesses II"), and witnessed the rise of [Atenism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atenism "Atenism"), one of the earliest forms of [monotheism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monotheism "Monotheism"). Despite later invasions by [Libyans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Libya "Ancient Libya"), [Nubians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Kush "Kingdom of Kush"), and [Assyrians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assyria "Assyria"), native dynasties eventually reasserted control.[^62]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ee/BD_Weighing_of_the_Heart.jpg/250px-BD_Weighing_of_the_Heart.jpg)

The Weighing of the Heart from the Book of the Dead of Ani

In 525 BCE, [Cambyses II of Persia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambyses_II_of_Persia "Cambyses II of Persia") conquered Egypt, beginning the [Achaemenid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire") [satrapy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satrapy "Satrapy") period (the [Twenty-seventh Dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twenty-seventh_Dynasty_of_Egypt "Twenty-seventh Dynasty of Egypt")). Although several revolts occurred, Egypt remained under Persian control until briefly regaining independence before falling again in 343 BCE.[^63] The [Thirtieth Dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirtieth_dynasty_of_Egypt "Thirtieth dynasty of Egypt") was the last native royal house. Following renewed Persian domination, [Alexander the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_the_Great "Alexander the Great") conquered Egypt in 332 BCE, after which his general [Ptolemy I Soter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemy_I_Soter "Ptolemy I Soter") established the [Ptolemaic dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemaic_dynasty "Ptolemaic dynasty").[^64]

### Ptolemaic and Roman Egypt (305 BCE–641 CE)

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/49/Denderah3_Cleopatra_Cesarion.jpg/250px-Denderah3_Cleopatra_Cesarion.jpg)

The Ptolemaic Queen Cleopatra VII and her son by Julius Caesar, Caesarion, at the Temple of Dendera

The [Ptolemaic Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ptolemaic_Kingdom "Ptolemaic Kingdom") was a [Hellenistic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenistic_civilization "Hellenistic civilization") state from southern Syria to [Cyrene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyrene,_Libya "Cyrene, Libya") and south to Nubia, with [Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexandria "Alexandria") as its capital and a centre of [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greeks "Greeks") culture and trade. The Ptolemies adopted pharaonic traditions to legitimise their rule, appearing on monuments in Egyptian style and participating in local religious life.[^65] [^66] The [Lighthouse of Alexandria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lighthouse_of_Alexandria "Lighthouse of Alexandria"), built c. 280 BCE, was one of the [Seven Wonders of the Ancient World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seven_Wonders_of_the_Ancient_World "Seven Wonders of the Ancient World"), later destroyed by earthquakes. The last ruler, [Cleopatra VII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cleopatra_VII "Cleopatra VII"), committed suicide after [Octavian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Octavian "Octavian") captured Alexandria, ending the dynasty and paving the way for Roman annexation. Native rebellions and dynastic disputes weakened the kingdom, facilitating its annexation by Rome.

Egypt was a wealthy imperial province of the [Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire"), supplying grain and hosting the major city of Alexandria.[^67] Governed with Roman administration and Hellenistic culture, its population primarily spoke [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koine_Greek "Koine Greek") in major cities and [Coptic Egyptian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coptic_language "Coptic language") in rural areas.[^68] [^69] Christianity reached Egypt in the 1st century, brought by [Saint Mark the Evangelist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint_Mark_the_Evangelist "Saint Mark the Evangelist").[^70] During [Diocletian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diocletian "Diocletian") 's reign (284–305 CE), the [New Testament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Testament "New Testament") had been translated into Egyptian and many Egyptian Christians were persecuted. By CE 451, a distinct [Coptic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coptic_Orthodox_Church_of_Alexandria "Coptic Orthodox Church of Alexandria") was firmly established.[^71]
