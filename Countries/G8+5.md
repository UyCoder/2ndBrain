The **Group of Eight + Five** (**G8+5**) was an international group that consisted of the leaders of the [heads of government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_government "Head of government") from the [G8](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_of_Eight "Group of Eight") nations ([Canada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada "Canada"), [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "France"), [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germany "Germany"), [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy "Italy"), [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan "Japan"), [Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia "Russia"), the [United Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom "United Kingdom"), and the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States")), plus the heads of government of the five leading [emerging economies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emerging_market "Emerging market") ([Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil "Brazil"), [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China"), [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India"), [Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico "Mexico"), and [South Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa "South Africa")). In March 2014, Russia was removed from the Group of 8 for [annexing Crimea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annexation_of_Crimea_by_the_Russian_Federation "Annexation of Crimea by the Russian Federation"),[^2] so the G8+5 in its original form is unlikely to reconvene with Russia present.

## February 2007 declaration

On February 16, 2007, The Global Legislators Organisation ([GLOBE International](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GLOBE "GLOBE")) held a meeting of the **G8+5 Climate Change Dialogue** at the GLOBE Washington Legislators Forum in [Washington, D.C.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington,_D.C. "Washington, D.C."), where a non-binding agreement was reached to cooperate on tackling [global warming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_warming "Global warming"). The group accepted that the existence of man-made [climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change "Climate change") was beyond doubt, and that there should be a global system of emission caps and [carbon emissions trading](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carbon_emissions_trading "Carbon emissions trading") applying to both [industrialized nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country "Developed country") and [developing countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country "Developing country"). The group hoped this policy to be in place by 2009, to supersede the [Kyoto Protocol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyoto_Protocol "Kyoto Protocol"), the first phase of which expires in 2012.[^3] [^4]

## Foundation

The G8+5 group was formed in 2005 when [Tony Blair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Blair "Tony Blair"), then [Prime Minister of the United Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_United_Kingdom "Prime Minister of the United Kingdom"), in his role as host of the [31st G8 summit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/31st_G8_summit "31st G8 summit") at [Gleneagles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gleneagles_Hotel "Gleneagles Hotel"), [Scotland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scotland "Scotland"), invited the leading emerging countries to join the talks. The hope was that this would form a stronger and more representative group that would inject fresh impetus into the trade talks at [Doha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doha "Doha"), and the need to achieve a deeper cooperation on climate change.

Following the meeting, the countries issued a joint statement looking to build a "new paradigm for international cooperation" in the future.

The G8+5 Climate Change Dialogue was launched on February 24, 2006, by the ([GLOBE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GLOBE "GLOBE")) [^5] in partnership with the *Com+* alliance of communicators for sustainable development.[^6]

## Institutionalization attempt

Following the [33rd G8 summit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/33rd_G8_summit "33rd G8 summit"), [Heiligendamm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heiligendamm "Heiligendamm") 2007, German chancellor [Angela Merkel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angela_Merkel "Angela Merkel") announced the establishment of the "Heiligendamm Process" through which the full institutionalization of the permanent dialogue between the G8 countries and the five greatest [emerging economies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emerging_economies "Emerging economies") will be implemented. This will include the establishment of a common G8 and G5 platform at the [OECD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OECD "OECD").[^7]

On August 28, 2007, French president [Nicolas Sarkozy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicolas_Sarkozy "Nicolas Sarkozy") in a foreign policy statement proposed that [Brazil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brazil "Brazil"), [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China"), [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India"), [Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico "Mexico") and [South Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa "South Africa") should become members of G8: "The G8 can't meet for two days and the G13 for just two hours.... That doesn't seem fitting, given the power of these five emerging countries."

Innovation, freedom of investment, development in Africa, and technology for reducing [CO <sub>2</sub> emissions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenhouse_gas "Greenhouse gas") were the four main issues this process visited in 2008 and 2009. A progress report was presented at the [2008 G8 Summit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/34th_G8_summit "34th G8 summit") in Japan; a final report on the results of the dialogue was put forward in [Italy in 2009](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/35th_G8_summit "35th G8 summit"). [German Chancellor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Chancellor "German Chancellor") [Angela Merkel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angela_Merkel "Angela Merkel") supported this process, stating, "\[W\]e cannot get by, or shape globalization in a humane way, without each other".

Nevertheless, as of 2008, a formal enlargement of the G8 was not a realistic political option, since the G8 member states have diverging positions on this issue. The United States and Japan have been against enlargement, the United Kingdom and France actively in favour, and Italy, Germany, Russia and Canada are reserved on the issue..
