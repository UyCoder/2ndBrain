**Malaysia** [^6] is a country in [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia"). A [federal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federation "Federation") [constitutional monarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy "Constitutional monarchy"), it consists of [13 states and three federal territories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/States_and_federal_territories_of_Malaysia "States and federal territories of Malaysia"), separated by the [South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea "South China Sea") into two regions: [Peninsular Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia "Peninsular Malaysia") on [Mainland Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_Southeast_Asia "Mainland Southeast Asia") and [East Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Malaysia "East Malaysia") on the island of [Borneo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borneo "Borneo"). Peninsular Malaysia shares land and [maritime borders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_boundary "Maritime boundary") with [Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand "Thailand"), as well as maritime borders with [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore "Singapore"), [Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam "Vietnam"), and [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia"); East Malaysia shares land borders with [Brunei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brunei "Brunei") and Indonesia, and maritime borders with the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines") and Vietnam. [Kuala Lumpur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur "Kuala Lumpur") is the country's national capital, [largest city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_and_towns_in_Malaysia_by_population "List of cities and towns in Malaysia by population"), and the seat of the [legislative branch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament_of_Malaysia "Parliament of Malaysia") of the [federal government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Malaysia "Government of Malaysia"), while [Putrajaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Putrajaya "Putrajaya") is the federal administrative capital, representing the seat of both the [executive branch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Malaysia#Executive "Government of Malaysia") and the [judicial branch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judiciary_of_Malaysia "Judiciary of Malaysia") of the federal government. With a population of over 34 million, it is the world's [43rd-most populous country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population "List of countries by population").

The country has [its origins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Malaysia "History of Malaysia") in the [Malay kingdoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_Kingdom_\(disambiguation\) "Malay Kingdom (disambiguation)"), which, from the 18th century on, became subject to the [British Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire "British Empire"), along with the British [Straits Settlements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Straits_Settlements "Straits Settlements") protectorate. During [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), [British Malaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Malaya "British Malaya"), along with other nearby British and American colonies, was occupied by the [Empire of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Japan "Empire of Japan"). Following three years of occupation, Malaya was briefly unified as the [Malayan Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malayan_Union "Malayan Union") in 1946 until 1948 when it was restructured as the [Federation of Malaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federation_of_Malaya "Federation of Malaya"). The country achieved independence on [31 August 1957](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Independence_Day_\(Malaysia\) "Independence Day (Malaysia)"). On [16 September 1963](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia_Day "Malaysia Day"), independent Malaya united with the then [British crown colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_colony "Crown colony") of [North Borneo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_Colony_of_North_Borneo "Crown Colony of North Borneo"), [Sarawak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_Colony_of_Sarawak "Crown Colony of Sarawak"), and [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colony_of_Singapore "Colony of Singapore") to become Malaysia. In August 1965, Singapore was [separated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore_in_Malaysia "Singapore in Malaysia") from the federation and became an [independent country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proclamation_of_Singapore "Proclamation of Singapore").

Malaysia is [tropical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical "Tropical") and is one of 17 [megadiverse countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megadiverse_countries "Megadiverse countries"); it is home to numerous [endemic species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildlife_of_Malaysia "Wildlife of Malaysia"). The country is [multiethnic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Malaysia "Ethnic groups in Malaysia") and [multicultural](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Malaysia "Culture of Malaysia"), which has a significant effect on its politics. About half the population is ethnically [Malay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Malays "Malaysian Malays"), with minorities of [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Chinese "Malaysian Chinese"), [Indians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Indian "Malaysian Indian"), and [indigenous peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orang_Asal "Orang Asal"). The official language is [Malaysian Malay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Malay "Malaysian Malay"), a standard form of the [Malay language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_language "Malay language"). [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_English "Malaysian English") remains an active second language. While recognising [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam") as the [official religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Official_religion "Official religion"), the constitution grants [freedom of religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_of_religion "Freedom of religion") to non-Muslims. The government is modelled on the [Westminster parliamentary system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westminster_parliamentary_system "Westminster parliamentary system"), and the legal system is based on [common law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_law "Common law"). The [head of state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_state "Head of state") is an [elected monarch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elected_monarch "Elected monarch"), chosen among the [nine state rulers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchies_of_Malaysia "Monarchies of Malaysia") every five years. The [head of government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_government "Head of government") is the [prime minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malaysia "Prime Minister of Malaysia").

[The country's economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Malaysia "Economy of Malaysia") was once heavily reliant on agriculture and primary commodities but is now driven by the services and manufacturing sectors. Malaysia ranks highly in [peacefulness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Peace_Index "Global Peace Index") and [passport strength](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henley_Passport_Index "Henley Passport Index") and has a [newly industrialised economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newly_industrialised_economy "Newly industrialised economy") that is relatively open and state-oriented. The country is a founding member of the [Organisation of Islamic Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation "Organisation of Islamic Cooperation") (OIC), the [East Asia Summit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Asia_Summit "East Asia Summit") (EAS), and the [Association of Southeast Asian Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_of_Southeast_Asian_Nations "Association of Southeast Asian Nations") (ASEAN), as well as a member of the [Non-Aligned Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement "Non-Aligned Movement") (NAM), the [Commonwealth of Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations "Commonwealth of Nations"), and the [Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Economic_Cooperation "Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation") (APEC).

## Etymology

The name [*Malaysia*](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Malaysia "wikt:Malaysia") is a combination of the word [*Malays*](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Malays "wikt:Malays") and the Latin-Greek suffix [*\-ia*](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-ia "wikt:-ia") / [\-ία](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/-%CE%AF%CE%B1 "wikt:-ία"),[^24] which can be translated as 'land of the Malays'.[^25] Similar-sounding variants have also appeared in accounts older than the 11th century, as [toponyms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toponym "Toponym") for areas in Sumatra or referring to a larger region around the Strait of Malacca.[^26] The Sanskrit text *[Vayu Purana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vayu_Purana "Vayu Purana")*, thought to have been in existence since the first millennium CE, mentioned a land named 'Malayadvipa', which was identified by certain scholars as the modern [Malay Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_Peninsula "Malay Peninsula").[^27] [^28] [^29] [^30] [^31] Other notable accounts are by Ptolemy's 2nd-century *[Geographia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographia "Geographia")*, which used the name *Malayu Kulon* for the west coast of [Golden Chersonese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Chersonese "Golden Chersonese"), and the 7th-century [Yijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yijing_\(monk\) "Yijing (monk)") 's account of *Malayu*.[^26]

At some point, the [Melayu Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melayu_Kingdom "Melayu Kingdom") took its name from the *Sungai Melayu*.[^32] [^33] *Melayu* then became associated with [Srivijaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Srivijaya "Srivijaya"),[^26] and remained associated with various parts of Sumatra, especially Palembang, where the founder of the [Malacca Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malacca_Sultanate "Malacca Sultanate") is thought to have come from.[^34] It is only thought to have developed into an [ethnonym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnonym "Ethnonym") as Malacca became a regional power in the 15th century. [Islamisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamisation "Islamisation") established an [ethnoreligious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnoreligious "Ethnoreligious") identity in Malacca, with the term *Melayu* beginning to appear as interchangeable with *Melakans*. It may have specifically referred to local Malay speakers who were loyal to the Malaccan Sultan. The initial Portuguese use of *Malayos* reflected this, referring only to the ruling people of Malacca. The prominence of traders from Malacca led *Melayu* to be associated with Muslim traders, and from there became associated with the wider cultural and linguistic group.[^26] Malacca and later [Johor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johor_Sultanate "Johor Sultanate") claimed they were the centre of Malay culture, a position supported by the British which led to the term *Malay* becoming more usually linked to the Malay peninsula rather than Sumatra.[^34]

Before the onset of European colonisation, the Malay Peninsula was known natively as *[Tanah Melayu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanah_Melayu "Tanah Melayu")* ('Malay Land').[^35] Under a racial classification created by German scholar [Johann Friedrich Blumenbach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johann_Friedrich_Blumenbach "Johann Friedrich Blumenbach"), the natives of [maritime Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_Southeast_Asia "Maritime Southeast Asia") were grouped into a single category, the [Malay race](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_race "Malay race").[^36] [^37] Following the expedition of French navigator [Jules Dumont d'Urville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jules_Dumont_d%27Urville "Jules Dumont d'Urville") to [Oceania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceania "Oceania") in 1826, he later proposed the terms of *Malaysia*, *[Micronesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Micronesia "Micronesia")*, and *[Melanesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melanesia "Melanesia")* to the *[Société de Géographie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soci%C3%A9t%C3%A9_de_G%C3%A9ographie "Société de Géographie")* in 1831, distinguishing these Pacific cultures and island groups from the existing term *[Polynesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynesia "Polynesia")*. Dumont d'Urville described Malaysia as "an area commonly known as the East Indies".[^38]

In 1850, the English ethnologist [George Samuel Windsor Earl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_Samuel_Windsor_Earl "George Samuel Windsor Earl"), writing in the *Journal of the Indian Archipelago and Eastern Asia*, proposed naming the islands of Southeast Asia as "Melayunesia" or "Indunesia", favouring the former.[^39] The name Malaysia gained some use to label what is now the Malay Archipelago.[^40] In modern terminology, *Malay* remains the name of an [ethnoreligious group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnoreligious_group "Ethnoreligious group") of [Austronesian people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austronesian_people "Austronesian people") predominantly inhabiting the Malay Peninsula and portions of the adjacent islands of [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia"), including the east coast of Sumatra, the coast of [Borneo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borneo "Borneo"), and smaller islands that lie between these areas.[^41]

The state that gained independence from the United Kingdom in 1957 took the name *the [Federation of Malaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federation_of_Malaya "Federation of Malaya")*, chosen in preference to other potential names such as *Malaysia* [^42] and *[Langkasuka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Langkasuka "Langkasuka")*,[^42] after the historic kingdom located at the upper section of the Malay Peninsula in the first millennium CE.[^43] [^44]

In 1963, the name *Malaysia* was adopted when the existing states of the Federation of Malaya, plus Singapore, North Borneo, and Sarawak, formed a new federation.[^45] [^7] One theory posits the name was chosen so that *si* represented the inclusion of Singapore, North Borneo, and Sarawak.[^45] Politicians in the Philippines contemplated renaming their state Malaysia before the modern country took the name.[^47]

## History

![Map showing the extent of the Malacca Sultanate, covering much of the Malay Peninsula and some of Sumatra](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/85/Malacca_Sultanate_en.svg/250px-Malacca_Sultanate_en.svg.png)

The Malacca Sultanate played a major role in spreading Islam throughout the Malay Archipelago.

Evidence of modern human habitation in Malaysia dates back 40,000 years.[^48] In the Malay Peninsula, the first inhabitants are thought to be [Negritos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Negritos "Negritos").[^49] [^50] Areas of Malaysia participated in the Maritime Jade Road between 2000 BC to 1000 AD.[^51] [^52] [^53] [^54] Traders and settlers from [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India") and [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") arrived as early as the first century AD, establishing trading ports and coastal towns in the second and third centuries. Their presence resulted in strong [Indian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India") and [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") influences on the local cultures, and the people of the Malay Peninsula adopted the religions of [Hinduism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism "Hinduism") and [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism"). [Sanskrit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanskrit "Sanskrit") inscriptions appear as early as the fourth or fifth century.[^55] The Kingdom of [Langkasuka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Langkasuka "Langkasuka") arose around the second century in the northern area of the Malay Peninsula, lasting until about the 15th century.[^43] Between the 7th and 13th centuries, much of the southern Malay Peninsula was part of the maritime [Srivijayan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Srivijaya "Srivijaya") empire. By the 13th and the 14th century, the [Majapahit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Majapahit "Majapahit") empire had successfully wrested control over most of the peninsula and the Malay Archipelago from Srivijaya.[^56] In the early 15th century, [Parameswara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parameswara_\(king\) "Parameswara (king)"), a runaway king of the former [Kingdom of Singapura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Singapura "Kingdom of Singapura") linked to the old Srivijayan court, founded the [Malacca Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malacca_Sultanate "Malacca Sultanate").[^57] The spread of Islam increased following Parameswara's conversion to that religion. Malacca was an important commercial centre during this time, attracting trade from around the region.[^58]

![Dutch fleet vs Portuguese armada](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2b/AMH-6472-KB_Battle_for_Malacca_between_the_VOC_fleet_and_the_Portuguese%2C_1606.jpg/250px-AMH-6472-KB_Battle_for_Malacca_between_the_VOC_fleet_and_the_Portuguese%2C_1606.jpg)

The Dutch fleet battling with the Portuguese armada as part of the Dutch–Portuguese War in 1606 to gain control of Malacca

In 1511, [Malacca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malacca "Malacca") was conquered by [Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Malacca "Portuguese Malacca"),[^58] after which it was taken by the [Dutch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_Malacca "Dutch Malacca") in 1641. In 1786, the [British Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Malaya "British Malaya") established a presence in Malaya, when the Sultan of Kedah leased [Penang Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penang_Island "Penang Island") to the British [East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_India_Company "East India Company"). The British obtained the town of Singapore in 1819,[^59] and in 1824 took control of Malacca following the [Anglo-Dutch Treaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Dutch_Treaty_of_1824 "Anglo-Dutch Treaty of 1824"). By 1826, the British directly controlled Penang, Malacca, Singapore, and the island of [Labuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_Colony_of_Labuan "Crown Colony of Labuan"), which they established as the crown colony of the [Straits Settlements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Straits_Settlements "Straits Settlements"). By the 20th century, the states of [Pahang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pahang "Pahang"), [Selangor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selangor "Selangor"), [Perak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perak "Perak"), and [Negeri Sembilan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Negeri_Sembilan "Negeri Sembilan"), known together as the [Federated Malay States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federated_Malay_States "Federated Malay States"), had British [residents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Resident_\(title\) "Resident (title)") appointed to advise the Malay rulers, to whom the rulers were bound to defer by treaty.[^60] The remaining five states on the peninsula, known as the [Unfederated Malay States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unfederated_Malay_States "Unfederated Malay States"), while not directly under British rule, also accepted British advisers around the turn of the 20th century. Development on the peninsula and Borneo were generally separate until the 19th century. Under British rule the [immigration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immigration "Immigration") of Chinese and Indians to serve as labourers was encouraged.[^61] The area that is now [Sabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabah "Sabah") came under British control as [North Borneo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Borneo "North Borneo") when both the [Sultan of Brunei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate_of_Brunei "Sultanate of Brunei") and the [Sultan of Sulu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate_of_Sulu "Sultanate of Sulu") transferred their respective territorial rights of ownership, between 1877 and 1878.[^62] In 1842, Sarawak was ceded by the Sultan of Brunei to [James Brooke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Brooke "James Brooke"), whose successors ruled as the [White Rajahs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_Rajahs "White Rajahs") over an independent [kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raj_of_Sarawak "Raj of Sarawak") until 1946, when it became a [crown colony](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_Colony_of_Sarawak "Crown Colony of Sarawak").[^63]

The [Klang War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Klang_War "Klang War") (1867–1874) began as a dispute between Raja Abdullah and Raja Mahadi over the Klang chieftaincy, with Mahadi rejecting Abdullah's appointment by Sultan [Abdul Samad of Selangor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdul_Samad_of_Selangor "Abdul Samad of Selangor"). The conflict drew in rival Chinese [secret societies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secret_societies "Secret societies") —the [Ghee Hin Kongsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghee_Hin_Kongsi "Ghee Hin Kongsi") largely backing Mahadi and the [Hai San Secret Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hai_San_Secret_Society "Hai San Secret Society") supporting Abdullah—as well as Bugis-descended and Malay chiefs who viewed Mahadi as more legitimate. Fighting spread after Mahadi captured the Klang Fort and Abdullah fled to Malacca, disrupting tin production, trade routes, and British commercial interests in the Klang Valley. At the same time, the neighbouring [Larut Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Larut_Wars "Larut Wars") (1861–1874) between the Hai San and Ghee Hin in Perak similarly destabilised tin-rich districts and alarmed British merchants. Appeals from Malay chiefs for mediation combined with British concerns over regional disorder paved the way for formal intervention, culminating in the [Pangkor Treaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pangkor_Treaty "Pangkor Treaty") of 1874 which established a system of British residents in the Malay states.[^64] As [Frank Swettenham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Swettenham "Frank Swettenham") later wrote, "Though the circumstances demanded intervention, we came in to the Malay States at the invitation of the Rulers to teach them a better form of government." [^65] [^66] [^67]

In the [Second World War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_World_War "Second World War"), the [Japanese Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_Japanese_Army "Imperial Japanese Army") [invaded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_Malaya "Japanese invasion of Malaya") and occupied [Malaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_occupation_of_Malaya "Japanese occupation of Malaya"), [North Borneo, Sarawak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_occupation_of_British_Borneo "Japanese occupation of British Borneo"), and [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_occupation_of_Singapore "Japanese occupation of Singapore") for over three years.[^68] During this time, ethnic tensions were raised and nationalism grew.[^69] Popular support for independence increased after Malaya was reconquered by Allied forces.[^70] Post-war British plans to unite the administration of Malaya under a single crown colony called the [Malayan Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malayan_Union "Malayan Union") met with strong opposition from the [Malays](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malays_\(ethnic_group\) "Malays (ethnic group)"), who opposed the weakening of the Malay rulers and the granting of citizenship to the [ethnic Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Chinese "Malaysian Chinese"). The Malayan Union, established in 1946, and consisting of all the [British possessions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_possessions "British possessions") in the Malay Peninsula except for [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colony_of_Singapore "Colony of Singapore"), was quickly dissolved and replaced on 1 February 1948 by the [Federation of Malaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federation_of_Malaya "Federation of Malaya"), which restored the autonomy of the rulers of the Malay states under British protection.[^71]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Police_in_Malayan_Emergency.jpg/250px-Police_in_Malayan_Emergency.jpg)

Malayan police officers questioning an ethnic Chinese civilian during the Malayan Emergency in 1949

During this time, the mostly ethnically Chinese rebels under the leadership of the [Malayan Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malayan_Communist_Party "Malayan Communist Party") launched guerrilla operations designed to force the British out of Malaya. The [Malayan Emergency (1948–1960)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malayan_Emergency "Malayan Emergency") involved a long anti-insurgency campaign by [Commonwealth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations "Commonwealth of Nations") troops in Malaya.[^72] On 31 August 1957, [Malaya became an independent member](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malayan_Declaration_of_Independence "Malayan Declaration of Independence") of the Commonwealth of Nations.[^73] Subsequently, a comprehensive plan was devised to unite Malaya with the crown colonies of North Borneo (known as [Sabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabah "Sabah") upon joining), [Sarawak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarawak "Sarawak"), and [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore "Singapore"). The envisioned federation was originally intended to take place on 31 August 1963, to coincide with the commemoration of Malayan independence. However, due to the necessity of conducting a survey on the level of support for the federation in Sabah and Sarawak by the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations"), as requested by opponents of the federation such as [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia") 's [Sukarno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sukarno "Sukarno") and the [Sarawak United Peoples' Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarawak_United_Peoples%27_Party "Sarawak United Peoples' Party"), the date of the federation was postponed until 16 September 1963.[^74] [^75]

The federation brought heightened tensions including a [conflict with Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia%E2%80%93Malaysia_confrontation "Indonesia–Malaysia confrontation") as well as continual conflicts against the Communists in Borneo and the Malay Peninsula, which escalated to the [Sarawak Communist Insurgency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarawak_Communist_Insurgency "Sarawak Communist Insurgency") and [Second Malayan Emergency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Malayan_Emergency "Second Malayan Emergency") together with several other issues such as the [cross-border attacks into Sabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cross_border_attacks_in_Sabah "Cross border attacks in Sabah") by [Moro pirates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moro_pirates "Moro pirates") from the southern islands of the Philippines, Singapore [being separated from the Federation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_Singapore_\(Malaysia\)#Separation "State of Singapore (Malaysia)") in 1965,[^76] [^77] [^78] and racial strife. This strife culminated in the [13 May race riots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/13_May_Incident "13 May Incident") in 1969.[^79] After the riots, the controversial [New Economic Policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_New_Economic_Policy "Malaysian New Economic Policy") was launched by Prime Minister [Tun Abdul Razak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tun_Abdul_Razak "Tun Abdul Razak"), trying to increase the share of the economy held by the *[bumiputera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bumiputera_\(Malaysia\) "Bumiputera (Malaysia)")*.[^80] Under Prime Minister [Mahathir Mohamad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahathir_Mohamad "Mahathir Mohamad") there was a period of rapid economic growth and urbanization beginning in the 1980s. The economy shifted from being agriculturally based to one based on manufacturing and industry. Numerous mega-projects were completed, such as the [Petronas Towers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petronas_Towers "Petronas Towers"), the [North–South Expressway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North%E2%80%93South_Expressway_\(Malaysia\) "North–South Expressway (Malaysia)"), the [Multimedia Super Corridor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multimedia_Super_Corridor "Multimedia Super Corridor"), and the new federal administrative capital of [Putrajaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Putrajaya "Putrajaya").[^45] [^81]

In the late 1990s, the [Asian financial crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_financial_crisis "Asian financial crisis") impacted the country, nearly causing their currency, stock, and property markets to crash; however, they later recovered.[^82] The [1MDB scandal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1MDB_scandal "1MDB scandal") was a major global [corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Malaysia "Corruption in Malaysia") scandal that implicated then-Prime Minister [Najib Razak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Najib_Razak "Najib Razak") in 2015.[^83] The scandal contributed to the first change in the ruling political party since independence in the [2018 general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Malaysian_general_election "2018 Malaysian general election").[^84] In the 2020s, the country was gripped by [a political crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020%E2%80%9321_Malaysian_political_crisis "2020–21 Malaysian political crisis") that coincided with health and [economic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic_in_Malaysia "Economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic in Malaysia") crises caused by the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Malaysia "COVID-19 pandemic in Malaysia").[^85] This was then followed by an earlier [general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Malaysian_general_election "2022 Malaysian general election") in November 2022, which resulted in the first hung parliament in the nation's history.[^86] On 24 November 2022, [Anwar Ibrahim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anwar_Ibrahim "Anwar Ibrahim") was sworn in as the 10th [Prime Minister of Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malaysia "Prime Minister of Malaysia"), leading a [grand coalition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_coalition#Malaysia "Grand coalition") government.[^87]

## Government and politics

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Istana_Negara_tepi.jpg/250px-Istana_Negara_tepi.jpg)

Istana Negara, the official residence and office of the King of Malaysia

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Perdana_Putra.jpg/250px-Perdana_Putra.jpg)

Perdana Putra, the office of the Prime Minister of Malaysia

Malaysia is a [federal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_monarchy "Federal monarchy") [constitutional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy "Constitutional monarchy") [elective monarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elective_monarchy "Elective monarchy") and the only federal country in [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia").[^88] The system of government is closely modelled on the [Westminster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westminster_system "Westminster system") parliamentary system, a legacy of [British rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Empire "British Empire").[^89] The head of state is the King, whose official title is the [Yang di-Pertuan Agong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yang_di-Pertuan_Agong "Yang di-Pertuan Agong"). The King is elected to a five-year term by and from among the nine hereditary [rulers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchies_of_Malaysia "Monarchies of Malaysia") of the [Malay states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_states "Malay states"). The other four states, which have titular [Governors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yang_di-Pertua_Negeri "Yang di-Pertua Negeri"), do not participate in the selection. By informal agreement the position is rotated among the nine,[^89] and has been held by [Ibrahim Iskandar of Johor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibrahim_Iskandar_of_Johor "Ibrahim Iskandar of Johor") since 31 January 2024. The King's role has been largely ceremonial since changes to the [constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Malaysia "Constitution of Malaysia") in [1994](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1993_amendments_to_the_Constitution_of_Malaysia "1993 amendments to the Constitution of Malaysia"), picking ministers and members of the upper house.[^90]

Legislative power is divided between federal and state legislatures. The bicameral federal [parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament_of_Malaysia "Parliament of Malaysia") consists of the [lower house](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lower_house "Lower house"), the [House of Representatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Representatives_of_Malaysia "House of Representatives of Malaysia") and the [upper house](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_house "Upper house"), the [Senate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senate_of_Malaysia "Senate of Malaysia").[^91] The 222-member House of Representatives is elected for a maximum term of five years from single-member constituencies. All 70 senators sit for three-year terms; 26 are elected by the 13 state assemblies, and the remaining 44 are appointed by the King upon the Prime Minister's recommendation.[^58] The parliament follows a multi-party system and the government is elected through a [first-past-the-post](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First-past-the-post "First-past-the-post") system.[^58] [^92] Parliamentary [elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elections_in_Malaysia "Elections in Malaysia") are held at least once every five years.[^58] Before 2018, only registered voters aged 21 and above could vote for the members of the House of Representatives and, in most of the states, for the state legislative chamber. Voting is not mandatory.[^93] In July 2019, a bill to lower the [voting age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voting_age "Voting age") to 18 years old was officially passed.[^94]

![Large building with a series of flags in front of it](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Perdana_Putra_building_2005.jpg/250px-Perdana_Putra_building_2005.jpg)

The Perdana Putra houses the office of Malaysia's Prime Minister.

[Executive power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_power "Executive power") is vested in the [Cabinet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabinet_of_Malaysia "Cabinet of Malaysia"), led by the [Prime Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malaysia "Prime Minister of Malaysia"). The prime minister must be a member of the House of Representatives, who in the opinion of His Majesty the King, commands the support of a majority of members. The Cabinet is chosen from members of both houses of Parliament.[^58] The Prime Minister is both the [head of cabinet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabinet_of_Malaysia "Cabinet of Malaysia") and the [head of government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_government "Head of government").[^90] As a result of the [2018 general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Malaysian_general_election "2018 Malaysian general election") Malaysia was governed by the [Pakatan Harapan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakatan_Harapan "Pakatan Harapan") (PH) [political alliance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_alliance "Political alliance"),[^84] although Prime Minister [Mahathir Mohamad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahathir_Mohamad "Mahathir Mohamad") resigned amid a [political crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020%E2%80%9321_Malaysian_political_crisis "2020–21 Malaysian political crisis") in 2020. In March 2020, the [Perikatan Nasional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perikatan_Nasional "Perikatan Nasional") (PN) coalition formed under Prime Minister [Muhyiddin Yassin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhyiddin_Yassin "Muhyiddin Yassin"),[^95] before Muhyiddin lost majority support and was replaced by deputy Prime Minister [Ismail Sabri Yaakob](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ismail_Sabri_Yaakob "Ismail Sabri Yaakob"), a veteran politician from the [United Malays National Organisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Malays_National_Organisation "United Malays National Organisation") (UMNO), in August 2021.[^96] [^97] As a result of the [2022 Malaysian general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Malaysian_general_election "2022 Malaysian general election"), a hung parliament was elected. [Anwar Ibrahim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anwar_Ibrahim "Anwar Ibrahim") of the PH coalition was appointed as the new Prime Minister to lead the coalition government of PH, [Barisan Nasional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barisan_Nasional "Barisan Nasional"), [Gabungan Parti Sarawak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabungan_Parti_Sarawak "Gabungan Parti Sarawak"), [Gabungan Rakyat Sabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabungan_Rakyat_Sabah "Gabungan Rakyat Sabah") and several other political parties and independents. Meanwhile, PN, the only political coalition not in the coalition government became the Opposition.

[Malaysia's legal system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law_of_Malaysia "Law of Malaysia") is based on [common law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_law "English law").[^58] Although [the judiciary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judiciary_of_Malaysia "Judiciary of Malaysia") is theoretically independent, its independence has been called into question and the appointment of judges lacks accountability and transparency.[^98] The highest court in the judicial system is the [Federal Court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Court_of_Malaysia "Federal Court of Malaysia"), followed by the [Court of Appeal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Court_of_Appeal_of_Malaysia "Court of Appeal of Malaysia") and two [high courts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_Courts_of_Malaysia "High Courts of Malaysia"), one for Peninsular Malaysia and one for East Malaysia. Malaysia also has a special court to hear cases brought by or against royalty.[^99]

[Race](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demographics_of_Malaysia "Demographics of Malaysia") is a significant force in politics.[^58] [Affirmative actions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Affirmative_action#Malaysia "Affirmative action") such as the [New Economic Policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_New_Economic_Policy "Malaysian New Economic Policy") [^80] and the [National Development Policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Development_Policy "National Development Policy") which superseded it, were implemented to advance the standing of the *bumiputera*, consisting of Malays and the indigenous tribes who are considered the original inhabitants of Malaysia, over non- *bumiputera* such as [Malaysian Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Chinese "Malaysian Chinese") and [Malaysian Indians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Indians "Malaysian Indians").[^100] These policies provide preferential treatment to *bumiputera* in employment, education, scholarships, business, and access to cheaper housing and assisted savings. However, it has generated greater interethnic resentment.[^101] There is ongoing [debate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion_in_Malaysia#Secularism "Religion in Malaysia") over whether the laws and society of Malaysia should reflect [Islamism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamism "Islamism") or [secularism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secularism "Secularism").[^102] Islamic criminal laws passed by the [Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pan-Malaysian_Islamic_Party "Pan-Malaysian Islamic Party") with the support of UMNO state assemblymen in the state legislative assembly of Kelantan have been unenforced by the federal government on the basis that criminal laws are the responsibility of the federal government.[^103] [^104] [^105]

After UMNO lost power at the [2018 Malaysian general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Malaysian_general_election "2018 Malaysian general election"), Malaysia's ranking increased by 9 places in the 2019 [Democracy Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy_Index "Democracy Index") to 43rd compared to the previous year; the country is classified as a 'flawed democracy'.[^106] Malaysia's ranking in the 2020 [Press Freedom Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Press_Freedom_Index "Press Freedom Index") increased by 22 places to 101st compared to the previous year, making it one of two countries in Southeast Asia without a 'Difficult situation' or 'Very Serious situation' with regards to press freedom.[^107] However, it fell 18 places the following year due to the policies of the PN government.[^108]

Malaysia is marked at 48th and 62nd place according to the 2021 [Corruption Perceptions Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_Perceptions_Index "Corruption Perceptions Index"), indicating above-average levels of [corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Malaysia "Corruption in Malaysia"). [Freedom House](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_House "Freedom House") noted Malaysia as "partly free" in its 2018 survey.[^109] A lawsuit filed by the [Department of Justice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Department_of_Justice "United States Department of Justice") alleged that at least $3.5 billion involving former prime minister [Najib Razak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Najib_Razak "Najib Razak") had been stolen from Malaysia's [1MDB](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1MDB "1MDB") state-owned fund, known as the [1Malaysia Development Berhad scandal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1Malaysia_Development_Berhad_scandal "1Malaysia Development Berhad scandal").[^110] [^111] [^112] As of 2025, it is ranked 13th on the [Global Peace Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Peace_Index "Global Peace Index").[^113]

Malaysia is a [federation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federation "Federation") of [13 states and three federal territories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/States_and_federal_territories_of_Malaysia "States and federal territories of Malaysia").[^114] Out of these, 11 states and two federal territories are in [Peninsular Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia "Peninsular Malaysia"), whereas the other two states and one federal territory comprise [East Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Malaysia "East Malaysia").

The country has three tiers of government— [federal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Malaysia "Government of Malaysia"), [state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_governments_of_Malaysia "State governments of Malaysia"), and local.[^115] Governance of the states is divided between the federal and the state governments, with different powers reserved for each, and the federal government has direct administration of the federal territories.[^116] Each state has a unicameral [State Legislative Assembly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_legislative_assemblies_of_Malaysia "State legislative assemblies of Malaysia") whose members are elected from single-member constituencies. State governments are led by [Chief Ministers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heads_of_state_governments_of_Malaysia "Heads of state governments of Malaysia"),[^58] who are state assembly members from the majority party in the assembly. In each of the states with a hereditary ruler, the Chief Minister is normally required to be a [Malay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malays_\(ethnic_group\) "Malays (ethnic group)"), appointed by the ruler upon the recommendation of the [Prime Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Malaysia "Prime Minister of Malaysia").[^117] Until [2018](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Malaysian_general_election "2018 Malaysian general election"), state elections were held concurrently with the general election by [convention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_convention_\(political_custom\) "Constitutional convention (political custom)"), except for those in [Sarawak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarawak "Sarawak").[^90] Following the [2020–2022 political crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020%E2%80%932022_Malaysian_political_crisis "2020–2022 Malaysian political crisis"), only [Pahang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pahang "Pahang"), [Perak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perak "Perak") and [Perlis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perlis "Perlis") opted to conduct their state elections simultaneously with the general elections.[^118]

Positioned below the federal and state governments, [local governments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_government_in_Malaysia "Local government in Malaysia") represent the lowest tier of governance in Malaysia.[^115] As of 2024, Malaysia is divided between 19 cities, 40 municipalities, 91 district-level councils, and four statutory agencies.[^119] While the federal constitution assigns local authorities to the exclusive jurisdiction of state governments,[^120] in practice, the [federal Ministry of Housing and Local Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Housing_and_Local_Government_\(Malaysia\) "Ministry of Housing and Local Government (Malaysia)") oversees the regulation of local laws and policies.[^115] [^121] [Mayors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mayor#Malaysia "Mayor") (or presidents for municipal and district councils) and [councillors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Councillor "Councillor") are appointed by the respective state governments, or in the case of the federal territories, by the federal government.[^122]

Federal laws assign land matters, including the delineation of [districts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_districts_in_Malaysia "List of districts in Malaysia"), to the purview of state governments.[^123] Except Perlis and the federal territories, each state is divided into districts, which are further subdivided into [mukims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mukim "Mukim"). In [Sabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabah "Sabah") and Sarawak, districts are grouped into divisions.[^124] In contrast to local governments that manage municipal administration and infrastructure development,[^125] districts are solely utilised for land taxation.[^126]

The 13 states are based on historical Malay kingdoms, and 9 of the 11 Peninsular states, known as the [Malay states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_states "Malay states"), retain their royal families. [The King](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_of_Malaysia "King of Malaysia") is elected by and from [the nine rulers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conference_of_Rulers "Conference of Rulers") to serve a five-year term.[^58] This King appoints governors serving a four-year term for the states without monarchies, after consultations with the chief minister of that state. Each state has its written constitution.[^127] Sabah and Sarawak have considerably more autonomy than the other states, most notably having separate immigration policies and controls, and unique residency status.[^128] [^129] [^130] Federal intervention in state affairs, lack of development, and disputes over oil [royalties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royalties "Royalties") have occasionally led to statements about [secession](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secession "Secession") from leaders in several states such as [Penang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penang "Penang"), [Johor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johor "Johor"), [Kelantan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kelantan "Kelantan"), Sabah and Sarawak, although these have not been followed up and no serious independence movements exist.[^131] [^132] [^133] [^134]

A founding member of [ASEAN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASEAN "ASEAN") [^135] and [OIC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OIC "OIC"),[^136] the country participates in many international organisations such as the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations") (UN),[^137] [APEC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/APEC "APEC"),[^138] the [D-8 Organization for Economic Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D-8_Organization_for_Economic_Cooperation "D-8 Organization for Economic Cooperation"),[^139] and NAM.[^140] It has chaired ASEAN, OIC, and NAM in the past.[^58] A former [British colony](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_colony "British colony"), it is also a member of the [Commonwealth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations "Commonwealth of Nations").[^141] [Kuala Lumpur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuala_Lumpur "Kuala Lumpur") was the site of the first [EAC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Asia_Summit "East Asia Summit") in 2005.[^142]

Malaysia's [foreign policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_policy "Foreign policy") is officially based on the principle of neutrality and maintaining peaceful relations with all countries, regardless of their political system.[^143] The government attaches a high priority to the security and stability of [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia"),[^142] and seeks to further develop relations with other countries in the region. Historically the government has tried to portray Malaysia as a progressive Islamic nation [^143] while strengthening relations with other [Islamic states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_state "Islamic state").[^142] A strong tenet of Malaysia's policy is national sovereignty and right of a country to control its domestic affairs.[^90] Malaysia signed the UN treaty on the [Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_on_the_Prohibition_of_Nuclear_Weapons "Treaty on the Prohibition of Nuclear Weapons").[^144] [^145]

The [Spratly Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spratly_Islands "Spratly Islands") are disputed by many states in the area, and [a large portion of the South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Territorial_disputes_in_the_South_China_Sea "Territorial disputes in the South China Sea") is claimed by [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China"). Unlike its neighbours of [Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam "Vietnam") and the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines"), Malaysia historically avoided conflicts with China.[^146] However, after the encroachment of Chinese ships in Malaysian territorial waters,[^147] and breach of airspace by their military aircraft, Malaysia has become active in condemning China.[^148] [^149] [Brunei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brunei "Brunei") and Malaysia in 2009 announced an end to claims of each other's land, and committed to resolve issues related to their maritime borders.[^150] The [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines") has a [dormant claim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Borneo_dispute "North Borneo dispute") to the eastern part of [Sabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabah "Sabah").[^151] [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore "Singapore") 's land reclamation has caused tensions,[^152] and minor maritime and land border disputes exist with [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia").[^151] [^153]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3e/Su30mkm_flying_at_lima_two_%28cropped%29.jpg/250px-Su30mkm_flying_at_lima_two_%28cropped%29.jpg)

Sukhoi Su-30MKM of the RMAF

The [Malaysian Armed Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Armed_Forces "Malaysian Armed Forces") has three branches: the [Malaysian Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Army "Malaysian Army"), [Royal Malaysian Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Malaysian_Navy "Royal Malaysian Navy") and the [Royal Malaysian Air Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_Malaysian_Air_Force "Royal Malaysian Air Force"). There is no conscription, and the required age for voluntary military service is 18. The military uses 1.5% of the country's GDP, and employs 1.23% of Malaysia's manpower.[^154] Malaysian peacekeeping forces of [MALBATT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MALBATT "MALBATT") have contributed to many U.N. peacekeeping missions, such as in [Congo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Operation_in_the_Congo "United Nations Operation in the Congo"), [Iran–Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_Military_Observer_Group "United Nations Iran–Iraq Military Observer Group"), [Namibia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Transition_Assistance_Group "United Nations Transition Assistance Group"), [Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Transitional_Authority_in_Cambodia "United Nations Transitional Authority in Cambodia"), [Bosnia and Herzegovina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Protection_Force "United Nations Protection Force"), [Somalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Operation_in_Somalia_II "United Nations Operation in Somalia II"), [Kosovo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Interim_Administration_Mission_in_Kosovo "United Nations Interim Administration Mission in Kosovo"), [East Timor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Integrated_Mission_in_East_Timor "United Nations Integrated Mission in East Timor"), and [Lebanon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Interim_Force_in_Lebanon "United Nations Interim Force in Lebanon").[^58] [^155]

The [Five Power Defence Arrangements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five_Power_Defence_Arrangements "Five Power Defence Arrangements") is a regional security initiative that has been in place for almost 40 years. It involves joint military exercises held among Malaysia, Singapore, Australia, New Zealand, and the United Kingdom.[^156] Joint exercises and war games have also been held with Brunei,[^157] China,[^158] India,[^159] Indonesia,[^160] Japan,[^161] and the United States.[^162] Malaysia, Philippines, Thailand, and Vietnam have agreed to host joint security force exercises to secure their maritime border and tackle issues such as illegal immigration, [piracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piracy "Piracy"), and [smuggling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smuggling "Smuggling").[^163] [^164] [^165] Previously there were fears that extremist militant activities in the Muslim areas of the [southern Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moro_conflict_in_the_Philippines "Moro conflict in the Philippines") [^166] and [southern Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Thailand_insurgency "South Thailand insurgency") [^167] would spill over into Malaysia. Because of this, Malaysia began to increase its border security.[^168] [^169]

### Human rights

[Homosexuality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homosexuality "Homosexuality") is [illegal in Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/LGBT_rights_in_Malaysia "LGBT rights in Malaysia"),[^170] [^171] and authorities have imposed punishments such as [caning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caning_in_Malaysia "Caning in Malaysia") and imprisonment.[^172] [^173] [Human trafficking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_trafficking "Human trafficking") and [sex trafficking in Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sex_trafficking_in_Malaysia "Sex trafficking in Malaysia") are significant problems.[^174] [^175] There have also been cases of vigilante executions and beatings against LGBT individuals in Malaysia.[^176] [^177] The illegality of homosexuality in Malaysia has also been the forefront of [Anwar Ibrahim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anwar_Ibrahim "Anwar Ibrahim") 's [sodomy trials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anwar_Ibrahim_sodomy_trials "Anwar Ibrahim sodomy trials"), which Anwar has called politically motivated, a characterization supported by the [Working Group on Arbitrary Detention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Working_Group_on_Arbitrary_Detention "Working Group on Arbitrary Detention"), along with [Amnesty International](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amnesty_International "Amnesty International") and the [Human Rights Watch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Rights_Watch "Human Rights Watch").[^178] [^179] [^180]

The [death penalty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capital_punishment_in_Malaysia "Capital punishment in Malaysia") is in use for serious crimes such as [murder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murder "Murder"), [terrorism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrorism "Terrorism"), and [drug trafficking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drug_trafficking "Drug trafficking"), some of which were once mandatory.[^181] [^182] However, in July 2023, following the passing of the [Abolition of Mandatory Death Penalty Act 2023](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abolition_of_Mandatory_Death_Penalty_Act_2023 "Abolition of Mandatory Death Penalty Act 2023"), mandatory death penalty was abolished for all crimes in Malaysia, thereby allowing judges to impose the capital punishment at their own discretion and on a case-by-case basis. The new law also abolished all [natural life imprisonment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Life_imprisonment "Life imprisonment"), replacing it with 30 to 40 years prison terms instead.[^183] [^184] [^185]

## Geography

![Relief map of Malaysia](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/79/Malaysia_relief_location_map.jpg/500px-Malaysia_relief_location_map.jpg)

Topographic map of Malaysia; Mount Kinabalu is the highest summit in the country.

Malaysia is the [66th largest country by total land area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_outlying_territories_by_total_area "List of countries and outlying territories by total area"), with a total area of 330,803 km <sup>2</sup> (127,724 sq mi).[^17] [West Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Malaysia "West Malaysia") shares [land borders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borders_of_Malaysia "Borders of Malaysia") with [Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand "Thailand"), while [East Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Malaysia "East Malaysia") shares land borders with [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia") and [Brunei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brunei "Brunei").[^25] [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore "Singapore") is separated from the West by the [Straits of Johor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Straits_of_Johor "Straits of Johor") and is linked to it by [a narrow causeway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johor%E2%80%93Singapore_Causeway "Johor–Singapore Causeway") and [a bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia%E2%80%93Singapore_Second_Link "Malaysia–Singapore Second Link"). The country also shares [maritime boundaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_boundaries "Maritime boundaries") with Indonesia, [Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam "Vietnam") [^186] and the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines").[^187] The land borders are defined in large part by geological features such as the [Perlis River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perlis_River "Perlis River"), the [Golok River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golok_River "Golok River") and the Pagalayan Canal, whilst some of the maritime boundaries are the subject of ongoing contention.[^25] Brunei forms what is almost an [enclave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclave "Enclave") in Malaysia,[^188] with the state of [Sarawak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarawak "Sarawak") dividing it into two parts. Malaysia is the only country with territory on both the [Asian mainland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "Asia") and the [Malay Archipelago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malay_Archipelago "Malay Archipelago").[^189] The [Strait of Malacca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Malacca "Strait of Malacca"), lying between [Sumatra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumatra "Sumatra") and Peninsular Malaysia, is one of the most important thoroughfares in global commerce, carrying 40% of the world's trade.[^190] The [southernmost point of mainland Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extreme_points_of_Asia "Extreme points of Asia") is located at [Tanjung Piai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanjung_Piai "Tanjung Piai"), in the state of [Johor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johor "Johor").[^191]

The two parts of Malaysia, separated from each other by the [South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea "South China Sea"), share a largely similar landscape in that both Peninsular and East Malaysia feature [coastal plains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coastal_plains "Coastal plains") rising to [hills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hills "Hills") and [mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountains "Mountains").[^25] Peninsular Malaysia, containing 40% of Malaysia's land area,[^189] extends 740 km (460 mi) from north to south, and its maximum width is 322 km (200 mi).[^192] It is divided between its east and west coasts by the [Titiwangsa Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Titiwangsa_Mountains "Titiwangsa Mountains"),[^193] rising to a peak elevation of 2,183 metres (7,162 ft) at [Mount Korbu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Korbu "Mount Korbu"),[^194] part of a series of [mountain ranges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountain_range "Mountain range") running down the centre of the peninsula.[^189] These mountains are heavily [forested](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest "Forest"),[^195] and mainly composed of [granite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Granite "Granite") and other [igneous rocks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Igneous_rock "Igneous rock"). Much of it has been eroded, creating a [karst](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karst "Karst") landscape.[^189] The range is the origin of some of Peninsular Malaysia's river systems.[^196] The coastal plains surrounding the peninsula reach a maximum width of 50 kilometres (31 mi), and the peninsula's coastline is nearly 1,931 km (1,200 mi) long, although [harbours](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harbour "Harbour") are only available on the western side.[^192]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/St._John%27s_Peak_Mount_Kinabalu_3.jpg/500px-St._John%27s_Peak_Mount_Kinabalu_3.jpg)

Climbers ascending toward Low's Peak before sunrise on Mount Kinabalu, with the distant city lights of Kota Kinabalu visible far right, Sabah, Borneo

East Malaysia, on the island of [Borneo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borneo "Borneo"), has a coastline of 2,607 km (1,620 mi).[^25] It is divided between coastal regions, hills and [valleys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valley "Valley"), and a mountainous interior.[^189] The [Crocker Range](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crocker_Range "Crocker Range") extends northwards from Sarawak,[^189] dividing the state of [Sabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabah "Sabah"). It is the location of the 4,095 m (13,435 ft) high [Mount Kinabalu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Kinabalu "Mount Kinabalu"),[^197] [^198] the [tallest mountain in Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_mountains_in_Malaysia "List of mountains in Malaysia"). Mount Kinabalu is located in the [Kinabalu National Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kinabalu_National_Park "Kinabalu National Park"), which is protected as one of the four [UNESCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO "UNESCO") [World Heritage Sites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Heritage_Site "World Heritage Site") [in Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_World_Heritage_Sites_in_Malaysia "List of World Heritage Sites in Malaysia").[^199] The highest mountain ranges form the border between Malaysia and Indonesia. Sarawak contains the Mulu Caves, the largest cave system in the world, in the [Gunung Mulu National Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gunung_Mulu_National_Park "Gunung Mulu National Park"), another World Heritage Site.[^189] Also in Sarawak is the [Rajang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rajang_River "Rajang River"), the [largest river in Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_rivers_of_Malaysia "List of rivers of Malaysia").

Around the two halves of Malaysia are [numerous islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_islands_of_Malaysia "List of islands of Malaysia"), the largest of which is [Banggi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banggi "Banggi").[^200] The local climate is [equatorial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_climate "Tropical climate") and characterised by the annual southwest (April to October) and northeast (October to February) [monsoons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsoon "Monsoon").[^192] The temperature is moderated by the presence of the surrounding oceans.[^189] Humidity is usually high, and the average annual rainfall is 250 cm (98 in).[^192] The climates of the Peninsula and the East differ, as the climate on the peninsula is directly affected by wind from the mainland, as opposed to the more maritime weather of the East. Local climates can be divided into three regions, highland, lowland, and coastal.[^189] [Climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_in_Malaysia "Climate change in Malaysia") will cause [sea level rise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_level_rise "Sea level rise") and increased rainfall, increasing flood risks and leading to droughts.[^201]

### Biodiversity and conservation

![Tiger resting on an artificial log platform](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/MalayanTiger.jpg/250px-MalayanTiger.jpg)

A Malayan tiger in the National Zoo of Malaysia

Malaysia signed the Rio [Convention on Biological Diversity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convention_on_Biological_Diversity "Convention on Biological Diversity") on 12 June 1993, and became a party to the convention on 24 June 1994.[^202] It has subsequently produced a [National Biodiversity Strategy and Action Plan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity_action_plan "Biodiversity action plan"), which was received by the convention on 16 April 1998.[^203] The country is [megadiverse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megadiverse "Megadiverse") with a high number of species and high levels of [endemism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endemism "Endemism").[^204] It is estimated to contain 20% of the world's animal species.[^205] High levels of endemism are found on the diverse forests of [Borneo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borneo "Borneo") 's mountains, as species are isolated from each other by lowland forest.[^189]

There are about 210 mammal species in the country.[^197] Over 620 species of birds have been recorded in [Peninsular Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsular_Malaysia "Peninsular Malaysia"),[^205] with many endemic to the mountains there. A high number of endemic bird species are also found in [Malaysian Borneo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_Borneo "Malaysian Borneo").[^189] 250 [reptile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reptile "Reptile") species have been recorded in the country, with about 150 species of [snakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Snake "Snake") [^206] and 80 species of [lizards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lizard "Lizard").[^197] There are about 150 species of [frogs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frog "Frog"),[^197] and thousands of [insect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insect "Insect") species.[^197] The [Exclusive economic zone of Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone_of_Malaysia "Exclusive economic zone of Malaysia") is 334,671 km <sup>2</sup> (129,217 sq mi) and 1.5 times larger than its land area. It is mainly in the [South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea "South China Sea").[^207] [^208] Some of its waters are in the [Coral Triangle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coral_Triangle "Coral Triangle"), a biodiversity hotspot.[^209] The waters around the island of [Sipadan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sipadan "Sipadan") are the most biodiverse in the world.[^205] Bordering East Malaysia, the [Sulu Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sulu_Sea "Sulu Sea") is a biodiversity hotspot, with around 600 [coral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coral "Coral") species and 1200 [fish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fish "Fish") species.[^210] The unique biodiversity of Malaysian Caves always attracts lovers of [ecotourism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecotourism "Ecotourism") from all over the world.[^211]

Nearly 4,000 species of [fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fungi "Fungi"), including [lichen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lichen "Lichen") -forming species, have been recorded from Malaysia. Of the two fungal groups with the largest number of species in Malaysia, the [Ascomycota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ascomycota "Ascomycota") and their asexual states have been surveyed in some habitats (decaying wood, marine, and freshwater ecosystems, as parasites of some plants, and as agents of biodegradation), but have not been or have been only poorly surveyed in other habitats (as endobionts, in soils, on dung, as human and animal pathogens); the [Basidiomycota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basidiomycota "Basidiomycota") are only partly surveyed: [bracket fungi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bracket_fungi "Bracket fungi"), mushrooms and toadstools have been studied, but Malaysian rust and smut fungi remain little known. Without a doubt, many more fungal species in Malaysia are yet to be recorded, and it is likely that many of those, when found, will be new to science.[^212]

![Red flower made of 5 petals surrounding a depressed centre, on the forest floor surrounded by dead leaves and small green plants](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/A_Rafflesia%2C_the_biggest_flower_in_the_world.jpg/250px-A_Rafflesia%2C_the_biggest_flower_in_the_world.jpg)

Some species of Rafflesia can grow up to 1 m (3 ft 3 in) in diameter, making them the largest flowers in the world.

About two-thirds of Malaysia was covered in [forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest "Forest") as of 2007,[^192] with some forests believed to be 130 million years old.[^197] The forests are dominated by [dipterocarps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dipterocarpaceae "Dipterocarpaceae").[^213] Lowland forest covers areas below 760 m (2,490 ft),[^192] and formerly East Malaysia was covered in [such rainforest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Borneo_lowland_rain_forests "Borneo lowland rain forests"),[^213] which is supported by its hot wet climate.[^189] There are around 14,500 species of [flowering plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flowering_plant "Flowering plant") and [trees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tree "Tree").[^197] Besides [rainforests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rainforest "Rainforest"), there are over 1,425 km <sup>2</sup> (550 sq mi) of [mangroves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mangroves "Mangroves") in Malaysia,[^192] and a large amount of peat forest. At higher altitudes, [oaks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oak "Oak"), [chestnuts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chestnut "Chestnut"), and [rhododendrons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhododendron "Rhododendron") replace [dipterocarps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dipterocarpaceae "Dipterocarpaceae").[^189] There are an estimated 8,500 species of [vascular plants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vascular_plant "Vascular plant") in Peninsular Malaysia, with another 15,000 in the East.[^214] The forests of East Malaysia are estimated to be the habitat of around 2,000 tree species, and are one of the most biodiverse areas in the world, with 240 different species of trees every hectare.[^189] These forests host many members of the *[Rafflesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rafflesia "Rafflesia")* genus, the largest flowers in the world,[^213] with a maximum diameter of 1 m (3 ft 3 in).[^215]

[Logging](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logging "Logging"), along with cultivation practices, has devastated tree cover, causing severe environmental degradation in the country. Over 80% of [Sarawak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarawak "Sarawak") 's rainforest has been logged.[^189] Floods in East Malaysia have been worsened by the loss of trees, and over 60% of the peninsula's forests have been cleared.[^215] With current rates of [deforestation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_and_environmental_impact_of_palm_oil#Deforestation "Social and environmental impact of palm oil"), mainly for the [palm oil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palm_oil_production_in_Malaysia "Palm oil production in Malaysia") industry, the forests are predicted to be extinct by 2020.[^189] [^216] [Deforestation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deforestation_in_Malaysia "Deforestation in Malaysia") is a major problem for animals, fungi and plants, having caused species such as *[Begonia eiromischa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Begonia_eiromischa "Begonia eiromischa")* to go extinct.[^217] Most remaining forest is found inside reserves and national parks.[^215] Habitat destruction has proved a threat for [marine life](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_life "Marine life").[^210] [Illegal fishing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illegal_fishing "Illegal fishing") is another major threat,[^210] with fishing methods such as [dynamite fishing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynamite_fishing "Dynamite fishing") and poisoning depleting marine ecosystems.[^218] [Leatherback turtle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leatherback_turtle "Leatherback turtle") numbers have dropped by 98% since the 1950s.[^206] Hunting has also been an issue for some animals,[^215] with [overconsumption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overconsumption "Overconsumption") and the use of animal parts for profit endangering many animals, from marine life [^210] to [tigers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiger "Tiger").[^217] Marine life is also detrimentally affected by uncontrolled tourism.[^219]

The [Malaysian government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysian_government "Malaysian government") aims to balance economic growth with environmental protection, but has been accused of favouring big business over the environment.[^215] Some state governments are now trying to counter the environmental impact and pollution created by deforestation;[^213] and the federal government is trying to cut logging by 10% each year. A total of [28 national parks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_national_parks_of_Malaysia "List of national parks of Malaysia") have been established, 23 in East Malaysia and five in the peninsula.[^215] Tourism has been limited in biodiverse areas such as the island of Sipadan.[^219] [Wildlife trafficking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildlife_trafficking "Wildlife trafficking") is a large issue, and the Malaysian government has held talks with the governments of [Brunei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Brunei "Government of Brunei") and [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Indonesia "Government of Indonesia") to standardise anti-trafficking laws.[^220]

## Economy

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/GDP_per_capita_development_of_Malaysia.svg/330px-GDP_per_capita_development_of_Malaysia.svg.png)

Development of real GDP per capita, 1820 to 2018

Malaysia is a relatively [open](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Open_economy "Open economy") state-oriented and [newly industrialised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newly_industrialised_country "Newly industrialised country") [market economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market_economy "Market economy").[^221] It has the world's [36th-largest economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\) "List of countries by GDP (nominal)") by nominal GDP and the [31st-largest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(PPP\) "List of countries by GDP (PPP)") by [PPP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purchasing_power_parity "Purchasing power parity"). In 2017, the large [service sector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Service_sector "Service sector") contributed to 53.6% of total [GDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GDP "GDP"), the industrial sector 37.6%, and the small agricultural sector roughly 8.8%.[^222] Malaysia has a low official [unemployment rate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_unemployment_rate "List of countries by unemployment rate") of 3.4% as of 2024.[^223] Its foreign exchange reserves are the world's [24th-largest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_foreign-exchange_reserves "List of countries by foreign-exchange reserves").[^224] It has a labour force of about 15 million, which is the world's [34th-largest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_labour_force "List of countries by labour force").[^225] Malaysia's large [automotive industry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Automotive_industry_in_Malaysia "Automotive industry in Malaysia") ranks as the world's [22nd-largest by production](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_motor_vehicle_production "List of countries by motor vehicle production").[^226]

Malaysia is the world's 23rd-largest [exporter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_exports "List of countries by exports") and 25th-largest [importer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_imports "List of countries by imports").[^227] [^228] However, economic inequalities exist between different ethnic groups.[^229] The Chinese make up about one-quarter of the population, but account for 70% of the country's market capitalisation.[^230] Chinese businesses in Malaysia are part of the larger [bamboo network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bamboo_network "Bamboo network"), a network of [overseas Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overseas_Chinese "Overseas Chinese") businesses in the Southeast Asian market sharing common family and cultural ties.[^231]

International trade, facilitated by the shipping route in adjacent [Strait of Malacca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Malacca "Strait of Malacca"), and manufacturing are the key sectors.[^232] [^233] [^190] Malaysia is an exporter of natural and agricultural resources, and petroleum is a major export.[^58] Malaysia has once been the largest producer of [tin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tin "Tin"),[^234] [rubber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rubber "Rubber") and [palm oil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palm_oil "Palm oil") in the world. Manufacturing has a large influence in the country's economy,[^233] although Malaysia's economic structure has been moving away from it.[^235] Malaysia remains one of the world's largest producers of palm oil.[^236]

[Tourism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism_in_Malaysia "Tourism in Malaysia") is the third-largest contributor to Malaysia's GDP, after the manufacturing and commodities sectors.[^237] In 2019, the sector contributed about 15.9% to the total GDP. According to the [World Tourism Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Tourism_Organization "World Tourism Organization"), Malaysia was the fourteenth-most visited country in the world, and the fourth-most visited country in Asia in 2019, with over 26.1 million visits.[^238] Malaysia was ranked 38th in the Travel and Tourism Competitiveness Report 2019.[^239] Its international tourism receipts in 2019 amounted to $19.8 billion.[^238]

The country has developed into a centre of [Islamic banking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_banking "Islamic banking") and has the highest number of female workers in that industry.[^240] Knowledge-based services are also expanding.[^235] In 2020, Malaysia exported high-tech products worth $92.1 billion, the second-highest in ASEAN, after Singapore.[^241] Malaysia was ranked 34th in the [Global Innovation Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Innovation_Index "Global Innovation Index") in 2025 [^242] [^243], and 32nd in the [Global Competitiveness Report](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Competitiveness_Report "Global Competitiveness Report") in 2022.[^244]
