The **construction of theaters, opera houses, and other cultural facilities by China** as gifts to foreign countries is a part of China's [foreign aid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_foreign_aid "China foreign aid") program.[^1] In a white paper published by China in 2009 on its aid projects in the area of civil construction, the building of cultural facilities is one of the types identified among a total of 2,025 projects stated as built by a Chinese grant or no-interest loan to the recipient country.[^1]

- [Algeria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria "Algeria")
	- A national opera house in [Algiers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algiers "Algiers") is under construction as a US$40 million gift by China to Algeria.[^2] The foundation stone for the 1400-seat venue was laid in a ceremony in November 2012.[^2]
- [Cameroon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cameroon "Cameroon")
	- The Palais des Congrès de Yaoundé was built by China and opened in 1982. The venue is a 1,500-seat multipurpose performance hall with "an ultra-modern stage boasting the country's best sound and lighting equipment." [^3]
- [Cape Verde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cape_Verde "Cape Verde")
	- [National Auditorium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Auditorium_\(Cape_Verde\) "National Auditorium (Cape Verde)") [^4]
- [Ghana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghana "Ghana")
	- The [National Theatre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Theatre_\(Accra\) "National Theatre (Accra)") in [Accra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accra "Accra") was opened in January 1993 after construction by China.[^5] The theatre is a gift as the loan from China funding the construction would later be cancelled in 2007. China granted a further US$2 million to refurbish the theatre for Ghana's golden jubilee celebrations.
		- The Drama Studio at the [University of Ghana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Ghana "University of Ghana") at Legon was built under the program as the original work on the National Theatre.[^5]
- [Mauritius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauritius "Mauritius")
	- The Plaza Theatre in Rose Hill was renovated in 2008 using funds provided by China in the form of a non-interest loan.[^6]
- [Senegal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senegal "Senegal")
	- The Grand Theatre in [Dakar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dakar "Dakar") was constructed from 2008 to 2011 by [Complant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Complant "Complant") as a gift.[^7] The six-storey, 1800-seat theatre was built at a cost of 16 billion CFA francs, of which China paid 14 billion CFA francs and Senegal contributed the rest.[^7]
		- Construction on a Museum of Black Civilization located in Dakar began in 2011, funded by a grant of $US30 million from China.[^8] The contractor for the project is the [Shanghai Construction Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai_Construction_Group "Shanghai Construction Group").
- [Somalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Somalia "Somalia")
	- [National Theatre of Somalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Theatre_of_Somalia "National Theatre of Somalia") was built by China as a gift to Somalia in 1967.[^9]
- [Sri Lanka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lanka "Sri Lanka")
	- [Nelum Pokuna Mahinda Rajapaksa Theatre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nelum_Pokuna_Mahinda_Rajapaksa_Theatre "Nelum Pokuna Mahinda Rajapaksa Theatre") is theatre in [Colombo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colombo "Colombo") built in 2011 by China as gift to enhance ties with [Sri Lanka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sri_Lanka "Sri Lanka").[^10] Construction of the 1288-seat venue was handled by the [Yanjian Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yanjian_Group "Yanjian Group").[^10]
- [Trinidad and Tobago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trinidad_and_Tobago "Trinidad and Tobago")
	- National Academy for the Performing Arts in [Port of Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port_of_Spain "Port of Spain")
