The [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") (PRC) and [Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia "Indonesia") established diplomatic relations in 1950, and re-established relations in 1990 following a break in 1967. For many centuries, the two regions were largely tied together through informal trade. Under [Sukarno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sukarno "Sukarno") ’s presidency, relations with China deepened. In the early 1960s, Sukarno embarked on a series of foreign policies under a banner of [anti-imperialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-imperialism "Anti-imperialism") and a personal championing of the [Non-Aligned Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement "Non-Aligned Movement"). These developments led to increasing friction with the West and closer relations both with the China and the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union"). Sukarno's [Guided Democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guided_Democracy "Guided Democracy") relied on two conflicting pillars of support, the military and the [Communist Party of Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Indonesia "Communist Party of Indonesia") (PKI). He favored the latter, which grew to be the largest political party with 3 million members and in August 1965, Sukarno declared his intention to commit Indonesia to an anti-imperialist alliance with China and other communist regimes and warned the military not to interfere.[^1]

The military, nationalists, and Muslim groups objected to the prominence of the PKI, fearing the imminent establishment of a communist state in Indonesia and regarded Sukarno's close alliance with China as compromising Indonesia's sovereignty. This culminated in the progressive sidelining and eventual ousting of Sukarno from 1965 until 1967 by [Suharto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suharto "Suharto") and his supporters, who instigated a prolonged persecution and eventual elimination of the PKI with estimates of killings varying from 500,000 to 1 million.[^2] [^3] [^4] In 1967, Suharto introduced orders banning Chinese literature, culture and characters, encouraging the abandonment of Chinese names and suspended diplomatic relationships with the PRC, to be restored only in 1990.[^5] [^6]

An important factor in relations is the small, only 3% of the population, but long-established and economically influential Chinese diaspora. This group has been keen to take advantage of trading opportunities with China, which has become Indonesia's biggest trading partner since the signing of the Indonesia-China Strategic Partnership in 2005. Trade flows increased steadily, turning in 2008 into a rising trade deficit for Indonesia. Two-way investment was boosted by President [Joko Widodo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joko_Widodo "Joko Widodo") and Chinese leader [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") ’s close personal relationship, leading to a surge in investment under China's [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative") (BRI). Inbound investment from [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China"), including [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong"), in 2019–2020, ranked second after [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore "Singapore").[^7] Both countries are among the largest nations in Asia in terms of both area and population. China is the second-most populous nation in the world, while Indonesia has the 4th largest population.[^8] China is Indonesia's largest trading partner. The annual trade between China and Indonesia was $135 billion as of 2024.

China has an embassy in [Jakarta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jakarta "Jakarta") and consulates in [Surabaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surabaya "Surabaya") and [Medan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medan "Medan"), while Indonesia has an embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and consulates in [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou"), [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai"), and [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong"). Both nations are members of the [APEC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/APEC "APEC"), [BRICS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BRICS "BRICS") and the [G20](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G20 "G20").

## History

### Kingdom era

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/47/Zheng_He_statue.jpg/250px-Zheng_He_statue.jpg)

The Zheng He memorial statue in Sam Poo Kong temple, Semarang, commemorate the Ming naval voyage to Indonesian archipelago.

The relations between imperial China and ancient Indonesia commenced during the 7th century, possibly earlier. Indonesia was part of the maritime [Silk Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silk_Road "Silk Road") connecting China with [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India") and the [Arab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab "Arab") world. Numerous [Chinese ceramics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_ceramics "Chinese ceramics") have been discovered throughout Indonesia, suggesting ancient trade links between the two regions. The [National Museum of Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Museum_of_Indonesia "National Museum of Indonesia") has one of the most complete collections of Chinese ceramics discovered outside of China, dated from the [Han](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty"), [Tang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_dynasty "Tang dynasty"), [Song](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Song_dynasty "Song dynasty"), [Yuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuan_dynasty "Yuan dynasty"), [Ming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty") and [Qing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty") dynasties, spanning for almost two millennia.[^9] This particular collection provides a good insight into Indonesia's maritime trade over the centuries. Research indicates that the Chinese sailed to India via Indonesia as early as [Western Han](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Han "Western Han") period (205 BC to 220 AD) as a part of the maritime silk road and firm trade relations were subsequently established.[^10] Traditionally, the Indonesian archipelago, identified by ancient Chinese geographer as [Nanyang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanyang_\(region\) "Nanyang (region)"), was the source of spices such as [cloves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloves "Cloves"), [cubeb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cubeb "Cubeb"), and [nutmeg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nutmeg "Nutmeg"), raw materials such as [sandalwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandalwood "Sandalwood"), [gold](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gold "Gold") and [tin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tin "Tin"), as well as exotic rare items such as [ivory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivory "Ivory"), [rhino horn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhino_horn "Rhino horn"), [tiger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiger "Tiger") fur and bone, exotic birds and colorful feathers. While the fine [silk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silk "Silk") and ceramics of China was sought by ancient Indonesian kingdoms. Indonesia also played some role in the expansion of [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism") from India to China. A Chinese monk, [I-Tsing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/I-Tsing "I-Tsing"), visited [Srivijaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Srivijaya "Srivijaya") in 671 for 6 months during his mission to acquire sacred Buddhist texts from India.[^11] [^12] Other Chinese accounts and chronicles also mention several ancient states in Indonesia today. Chinese records mentioned about Kunlun sailors, presumably Cham or Malays sailors, that sailed and traded many products across ports in Southeast and South Asia. The Malay sailors are most probably hailed from Srivijayan empire in Sumatra. Ancient Indonesian sailors seem to actively sought trade around Southeast Asia and as far as China and India.[^13] Since I-Tsing, numbers of Chinese travelers such as [Chou Ju-kua](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chou_Ju-kua "Chou Ju-kua") began to visit and wrote about the Indonesian archipelago.

Most ancient Chinese-Indonesia relations were trade-motivated and throughout their shared history, most were harmonious and peaceful, with one exception. In 1293, Kublai Khan of [Yuan dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuan_dynasty "Yuan dynasty") [sent a massive expedition of 1,000 ships to Java](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_invasion_of_Java "Mongol invasion of Java") to punish the defiant king [Kertanegara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kertanegara "Kertanegara") of [Singhasari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singhasari "Singhasari").[^14] The naval expedition, however, was a failure as Java rose to be [Majapahit empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Majapahit_empire "Majapahit empire") instead. Maritime empires such as [Srivijaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Srivijaya "Srivijaya"), Majapahit, and later [Malacca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malacca_Sultanate "Malacca Sultanate") sought trade permits to establish relations with lucrative Chinese markets. The numbers of Chinese immigrants and Chinese influences in the archipelago reached a new height, with the massive [Ming dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty") [naval expedition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treasure_voyages "Treasure voyages") led by admiral [Zheng He](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zheng_He "Zheng He") that visited Java, Sumatra, and the Malay peninsula in early the 15th century. Zheng He's translator [Ma Huan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ma_Huan "Ma Huan") wrote a detailed description of Majapahit and where the king of Java lived.[^15] The report was composed and collected in *[Yingya Shenglan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yingya_Shenglan "Yingya Shenglan")*, which provides a valuable insight on the culture, customs, also various social and economic aspects of *Chao-Wa* (Java) during Majapahit period.[^16] The Chinese naval expedition contributed to the establishment of [overseas Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overseas_Chinese "Overseas Chinese") settlements in Indonesia, such as [Semarang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semarang "Semarang"), [Tuban](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuban "Tuban") and [Rembang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rembang "Rembang") which have had significant Chinese populations since Majapahit era.

During the colonial [Dutch East Indies Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies_Company "Dutch East Indies Company") and [Dutch East Indies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies "Dutch East Indies") era, significant Chinese settlers began to fill labor needs and seek a new life in the Indonesian archipelago. Most of [Chinese Indonesian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Indonesian "Chinese Indonesian") immigrants came from the provinces of [Southern China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_China "Southern China"), such as [Fujian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fujian "Fujian") and [Guangdong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangdong "Guangdong"). Significant [Chinese settlements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_town "China town") were established in West Kalimantan, the east coast of Sumatra, and the northern coast of Java. In 1932, China declared that its southernmost territory was the Paracels, not the seas surrounding the Natuna islands.[^17] [^18] [^19]

### Post-Independence

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/93/Collectie_Fotocollectiie_Afdrukken_ANEFO_Rousel%2C_fotonummer_157-1011%2C_Bestanddeelnr_157-1011.jpg/250px-Collectie_Fotocollectiie_Afdrukken_ANEFO_Rousel%2C_fotonummer_157-1011%2C_Bestanddeelnr_157-1011.jpg)

President Sukarno of Indonesia greeted at Beijing airport by Mao Zedong flocked by Indonesian-Chinese flags

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/Sukarno_with_Mao_Zedong%2C_Bung_Karno_Penjambung_Lidah_Rakjat_244.jpg/250px-Sukarno_with_Mao_Zedong%2C_Bung_Karno_Penjambung_Lidah_Rakjat_244.jpg)

Mao Zedong and Sukarno

After Indonesia's independence in 1945 and the acknowledgement of its sovereignty from the [Dutch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netherlands "Netherlands") in 1949, Indonesia established diplomatic relations with China (previously with [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China "Republic of China") and later with [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China")) in 1950.[^20] It was the first country in Southeast Asia to establish official diplomatic relations with the PRC.[^21] During the [Sukarno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sukarno "Sukarno") administration, China and Indonesia enjoyed close relations.

In the 1950s to 1960s the [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") (CCP) had close relations with their [Indonesian counterparts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Indonesia "Communist Party of Indonesia"). Sukarno supported and won the bid for the [1962 Asian Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1962_Asian_Games "1962 Asian Games") held in [Jakarta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jakarta "Jakarta"). Political tensions arose when Indonesia refused entry to a delegation from [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan"). After the [International Olympic Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Olympic_Committee "International Olympic Committee") imposed sanctions on Indonesia for this exclusionary policy, due to Indonesia's firm support for the [One China policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China "One China"). Sukarno retaliated by organizing a "non-imperialist" competitor event to the [Olympic Games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olympic_Games "Olympic Games"), called the *Games of New Emerging Forces* ([GANEFO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GANEFO "GANEFO")). Sukarno responded by accusing the IOC of being political in its refusal to accept the People's Republic of China; The IOC was merely an "imperialist and colonialist tool." [^22]

In January 1965, Sukarno, [withdrew Indonesia from the United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesia_and_the_United_Nations "Indonesia and the United Nations") after accusations that it was "dominated by imperialism." He attempted to establish a rival UN organization called the Conference of New Emerging Powers ([CONEFO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CONEFO "CONEFO")) with the support of the People's Republic of China, which was not yet a member of the UN,[^23] and he formed a new alliance of Indonesia with the new emergent forces (NEFO) countries with China, North Korea, North Vietnam, and Cambodia which he called the " [Beijing-Pyongyang-Hanoi-Phnom Penh-Jakarta Axis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jakarta%E2%80%93Peking_Axis "Jakarta–Peking Axis")." With the government heavily indebted to the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union"), Indonesia became increasingly dependent on China for support.[^24] Sukarno increasingly spoke of the importance of the Beijing-Jakarta axis.[^24] However, after an unsuccessful [coup attempt in 1965](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/30_September_Movement "30 September Movement") resulted in the fall of Sukarno and [the consolidation of power by right-wing military general Suharto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transition_to_the_New_Order "Transition to the New Order"), Indonesia severed diplomatic relations, maintaining that Communist China was partly responsible for the coup attempt.[^25] Diplomatic relations however, were restored and resumed in 1990, resulting in the normalization of China-Indonesia ties.

### Contemporary history

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/1967-07_1967%E5%B9%B44%E6%9C%88_%E6%B0%91%E4%BC%97%E5%88%B0%E5%8D%B0%E5%B0%BC%E9%A9%BB%E5%8D%8E%E5%A4%A7%E4%BD%BF%E9%A6%86%E9%97%A8%E5%89%8D%E6%8A%97%E8%AE%AE%E6%B8%B8%E8%A1%8C.jpg/250px-1967-07_1967%E5%B9%B44%E6%9C%88_%E6%B0%91%E4%BC%97%E5%88%B0%E5%8D%B0%E5%B0%BC%E9%A9%BB%E5%8D%8E%E5%A4%A7%E4%BD%BF%E9%A6%86%E9%97%A8%E5%89%8D%E6%8A%97%E8%AE%AE%E6%B8%B8%E8%A1%8C.jpg)

Protest at the Indonesian embassy in Beijing in April 1967 following Suharto's assumption of presidential power

China and Indonesia established diplomatic relations on April 13, 1950, which were suspended on October 30, 1967, due to the occurrence of the [September 30 event](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/30_September_Movement "30 September Movement") of 1965, the subsequent 1967 [seizure of power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supersemar "Supersemar") by Lt. General [Suharto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suharto "Suharto") which appointed him to the office of acting president, the stepping down of President [Sukarno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sukarno "Sukarno") and the eventual beginning of the [New Order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Order_\(Indonesia\) "New Order (Indonesia)") era, which, under Suharto's presidency, would last thirty-two years.[^26]

Entering the 1970s, relations between China and the West began to improve, especially after the visit of [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States") President [Richard Nixon to Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1972_visit_by_Richard_Nixon_to_China "1972 visit by Richard Nixon to China") after years of US diplomatic policy that favored the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China "Republic of China") in [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan").[^27] Previously, this policy was also followed by the acceptance of the [People's Republic of China at the UN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_Resolution_2758_\(XXVI\) "United Nations General Assembly Resolution 2758 (XXVI)").[^28] The improving relations between the People's Republic of China and the West certainly influenced global politics at that time, especially Indonesia. In 1985, Indonesia held the 30th anniversary of the Asian-African Conference and invited representatives from countries that had attended the conference since 1955, including China.[^29] Chinese Foreign Minister [Wu Xueqian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wu_Xueqian "Wu Xueqian") was specially invited to attend the event and as a sign of the warming of relations between [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and [Jakarta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jakarta "Jakarta").[^30] China and Indonesia relations began to improve with the reopening of trade relations between the two countries in 1985.[^31]

[Bilateral relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilateral_relations "Bilateral relations") have improved since the 1980s. Foreign Minister [Qian Qichen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qian_Qichen "Qian Qichen") of China met with President Suharto and State Minister Moerdiono of Indonesia in 1989 to discuss the resumption of diplomatic relations of the two countries. In December 1989, the two sides held talks on the technical issues regarding the normalization of bilateral relations and signed the Minutes. Foreign Minister [Ali Alatas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ali_Alatas "Ali Alatas") of Indonesia visited China on invitation on 3 July 1990 and the two sides issued the "Communiqué on the Restoration of Diplomatic Relations between the Two Countries".[^32] Premier [Li Peng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Peng "Li Peng") visited Indonesia on invitation on August 6, 1990. In his talks with President Suharto, the two sides expressed their willingness to improve relations between the two countries on the basis of the Five Principles of Peaceful Co-Existence and the [Ten Principles of the Bandung Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bandung_Conference "Bandung Conference"). On 8 August, Foreign Ministers of China and Indonesia on behalf of their respective governments, signed the Memorandum of Understanding on the Resumption of Diplomatic Relations. The two sides declared the formal resumption of the diplomatic relations between China and Indonesia that day.[^33]

China's cautious response to the [1998 anti-Chinese riots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/May_1998_riots_of_Indonesia "May 1998 riots of Indonesia") caused an uproar among human rights groups. Following protests at the Indonesian embassy in Beijing in August, [Foreign Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_Minister_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Foreign Minister of the People's Republic of China") [Tang Jiaxuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_Jiaxuan "Tang Jiaxuan") made a direct appeal to the Indonesian government to ensure the protection of Chinese Indonesian communities.[^34]

In July 2012, Indonesia and China conducted a bilateral antiterrorism exercise.[^35] In September 2017, two giant pandas, Cai Tao and Hu Chun, arrived in [Jakarta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jakarta "Jakarta") from [Sichuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sichuan "Sichuan") province to be placed in [Taman Safari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taman_Safari "Taman Safari") in [Bogor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bogor "Bogor") as part of the 60th anniversary celebrations of China–Indonesia bilateral relations.[^36]

## Sovereignty and human rights issues

Indonesia follows the [one China principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_principle "One China principle"), and recognizes [government of the People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_China "Government of China") as the sole legal government representing the whole of China and Taiwan as "an inalienable part" of China. Indonesia also supports all efforts by the PRC to "achieve [national reunification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_unification "Chinese unification") ", considers issues related to [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") and [Tibet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region "Tibet Autonomous Region") as internal affairs of China and "firmly supports China’s efforts to maintain development and stability" in Xinjiang and Tibet.[^37]

In December 2018, the issue of China's [human rights abuses against the Uyghur minority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Uyghurs_in_China "Persecution of Uyghurs in China") was brought up in parliament. Indonesian Vice President [Jusuf Kalla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jusuf_Kalla "Jusuf Kalla") said: "we don't want to intervene in the domestic affairs of another country." [^38] In November 2021, the Chinese embassy in Jakarta presented testimonies from Uyghur government and community representatives in [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") as well as those from [Nahdlatul Ulama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nahdlatul_Ulama "Nahdlatul Ulama") to counter Western allegations of abuse.[^39] China has also granted scholarships for [santri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Santri "Santri") to study in the country, leading some of them to issue defenses of its Xinjiang policy in Indonesian media.[^40]

## Trade and investment

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Belt_and_Road_Initiative_participant_map.svg/250px-Belt_and_Road_Initiative_participant_map.svg.png)

Countries which signed cooperation documents related to the Belt and Road Initiative

China and Indonesia are both parties to China-ASEAN free trade agreement and are both parties to the [Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_Comprehensive_Economic_Partnership "Regional Comprehensive Economic Partnership") (RCEP).[^41]<sup><span title="Page: 15">: 15</span> </sup> As of 2025, China and Indonesia do not have a bilateral free trade agreement.[^41]<sup><span title="Page: 15">: 15</span></sup>

Trade between China and Indonesia is on the rise, especially after the implementation of [ACFTA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASEAN%E2%80%93China_Free_Trade_Area "ASEAN–China Free Trade Area") since early 2010. Indeed, while in 2003 trade between Indonesia and China reached only US$3.8 billion, in 2010 it multiplied almost 10 times and reached US$36.1 billion.[^42] China's [transformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reform_and_opening_up "Reform and opening up") into the fastest growing country in the 21st century has led to an increase of foreign investments in the [bamboo network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bamboo_network "Bamboo network"), a network of [overseas Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Overseas_Chinese "Overseas Chinese") businesses operating in the markets of Southeast Asia that share common family and cultural ties.[^43] [^44] [^8] China is Indonesia's largest trading partner. The annual trade between China and Indonesia was $135 billion as of 2024.[^45]

China has remained on top of Indonesia's key major trading partners, serving as the country's largest export and import market. China serves as Indonesia's largest export destination after overtaking Japan and United States, reaching US$16.8 billion.[^46] [^47] [^48] China is also Indonesia's most important source of imports, reaching US$30.8 billion, or 22.7% of Indonesian imports in 2016.[^49] The balance however was in favour of China as Indonesia booked a trade deficit of US$14 billion in 2016.[^50]

In Indonesia, Chinese companies invest in sectors including mining, hydropower, solar panel production, electric vehicle batteries, and industrial silicon.[^51]<sup><span title="Page: 106">: 106</span> </sup> As of 2025, Chinese investment in Indonesia is predominantly mining-related.[^41]<sup><span title="Page: 15">: 15</span> </sup> Illegal mining operations by the Chinese have contributed to pollution in Indonesia.[^52] A number of BRI agreements between the two countries encourage Chinese investment in Indonesia's mining and downstream industries with preferential financing for such ventures.[^41]<sup><span title="Page: 18">: 18</span></sup>

From China's perspective, since 2010 ASEAN as a whole has become its fourth-largest trading partner after the European Union, Japan and the United States. Among ASEAN member countries, Indonesia was China's fourth-largest trading partner, which, according to data as of May 2010 from the Ministry of Commerce of the People's Republic of China, amounted to US$12.4 billion, after [Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia "Malaysia") (US$22.2 billion), [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore "Singapore") (US$17.9 billion) and [Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand "Thailand") (US$15.7 billion).[^42]

In April 2011, Indonesia and China signed a [memorandum of understanding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memorandum_of_understanding "Memorandum of understanding") on the development of industrial technology and Chinese investment in related sectors in Indonesia.[^35]<sup><span title="Page: 160">: 160</span></sup>

Being the second-largest [donor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_foreign_aid "Chinese foreign aid") of [foreign aid to Indonesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_aid_to_Indonesia "Foreign aid to Indonesia") after Singapore, China has also financed and developed multiple infrastructure projects in the country to create more growth in its economy, particularly in the utility, transportation, industry and tourism, with surging inflows of aid in recent years.[^53]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e7/KCIC_400_Series.jpg/250px-KCIC_400_Series.jpg)

KCIC400AF in Halim station, East Jakarta. The Jakarta-Bandung HSR is the first Chinese overseas high-speed rail project as a prestigious example of Belt and Road Initiative

On late September 2015, Indonesia awarded a multibillion-dollar Jakarta-Bandung [high-speed railway project](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-speed_rail_in_Indonesia "High-speed rail in Indonesia") to China.[^54] [^55] [^56] It was said that China's offer to build the Jakarta-Bandung line without requiring loan guarantee nor funding from Indonesia was the tipping point of Jakarta's decision.[^57] [^58] The Jakarta-Bandung high-speed rail was planned to begin its operations to public in 2019.[^59] However, the project was stalled for several years due to land acquisition problem and ensuing [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic"). Jakarta-Bandung High-speed rail officially opened its service on 2 October 2023.[^60]

In late 2021, the president of Indonesia Jokowi Widodo broke ground on a $132 billion U.S. "green" industrial estate to be constructed on Borneo with investments from China and the United Arab Emirates and electrified by a hydropower plant funded by China.[^61]

In 2024, Indonesia imposed tariffs of up to 200 percent on a range of Chinese goods.[^62] In 2024, The Financial Times reported that Indonesia is attempting to decrease Chinese investment in new nickel mining and processing projects. This strategy aims to help Indonesia's industry become eligible for tax incentives in the United States, aligning with the Biden administration's efforts to limit Beijing's impact on the electric vehicle supply chain. The Indonesian government and industry stakeholders are restructuring nickel investment deals to position Chinese companies as minority shareholders.[^63]

On May 25, 2025, Chinese [Premier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Premier_of_China "Premier of China") [Li Qiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Qiang "Li Qiang") met with [President](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Indonesia "President of Indonesia") [Prabowo Subianto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prabowo_Subianto "Prabowo Subianto") to discuss expanding trade relations amid a divisive global market and the [mass installation of tariffs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tariffs_in_the_second_Trump_administration "Tariffs in the second Trump administration") against foreign nations by the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States") under [President](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_the_United_States "President of the United States") [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump "Donald Trump").[^64] The visit in Jakarta, which included 60 prominent Chinese businessmen, was noted by Premier Li as occurring in the same year as the 70th anniversary of the founding of the [Non-Aligned Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement "Non-Aligned Movement").[^64] At the talks, Premier Li encouraged Chinese investment in the Indonesian market.[^64]

## Culture

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e3/Batik_pattern_-_qilin.jpg/250px-Batik_pattern_-_qilin.jpg)

Batik Pesisiran with the image of qilin, demonstrate Chinese-influenced images, testify the centuries-old relations between China and Indonesia.

Since ancient times, [Indonesian culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesian_culture "Indonesian culture") has absorbed many elements of [Chinese culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_culture "Chinese culture"), including [Chinese loanwords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_loan_words_in_Indonesian#From_Chinese "List of loan words in Indonesian") that relate to a variety of topics, including [cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Indonesian_cuisine "Chinese Indonesian cuisine"), and art, seen through the Javanese *[Batik Pesisiran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batik#Pesisir_Batik_\(Coastal_Batik\) "Batik")* (coastal batik), which depicts Chinese images such as the cloud, [phoenix](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenix_\(mythology\) "Phoenix (mythology)"), [dragon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dragon "Dragon"), qilin, to [peony](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peony "Peony") flower.Chinese people began to settle in Indonesian coastal cities as early as [Srivijaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Srivijaya "Srivijaya") (c. 7th century) and [Majapahit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Majapahit "Majapahit") (c. 14th century) period. And later accelerated during [Dutch East Indies Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_East_Indies_Company "Dutch East Indies Company") era (c. 17th century). Chinese immigrants brought with them Chinese culture of their homeland, intermarrying with local women and creating a hybrid *[peranakan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peranakan "Peranakan")* culture, still observable today in some of Indonesian cities and in neighboring [Malaysia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malaysia "Malaysia") and [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore "Singapore"). Today, there are around 2,832,510 [Chinese Indonesians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Indonesian "Chinese Indonesian"), according to the 2010 census, forming 1.2% of the Indonesian population.

## Tiongkok

***Tiongkok*** (中国) is the [Indonesian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesian_language "Indonesian language") [term for China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Names_of_China "Names of China"), originating from the [Min Nan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Min_Nan "Min Nan") (the local dialect of Southern [Fujian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fujian "Fujian")) version of the word *Zhongguo* (中国), which is the term used to refer to China in [Mandarin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandarin_Chinese "Mandarin Chinese"). The word—in its romanized form (*Tiongkok*)—was used in [Indonesian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesian_language "Indonesian language") by the [Indonesian government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics_of_Indonesia "Politics of Indonesia") to refer to [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") up until 1972,[^68] but its use ceased following a period of hostile relations in the 1960s. The authoritarian, anti-Chinese [New Order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Order_\(Indonesia\) "New Order (Indonesia)") government mandated the replacement of the term *Tiongkok*, as well as *Tionghoa* (中华), with *"Cina"*. Many [Chinese Indonesians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Indonesian "Chinese Indonesian") felt that the term (in reference to them) was derogatory and racist, connoting "backwardness, humiliation, queues and bound feet".[^69] After the fall of [President](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Indonesia "President of Indonesia") [Suharto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suharto "Suharto") in 1998, the use of *Tiongkok* has seen a re-emergence.[^70]

On 14 March 2014, Indonesian President [Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Susilo_Bambang_Yudhoyono "Susilo Bambang Yudhoyono") signed Presidential Decree (*Keputusan Presiden*) No. 12/2014 to change the legal use of the Indonesian-language term to refer to China. Changes included replacing the term *Cina* or *China* to *Tiongkok* to refer to China as a country, and *Tionghoa* to refer to Chinese people, or Chinese descents. This change was meant to eradicate discrimination and prejudice towards Chinese Indonesians.[^71]

## South China Sea

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5d/South_China_Sea_claims_map.svg/250px-South_China_Sea_claims_map.svg.png)

Territorial claims in the South China Sea

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e6/An_Indonesian_F-16_jet_fighter_flying_over_a_Chinese_Navy_Type_903_replenishment_ship_off_the_coast_of_Natuna_Islands_2020.jpg/250px-An_Indonesian_F-16_jet_fighter_flying_over_a_Chinese_Navy_Type_903_replenishment_ship_off_the_coast_of_Natuna_Islands_2020.jpg)

An Indonesian Air Force F-16 Fighting Falcon flying over what appeared to be a Chinese Navy Type 903 replenishment ship on the disputed region off the coast of Natuna, Riau Islands

Despite Indonesia's position as a non-claimant state in the South China Sea dispute,[^72] two countries were inevitably involved in this territorial dispute; as parts of China's [unilaterally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unilateralism "Unilateralism") claimed [nine-dash line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nine-dash_line "Nine-dash line") is intersecting with Indonesia's [exclusive economic zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone "Exclusive economic zone") near [Natuna islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natuna_islands "Natuna islands").[^73]

In 1932, China sent a [Note Verbale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_correspondence "Diplomatic correspondence") to France, declaring that China's southernmost territory was the Paracels for the first time.[^74] [^75] [^76] In 1943, China again reiterated through its government-published book, China Handbook (1937–1943), that its southernmost territory was the Paracels, specifically Triton Island.[^77] In 1947, China revised its Handbook, stating that its southernmost territory was the Spratlys. In its revised 1947 Handbook and acknowledging that other states have claims to the Spratleys.[^78] During the same year, it began using a map with a dash-line to claim almost the entire sea, including the waters of the Natuna islands.[^79]

In March 2016, the two countries were involved in confrontation near Natuna Islands as Indonesian maritime authority that tried to capture a Chinese trawler accused for [illegal fishing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illegal_fishing "Illegal fishing") was prevented by Chinese coast guard.[^80] Indonesia insists to prosecute Chinese trawler crew, despite Beijing's demand to release them. An Indonesian official said that the "traditional fishing grounds" was not recognised under the UNCLOS. This incident prompted Indonesian military to increase its presence in Natuna area.[^81] On 23 June 2016, Indonesian President [Joko Widodo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joko_Widodo "Joko Widodo") visited Natuna islands on a warship, this was meant to send a "clear message" that Indonesia was "very serious in its effort to protect its sovereignty".[^82]

Following the [Permanent Court of Arbitration ruling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines_v._China "Philippines v. China") on 12 July 2016, Indonesia called on all parties involved in the territorial dispute to exercise self-restraint and to respect [applicable international laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNCLOS "UNCLOS").[^83] The international ruling invalidated the dash-line utilized by China to claim the waters of the Natuna islands.[^84] [^85] Of the 556 vessels Indonesia had destroyed from October 2014 to 2019 for violating rules, 3 were from China while 9 other Chinese vessels escaped Indonesian custody. The amount of Chinese vessels destroyed was relatively small compared to vessels from countries like Vietnam (312), Philippines (91), and Malaysia (87).[^86]

In late 2019, China intensified its military activity in the waters of Indonesia's Natuna islands, which China claims as its territory.[^87] In January 2020, Chinese fishing boats, escorted by Chinese coast guard vessels, once again conducted fishing off the coast of northern islands of Natuna in waters claimed by Indonesia as its [exclusive economic zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone "Exclusive economic zone") (EEZ).[^88] This led to a stand-off between the countries and Indonesia's decision to send its fishermen to join warships in the area to help defend against Chinese vessels.[^89] Indonesia had deployed two *[Kapitan Pattimura-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parchim-class_corvette "Parchim-class corvette")* anti-submarine corvettes at Great Natuna Island in early January, and later added one *[Martadinata-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martadinata-class_frigate "Martadinata-class frigate")* guided-missile frigate, two *[Bung Tomo-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bung_Tomo-class_corvette "Bung Tomo-class corvette")* corvettes, one *[Ahmed Yani-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmad_Yani-class_frigate "Ahmad Yani-class frigate")* frigate, one *[Makassar-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Makassar-class_landing_platform_dock "Makassar-class landing platform dock")* landing platform dock (LPD), one *[Cakra-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cakra-class_submarines "Cakra-class submarines")* diesel submarine and four [F-16](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Dynamics_F-16_Fighting_Falcon "General Dynamics F-16 Fighting Falcon") C/D fighter jets to the deployment even when China vessels appeared to have backed down from the region.[^90] Indonesia responded by invoking the [South China Sea Arbitration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea_Arbitration "South China Sea Arbitration") ruling.[^91]

In December 2021, China demanded Indonesia to stop its oil exploration activities in the waters of the Natuna islands, which China claims on the basis of the nine-dash line.[^92] China sent [coast guard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Coast_Guard "China Coast Guard") vessels to the area.[^93] [^94] Indonesia responded by deploying its navy to the area and completed its exploratory drilling.[^95] In October 2024, China sent its coast guard to an area where Indonesia was conducting exploratory drilling.[^96] Indonesia's navy deployed to protect the drilling, which was completed.[^95]

During a November 2024 state visit by Indonesian President [Probowo Subianto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prabowo_Subianto "Prabowo Subianto") to China, Indonesia and China signed a memorandum of understanding for "joint maritime development" in the area of the two countries "overlapping claims" near the Natuna Islands.[^97] Indonesia's Foreign Ministry subsequently issued a statement that the memorandum did not impact Indonesia's sovereignty or rights in the area and stating that in Indonesia's view the Chinese claims do not have a legal basis.[^98] [^97] Critics of the memorandum quoted by [Voice of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voice_of_America "Voice of America") and *[South China Morning Post](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Morning_Post "South China Morning Post")* contended that the wording could support China's position regarding the South China Sea claims.[^98] [^97]

## Espionage

In January 2021, Indonesian fishermen reported findings of underwater drones or gliders. This finding is thought to be related to a glider belonging to China that was previously found by fishermen around the waters of [Selayar Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Selayar_Island "Selayar Island"), [South Sulawesi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Sulawesi "South Sulawesi"). Foreign media have highlighted these findings. Most of them call this incident a secret Chinese mission in Indonesian waters. *[The Guardian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Guardian "The Guardian")*, in its Espionage rubric, details previously that a Chinese-made glider was also found by fishermen in [Tanjung Pinang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanjung_Pinang "Tanjung Pinang"), [Riau Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riau_Islands "Riau Islands") in March 2019. Furthermore, the finding also occurred in [Masalembo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Masalembu_Islands "Masalembu Islands"), [East Java](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Java "East Java") in January 2020.[^99]

## Public opinion

According to a 2014 BBC World Service Poll, 52% of Indonesians expressed a mainly positive view of China compared to 28% expressing a mainly negative view.[^100] A Pew Research Center poll in 2019 found that 36% of Indonesians expressed a favourable view of China while 36% expressed an unfavourable view.[^101] [^102] According to a December 2021 survey from the Australian [Lowy Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lowy_Institute "Lowy Institute") think tank, Indonesians were becoming increasingly wary of China and Chinese investments, with six in 10 of the 3,000 respondents agreeing that Jakarta should join with other nations to limit Beijing's rise. Only 43% of Indonesians polled said they felt [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") ’s growth was good for Indonesia – down from 54% in 2011.[^103] [^104] According to a [Morning Consult](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morning_Consult "Morning Consult") poll conducted in July 2022, 46% of Indonesians viewed China favourably while 18% viewed the country unfavourably.[^105] A survey published in 2025 by the [Pew Research Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pew_Research_Center "Pew Research Center") found that 65% of Indonesian people had a favorable view of China, while 32% had an unfavorable view.[^106]
