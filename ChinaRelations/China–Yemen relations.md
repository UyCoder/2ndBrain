**China–Yemen relations** refer to the bilateral relations of the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") (PRC) and [Yemen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemen "Yemen").[^1] The two countries established formal diplomatic relations in 1956 and signed a treaty of friendship in 1958, with an agreement to cooperate in commercial, technical and cultural development.

## History

Yemen established formal diplomatic relations with the PRC on 24 September 1956 and was the third Arab country to do so.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 162">: 162</span> </sup> China and Yemen also entered into trade agreements and cultural agreements at that time.[^3]<sup><span title="Page: xxiv">: xxiv</span></sup>

In a 1958 agreement, China issued Yemen an interest-free loan of 70 million Swiss francs with which Yemen could purchase supplies from China. At the same time, the city of [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") provided an interest-free loan of $16.3 million to help fund development projects in Yemen.[^4] Another treaty of friendship was signed on June 9, 1964, along with additional agreements of cooperation in economic, technical and cultural development. China provided support in building factories and roads, and Beijing provided Yemen another interest-free loan, in the amount of $500,000.[^5]

In 1969, China opened its embassy in [South Yemen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Yemen "South Yemen").[^3]<sup><span title="Page: xxvii">: xxvii</span> </sup> Seeking to deepen its relations with South Yemen, China supported the [Popular Front for the Liberation of the Occupied Arabian Gulf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular_Front_for_the_Liberation_of_the_Occupied_Arabian_Gulf "Popular Front for the Liberation of the Occupied Arabian Gulf") (PFLOAG), which China viewed as the proteges of the [National Liberation Front](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Liberation_Front_\(South_Yemen\) "National Liberation Front (South Yemen)").[^3]<sup><span title="Pages: 58–59">: 58–59</span></sup>

As part of its strategy of countering Soviet influence in the region, [China provided aid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_foreign_aid "Chinese foreign aid") to both South Yemen and [North Yemen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemen_Arab_Republic "Yemen Arab Republic").[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 162">: 162</span></sup>

From 2000 through 2014, Yemen was the top recipient of [Chinese foreign aid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_foreign_aid "Chinese foreign aid") in the Middle East, receiving approximately US$282 million during that period.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 170">: 170</span></sup>

Yemen participates in the [China-Arab States Cooperation Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Arab_States_Cooperation_Forum "China–Arab States Cooperation Forum") (CASCF).[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 58">: 58</span></sup>

When the [Yemeni civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemeni_civil_war_\(2014%E2%80%93present\) "Yemeni civil war (2014–present)") began in 2015, China evacuated 600 Chinese from Yemen.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 43">: 43</span> </sup> This event contributed to Chinese concerns about regional stability.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 257">: 257</span> </sup> Along with the earlier evacuation of Chinese citizens from Libya, it contributed to the decision to develop the [People's Liberation Army Support Base in Djibouti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army_Support_Base_in_Djibouti "People's Liberation Army Support Base in Djibouti") to facilitate future evacuations from conflicts in the region.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 232">: 232</span></sup>

Discussions in CASCF regarding Yemen increased following the outbreak of the Civil War, with the 2016 CASCF declaration calling for Yemen's unity and territorial integrity and opposing foreign interference in Yemen's internal affairs.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 65">: 65</span> </sup> In 2018, CASCF emphasized the humanitarian crisis in Yemen and called for increased humanitarian and medical assistance for the Yemeni people.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 65">: 65</span></sup>

In 2016, Yemen expressed support for China's position on [territorial disputes in the South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Territorial_disputes_in_the_South_China_Sea "Territorial disputes in the South China Sea").[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 71">: 71</span></sup>

China provided US$22.5 million in relief to the Yemeni government in 2017.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 163">: 163</span> </sup> Between 2017 and 2020, China provided more than 11,700 tons of food.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 163">: 163</span></sup>

In June 2020, Yemen was one of 53 countries that backed the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law") at the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations").[^7]

Chinese diplomatic discourses generally avoid criticizing the Houthis by name.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 164">: 164</span> </sup> China typically makes its criticisms of the Houthi movement via indirect statements, which condemn actions without specifically naming the party responsible.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 164">: 164</span> </sup> During the [Houthi attacks on commercial vessels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Houthi_attacks_on_commercial_vessels "Houthi attacks on commercial vessels") in response to the [Gaza War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_war "Gaza war"), China stated that the [Houthis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Houthis "Houthis") should respect the navigation rights of the merchant ships of all countries in the Red Sea in accordance with international law and "immediately cease their acts of harassment." [^2]<sup><span title="Page: 141">: 141</span> </sup> China requested that [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Iran_relations "China–Iran relations") use its influence with the Houthi movement in attempt to restrain Houthi attacks on civilian ships.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 141">: 141</span> </sup> The U.S. government accused China of assisting the Houthis by providing them with [geospatial intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geospatial_intelligence "Geospatial intelligence") for ship targeting via the [Chang Guang Satellite Technology Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chang_Guang_Satellite_Technology_Corporation "Chang Guang Satellite Technology Corporation").[^8] [^9] [^10]

After the [2024 floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Yemen_floods "2024 Yemen floods") in [Hadramaut Governorate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadhramaut_Governorate "Hadhramaut Governorate"), China provided medical and relief materials including ventilators, defibrillators, x-ray equipment, and more.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 163">: 163</span></sup>

Effective 1 December 2024, China eliminated tariffs for goods imported from all of the countries that the United Nations categorizes as [least developed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Least_developed_countries "Least developed countries") and with which China has diplomatic relations, including Yemen.[^11]

## Economic relations

[Chinese state-owned enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State-owned_enterprises_of_China "State-owned enterprises of China") in the energy sector, [CNPC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_National_Petroleum_Corporation "China National Petroleum Corporation"), [SINOPEC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinopec "Sinopec"), and [CNOOC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_National_Offshore_Oil_Corporation "China National Offshore Oil Corporation") have operations in Yemen.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 163">: 163</span></sup>

[SINOHYDRO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinohydro "Sinohydro") has completed projects in Yemen.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 165">: 165</span></sup>

Yemen has announced the establishment of a direct shipping line between China and the port of [Hodeidah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hodeidah "Hodeidah"). The sea route, operated by a Chinese company, connects three major Chinese ports, including [Qingdao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qingdao "Qingdao"), to the port of Hodeidah. The project aims to rebuild port infrastructure and strengthen trade relations between the two countries. The shipping line will provide comprehensive logistics services from the manufacturing plants in China to the destination in Yemen. According to analysts, this development could reduce Yemen's dependence on alternative ports and increase the country's trade revenues.[^12]
