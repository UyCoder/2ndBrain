**China–Laos relations** ([Lao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lao_language "Lao language"): ສາຍພົວພັນ ລາວ-ຈີນ, [simplified Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters "Simplified Chinese characters"): 中老关系; [traditional Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters "Traditional Chinese characters"): 中寮關係) refers to the [current and historical relationship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilateralism "Bilateralism") between [Lao People's Democratic Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lao_People%27s_Democratic_Republic "Lao People's Democratic Republic") and the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China").

## History

The [Lao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lao_people "Lao people") kingdom of [Lan Xang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lan_Xang "Lan Xang") and its successor states were [tributaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tributary_system_of_China "Tributary system of China") of [Ming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty") and later [Qing China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty"). In the late 15th century, the Chinese [backed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i_Vi%E1%BB%87t%E2%80%93Lan_Xang_War_\(1479%E2%80%931484\) "Đại Việt–Lan Xang War (1479–1484)") Lan Xang against their common rival, the [Vietnamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i_Vi%E1%BB%87t "Đại Việt"). Chinese traders operated in Lan Xang like any other [Southeast Asian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia") country, however, Lan Xang also proved to be important as a participant in the [Tea-Horse Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tea_Horse_Road "Tea Horse Road") trade.

### Modern times

Relations between the two states were re-established in 1953 with the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") (Nationalist China or Taiwan) as the sole legitimate government of China. On 25 April 1961, Laos switched recognition to the PRC government in Beijing.[^1] However, on 16 May 1962, the royal government severed diplomatic relations with the PRC and restored relations with the Republic of China. Recognition was changed again in 1975 when the new Lao communist government re-established relations with the PRC.[^2]

Vietnam's invasion of [Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambodia "Cambodia") in December 1978 to unseat the [Khmer Rouge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khmer_Rouge "Khmer Rouge") regime provoked China into a limited invasion of Vietnam—approximately nineteen kilometers deep—to "teach Vietnam a lesson." Laos was caught in a dangerous bind, not wanting to further provoke China, but not able to oppose its special partner, Vietnam. The Laotian leadership survived the dilemma by making slightly delayed pronouncements in support of Vietnam after some intraparty debate and by sharply reducing [diplomatic relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_relations "Diplomatic relations") with China to the [chargé d'affaires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charg%C3%A9_d%27affaires "Chargé d'affaires") level—without a full break. The low point in China-Laotian relations came in 1979, with reports of Chinese assistance and training of [Hmong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hmong_people "Hmong people") resistance forces under General [Vang Pao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vang_Pao "Vang Pao") in China's [Yunnan Province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yunnan_Province "Yunnan Province").[^3]

This hostile relationship gradually softened, however, and in 1989 Diplomatic and party-to-party relations were normalized, and Prime Minister [Kaysone Phomvihane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaysone_Phomvihane "Kaysone Phomvihane") paid a state visit to [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing"). In 1991 Kaysone chose to spend his vacation in China rather than make his customary visit to the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union"). Trade expanded from the local sale of consumer goods to the granting of eleven investment licenses in 1991—including an automotive assembly plant. Following the establishment of the Laotian-Chinese Joint Border Committee in 1991, meetings held during 1992 resulted in an agreement delineating their common border. China's commercial investments and trade with Laos expanded quietly, but not dramatically, in 1993 and 1994.[^3]

In November 2010, Laos and China signed a security cooperation agreement.[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 159">: 159</span> </sup> [CCP general secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") held talks with [LPRP general secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Lao_People%27s_Revolutionary_Party "General Secretary of the Lao People's Revolutionary Party") [Bounnhang Vorachit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bounnhang_Vorachit "Bounnhang Vorachit") in 2016, seeking further coordination in international affairs.[^5] In June 2020, Laos was one of 53 countries that backed the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law") at the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations").[^6] In December 2025, chairman of the National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference [Wang Huning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Huning "Wang Huning") visited Laos to attended celebrations for the 50th anniversary of the Lao People's Democratic Republic, where he met with LPRP general secretary Thongloun Sisoulith.[^7]

## Economy

Relations have consisted of [trade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade "Trade") and [aid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aid "Aid"), largely focused on road construction in the northern provinces of Laos, without directly challenging the interests of [Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand "Thailand") or [Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam "Vietnam") in the central and southern regions. China remains Laos' largest creditor, accounting for approximately half of Laos' government debt as of 2023.[^8]

The [Golden Triangle Special Economic Zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Triangle_Special_Economic_Zone "Golden Triangle Special Economic Zone") is a significant point of economic contact between China and Laos.[^9]<sup><span title="Page: 103">: 103</span> </sup> The Chinese firm Kings Romans group was granted a 99-year lease to develop the [SEZ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_economic_zone "Special economic zone") into a gambling and tourist destination.[^9]<sup><span title="Page: 103">: 103</span></sup>

Effective 1 December 2024, China eliminated [tariffs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tariff "Tariff") for goods imported from all of the countries that the United Nations categorizes as [least developed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Least_developed_countries "Least developed countries") and with which China has diplomatic relations, including Laos.[^10]

### Belt and Road Initiative

In 2015, Laos joined the People's Republic of China global infrastructure project the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative") (BRI). During a 2017 visit to Vientiane by Xi, the two countries announced the Laos-China Economic Corridor.[^11]<sup><span title="Page: 129">: 129</span> </sup> The most important BRI project in Laos is the [Vientiane-Boten Railway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vientiane-Boten_railway "Vientiane-Boten railway") which was completed in 2021.[^12]<sup><span title="Page: 212">: 212</span> </sup> It runs from the capital of Laos to the Chinese border.[^12]<sup><span title="Page: 212">: 212</span> </sup> It is part of the broader China-Laos Railway, which the two countries operate as a joint venture through the Laos-China Railway Company.[^11]<sup><span title="Page: 129">: 129</span> </sup> Like China, Laos is a major supporter of the [Pan-Asian Railway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pan-Asian_railway "Pan-Asian railway"), given its desire to move from a land-locked economy to a land-linked economy.[^13]<sup><span title="Pages: 50–51">: 50–51</span></sup>

## Political relations

The shared ideology of communism helps facilitate cooperation between Laos and China.[^12]<sup><span title="Page: 211">: 211</span></sup>

Laos follows the [one China principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_principle "One China principle") and considers Taiwan to be "an inalienable part" of China. Laos also supports all efforts by the PRC to "achieve [national reunification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_unification "Chinese unification") " and opposes [Taiwan independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan_independence_movement "Taiwan independence movement").[^14] Within [ASEAN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASEAN "ASEAN"), Laos is often supportive of China, particularly in decreasing the possibility of an ASEAN consensus for taking a harsher stance against China on [territorial disputes in the South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Territorial_disputes_in_the_South_China_Sea "Territorial disputes in the South China Sea").[^12]<sup><span title="Page: 211">: 211</span></sup>
