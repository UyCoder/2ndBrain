**China–Kuwait relations** refers to the [bilateral relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilateral_relations "Bilateral relations") between the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") (PRC) and the [State of Kuwait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuwait "Kuwait").

## History

The two countries established diplomatic relations on March 22, 1971.[^1] [^2]<sup><span title="Page: 101">: 101</span> </sup> Kuwait was the first Gulf country to recognize the PRC.[^3] In 1990, China voted for every [United Nations Security Council resolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_resolution "United Nations Security Council resolution") condemning the [Iraqi invasion of Kuwait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraqi_invasion_of_Kuwait "Iraqi invasion of Kuwait"),[^4] but abstained one put foreign troops on the ground due to its non-interference policy. Kuwait was the first Gulf country to sign a military cooperation deal with China in 1995. In the 1990s, Kuwait bought $186 million worth of Chinese arms.[^3] Kuwait has supported China on political issues such as Western sanctions over Tiananmen, [Hong Kong National Security Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law"), and China's policies in [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang").[^3]

In 2018, China and Kuwait signed a strategic partnership.[^5]<sup><span title="Page: 211">: 211</span> </sup> In July 2018, the [State Administration for Science, Technology and Industry for National Defence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Administration_for_Science,_Technology_and_Industry_for_National_Defence "State Administration for Science, Technology and Industry for National Defence") signed an agreement with the [Government of Kuwait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_Kuwait "Government of Kuwait") to increase cooperation in the [defense industry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defense_industry "Defense industry").[^6] China also agreed to increase investment in the Kuwaiti energy sector.[^6] In July 2019, UN ambassadors of 37 countries, including Kuwait, have signed a joint letter to the [UNHRC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNHRC "UNHRC") defending China's [treatment of Uyghurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Uyghurs_in_China "Persecution of Uyghurs in China") in the [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") region.[^7] In June 2020, Kuwait was one of 53 countries backed the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law") at the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations").[^8] On March 22, 2021, [General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") and Kuwaiti Emir [Nawaf Al-Ahmad Al-Jaber Al-Sabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nawaf_Al-Ahmad_Al-Jaber_Al-Sabah "Nawaf Al-Ahmad Al-Jaber Al-Sabah") exchanged congratulatory messages on the 50th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between China and Kuwait. [Chinese Premier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Premier_of_China "Premier of China") [Li Keqiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Keqiang "Li Keqiang") and Kuwaiti Prime Minister [Sabah Al-Ahmad Al-Jaber Al-Sabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sabah_Al-Ahmad_Al-Jaber_Al-Sabah "Sabah Al-Ahmad Al-Jaber Al-Sabah") exchanged congratulatory messages.[^9]

## Economic relations

The governments of China and Kuwait have signed a trade agreement, an agreement on the encouragement and protection of investment, an agreement on the establishment of a joint economic and trade committee, and an agreement on the avoidance of double taxation. Since 1955, the two countries have started direct private trade. In 2008, the trade volume between China and Kuwait was US$6.784 billion, of which China exported US$1.744 billion and imported US$5.04 billion. China's main exports are mechanical and electrical products, high-tech products, textiles, etc., and its main imports are crude oil and petrochemical products.[^10] As of 2022, Kuwait ran a trade deficit with China.[^3]

[Kuwait Investment Authority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuwait_Investment_Authority "Kuwait Investment Authority") (KIA) bought a $720 million stake in [Industrial and Commercial Bank of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_and_Commercial_Bank_of_China "Industrial and Commercial Bank of China") in 2006. By 2011, KIA had around $5.6 billion invested in Chinese companies, including the [Agricultural Bank of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agricultural_Bank_of_China "Agricultural Bank of China") and [CITIC Securities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CITIC_Securities "CITIC Securities").[^3] Since 1982, the [Kuwait Fund for Arab Economic Development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuwait_Fund_for_Arab_Economic_Development "Kuwait Fund for Arab Economic Development") has lent nearly $1 billion to China to finance provincial infrastructure projects.[^3]
