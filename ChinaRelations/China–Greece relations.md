The [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") (PRC) and [Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greece "Greece") established official diplomatic relations in 1972. The PRC has an embassy in [Athens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Athens "Athens"). Greece has an embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and three general consulates in [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou"), [Hong-Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong-Kong "Hong-Kong") and since 2005 in [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai"). The two countries formed a strategic partnership in 2006.

In the early years of the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War"), Greece, like most other Western European countries, recognized the [Chinese Nationalist Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuomintang "Kuomintang") of [Chiang Kai-shek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek "Chiang Kai-shek") as being the legitimate governing authority of China, despite Chiang only controlling a [rump state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rump_state "Rump state") on [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan"). Greek forces fought against Chinese forces during the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War").

In June 1972, in the aftermath of [Richard Nixon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Nixon "Richard Nixon") 's visit to [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and the People's Republic of China's admission to the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations"), Greece switched recognition to the People's Republic, cutting off relations with Taiwan.[^1] Today, Taiwan maintains a "Taipei Representative Office in Athens", which is active in organizing various events and making statements to the Greek media; however, Greece adheres to a [One China policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_policy "One China policy"), and Taiwanese officials in Athens do not enjoy diplomatic or consular status.[^2]

In the 1980s, bilateral relations were strengthened when Greek Prime Minister [Andreas Papandreou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andreas_Papandreou "Andreas Papandreou") sought allies beyond Europe and the US in his policy of confronting [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") over the [Cyprus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyprus_dispute "Cyprus dispute") and [Aegean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_dispute "Aegean dispute") disputes, and courted China under [Deng Xiaoping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping "Deng Xiaoping"). Greek shipowners also played an important role, by ordering many of their ships to be built in Chinese shipyards beginning in the 1980s, instead of British, German and Japanese shipyards (as had been the case since the late 19th century).[^1]

The [Port of Piraeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port_of_Piraeus "Port of Piraeus") (under Chinese management since 2009 and majority Chinese ownership since 2016) is important from a geostrategic view for China, as it helps China's transactions with the whole of Europe.

## History of bilateral relations

### Ancient

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/Sampul_tapestry_%28complete%29.jpg/120px-Sampul_tapestry_%28complete%29.jpg)

Part of the Sampul tapestry with Hellenistic features. Now in the Xinjiang Museum

Ancient Chinese people had contact with the [Indo-Greeks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Greeks "Indo-Greeks"). [Dayuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dayuan "Dayuan") (meaning "Great [Ionians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionians "Ionians") "), was described in the [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_literature "Chinese literature") historical works of *[Records of the Grand Historian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Records_of_the_Grand_Historian "Records of the Grand Historian")* and the *[Book of Han](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_Han "Book of Han")*. It is mentioned in the accounts of the famous Chinese [explorer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Explorer "Explorer") [Zhang Qian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhang_Qian "Zhang Qian") in 130 BCE and the numerous embassies that followed him into Central Asia. The country of Dayuan is generally accepted as relating to the [Ferghana Valley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferghana_Valley "Ferghana Valley"), and its Greek city [Alexandria Eschate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexandria_Eschate "Alexandria Eschate"). These Chinese accounts describe the Dayuan as urbanized dwellers with [Caucasian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasian_race "Caucasian race") features, living in walled cities and having "customs identical to those of the [Greco-Bactrians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greco-Bactrians "Greco-Bactrians") ". [Strabo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strabo "Strabo") writes that Bactrian Greeks "extended their empire even as far as the [Seres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serica "Serica") (Chinese) and the [Phryni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phryni "Phryni") ".[^3] The [War of the Heavenly Horses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_the_Heavenly_Horses "War of the Heavenly Horses") (104–101 BC) was a war between Dayuan and the [Han dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty"). The [Sampul tapestry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sampul_tapestry "Sampul tapestry") found at the [Tarim Basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarim_Basin "Tarim Basin") settlement of [Sampul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sampul "Sampul") in [Lop County](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lop_County "Lop County"), [Hotan Prefecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hotan_Prefecture "Hotan Prefecture"), [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang"), has many [Hellenistic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenistic "Hellenistic") features.

Following the [ancient Roman embassies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Roman_relations "Sino-Roman relations") to China recorded in [ancient Chinese histories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twenty-four_Histories "Twenty-four Histories"), there appear to have been contacts between the [Byzantine Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire "Byzantine Empire") and several [dynasties of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynasties_of_China "Dynasties of China"), beginning with the [Tang dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_dynasty "Tang dynasty") (618–907 AD).[^4] [Theophylact Simocatta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theophylact_Simocatta "Theophylact Simocatta") wrote a generally accurate depiction of the [reunification of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_China#Mid-imperial_China "History of China") by [Emperor Wen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emperor_Wen_of_Sui "Emperor Wen of Sui") (r. 581–604 AD) of the [Sui dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sui_dynasty "Sui dynasty"), with the conquest of the rival [Chen dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chen_dynasty "Chen dynasty") in [southern China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_and_Southern_dynasties "Northern and Southern dynasties"), correctly placing these events within the reign period of Byzantine ruler [Maurice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurice_\(emperor\) "Maurice (emperor)").[^5] From Chinese records it is known that [Michael VII Doukas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_VII_Doukas "Michael VII Doukas") (Mie li yi ling kai sa 滅力伊靈改撒) of *Fu lin* (拂菻; i.e. [Byzantium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantium "Byzantium")) dispatched a diplomatic mission to China that eventually arrived in 1081, during the reign of [Emperor Shenzong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emperor_Shenzong_of_Song "Emperor Shenzong of Song") of the [Song dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Song_dynasty "Song dynasty") (960–1279 AD), centuries before [Marco Polo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marco_Polo "Marco Polo") 's expedition.[^6] [Kublai Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kublai_Khan "Kublai Khan"), the [Mongol-ruler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_Empire "Mongol Empire") who founded the [Yuan dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuan_dynasty "Yuan dynasty") (1271–1368 AD) of China not only maintained correspondence with the Byzantine Greeks but hosted some of them at his court in [Khanbaliq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanbaliq "Khanbaliq") (modern [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing")). The *[History of Yuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Yuan "History of Yuan")* (chapter 134) records that a certain Ai-sie (transliteration of either Joshua or Joseph) from the country of Fu lin (i.e. the Byzantine Empire), initially in the service of [Güyük Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BCy%C3%BCk_Khan "Güyük Khan"), was well-versed in Western languages and had expertise in the fields of [Greek medicine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_medicine "Byzantine medicine") and [astronomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_astronomy "Byzantine astronomy") that convinced Kublai Khan to offer him a position as the director of medical and astronomical boards.[^7] Kublai Khan eventually honored Ai-sie with the [noble title](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_nobility "Chinese nobility") of Prince of Fu lin ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 拂菻王; *Fú lǐn wáng*).[^7] In his biography within the *History of Yuan* his children are mentioned by their [Chinese names](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_name "Chinese name"), which bear similarities to the [Christian names](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_name "Christian name") Elias (*Ye-li-ah*), Luke (*Lu-ko*), and Antony (*An-tun*), with a daughter named *A-na-si-sz*.[^7]

Τhe Sino-Hellenic contacts since Hellenistic times has recently been reinforced by the interdisciplinary study of prestige gold provided which a new interpretive framework for understanding trans-cultural contact between Han China and the Hellenistic world. The contextual analysis of the gold artefacts with foreign features presented in the current paper shows that the quest for exotica along with the desire for “heavenly horses” among the ruling elites acted as the driving force that led to an unprecedented extent of imperial expansion of the Han court in Central Asia, as well as the establishment of a vast trading network during the first century BCE.[^8] Today these ancient relations are unfolded and strengthened with the Sino-Hellenic Academic Project.[^9]

### Modern

#### Korean War

During the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War") the two countries were enemies and their forces fought each other, notably at the [Battle of Chosin Reservoir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Chosin_Reservoir "Battle of Chosin Reservoir"). The [Greek Expeditionary Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_Expeditionary_Force_\(Korea\) "Greek Expeditionary Force (Korea)") was part of the [United Nations Command](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Command "United Nations Command"), which fought against the PRC's [People's Volunteer Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Volunteer_Army "People's Volunteer Army") and North Korean forces.[^10]

#### Greek shipowners during the Cold War

Although China and Greece were enemies in the early years of the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War"), with their militaries directly confronting each other in the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War"), many Greek shipowners greatly helped the Chinese Communist regime of [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong "Mao Zedong") during these same years by breaking the trade embargo imposed on China by most Western countries (Greece among them) and secretly carrying cargoes to Chinese ports. During the Korean War, [freight rates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freight_rate "Freight rate") rocketed as demand for supplies reached enormous heights. Greek shipowners made their tonnage available to Mao's government, in a win-win situation, as Mao successfully broke the embargo and secured vital supplies while the shipowners enjoyed excellent profits.[^11]

The most prominent example was that of [Aristotle Onassis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aristotle_Onassis "Aristotle Onassis"), who used American-made ships (the [T2 Tankers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T2_Tanker "T2 Tanker")) of his private fleet (then the largest privately owned fleet in the world) flying the [Honduran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honduras "Honduras"), [Panamanian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panama "Panama") and [Liberian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberia "Liberia") flags to transport cargoes to Chinese ports. In a similar operation coordinated with the Chinese Communist intelligence services, Onassis used his tankers to transport [petroleum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petroleum "Petroleum") to China, in ships flying the [Saudi flag](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia").[^11]

#### Andreas Papandreou and Deng Xiaoping

[Andreas Papandreou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andreas_Papandreou "Andreas Papandreou"), who came to power in 1981, aggressively courted China as part of his policy of finding allies beyond Greece's traditional partners in Western Europe and the US to confront [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") over the [Cyprus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyprus_dispute "Cyprus dispute") and [Aegean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aegean_dispute "Aegean dispute") disputes. His April 1986 visit to [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing"), followed by the visit to [Athens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Athens "Athens") of Chinese Premier [Zhao Ziyang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhao_Ziyang "Zhao Ziyang") (the first such visit by a Chinese head of government), were important milestones in developing bilateral relations. Although Papandreou was able to earn diplomatic support from Chinese paramount leader [Deng Xiaoping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping "Deng Xiaoping") on some of his initiatives, the more long-lasting impact of this visit was felt in the economic and commercial fields, especially in [shipping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shipping_industry "Shipping industry").[^1]

#### European Union era

China and Greece raised their bilateral relations to a [strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "Diplomatic partnerships of China") in 2006.[^12]<sup><span title="Page: 161">: 161</span></sup>

Since 2010, China has strengthened its relationship with Greece, following the increased strain in the European Union-Greece relationship after [Greece's bailout](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Economic_Adjustment_Programme_for_Greece "First Economic Adjustment Programme for Greece").[^13]<sup><span title="Page: 67">: 67</span></sup>

In 2014, 78 Chinese citizens were among those evacuated from Libya by the Greek navy frigate Salamis. China thanked Greece for its help.[^14]

In 2017, Greece, under Prime Minister [Alexis Tsipras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexis_Tsipras "Alexis Tsipras"), prevented the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") from issuing statements condemning Chinese aggression in the [South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea "South China Sea") and [its human rights record](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_China "Human rights in China"), moves widely attributed as a response to Chinese investment in the [Port of Piraeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port_of_Piraeus "Port of Piraeus").[^15] [^16] [^17]

In August 2018, Greece joined the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative").[^18]

On the 12th of April, 2019, Greece officially joined China's ' [Cooperation between China and Central and Eastern European Countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cooperation_between_China_and_Central_and_Eastern_European_Countries "Cooperation between China and Central and Eastern European Countries") ', becoming the 17th European Nation to join the initiative, making it 17+1.[^19] This move has further developed relations between China and Greece.[^20]

In 2019, the [Bank of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bank_of_China "Bank of China") opened its first branch in Greece.[^21]

On July 8, 2021, the Greek PM [Kyriakos Mitsotakis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyriakos_Mitsotakis "Kyriakos Mitsotakis") expressed Greece's appreciation of China's firm stance regarding a settlement of the [Cyprus dispute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyprus_dispute "Cyprus dispute").[^22]

##### Espionage

In July 2025, Greek authorities arrested four Chinese nationals at [Tanagra Air Base](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tanagra_Air_Base "Tanagra Air Base") for taking photos of military facilities.[^23] [^24] In February 2026, the [Hellenic National Defence General Staff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenic_National_Defence_General_Staff "Hellenic National Defence General Staff") announced that Greek authorities had arrested a member of the [Hellenic Air Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenic_Air_Force "Hellenic Air Force") for allegedly leaking "highly sensitive" information to China.[^25] [^26] The air force officer previously participated in [MIM-104 Patriot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MIM-104_Patriot "MIM-104 Patriot") modernization efforts.[^27]

## Chinese investments in Greece

In October 2009, [COSCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COSCO "COSCO") leased for 30 years part of the [Port of Piraeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port_of_Piraeus "Port of Piraeus"), the cargo level two years later was three times higher than before.[^28] COSCO has a 67% ownership stake in the Port of Piraeus.[^29] [^30]

In 2010 a $123 million contract between [Helios Plaza](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Helios_Plaza&action=edit&redlink=1 "Helios Plaza (page does not exist)") and BCEGI, a subsidiary of [Beijing Construction Engineering Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing_Construction_Engineering_Group "Beijing Construction Engineering Group"), real estate company and construction contractor. Helios is developing a hotel and commercial complex for tourism in [Piraeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piraeus "Piraeus"), Athens' largest port town. [Huawei Technologies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huawei_Technologies "Huawei Technologies"), a Chinese [telecom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telecommunication "Telecommunication") invested with [Hellenic Telecommunications Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hellenic_Telecommunications_Organization "Hellenic Telecommunications Organization").[^31]

In March 2015 Deputy Greek Prime Minister Yannis Dragasakis and Minister of Foreign Affairs Nikos Kotzias paid an official visit to China on 25 March 2015. Within this context, Minister Kotzias met with the Foreign Minister of China, Wang Yi.[^32]

## Cultural connections

In 1978, China and Greece signed the “China-Greece Cultural Exchange and Cooperation Agreement”, followed by the “China-Greece Science and Technology Exchange and Cooperation Agreement” in 1979, and the “China-Greece Tourism Cooperation Agreement” in 1988.[^33]

Since China and Greece have signed an agreement on scientific and technological exchanges and cooperation in 1979, they both have successively convened 10 scientific and technological cooperation mixed committees.[^34] The cooperation projects involved agriculture, energy, geosciences, oceanography, biology, medicine.[^33] In the fields of sociology, [materials science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Materials_science "Materials science") and [basic research](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_research "Basic research"), some cooperative projects have achieved research results and economic and social benefits. The two countries also have exchanges in protection of cultural relics, museum construction, and press groups.[^33]

In 2005, the "Memorandum of Understanding on China-Greece Education Cooperation" was signed.[^33]

In 2012, on the 40th anniversary of the establishment of diplomatic relations between the People's Republic of China and Greece many commemoratory events were planned in both countries, such as a joint philatelic publication, circulated by the Greek and Chinese postal services. A variety of events were also organized including a Greek cinema week and an exhibition on the lives and works of [Socrates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socrates "Socrates") and [Lao Tzu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laozi "Laozi").[^35]

## Public opinion

A survey published in 2025 by the [Pew Research Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pew_Research_Center "Pew Research Center") found that 56% of Greek people had a favorable view of China, while 36% had an unfavorable view. It also found that 63% of Greek people in the 18-35 age group had positive opinions of China.[^42]
