Since its [founding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Proclamation of the People's Republic of China") in 1949, the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") (PRC) has had a diplomatic tug-of-war with its rival in [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_of_Taiwan "Geography of Taiwan"), the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") (ROC). Throughout the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War"), both governments claimed to be the sole legitimate government of all China and allowed countries to recognize either one or the other. Until the 1970s, most Western countries in the [Western Bloc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Bloc "Western Bloc") recognized the ROC while the [Eastern Bloc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Bloc "Eastern Bloc") and [Third World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_World "Third World") countries generally recognized the PRC. This gradually shifted and today only [11 UN member states recognize the ROC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_recognition_of_the_Republic_of_China "International recognition of the Republic of China") while the PRC is recognized by the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations"), as well as 181 UN member states (including 18 [G20](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G20 "G20") member states [^1]), [Cook Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cook_Islands "Cook Islands"), [Niue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Niue "Niue") and the [State of Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_Palestine "State of Palestine"). The PRC maintains the requirement of recognizing its view of the [One China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China "One China") policy to establish or maintain diplomatic relations.

## Recognition of the PRC before it was seated at the UN

### 1949

The PRC was established on 1 October 1949, when the [Chinese Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War "Chinese Civil War") was still underway, and the seat of [Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nationalist_government "Nationalist government") of the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") in [Canton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou") was not [relocated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retreat_of_the_government_of_the_Republic_of_China_to_Taiwan "Retreat of the government of the Republic of China to Taiwan") to [Taipei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taipei "Taipei") until December 1949. All the countries that recognized the new PRC government in 1949 were [communist states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_state "Communist state").

## Recognition of the PRC after it was seated at the UN

The Republic of China, which had occupied [China's seat at the United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_and_the_United_Nations "China and the United Nations") since 1945, was effectively expelled on 25 October 1971, through [Resolution 2758](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_Resolution_2758 "United Nations General Assembly Resolution 2758") approved in the general assembly of said date. Its seat was taken over by the People's Republic of China from 15 November 1971, and the migration of relations to the PRC soon followed among members of the [Western Bloc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Bloc "Western Bloc"), except for the United Kingdom, France, Canada, Italy and the [Scandinavian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scandinavia "Scandinavia") Countries which had previously established diplomatic relations.
