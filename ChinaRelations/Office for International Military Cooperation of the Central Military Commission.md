The **Office for International Military Cooperation of the Central Military Commission** is a functional department under the [Central Military Commission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Military_Commission_\(China\) "Central Military Commission (China)") of the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") of [corps grade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grades_of_the_armed_forces_of_China "Grades of the armed forces of China"), charged with coordinating the [People's Liberation Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army "People's Liberation Army") 's relationships with foreign militaries.[^1] It grew out of the PLA's Foreign Affairs Office, founded in 1951. It was founded on January 11, 2016, under [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") 's [military reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_People%27s_Republic_of_China_military_reform "2015 People's Republic of China military reform"). Its current director is [Major General](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_General "Major General") [Li Bin](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Li_Bin_\(General\)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Li Bin (General) (page does not exist)").

## History

The External Liaison Office of the People's Revolutionary Military Commission was founded in 1951. After the [Ministry of National Defense](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_National_Defense_\(China\) "Ministry of National Defense (China)") was founded in 1954, the PLA used the External Liaison Office as a base to establish the Foreign Affairs Office (FAO), under the dual authority of the [Intelligence Bureau of the General Staff Department](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intelligence_Bureau_of_the_Joint_Staff_Department "Intelligence Bureau of the Joint Staff Department") and the General Office of the Ministry of National Defense (MND); in 1959, it was placed solely under the Ministry's General Office. In January 1964, the FAO was expanded to become the Foreign Affairs Bureau of the General Office of the MND. It created its own General Office, as well as three divisions; a military attaché division, a military assistance division, and a research division.[^1]

In 1965, the bureau became subordinate to the General Staff Department and underwent unknown reorganizational changes. In December 1998, the bureau's name was changed to the Foreign Affairs Office; it was also promoted to become a corps-level organization. The PLA went through [significant organizational restructuring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2015_People%27s_Republic_of_China_military_reform "2015 People's Republic of China military reform") on 11 January 2016; and the Office was renamed to the Office for International Military Cooperation and became the 13th ranking department of the Central Military Commission.[^1] [^2] It is also believed to be " [one institution with two names](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_institution_with_two_names "One institution with two names") " with the Office for International Military Cooperation of the Ministry of National Defense.[^1]

## Organization

After the 2015 reforms, the Office of International Military Cooperation is organized as below:

### Functional Departments

- [General Office](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Office "General Office") (综合局)
- [African Bureau](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=African_Bureau&action=edit&redlink=1 "African Bureau (page does not exist)") (亚洲局) [^3]
- [Eurasian Bureau](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Eurasian_Bureau&action=edit&redlink=1 "Eurasian Bureau (page does not exist)") (欧亚局) [^4] [^5]
- [Americas and Oceania Bureau](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Americas_and_Oceania_Bureau&action=edit&redlink=1 "Americas and Oceania Bureau (page does not exist)") (美洲大洋洲局) [^6]
- [Treaty Affairs Bureau](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Treaty_Affairs_Bureau&action=edit&redlink=1 "Treaty Affairs Bureau (page does not exist)") (履约事务局) [^7]

### Directly Subordinate Units

- [Security Cooperation Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Security_Cooperation_Center&action=edit&redlink=1 "Security Cooperation Center (page does not exist)") (安全合作中心)
- [Arms Control and Compliance Affairs Office](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Arms_Control_and_Compliance_Affairs_Office_\(China\)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Arms Control and Compliance Affairs Office (China) (page does not exist)") (军控和履约事务办公室) [^1]
	- [Compliance Affairs Bureau](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Compliance_Affairs_Bureau_of_the_Arms_Control_and_Compliance_Affairs_Office_\(China\)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Compliance Affairs Bureau of the Arms Control and Compliance Affairs Office (China) (page does not exist)") (履约事务局)

### External Units

- [Military Attaches in PRC's diplomatic missions](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Military_Attaches_in_PRC%27s_diplomatic_missions&action=edit&redlink=1 "Military Attaches in PRC's diplomatic missions (page does not exist)") (各驻外外交代表机构武官处)
- [Military Attache in PRC's United Nations delegation](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Military_Attache_in_PRC%27s_United_Nations_delegation&action=edit&redlink=1 "Military Attache in PRC's United Nations delegation (page does not exist)") (中国常驻联合国代表团军事参谋团)
