The [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") and [Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungary "Hungary") (at the time, the [Hungarian People's Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_People%27s_Republic "Hungarian People's Republic")) established diplomatic relations in October 1949.

The PRC has an embassy in [Budapest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budapest "Budapest"). Hungary has an [embassy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embassy_of_Hungary,_Beijing "Embassy of Hungary, Beijing") in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and [consulates-general](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consulate-general "Consulate-general") in [Chongqing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chongqing "Chongqing"), [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou"), [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong") and [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai"). In addition, Hungary also has an unofficial diplomatic outpost for the Taiwan-related interests in the form of the [Hungarian Trade Office](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Hungarian_Trade_Office&action=edit&redlink=1 "Hungarian Trade Office (page does not exist)") in Taipei while the ROC has the [Taipei Representative Office in Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taipei_Representative_Office_in_Hungary "Taipei Representative Office in Hungary") in Budapest.

Relations have been generally positive, especially under Hungarian prime minister [Viktor Orbán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viktor_Orb%C3%A1n "Viktor Orbán"). In 2017, the two countries declared their relationship a [comprehensive strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "Diplomatic partnerships of China").

## History

In 1869, [Austria-Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austria-Hungary "Austria-Hungary") established diplomatic relations with the [Qing dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty").[^1]

### Austro-Hungarian concession of Tianjin

The [Austro-Hungarian concession of Tianjin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austro-Hungarian_concession_of_Tianjin "Austro-Hungarian concession of Tianjin") ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters "Simplified Chinese characters"): 天津奥租界; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Tiānjīn ào zūjiè*, [German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_language "German language"): *österreichisch-ungarische Konzession*, [Hungarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_language "Hungarian language"): *Osztrák–magyar tiencsini koncesszió*) was a territory ([concession](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concessions_and_leases_in_international_relations "Concessions and leases in international relations")) in the [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") city of [Tientsin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tianjin "Tianjin") occupied by [Austria-Hungary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austria-Hungary "Austria-Hungary") between 1902 and 1920. It had been obtained by Austria-Hungary after the signing of the [Boxer Protocol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boxer_Protocol "Boxer Protocol") at the conclusion of the conflict between China and the [Alliance of Eight Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eight-Nation_Alliance "Eight-Nation Alliance"), which had sent an international expeditionary force to quell the [Boxer Rebellion of 1901](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion "Boxer Rebellion"). Although the Austro-Hungarian occupation corps had been present from the previous year, the concession formally began on 27 December 1902. It is the shortest lived concession, having existed for only 14 years.

### Cold War

On October 3, 1949, the [Hungarian People's Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_People%27s_Republic "Hungarian People's Republic") recognized the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China"). On October 6, the two countries established diplomatic relations.[^2] During the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War"), the Hungarian People's Republic sent medical teams to Pyongyang to treat wounded North Korean and Chinese [People's Volunteer Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Volunteer_Army "People's Volunteer Army") troops.[^3] At the height of the [Hungarian Revolution of 1956](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_Revolution_of_1956 "Hungarian Revolution of 1956"), Chinese premier [Zhou Enlai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhou_Enlai "Zhou Enlai") encouraged Soviet premier [Nikita Khrushchev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikita_Khrushchev "Nikita Khrushchev") to intervene against the Hungarian revolutionaries.[^4]

In 1989, [Hungarian government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hungarian_government "Hungarian government"), which was undergoing political reform, reacted strongly to the [1989 Tiananmen Square protests and massacre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1989_Tiananmen_Square_protests_and_massacre "1989 Tiananmen Square protests and massacre"), condeming the Chinese government's actions. Demonstrations were held outside the Chinese embassy. Hungary was the only country in Europe to have substantially reduced relations with China in the aftermath of the events.[^5]

### Under Viktor Orbán

During the government of [Viktor Orbán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viktor_Orb%C3%A1n "Viktor Orbán"), Hungary strengthened its ties with China while other [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") member states considered "de-risking" their relations with China.[^6] In 2017, China and Hungary declared their relationship a [comprehensive strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "Diplomatic partnerships of China").[^7]<sup><span title="Page: 161">: 161</span> </sup> Since at least 2017, Hungarian police and other governmental institutions have used Chinese surveillance equipment from [Dahua Technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dahua_Technology "Dahua Technology") and [Hikvision](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hikvision "Hikvision").[^8]

In 2021, it was revealed that [Fudan University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fudan_University "Fudan University") was planning a campus in Budapest. This led to pushback in the country and to protests, leading the Hungarian government to delay the plans and promise that it will hold a referendum on the issue.[^9] On 22 May 2022, the [Constitutional Court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_Court_of_Hungary "Constitutional Court of Hungary") ruled the referendum as unconstitutional as it concerns an international agreement. Hungarian officials also promised to resume the project after the victory of the ruling [Fidesz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fidesz "Fidesz") during the [2022 parliamentary elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Hungarian_parliamentary_election "2022 Hungarian parliamentary election").[^9]

Hungary has formally blocked the European Union from formally criticizing China's actions in [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong") in both 2020 and 2021.[^10] [^7]<sup><span title="Page: 162">: 162</span></sup>

On 20 February 2023, [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") (CCP) [Politburo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politburo_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "Politburo of the Chinese Communist Party") member and [top diplomat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Office_of_the_Central_Foreign_Affairs_Commission "Office of the Central Foreign Affairs Commission") [Wang Yi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yi "Wang Yi") visited Budapest to meet with prime minister Orbán. During the meeting, Hungarian foreign minister [Péter Szijjártó](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/P%C3%A9ter_Szijj%C3%A1rt%C3%B3 "Péter Szijjártó") stated that "\[w\]hen we have faced crises in recent years, Hungary has always come out of them stronger than it went into them, but Hungarian-Chinese cooperation has played an absolutely indispensable role in this".[^10] On 27 February 2023, Orbán backed the peace plan released by Wang Yi about ending the [Russian invasion of Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine "Russian invasion of Ukraine").[^11]

[CCP general secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") visited Hungary in May 2024 and met with Orbán.[^12] During [the visit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_visits_by_Xi_Jinping_to_France,_Serbia_and_Hungary "State visits by Xi Jinping to France, Serbia and Hungary"), the two countries entered into an "all-weather comprehensive strategic partnership for a new era" as well as 18 other agreements and memoranda of understanding.[^12] [^13]

In October 2025, it was reported that Chinese state-backed [advanced persistent threat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_persistent_threat "Advanced persistent threat") group Mustang Panda had targeted Hungarian and other European diplomats as part of a coordinated [cyber-espionage campaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyberwarfare_and_China "Cyberwarfare and China").[^14]

## Economic relations

Under Viktor Orbán, Hungary joined the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative") in 2015. In the same year, it was the largest recipient of Chinese outbound direct investment with around $571 million.[^15] As of at least 2024, Hungary is one of the strongest European supporters of the BRI.[^16] Between 2022 and 2024, Hungary received nearly a quarter of [Chinese foreign direct investment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_direct_investment_in_China#Outbound_FDI "Foreign direct investment in China") to Europe.[^17] Hungarian foreign minister Szijjártó stated in 2023 that Hungarian-Chinese trade was over $10 billion.[^10]

Hungary hosts the largest supply center of [Huawei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huawei "Huawei") outside of China.[^10] In 2022, Chinese battery company [CATL](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CATL "CATL") agreed to invest $7.5 billion to build a factory in [Debrecen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Debrecen "Debrecen").[^10] Concerns were raised among locals about environmental and health effects of the factory.[^18] Hungary also has several Chinese electric vehicle battery plants.[^12] In 2023, Chinese electric vehicle company [BYD Auto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BYD_Auto "BYD Auto") chose [Szeged](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Szeged "Szeged") as its first large factory in Europe.[^17] In March 2025, the [European Commission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Commission "European Commission") opened a probe into whether the BYD Auto plant had received unfair state subsidies from China.[^19]

## Healthcare

Hungary was the first EU member to approve the use of the [COVID-19 vaccine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinopharm_BIBP_COVID-19_vaccine "Sinopharm BIBP COVID-19 vaccine") developed by China's [Sinopharm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinopharm_\(company\) "Sinopharm (company)").[^7]<sup><span title="Page: 169">: 169</span></sup>

## Taiwan

Hungary follows the [one China principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_principle "One China principle"). It recognizes the People's Republic of China as the sole government of China and [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") as an integral part of China's territory.[^20] In 1990, Hungary and Taiwan established representative offices in [Tapei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taipei "Taipei") and Budapest, respectively.[^21] [^22]

## Public opinion

A survey published in 2025 by the [Pew Research Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pew_Research_Center "Pew Research Center") found that 51% of Hungarians had a favorable view of China, while 42% had an unfavorable view.[^23]

## Resident diplomatic missions

- China has an embassy in [Budapest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budapest "Budapest").
- Hungary has an embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and consulates-general in [Chongqing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chongqing "Chongqing"), [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou"), [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong") and [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai").
