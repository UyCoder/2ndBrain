The **National Immigration Administration** (**NIA**), alternatively known as the **Exit and Entry Administration of the People's Republic of China** (for [mainland China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_China "Mainland China") 's regional border controls with [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong"), [Macau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macau "Macau"), and [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan")), is a [sub-ministry-level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_service_of_China#Levels_and_ranking_system "Civil service of China") executive agency administrated by the [Ministry of Public Security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Public_Security_\(China\) "Ministry of Public Security (China)"). The administration is responsible for coordinating and formulating immigration policies and their implementation, [border control](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Border_control "Border control") (and [border patrol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Border_guard "Border guard") along with [drug interdiction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drug_interdiction "Drug interdiction")),[^1] [^2] administering foreigners' stay, management on refugees and nationality, taking the lead in coordinating the administering of foreigners who illegally enter, stay or are employed in China, and the repatriation of illegal immigrants.[^3] [^4] The NIA is also responsible for manning border checkpoints.[^5]

In 2025, the NIA had an annual budget of 62,567,100,300 Renminbi.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 10">: 10</span></sup>

## History

In July 1998, nine cities including [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing"), [Tianjin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tianjin "Tianjin"), [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai"), [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou"), [Shenzhen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shenzhen "Shenzhen"), [Zhuhai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhuhai "Zhuhai"), [Xiamen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xiamen "Xiamen"), [Haikou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haikou "Haikou"), and [Shantou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shantou "Shantou") established the entry-exit border checkpoints, which were vertically led by the **[Ministry of Public Security Exit and Entry Bureau](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ministry_of_Public_Security_Exit_and_Entry_Bureau&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ministry of Public Security Exit and Entry Bureau (page does not exist)")**.[^7] In 2011, according to the Central Office document on the "approval on the adjustment of Beijing in seven vocational institutions specifications preparing Frontier Guard Inspection Station" (Central Office Document No. 54), Shenzhen FGIS and seven other professional Frontier Inspection Station Specifications adjusted from the deputy department level to the main department level with the institutional affiliation and staffing remaining unchanged.[^8]

Prior to the 2018 People's Armed Police reform, the [People's Armed Police Border Defense Corps](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Border_Defense_Corps_\(China\)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Border Defense Corps (China) (page does not exist)") ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters "Simplified Chinese characters"): 武警边防部队; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Wǔjǐng Biānfáng Bùdùi*), more commonly known as the **Ministry of Public Security Border Defense Corp** s ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 公安边防部队) due to it being a [Ministry of Public Security Active Service Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Public_Security_Active_Service_Forces "Ministry of Public Security Active Service Forces"), was formerly China's primary [border patrol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Border_guard "Border guard") force overseen by the [Ministry of Public Security Border Control Department](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ministry_of_Public_Security_Border_Control_Department&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ministry of Public Security Border Control Department (page does not exist)").[^9]

After the 2018 people's armed police reforms, the **[Ministry of Public Security Exit and Entry Bureau](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ministry_of_Public_Security_Exit_and_Entry_Bureau&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ministry of Public Security Exit and Entry Bureau (page does not exist)")** and **[Ministry of Public Security Border Control Department](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ministry_of_Public_Security_Border_Control_Department&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ministry of Public Security Border Control Department (page does not exist)")** (with the [People's Armed Police Border Defense Corps](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Border_Defense_Corps_\(China\)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Border Defense Corps (China) (page does not exist)")) merged to form the national immigration administration on April 2, 2018,[^2] with the latter's personnel becoming part of the People's Police.[^10]

On April 1, 2021, with the approval of the [Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party"), the National Immigration Management Agency will use its own flag and logo from April 2, 2021, onward.[^11]

### Police tactical units

The [Hulunbuir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hulunbuir "Hulunbuir") Border Management Detachment of the [Inner Mongolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inner_Mongolia "Inner Mongolia") General Station of Exit and Entry Frontier Inspection operates the [Barga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barga_\(tribe\) "Barga (tribe)") Commando Unit ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 巴尔虎突击队; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Bā'ěr hǔ tújí duì*), a police tactical unit used for [border patrol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Border_guard "Border guard"), [CQB](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Close-quarters_battle "Close-quarters battle") [anti-terrorism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Counterterrorism "Counterterrorism"), anti-hijacking, apprehending dangerous suspects, [search and rescue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Search_and_rescue "Search and rescue"), [bomb disposal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bomb_disposal "Bomb disposal") and security at large events.[^16] [^17] [^18] [^19] It operates [K9s](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Police_dog "Police dog"), [infantry mobility vehicles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Infantry_mobility_vehicle "Infantry mobility vehicle") along with [submachine guns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Submachine_gun "Submachine gun") and [sniper rifles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sniper_rifle "Sniper rifle").[^16] [^19]

## Reforms

From 1 April 2019 onwards, all mainland citizens can apply and receive passports and exit and entry permits from the NIA offices anywhere in the country, instead of requiring citizens to go to their [Hukou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hukou "Hukou") city/province to apply. Also, exit and entry permits to Hong Kong, Macau and Taiwan can be applied to, received, and delivered within a few minutes on automatic machines.[^22]

## Line of duty casualties

Due to the NIA being responsible for border patrol, there are occasionally gunfights between NIA officers and drug traffickers on the China–Myanmar border, most famously on December 4, 2021, when ethnic [Hani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hani_people "Hani people") officer Cai Xiaodong (蔡晓东) of the [Xishuangbanna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xishuangbanna_Dai_Autonomous_Prefecture "Xishuangbanna Dai Autonomous Prefecture") border management detachment was shot and killed by a drug trafficker on the [China Myanmar border](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Myanmar_border "China–Myanmar border"). He was posthumously awarded martyr status.[^23] [^24] [^25]

In 2021, 7 NIA officers(both sworn and auxiliary) were killed in the line of duty, with a further 49 being injured [^26]

In 2022, 6 NIA officers(both sworn and auxiliary) were killed in the line of duty, with a further 40 being injured [^27]

In 2023, 12 NIA officers(both sworn and auxiliary) were killed in the line of duty, with a further 77 being injured.[^28]
