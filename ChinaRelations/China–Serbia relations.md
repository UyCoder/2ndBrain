[China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") and [Serbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbia "Serbia") maintain diplomatic relations established between People's Republic of China and [SFR Yugoslavia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SFR_Yugoslavia "SFR Yugoslavia") in 1955. From 1956 to 2006, China [maintained relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Yugoslavia_relations "China–Yugoslavia relations") with the Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia (SFRY) and the Federal Republic of Yugoslavia (FRY) (later [Serbia and Montenegro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbia_and_Montenegro "Serbia and Montenegro")), of which Serbia is considered [shared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agreement_on_Succession_Issues_of_the_Former_Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia "Agreement on Succession Issues of the Former Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia") (SFRY) or sole (FRY) legal [successor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Succession_of_states "Succession of states").[^1] In recent decades, the two countries have held a very close relationship raising it to the level of [strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "Diplomatic partnerships of China") since 2009.[^2] [^3] [^4]

## History

China supported the Serbia (then part of Federal Republic of Yugoslavia) during the [Kosovo War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kosovo_War "Kosovo War") and opposed the [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Atlantic_Treaty_Organization "North Atlantic Treaty Organization") airstrikes against targets in Serbia and Montenegro.[^5] China believed that Milošević was acting to prevent the secession of Kosovo by Albanian separatists from Serbia, and thus supported his actions as preserving the FRY's territorial integrity.[^6] China opposed NATO intervention in Kosovo on the basis that it [set a dangerous precedent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kosovo_precedent "Kosovo precedent") that Chinese officials believed could in the future afflict China should riots occur in [Tibet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibet "Tibet") or [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") and then result in bombings.[^6] PRC opposition to the NATO actions intensified after the [United States bombed the Chinese embassy in Belgrade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_bombing_of_the_Chinese_embassy_in_Belgrade "United States bombing of the Chinese embassy in Belgrade") during the war.[^5]

## Political relations

Serbia follows the [one China principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_principle "One China principle"), and recognizes [government of the People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_China "Government of China") as the sole legal government representing the whole of China and Taiwan as "an inalienable part" of China. Serbia also supports all efforts by the PRC to "achieve [national reunification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_unification "Chinese unification") " and opposes [Taiwan independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan_independence_movement "Taiwan independence movement").[^7]

Under president [Aleksandar Vučić](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleksandar_Vu%C4%8Di%C4%87 "Aleksandar Vučić"), Serbia has sought closer cooperation with China.[^8] After meeting with Chinese [Minister of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China") [Wang Yi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yi_\(politician\) "Wang Yi (politician)"), he secured Chinese help in combating the [COVID-19 pandemic in Serbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Serbia "COVID-19 pandemic in Serbia") through the delivery of [PPE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Personal_protective_equipment "Personal protective equipment") and [CoronaVac vaccine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CoronaVac "CoronaVac") doses,[^9] which contributed to Serbia leading COVID-19 vaccination rates in Europe.[^10] [^11]

In 2019, UN ambassadors of 37 countries, including Serbia, signed a joint letter to the [United Nations Human Rights Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Rights_Council "United Nations Human Rights Council") defending [China's persecution of Uyghurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Uyghurs_in_China "Persecution of Uyghurs in China").[^12] [^13] Serbia was one of 16 countries that defended China in 2019 but did not do so in 2020.[^14]

In 2024, on occasion of the 25th anniversary of the United States bombing of the Chinese embassy in Belgrade, [Chinese leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paramount_leader "Paramount leader") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") wrote an article in the Serbian newspaper *[Politika](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politika "Politika")* in which he stated, "The friendship between China and Serbia which is soaked in blood that the two peoples spilled together has become a joint memory of the two peoples and will encourage both parties to make together huge steps forward." [^15] In September 2025, China ratified an extradition treaty it signed with Serbia in 2024.[^16]

### China's stance on Kosovo

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Serbia_adm_location_map.svg/250px-Serbia_adm_location_map.svg.png)

Serbia (yellow) and Kosovo (striped)

China backs Serbia's position regarding [Kosovo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kosovo "Kosovo"). The [Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China") has made a statement stressing that the PRC "expresses grave concern" over Kosovo's unilateral declaration of independence. The spokesman [Liu Jianchao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liu_Jianchao "Liu Jianchao") 's remarks go on to add that "The resolution of the Kosovo issue bares \[*[sic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sic "Sic")*\] on peace and stability of the Balkan region, the fundamental norms governing international relations as well as the authority and role of the [UN Security Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council "United Nations Security Council"). China always believes that a plan acceptable to both Serbia and Kosovo through negotiations is the best way to resolve this issue. The unilateral move taken by Kosovo will lead to a series of consequences. China is deeply worried about its severe and negative impact on peace and stability of the Balkan region and the goal of establishing a multi-ethnic society in Kosovo. China calls upon Serbia and Kosovo to continue negotiations for a proper resolution within the framework of the international law and work together to safeguard peace and stability of the Balkan region. The international community should create favorable conditions for that." [^17] [^18]

Serbian Deputy Prime Minister [Božidar Đelić](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bo%C5%BEidar_%C4%90eli%C4%87 "Božidar Đelić") told reporters after an April 2009 meeting in Beijing with Politburo member [Liu Yandong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liu_Yandong "Liu Yandong") that China reiterated its support to help Serbia preserve her territorial integrity. "Just as Serbia supports the [one China policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_policy "One China policy"), China supports Serbia as its best and most stable friend in southeastern Europe." [^19] Ambassador of China to Serbia, Wei Jinghua, stated in 2009 that "China respects the sovereignty and territorial integrity of Serbia and understands the great concern of Serbia on the issue of Kosovo. We support the negotiations between Belgrade and Pristina that would bring a mutually acceptable solution, in accordance with international law, the [UN Charter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charter_of_the_United_Nations "Charter of the United Nations") and UN resolutions." [^20]

## Economic relations

China is Serbia's second biggest trading partner. Trade between the two countries amounted to [$](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar "United States dollar") 6.1 billion in 2022 with Chinese merchandise exports to Serbia standing at almost $5 billion while Serbian exports stood at $1.1 billion. Since 2024, China and Serbia have a [free trade agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_trade_agreement "Free trade agreement").[^15]

Chinese companies invested $5.6 billion in Serbia in the last decade and employ 38,000 people.[^22] [^23] [^24] Chinese corporations making investments in Serbia include [Zijin Mining](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zijin_Mining "Zijin Mining") (owner of [Serbia Zijin Bor Copper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbia_Zijin_Bor_Copper "Serbia Zijin Bor Copper") cooper mining complex in [Bor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bor,_Serbia "Bor, Serbia")), [Hesteel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hesteel_Group "Hesteel Group") (owner of [Hesteel Serbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hesteel_Serbia "Hesteel Serbia") steel plant in [Smederevo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smederevo "Smederevo")), [Hisense](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hisense "Hisense") (electrical appliances factory in [Valjevo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valjevo "Valjevo")), [Linglong Tire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Linglong_Tire "Linglong Tire") (tire factory in [Zrenjanin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zrenjanin "Zrenjanin")), and Minth (automotive parts factories in [Šabac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C5%A0abac "Šabac") and [Loznica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Loznica "Loznica")).

Since early 2010s China has invested US$10 billion in Serbian infrastructure and energy mainly through China's [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative"), of which Serbia was one of major beneficiaries with projects including Serbian section of the [Budapest–Belgrade railway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budapest%E2%80%93Belgrade_railway "Budapest–Belgrade railway") (built by [CCCC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Communications_Construction_Company "China Communications Construction Company")), 350 [MW](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megawatt "Megawatt") B3 unit of [Kostolac thermal power-plant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TPP_Kostolac "TPP Kostolac") (built by [CMEC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Machinery_Engineering_Corporation "China Machinery Engineering Corporation")), Belgrade– [Ljig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ljig "Ljig") and [Čačak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%8Ca%C4%8Dak "Čačak") - [Požega](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Po%C5%BEega,_Serbia "Požega, Serbia") segments of [A2 motorway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A2_motorway_\(Serbia\) "A2 motorway (Serbia)") (built by [Shandong Gaosu Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shandong_Gaosu_Group "Shandong Gaosu Group")), [Pupin Bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pupin_Bridge "Pupin Bridge") in Belgrade (built by [CRBC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Road_and_Bridge_Corporation "China Road and Bridge Corporation")).[^15] [^25] As of 2024, Chinese companies are building several large infrastructure projects including Line 1 of [Belgrade Metro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgrade_Metro "Belgrade Metro") (built by [Power China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_Construction_Corporation_of_China "Power Construction Corporation of China")), the National Stadium and exhibition halls for [Expo 2027](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expo_2027 "Expo 2027") in Belgrade (built by Power China), A6 motorway connecting Belgrade and [Novi Sad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Novi_Sad "Novi Sad") through the town of Zrenjanin (built by Shandong Gaosu Group).

The Serbian [flag carrier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flag_carrier "Flag carrier") [Air Serbia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_Serbia "Air Serbia") operates flights between Belgrade and [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai") and Belgrade and [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou").[^26] [^27] The Chinese carriers [China Southern Airlines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Southern_Airlines "China Southern Airlines") and [Hainan Airlines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hainan_Airlines "Hainan Airlines") operate flights between Belgrade and Guangzhou and Belgrade and [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing_Capital_International_Airport "Beijing Capital International Airport") respectively.[^28] [^29]

## Military cooperation

In recent years, Serbia has purchased from China air defense missile systems of medium and short range ([HQ-22](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HQ-22 "HQ-22") and [HQ-17](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HQ-17 "HQ-17")) as well as combat drones (CH-95 and [CH-92](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CASC_Rainbow#CH-92 "CASC Rainbow")).[^30] [^31] In 2025, the Serbian and Chinese militaries conducted joint training exercises in China, an operation which was code-named "Peace Guardian 2025".[^32]

## Cultural cooperation

[Confucius Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucius_Institute "Confucius Institute"), cultural center devoted to the [Chinese culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_culture "Chinese culture") and language has been operating in Belgrade since 2006.[^33]

## Immigration from China

There is a relatively present Chinese [diaspora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diaspora "Diaspora") in Serbia; estimates are that up to 15,000 [Chinese people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_people "Chinese people") live in Serbia, mainly in Belgrade, [Bor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bor,_Serbia "Bor, Serbia"), and [Zrenjanin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zrenjanin "Zrenjanin").[^34] First wave of Chinese immigration to Serbia mainly occurred during the late 1990s and was driven by relaxed visa policies, primarily traders from [Zhejiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhejiang "Zhejiang") and [Fujian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fujian "Fujian") who settled in Belgrade. Second wave is largely tied to economic activities, investments of Chinese companies in mining and manufacturing, and is concentrated in towns of Bor and Zrenjanin.

## Travel regime

In 2017, Serbia and China mutually abolished the requirement of obtaining an entry visa for its citizens.[^35]

## Resident diplomatic missions

- China has an embassy in [Belgrade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgrade "Belgrade").
- Serbia has an embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and a consulate general in [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai").
