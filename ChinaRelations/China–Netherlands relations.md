The [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") (PRC) and the [Netherlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netherlands "Netherlands") commenced official diplomatic relations in November 1954.[^1] In May 1972, diplomatic mission was increased to [ambassadorial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambassador "Ambassador") level.[^1] On 11 May 1981, the diplomatic relations was downgraded to the [charge d'affaires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charge_d%27affaires "Charge d'affaires") level due to the Dutch government ratifying the construction of two submarines for Taiwan by Dutch companies. Relations remained muted till 1 February 1984, when China and the Netherlands restored [ambassadorial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambassador "Ambassador") diplomatic relations.[^1]

## History

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/President_Xi_Jinping.jpg/250px-President_Xi_Jinping.jpg)

Dutch Prime Minister Mark Rutte with Chinese leader Xi Jinping in December 2013

China-Dutch relations began prior to the founding of the People's Republic of China in the 17th and 18th century when Dutch traders of the [Verenigde Oostindische Compagnie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Verenigde_Oostindische_Compagnie "Verenigde Oostindische Compagnie") (VOC) setup trading post in [Canton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou") and also in the western coast of [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_of_Taiwan "Geography of Taiwan").[^2] [Johan Nieuhof](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Johan_Nieuhof "Johan Nieuhof") 's account of the first VOC embassy to [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") in 1655–1657—and the objections against it raised by members of the [Jesuit China Mission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesuit_China_Mission "Jesuit China Mission") —became the influential and much-translated 1665 *[Embassy from the East-India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embassy_from_the_East-India_Company "Embassy from the East-India Company")*.

### Cold War

During the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War"), the Netherlands' [Regiment van Heutsz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regiment_van_Heutsz "Regiment van Heutsz") under the [United Nations Command](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Command "United Nations Command") fought the People's Republic of China's [People's Volunteer Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Volunteer_Army "People's Volunteer Army") in multiple battles.[^3]

PRC–Netherlands began in 1954 to establish the diplomatic relations of the charge d'affaires level. On 18 May 1972, Sino-Dutch relations were upgraded to the [ambassadorial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambassadorial "Ambassadorial") level.[^4] In the 1980s Taiwan ordered two submarines from a Dutch shipyard which were delivered despite tremendous Chinese pressure.[^5] China accused the Netherlands of colluding with American President [Ronald Reagan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ronald_Reagan "Ronald Reagan") and downgraded relations to the [charge d'affaires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charge_d%27affaires "Charge d'affaires") level with the Netherlands on 11 May 1981, while threatening to do the same with the US.[^6] In 1984, the Netherlands agreed not to export additional military goods in order to restore relations to the [ambassadorial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambassadorial "Ambassadorial") level.[^7]

### 21st century

In March 2014, [Chinese Communist Party general secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") made the first [state visit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_visit "State visit") of China to the Netherlands.[^8]

In July 2019, the UN ambassadors from 22 nations, including Netherlands, signed a joint letter to the [UNHRC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Rights_Council "United Nations Human Rights Council") condemning China's alleged mistreatment of the [Uyghurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uyghurs "Uyghurs") and other minorities, urging the Chinese government to close the [Xinjiang internment camps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang_internment_camps "Xinjiang internment camps").[^9] [^10] The PRC continues to deny the allegations, with support from several Muslim countries like [Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia"), [Burkina Faso](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burkina_Faso "Burkina Faso"), [Qatar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qatar "Qatar"), [UAE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UAE "UAE"), [Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq "Iraq"), [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan "Pakistan") and [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt "Egypt"). Many Chinese allies such as [Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia "Russia"), [Belarus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belarus "Belarus"), [DPRK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/DPRK "DPRK"), [Cuba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuba "Cuba"), [Angola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Angola "Angola"), [Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam "Vietnam") and [Laos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laos "Laos") have strongly supported China on the issue.

In February 2021, the [Dutch House of Representatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_House_of_Representatives "Dutch House of Representatives") voted to recognize the Chinese government's treatment of its Uyghur Muslim minority as [genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Uyghurs_in_China "Persecution of Uyghurs in China"), becoming the first country in the European Union to do so.[^11]

In February 2024, the [Netherlands Defence Intelligence and Security Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Netherlands_Defence_Intelligence_and_Security_Service "Netherlands Defence Intelligence and Security Service") and the [General Intelligence and Security Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Intelligence_and_Security_Service "General Intelligence and Security Service") stated that [Chinese state hackers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyberwarfare_and_China "Cyberwarfare and China") penetrated a Dutch military network the prior year.[^12] The same month, Dutch trade minister [Geoffrey van Leeuwen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geoffrey_van_Leeuwen "Geoffrey van Leeuwen") justified export controls on [ASML](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASML_Holding "ASML Holding") tools to China on the grounds that they would be used for "high-value weapons systems and [weapons of mass destruction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_and_weapons_of_mass_destruction "China and weapons of mass destruction")." [^13] In April 2024, the Dutch Military Intelligence and Security Service stated that [Chinese spies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_intelligence_activity_abroad "Chinese intelligence activity abroad") were actively targeting the Netherlands' semiconductor, aerospace and maritime industries in order to strengthen China's armed forces.[^14]

In June 2024, the Dutch [Ministry of Defence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Defence_\(Netherlands\) "Ministry of Defence (Netherlands)") accused China of creating an "unsafe situation" when its fighter jets encircled Dutch frigate [HNLMS Tromp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HNLMS_Tromp_\(F803\) "HNLMS Tromp (F803)") in international waters in the [East China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_China_Sea "East China Sea").[^15] [^16] In October 2024, the Dutch [National Coordinator for Security and Counterterrorism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Coordinator_for_Security_and_Counterterrorism "National Coordinator for Security and Counterterrorism") stated that Chinese state-backed cyberattacks had increasingly moved from cyberespionage to also prepare for sabotage.[^17]

In May 2025, Dutch defense minister [Ruben Brekelmans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruben_Brekelmans "Ruben Brekelmans") stated that Chinese espionage on the Netherlands' semiconductor sector is "intensifying." [^18] [^19] In October 2025, the Dutch [Ministry of Economic Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Economic_Affairs_\(Netherlands\) "Ministry of Economic Affairs (Netherlands)") took control of Nexperia's governance using the powers of the [Goods Availability Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goods_Availability_Act "Goods Availability Act"), citing national security and European economic security.[^20] [^21] The Chinese government subsequently banned Nexperia from exporting products that it makes in China.[^22] In April 2026, the Dutch head of military intelligence stated that Chinese espionage is increasingly targeting Western military industries.[^23] In May 2026, China's [People's Liberation Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army "People's Liberation Army") stated that it used [electronic warfare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electronic_warfare "Electronic warfare") attacks on the Dutch frigate *[De Ruyter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HNLMS_De_Ruyter_\(F804\) "HNLMS De Ruyter (F804)")* to drive it out of [disputed areas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Territorial_disputes_in_the_South_China_Sea "Territorial disputes in the South China Sea") in the [South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea "South China Sea") near the [Paracel Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paracel_Islands "Paracel Islands").[^24] [^25]

## Trade

Netherlands export to China includes petrochemicals, machinery, transport equipment, food, high technology and fossil fuels.[^26] China's export to the Netherlands includes computer and consumer electronics, toys and clothes.[^26] [^27] [^28]

## Resident diplomatic missions

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2a/Dutch_Embassy_in_Beijing.JPG/250px-Dutch_Embassy_in_Beijing.JPG)

Embassy of the Netherlands in Beijing

- China has an embassy in [The Hague](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hague "The Hague").
- the Netherlands has an embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and consulates-general in [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou"), [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong") and [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai").

## Public opinion

A survey published in 2025 by the [Pew Research Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pew_Research_Center "Pew Research Center") found that 66% of Dutch people had an unfavorable view of China, while 30% had a favorable view. It also found that 45% of Dutch people in the 18-35 age group had positive opinions of China.[^29]
