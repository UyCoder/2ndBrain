**China–Timor-Leste** relations were established shortly following [Timor-Leste](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timor-Leste "Timor-Leste") 's independence on May 20, 2002.[^1]<sup><span title="Page: 107">: 107</span> </sup> However, [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") had established a representative office in [Dili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dili "Dili") in 2000, when it was still under [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations") [administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Transitional_Administration_in_East_Timor "United Nations Transitional Administration in East Timor").[^2]

## History

As early as the 10th century CE, Timorese [sandalwood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandalwood "Sandalwood") was being transported to China through the [Straits of Malacca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Straits_of_Malacca "Straits of Malacca").

The Chinese official [Zhao Rukuo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhao_Rugua "Zhao Rugua") in 1225 CE mentioned Timor in his collected notes from traders as a location rich in sandalwood, the *[Zhu Fan Zhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhu_Fan_Zhi "Zhu Fan Zhi")*.[^3] Although *Santalum album* is found across many parts of the Pacific and Indian Ocean regions, the highest quality white sandalwood was considered to come from Timor, the [Lesser Sunda Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lesser_Sunda_Islands "Lesser Sunda Islands"). Around 1350, Chinese authors wrote in the Tao-i chin-lueh:

*In Timor's mountains the only trees that grow are sandalwood. The wood is traded with those further west for silver, iron, goblets and coloured fabric. In total, there are twelve locations that are used as harbours.*

The Chinese traded porcelain, glass and silver with the Timorese for the sandalwood, which was used in China for ritual and medicinal purposes. For centuries the Chinese traders remained the only foreigners who ventured into the interior of the island of Timor, although they generally only remained in Timor as long as it took to do business, it being located too far from the trade routes between China, India and the larger islands for them to settle there.

When East Timor was under [Portuguese rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Timor "Portuguese Timor"), [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan"), as the "Republic of China", had a Consulate in [Dili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dili "Dili").[^4] However, when [Fretilin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fretilin "Fretilin") unilaterally declared the territory's independence as the Democratic Republic of East Timor, on 28 November 1975, the People's Republic of China was one of sevem countries in the world (alongside [Albania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Socialist_Republic_of_Albania "People's Socialist Republic of Albania") and five [Lusophone African](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusophone_Africa "Lusophone Africa") nations) to recognise the new state.[^5]

Following the [Indonesian invasion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indonesian_invasion_of_East_Timor "Indonesian invasion of East Timor") on 7 December 1975, China, as a permanent member of the [UN Security Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UN_Security_Council "UN Security Council"), supported [United Nations Security Council Resolution 384](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_384 "United Nations Security Council Resolution 384") deploring the invasion, upholding the territory's right to [self-determination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-determination "Self-determination") and calling on Indonesia to withdraw.[^6]

Since Timor-Leste's independence in 2002, China has financed the construction of the Presidential Palace in [Dili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dili "Dili"), as well as the Ministry of Foreign Affairs and the residential headquarters of the [Defence Force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timor_Leste_Defence_Force "Timor Leste Defence Force").[^7]

Timor-Leste and China both participate in the multi-lateral group [Forum Macao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forum_Macao "Forum Macao"), which China formed in 2003 to increase economic and commercial cooperation between China and the Portuguese-speaking countries.[^8]<sup><span title="Page: 62">: 62</span></sup>

In 2003, Beijing signed a deal with the [Community of Portuguese Language Countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_of_Portuguese_Language_Countries "Community of Portuguese Language Countries"), of which Timor-Leste is a member, to increase trade and economic development among the countries.[^9]

Timor-Leste's dismissal of hundreds of soldiers resulted in demonstrations and then riots in [Dili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dili "Dili"), prompting the Chinese embassy to shelter and then evacuate Chinese citizens.[^10]<sup><span title="Page: 79">: 79</span></sup>

In 2006, the then [President](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Timor-Leste "President of Timor-Leste") [Xanana Gusmão](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xanana_Gusm%C3%A3o "Xanana Gusmão") called China "a “reliable friend” and had committed Timor-Leste to a [One China policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_policy "One China policy").[^11]

In 2014, the two countries issued a joint communiqué reaffirming that Timor-Leste recognised the Government of the People's Republic of China as "the sole lawful Government representing the whole of China", that [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") was "an inalienable part of the Chinese territory", and that Timor-Leste would not establish "any form of official relationship or conduct any form of official contacts" with [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan").[^12]

There is also increased military cooperation between the two countries, with the 2008 purchase of two [Shanghai-Class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Type_062_class_gunboat "Type 062 class gunboat") [patrol boats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patrol_boats "Patrol boats") from a Chinese company.[^7] [^13] These boats were initially to be crewed by Chinese sailors, while the Chinese trained the Timorese to guard their coasts.[^13] In addition, China signed a contract providing US$9 million toward the building of a new headquarters for the military in Timor-Leste.[^7]
