**China–Djibouti relations** refers to the [current and historical relationship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilateralism "Bilateralism") between the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") and [Djibouti](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Djibouti "Djibouti"). China and Djibouti established relations on January 8, 1979.[^1]<sup><span title="Page: 348">: 348</span> </sup> China has financed a number of public works projects in Djibouti, including a stadium, the offices of the [Ministry of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(Djibouti\)&action=edit&redlink=1 "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Djibouti) (page does not exist)") and the People's Palace.[^2] In September 2010, [Type 920 Hospital Ship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Type_920_Hospital_Ship "Type 920 Hospital Ship"), also known as the "Peace Ark", visited Djibouti.[^3]

In 2017, China and Djibouti agreed to a [strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "Diplomatic partnerships of China").[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 211">: 211</span></sup>

## Economic development

Since the first [Forum on China Africa Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forum_on_China_Africa_Cooperation "Forum on China Africa Cooperation") in 2000, Beijing has delivered $16.6 million in development finance to Djibouti.[^5] Major Chinese aid projects in Djibouti include:

- $8.2 million to fund the construction of a hospital in Arta [^5]
- A $2.41 million grant for construction of a new headquarters for the Djiboutian Foreign Minister [^5]
- $1.75 million in [food aid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_aid "Food aid") during a drought in Djibouti in 2005 [^5]

### Ethiopia-Djibouti Potable Water Project

In 2017, China announced the launch of a cross-border potable water project between Ethiopia and Djibouti. The project will include the installation of a 102 kilometer long pipeline to draw groundwater from the Ethiopian town of [Hadagalla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadagalla "Hadagalla") to be provided to the towns of Ali-Sabieh, Dikhil, Arta and [Djibouti city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Djibouti_city "Djibouti city").[^6]

### Spaceport

In January 2023, Djibouti signed a deal with the Hong Kong Aerospace Technology Group to build the first African [spaceport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spaceport "Spaceport").[^7] The agreement followed a January 7th visit of a delegation from the company led by Vice President Allen Fund to meet with the Djiboutian government and Chinese Ambassador to Djibouti Hu Bin.[^8] The $1 billion [USD](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_dollar "United States dollar") project, expected to be completed in 2028, provides the Chinese company full ownership of the spaceport for 30 years, after which the facility will be handed over to Djibouti.[^9] Djibouti represents an advantageous location for [orbital launches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbital_spaceflight "Orbital spaceflight") as its proximity to the [Earth's equator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Equator "Equator") (10° [latitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latitude "Latitude")) and the consequential increased [rotational speed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Earth%27s_rotation "Earth's rotation") requires less fuel for launches.[^10] [^11]

## Military development

### Support base

In 2016, construction began on a [Chinese naval](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army_Navy "People's Liberation Army Navy") [outpost](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_naval_base_in_Djibouti "Chinese naval base in Djibouti") in Djibouti. The site was slated to become China's first overseas military installation, with an estimated cost of $600 million. According to Djiboutian Minister of Foreign Affairs [Mahmoud Ali Youssouf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahmoud_Ali_Youssouf "Mahmoud Ali Youssouf"), the 90-acre plot would likely house only 300 Chinese troops and would require the Chinese government to pay the Djiboutian government $20 million annually for ten years with an option for an additional period of ten years.[^12] [^13] [^14] [^15]

## Medical cooperation

On its 2017 mission to Africa, the [People's Liberation Army Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army_Navy "People's Liberation Army Navy") hospital ship *[Peace Ark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_Ark "Peace Ark")* traveled to Djibouti where it treated 7,841 Djiboutian patients.[^1]*<sup><span title="Page: 284">: 284</span></sup>*

## Sovereignty issues

Djibouti follows the [one China principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_principle "One China principle"). It recognizes the People's Republic of China as the sole government of China and [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") as an integral part of China's territory, and supports all efforts by the PRC to "achieve [national reunification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_unification "Chinese unification") ". It also considers [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong"), [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") and [Tibet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region "Tibet Autonomous Region") to be China's internal affairs.[^16]

In July 2019, UN ambassadors from 50 countries, including Djibouti, have signed a joint letter to the [UNHRC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Rights_Council "United Nations Human Rights Council") defending China's [treatment of Uyghurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang_re-education_camps "Xinjiang re-education camps") and other Muslim minority groups in the [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") region.[^17] In June 2020, Djibouti was one of 53 countries that backed the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law") at the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations").[^18]
