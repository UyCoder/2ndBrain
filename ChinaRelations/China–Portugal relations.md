**China–Portugal relations** [^1], can be traced to 1514 during the [Ming dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty") of China. Relations between the modern political entities of the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") and the [Portuguese Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Republic "Portuguese Republic") officially began on 2 February 1979.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 85">: 85</span> </sup> China and Portugal established a [comprehensive strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "Diplomatic partnerships of China") in 2005.[^3] Both nations maintain friendly relations, which is due to three main reasons – the first being the [Portuguese handover of Macau in 1999](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transfer_of_sovereignty_over_Macau "Transfer of sovereignty over Macau"), the second being the Portuguese prominence in the [Lusophone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusophone "Lusophone"), which includes nations China wishes to promote relations with, and third being the extensive history of Portuguese presence in Asia.[^4]

## Economic relations

Trade between the two countries have increased since the resolution of the longstanding issue of Macau's future and the economic reforms of [Deng Xiaoping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping "Deng Xiaoping") in the early 1980s. In 2002, trade between the two countries was valued at $380 million.[^5]

China's exports to Portugal are textile goods, garments, shoes, plastics, acoustic equipment, steel materials, ceramic goods and lighting equipment.[^5] China is Portugal's ninth-largest trading partner.[^6] [^7]

Portugal's exports to China are electric condensers and accessory parts, primary plastics, paper, medicinal, textile goods and wine.[^5] [^6] [^8]

Portugal participated in Shanghai's [Expo 2010](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expo_2010 "Expo 2010") to boost bilateral trade further.[^9]

## Cultural relations

During the celebration of the Year of the Rooster, the Chinese [Lunar New Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lunar_New_Year "Lunar New Year") that fell on 28 January 2017, a huge rooster, the [symbol of Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rooster_of_Barcelos "Rooster of Barcelos"), created by famous Portuguese artist [Joana Vasconcelos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joana_Vasconcelos "Joana Vasconcelos"), was ferried to China from [Lisbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lisbon "Lisbon") to congratulate the Chinese with New Year greetings.[^10]

## History

### Contemporary era

Following World War II, the United Nations expected its member states to relinquish any colonies. Portuguese Prime Minister [Antonio Salazar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ant%C3%B3nio_de_Oliveira_Salazar "António de Oliveira Salazar") sought to resist UN pressure to relinquish Macau.[^11]<sup><span title="Page: 84">: 84</span> </sup> In 1951, the Salazar regime sought to re-characterize Macau not as a colony but as an [overseas province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_divisions_of_Portugal#Former_regions "Political divisions of Portugal") of Portugal, which it viewed as part of a plural-continental but nonetheless unified and indivisible Portuguese state.[^11]<sup><span title="Page: 84">: 84</span></sup>

The Portuguese *[Estado Novo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Estado_Novo_\(Portugal\) "Estado Novo (Portugal)")* adopted the idea of [Lusotropicalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusotropicalism "Lusotropicalism"), which sought to cast [Portugal's imperialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Empire "Portuguese Empire") as a "better" form of colonialism.[^12]<sup><span title="Page: 101">: 101</span> </sup> It contended that Portugal peacefully fostered beneficial race-mixing, while the northern European countries had engaged in true colonialism.[^12]<sup><span title="Page: 101">: 101</span> </sup> As applied by the Portuguese to Macau, this narrative was favorably viewed in China as consistent with the narrative that Portugal had not colonized Macau, but had only been "allowed" to administer Macau.[^12]<sup><span title="Page: 101">: 101</span></sup>

After the [Carnation Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carnation_Revolution "Carnation Revolution"), Portugal began its process of [decolonization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Decolonization "Decolonization").[^13] Over the next several years, Portugal made two offers to return Macau, both of which were rejected by the Chinese government.[^11]<sup><span title="Page: 85">: 85</span> </sup> In 1979, following the formal establishment of diplomatic relations, the two countries reached a secret agreement to characterise Macau as a "Chinese territory under Portuguese administration".[^11]<sup><span title="Page: 85">: 85</span></sup>

Relations between Portugal and China began to improve as talks in relation to Macau's future were conducted, and a final agreement was reached, with Macau being returned to Chinese sovereignty in 1999.[^5] [^14] After Macau was returned to China, its ties with Portugal have largely been based on cultural and economic exchanges.[^15]

China and Portugal both participated in the multi-lateral group [Forum Macao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forum_Macao "Forum Macao"), which China formed in 2003 in order to increase economic and commercial cooperation between China and the [Portuguese-speaking countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese-speaking_world "Portuguese-speaking world").[^16]<sup><span title="Page: 62">: 62</span></sup>
