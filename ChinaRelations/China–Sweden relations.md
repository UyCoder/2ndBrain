[Sweden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweden "Sweden") was the first, followed by [Denmark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Denmark "Denmark"), [Western country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_country "Western country") to establish official diplomatic relations with the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China"), on 9 May 1950.[^1] [^2]

## History

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Embassy_of_People%27s_republic_of_China%2C_Stockholm%2C_Sweden.jpg/250px-Embassy_of_People%27s_republic_of_China%2C_Stockholm%2C_Sweden.jpg)

Chinese embassy in Stockholm

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/13/Goteborg_konsulat_ChRL.jpg/250px-Goteborg_konsulat_ChRL.jpg)

Chinese Consulate-General in Gothenburg

Sweden's and China's ties goes back to the 17th century.[^3] Sweden traded with China, and this was recorded by Nils Matsson Kiöping, who visited southern China on the *[Götheborg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%B6theborg_\(ship\) "Götheborg (ship)")* in 1654 and wrote accounts of his journeys to China upon his return to Sweden.[^3] The [Swedish East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swedish_East_India_Company "Swedish East India Company") traded with China from 1731 to 1813.

New research on 1913-1917 post- [Qing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty") relations claim that the head of the Swedish Geological Survey, Johan Gunnar Andersson's work was part of Sweden's ‘extractive vision’ which dealt with aiding exploitative interests of Swedish industrial and foreign-policy actors by seeking to secure, for Sweden, a quasi-colonial presence in Republican China, centering on large-scale extraction of Chinese iron ore, profit-maximizing iron exports throughout the Pacific region and construction and operation of China's largest steel mills and weapons factories.[^4]

### 20th century

Sweden was the first [Western country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_country "Western country") to establish official diplomatic relations with the People's Republic of China, which took place on 9 May 1950.[^1] [^2] For this occasion, chairman [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong "Mao Zedong") decided to personally receive the Swedish ambassador, [Torsten Hammarström](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Torsten_Hammarstr%C3%B6m "Torsten Hammarström"), as he presented his letter of credentials,[^5] which was quite unusual, and a sign that China attached great importance to this diplomatic breakthrough.[^6]

### 2000s

A modern replica of the [East Indiaman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Indiaman "East Indiaman") [Götheborg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%B6theborg_\(ship\) "Götheborg (ship)") was constructed and departed from [Gothenburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gothenburg "Gothenburg"), Sweden, in 2005. The ship reached Shanghai, China, in 2006. The ship was welcomed in Shanghai by [King Carl XVI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_Carl_XVI "King Carl XVI") and [Queen Silvia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen_Silvia "Queen Silvia") of Sweden who made an official visit to China that year.[^2]

### 2010s

The [disappearance of five Hong Kong booksellers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Causeway_Bay_Books_disappearances "Causeway Bay Books disappearances") including notably the extrajudicial rendition of author–publisher [Gui Minhai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gui_Minhai "Gui Minhai"), a Swedish national, from his residence in Thailand in late 2015 would catalyse sharp deterioration in relations. The Chinese government had been silent about holding him in custody for three months, at which point a controversial video confession was broadcast on mainland media.[^7] On 5 January 2016, the [Minister for Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_for_Foreign_Affairs_\(Sweden\) "Minister for Foreign Affairs (Sweden)") stated that they took a "serious view" on Gui's disappearance.[^8]

On 2 September 2018, a Chinese tourist named Zeng caused a diplomatic incident because he had incorrectly booked a hostel accommodation. Zeng and his elderly parents arrived a day early. They were not allowed to sleep in the lobby overnight, and were forcibly ejected by police when they refused to leave. Zeng accused the Swedish police of having used excess violence. The Chinese Foreign Ministry responded with a security warning on travel to Sweden.[^9]

#### Uyghurs

In July 2019, British [UN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations") [Ambassador](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ambassador "Ambassador") [Karen Pierce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karen_Pierce "Karen Pierce") delivered a joint international statement on [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") at the [United Nations General Assembly's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly "United Nations General Assembly") Third Committee on behalf of 23 countries, including Sweden. The countries said they shared concerns raised by the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations") [International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Convention_on_the_Elimination_of_All_Forms_of_Racial_Discrimination "International Convention on the Elimination of All Forms of Racial Discrimination") regarding "credible reports of mass detention; efforts to restrict cultural and religious practices; [mass surveillance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_surveillance "Mass surveillance") disproportionately targeting ethnic [Uyghurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uyghurs "Uyghurs"); and other human rights violations and abuses." They called on [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") to comply with its national and international obligations to respect human rights, including freedom of religion, and allow UN human rights monitors access to [internment camps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang_internment_camps "Xinjiang internment camps").[^10] [^11] [^12]

#### Continued conflict over Gui Minhai

In November 2019, Chinese ambassador [Gui Congyou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gui_Congyou "Gui Congyou") threatened Sweden saying that "We treat our friends with fine wine, but for our enemies we use shotguns." over the decision by [Swedish PEN](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Swedish_PEN&action=edit&redlink=1 "Swedish PEN (page does not exist)") to award [Gui Minhai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gui_Minhai "Gui Minhai") the [Tucholsky Prize](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Tucholsky_Prize&action=edit&redlink=1 "Tucholsky Prize (page does not exist)"). All eight major Swedish political parties condemned the ambassador's threats. On 4 December, after the prize had been awarded, Ambassador Gui said that one could not both harm China's interests and benefit economically from China; when asked to clarify his remarks he said that China would impose trade restrictions on Sweden. These remarks were backed up by the Chinese Foreign Ministry in Beijing.[^13]

The Swedish Ambassador to China, [Anna Lindstedt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anna_Lindstedt "Anna Lindstedt"), was recalled for arranging a meeting between the daughter of Gui Minhai and two businessmen representing Beijing without the approval or knowledge of the Swedish Foreign Ministry and indicted for unauthorised contacts with a foreign power.[^14]

### 2020s

In January 2020, the Chinese Embassy announced that they would be denying Chinese visas to reporters who criticize China or, as they put it, damage the relationship between China and Sweden. The Ambassador called Sweden's press "lightweight" and said they should not have picked a fight with China who is a "heavyweight" and will now retaliate.[^15]

In February 2020, Sweden's foreign ministry summoned China's ambassador to demand the release of Gui Minhai, a day after he was sentenced to 10 years in jail on charges of illegally providing intelligence to foreigners.[^16]

In 2020, amid the straining of the relations between both countries, Sweden shut down the last of the [Confucius teaching programmes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucius_Institute "Confucius Institute") in the country.[^17] Cities such as Gothenburg, Linköping, Luleå and Västerås have not extended long-standing twinning or partnership agreements with Chinese cities – Shanghai, Guangzhou, Xian and Jinan respectively. The agreement has been renewed continuously but officially expired at the end of 2019.[^18] [^19]

In June 2020, Sweden openly opposed the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law").[^20] [^21] In 2024, the [Jamestown Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamestown_Foundation "Jamestown Foundation") reported that over 100 organizations of the [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") 's [united front](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_front_\(China\) "United front (China)") were in operation in Sweden, including a center dedicated to [technology transfer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Technology_transfer "Technology transfer") as well as cultural, educational, professional, and political groups.[^22] the In April 2024, Sweden deported a Chinese united front activist on national security grounds.[^23] In November 2024, Swedish police opened an investigation into a Chinese shipping vessel, the *[Yi Peng 3](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yi_Peng_3 "Yi Peng 3")*, in the [Baltic Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltic_Sea "Baltic Sea") after it was found be in the vicinity of two severed undersea fiber-optic data cables and suspected of sabotage.[^24] [^25] China's [Ministry of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (China)") refused to permit Swedish prosecutors to board the vessel.[^26]

In March 2026, [Sweden's Security Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swedish_Security_Service "Swedish Security Service") (SAPO) identified China as one of the country's three main security threats alongside Iran and Russia.[^27]

## Trade relations

In 2006, the trade value between the two countries added up to $6.73 billion. As of 2016 Sweden had become China's ninth-largest trading partner in the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") and China had been Sweden's largest trade partner in Asia for four consecutive years.[^2]

## Public opinion

A survey published in 2025 by the [Pew Research Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pew_Research_Center "Pew Research Center") found that 79% of Swedes had an unfavorable view of China, while 18% had a favorable view.[^28]
