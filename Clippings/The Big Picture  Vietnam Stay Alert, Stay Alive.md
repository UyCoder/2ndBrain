“Stay Alert, Stay Alive,” rang through the jungles of South Vietnam as replacement troops of the 1st Brigade, 101st Airborne Division reported for duty. This episode of the Big Picture explores a 6-day, 80-hour, four-phase training program that gave soldiers their “baptism of fire” before engaging with the Viet Cong or North Vietnamese forces. The first phase included physical training to prepare soldiers to survive the weather and terrain of Vietnam. Phase two trained combat-experienced squad leaders in a post-graduate course. The third phase consisted of live-fire exercises. The final phase was a review and analysis of the operations to improve training for other soldiers. Watch this episode of the Big Picture to learn how the “Stay Alert, Stay Alive” program took soldiers through the gauntlet of what they might encounter when fighting the guerillas in the jungles of Vietnam.
