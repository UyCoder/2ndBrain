The **Shanghai Cooperation Organisation** (**SCO**) is a [Eurasian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasia "Eurasia") [political](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics "Politics"), [economic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy "Economy") and [international security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_security "International security") organisation of ten member states. It focuses on political, economic, security and counter-terrorism cooperation.

It is the world's largest [regional organisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_organization "Regional organization") in terms of [geographic scope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography "Geography") and [population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_population "World population"), covering at least 24% of the world's total area (65% of Eurasia) [^9] and 42% of the world population. As of 2024, its combined nominal [GDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_domestic_product "Gross domestic product") accounts for around 23%, while its [GDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gross_domestic_product "Gross domestic product") based on [PPP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purchasing_power_parity "Purchasing power parity") comprises approximately 36% of the world's total.

The SCO is the successor to the Shanghai Five, formed in 1996 between [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China"), [Kazakhstan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kazakhstan "Kazakhstan"), [Kyrgyzstan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyrgyzstan "Kyrgyzstan"), [Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia "Russia"), and [Tajikistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajikistan "Tajikistan"). In June 2001, the leaders of these nations and [Uzbekistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uzbekistan "Uzbekistan") met in [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai") to announce the SCO, a new organisation with deeper political and economic cooperation. In June 2017, it expanded to eight states, with [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India") and [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan "Pakistan"). [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran") joined the group in July 2023, and [Belarus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belarus "Belarus") in July 2024. Several countries are engaged as observers or dialogue partners. Its most recent meeting was held in [September 2025](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Tianjin_SCO_summit "2025 Tianjin SCO summit") in [Tianjin, China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tianjin,_China "Tianjin, China").

The SCO is governed by the Heads of State Council (HSC), its supreme decision-making body, which meets once a year. The organisation also contains the [Regional Anti-Terrorist Structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_Anti-Terrorist_Structure "Regional Anti-Terrorist Structure") (RATS).

## Origins

### The Shanghai Five

The Shanghai Five group was created on 26 April 1996 when the heads of states of China, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan, Russia and Tajikistan signed the Treaty on Deepening Military Trust in Border Regions in [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai").[^10] [^11]

On 24 April 1997 the same countries signed the Treaty on Reduction of Military Forces in Border Regions in a meeting in [Moscow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moscow "Moscow"), Russia.[^12] On 20 May 1997, [Russian President](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Russia "President of Russia") [Boris Yeltsin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boris_Yeltsin "Boris Yeltsin") and [Chinese leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paramount_leader "Paramount leader") [Jiang Zemin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jiang_Zemin "Jiang Zemin") signed a declaration on a " [multipolar world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polarity_\(international_relations\) "Polarity (international relations)") ".[^13]

Subsequent annual summits of the Shanghai Five group occurred in [Almaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almaty "Almaty"), Kazakhstan in 1998, in [Bishkek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bishkek "Bishkek"), Kyrgyzstan in 1999, and in [Dushanbe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dushanbe "Dushanbe"), Tajikistan in 2000. At the Dushanbe summit, members agreed to "oppose intervention in other countries' internal affairs on the reason of 'humanitarianism' and 'protecting human rights;' and support the efforts of one another in safeguarding the five countries' national independence, sovereignty, territorial integrity, and social stability." [^11] The Shanghai Five structure helped speed up the members' resolution of border disputes, agree on military deployments in border areas, and address security threats.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 95">: 95</span></sup>

### Developing institutional forms

In 2001, the annual summit returned to Shanghai and the group was institutionalised.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 95">: 95</span> </sup> The five member nations first admitted [Uzbekistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uzbekistan "Uzbekistan") in the Shanghai Five mechanism.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 95">: 95</span> </sup> On 15 June 2001, all six heads of state signed the *Declaration of Shanghai Cooperation Organisation*, praising the role played thus far by the Shanghai Five mechanism and aiming to transform it to a higher level of cooperation.[^7] From 2001 to 2008, the SCO developed rapidly, establishing a number of permanent bodies and *ad hoc* initiatives dealing with economic and security matters.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 95">: 95</span></sup>

In June 2002, the heads of the SCO member states met in [Saint Petersburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint_Petersburg "Saint Petersburg"), Russia and signed the *SCO Charter* which expounded on the organisation's purposes, principles, structures and forms of operation. It entered into force on 19 September 2003.[^15] By 2003, a Council of Heads of State, a Council of Heads of Government and a Council of Foreign Ministers, as well as a permanent Secretariat based in Beijing was formed.[^16]<sup><span title="Page: 500">: 500</span></sup>

In July 2005, at the summit in [Astana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astana "Astana"), Kazakhstan, with representatives of India, Iran, [Mongolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolia "Mongolia") and Pakistan attending an SCO summit for the first time, [Nursultan Nazarbayev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nursultan_Nazarbayev "Nursultan Nazarbayev"), the president of the Kazakhstan, greeted the guests in words that had never been used before in any context: "The leaders of the states sitting at this negotiation table are representatives of half of humanity".[^17] By 2007, the SCO had initiated over twenty large-scale projects related to transportation, energy and telecommunications and held regular meetings of security, military, defence, foreign affairs, economic, cultural, banking, and other officials from its member states.[^18]

In July 2015, in [Ufa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ufa "Ufa"), Russia, the SCO decided to admit India and Pakistan as full members. In June 2016 in [Tashkent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tashkent "Tashkent"), both signed the memorandum of obligations, thereby starting the process of joining the SCO.[^19] In June 2017, at a summit in Kazakhstan, India and Pakistan officially joined SCO as full members.[^20] [^21]

In 2004, the SCO established relations with the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations") (where it is an observer in the [General Assembly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly "United Nations General Assembly")), the [Commonwealth of Independent States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States "Commonwealth of Independent States") in 2005, the [Association of Southeast Asian Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_of_Southeast_Asian_Nations "Association of Southeast Asian Nations") (ASEAN) in 2005, the [Collective Security Treaty Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collective_Security_Treaty_Organization "Collective Security Treaty Organization") in 2007, the [Economic Cooperation Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_Cooperation_Organization "Economic Cooperation Organization") in 2007, the [United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Office_on_Drugs_and_Crime "United Nations Office on Drugs and Crime") in 2011, the [Conference on Interaction and Confidence-Building Measures in Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conference_on_Interaction_and_Confidence-Building_Measures_in_Asia "Conference on Interaction and Confidence-Building Measures in Asia") (CICA) in 2014, and the [United Nations Economic and Social Commission for Asia and the Pacific](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Economic_and_Social_Commission_for_Asia_and_the_Pacific "United Nations Economic and Social Commission for Asia and the Pacific") (ESCAP) in 2015.[^22] in 2018, SCO [Regional Anti-Terrorist Structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_Anti-Terrorist_Structure "Regional Anti-Terrorist Structure") (RATS) has established relations with the [African Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Union "African Union") 's African Centre for the Study and Research on Terrorism (ACSRT).[^23]

The Secretariat of the SCO, headquartered in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing"), China, is the primary executive body of the organisation. It serves to implement organisational decisions and decrees, drafts proposed documents (such as declarations and agendas), function as a document depository for the organisation, arranges specific activities within the SCO framework, and promotes and disseminates information about the SCO. The SCO secretary-general is elected to a three-year term.[^30] [Nurlan Yermekbayev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nurlan_Yermekbayev "Nurlan Yermekbayev") of Kazakhstan became the current secretary-general on 1 January 2025.

The [Regional Anti-Terrorist Structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_Anti-Terrorist_Structure "Regional Anti-Terrorist Structure") (RATS) Executive Committee, headquartered in [Tashkent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tashkent "Tashkent"), Uzbekistan, is a permanent organ of the SCO which serves to promote cooperation of member states against the [three evils](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_evils "Three evils") of [terrorism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrorism "Terrorism"), [separatism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Separatism "Separatism") and [extremism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extremism "Extremism"). The Director of SCO RATS Executive Committee is elected to a three-year term. [Ularbek Sharsheev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ularbek_Sharsheev "Ularbek Sharsheev") of Kyrgyzstan became the current Director on 1 January 2025. Each member state also sends a permanent representative to RATS.[^31]

The official languages of the SCO are [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_Chinese "Standard Chinese") and [Russian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language").[^7]

The SCO has a youth group, which is entitled the **Score Foundation**.[^32]

#### Countries showing interest in joining the SCO

##### Turkey

In 2010, the SCO approved a procedure for admitting new members.[^69] In 2011, [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") applied for dialogue partner status,[^70] which it obtained in 2013. At the same time, Turkey is a [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO") member and the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") candidate country. Turkish prime minister [Recep Tayyip Erdoğan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan "Recep Tayyip Erdoğan") has stated that he has discussed the possibility of abandoning Turkey's candidacy of [accession to the](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accession_of_Turkey_to_the_European_Union "Accession of Turkey to the European Union") [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") in return for full membership in the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation.[^71] This was reinforced again on 21 November 2016, after the [European Parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Parliament "European Parliament") voted unanimously to suspend accession negotiations with Turkey.[^72] Two days later, on 23 November 2016, Turkey was granted the chairmanship of SCO energy club for the 2017 period. That made Turkey the first country to chair a club in the organisation without full membership status. In 2022, at the 22nd summit of the SCO, the Turkish president said that Turkey would seek full SCO membership status.[^73] On 11 July 2024, Turkish president [Recep Tayyip Erdoğan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan "Recep Tayyip Erdoğan") stated in a statement to the U.S. *[Newsweek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newsweek "Newsweek")* magazine that they did not consider Turkey's membership in NATO as an alternative to the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation and [BRICS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BRICS "BRICS").[^74]

##### Vietnam

In 2011, [Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam "Vietnam") expressed interest in obtaining observer status (but has not applied for it).[^70] Vietnamese PM Pham Minh Chinh attended the largest-ever Shanghai Cooperation Organization (SCO) summit in history in Tianjian from August 31 to September 1, 2025. On April 17, 2026, it was reported that during a state visit to China by Vietnamese President [Tô Lâm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T%C3%B4_L%C3%A2m "Tô Lâm"), Vietnam "expressed its readiness to maintain exchanges on the possibility of becoming a partner of the SCO." [^75]

##### Ukraine

In 2012, [Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ukraine "Ukraine") expressed interest in obtaining observer status. However, since the deposition of President [Viktor Yanukovych](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viktor_Yanukovych "Viktor Yanukovych") and [increased tensions with Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Ukrainian_War "Russo-Ukrainian War"), no application has been submitted and there are no current plans to incorporate Ukraine into the organisation.[^76] [^77]

[Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan "Azerbaijan") expects to receive observer status according to Azerbaijani Foreign Ministry spokesperson Aykhan Hajizada. Azerbaijan will probably become a full member of the SCO in a little while, President of the Republic of Kazakhstan Kassym-Jomart Tokayev said during his meeting with President of the Republic of Azerbaijan Ilham Aliyev on 3 July 2024 in Astana.[^78] [^79]

During [2025 Tianjin SCO summit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2025_Tianjin_SCO_summit "2025 Tianjin SCO summit"), [CCP general secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") met [Burmese military ruler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Security_and_Peace_Commission "State Security and Peace Commission") [Min Aung Hlaing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Min_Aung_Hlaing "Min Aung Hlaing") to express his support to promote [Myanmar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar "Myanmar") to be a full member.[^80]

## Activities

### Cooperation on security

As of 2023, the SCO is primarily centred on security-related concerns, describing the main threats it confronts as being [terrorism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrorism "Terrorism"), [separatism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Separatism "Separatism") and [extremism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extremism "Extremism"). It has addressed regional [human trafficking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_trafficking "Human trafficking") and [weapons trafficking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arms_trafficking "Arms trafficking") and created terrorist blacklists.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 96">: 96</span></sup>

At SCO summit, held in Tashkent, Uzbekistan, on 16–17 June 2004, the Regional Anti-Terrorist Structure (RATS) was established. On 21 April 2006, the SCO announced plans to fight cross-border drug crimes under the counter-terrorism rubric.[^81]

In October 2007, the SCO signed an agreement with the [Collective Security Treaty Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collective_Security_Treaty_Organization "Collective Security Treaty Organization") (CSTO), in the Tajik capital of Dushanbe, to broaden cooperation on issues such as security, crime, and drug trafficking.[^82]

As of 2010, the organisation was opposing [cyberwarfare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyberwarfare "Cyberwarfare"), saying that the dissemination of information "harmful to the spiritual, moral and cultural spheres of other states" should be considered a "security threat". An accord adopted in 2009 defined " [information war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_warfare "Information warfare") ", in part, as an effort by a state to undermine another's "political, economic, and social systems".[^83] *[The Diplomat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Diplomat_\(magazine\) "The Diplomat (magazine)")* reported in 2017 that SCO has foiled 600 terror plots and extradited 500 terrorists through RATS.[^84] The 36th meeting of the Council of the RATS decided to hold a joint anti-terror exercise, Pabbi-Antiterror-2021, in Pakistan in 2021.[^85]

At the summit in [Astana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astana "Astana"), Kazakhstan, in July 2024, the SCO called for the creation of a fair, multipolar world order based on the key role of the United Nations, international law and the aspiration of sovereign states towards a mutually beneficial partnership.[^86]

### Military activities

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Putin_and_Hu_JintaoPeace_Mission_2007.jpg/250px-Putin_and_Hu_JintaoPeace_Mission_2007.jpg)

SCO leaders at Peace Mission 2007. Hu Jintao, Vladimir Putin Nursultan Nazarbayev and Islam Karimov

As of 2009, the organisation's activities expanded to include increased military cooperation, [intelligence sharing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intelligence_sharing "Intelligence sharing"), and [counterterrorism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Counterterrorism "Counterterrorism").[^87] At the same time, leaders of SCO states repeatedly stated that the SCO was not a military alliance.[^88]

As of 2023, the SCO had not provided military support in any actual conflicts.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 100">: 100</span> </sup> However, as of 2017, military exercises have regularly been conducted among members to promote cooperation and coordination against terrorism and other external threats, and to maintain regional peace and stability.[^7] There have been a number of SCO joint military exercises. The first of these was held in 2003, with the first phase taking place in Kazakhstan and the second in China. Since then China and Russia have teamed up for large-scale war games in [Peace Mission 2005](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_Mission_2005 "Peace Mission 2005"), Peace Mission 2007 and Peace Mission 2009, under the auspices of the Shanghai Cooperation Organisation. More than 4,000 soldiers participated at the joint military exercises in Peace Mission 2007, which took place in [Chelyabinsk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chelyabinsk "Chelyabinsk"), Russia near the [Ural Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ural_Mountains "Ural Mountains"), as was agreed upon in April 2006 at a meeting of SCO defence ministers.[^89] [^90] In 2010, Russian defence minister [Sergei Ivanov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sergei_Ivanov "Sergei Ivanov") said that the exercises would be transparent and open to media and the public. Following the war games' successful completion, Russian officials began speaking of India joining such exercises in the future and the SCO taking on a military role. Peace Mission 2010, conducted 9–25 September at Kazakhstan's Matybulak training area, saw over 5,000 personnel from China, Russia, Kazakhstan, Kyrgyzstan and Tajikistan conduct joint planning and operational maneuvers.[^91]

The SCO has served as a platform for larger military announcements by members. During the 2007 war games in Russia, with leaders of SCO member states in attendance including Chinese leader [Hu Jintao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hu_Jintao "Hu Jintao"), Russia's president [Vladimir Putin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Putin "Vladimir Putin") used the occasion to take advantage of a captive audience. Russian strategic bombers, he said, would resume regular long-range patrols for the first time since the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War"). "Starting today, such tours of duty will be conducted regularly and on the strategic scale", Putin said. "Our pilots have been grounded for too long. They are happy to start a new life".[^92] [^93]

In June 2014, in the Tajik capital Dushanbe, the idea was brought up to merge the SCO with the [Collective Security Treaty Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collective_Security_Treaty_Organization "Collective Security Treaty Organization"). However, as of late 2022, in the wake of [Russian invasion of Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine "Russian invasion of Ukraine") in 2022, many SCO and even CSTO members had distanced themselves from military cooperation with Russia.[^94]

### Economic cooperation

In September 2003, a Framework Agreement to enhance economic cooperation was signed by the SCO member states. At the same meeting the [premier of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Premier_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Premier of the People's Republic of China"), [Wen Jiabao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wen_Jiabao "Wen Jiabao"), proposed a long-term objective to establish a [free trade area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_trade_area "Free trade area") in the SCO, while other more immediate measures would be taken to improve the flow of goods in the region.[^95] [^96] A follow-up plan with 100 specific actions was signed one year later, on 23 September 2004.[^97]

In October 2005, during the Moscow Summit of the SCO, the Secretary General of the Organisation said that the SCO would prioritise joint energy projects; including in the oil and gas sector, the exploration of new [hydrocarbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydrocarbon "Hydrocarbon") reserves, and joint use of water resources. The creation of the [SCO Interbank Consortium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/SCO_Interbank_Consortium "SCO Interbank Consortium") was also agreed upon in order to fund future joint projects. In February 2006, the first meeting of the SCO Interbank Association was held in Beijing.[^98] [^99] In November 2006, at *The SCO: Results and Perspectives*, an international conference held in Almaty, the representative of the [Russian Foreign Ministry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Foreign_Ministry "Russian Foreign Ministry") announced that Russia was developing plans for an SCO "Energy Club".[^100] in November 2007, Moscow reiterated the need for this "energy club" at an SCO summit. Other SCO members, however, did not commit themselves to the idea.[^101] During the 2008 summit it was stated that "Against the backdrop of a slowdown in the growth of world economy pursuing a responsible currency and financial policy, control over the capital flowing, ensuring food and [energy security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_security "Energy security") have been gaining special significance".[^102]

At the 2007 SCO summit, Iranian vice president [Parviz Davoodi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parviz_Davoodi "Parviz Davoodi") addressed an initiative that had been garnering greater interest when he said, "The Shanghai Cooperation Organisation is a good venue for designing a new banking system which is independent from international banking systems".[^103]

President Putin included these comments:

> We now clearly see the defectiveness of the monopoly in world finance and the policy of economic selfishness. To solve the current problem Russia will take part in changing the global financial structure so that it will be able to guarantee stability and prosperity in the world and to ensure progress.  
> The world is seeing the emergence of a qualitatively different [geo-political](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geo-political "Geo-political") situation, with the emergence of new centers of economic growth and political influence.  
> We will witness and take part in the transformation of the global and regional security and development architectures adapted to new realities of the 21st century, when stability and prosperity are becoming inseparable notions.[^104]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/The_Prime_Minister%2C_Dr._Manmohan_Singh_in_a_group_photo_with_the_Heads_of_State_Council_of_Shanghai_Cooperation_Organization_%28SCO%29%2C_in_Yekaterinburg_on_June_16%2C_2009.jpg/250px-thumbnail.jpg)

Leaders present at the SCO summit in Yekaterinburg, Russia in 2009

In June 2009, at the Yekaterinburg Summit, China announced plans to provide a US$10 billion loan to other SCO member states to shore up the struggling economies of its members affected by the [2008 financial crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_financial_crisis "2008 financial crisis").[^105] The summit was held together with the [first BRIC summit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1st_BRIC_summit "1st BRIC summit"), and the China–Russia joint statement said that they want a bigger quota in the [International Monetary Fund](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund "International Monetary Fund").[^106]

In 2014, the [Eurasian Economic Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasian_Economic_Union "Eurasian Economic Union") was founded in which Russia, Kazakhstan and Kyrgyzstan are members.

During the 2019 Bishkek summit, [Pakistani Prime Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistani_Prime_Minister "Pakistani Prime Minister") [Imran Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imran_Khan "Imran Khan") suggested taking steps to trade in local currencies instead of U.S. dollars and setting up financial institutions including an SCO bank.[^107]

In June 2022, Iran's Deputy Foreign Minister for Economic Diplomacy Mehdi Safari suggested creating a single SCO currency to facilitate trade and financial transactions among SCO members.[^108]

As part of the SCO's economic agenda, it has established a relatively successful student exchange programme called the SCO University.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 95">: 95</span></sup>

### Cultural cooperation

Culture ministers of the SCO met for the first time in Beijing on 12 April 2002, signing a joint statement for continued cooperation. The third meeting of the Culture Ministers took place in Tashkent, Uzbekistan, on 27–28 April 2006.[^109] [^110]

An SCO Arts Festival and Exhibition was held for the first time during the Astana Summit in 2005. Kazakhstan suggested an SCO [folk dance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Folk_dance "Folk dance") festival to take place in 2008, in Astana.[^111]

### SCO+

The SCO+ forum format was initiated by the [United Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Russia "United Russia") party in October 2020. This format includes inter-party interaction not only of the countries of the Shanghai Cooperation Organization (members, observers, candidates) but also of the [CIS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States "Commonwealth of Independent States") and [BRICS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BRICS "BRICS") countries.

It was first used during the SCO+ international inter-party forum "Economy for People" on 22–23 October 2020.[^112] The forum was attended by speakers from 25 countries, including the chairman of the United Russia party, [Dmitry Medvedev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dmitry_Medvedev "Dmitry Medvedev"), ministers of the SCO countries, Serbian president [Aleksandar Vučić](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aleksandar_Vu%C4%8Di%C4%87 "Aleksandar Vučić"), and ambassadors and diplomats of the CIS and BRICS countries. Russian president [Vladimir Putin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Putin "Vladimir Putin") sent greetings to the forum participants.[^113]

## Summits

According to the Charter of the SCO, summits of the Council of Heads of State shall be held annually at alternating venues. The locations of these summits follow the alphabetical order of the member state's name in Russian.[^116] The charter also dictates that the Council of Heads of Government (that is, the Prime Ministers) shall meet annually in a place decided upon by the council members. The Council of Foreign Ministers is supposed to hold a summit one month before the annual summit of Heads of State. Extraordinary meetings of the Council of Foreign Ministers can be called by any two member states.[^116]
