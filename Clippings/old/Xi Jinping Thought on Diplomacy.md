**Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy** ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 习近平外交思想) [^1] is the current [diplomatic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomacy "Diplomacy") and [foreign policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_policy "Foreign policy") doctrine of the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China"). It is a part of the larger [Xi Jinping Thought](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping_Thought "Xi Jinping Thought"), which is a component of modern [Chinese Communist Party ideology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ideology_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "Ideology of the Chinese Communist Party") derived from the speeches of [CCP general secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping"). It was established during the Central Conference on Work Relating to Foreign Affairs, held in June 2018.

According to Chinese foreign minister [Wang Yi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yi_\(politician\) "Wang Yi (politician)"), Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy is "the fundamental guideline for China's diplomatic work is an epoch-making milestone in the diplomatic theory of New China." [^2] The main point of Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy is to orient as much of diplomacy as possible to the [bilateral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilateral_relations "Bilateral relations") level, while still supporting the formal architecture of the international system.[^3] In terms of China's foreign policy, Xi Jinping's " [major-country diplomacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major-country_diplomacy "Major-country diplomacy") " doctrine has replaced the earlier Deng Xiaoping era slogan of " [keep a low profile and build up power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taoguang_Yanghui "Taoguang Yanghui") " and has legitimized a more active role for China on the world stage, particularly with regard to reform of the international order, engaging in open ideological competition with the West, and assuming a greater responsibility for global affairs in accordance with China's rising power and status.[^4]

## History

During the first five years of Xi Jinping's leadership the budget for diplomacy doubled.[^5]

In 2017, [state councillor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_councillor "State councillor") [Yang Jiechi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yang_Jiechi "Yang Jiechi") incorporated the term " [great changes unseen in a century](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_changes_unseen_in_a_century "Great changes unseen in a century") " into the CCP's rhetoric, describing it as a guiding tenet of Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 29">: 29</span></sup>

Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy was officially adopted during the Central Conference on Work Relating to Foreign Affairs in June 2018.[^7] [^8]

In July 2020, the [Ministry of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (China)") inaugurated a [research center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research_center "Research center") named the [Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy Studies Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping_Thought_on_Diplomacy_Studies_Center "Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy Studies Center").[^9] The center is part of the [China Institute of International Studies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Institute_of_International_Studies "China Institute of International Studies").[^10]<sup><span title="Page: 30">: 30</span></sup>

## Content

According to the Central Foreign Affairs Work Conference held on 22-23 June 2018, Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy has ten insistences. These are insisting on:[^8]

1. strengthening the centralized and unified leadership of the Party over foreign affairs with the maintenance of the authority of the [Party Central Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Committee_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "Central Committee of the Chinese Communist Party") as the overall guide;
2. promoting China's [major-country diplomacy with Chinese characteristics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major-country_diplomacy "Major-country diplomacy") with the mission of realizing the [great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_rejuvenation_of_the_Chinese_nation "Great rejuvenation of the Chinese nation");
3. promoting the building of a [community of common destiny for humankind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_of_Common_Destiny "Community of Common Destiny") with the purpose of maintaining world peace and promoting common development;
4. strengthening strategic confidence with [socialism with Chinese characteristics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialism_with_Chinese_characteristics "Socialism with Chinese characteristics") as the fundamental principle;
5. promoting the construction of the " [Belt and Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative") " with the principle of extensive consultation, joint construction and shared benefits;
6. taking the path of peaceful development based on mutual respect and win-win cooperation;
7. building a global partnership based on deepening diplomatic layout;
8. leading the reform of the global governance system with the concept of fairness and justice;
9. safeguarding national sovereignty, security and development interests with the [core interests of the country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%27s_core_interests "China's core interests") as the bottom line;
10. shaping the unique style of China's diplomacy with the combination of the fine traditions of foreign affairs and the characteristics of the times as the direction.

## Reception

Foreign observers have noted that China and the rest of the world play by different rules under Xi Jinping Thought on Diplomacy. This perceived endorsement of Chinese [exceptionalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exceptionalism "Exceptionalism") has been criticized as problematic.[^11] [Wolf warrior diplomacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wolf_warrior_diplomacy "Wolf warrior diplomacy") seems to be positively perceived by the domestic audience, especially those with a more [Chinese nationalist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_nationalism "Chinese nationalism") point of view.[^12]

In terms of theory, parallels have been drawn to [Maoist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maoist "Maoist") international relations theory,[^13] in particular to [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong "Mao Zedong") 's [Three Worlds Theory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Worlds_Theory "Three Worlds Theory").[^3]
