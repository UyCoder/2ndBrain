The [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") and [Sultanate of Oman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oman "Oman") established diplomatic relations in 1978. They formed a strategic partnership in 2018.

## History

During the [Dhofar Rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhofar_War "Dhofar War"), [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") supported the [Popular Front for the Liberation of Oman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhofar_Liberation_Front "Dhofar Liberation Front"). The two countries established [diplomatic relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_relations "Diplomatic relations") on May 25, 1978.[^1] [^2] [^3]

Chinese officials visiting Oman include: [Jia Qinglin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jia_Qinglin "Jia Qinglin"), Chairman of the [National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Committee_of_the_Chinese_People%27s_Political_Consultative_Conference "National Committee of the Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference") (CPPCC) (November 2010); [Zhang Gaoli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhang_Gaoli "Zhang Gaoli"), [Secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Party_Secretary_of_Tianjin "Party Secretary of Tianjin") of the [Tianjin Municipal Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tianjin_Municipal_Committee_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "Tianjin Municipal Committee of the Chinese Communist Party") (September 2011); [Han Qide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_Qide "Han Qide"), [Vice Chairman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vice_Chairperson_of_the_Standing_Committee_of_the_National_People%27s_Congress "Vice Chairperson of the Standing Committee of the National People's Congress") of the [Standing Committee of the National People's Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standing_Committee_of_the_National_People%27s_Congress "Standing Committee of the National People's Congress") (February 2012); Ailigen Yiminbahai, Vice Chairman of the [National People's Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_People%27s_Congress "National People's Congress") (March 2016); [Wang Yong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yong_\(born_1957\) "Wang Yong (born 1957)"), [State Councilor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Councilor_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "State Councilor of the People's Republic of China") (May 2016); [Chen Xiaoguang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chen_Xiaoguang "Chen Xiaoguang"), Vice Chairman of the National Committee of the CPPCC (September 2018); [Wang Yang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yang_\(politician\) "Wang Yang (politician)"), Chairman of the National Committee of the CPPCC (November 2019); [Wang Yi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yi "Wang Yi"), [State Councilor and Foreign Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_of_Foreign_Affairs_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Minister of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China") (March 2021); and [Wei Fenghe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wei_Fenghe "Wei Fenghe"), State Councilor and [Minister of National Defense](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_of_National_Defense_\(China\) "Minister of National Defense (China)") (April 2022).[^4]

Omani officials visiting China include: Said, Vice Chairman of the Omani National Committee (May 2010); Monzeri, Chairman of the National Committee (attended the Oman National Pavilion Day at the [Shanghai World Expo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expo_2010 "Expo 2010") in July 2010); [Minister of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yusuf_bin_Alawi_bin_Abdullah "Yusuf bin Alawi bin Abdullah") Alawi (came to China in June 2014 to attend the 6th Ministerial Conference of the [China-Arab Cooperation Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Arab_States_Cooperation_Forum "China–Arab States Cooperation Forum"), visited China in May 2018, and came to China in July 2018 to attend the 8th Ministerial Conference of the [China-Arab Cooperation Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Arab_States_Cooperation_Forum "China–Arab States Cooperation Forum")); [Consultative Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Khalid_bin_Hilal_bin_Nasser_Al_Mawali&action=edit&redlink=1 "Khalid bin Hilal bin Nasser Al Mawali (page does not exist)") Chairman Mawali (May 2016); and [Omani Foreign Minister Badr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Badr_bin_Hamad_Al_Busaidi "Badr bin Hamad Al Busaidi") Badr (January 2022).

In 2018, China and Oman agreed to a [strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "Diplomatic partnerships of China").[^5]<sup><span title="Page: 211">: 211</span></sup>

On March 29, 2021, Chinese Foreign Minister [Wang Yi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yi "Wang Yi") met with Omani [Deputy Prime Minister Fahd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fahd_bin_Mahmoud_al_Said "Fahd bin Mahmoud al Said") and the Minister of the [Omani Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate_of_Oman "Sultanate of Oman") 's Office Sultan in [Muscat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muscat "Muscat").[^6] [^7] He then held talks with [Omani Foreign Minister Badr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Badr_bin_Hamad_Al_Busaidi "Badr bin Hamad Al Busaidi"), after which the two sides announced they had reached an agreement on signing a comprehensive [visa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visa_waiver_program "Visa waiver program") agreement.[^8] [^9] Prior to the talks, the two sides jointly witnessed the signing of the implementation plan of the [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") - [Arab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_world "Arab world") government agreement on culture, news and health, and attended the unveiling ceremony of the model of the [Zheng He](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zheng_He "Zheng He") monument.[^10]

## Economic relations

In the 2010s, [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") invested heavily in projects in [Oman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oman "Oman") through the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative"). It purchased land in [Duqm Port](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duqm_Port "Duqm Port") and invested in the first phase of the [Duqm Special Economic Zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Duqm_Special_Economic_Zone_Authority&action=edit&redlink=1 "Duqm Special Economic Zone Authority (page does not exist)"), intended to be the site of the largest [oil storage facility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oil_storage "Oil storage") in the [Middle East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_East "Middle East"). [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") stated its intention to build facilities such as an [Oil refinery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oil_refinery "Oil refinery"), while also reportedly preparing to build a [naval base](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naval_base "Naval base").[^11]
