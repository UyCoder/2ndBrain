[Brunei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brunei "Brunei") and [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") established formal [diplomatic relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_relations "Diplomatic relations") in 1991. Brunei has an embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing"), and [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") has an embassy in [Bandar Seri Begawan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bandar_Seri_Begawan "Bandar Seri Begawan").[^1]

## History

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/The_Tomb_of_the_King_of_Boni_%28Brunei%29_Nanjing.JPG/250px-The_Tomb_of_the_King_of_Boni_%28Brunei%29_Nanjing.JPG)

The tomb of a chieftain from Borneo in Nanjing, China.

Direct contact between China and Brunei dates back to at least the 10th century, as evidenced by the discovery of Chinese artifacts at [Kampong Limau Manis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kampong_Limau_Manis "Kampong Limau Manis") in Brunei Darussalam. Over 50,000 ceramic shards, dated between the 10th and 14th centuries, have been unearthed at the site, indicating a long-standing relationship between the two regions.[^2]

In 1932, China declared the [Paracel Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paracel_Islands "Paracel Islands") as its southernmost territory, excluding the [Spratly Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spratly_Islands "Spratly Islands"), where Brunei has a claim.[^3] [^4]

During Brunei's period as a British protectorate (19th century–1984), direct relations with China were limited. After gaining independence on 1 January 1984,[^5] Brunei joined [ASEAN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_of_Southeast_Asian_Nations "Association of Southeast Asian Nations") in 1991, formalizing diplomatic relations with China.[^1]

In June 2013, the Chinese naval hospital ship *[Peace Ark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_hospital_ship_Daishan_Dao "Chinese hospital ship Daishan Dao")* visited Brunei.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 162">: 162</span></sup>

In 2019, [Chinese leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paramount_leader "Paramount leader") and [General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") visited Brunei. His visit was described in local media as a significant diplomatic event.[^7] Chinese investments such as the Hengyi Petrochemical Complex and StarCity Brunei have been noted for their contributions to the local economy.[^8]

## Economic relations

In 2011, trade between China and Brunei reached approximately US$1.3 billion,.[^5] The two nations have focused on cooperation in areas such as infrastructure development, agriculture, and fisheries.[^9]

In 2014, the two countries established the Brunei-Guangxi Economic Corridor, with pledged joint investments of approximately US$500 million.[^10]
