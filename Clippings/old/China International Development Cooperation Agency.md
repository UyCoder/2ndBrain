The **China International Development Cooperation Agency** (**CIDCA**), branded as **China Aid**,[^1] is the foreign aid and international development agency of the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") (PRC). It is a deputy ministerial-level agency affiliated with the [State Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Council_of_China "State Council of China"). Its headquarters are in [Dongcheng, Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dongcheng,_Beijing "Dongcheng, Beijing").

## History

On 17 March 2018, the first session of the [13th National People's Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/13th_National_People%27s_Congress "13th National People's Congress") adopted a plan as part of the [deepening the reform of the Party and state institutions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deepening_the_reform_of_the_Party_and_state_institutions "Deepening the reform of the Party and state institutions") that stipulated the creation of CIDCA.[^2] On 18 April 2018, the CIDCA held an unveiling ceremony at which director of the [Central Foreign Affairs Commission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Foreign_Affairs_Commission "Central Foreign Affairs Commission") Offıce [Yang Jiechi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yang_Jiechi "Yang Jiechi") and [Minister of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Minister of Foreign Affairs (China)") [Wang Yi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yi_\(politician\) "Wang Yi (politician)") delivered a speech. After the unveiling ceremony, the CIDCA held its founding meeting.[^3]

The agency was created to "achieve better coordination and greater impact" of [China's aid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_foreign_aid "Chinese foreign aid") programs, particularly in support of the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative"). It was formerly the Department of Foreign Aid of the [Ministry of Commerce](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Commerce_\(China\) "Ministry of Commerce (China)") (MOFCOM).[^4] [^5]

Former vice foreign minister [Luo Zhaohui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luo_Zhaohui "Luo Zhaohui") was appointed CIDCA's director in 2021.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 74">: 74</span></sup>

As of at least 2024, CIDCA has placed greater emphasis on using foreign aid to advance [foreign policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_policy_of_China "Foreign policy of China") objectives as opposed to [foreign trade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade_policy_of_China "Trade policy of China") objectives.[^6]<sup><span title="Page: 18">: 18</span></sup>

Following the cutbacks and *de facto* abolition of [USAID](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Agency_for_International_Development "United States Agency for International Development") under the [Second Trump administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Trump_administration "Second Trump administration") for much of 2025, multiple analysts speculate that CIDCA could fill in the void on foreign aid while strategically increasing its influence.[^7] [^8]

## Functions

The CIDCA is China's principle foreign aid agency, and is in charge of foreign aid policy, foreign aid reform, and the planning, negotiation, monitoring, and evaluation of various foreign aid projects.[^9] In December 2021, the joint *Measures for the Administration of Foreign Aid* issued by CIDCA, MOFCOM, and the [Ministry of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (China)") state that CIDCA is in charge of drafting aid policies, guidelines, annual plans, and budgets.[^6]<sup><span title="Pages: 146–147">: 146–147</span> </sup> According to the *Measures*, MOFCOM is in charge of implementing foreign aid projects and selecting the firms to undertake them while the Ministry of Foreign Affairs makes recommendations based on diplomatic needs and its consulates and embassies supervise overseas projects.[^6]<sup><span title="Pages: 146–147">: 146–147</span></sup>

## Organizational structure

The CIDCA is a deputy ministerial-level agency affiliated with the [State Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Council_of_China "State Council of China").[^10] Its headquarters are in [Dongcheng District](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dongcheng,_Beijing "Dongcheng, Beijing").[^11] It operates under the supervision of the [Minister of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Minister of Foreign Affairs (China)") under their capacity as [State Councilor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_councillor "State councillor").[^9] According to the "Regulations on the Functional Configuration, Internal Organizations and Staffing of the National International Development Cooperation Agency", the internal organization of the International Development Cooperation Agency is at the deputy department–bureau level, and the following organizations are set up:[^12]

### Departments

- General Department
- Policy and Planning Department
- Regional First Department
- Regional Second Department
- Supervision and Evaluation Department
- International Cooperation Department
- Institutional Party Committee (Human Resources Department)
