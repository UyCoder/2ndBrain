In 2025, Kyrgyzstan’s licensed cryptocurrency market moved more than $30 billion — more than the country’s entire GDP.  
  
In this episode, Bruce Pannier explains how Kyrgyzstan became Central Asia’s most ambitious crypto state: launching gold-backed and som-backed stablecoins, building a national crypto reserve, and positioning itself as a regional digital-finance hub.  
  
But the boom comes with major risks. Kyrgyzstan’s crypto corridor now sits at the crossroads of migrant remittances, Russian sanctions evasion networks, Western financial pressure, and IMF scrutiny.
