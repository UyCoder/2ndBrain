**Bahrain–China relations** refer to the [bilateral relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilateral_relations "Bilateral relations") between the [Kingdom of Bahrain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bahrain "Bahrain") and the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China").

## History

On April 18, 1989, [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") and [Bahrain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bahrain "Bahrain") formally established diplomatic relations. After the establishment of diplomatic relations, the relationship between the two countries developed smoothly.[^1] [Bahrain Prime Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Bahrain "Prime Minister of Bahrain") [Khalifa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salman_bin_Hamad_Al_Khalifa "Salman bin Hamad Al Khalifa") visited China in 2002. On March 29, 2021, [Chinese Foreign Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Minister of Foreign Affairs (China)") [Wang Yi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yi "Wang Yi") arrived in Bahrain.[^2] [Bahraini King Hamad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamad_bin_Isa_Al_Khalifa "Hamad bin Isa Al Khalifa") hosted a banquet for [Wang Yi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yi "Wang Yi").[^3] Subsequently, the [Crown Prince of Bahrain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_Prince_of_Bahrain "Crown Prince of Bahrain") and [Prime Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_Pakistan "Prime Minister of Pakistan") [Salman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salman_bin_Hamad_Al_Khalifa "Salman bin Hamad Al Khalifa") met with Wang Yi in [Manama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manama "Manama"). On the same day, Wang Yi held talks with [Bahraini Foreign Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(Bahrain\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Bahrain)") [Zayani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdullatif_bin_Rashid_Al_Zayani "Abdullatif bin Rashid Al Zayani"). Both sides expressed their willingness to strengthen the connection between the "2030 Economic Development Vision" and the " [Belt and Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative") " initiative and expand cooperation between the two countries in trade, investment, energy, science and technology, infrastructure and other fields.[^4] Finally, the two sides signed the China-Bahrain Cultural Center Agreement and the Implementation Plan for Cultural Cooperation between the Two Countries. During the visit of the [King of Bahrain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_of_Bahrain "King of Bahrain") to China in May 2024, China and Bahrain established a comprehensive strategic partnership.[^5] [^6]

## Economic relations

The economic relationship between the People's Republic of China and the Kingdom of Bahrain has grown steadily since the establishment of diplomatic relations on April 18, 1989.[^7] Over the years, the two nations have expanded their cooperation across sectors such as trade, investment, infrastructure development, and cultural exchange.

### Trade and investment

In 2022, the total trade volume between China and Bahrain reached approximately US$2.36 billion. China's exports to Bahrain were valued at around US$2.25 billion, while imports from Bahrain stood at US$110 million, with raw aluminum constituting a major portion of Bahrain's exports to China.[^8] China is Bahrain's largest import partner, supplying mechanical and electrical products, high-tech items, and textiles.

### Strategic partnership

In May 2024, [General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") and [King](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_of_Bahrain "King of Bahrain") [Hamad bin Isa Al Khalifa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamad_bin_Isa_Al_Khalifa "Hamad bin Isa Al Khalifa") announced the establishment of a comprehensive strategic partnership between China and Bahrain. The agreement aims to strengthen cooperation in energy, transportation, investment, renewable energy, and the digital economy.[^9] Both nations also emphasized expanding cultural and people-to-people exchanges.

### Infrastructure and development projects

Chinese firms have participated extensively in Bahrain's infrastructure sector. [The China Machinery Engineering Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Machinery_Engineering_Corporation "China Machinery Engineering Corporation") and Power Construction Corporation of China have initiated projects valued at a combined US$1.42 billion, including major real estate and utilities developments.[^8] In January 2024, the first phase of a housing project in Sitra, providing nearly 3,000 units, was inaugurated.

### Financial Collaboration

In April 2024, [Bahrain-based Investcorp](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Investcorp "Investcorp"), in partnership with China's sovereign wealth fund [China Investment Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Investment_Corporation "China Investment Corporation") (CIC), launched a US$1 billion fund targeting high-growth sectors such as consumer goods, healthcare, logistics, and business services across the Gulf and China.[^10]

### Cultural and educational exchanges

In 2014, the [University of Bahrain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/University_of_Bahrain "University of Bahrain") established a [Confucius Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucius_Institute "Confucius Institute") to promote Chinese language and culture.[^7] In addition, both countries have signed agreements to open cultural centers in their respective capitals, enhancing mutual cultural understanding.
