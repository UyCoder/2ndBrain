Diplomatic relations between [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") and [Mongolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolia "Mongolia") were formally established in October 1949. During the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War"), relations were determined by the relations between China and the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union"), Mongolia's [other neighbour and main ally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_sphere_of_influence "Soviet sphere of influence") until [early 1990](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolian_Revolution_of_1990 "Mongolian Revolution of 1990"). With the rapprochement between the USSR and China in the late 1980s, Chinese-Mongolian relations also began to improve. Since the 1990s, China has become Mongolia's biggest trading partner, and a number of Chinese businesses operate in Mongolia.

## Background

### Historical

The [Han](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_Chinese "Han Chinese") and [Mongol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongols "Mongols") (as well as their ancestors, the [Proto-Mongols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Mongols "Proto-Mongols")) peoples have been in contact with each other for millennia.

Throughout history, polities in the [Central Plains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhongyuan "Zhongyuan") and the [Mongolian Plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolian_Plateau "Mongolian Plateau") have had complicated relations. The [Great Wall of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Wall_of_China "Great Wall of China") was constructed to ward off attacks by nomads from the north, notably during the [Han](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty") and [Ming](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty") dynasties. The [Tang dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_dynasty "Tang dynasty"), following its defeat of the [Xueyantuo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xueyantuo "Xueyantuo"), established the [Protectorate General to Pacify the North](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protectorate_General_to_Pacify_the_North "Protectorate General to Pacify the North") in 647 to rule the Mongolian Plateau.

In 1271, the Mongols under [Kublai Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kublai_Khan "Kublai Khan"), grandson of [Genghis Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genghis_Khan "Genghis Khan"), established the [Yuan dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuan_dynasty "Yuan dynasty") as a Chinese dynasty and conquered all of [China proper](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_proper "China proper") in 1279. In 1368, the Ming dynasty successfully overthrew the Yuan dynasty and the remnant Yuan imperial court was forced to retreat north, thereby forming the [Northern Yuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Yuan_dynasty "Northern Yuan dynasty").

The [Ming Great Wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_Great_Wall "Ming Great Wall") was strengthened and the period was characterized by repeated Ming raids into Northern Yuan territory and vice versa. During the [transition from Ming to Qing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transition_from_Ming_to_Qing "Transition from Ming to Qing"), the last monarch of all Mongols [Ligdan Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ligdan_Khan "Ligdan Khan") allied with the Ming against the [Qing dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty") until Ligdan was defeated by Qing forces and [Inner Mongolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inner_Mongolia "Inner Mongolia") was conquered by the [Manchus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchus "Manchus") in 1635. In 1644, the Ming dynasty was overthrown by peasant rebels under [Li Zicheng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Zicheng "Li Zicheng"), who established the short-lived [Shun dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shun_dynasty "Shun dynasty") which would soon be defeated by the Qing dynasty. After 1691, [Outer Mongolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_Mongolia "Outer Mongolia") were incorporated into the Qing empire during the [Dzungar–Qing Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dzungar%E2%80%93Qing_Wars "Dzungar–Qing Wars").

### Republic of China

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f9/%D0%9C%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B3%D0%BE%D0%BB%D1%8B%D0%BD_%D3%A9%D3%A9%D1%80%D1%82%D3%A9%D3%A9_%D1%8D%D0%B7%D1%8D%D1%80%D1%85%D1%8D%D1%85_%D0%B7%D0%B0%D1%81%D0%B3%D0%B8%D0%B9%D0%BD_%D1%83%D1%81%D1%82%D0%B3%D0%B0%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%BD_%D1%82%D1%83%D1%85%D0%B0%D0%B9_%D1%91%D1%81%D0%BB%D0%BE%D0%BB.png/250px-%D0%9C%D0%BE%D0%BD%D0%B3%D0%BE%D0%BB%D1%8B%D0%BD_%D3%A9%D3%A9%D1%80%D1%82%D3%A9%D3%A9_%D1%8D%D0%B7%D1%8D%D1%80%D1%85%D1%8D%D1%85_%D0%B7%D0%B0%D1%81%D0%B3%D0%B8%D0%B9%D0%BD_%D1%83%D1%81%D1%82%D0%B3%D0%B0%D1%81%D0%B0%D0%BD_%D1%82%D1%83%D1%85%D0%B0%D0%B9_%D1%91%D1%81%D0%BB%D0%BE%D0%BB.png)

Ceremony of the destruction of Mongolia's autonomy in 1920

[Bogd Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bogd_Khanate "Bogd Khanate") in Outer Mongolia declared its independence in 1911 after more than 200 years of Qing rule.[^1] The [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") was established in 1912 after the fall of the Qing dynasty. During this period, the [Beiyang government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beiyang_government "Beiyang government") of the Republic of China, as the successor to the Qing, claimed Outer Mongolia as Chinese territory. This claim was provided for in the [Imperial Edict of the Abdication of the Qing Emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_Edict_of_the_Abdication_of_the_Qing_Emperor "Imperial Edict of the Abdication of the Qing Emperor") signed by the [Empress Dowager Longyu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empress_Dowager_Longyu "Empress Dowager Longyu") on behalf of the six-year-old [Xuantong Emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puyi "Puyi"): "\[...\] the continued territorial integrity of the lands of the five races, [Manchu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchu_people "Manchu people"), Han, Mongol, [Hui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hui_people "Hui people"), and [Tibetan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibetan_people "Tibetan people") into one great Republic of China" (\[...\] 仍合滿、漢、蒙、回、藏五族完全領土，為一大中華民國).[^2] [^3] [^4] However, the Chinese government lacked any stable control over the region due to massive civil wars in the south and the rise of regional warlords in the [Warlord Era](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Warlord_Era "Warlord Era"). Consequently, Outer Mongolia sought Russian support to claim its independence. In 1919, Chinese general [Xu Shuzheng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xu_Shuzheng "Xu Shuzheng") advanced into Outer Mongolia and [annulled its independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupation_of_Mongolia "Occupation of Mongolia"). In 1921, Chinese forces were driven out by [White Russian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_movement "White movement") forces led by Baron [Roman von Ungern-Sternberg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_von_Ungern-Sternberg "Roman von Ungern-Sternberg").[^5] Some months later they were driven out by the [Red Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Army "Red Army") of the Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic, the Far Eastern Republic and pro-Soviet Mongolian forces. In 1924, the [Mongolian People's Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolian_People%27s_Republic "Mongolian People's Republic") was proclaimed. With the onset of the [Second Sino-Japanese War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War "Second Sino-Japanese War"), little effort was given to reestablish Chinese control over Outer Mongolia.

Following the end of World War II, the Republic of China, led by the [Kuomintang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuomintang "Kuomintang"), was forced to formally accept Outer Mongolian independence under Soviet pressure. Recognition of Mongolian independence was stipulated in the Sino-Soviet agreement of August 14, 1945. The Chinese government [officially recognized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_recognition "Diplomatic recognition") Mongolian independence in January 1946.[^6] In 1949, the [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") won the [Chinese Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War "Chinese Civil War") and maintained the policy of recognizing Mongolia's independent status.

## Cold War

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/24/1952-10_1952%E5%B9%B49%E6%9C%8828%E6%97%A5%E8%92%99%E5%8F%A4%E5%9B%BD%E6%80%BB%E7%90%86%E6%B3%BD%E7%99%BB%E5%B7%B4%E5%B0%94%E8%AE%BF%E9%97%AE%E4%B8%AD%E5%9B%BD_%E5%91%A8%E6%81%A9%E6%9D%A5_%E6%B3%BD%E7%99%BB%E5%B7%B4%E5%B0%94_%E6%8B%89%E5%A7%86%E8%8B%8F%E4%BC%A6.png/250px-1952-10_1952%E5%B9%B49%E6%9C%8828%E6%97%A5%E8%92%99%E5%8F%A4%E5%9B%BD%E6%80%BB%E7%90%86%E6%B3%BD%E7%99%BB%E5%B7%B4%E5%B0%94%E8%AE%BF%E9%97%AE%E4%B8%AD%E5%9B%BD_%E5%91%A8%E6%81%A9%E6%9D%A5_%E6%B3%BD%E7%99%BB%E5%B7%B4%E5%B0%94_%E6%8B%89%E5%A7%86%E8%8B%8F%E4%BC%A6.png)

Yumjaagiyn Tsedenbal in Beijing in 1952

The People's Republic of China established [diplomatic relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_relations "Diplomatic relations") with Mongolia on October 16, 1949, and both nations signed a border treaty in 1962.[^7] With the [Sino-Soviet split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Soviet_split "Sino-Soviet split"), Mongolia aligned itself with the Soviet Union and asked for the deployment of Soviet forces, leading to security concerns in China.[^8] There were political rhetoric from sections of the Chinese Communist Party (including remarks made by Mao Zedong in 1936) that [Outer Mongolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_Mongolia "Outer Mongolia") would automatically become a part of a future Chinese federation, drawing suspicion from Marshal [Khorloogiin Choibalsan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khorloogiin_Choibalsan "Khorloogiin Choibalsan").[^9] While a border treaty was signed between Mongolia and the People's Republic of China in 1962, it was used by Mongolian leader [Yumjaagiin Tsedenbal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yumjaagiin_Tsedenbal "Yumjaagiin Tsedenbal") to map out permanent boundaries between Mongolia and China. Tsedenbal was upset over Chinese workers in Mongolia, who had become rebellious and were declaring strikes. China, in response, curbed economic aid, and by 1964, pulled out its workers from the country. Mongolia then turned to the Soviet Union for protection.[^10] [^11] During the [Cultural Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultural_Revolution "Cultural Revolution"), [Red Guards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Guards "Red Guards") attacked and set the Mongolian ambassador's car on fire in Beijing.[^12]

As a result, bilateral ties remained tense until 1984, when a high-level Chinese delegation visited Mongolia and both nations started to survey and demarcate their borders. Mongolian General Secretary [Jambyn Batmönkh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jambyn_Batm%C3%B6nkh "Jambyn Batmönkh"), during a meeting with President [Kim Il Sung](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kim_Il_sung "Kim Il sung") while on a [state visit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_visit "State visit") to [Pyongyang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyongyang "Pyongyang") in November 1986 states that "renewing the development of China-Mongolian relations is important for our two countries’ people's common interest".[^13] In 1986, a series of agreements to bolster trade and establish transport and air links was signed.[^8] In 1988, both nations signed a treaty on border control.[^14] Mongolia also started a more independent policy and pursued more friendly ties with China.[^8]

## Recent period

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Belt_and_Road_Initiative_participant_map.svg/250px-Belt_and_Road_Initiative_participant_map.svg.png)

Countries which signed cooperation documents related to the Belt and Road Initiative

In the [post-Cold War era](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Post%E2%80%93Cold_War_era "Post–Cold War era"), China has taken major steps to normalise its relationship with Mongolia, emphasizing its respect for Mongolia's sovereignty and independence. In 1994, [Chinese Premier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prime_Minister_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Prime Minister of the People's Republic of China") [Li Peng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Peng "Li Peng") signed a treaty of friendship and cooperation. China has become Mongolia's biggest trade partner and source of [foreign investment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_direct_investment "Foreign direct investment").[^15] Bilateral trade reached US$1.13 billion by the first nine months of 2007, registering an increase of 90% from 2006.[^16] China offered Mongolia permission to use the [Port of Tianjin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port_of_Tianjin "Port of Tianjin"), allowing access to trade within the [Asia Pacific](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia_Pacific "Asia Pacific") region.[^15] China also expanded its investments in Mongolia's mining industries, giving it access to the country's natural resources.[^15] [^16]

Mongolia participates in the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative").[^17] The BRI has been an important factor in the growing Mongolian view that China is an economic partner rather than a threat to its territorial integrity.[^18]<sup><span title="Page: 208">: 208</span> </sup> Mongolia cooperates in the development of the [China-Mongolia-Russia Economic Corridor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Mongolia%E2%80%93Russia_Economic_Corridor "China–Mongolia–Russia Economic Corridor"), one of the six major land corridors of the BRI.[^19]<sup><span title="Page: 39">: 39</span> </sup> In January 2024, Mongolia stated that it seeks to institute a summit where the three countries can further develop economic opportunities, including the corridor.[^20]

In January 2024, Mongolian Prime Minister [Luvsannamsrai Oyun-Erdene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luvsannamsrain_Oyun-Erdene "Luvsannamsrain Oyun-Erdene") described relations between Mongolia and China as at their highest level and described the two countries as working towards a [comprehensive strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "Diplomatic partnerships of China").[^20]

The Gashuunsukhait–Gantsmod railway is a 19.5 km cross-border extension of the existing Sino-Mongolian rail network, first proposed in 2009. In May 2025, Mongolia's parliament ratified a strategic partnership with China to construct the link, work formally beginning on 14 May 2025, with the aim of eliminating the need for truck haulage across the border. Once operational, the new connection was expected to increase annual coal transport capacity by 30 million tonnes, facilitate the expansion of the [Tavan Tolgoi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tavan_Tolgoi "Tavan Tolgoi") coal mine, and, if later integrated with the Khangi–Mandal and Shiveekhüren–Sekhen crossings, could raise Mongolia's total coal-export capacity to 120 million tonnes by 2030. Construction was set to be overseen by Mongolia's state-owned [Erdenes-Tavantolgoi JSC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erdenes_Tavantolgoy "Erdenes Tavantolgoy") and [China Energy.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CHN_Energy_Investment_Group "CHN Energy Investment Group")[^21]

## Sovereignty issues

Mongolia follows the [one China principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_principle "One China principle"), and recognizes [government of the People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_China "Government of China") as the sole legal government representing the whole of China and Taiwan as "an inalienable part" of China. Mongolia also supports all efforts by the PRC to "achieve peaceful [national reunification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_unification "Chinese unification") " and considers issues related to [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong"), [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") and [Tibet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region "Tibet Autonomous Region") as internal affairs of China.[^22]
