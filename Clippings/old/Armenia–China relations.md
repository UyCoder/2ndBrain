The first references to Armenian-Chinese contact are found in the works of 5th-century historian [Moses of Chorene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moses_of_Chorene "Moses of Chorene") and 6th-century geographer and mathematician [Anania Shirakatsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anania_Shirakatsi "Anania Shirakatsi").[^1] Following the [dissolution of the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union "Dissolution of the Soviet Union"), the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") officially recognized [Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenia "Armenia") on December 27, 1991. Diplomatic relations between Armenia and the People's Republic of China were established on April 6, 1992. The Embassy of China to Armenia was established in July 1992, while the Embassy of Armenia to China started its activities on August 10, 1996.[^2] The [Armenian Ambassador to China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Ambassador_to_China "Armenian Ambassador to China") resides in the Beijing embassy.

Presidents of Armenia [Levon Ter-Petrosyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levon_Ter-Petrosyan "Levon Ter-Petrosyan") and [Robert Kocharyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Kocharyan "Robert Kocharyan") visited the P.R. of China in May 1996 and September 2004. President [Serzh Sargsyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serzh_Sargsyan "Serzh Sargsyan") was in China in May 2010 to participate in the opening ceremony of the "Shanghai World [Expo 2010](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expo_2010 "Expo 2010") ". High-level visits from China to Armenia included members of the [Politburo Standing Committee of the Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politburo_Standing_Committee_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "Politburo Standing Committee of the Chinese Communist Party") [Luo Gan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luo_Gan "Luo Gan"), in September 2003, and [Li Changchun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Changchun "Li Changchun"), in April 2011.

## History

The first relations between the [Armenian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_people "Armenian people") and [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_people "Chinese people") people can be traced back to around AD 1000. In [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"), "Armenia" is pronounced " *Ya-mei-ni-ya* " ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 亚美尼亚; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Yàměiníyà*). These four characters literally mean "the beautiful maid of [Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "Asia")." In [Armenian legends](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_folklore "Armenian folklore") and fairy tales, China is called the country of *Chenes*, *Chinumachin*, or *Chinastan*.[^3] [^4] Some ancient [Armenian literary works](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_literature "Armenian literature") have traces of [Chinese cultural](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_culture "Chinese culture") icons. For example, there are [qilin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qilin "Qilin"), [fenghuang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fenghuang "Fenghuang") (Chinese phoenix), and [dharmachakra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dharmachakra "Dharmachakra") designs in the [Armenian Bible](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Bible "Armenian Bible").

Armenians visited [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") for [trade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade "Trade") and imported [silk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silk "Silk"), [porcelain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porcelain "Porcelain"), [jade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jade "Jade"), [embroidered fabrics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embroidery "Embroidery"), and other goods to bring back to [Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenia "Armenia") via the [Silk Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silk_Road "Silk Road"). In China, there was high demand for Armenian medicinal products, vegetables, mineral paints, and insects, especially the [Armenian cochineal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_cochineal "Armenian cochineal") (*Porphyrophora hamelii*), which was used to dye the finest [Chinese silk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_silk "Chinese silk") and [Indian silk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_silk "Indian silk") fabrics.

The Chinese porcelains and celadonite that were discovered during archeological excavations of the Armenian cities of [Garni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garni "Garni"), [Dvin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dvin_\(ancient_city\) "Dvin (ancient city)"), [Ani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ani "Ani"), and the [Amberd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amberd "Amberd") fortress are evidence of early-medieval Armenian-Chinese economic trade.

[Movses Khorenatsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Movses_Khorenatsi "Movses Khorenatsi"), [Anania Shirakatsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anania_Shirakatsi "Anania Shirakatsi"), [Stepanos Orbelian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stepanos_Orbelian "Stepanos Orbelian"), and [King Hethum I of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hethum_I,_King_of_Armenia "Hethum I, King of Armenia") wrote about China, Chinese culture, and the Chinese people.

During the time of the [Mongol Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_Empire "Mongol Empire"), the relations between Armenia and China were furthered. Many Armenians began to settle in China. In 1688 the British [East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_India_Company "East India Company") agreed to permit Armenians access to Chinese maritime trade. The Armenians made full usage of this agreement with the British established maritime trade hubs in [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai") and [Macao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macao "Macao").

On 27 December 1991, China officially recognized Armenia as an independent state. On April 6, 1992, many [Central Asian countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia") established diplomatic relations with China. In July 1992, China established an embassy in the Armenian Capital of [Yerevan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yerevan "Yerevan"). In 1996, Armenia established their embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing").

The data in the chart above shows that the trade volume between Armenia and China, although still relatively small, has grown significantly over the last decade and exceeded US$400 million by 2008. There was a significant slump in trade volume in 2009, probably due to the [Great Recession](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Recession "Great Recession"). Armenia's main export is ore, while the products imported from China to Armenia are diverse and include clothes, shoes, machinery, chemicals, equipment, construction materials, furniture, food etc.[^6]

### Industry

In May 2010, Shanna (Shanxi-Nairit) Synthetic Rubber Co., jointly funded by Shanxi Synthetic Rubber Group and Armenia's Nairit LLC, was established and started production.[^7] Armenia's president attended the inauguration of the Shanxi-Nairit joint venture, marking the commencement of the company's chloroprene rubber production in Datong.[^8] Shanxi-Nairit joint venture was created based on an agreement signed in 2003 by the Shanxi Synthetic Rubber Company (China) and Nairit LLC (RA). Nairit LLC holds 40 percent of Shanxi-Nairit's shares.

China and Armenia have signed some bilateral cooperation agreements related to agriculture and scientific research, including the "Xinjiang-Armenia Top-quality Fruit Tree Planting Technical Cooperation", the "Central Asian Sci-Tech Exchange and Cooperation on Grapes", and the "Study on Vinyl Acetate and Its Byproducts".[^9]

## Education and culture

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/Armenian-Chinese_Friendship_School_%2802%29.jpg/250px-Armenian-Chinese_Friendship_School_%2802%29.jpg)

Chinese-Armenian Friendship School in Yerevan

The Chinese and Armenian governments have agreed to send 15 exchange students to study in each other's country each year. However, the two countries have not yet signed an agreement ensuring that the exchange students can receive credit for their work complete abroad. At present, there are no direct flights between Armenia and China.\[11\].[^10]

Every year, China celebrates Armenian Cultural Day and Armenia celebrates Chinese Cultural Day. Armenians are very interested in Chinese culture and enjoy a variety of Chinese theatrical performances.[^11]

[Confucius Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucius_Institute "Confucius Institute") opened at [Yerevan State Linguistic University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yerevan_State_Linguistic_University "Yerevan State Linguistic University") in 2008.[^12] Another Confucius Institute opened elsewhere in Armenia. Additionally, in 2018, the [Chinese-Armenian Friendship School](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese-Armenian_Friendship_School "Chinese-Armenian Friendship School") opened in Yerevan.[^13]

## Military

Armenia has established diplomatic relations and military-technical collaborations with China. This includes the importation of Chinese-made weapons and the training of Armenian officers at Chinese military academies. Additionally, China provides 10 million yuan annually in military assistance for non-lethal activities aimed at enhancing security around the infrastructure of the [Silk Road Economic Belt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative") (SREB).

According to [Chang Wanquan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chang_Wanquan "Chang Wanquan"), Minister of the PRC's Ministry of Defense, the Chinese and Armenian militaries have maintained the trend of conducting friendly exchanges and pragmatic cooperation, and the Chinese military has promoted the steady development of the relations between the two armed forces. According to [Seyran Ohanyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seyran_Ohanyan "Seyran Ohanyan"), former [Defense Minister of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defense_Minister_of_Armenia "Defense Minister of Armenia"), Armenia regards China as a reliable friend and partner. He expressed satisfaction with the development of the bilateral military relations seen in recent years and he expressed his hopes to promote cooperation between the two militaries in a variety of areas. On 18 January 2012, China and Armenia signed a Military Cooperation Agreement between the two countries.[^14] In 2013, Ohanyan held talks with Xu Qiang, Vice Chairman of the [Central Military Commission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Military_Commission_\(China\) "Central Military Commission (China)"), and [Chang Wanquan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chang_Wanquan "Chang Wanquan"), Minister of the PRC's Ministry of Defense.[^15]

Armenian defense ministers regularly participate in the [Beijing Xiangshan Forums](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing_Xiangshan_Forums "Beijing Xiangshan Forums") to discuss military-technical cooperation with China. For example, in October 2023, the current Minister of Defence met with Colonel General [He Weidong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/He_Weidong "He Weidong"), a member of the Politburo and Vice Chairman of the Central Military Commission of the People's Republic of China. According to Armenia's Ministry of Defence, the two officials discussed potential future collaboration.[^16]

## Armenian genocide

At present, China has not recognized the [Armenian genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_genocide "Armenian genocide").[^17] During the Armenian genocide, China hosted a small number of Armenian refugees, mostly concentrated around [Harbin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harbin "Harbin") and [Tianjin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tianjin "Tianjin"), prompting expressions of gratitude from the survivors.[^18] In 2011, Chinese Foreign Minister [Yang Jiechi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yang_Jiechi "Yang Jiechi") laid flowers on the [Armenian Genocide memorial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsitsernakaberd "Tsitsernakaberd") in [Yerevan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yerevan "Yerevan").[^19] In 2015, the Armenian community of China organized an important series of cultural and artistic events in [Nanjing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanjing "Nanjing") to commemorate the centennial of the Armenian genocide. The aim was to share Armenian history, art, and culture with the Chinese people during this significant occasion.[^20] [^21]
