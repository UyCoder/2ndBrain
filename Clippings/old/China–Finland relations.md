**China–Finland relations** are the foreign relations between [Finland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finland "Finland") and [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China").

## History

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/%C3%84ht%C3%A4ri_Zoo%2C_Finland_%2839303610090%29.jpg/250px-%C3%84ht%C3%A4ri_Zoo%2C_Finland_%2839303610090%29.jpg)

Giant panda in the Ähtäri Zoo that Finland received as a gift from China. The pandas were returned to China in 2024.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/China_Center_Kouvola.jpg/250px-China_Center_Kouvola.jpg)

Paifang gate to the former Kouvola 's China Center.

Along with [Sweden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China%E2%80%93Sweden_relations "People's Republic of China–Sweden relations") and [Denmark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China%E2%80%93Denmark_relations "People's Republic of China–Denmark relations"), Finland was one of the first [Western countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_world "Western world") to recognize the People's Republic of China and form diplomatic relations with the country in 1950.[^1] The embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") was opened in April 1952, and the first resident Finnish ambassador to China, Helge von Knorring, presented his [letter of credence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Letter_of_credence "Letter of credence") to [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong "Mao Zedong") on 9 May 1952.[^1]

Later that same year, an economic department headed by [Olavi J. Mattila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olavi_J._Mattila "Olavi J. Mattila") was opened at the embassy to foster the development of trade relations. As a consequence, Finland became the first capitalist country to sign a bilateral trade agreement with the People's Republic of China in 1953.[^1]

These steps, as well as Finland's staunch support for [PRC's membership in the UN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_and_the_United_Nations#The_People.27s_Republic_of_China_in_the_UN "China and the United Nations"), formed a solid basis to the nations' relations well into the 1980s.[^1] Since the early 1990s, there has been at least one official [minister-level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabinet_of_Finland "Cabinet of Finland") state visit from Finland to China each year.[^1]

In January 2018, China unveiled its [Arctic policy](https://english.www.gov.cn/archive/white_paper/2018/01/26/content_281476026660336.htm) document, which outlined and rationalized Beijing's interests in Arctic matters and details its proposal for a "Polar Silk Road" as a continuation of its [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative") (BRI). However, Finland has not officially engaged in the BRI, notwithstanding the attempts of certain private Finnish organizations to associate themselves with the BRI.[^2]

In 2017, [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") made a visit to Finland. In that same year, China leased two giant pandas to [Ähtäri Zoo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%84ht%C3%A4ri_Zoo "Ähtäri Zoo") as a gesture of goodwill and friendship to commemorate Finland's centenary of independence. This act was recognized at the time as a furtherance of China's ' [panda diplomacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panda_diplomacy "Panda diplomacy") '. However, the financial challenges faced by the zoo led to [the pandas' premature departure in 2024](https://yle.fi/a/74-20126737).

President of the Republic of Finland, [Alexander Stubb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Stubb "Alexander Stubb"), paid a state visit to China from 28 to 31 October 2024. President Stubb met President of China, [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping"), in Beijing.[^3]

In November 2024, Finnish authorities investigated a Chinese shipping vessel, the *Yi Peng 3*, in the [Baltic Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltic_Sea "Baltic Sea") after it was found in the vicinity of two severed undersea fiber-optic data cables and suspected of sabotage.[^4]

## Human rights

### Hong Kong national security law

In June 2020, Finland openly opposed the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law").[^5]

## Trade

Finland and China have had an agreement on economic, industrial, scientific and technological co-operation since 1973, and the agreement was last revised in 2005.[^1] The two principal trade organizations between the countries are Finland–China Trade Association and the [China Council for Promotion of International Trade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Council_for_Promotion_of_International_Trade "China Council for Promotion of International Trade") (CCPIT).

One of the fastest growing areas of trade between the two countries is in [environmental protection](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_protection "Environmental protection") and [information technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Information_technology "Information technology").[^6]

## Spying in Finland

China and Russia are suspected of large-scale spying of the IT networks at the [Finnish Ministry for Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finnish_Ministry_for_Foreign_Affairs "Finnish Ministry for Foreign Affairs"). The spying focused on data traffic between Finland and the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union"), and is believed to have continued for four years. The spying was uncovered in spring 2013, with the [Finnish Security Intelligence Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finnish_Security_Intelligence_Service "Finnish Security Intelligence Service") (Supo) investigating the breach.[^7]
