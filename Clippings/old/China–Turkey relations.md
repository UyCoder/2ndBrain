**China–Turkey relations** ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters "Simplified Chinese characters"): [中国](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E4%B8%AD%E5%9B%BD "wikt:中国") – [土耳其](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%9C%9F%E8%80%B3%E5%85%B6 "wikt:土耳其") [关系](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%85%B3%E7%B3%BB "wikt:关系"); [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Zhōngguó-Tǔěrqí Guānxì*; [Turkish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_language "Turkish language"): *Çin–Türkiye ilişkileri*) are the [international relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_relations "International relations") between the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") (PRC) and [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey"). In modern times, official diplomatic relations were established in 1934 between the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") and Turkey. Turkey recognized the PRC on 5 August 1971.

Turkey conforms to the [One-China policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One-China_policy "One-China policy") and recognizes the PRC as the sole legal representative of [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") and does not recognize the [legitimacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_status_of_Taiwan "Political status of Taiwan") of [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") (ROC) based in Taiwan. China has an [embassy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Embassy_of_China,_Ankara "Embassy of China, Ankara") in [Ankara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ankara "Ankara"), and a consulate–general in [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul") whereas Turkey has an embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and consulate–generals in [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong"), [Chengdu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chengdu "Chengdu"), [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou") and [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai"). China is a founding and the leading member of the [Shanghai Cooperation Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai_Cooperation_Organisation "Shanghai Cooperation Organisation") while Turkey is a SCO partner.[^1]

## History

### Ancient history

Historically, Chinese relations with Turkic nomadic tribes encompassed many different facets that affected their relations, although the relationship by far was mostly described in a negative light.[^2] This was stemmed by historical wars between various Chinese dynasties against various Turkic entities by that time, began from the [Han–Xiongnu War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han%E2%80%93Xiongnu_War "Han–Xiongnu War") when the [Xiongnu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xiongnu "Xiongnu") (" [Huns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huns "Huns") "), the ancestors of modern Turkic and Mongolian nomadic tribes, conflicted with [Han dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty").[^3]

The conflict between nomadic people, which the Turks were part of, and the Chinese, intensified under the [Tang dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_dynasty "Tang dynasty"), when the Tang dynasty launched two punitive expeditions against Turkic people ([Tang campaign against the Eastern Turks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_campaign_against_the_Eastern_Turks "Tang campaign against the Eastern Turks") and [Tang campaigns against the Western Turks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_campaigns_against_the_Western_Turks "Tang campaigns against the Western Turks")), as well as the Turks allied with Korean [Goguryeo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goguryeo "Goguryeo") against China.[^4] [^5] The Tang won both campaigns, and also destroyed Xueyantuo and Uighur Khanate with the help of its allies. On the other hand, China failed to eliminate Turkic resistance, which was detrimental of paving the way to the defeats of the dynasty and Chinese power. During the [Battle of Talas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Talas "Battle of Talas"), the Turks betrayed the Chinese Empire and joined the Arabs, ultimately expelled the Chinese out of [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia").[^6]

### Ottoman relations with the Ming and Qing dynasties

In the 16th century, there emerged travelogues of both Ottoman travelers to China, such as the merchant Ali Akbar and Chinese travelers to the Ottoman world, such as the scholar-official Ma Li, often portraying each other's empires as being highly similar to their own.[^7] [^8] A 16th century Chinese [gazetteer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gazetteer "Gazetteer"), *Shaanxi tongzhi*, claims that there were Han-Chinese people living in a number of Ottoman controlled towns and cities such as [Beirut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beirut "Beirut"), [Tartus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tartus "Tartus"), [Konya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Konya "Konya"), and [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul").[^7]

According to the official history of the [Ming dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty"), some self-proclaimed Ottoman envoys visited Beijing to pay tribute to the Ming emperor in 1524.[^7] [^9] However, these envoys were most likely just Central and Western Asian merchants trying to conduct trade in China, since pretending to be envoys was the only way to enter the Chinese border pass.[^7] One of these merchants was [Ali Akbar Khitai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%27Ali_Akbar_Khata%27i "'Ali Akbar Khata'i"), who visited the [Ming dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty") during the reign of [Emperor Zhengde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhengde_Emperor "Zhengde Emperor"). [Ali Akbar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%27Ali_Akbar_Khata%27i "'Ali Akbar Khata'i") later wrote the book *Khitay namah* and dedicated it to Sultan [Suleiman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suleiman_the_Magnificent "Suleiman the Magnificent").[^7] The *[Ming Shilu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_Shilu "Ming Shilu")* also records Ottoman envoys reaching China in 1423, 1425, 1427, 1443–1445, 1459, 1525–1527, 1543–1544, 1548, 1554, 1559, 1564, 1576, 1581, and 1618.[^10] Some of these missions may have been from [Uzbekistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanate_of_Bukhara "Khanate of Bukhara"), [Moghulistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moghulistan "Moghulistan"), or [Kara Del](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Del "Kara Del") because the Ottomans were known in China as the rulers of five realms: [Turfan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turpan "Turpan"), [Samarqand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samarkand "Samarkand"), [Mecca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mecca "Mecca"), Rum and [Hami](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hami "Hami").[^10]

According to traders in the [Gujarat Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gujarat_Sultanate "Gujarat Sultanate"), the Chinese Emperor ordered all [Chinese Muslims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_in_China "Islam in China") to read the [khutba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khutbah "Khutbah") in the name of the [Ottoman Sultan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_sultans_of_the_Ottoman_Empire "List of sultans of the Ottoman Empire"), thus preventing religious disputes from spreading across his territory.[^11]

[Kaiser Wilhelm II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilhelm_II,_German_Emperor "Wilhelm II, German Emperor") was so alarmed by the [Chinese Muslim troops](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kansu_Braves "Kansu Braves") in the [Boxer Rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boxer_Rebellion "Boxer Rebellion") that he requested the Caliph [Abdul Hamid II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdul_Hamid_II "Abdul Hamid II") of the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") to find a way to stop the Muslim troops from fighting. The Caliph agreed to the Kaiser's request and sent Enver Pasha (*not* the [future Young Turk leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enver_Pasha "Enver Pasha")) to [Qing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty") China in 1901, but the rebellion had ended by that time.[^12] [^13] [^14]
