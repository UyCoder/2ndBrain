**China–Palestine relations**, also referred to as **Sino–Palestinian relations**, encompass the [bilateral relationship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilateral_relationship "Bilateral relationship") between the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") and [Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine "Palestine") dating back to the early years of the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War"). China supports the creation of a "sovereign and independent Palestinian state" based on the [1967 borders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Line_\(Israel\) "Green Line (Israel)") with [East Jerusalem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Jerusalem "East Jerusalem") as its capital.

During the era of [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong "Mao Zedong"), China's [foreign policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_policy_of_China "Foreign policy of China") was in support of [Third World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_World "Third World") [national liberation movements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_liberation_movements "National liberation movements"), with China extending support towards the [Palestine Liberation Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine_Liberation_Organization "Palestine Liberation Organization") (PLO). In this period, China supported both [Fatah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatah "Fatah"), and smaller militant organizations such as the [Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular_Front_for_the_Liberation_of_Palestine "Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine") (PFLP) and the [Democratic Front for the Liberation of Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Front_for_the_Liberation_of_Palestine "Democratic Front for the Liberation of Palestine") (DFLP). In the post-Mao era, China continued to support the PLO in international forums, though it dropped its support for militant organizations. China has recognized the State of Palestine since 1988 and was one of the first countries to do so.

[Palestinian leaders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Palestine "President of Palestine") [Yasser Arafat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yasser_Arafat "Yasser Arafat") and [Mahmoud Abbas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahmoud_Abbas "Mahmoud Abbas") both visited China in official capacities, and relations between the two countries have been considered as cordial. In an effort to maintain what it views as a balanced position and avoid alienating Hamas, China adheres to a policy of never referring to the group as a terrorist organization.

## History

After the victory of the [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") (CCP) in the [Chinese Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War "Chinese Civil War") in 1949, the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") (PRC) was [proclaimed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proclamation_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Proclamation of the People's Republic of China") under [CCP chairman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chairman_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "Chairman of the Chinese Communist Party") [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong "Mao Zedong"). The PRC recognized the State of [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel "Israel"), but during the 1950s and 1960s, the PRC began to support the Arabs and Palestinians.[^1] China began voicing support for Palestine at the 1955 [Bandung Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bandung_Conference "Bandung Conference"), at which Premier [Zhou Enlai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhou_Enlai "Zhou Enlai") stated, "\[T\]here was a parallel between the problems of Palestine and Formosa; neither could be solved peacefully unless intervention by outside forces was excluded; China was suffering from the same problem as the Arab countries." [^2]<sup><span title="Page: 61">: 61</span> </sup> In his closing statement at the conference, Zhou stated that China supported "the struggle of the Arab people of Palestine for human rights" and avoided condemning Israel as a state.[^3]<sup><span title="Page: xxvi">: xxvi</span></sup>

The 1960s and 1970 were a period of heightened PRC support for Palestine, including both diplomatic and material support, and in 1965 recognized the [Palestine Liberation Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine_Liberation_Organization "Palestine Liberation Organization") (PLO) as the representative of the Palestinian people.[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 129">: 129</span> </sup> China had also established close relations with the [Fatah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatah "Fatah") party as well. The CCP also supported Palestinian armed groups such as the [Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular_Front_for_the_Liberation_of_Palestine "Popular Front for the Liberation of Palestine") (PFLP) as well as the [Democratic Front for the Liberation of Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Front_for_the_Liberation_of_Palestine "Democratic Front for the Liberation of Palestine") (DFLP).

In March 1965, [Ahmad Shukeiri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmad_Shukeiri "Ahmad Shukeiri") visited Beijing and negotiated the PLO's first arms agreement with China.[^3]<sup><span title="Page: 103">: 103</span> </sup> According to some historical views, including statements by [Yasser Arafat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yasser_Arafat "Yasser Arafat"), China was the earliest non-Arab state supporter of the PLO.[^3]<sup><span title="Pages: 103–104">: 103–104</span> </sup> From 1965 to 1971, China held yearly military training for [Palestinian fedayeen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestinian_fedayeen "Palestinian fedayeen"), including instruction in [Mao Zedong Thought](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maoism "Maoism") on guerilla warfare and [people's war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_war "People's war").[^3]<sup><span title="Pages: 115–116">: 115–116</span> </sup> The historical record regarding the extent of China's military support for the Palestinian liberation movements is unclear; Palestinian fedayeen leaders had reasons to exaggerate the significance of Chinese assistance, as did Arab, Israeli, and Western sources for different reasons.[^3]<sup><span title="Page: 104">: 104</span></sup>

The PLO established a diplomatic office in China on 22 March 1965.[^5] [^6] China provided formal diplomatic status to the PLO office in China.[^3]<sup><span title="Page: 17">: 17</span> </sup> As the PLO's relations with China developed, it became involved in with [Sino-Soviet split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Soviet_split "Sino-Soviet split") because China, having supplied arms to the PLO, sought diplomatic and rhetorical assistance from the PLO in return.[^3]<sup><span title="Page: 28">: 28</span> </sup> China hoped that its support of Palestine would help improve its Middle Eastern ties while making the Soviet diplomatic position in the Middle East worse.[^3]<sup><span title="Page: 124">: 124</span> </sup> [Palestinian-Soviet relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine%E2%80%93Soviet_Union_relations "Palestine–Soviet Union relations") also impacted Sino-Palestinian relations, as the Palestinian organizations sought help from China when the Soviet Union was more reserved.[^3]<sup><span title="Page: 124">: 124</span> </sup> The PRC strongly supported Yasser Arafat and the PLO, providing arms and training.[^1]

Mao Zedong linked the existence of Israel to [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan"), and described them as "bases of imperialism in Asia." [^7] In 1965, Mao stated:[^3]<sup><span title="Page: 8">: 8</span></sup>

> Imperialism is afraid of China and the Arabs. Israel and Formosa are bases of Imperialism in Asia. You are the front gate of the great continent, and we are the rear. Their goal is the same... Asia is the biggest continent in the world, and the West wants to continue exploiting it. The West does not like us, and we must understand this fact. The Arab battle against the West is the battle against Israel. So, boycott Europe and America.

During the early [Cultural Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultural_Revolution "Cultural Revolution"), China curtailed its diplomatic activities, including withdrawing its ambassadors from all of the Arab countries other than [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Egypt_relations "China–Egypt relations").[^3]<sup><span title="Page: xxvi">: xxvi</span> </sup> China continued to maintain its diplomatic ties to the Palestinian organizations and other non-state groups which China also viewed as liberation groups.[^3]<sup><span title="Page: xxvi">: xxvi</span></sup>

After the PRC was admitted to the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations") as a member in 1971, it continued to support the Palestinian cause.[^6] In 1971, China hosted a "Palestine International Week" which included public rallies, exhibitions, and documentary screenings on the Palestinian people; the [Chinese People's Association for Friendship with Foreign Countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_People%27s_Association_for_Friendship_with_Foreign_Countries "Chinese People's Association for Friendship with Foreign Countries") organized the event with the [Arab League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League "Arab League") and PLO.[^3]<sup><span title="Pages: 91–92">: 91–92</span> </sup> An embassy of the PLO was opened in Beijing during the summer of 1974.[^6]

The PRC supported the 1975 [UN General Assembly Resolution 3379](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_Resolution_3379 "United Nations General Assembly Resolution 3379") which had equated [Zionism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zionism "Zionism") with racism.[^8] The resolution was revoked with [Resolution 4686](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UN_General_Assembly_Resolution_4686 "UN General Assembly Resolution 4686") in 1991.

After the [death of Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_and_state_funeral_of_Mao_Zedong "Death and state funeral of Mao Zedong") in 1976 and the gradual rise of [Deng Xiaoping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deng_Xiaoping "Deng Xiaoping") to power, China reduced support for Palestinian militant groups, eventually cutting off support, and later supported the [Camp David Accords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camp_David_Accords "Camp David Accords") in 1978. The PRC still supported the Palestinians and their cause, albeit in a more limited fashion, and it strongly supported the 1988 [Palestinian Declaration of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestinian_Declaration_of_Independence "Palestinian Declaration of Independence") by Yasser Arafat in [Algiers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algiers "Algiers"), [Algeria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Algeria "Algeria") despite the objections by both Israel and the United States. The PRC [recognised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_recognition_of_the_State_of_Palestine "International recognition of the State of Palestine") [Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_Palestine "State of Palestine") in 1988.[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 130">: 130</span> </sup> It was one of the earliest states to do so.[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 130">: 130</span></sup>

China has assigned formal diplomatic staff to Palestine since 1990. Initially diplomatic affairs were conducted through the Chinese embassy in Tunisia. In December 1995, China has established a foreign office located in the [Gaza Strip](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_Strip "Gaza Strip") that acted as a *de facto* embassy and liaison office to the Palestinian Liberation Organization; however, the Ambassador to Tunisia continued to act as the main diplomatic officer to Palestine until 2008. In May 2004, the office, officially named Office of the People's Republic of China to the State of Palestine, was moved to Ramallah. The director of the office is accorded ambassadorial ranks in the Chinese foreign service.[^9]

## Contemporary relations

Under Deng's successors, the PRC has continued its relations with both Israel and the Arab States. Under CCP general secretaries [Jiang Zemin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jiang_Zemin "Jiang Zemin") and [Hu Jintao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hu_Jintao "Hu Jintao"), China has supported the Middle East peace process and the [Oslo Accords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oslo_Accords "Oslo Accords") in principle. Yasser Arafat visited China on 14 occasions.[^5]

After the 2006 [Palestinian legislative elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2006_Palestinian_legislative_election "2006 Palestinian legislative election"), the PRC referred to [Hamas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamas "Hamas") as the democratically elected representatives of the Palestinian people.[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 130">: 130</span> </sup> The PRC invited the Hamas Foreign Minister [Mahmoud al-Zahar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahmoud_al-Zahar "Mahmoud al-Zahar") to attend the China-Arab Cooperation Forum in June 2006 ignoring protests by both the United States and Israel but received praise from Mahmoud Abbas.[^10] The PRC continued to focus its Palestinian diplomacy with the PLO, however.[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 130">: 130</span> </sup> Chinese contacts with Hamas decreased following Hamas' defeat of Fatah in the [2007 Battle of Gaza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Gaza_\(2007\) "Battle of Gaza (2007)").[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 130">: 130</span> </sup> As part of its view of maintaining a balanced posture and avoiding alienating Hamas, the PRC's policy is to never label Hamas as a terrorist organization.[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 131">: 131</span></sup>

After the 2008–2009 [Gaza War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_War_\(2008%E2%80%932009\) "Gaza War (2008–2009)"), [Chinese Foreign Ministry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (China)") spokesperson [Qin Gang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_Gang "Qin Gang") urged both parties to solve disputes through dialogue and denounces the use of military force in solving conflicts.[^11] After the 31 May, [2010 Gaza flotilla raid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2010_Gaza_flotilla_raid "2010 Gaza flotilla raid") the [Chinese Foreign Ministry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (China)") spokesperson [Ma Zhaoxu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ma_Zhaoxu "Ma Zhaoxu") strongly condemned Israel and urged Israel to seriously implement the [UN Security Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UN_Security_Council "UN Security Council") resolutions and to improve the situation in the Gaza Strip by lifting the blockade.[^12]

During the November 2012 [Operation Pillar of Defense](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Pillar_of_Defense "Operation Pillar of Defense") in the Gaza Strip, a Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesperson told reporters in a news conference that China expressed "concern" to the clashes and urge all sides, particularly Israel, to display restraint and avoid civilian casualties.[^13] On 29 November 2012, China voted in favor of [UN General Assembly Resolution 67/19](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_resolution_67/19 "United Nations General Assembly resolution 67/19") Palestine to [non-member observer state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_General_Assembly_observers#Non-member_observers "United Nations General Assembly observers") status in the United Nations.[^14] In 2014, [General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") stated that he supported Palestinian statehood.[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 29">: 29</span></sup>

During the [2014 Gaza War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Gaza_War "2014 Gaza War"), Chinese Foreign Ministry spokesperson [Hong Lei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Lei_\(diplomat\) "Hong Lei (diplomat)") on 9 July 2014 in response to the violence said: "We believe that to resort to force and to counter violence with violence will not help resolve problems other than pile up more hatred. We urge relevant parties to bear in mind the broader picture of peace and the lives of the people, immediately realize a ceasefire, stick to the strategic choice of peace talks and strive for an early resumption of talks." [^15]

China voted in favor of [UN Security Council Resolution 2334](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_2334 "United Nations Security Council Resolution 2334") condemning Israeli settlement building on the West Bank and typically takes positions sympathetic to the Palestinian cause at the United Nations. In early 2016, [CCP general secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") reasserted China's support for "the establishment of a Palestinian state with its capital being [eastern Jerusalem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Jerusalem "Eastern Jerusalem") " in a meeting with the [Arab League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League "Arab League").[^16] Xi also announced an aid project of 50 million yuan ($7.6 million) for a solar power stations in the Palestinian territories.[^16]

[Chinese Foreign Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Minister of Foreign Affairs (China)") [Wang Yi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Yi "Wang Yi") called the lack of "an independent \[Palestinian\] state with full sovereignty" a "terrible injustice" in an April 2017 meeting between [Palestinian Foreign Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_and_Expatriates_\(State_of_Palestine\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Expatriates (State of Palestine)") [Riyad al-Maliki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riyad_al-Maliki "Riyad al-Maliki"). Wang went on to say that China supports Palestinians' efforts to create an independent state based on the borders set before the [1967 Six-Day War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six-Day_War "Six-Day War") as well as the establishment of its future capital in East Jerusalem.[^17] In July 2017, Xi delivered a further formalization of China's positions in his "Four Points" on the "issue of Israel-Palestine conflict", the first of which was that China supported the establishment of an independent, sovereign Palestine within the framework of the [two-state solution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Two-state_solution "Two-state solution") based on the 1967 borders, with East Jerusalem as its capital.[^18] [^19]

In July 2019, Palestine was one of the 54 countries which issued a joint statement supporting [China's policies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang_internment_camps "Xinjiang internment camps") in [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") at the United Nations (UN).[^20] A year after in June 2020, Palestine also backed the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law") at the UN.[^21] After Palestinian ambassador to China [Fariz Mehdawi](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Fariz_Mehdawi&action=edit&redlink=1 "Fariz Mehdawi (page does not exist)") visited Xinjiang in 2021, he praised China's upkeep of mosques on Chinese state media.[^22] Mehdaw's comments were criticised by [Radio Free Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_Free_Asia "Radio Free Asia") journalist [Shohret Hoshur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shohret_Hoshur "Shohret Hoshur") who cited a past interview with Uyghur mother Patigul Ghulam saying the Uyghurs were in a worse situation than the Palestinians.[^23]

[Palestine's President](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_the_State_of_Palestine "President of the State of Palestine") [Mahmoud Abbas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mahmoud_Abbas "Mahmoud Abbas") has visited China on five occasions as of June 2023 [^24] and has voiced support for [Beijing's policies toward Muslim minorities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Uyghurs_in_China "Persecution of Uyghurs in China") in Xinjiang.[^25] During his fifth visit on 13 June 2023, he met with Chinese leader Xi Jinping and Chinese premier [Li Qiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Qiang "Li Qiang") to discuss the latest developments in Palestine as well as other regional and international issues. China has stated its willingness to assist in facilitating peace talks between Israel and Palestine.[^26] During the trip, China announced a [strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "Diplomatic partnerships of China") with the Palestinian Authority [^27] and Xi proposed a three-point proposal to solve the [Israeli–Palestinian conflict](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israeli%E2%80%93Palestinian_conflict "Israeli–Palestinian conflict"), calling for a Palestinian state on the basis of 1967 borders, with East Jerusalem as its capital; humanitarian aid to Palestine; and the convening of a "larger, more authoritative, more influential international peace conference" to promote talks.[^24] [^28]

On 11 October early into [Gaza war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gaza_war "Gaza war"), China reiterated its support for a two-states solution and called for a ceasefire in the fighting.[^29] Following Russia and China's veto against a US draft resolution on 25 October in the [UN Security Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UN_Security_Council "UN Security Council"), Hamas leader [Ismail Haniyeh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ismail_Haniyeh "Ismail Haniyeh") issued a statement praising the two countries' position.[^30] [^31] In January 2024, Israel reported that it discovered a stockpile of Chinese weaponry used by Hamas.[^32] [^33]

On 19 March 2024, [Chinese Foreign Ministry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (China)") ambassador Wang Kejian met with Hamas leader Ismail Haniyeh in Qatar, where they "exchanged views on the Gaza conflict and other issues".[^34] Following talks mediated by China, on 23 July 2024, Palestinian factions including Hamas and Fatah reached an agreement to end their divisions and form an interim unity government, which they announced in the "Beijing Declaration".[^35] The Palestinian reconciliation talks hosted by China generally increased international perceptions that China was a credible intermediary on these issues.[^4]<sup><span title="Page: 91">: 91</span></sup>

In November 2025, [Xinhua News Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinhua_News_Agency "Xinhua News Agency") and [Wafa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wafa "Wafa") signed a cooperation agreement.[^36] On 31 December, Palestine also reiterated its support of China's [One-China policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One-China_policy "One-China policy") and rejected any interference in China's internal affairs. It also praised China's international policies to have contributed to regional and global stability.[^37]

### Relations in the China-Arab States Cooperation Forum

Throughout the development of [China-Arab States Cooperation Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Arab_States_Cooperation_Forum "China–Arab States Cooperation Forum"), Arab states have urged China to use the forum to strengthen its support for Palestine; CASCF's statements of support for Palestine have grown stronger over time.[^2]<sup><span title="Pages: 61–62">: 61–62</span></sup>

CASCF's founding declaration in 2004 states, "China stresses support for the Middle Eastern peace process, land for peace principle and the Beirut Summit [Arab Peace Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Peace_Initiative "Arab Peace Initiative")." [^2]<sup><span title="Page: 61">: 61</span> </sup> The action plan from the 2004 summit called for strengthening the UN's peace process role and an independent state for Palestinians.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 61">: 61</span> </sup> The 2008 CASCF declaration went further, calling on Israel to end the occupation of lands occupied since 1967, for the work of the international community to lift the blockade on Palestinians, and for Israel to stop building settlements.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 62">: 62</span> </sup> In response to united pressure from the Arab states, CASCF agreed in 2010 to call specifically for an end of Israel's occupation in [East Jerusalem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Jerusalem "East Jerusalem").[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 62">: 62</span> </sup> The 2012 CASCF declaration called for Palestine to become a full member of the UN and of [UNESCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO "UNESCO").[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 62">: 62</span></sup>

Subsequent CASCF declarations have echoed these positions.[^2] <sup><span title="Pages: 63–64">: 63–64</span> </sup> The 2018 declaration calls on all states to implement [UN Security Council Resolution 2334 (2016)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_2334 "United Nations Security Council Resolution 2334") (which condemns measures designed to change the demographic make-up of occupied Palestinian territory, including East Jerusalem) and explicitly criticizes the United States for moving its embassy in Israel to Jerusalem.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 63">: 63</span></sup>

During the 2024 CASCF Summit, Xi stated that a two-state solution must be upheld and that [war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israeli_invasion_of_the_Gaza_Strip_\(2023%E2%80%93present\) "Israeli invasion of the Gaza Strip (2023–present)") must not continue indefinitely.[^38] China pledged a further US$69 million in emergency humanitarian relief for Palestine and US$3 million to the [United Nations Relief and Works Agency for Palestine Refugees in the Near East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNRWA "UNRWA").[^38]

## Economic relations

Palestine and China began negotiating a [free trade agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_trade_agreement "Free trade agreement") in 2019.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 176">: 176</span> </sup> The discussions coincided with China's beginning of free trade agreement negotiations with Israel.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 176">: 176</span> </sup> According to academic Dawn C. Murphy, China likely proceeded concurrently with both countries in order to avoid perceptions of favoritism.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 176">: 176</span> </sup> As of 2022, Palestine and China had not yet concluded their free trade agreement discussions.[^2]<sup><span title="Page: 173">: 173</span></sup>
