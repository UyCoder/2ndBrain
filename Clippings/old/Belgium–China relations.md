**Belgium–China relations** began in the early 1970s about 20 years after [mainland China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_China "Mainland China") came under communist rule. The [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") (PRC) has an embassy in [Brussels](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brussels "Brussels") while Belgium has an embassy in Beijing and consulates in [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou"), [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong") and [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai").

## History

During the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War"), the [Belgian Volunteer Corps for Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgian_Volunteer_Corps_for_Korea "Belgian Volunteer Corps for Korea") under the [United Nations Command](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Command "United Nations Command") fought the PRC's [People's Volunteer Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Volunteer_Army "People's Volunteer Army") in multiple battles.[^1]

Diplomatic relations between the People's Republic of China and the Belgium were established on 25 October 1971.[^2] The diplomatic relationship began to improve and grow during the 1980s with visits from high-ranking governments from both sides such as [Zhu Rongji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhu_Rongji "Zhu Rongji") in April 1991 and Vice-premier [Qian Qichen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qian_Qichen "Qian Qichen") in March 1992.[^2] From the Belgium side, Crown [Prince Albert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_II_of_Belgium "Albert II of Belgium") has visited China in May 1993 and the king of Belgium, Crown [Prince Philippe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippe_of_Belgium "Philippe of Belgium") visited in November 1996 and May 2000. The former prime ministers [Jean-Luc Dehaene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jean-Luc_Dehaene "Jean-Luc Dehaene") visited China in November 1998 and Prime Minister [Guy Verhofstadt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guy_Verhofstadt "Guy Verhofstadt") visited in March 2002.[^2]

China's Vice President [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") has been to Belgium in October 2009 to enhance bilateral cooperation in terms of trade, human and cultural exchanges.[^3] [^4]

The bilateral relationship has increased with economic trade between the two countries. Belgium has a pavilion in the Shanghai [Expo 2010](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expo_2010 "Expo 2010").[^5]

In July 2019, the UN ambassadors from 22 nations, including Belgium, signed a joint letter to the [UNHRC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Rights_Council "United Nations Human Rights Council") condemning the Chinese government's [persecution of the Uyghurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persecution_of_Uyghurs_in_China "Persecution of Uyghurs in China") as well as its mistreatment of other minority groups, urging it to close the [Xinjiang internment camps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang_internment_camps "Xinjiang internment camps").[^6] [^7]

In 2019 as punishment for visiting [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") President of the Senate [Jacques Brotchi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacques_Brotchi "Jacques Brotchi") received a lifetime ban from entering China.[^8]

In June 2020, Belgium openly opposed the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law").[^9]

### Espionage

In December 2023, Belgian Prime Minister [Alexander De Croo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_De_Croo "Alexander De Croo") referred to China as a "sometimes very hostile" country following allegations that the [Ministry of State Security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_State_Security_\(China\) "Ministry of State Security (China)") had recruited Belgian politician [Frank Creyelman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Creyelman "Frank Creyelman") for espionage.[^10]

In April 2024, the Belgian [Foreign Ministry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Public_Service_Foreign_Affairs "Federal Public Service Foreign Affairs") summoned the Chinese envoy after reports emerged that Chinese spies ([APT31](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyberwarfare_and_China "Cyberwarfare and China")) hacked the laptops of key lawmakers such as the head of the Belgian Foreign Affairs Committee and former prime minister [Guy Verhofstadt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guy_Verhofstadt "Guy Verhofstadt").[^11] [^12] [^13] In April 2025, Belgian media reported that Chinese intelligence attempted to coerce the mayor of [Huy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huy "Huy"), Eric Dosogne, in 2020 to spy on [Samuel Cogolati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samuel_Cogolati "Samuel Cogolati"), a leader of [Ecolo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecolo "Ecolo") and prominent member of the [Inter-Parliamentary Alliance on China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inter-Parliamentary_Alliance_on_China "Inter-Parliamentary Alliance on China").[^14] In October 2025, it was reported that Chinese state-backed [advanced persistent threat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_persistent_threat "Advanced persistent threat") group Mustang Panda had targeted Belgian and other European diplomats as part of a [cyber-espionage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyber_espionage "Cyber espionage") campaign.[^15]

## Trade

The trade between the two countries in 2013 came to a value of EUR20.2 billion.[^16] China is Belgium's sixth biggest trading partner which makes up 4.1% of Belgium's trade.[^17] The trade between the two countries in 2008 exceeded 20 billion and growing on average 20% every year.[^18]

## Chinese people in Belgium

[Qian Xiuling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qian_Xiuling "Qian Xiuling") (1912–2008), or Siou-Ling Tsien de Perlinghi, was an immigrant to Belgium from the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") who helped to save nearly 100 Belgian people from execution by the Nazis during World War II. She won a medal in Belgium and had a street named after her.[^19]
