Official diplomatic relations between the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") (PRC) and [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt "Egypt") were established on May 30, 1956. Early in the bilateral relationship, China viewed Egypt and [Gamal Abdel Nasser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamal_Abdel_Nasser "Gamal Abdel Nasser") as central to China's diplomacy in the region. Relations declined as a result of issues related to the intensifying China-Soviet rivalry and further declined following the Arab defeat in the [Six-Day War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six-Day_War "Six-Day War"). After [Nasser's death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_and_state_funeral_of_Gamal_Abdel_Nasser "Death and state funeral of Gamal Abdel Nasser") in 1970, China made efforts to improve their relationship.

In 1999, the two countries signed a [strategic partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_diplomatic_partnerships_of_China "List of diplomatic partnerships of China"). They signed further cooperation agreements in 2006 and a comprehensive strategic partnership in 2014. China and Egypt collaborate in the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative"), including through the establishment of a [special economic zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_economic_zone "Special economic zone") in Egypt, and engage in space cooperation. In addition to their bilateral relationship, China and Egypt both collaborate in the multi-lateral cooperation forums the [China-Arab States Cooperation Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Arab_States_Cooperation_Forum "China–Arab States Cooperation Forum") and the [Forum on China-Africa Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forum_on_China%E2%80%93Africa_Cooperation "Forum on China–Africa Cooperation").

## History

[Fatimid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatimid "Fatimid") Caliph [Al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Hakim_bi-Amr_Allah#Foreign_affairs "Al-Hakim bi-Amr Allah") sent a delegation to [Song dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Song_dynasty "Song dynasty") China led by [Domiyat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domiyat "Domiyat").

The [Mamluk Sultan of Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultan_of_Egypt "Sultan of Egypt") ordered [Jidda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeddah "Jeddah") to treat Chinese traders honorably upon their arrival in the early 15th century.[^1]

During the [Qing dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty"), [Yusuf Ma Dexin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yusuf_Ma_Dexin "Yusuf Ma Dexin") visited Egypt in the 1840s.

### Republic of China

The [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") sent Hui Muslims like [Muhammad Ma Jian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ma_Jian "Muhammad Ma Jian") and other Hui Muslim students to study at [Al-Azhar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Azhar "Al-Azhar") in Egypt.[^2] The Fuad Muslim Library in China was named after [Fuad I of Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuad_I_of_Egypt "Fuad I of Egypt") by the [Chinese Muslim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hui_people "Hui people") Ma Songting.[^3]

Imam [Wang Jingzhai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Jingzhai "Wang Jingzhai") studied at Al-Azhar University in Egypt along with several other Chinese Muslim students, the first Chinese students in modern times to study in the Middle East.[^4] Wang recalled his experience teaching at madrassas in the provinces of Henan (Yu), Hebei (Ji), and Shandong (Lu) which were outside of the traditional stronghold of Muslim education in northwest China, and where the living conditions were poorer and the students had a much tougher time than the northwestern students.[^5] In 1931 China sent five students to study at Al-Azhar in Egypt, among them was [Muhammad Ma Jian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ma_Jian "Muhammad Ma Jian") and they were the first Chinese to study at Al-Azhar.[^6] [^7] [^8] [^9] Na Zhong, a descendant of [Nasr al-Din (Yunnan)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nasr_al-Din_\(Yunnan\) "Nasr al-Din (Yunnan)") was another one of the students sent to Al-Azhar in 1931, along with Zhang Ziren, Ma Jian, and Lin Zhongming.[^10]

A False [Hadith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadith "Hadith") (圣训), a saying of the prophet [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad "Muhammad"), spread to China, which says "Loving your country is part of loving the Faith" ([traditional Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters "Traditional Chinese characters"): 愛護祖國是屬於信仰的一部份; [simplified Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters "Simplified Chinese characters"): 爱护祖国是属于信仰的一部份; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *àihù zǔguó shì shǔyú xìnyǎng de yī bùfèn*; [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language "Arabic language"): حب الوطن من الایمان **ḥubb al-waṭan min al-imān**). It is not a real Hadith but was a popular slogan among Arabic speakers in Middle East in the 19th-20th centuries. It spread to China via Hui Muslim students like [Muhammad Ma Jian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ma_Jian "Muhammad Ma Jian") who studied at [Al-Azhar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Azhar "Al-Azhar") in Egypt.[^11]

Hui Muslim General [Ma Bufang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ma_Bufang "Ma Bufang") and his retinue including [Ma Chengxiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ma_Chengxiang "Ma Chengxiang") moved to Egypt before being appointed as ambassador to Saudi Arabia.

### People's Republic of China

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fc/1964-03_1964%E5%B9%B4_%E4%B8%AD%E5%9B%BD%E8%AE%BF%E9%97%AE%E9%98%BF%E8%81%94%E9%85%8B_%E5%91%A8%E6%81%A9%E6%9D%A5_%E7%BA%B3%E8%B5%9B%E5%B0%94_%E9%99%88%E6%AF%85.jpg/250px-1964-03_1964%E5%B9%B4_%E4%B8%AD%E5%9B%BD%E8%AE%BF%E9%97%AE%E9%98%BF%E8%81%94%E9%85%8B_%E5%91%A8%E6%81%A9%E6%9D%A5_%E7%BA%B3%E8%B5%9B%E5%B0%94_%E9%99%88%E6%AF%85.jpg)

Nasser and Zhou, 1964

Chinese Premier [Zhou Enlai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhou_Enlai "Zhou Enlai") first met Egyptian President [Gamal Abdel Nasser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gamal_Abdel_Nasser "Gamal Abdel Nasser") at the [Asian-African Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian-African_Conference "Asian-African Conference") in [Bandung](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bandung "Bandung").[^12] On May 30, 1956, Nasser defied U.S. pressure and displeasure by recognizing [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China").[^13] [^12] The same year, China and Egypt entered into trade and cultural agreements.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: xxiv">: xxiv</span> </sup> Zhou visited Egypt three times during his tenure.[^12] China initially viewed Egypt as the central country in the region for its relations and considered Nasser as important national-bourgeois leader.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: xxiv">: xxiv</span></sup>

During the [Suez Crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suez_Crisis "Suez Crisis") in 1956, China made strong statements in support of Egypt, condemned [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93France_relations "China–France relations") and [Britain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93United_Kingdom_relations "China–United Kingdom relations"), but avoided referencing [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Israel_relations "China–Israel relations") in its condemnations.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: xxxvii">: xxxvii</span> </sup> As part of its show of support for Egypt, China stated that it would provide volunteers to help Egypt fight colonists; the Egyptian ambassador stated that Egypt had not asked for volunteers, and the idea was not raised again.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 122">: 122</span> </sup> China provided foreign aid to Egypt during the crisis, making Egypt the first Middle Eastern country to receive [aid from China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_foreign_aid "Chinese foreign aid").[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 168">: 168</span></sup>

In 1958, China-Egypt relations became less close as the increasing [Sino-Soviet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Soviet_split "Sino-Soviet split") rivalries resulted in disagreement with Nasser.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: xxv">: xxv</span> </sup> Nasser's suppression of communists in Egypt also strained relations, as did his criticism of [Abdul-Karim Qasim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdul-Karim_Qasim "Abdul-Karim Qasim") and the [First Republic of Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Republic_of_Iraq "First Republic of Iraq"), which China supported.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: xxv">: xxv</span></sup>

Bilateral relations also worsened after the Arab defeat in the [Six-Day War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six-Day_War "Six-Day War").[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 48">: 48</span> </sup> China unsuccessfully urged Nasser not to accept a ceasefire and instead to engage in a [people's war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_war "People's war").[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 48">: 48</span> </sup> Nasser rejected the Chinese advice, stating that the [United Arab Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Republic "United Arab Republic") had lost its army and that continuing to fight would provide Israel with the opportunity to destroy Egypt.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 48">: 48</span> </sup> Nasser wrote to Mao that people's war was not a viable strategy for Egypt because "this is a desert, and we cannot wage a people's war in Sinai for the simple reason that it is unpopulated." [^14]<sup><span title="Page: 48">: 48</span></sup>

Relations further worsened in 1968.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 48">: 48</span> </sup> In February, China demanded that Egypt return a defector to China, which Egypt declined to do in the absence of an [extradition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extradition "Extradition") treaty between the countries.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 48">: 48</span> </sup> In November, Egypt's foreign minister alleged that China's ambassador to Egypt was providing funds and printing facilities for students who were rioting in Alexandria.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 48">: 48</span></sup>

After Nasser's death in October 1970, China sought to improve its relations with Egypt.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 89">: 89</span></sup>

In 1971, Egypt supported China's bid for a permanent seat in the United Nations and it voted in favour to admit Beijing and replace Taipei.

China and Egypt signed a strategic partnership in 1999.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 248">: 248</span> </sup> The countries signed additional cooperation agreements in 2006.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 210">: 210</span></sup>

In the aftermath of the [Yom Kippur War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yom_Kippur_War "Yom Kippur War"), China provided Egypt with economic aid and food aid (100.000 tons of cereals) and military equipment.[^16] Egyptian government maintained cordial relations with the China even after Nasser's successor, [Anwar Sadat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anwar_Sadat "Anwar Sadat"), broke with the USSR in favor of a partnership with the U.S., [Hosni Mubarak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hosni_Mubarak "Hosni Mubarak") first visited China in 1976 as vice president, during which he was received by [Chairman of the Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chairman_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "Chairman of the Chinese Communist Party") [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong "Mao Zedong") and provision of spare parts for Egypt's Soviet-supplied Tupolev bombers and MIG fighters.[^17] [^18] [^19]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/Mubarak_and_Mao_Zedong.jpg/250px-Mubarak_and_Mao_Zedong.jpg)

Egyptian Vice President Hosni Mubarak and CCP Chairman Mao Zedong, 1976

In 2012, Egyptian president [Mohamed Morsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mohamed_Morsi "Mohamed Morsi") chose China for his first official visit outside the Middle East.[^20]

In 2014, the two countries signed a comprehensive strategic partnership.[^21]

[General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") visited Egypt during his [January 2016 state visit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_international_trips_made_by_Xi_Jinping "List of international trips made by Xi Jinping") to the region.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 110">: 110</span></sup>

Egypt follows the [one China principle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_principle "One China principle"). It recognizes the People's Republic of China as the sole government of China and [Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") as an integral part of China's territory, and supports all efforts by the PRC to "achieve [national reunification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_unification "Chinese unification") ". It also considers [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong"), [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") and [Tibet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibet_Autonomous_Region "Tibet Autonomous Region") to be China's internal affairs.[^22]

In July 2019, UN ambassadors of 37 countries, including Egypt, have signed a joint letter to the [UNHRC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Rights_Council "United Nations Human Rights Council") defending China's [treatment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang_re-education_camps "Xinjiang re-education camps") of [Uyghurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uyghurs "Uyghurs") and other Muslim minority groups in the [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") region.[^23] The letter also praised [China's human rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_China "Human rights in China") achievements.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 269">: 269</span> </sup> [Western media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_media "Western media") outlets reported that Egypt had aided in deporting Uyghurs to China;[^24] [^25] however, in July 2017, [Al-Azhar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Azhar "Al-Azhar"), Egypt's leading Islamic institution, denied that any Uyghurs had been arrested from within its campus or other buildings belonging to the organization itself.[^26] In June 2020, Egypt was one of 53 countries that backed a statement supporting the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law") at the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations").[^27]

## Economic relations

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/Belt_and_Road_Initiative_participant_map.svg/250px-Belt_and_Road_Initiative_participant_map.svg.png)

Countries which signed cooperation documents related to the Belt and Road Initiative

Bilateral trade reached about $4 billion [U.S. dollars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U.S._dollars "U.S. dollars") in 2007, up from $3.19b in 2006.[^28] In 2010, it was worth US$7.0 billion.[^29] In 2011 Egypt was the 5th largest trading partner of China in Africa and in the first 8 months of 2012 it was the 4th.[^30]

From 2000 to 2012, there are approximately 39 Chinese official development finance projects identified in Egypt through various media reports.[^31] These projects range from jointly constructing an industrial park in the Northwest Suez Economic Zone beginning June 1, 2000,[^32] to the construction of a Chinese language school in Cairo in 2002 through a US$4 million grant from the Chinese government.[^33] In 2016, Egyptian president Elsisi made a visit to China and signed a number of deals there including [New Administrative Capital of Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Administrative_Capital_of_Egypt "New Administrative Capital of Egypt").[^34]

In November 2020, Egypt and China signed a protocol of cooperation to utilise and market a land plot owned by the Suez Canal Authority in Ain Sokhna.[^35] The China-Egypt Suez Economic and Trade Cooperation Zone increased Chinese investment in, and industrial collaboration with, Egypt.[^36]<sup><span title="Page: 44">: 44</span> </sup> It focuses on the production of textiles and garments and petroleum equipment as well as electronics and automobile assembly.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 178">: 178</span> </sup> This [special economic zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_economic_zone "Special economic zone") (SEZ) is part of China and Egypt's [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative") (BRI) cooperation.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 247">: 247</span> </sup> Among the other SEZs established with Chinese cooperation in the Middle East and Africa, it is promoted by the [Forum on China-Africa Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forum_on_China%E2%80%93Africa_Cooperation "Forum on China–Africa Cooperation") (FOCAC).[^15]<sup><span title="Pages: 247–248">: 247–248</span></sup>

China aligns its BRI economic cooperation in Egypt with the points of emphasis in Egypt's Vision 2030 national development plan.[^36]<sup><span title="Page: 66">: 66</span> </sup> China's BRI projects with Egypt therefore focus on developing labor-intensive manufacturing and traditional infrastructure in Egypt.[^36]<sup><span title="Page: 66">: 66</span></sup>

Chinese tourists are one of the major source of income for [Egypt's tourism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tourism_in_Egypt "Tourism in Egypt"). Egypt's tourism sector had significant growth in 2024, with a reported 65% rise in visitors from China, as stated by the Egyptian Tourism Authority. It is anticipated that more than 300,000 Chinese tourists travel to the country in 2024.[^37]

In addition to cooperation with China in the multi-lateral format of FOCAC, Egypt and China also cooperate through their membership in the [China-Arab States Cooperation Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Arab_States_Cooperation_Forum "China–Arab States Cooperation Forum") (CASCF).[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 47">: 47</span></sup>

### Energy

[China National Petroleum Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_National_Petroleum_Corporation "China National Petroleum Corporation") (CNPC), a [state-owned enterprise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State-owned_enterprises_of_China "State-owned enterprises of China"), provides services in Egypt.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 162">: 162</span> </sup> China Petroleum Corporation ([SINOPEC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinopec "Sinopec")), also a state-owned company, has facilities in Egypt.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 163">: 163</span></sup>

### Foreign aid

Egypt was the first Middle Eastern country to receive foreign aid from China.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 168">: 168</span></sup>

From 2000 to 2014, Egypt was one the top recipients of Chinese foreign aid in the Middle East, receiving US$43 million.[^15]<sup><span title="Page: 170">: 170</span></sup>

## Military

1970–80s, China delivered [B-6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xian_H-6 "Xian H-6") bomber and [F-6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shenyang_J-6 "Shenyang J-6") fighter to Cairo and received several [MiG-23](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MiG-23 "MiG-23") from Egypt.[^19] [^38] [^39] [Egyptian navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_navy "Egyptian navy") received ships in the 1980s from China including submarines and frigates.[^40] China has also helped Egypt develop its own missile systems.[^41] [^42]

Egypt was one of the top Middle Eastern purchasers of Chinese conventional arms from 1978 to 2019.[^15]<sup><span title="Pages: 226–227">: 226–227</span></sup>

The [K-8E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K-8E "K-8E") is an Egyptian variant of the Chinese [Hongdu JL-8](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hongdu_JL-8 "Hongdu JL-8"), exported as the Karakorum-8 (or K-8) to (among others) [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan "Pakistan"), [Zambia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zambia "Zambia") and [Myanmar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar "Myanmar"). The K-8E's manufacture at the [Arab Organization for Industrialization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Organization_for_Industrialization "Arab Organization for Industrialization") (AOI) Aircraft Factory began in 2000, under an agreement between the AOI and [CATIC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_National_Aero-Technology_Import_%26_Export_Corporation "China National Aero-Technology Import & Export Corporation"), the Chinese state-owned aerospace manufacturer, to produce a total of 60 K-8s over five years. The contract, valued at US$347.4 million, was signed in 1999 in connection with the [state visit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_visit "State visit") by [Chinese leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paramount_leader "Paramount leader") [Jiang Zemin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jiang_Zemin "Jiang Zemin") that year.[^43] Initially, most of the parts were manufactured in China and the aircraft were assembled in Egypt, but by the end of the programme the manufacture was to be carried out entirely at the AOI Aircraft Factory.[^44] It is located in [Helwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Helwan "Helwan").[^45] In May 2012 six Egyptian drones of [ASN-209](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASN-209 "ASN-209") was built in collaboration with a Chinese defence manufacturer during the first phase and are fully operational under the [Egyptian armed forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_armed_forces "Egyptian armed forces") according to Hamdy Weheba.[^46] [^47]

China and Egypt held first joint naval drills in June 2015.[^40] [^48]

In 2018, the [Egyptian air force](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_air_force "Egyptian air force") first publicly demonstrated its Chinese-made [CAIG Wing Loong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CAIG_Wing_Loong "CAIG Wing Loong") drones.[^49]

On 19 April 2025**,** Egypt and China held their first joint drill near the Israeli border**.**[^50] Additional joint air force exercises - namely fighter jet exercises - were conducted in May 2025.[^51]

## Education

[Peking University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peking_University "Peking University") has a long history of friendly cooperation with [Cairo University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairo_University "Cairo University"). As early as 1986, the two universities signed an inter-school exchange agreement, after which they have completed the renewal of the Inter-school cooperation Agreement in year 2000. On 29 December 2007, the two sides signed an implementary agreement of establishing Confucius Institute through cooperation, and held a grand opening ceremony for the new institute, which had the honor of the attendance of Mr. Wu Chunhua Chinese Ambassador in Egypt, Mr. Ali Abd el-Rahman Yousef President of Cairo University, Mr. Zhang Guoyou Vice President of Peking University and other leaders. On 18 March 2008, the [Confucius Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucius_Institute "Confucius Institute") in Cairo University---the first Confucius Institute established both in Egypt and North Africa—started its recruitment and Chinese language courses.[^52]

## Space cooperation

China provided Egypt's space program with grants in 2016 ($23 million), 2018 ($45 million), and 2019 ($72 million).[^13]<sup><span title="Page: 302">: 302</span> </sup> China also cooperates with the [Egyptian Space Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_Space_Agency "Egyptian Space Agency") in developing the Assembly and Integration Centre in Egypt's Space City.[^13]<sup><span title="Page: 303">: 303</span></sup>
