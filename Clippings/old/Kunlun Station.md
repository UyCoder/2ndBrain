**Kunlun Station** ([simplified Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Simplified_Chinese_characters "Simplified Chinese characters"): 昆仑站; [traditional Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters "Traditional Chinese characters"): 崑崙站; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Kūnlún Zhàn*) is the southernmost of five [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") research stations in [Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica"). When it is occupied during the summer, it is the second-southernmost [research base in Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Research_stations_in_Antarctica "Research stations in Antarctica"), behind only the American [Amundsen–Scott South Pole Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amundsen%E2%80%93Scott_South_Pole_Station "Amundsen–Scott South Pole Station") at the geographical [South Pole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Pole "South Pole"). When Kunlun is not in operation, the year-round Russian [Vostok Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vostok_Station "Vostok Station") is the second-southernmost base in Antarctica.

It is located at 4087 m above sea level on the [East Antarctic Ice Sheet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Antarctic_Ice_Sheet "East Antarctic Ice Sheet"), making it the highest base in Antarctica.[^2] It is only 7.3 km southwest of [Dome A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dome_A "Dome A"), the highest point on the [Antarctic Plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic_Plateau "Antarctic Plateau"). The station was officially opened on January 27, 2009. Fully constructed, the station is planned to cover an area of 558 m <sup>2</sup>. The main building, covering 236 m <sup>2</sup>, is planned to be erected in April 2009.[^3]

The site is one of the coldest in the world, with temperatures occasionally reaching −80 °C (−112 °F) in the winter. It is indicated from satellite measurements that places nearby could reach a world record −90 °C (−130 °F) temperature.

In April 2012 the first of three [Antarctica Schmidt telescopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica_Schmidt_telescopes "Antarctica Schmidt telescopes") (AST3) was installed at Kunlun Station. The other two were planned for installation in 2013 and 2014. A bigger optical telescope, [Kunlun Dark Universe Survey Telescope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kunlun_Dark_Universe_Survey_Telescope "Kunlun Dark Universe Survey Telescope") (KDUST), is planned to be installed by 2025.
