The [Republic of Chile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chile "Chile") and the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") (PRC) established formal diplomatic relations in 1970. Previously, Chile had recognized the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") since 1915. Both nations are members of the [Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Economic_Cooperation "Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation") and the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations").

## History

Relations between the People's Republic of China and Chile were established on 15 December 1970, shortly following the [election of Salvador Allende](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1970_Chilean_presidential_election "1970 Chilean presidential election"), and Chile became the first South American country to recognize the mainland Chinese government.[^1] [^2] Following the [1973 Chilean coup d'état](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1973_Chilean_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "1973 Chilean coup d'état") which saw the overthrow of the Allende government, China was one of only two Communist countries (the other was Romania) [^3] not to have severed ties with [Augusto Pinochet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Augusto_Pinochet "Augusto Pinochet") 's new regime, due to the latter's continued endorsement of the [One China Policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_China_Policy "One China Policy").[^4] As a result, China chose not to recall its ambassador in Chile, while replacing the Allende appointed ambassador [Armando Uribe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armando_Uribe "Armando Uribe") with a Pinochet appointed one. China also chose to represent the affairs of Chile in the [Democratic People's Republic of Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_People%27s_Republic_of_Korea "Democratic People's Republic of Korea") (DPRK aka North Korea) and the affairs of the DPRK in Chile.[^5]

The continued relationship was built on pragmatism and non-interference.[^4] China supported [Chile's claim of sovereignty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chilean_Antarctic_Territory "Chilean Antarctic Territory") over [Antarctic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic "Antarctic"), and in turn, Chile allowed the Chinese to build the [Great Wall research station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic_Great_Wall_Station "Antarctic Great Wall Station") inside Chile's territorial claims. There was also a failed attempt at a joint venture to produce weapons between [Norinco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Norinco "Norinco") and [FAMAE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FAMAE "FAMAE"), aimed at reducing Chile's military dependence on the United States.[^4] Pinochet has nurtured his relationship with China.[^6] Pinochet visited China in 1993 and 1997.[^7] Despite his vehement [anti-communism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-communism "Anti-communism"), Pinochet has praised China's system of government, saying that "I saw that Chinese Communism was *patriotic* Communism, not the [Communism of Mao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maoism "Maoism"). I opened up the doors to Chinese commerce, letting them hold an exposition here, in which they brought everything they had—and they sold everything they brought".[^8]

Following the end of the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War") and [termination of the Pinochet regime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chilean_transition_to_democracy "Chilean transition to democracy") in 1990, bilateral relations continued, with the new Chilean government pursuing a policy of free trade, and supported China's entrance into the [World Trade Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Trade_Organization "World Trade Organization").[^4]

## Bilateral relations

Chile is one of the first Latin American countries which began trade and economic exchanges with China after the Chinese civil war.[^9] Bilateral trade between the two countries began as early as 1961 when China established the Commercial News Office of Chinese Import and Export Corporation. In 1965 was re-established as the Commercial Office of China Council for the Promotion of International Trade.[^9]

Since the establishment of the diplomatic relations in 1970, bilateral economic relations have developed considerably. Chile became China's third largest Latin American trading partner behind Brazil and Mexico. Bilateral trade volume set a record at $2.565 billion in 2002.[^9]

Chinese exports to Chile is primary in textiles, clothing, ceramics, chemicals and medicine, tools and consumer electronics.[^9]

Chilean exports to China are mainly in copper,[^10] niter, pulp, paper, fish meal and timber. This has also expanded to red wine, marine alga, potassium sulphate and fruit produce.[^9] [^11]

Chile and China signed a [free trade agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_trade_agreement "Free trade agreement") in 2008.[^12]<sup><span title="Page: 69">: 69</span> </sup> In 2018, Chinese company purchased controlling stake in [Sociedad Química y Minera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sociedad_Qu%C3%ADmica_y_Minera "Sociedad Química y Minera") (SQM), SQM being at the time is Chile lithium producer and world's biggest lithium producer.[^13] As of 2025, Chinese investment in Chile is primarily in the resources sector and predominantly mining-related.[^14]<sup><span title="Page: 15">: 15</span> </sup> In 2026 Tianqi Lithium lost in Chileans courts a challenge to the dismembering of SQM's lithium operation in [Salar de Atacama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salar_de_Atacama "Salar de Atacama") into the new public-private joint venture [Nova Andino Litio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nova_Andino_Litio "Nova Andino Litio").[^15]

In February 2016, Chile denied the Chinese ship *Silk Road Ark* permission to provide medical services to Chilean residents on board.[^16]

Incipient plans for a China–Chile submarine [optical fiber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Optical_fiber "Optical fiber") cable were the subject a controversy during the last days of the [Gabriel Boric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gabriel_Boric "Gabriel Boric") government and the entrant government of [José Antonio Kast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Antonio_Kast "José Antonio Kast"), provoking also diplomatic sanctions of the United States against a handfull of Chilean officials.[^17]

In March 2026 José Antonio Kast's government signed an agreement with the United States with regards to the exploitation and commercialization of rare-earth metals. This move has been interpreted by analysts an attempt by the United States to diminish [Chinese influence in the rare-earth metals market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rare-earth_industry_in_China "Rare-earth industry in China").[^18]

### Cerro Ventarrones observatory

In 2023, the [Catholic University of the North](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_University_of_the_North "Catholic University of the North") and the [National Astronomical Observatories of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Astronomical_Observatories_of_China "National Astronomical Observatories of China") signed an agreement to develop an astronomical observatory in Cerro Ventarrones, [Antofagasta Region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antofagasta_Region "Antofagasta Region").[^19] [^20] In April 2025, the Chilean government froze development of the project.[^20] [^21] The government's action followed reporting on the lack of transparency around it and potential [military intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_intelligence "Military intelligence") uses by the [People's Liberation Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army "People's Liberation Army").[^19] [^22]
