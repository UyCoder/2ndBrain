**China–East Germany relations** refers to historical [foreign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_relations "International relations") and [bilateral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilateralism "Bilateralism") relations between the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") (PRC) and the [German Democratic Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_Democratic_Republic "German Democratic Republic") (East Germany). Diplomatic relations were established on 27 October 1949, shortly after the establishment of both the PRC and the GDR, and were maintained until [German reunification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_reunification "German reunification") on 3 October 1990.

## History

The [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") was proclaimed by [Mao Zedong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mao_Zedong "Mao Zedong") in 1949, the same year that Germany Democratic Republic was founded. Due to their shared socialist orientation, both states established diplomatic relations in the first year of their existence. Both states subsequently established trade relations. Until 1971, the GDR remained the only German state with diplomatic relations with the People's Republic. The German Democratic Republic had good relations with the PRC, despite the [Sino-Soviet Split](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Soviet_Split "Sino-Soviet Split") that occurred for most of the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War") until the [1989 Sino-Soviet Summit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1989_Sino-Soviet_Summit "1989 Sino-Soviet Summit"). However, the split strained relations with the GDR, which, like most socialist states, sided with the Soviets.[^1]

Since the March 1982 speech on Sino-Soviet rapprochement by General Secretary [Leonid Brezhnev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonid_Brezhnev "Leonid Brezhnev") to the [Communist Party of Uzbekistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Uzbekistan "Communist Party of Uzbekistan") in [Tashkent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tashkent "Tashkent"), Sino-East German relations began to steadily improve. In June 1986, Foreign Minister [Wu Xueqian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wu_Xueqian "Wu Xueqian") visited [East Berlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Berlin "East Berlin") in the highest-level Chinese delegation to [Eastern Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Europe "Eastern Europe") since the 1961 split.[^2] Moreover, Chairman [Erich Honecker](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erich_Honecker "Erich Honecker") visited [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") in early October 1986, where he was met by Chinese President [Li Xiannian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Xiannian "Li Xiannian") with in a welcoming ceremony on [Tiananmen Square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiananmen_Square "Tiananmen Square") a [military band](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_band "Military band") and a marchpast by the [People's Liberation Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Liberation_Army "People's Liberation Army") [honor guard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Honor_guard "Honor guard"). The visit became the first official visit by an [Eastern Bloc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Bloc "Eastern Bloc") leader to the PRC.[^3] [^4] During the visit, Honecker tried to mediate between the Soviet Union and the People's Republic.[^5] When the [Tiananmen square massacre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiananmen_massacre "Tiananmen massacre") occurred in 1989, the GDR approved the violence against the demonstrators in Beijing. The [Volkskammer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volkskammer "Volkskammer") passed a resolution in which the GDR announced its support for the suppression of the “counter-revolutionary unrest”.[^6] In October 1989, Chinese Vice Premier [Yao Yilin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yao_Yilin "Yao Yilin") visited East Germany to attend the military parade commemorating the [40th anniversary of the GDR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_German_Republic_Day_Parade_of_1989 "East German Republic Day Parade of 1989").[^7]<sup><span title="Pages: 274">: 274</span> </sup> The first freely elected Volkskammer reversed the resolution supporting China's actions in Tiananmen Square in 1990 and condemned the violence.[^5]
