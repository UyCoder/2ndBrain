**Taishan Station** ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 泰山站; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Tàishān Zhàn*) is the fourth of the five [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") research stations in [Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica").

Officially opened on February 8, 2014, it is the fourth Chinese research station in Antarctica following [Great Wall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic_Great_Wall_Station "Antarctic Great Wall Station"), [Zhongshan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic_Zhongshan_Station "Antarctic Zhongshan Station") and [Kunlun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctic_Kunlun_Station "Antarctic Kunlun Station") stations. The fifth, [Qinling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qinling_Station_\(Antarctica\) "Qinling Station (Antarctica)"), followed in February 2024.

The site is located 2,621 m above sea level in [Princess Elizabeth Land](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Princess_Elizabeth_Land "Princess Elizabeth Land"), 522 km and 600 km to Zhongshan and Kunlun stations respectively.[^2] One of its functions is to serve as a relay point between the two stations.
