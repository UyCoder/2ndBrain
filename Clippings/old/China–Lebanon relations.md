**China–Lebanon relations** ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 中黎关系; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Zhōng lí guānxì*; [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language "Arabic language"): العلاقات الصينية اللبنانية), also referred to as the **Sino-Lebanese relations**,[^1] refer to the bilateral relationship between [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") and [Lebanon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanon "Lebanon").

While initially hostile due to Lebanon's more "West-leaning" ties in the 1950s, relations between Lebanon and China were first established in 1971, with Lebanon becoming one of the first countries to form formal ties with China. The first [Confucius Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucius_Institute "Confucius Institute") in the [Middle East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_East "Middle East") was opened in Lebanon in 2006. In 2013, China became Lebanon's top trading partner, accounting for 40% of Lebanese imports.

## History

### Initial hostility and first relations (1949–1972)

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/8/87/Ma_Haide_%28cropped%29.jpg)

Ma Haide in 1944

[Lebanese-American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanese_Americans "Lebanese Americans") doctor [Ma Haide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ma_Haide "Ma Haide") (born George Hatem), was the first foreigner to become a [citizen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_nationality_law "Chinese nationality law") of the newly formed [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") in 1949; he was the only Westerner to fight for the [Chinese Red Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Red_Army "Chinese Red Army").[^2]

Lebanon did not recognise the [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") (CCP) as the central authority of the People's Republic of China following its formation in 1949.[^3] [^4] China, who backed the North Korean troops against the US forces in the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War") in 1951, was labelled an "aggressor" by Lebanon following their intervention.[^3] [^4] In the 1950s, Lebanon's relations with China were hostile, as they looked to strengthen ties with the West and the US.[^3] Following the [Bandung Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bandung_Conference "Bandung Conference") in 1955, China and Lebanon negotiated a trade agreement; this was the first sign of positive relations between the two countries.[^3]

The two countries first established diplomatic relations on 9 November 1971; Lebanon became one of the first countries to formally establish relations with China.[^1] In 1972, China opened a branch of the [National Bank of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Bank_of_China "National Bank of China") in [Beirut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beirut "Beirut"), Lebanon.[^3] Later that year, a trade agreement was established, following a visit to [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") by the Lebanese [Minister of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_and_Emigrants_\(Lebanon\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs and Emigrants (Lebanon)"), [Khalil Abou Hamad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khalil_Abou_Hamad "Khalil Abou Hamad").[^1] Due to the outbreak of the [Lebanese Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanese_Civil_War "Lebanese Civil War"), further developments in the relations between the two countries were delayed until the early 2000s.[^1]

### Growth in relations and formal deals (2004–2018)

Lebanon is a member of the multilateral cooperation group between China and the Arab States, the [China–Arab States Cooperation Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Arab_States_Cooperation_Forum "China–Arab States Cooperation Forum") (CASCF), formed in 2004.[^5] The Arab countries coordinate their activities with CASCF through the [Arab League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League "Arab League").[^5] This facilitates coordination on collective projects like railway and nuclear power projects, and provides a way for the Arab countries to present a unified voice in their dealings with China.[^5] CASCF areas of cooperation include political issues, including Lebanon and [Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syria "Syria") 's respective territorial disputes with [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel "Israel").[^6]

In 2005, China and Lebanon signed a tourism cooperation deal, which intended to increase investment in reciprocal touristic sectors.[^1] China opened the first [Confucius Institute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucius_Institute "Confucius Institute") in the Middle East at [Saint Joseph University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saint_Joseph_University "Saint Joseph University") in Beirut in 2006.[^7] [^8] It has been regarded as "an important platform for teaching Chinese language and disseminating Chinese culture in Lebanon" by the [Ministry of Foreign Affairs of the People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (China)").[^9]

During the [2006 Lebanon War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2006_Lebanon_War "2006 Lebanon War"), China evacuated 167 of its citizens from Lebanon.[^10] In line with China's [policy of non-interference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five_Principles_of_Peaceful_Coexistence "Five Principles of Peaceful Coexistence"), [Liu Zhenmin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liu_Zhenmin "Liu Zhenmin"), Deputy Permanent Representative of China to the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations"), criticised both Israel and [Hezbollah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hezbollah "Hezbollah") 's actions in the war.[^3] [China's Special Envoy on the Middle East Issue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%27s_Special_Envoy_on_the_Middle_East_Issue "China's Special Envoy on the Middle East Issue") at the time, [Sun Bigan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Bigan "Sun Bigan"), heavily criticized Israel for its disproportionate use of force, and stated that the UN resolutions being drafted at the time must take into account the views of Lebanon and the Arab countries.[^11] Also in 2006, Sun attended an international conference on Lebanon's reconstruction and China provided 150 tons of humanitarian assistance to Lebanon, including food, medicine, and electric generators.[^12] In 2008, CASCF documents highlighted solidarity and economic and political support to Lebanon.[^13] In 2010, CASCF called for implementation of the [UN Security Council resolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council_Resolution_1701 "United Nations Security Council Resolution 1701") on occupied territory in southern Lebanon.[^13] Qian Minjian, China's Ambassador to Lebanon, stated in 2012: "When it comes to the conflict with Israel, there is no doubt that Israel is the one occupying Lebanese land".[^14]

China became Lebanon's top trading partner in 2013, following a steady increase in two-way trade from the level of 2012.[^9] Indeed, China supplied 40% of Lebanese imports.[^15] In November 2013, vice chairperson of the [Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_People%27s_Political_Consultative_Conference "Chinese People's Political Consultative Conference") (CPPCC) [Luo Fuhe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luo_Fuhe "Luo Fuhe") attended the reception marking the 70th [Lebanese Independence Day](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanese_Independence_Day "Lebanese Independence Day").[^9] In 2016, Lebanese diplomats expressed their support for China's position on its [territorial disputes in the South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Territorial_disputes_in_the_South_China_Sea "Territorial disputes in the South China Sea").[^13] Also in 2016, China provided aid to Lebanon in order to assist Lebanon in helping [Syrian refugees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_refugees_in_Lebanon "Syrian refugees in Lebanon").[^16] Since 2016, China's Special Envoy on the [Syrian Crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_civil_war "Syrian civil war") Xie Xiaoyan has met regularly with Hezbollah about Syria, discussing efforts to fight [terrorism in Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrorism_in_Syria "Terrorism in Syria") and the importance of reaching political solutions.[^17] On 10 July 2018, [CCP general secretary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party") [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") pledged to provide CN¥600 million ($91 million) in financial aid to [Jordan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan "Jordan"), Lebanon, Syria and [Yemen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemen "Yemen"), as part of a model to revive economic growth in the [Middle East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_East "Middle East").[^18]

### Chinese aid in the Lebanese crisis (2019–present)

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6c/Lebanese_Embassy_in_Beijing.JPG/250px-Lebanese_Embassy_in_Beijing.JPG)

Embassy of Lebanon in Beijing, China

From 2000 to 2014, Lebanon had received US$20 million in aid from China.[^20] Following [Lebanon's liquidity crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanese_liquidity_crisis "Lebanese liquidity crisis") since 2019, its government has looked to China for economic investments, an approach strongly supported by Lebanese political party and militant group Hezbollah.[^21] Lebanese Prime Minister [Hassan Diab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hassan_Diab "Hassan Diab") stated in 2020 that China had offered to help end Lebanon's [electrical power crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_in_Lebanon "Energy in Lebanon") through its [state companies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State-owned_enterprises_of_China "State-owned enterprises of China").[^21] The Chinese government also offered to build [power stations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Power_station "Power station"), a [tunnel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunnel "Tunnel"), and a [railway](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rail_transport "Rail transport").[^21] Lebanese economist Hasan Moukalled valued the offered project at $12.5 billion.[^21] The Lebanese port city [Tripoli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tripoli,_Lebanon "Tripoli, Lebanon") has been seen as an important link in China's [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative").[^15]

This potential for further cooperation, though, has been viewed as coming at the cost of Lebanon's historic [ties with the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanon%E2%80%93United_States_relations "Lebanon–United States relations"), due to rising tensions between [China and the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93United_States_relations "China–United States relations").[^21] Hezbollah, known for their [anti-Americanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-Americanism "Anti-Americanism"), have advocated for a bigger role for China in Lebanon.[^21] Contrary to the United States,[^3] China does not view Hezbollah as a terrorist organisation, rather as a "legitimate effort of the Lebanese to protect their country and preserve its sovereignty".[^14]

Lebanon was also among the 53 countries supporting China's [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law") at the United Nations on 30 June 2020.[^22] In September 2021, on the 50th anniversary of the establishment of the bilateral relations between the two countries, Chinese leader Xi Jinping and Ambassador to Lebanon Qian Minjian further stressed China's assistance and cooperation with Lebanon.[^23] [^24]
