**China–Spain relations** have existed since the 16th century. In modern times, official diplomatic relations between [Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain "Spain") and the [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") were established in 1973.

## History

Contact between China and Spain first occurred between the [Ming dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty") of China and the [Spanish-ruled Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_Philippines_\(1521%E2%80%931898\) "History of the Philippines (1521–1898)"), in which Spain believed it could [take over China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empresa_de_China "Empresa de China").[^1] [^2]

When the Chinese pirate [Limahong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limahong "Limahong") attacked [Manila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manila "Manila") in 1574, officials in [Fujian Province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fujian_Province "Fujian Province") were willing to let the Spanish establish a trade port on an island south of [Xiamen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xiamen "Xiamen"), in return for Limahong's capture. However, the governor of the Philippines did not respond favourably, and the offer came to nothing when Limahong escaped from Manila.[^3]

In 1598, Cantonese officials allowed Spain to trade in [El Piñal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Pi%C3%B1al "El Piñal"), a port in the Pearl River Delta near [Macau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macau "Macau"). The [Portuguese in Macau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Macau "Portuguese Macau") reacted violently and chased away the Spanish from the area by arms in 1600.[^3]

In 1927, a treaty recognizing extraterritoriality was signed between the [Kingdom of Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bourbon_Restoration_in_Spain "Bourbon Restoration in Spain") and [Chiang Kai-shek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek "Chiang Kai-shek") 's Nationalist government. The Spanish consul general in [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai") was also the [minister plenipotentiary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_plenipotentiary "Minister plenipotentiary") to China.[^4] The [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China "Republic of China") had diplomatic relations with Spain under [Francisco Franco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francisco_Franco "Francisco Franco").[^5]

[Francoist Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francoist_Spain "Francoist Spain") 's official diplomatic relations with the People's Republic of China began in March 1973.[^6]

## Bilateral relations

According to the Chinese state-run [Xinhua News Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinhua_News_Agency "Xinhua News Agency"), the volume of trade between the two countries grew considerably in the 2000s. Total trade, at US$7.2 billion in 2004, had increased to $22.7 billion by October 2008, and China became Spain's sixth-largest trading partner.[^7]

Manuela Carmena and Xi Jinping during the latter's visit to Madrid in 2018

In 2018, during General Secretary of the Chinese Communist Party [Xi Jinping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xi_Jinping "Xi Jinping") 's state visit to Spain, Prime Minister [Pedro Sánchez](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedro_S%C3%A1nchez "Pedro Sánchez") refused to sign a memorandum of understanding on the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative").[^8]

In June 2019, Spain had extradited 94 [Taiwanese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan") nationals to [mainland China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_China "Mainland China") instead of Taiwan as part of "Operation Great Wall".[^9] By 2021, the number had increased to more than 200.[^10] Both countries signed an extradition agreement in 2006.[^9]

In July 2019, the UN ambassadors from 22 nations, including Spain, signed a joint letter to the [UNHRC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Human_Rights_Council "United Nations Human Rights Council") condemning China's alleged mistreatment of the [Uyghurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uyghurs "Uyghurs") as well as of other minority groups and urged the Chinese government to close the [Xinjiang internment camps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang_internment_camps "Xinjiang internment camps").[^11] [^12]

The [International Department](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Department_of_the_Chinese_Communist_Party "International Department of the Chinese Communist Party") of the [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") maintains relations with major Spanish political parties [Spanish Socialist Workers' Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party "Spanish Socialist Workers' Party") (PSOE) and the [People's Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Party_\(Spain\) "People's Party (Spain)").[^13] [Vox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vox_\(political_party\) "Vox (political party)") has maintained a critical stance toward the Chinese government.[^13]

During the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic"), it was reported in April 2020 that 640,000 Antigen Coronavirus Test Kits which themselves were replacements for previously supplied faulty kits, bought from a Chinese company, Bioeasy, had been found to be defective, prompting Spain to seek a refund.[^14] The Spanish Health Ministry reported that faulty face masks bought from another Chinese company, Garry Galaxy, had resulted in infections among health workers, requiring the isolation of over a thousand Spanish healthcare personnel.[^15]

In September 2024, [Spanish Prime Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Prime_Minister "Spanish Prime Minister") Pedro Sánchez held talks with [Chinese leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paramount_leader "Paramount leader") Xi Jinping to enhance bilateral relations, focusing on global conflicts of Ukraine and economic cooperation. The discussions addressed trade disputes, such as EU tariffs on Chinese electric vehicles and Spain's pork exports, while also emphasizing the importance of strengthening business and cultural ties between the two nations.[^16]

In July 2025, the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") raised cybersecurity concerns as the Spanish government contracts with [Huawei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huawei "Huawei") to manage and store its wiretaps for law enforcement and intelligence services.[^17] [^18] [^19]

## Resident diplomatic missions

- China has an embassy in [Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madrid "Madrid") and a consulate-general in [Barcelona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barcelona "Barcelona").
- Spain has an embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and consulates-general in [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou"), [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong") and [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai").

## Public opinion

A survey published in 2025 by the [Pew Research Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pew_Research_Center "Pew Research Center") found that 53% of Spanish people had an unfavorable view of China, while 37% had a favorable view. It also found that 53% of Spanish people in the 18-35 age group had positive opinions of China.[^20]
