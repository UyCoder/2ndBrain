[Bilateral relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bilateral_relations "Bilateral relations") between China and Italy date back to [Imperial China and Ancient Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Roman_relations "Sino-Roman relations") but the ties between [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy "Italy") and modern [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") only formally began on 27 November 1928 (began in 1913) and recognized the People's Republic on 6 November 1970.[^1] News of Italy's recognition of the People's Republic of China and consequent breaking of formal relations with the Republic of China ([Taiwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiwan "Taiwan")) spurred other European countries such as [Austria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austria "Austria") and [Belgium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgium "Belgium") to consider similar moves.[^2]

Italian Foreign Minister [Pietro Nenni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pietro_Nenni "Pietro Nenni") presented the proposal for the recognition of China in January 1969.[^3] The [Italian Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Communist_Party "Italian Communist Party") had invited [Chinese Communist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Communist_Party "Chinese Communist Party") (CCP) representatives to attend their 1969 party congress; however, the Chinese side declined the invitation.[^4] The two countries exchanged ambassadors in February of the following year.[^5]

Currently, China and Italy participate in high-level political exchanges. In September 2005, Chinese Defense Minister [Cao Gangchuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cao_Gangchuan "Cao Gangchuan") and Italian Deputy Defense Minister Salvatore Cicu expressed their hope for closer military cooperation between the two countries.[^6]

[Hongdu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hongdu "Hongdu") Aviation, one of China's major aircraft manufacturers, was first established as Sino-Italian National Aircraft Works (SINAW) in 1934, as a joint venture between the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") and the [Kingdom of Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy "Kingdom of Italy"). However, after the [Second Sino-Japanese War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Sino-Japanese_War "Second Sino-Japanese War") broke out in 1937, Italy became an ally of Japan and assisted Japan in its bombing of SINAW factories. The Chinese government confiscated Italian properties in December 1937 and all Italian employees of the company left by the end of the year.[^7] On March 24, 2019, Italy signed up to the [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative"), becoming the first [G7 nation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_of_Seven "Group of Seven") to do so.[^8]

[Chinese people in Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_people_in_Italy "Chinese people in Italy"), comprising both recent immigrants as well as persons of Chinese descent born in Italy, form a significant minority especially in [Lombardy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lombardy "Lombardy"), [Tuscany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tuscany "Tuscany"), and [Lazio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lazio "Lazio").

## History

The Roman historian [Florus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florus "Florus") describes the visit of numerous envoys, including *[Seres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serica "Serica")* (Chinese or central Asians), to the first Roman Emperor [Augustus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caesar_Augustus "Caesar Augustus"), who reigned between 27 BC and 14.[^9] [^10] However, [Henry Yule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_Yule "Henry Yule") speculated that these were more likely to have been private merchants than diplomats, since Chinese records insist that Gan Ying was the first Chinese to reach as far west as Tiaozhi (條支; [Mesopotamia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesopotamia "Mesopotamia")) in 97 AD. In 97 the Chinese general [Ban Chao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ban_Chao "Ban Chao") dispatched an envoy to [Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome "Rome") in the person of [Gan Ying](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gan_Ying "Gan Ying"). Gan Ying never made it to Rome. He might have reached the eastern coast of the [Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean "Mediterranean"),[^11] although he most likely went no further than the [Persian Gulf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_Gulf "Persian Gulf"). Monk turned diplomat [Rabban Bar Sauma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rabban_Bar_Sauma "Rabban Bar Sauma") traveled from Beijing to Rome in the 1280s.[^12] Andalò da Savignone, an Italian resident in the small Genoese colony in [Zaytun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quanzhou "Quanzhou"), was sent by the Khans from Beijing to Italy as a diplomat to the pope in 1336.[^13] Chinese diplomats have been active in Italy since the time of the [Qing dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty") and just a few decades after the [Unification of Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unification_of_Italy "Unification of Italy"). The Qing dynasty diplomat [Li Fengbao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Fengbao "Li Fengbao") was ambassador to Italy in the late 19th century, as were his contemporaries [Kung Chao-Yuan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kung_Chao-Yuan "Kung Chao-Yuan"), [Xue Fucheng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xue_Fucheng "Xue Fucheng") and [Xu Jingcheng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xu_Jingcheng "Xu Jingcheng"). The [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") would later send its own ambassadors after Italy's recognition of the Republic. Modern China's first ambassador was [Shen Ping](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Shen_Ping&action=edit&redlink=1 "Shen Ping (page does not exist)") (沈平), while the current ambassador is [Li Junhua](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Li_Junhua "Li Junhua") (李军华).[^14]

During the era of the Roman Empire, the first group of people claiming to be an ambassadorial mission of Romans to China was recorded in 16 AD in the *Book of the Later Han*. The embassy came to [Emperor Huan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emperor_Huan_of_Han "Emperor Huan of Han") of [Han China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_China "Han China") from "Andun" ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 安敦; Emperor [Antoninus Pius](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antoninus_Pius "Antoninus Pius") or [Marcus Aurelius Antoninus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marcus_Aurelius "Marcus Aurelius")), "king of Daqin" (Rome).[^15] [^16] In 226 AD China was visited by the Roman [Qin Lun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daqin#Naming_conventions "Daqin") (秦論), who wasn't a diplomat but a merchant. He visited the court of [Sun Quan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Quan "Sun Quan") in [Nanjing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanjing "Nanjing").[^17] [^18] Other Roman envoys followed in the 3rd century. One embassy from Daqin ([Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome "Rome")) is recorded as bringing tributary gifts to the Chinese [Jin Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Jin "Western Jin") (266–420 AD).[^18] [^19]

The most notable contact between China and Italy dates back to over 700 years ago, with Italian explorer [Marco Polo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marco_Polo "Marco Polo") famously sojourning in the country for seventeen years between 1271 and 1295,[^20] a few decades after [Giovanni da Pian del Carpine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giovanni_da_Pian_del_Carpine "Giovanni da Pian del Carpine") 's visit to [Karakorum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karakorum "Karakorum").[^21] Polo's visit was followed by the arrival in Beijing of [Giovanni da Montecorvino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giovanni_da_Montecorvino "Giovanni da Montecorvino"), the first [archbishop of Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archbishop_of_Beijing "Archbishop of Beijing"), in 1294.[^22] [Michele Ruggieri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michele_Ruggieri "Michele Ruggieri"), an Italian Jesuit priest from [Apulia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apulia "Apulia") and one of the founding figures of the [Jesuit China missions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesuit_China_missions "Jesuit China missions"), was the first European to enter the [Forbidden City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forbidden_City "Forbidden City").[^23] [^24]

The [Kingdom of Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy "Kingdom of Italy") established diplomatic relations with Qing Dynasty of China in 1866.[^25]

There was a Sino-Italian diplomatic incident that occurred in March 1899 refers *[Sanmen Bay Affair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanmen_Bay_Affair "Sanmen Bay Affair")*. Italy sought to occupy the [Sanmen Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanmen_County "Sanmen County") in front of Shanghai, but the Chinese government rejected their claim. In response, the Italian government presented an ultimatum to the Qing embassy and sent military forces to occupy the area, which led to a diplomatic crisis. This event is significant as it highlighted Italy's struggle for recognition and influence in China, and it may have influenced the Chinese government's approach to foreign powers, ultimately contributing to the Boxer Rebellion later that year.[^26] [^27] [^28] Italy received [its concession in Tientsin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_concession_of_Tientsin "Italian concession of Tientsin") on 7 September 1901. The concession was occupied by the Japanese in September 1943 after Italy surrendered to the Allies, and liberated in October 1945 by the Chinese Army.[^29] Italy officially renounced its claims to the concession in 1947.[^29] On October 16, 1866, a trade treaty between China an Italy was signed, and eight months later the ambassador Count [Vittorio Sallier de la Tour](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Vittorio_Sallier_de_la_Tour&action=edit&redlink=1 "Vittorio Sallier de la Tour (page does not exist)"), appointed Italian representative to the courts of [Jeddo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeddo,_Japan "Jeddo, Japan") and [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing"), arrived in East Asia.[^25] He and his successors [Alessandro Fè d'Ostiani](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alessandro_F%C3%A8_d%27Ostiani_\(politico\) "it:Alessandro Fè d'Ostiani (politico)") and [Raffaele Ulisse Barbolani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raffaele_Ulisse_Barbolani "Raffaele Ulisse Barbolani"), however, kept their seat in Japan.[^25] [Ferdinando de Luca](https://it.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinando_de_Luca "it:Ferdinando de Luca") was the first Italian ambassador to be headquartered in China, establishing the embassy in [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai"), which was moved to Beijing at the end of the 1880s.[^25] In 1913, Italy recognized [Sun Yat-sen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen "Sun Yat-sen") 's Republic, and thereafter maintained a position of neutrality in the various conflicts with different parties vying for power in China.[^25] Italy formally recognized [Kuomintang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuomintang "Kuomintang") 's [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)"), and on November 27, 1928, signed a new trade treaty, which supplanted the 1866 treaty.[^25] The negotiations were led by [Daniele Varè](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniele_Var%C3%A8 "Daniele Varè") and the treaty signed in [Nanjing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanjing "Nanjing"), China's new capital.[^25] [Chang Kai-shek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chang_Kai-shek "Chang Kai-shek") insisted that all the Occidental powers move their embassies in Nanjing, considering this action as the further, final acknowledgment of his power.[^25] Italy, as some other great powers, decided to split its embassy between Nanjing and Shanghai, in two physically detached offices.[^25]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/ff/Xue_Fucheng2.jpg/250px-Xue_Fucheng2.jpg)

Xue Fucheng, Chinese ambassador to Italy

At the end of 1937 [Fascist Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fascist_Italy_\(1922%E2%80%931943\) "Fascist Italy (1922–1943)") recognized [Manchukuo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchukuo "Manchukuo"), and in 1941 it recognized [Wang Jingwei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Jingwei "Wang Jingwei") 's [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wang_Jingwei_regime "Wang Jingwei regime"), with its capital in [Nanjing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanjing "Nanjing"), although the Italian ambassadors continued to reside in Shanghai for some more time.[^25] The Italian delegate Pier Pasquale Spinelli then decided to move the embassy's principal seat to Nanjing, while also reopening the detached offices of Beijing and Shanghai.[^25] In 1945, Mussolini's followers kept in touch with Wang Jingwei's government in Beijing, Shanghai and Nanjing, while the post-fascist [Ministry of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(Italy\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (Italy)") worked to resume contact with Chiang Kai-shek, to whom the new Italian representative [Sergio Fenoaltea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sergio_Fenoaltea "Sergio Fenoaltea") officially [presented his credentials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Letter_of_credence "Letter of credence") in October 1946 in Naijing.[^25] In 1945, the Italians were about to loan the offices of the closed embassy in Beijing to the Americans, which however didn't materialize because before the Americans arrived, a group of Chinese, on the nationalist side but following the orders of an independent general looking for riches, looted the embassy and damaged the building and the archives.[^25] After the [start of the offensive in 1947](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War#1947 "Chinese Civil War"), the CCP took Beijing in 1949 and shortly after Naijing.[^25] In November 1950, Fenoaltea, who had been pressuring Rome to let him come back to Italy, departed from China.[^25] Diplomatic relations were then led by [Ezio Mizzan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ezio_Mizzan "Ezio Mizzan") who, in February 1951, reported that the CCP police was restricting ever more his activities and movements and that the permanence of the Italian embassy in Naijing, Chiang Kai-shek's and Wang Jingwei's former capital, may cause political inconveniences.[^25] He advised Rome to take a firm decision either recognizing China, moving him and the embassy to Beijing, or moving all diplomats out.[^30] [^25] After initially leaning towards following such countries as Denmark, Norway and the United Kingdom in recognizing China, Italy chose the opposite, dismantling Naijing's embassy and breaking diplomatic relations,[^25] influenced by the counsel of the United States, which had initially pondered allowing Italy's recognition of China to pave the way for the inevitable recognition by them.[^31]

Italy and China wouldn't recognize each other until 1970.[^25] During the twenty-year break of diplomatic relations, Italy didn't have an embassy in China, and maintained relations with nationalist China, although it never sent an ambassador to Taipei either.[^30] In the twenty-year break, Italy "limited itself to giving some unspecified duties of observation of the Chinese question" to its general consulate in Hong Kong.[^30] In 1970, the news of Italy's recognition of the People's Republic of China and consequent breaking of formal relations with the Republic of China spurred other European countries such as [Austria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austria "Austria") and [Belgium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgium "Belgium") to consider similar moves.[^2]

In March 2019, during Chinese President Xi Jinping's visit to [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy "Italy"), [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") signed a [memorandum of understanding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memorandum_of_understanding "Memorandum of understanding") on China's [Belt and Road Initiative (BRI)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative") with Italy. Italy became the only G7 country to join the BRI.[^32] In July 2023 declared its intention to quit the BRI.[^33] Prime Minister [Giorgia Meloni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giorgia_Meloni "Giorgia Meloni") stated that the project was not of any real benefit to Italy's economy.[^34] The [Ministry of Foreign Affairs of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs_\(China\) "Ministry of Foreign Affairs (China)") described Italy's actions as "disrupting cooperation and creating divisions".[^35]<sup><span title="Page: 71">: 71</span> </sup> Publishing in 2025, academic Chuchu Zhang writes that bilateral economic cooperation between Italy and China was not disrupted by Italy's intent to withdraw, and trade between the two countries increased through February 2024.[^35]<sup><span title="Page: 70">: 70</span></sup>

In March 2020, Chinese state owned TV channel [CGTN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CGTN "CGTN") and Chinese newspaper [Global Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_Times "Global Times") were reported to have attempted to spread a disinformation campaign that the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic "COVID-19 pandemic") may have originated in Italy after the United States.[^36] [^37]

On 13 March 2020, China sent medical supplies, including masks and respirators to Italy, together with a team of Chinese medical staff to help Italy and fight the virus across the country.[^38] [^39] [^40] After China donated millions of masks, China sold masks to Italy. These were not donations but rather paid products and services.[^41] [^42] On March 24, 2020, Italian Foreign Minister [Luigi Di Maio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luigi_Di_Maio "Luigi Di Maio") stated in an interview that after the outbreak in Italy, China was the first to provide Italy with medical supplies and dispatch medical experts. When the virus broke out, Italy was accused of dumping materials used to protect Italians at a low price because it presented 40,000 masks to China. Today, China is giving back millions of masks.[^43] Italian Prime Minister Giuseppe Conte thanked China for its support and assistance.[^44] However, in April 2020 after the tragedy, it was reported that the PPE kits sold by China to Italy were the same that Italy had earlier donated to China during the initial spread of the [COVID-19 in China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_mainland_China "COVID-19 pandemic in mainland China").[^45] [^46] The Chinese Ministry of Foreign Affairs responded: This is false news. The Italian government did not respond yet.[^47]

In May 2020, Chinese diplomats [Hua Chunying](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hua_Chunying "Hua Chunying") and [Zhao Lijian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhao_Lijian "Zhao Lijian") each tweeted a video of Italians chanting "Grazie, Cina" with [China's national anthem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/March_of_the_Volunteers "March of the Volunteers") playing in the background. Analysis by the *[Financial Times](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Financial_Times "Financial Times")* suggests that the videos were doctored, raising concerns about [Chinese propaganda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Propaganda_in_China "Propaganda in China") activities in the European Union [^48] [^49] and attempts to undermine [Europe's response to the health crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union_response_to_the_COVID-19_pandemic "European Union response to the COVID-19 pandemic") and project China and Russia as the only ones with a robust strategy to combat COVID-19.[^50]

In March 2026, the Italian [Ministry of the Interior](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Interior_\(Italy\) "Ministry of the Interior (Italy)") ordered the expulsion of eight Chinese nationals suspected of spying on dissidents and conducting [transnational repression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transnational_repression_by_China "Transnational repression by China") on Italian soil.[^51]

## Security concerns

In March 2019, Italy's parliamentary [Comitato parliamentare per la sicurezza della Repubblica (COPASIR)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COPASIR "COPASIR"), the body of the Italian Parliament to oversee the Italian intelligence agencies raised concerns that Italy joining the Belt and Road Initiative (BRI) project could present security risks for Italy given that infrastructure, telecoms, finance were all in the BRI agreement package to be signed with China.[^52] The chief of COPASIR also raised the concerns of "evaluation of possible risks" in the area of cyber security.[^53]

Concerns have been raised by the EU and the US over technology transfer and protection of [intellectual property](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intellectual_property "Intellectual property") of European partners as well as Chinese involvement in Italian telecommunications networks raising fears regarding [network security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Network_security "Network security") in Europe and the US.[^54] [^52]

In October 2020, in signs of Italy toughening its stance with respect to communications and network security, the Italian government vetoed a [5G](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/5G "5G") deal between Italian telecom firm [Fastweb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fastweb_\(telecommunications_company\) "Fastweb (telecommunications company)") and China's [Huawei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huawei "Huawei").[^55] Earlier in July, Italy's largest telecom company [Telecom Italia (TIM)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Telecom_Italia "Telecom Italia") had left Huawei out of an invitation to tender for a contract to supply 5G equipment for its core network, where sensitive data was processed.[^55]

In November 2024, Italy opened an investigation into [Sinochem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinochem "Sinochem"), the largest shareholder of [Pirelli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pirelli "Pirelli"), for potential violations of restrictions to protect Italian national strategic assets.[^56] Citing concerns about sales into the U.S. market with a Chinese state shareholder, the Italian government aims to make Sinochem a passive investor in Pirelli.[^57] [^58]

### Cyberwarfare

Between 2024 and 2025, cyber-intrusions linked to China into Italy's [Ministry of the Interior](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ministry_of_the_Interior_\(Italy\) "Ministry of the Interior (Italy)") compromised the identity of 5,000 [DIGOS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divisione_Investigazioni_Generali_e_Operazioni_Speciali "Divisione Investigazioni Generali e Operazioni Speciali") officers.[^59] In July 2025, a hacker allegedly working with [Hafnium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hafnium_\(group\) "Hafnium (group)"), Xu Zewei, was arrested in Milan.[^60] Italy extradited Xu to the United States in April 2026.[^61]

## Resident diplomatic missions

- China has an embassy in [Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome "Rome") and consulates-general in [Florence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florence "Florence") and [Milan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milan "Milan").[^62]
- Italy has an embassy in [Beijing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beijing "Beijing") and consulates-general in [Chongqing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chongqing "Chongqing"), [Guangzhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangzhou "Guangzhou"), [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong") and [Shanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai "Shanghai").[^63]
