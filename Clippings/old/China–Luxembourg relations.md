The [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_China "People's Republic of China") (PRC) and [Luxembourg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luxembourg "Luxembourg") officially established diplomatic relations on November 16, 1972.[^1] [^2]

## History

Luxembourg established official diplomatic relations with the [Nationalist government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nationalist_government "Nationalist government") of the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") (ROC) in 1949. After the [establishment of the People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Establishment_of_the_People%27s_Republic_of_China "Establishment of the People's Republic of China") (PRC) and the ROC's loss in the [Chinese Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_Civil_War "Chinese Civil War"), Luxembourg continued to recognize the ROC government as a recognized representative of China. During the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War"), [Luxembourg sent soldiers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luxembourg_in_the_Korean_War "Luxembourg in the Korean War") to fight with the [Belgian Volunteer Corps for Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belgian_Volunteer_Corps_for_Korea "Belgian Volunteer Corps for Korea") against [North Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Korea "North Korea") and the PRC's [People's Volunteer Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Volunteer_Army "People's Volunteer Army").[^3]

In November 1972, the government of Luxembourg switched recognition from the ROC to the PRC. Mutual visits commenced in the 1980s with the [Grand Duke of Luxembourg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Duke_of_Luxembourg "Grand Duke of Luxembourg") visiting China and the high-ranking officials of the State Council of the People's Republic of China visited Luxembourg.[^1] [^4]

In 1989, Luxembourg followed the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") and imposed sanctions on China. Relations gradually improved from 1991 onwards.[^1]

## Economic relations

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/64/Ambassade_Peking.jpg/250px-Ambassade_Peking.jpg)

Embassy of Luxembourg in China

The [trade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_trade "International trade") value between the two countries stood at $101 million in 2002.[^1] [^2]

Luxembourg exported [steel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steel "Steel") material, mechanic and electric products to China. Chinese exports to Luxembourg comprise [textile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Textile "Textile"), garments, plastic products and toys.[^1] [^2]

Sino-Luxembourg trade relations have also focused on reducing [trade barriers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade_barrier "Trade barrier") to investments.[^5] In March 2019, Luxembourg signed an agreement with China to cooperate on its [Belt and Road Initiative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belt_and_Road_Initiative "Belt and Road Initiative"), making it one of only a few Western European nations to do so.[^6]

## Human rights criticisms

### Hong Kong national security law

In June 2020, Luxembourg openly opposed the [Hong Kong national security law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Hong_Kong_national_security_law "2020 Hong Kong national security law").[^7]
