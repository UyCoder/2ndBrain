**Zhongshan Station** ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): 中山站; [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Zhōngshān Zhàn*) is the second [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") research station in [Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antarctica "Antarctica") and was opened on February 26, 1989.

## Overview

*Zhongshan Station* is named after [Sun Yat-sen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sun_Yat-sen "Sun Yat-sen"), who served as the [provisional first president of the Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provisional_Government_of_the_Republic_of_China_\(1912\) "Provisional Government of the Republic of China (1912)") in 1912.[^2] It is managed by the [Polar Research Institute of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polar_Research_Institute_of_China "Polar Research Institute of China") (PRIC). It is located in the [Larsemann Hills](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Larsemann_Hills "Larsemann Hills") by [Prydz Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prydz_Bay "Prydz Bay") in [East Antarctica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Antarctica "East Antarctica"), and is near the Russian [Progress II Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Progress_Station "Progress Station") and the Romanian [Law-Racoviță-Negoiță Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law-Racovi%C8%9B%C4%83-Negoi%C8%9B%C4%83_Station "Law-Racoviță-Negoiță Station").

The station can accommodate 60 summering personnel [^1] and 25 wintering personnel. It is a base for research on marine, glaciological, geological, and atmospheric sciences and for expeditions inland, such as to the [Kunlun Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kunlun_Station "Kunlun Station") at [Dome A](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dome_A "Dome A"). It is supplied by annual visits of the support vessel [*Xue Long*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xue_Long "Xue Long"). The station also hosts a [ground station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ground_station "Ground station") build by [China Aerospace Science and Industry Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_Aerospace_Science_and_Industry_Corporation "China Aerospace Science and Industry Corporation").[^3]

## History

Four years after establishing the [Great Wall Station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Wall_Station_\(Antarctica\) "Great Wall Station (Antarctica)"), China's first research base in Antarctica, [Guo Kun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guo_Kun "Guo Kun") led another expedition to Antarctica, with the mission to establish a second base. The team set out from [Qingdao](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qingdao "Qingdao") in November 1988 on the ship *Jidi*. After reaching [Prydz Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prydz_Bay "Prydz Bay") in Antarctica, the ship encountered a major [icefall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Icefall "Icefall") in the night of 14 January 1989.[^4] [^5] She missed being directly hit by ice by just two or three meters, and became trapped by icebergs for seven days. Many team members wrote their wills and were ready to die. The icebergs shifted on the seventh day and temporarily created a 30-meter-wide opening, and the team escaped. The opening lasted for just two hours before being closed again.[^4] The team proceeded to construct the Zhongshan Station at Prydz Bay in only 28 days, and it was opened on 26 February 1989.[^4] [^5]
