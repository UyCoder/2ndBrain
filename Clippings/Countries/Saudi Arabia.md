**Saudi Arabia**,[^4] officially the **Kingdom of Saudi Arabia** (**KSA**),[^5] is a country in [West Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Asia "West Asia"). Located in the centre of the [Middle East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_East "Middle East"), it covers the bulk of the [Arabian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula "Arabian Peninsula") and has a land area of about 2,150,000 km <sup>2</sup> (830,000 sq mi), making it the [fifth-largest country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Asian_countries_by_area "List of Asian countries by area") in [Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "Asia"), the largest in the Middle East, and the [twelfth-largest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_area "List of countries and dependencies by area") in the world. It is bordered by the [Red Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea "Red Sea") to the west; [Jordan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan "Jordan"), [Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq "Iraq"), and [Kuwait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuwait "Kuwait") to the north; the [Persian Gulf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_Gulf "Persian Gulf"), [Bahrain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bahrain "Bahrain"), [Qatar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qatar "Qatar") and the [United Arab Emirates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates "United Arab Emirates") to the east; [Oman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oman "Oman") to the southeast; and [Yemen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemen "Yemen") to [the south](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia%E2%80%93Yemen_border "Saudi Arabia–Yemen border"). The [Gulf of Aqaba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Aqaba "Gulf of Aqaba") in the northwest separates Saudi Arabia from [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt "Egypt") and [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel "Israel").[^24] [^25] Saudi Arabia is the only country with a coastline along both the [Red Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Sea "Red Sea") and the [Arabian Gulf](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Gulf "Arabian Gulf"), and most of its terrain consists of arid desert, lowland, steppe, and mountains. The capital and largest city is [Riyadh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riyadh "Riyadh"); other major cities include [Jeddah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeddah "Jeddah") and the two holiest cities in [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam"), [Mecca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mecca "Mecca") and [Medina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medina "Medina"). With a population of 33.7 million, Saudi Arabia is the sixth most populous country in the [Arab world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_world "Arab world").

[Pre-Islamic Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pre-Islamic_Arabia "Pre-Islamic Arabia"), the territory that constitutes modern-day Saudi Arabia, was the site of several ancient cultures and civilizations; the [archaeological study](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaeology_of_the_Arabian_Peninsula "Archaeology of the Arabian Peninsula") of the [prehistory of Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prehistoric_Arabia "Prehistoric Arabia") has identified some of the earliest traces of human activity outside [Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "Africa").[^26] [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam") emerged in what is now Saudi Arabia in the early [seventh century](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/7th_century "7th century"). Islamic prophet [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad "Muhammad") united the population of the [Arabian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula "Arabian Peninsula") and created a single Islamic religious polity. Following his death in 632, his followers expanded Muslim rule beyond Arabia, [conquering](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_Muslim_conquests "Early Muslim conquests") territories in [North Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa "North Africa"), [Central](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia"), [South Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia "South Asia") and [Iberia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula "Iberian Peninsula") within decades.[^27] [^28] [^29] Arab dynasties originating from modern-day Saudi Arabia founded the [Rashidun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashidun_Caliphate "Rashidun Caliphate") (632–661), [Umayyad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate "Umayyad Caliphate") (661–750), [Abbasid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbasid_Caliphate "Abbasid Caliphate") (750–1517), and [Fatimid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatimid_Caliphate "Fatimid Caliphate") (909–1171) caliphates, as well as [numerous other Muslim states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Muslim_states_and_dynasties "List of Muslim states and dynasties") in Asia, [Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_in_Africa#History "Islam in Africa"), and [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_in_Europe#Western_Europe_and_the_Mediterranean_Region "Islam in Europe").

Saudi Arabia was founded in 1932 by [King Abdulaziz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibn_Saud "Ibn Saud"), who united [Hejaz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hejaz "Hejaz"), [Najd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Najd "Najd"), parts of [Eastern Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Arabia "Eastern Arabia") and [South Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Arabia "South Arabia") ([Asir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asir "Asir")) into a single state through [a series of military and political campaigns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unification_of_Saudi_Arabia "Unification of Saudi Arabia") beginning in 1901. The country has since been governed as an [absolute monarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absolute_monarchy "Absolute monarchy") under the [House of Saud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Saud "House of Saud").[^30] In its [Basic Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_Law_of_Saudi_Arabia "Basic Law of Saudi Arabia"), Saudi Arabia defines itself as a sovereign Arab [Islamic state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_state "Islamic state") with [Islam as its official religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_in_Saudi_Arabia "Islam in Saudi Arabia") and [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic "Arabic") as its official language. The ultraconservative [Wahhabi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wahhabism "Wahhabism") religious movement within [Sunni Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunni_Islam "Sunni Islam") was the prevailing political and cultural force until the 2000s.[^31] [^32] The Saudi government has attracted criticism for various policies such as its [intervention in the Yemeni Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi-led_intervention_in_the_Yemeni_civil_war "Saudi-led intervention in the Yemeni civil war"), restrictions on political and civil liberties, and poor [human rights record](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_Saudi_Arabia "Human rights in Saudi Arabia").[^33] [^34] [^35] Since the mid-2010s, Saudi Arabia has instituted limited and gradual reforms to liberalize social, cultural, and religious life.[^36]

Saudi Arabia is considered both a [regional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_power "Regional power") and [middle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_power "Middle power") power,[^37] [^38] in addition to being a [major non-NATO ally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_non-NATO_ally "Major non-NATO ally") of the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States").[^39] Since [petroleum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Petroleum "Petroleum") was [discovered in the country in 1938](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dammam_No._7 "Dammam No. 7"),[^40] [^41] the kingdom has become the world's [second-largest oil producer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_oil_production "List of countries by oil production") and leading [oil exporter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_oil_exports "List of countries by oil exports"), controlling the world's [second-largest oil reserves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_proven_oil_reserves#Countries "List of countries by proven oil reserves") and [sixth-largest gas reserves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_natural_gas_proven_reserves "List of countries by natural gas proven reserves").[^42] Saudi Arabia is categorized as a [World Bank high-income economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Bank_high-income_economy "World Bank high-income economy") and is the only Arab country among the [G20](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G20 "G20") major economies.[^43] [^44] The [Saudi economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Saudi_Arabia "Economy of Saudi Arabia") is the [largest in the Middle East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_the_Middle_East "Economy of the Middle East") and the world's [nineteenth-largest by nominal GDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\) "List of countries by GDP (nominal)") and [seventeenth-largest by PPP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(PPP\) "List of countries by GDP (PPP)"). Ranking very high in the [Human Development Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index "Human Development Index"),[^45] Saudi Arabia offers [free university tuition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Higher_education_in_Saudi_Arabia "Higher education in Saudi Arabia"), no personal income tax,[^46] and [free universal health care](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_care_in_Saudi_Arabia "Health care in Saudi Arabia"). With its [dependence on foreign labour](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_workers_in_Saudi_Arabia "Foreign workers in Saudi Arabia"), Saudi Arabia has the world's [third-largest immigrant population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states_by_immigrant_and_emigrant_population "List of sovereign states by immigrant and emigrant population"), with foreign-born residents comprising roughly 40% of the population.[^47] [Saudi Arabians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudis "Saudis") are among [the world's youngest people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Youth_in_Saudi_Arabia "Youth in Saudi Arabia"), with approximately half being under 25 years old.[^48] [^49] Saudi Arabia is a member of the [Gulf Cooperation Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_Cooperation_Council "Gulf Cooperation Council"), [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations"), [Organisation of Islamic Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation "Organisation of Islamic Cooperation"), [Arab League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_League "Arab League"), and [OPEC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/OPEC "OPEC"), as well as a dialogue partner of the [Shanghai Cooperation Organisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai_Cooperation_Organisation "Shanghai Cooperation Organisation").

## Etymology

Following the amalgamation of the [Kingdom of Hejaz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hejaz "Kingdom of Hejaz") and [Nejd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate_of_Nejd "Sultanate of Nejd"), [Abdulaziz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibn_Saud "Ibn Saud") issued a royal decree on 23 September 1932 naming the new state *al-Mamlaka al-ʿArabiyya as-Suʿūdiyya* ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic "Arabic") المملكة العربية السعودية), which is normally translated as "the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia" in English,[^50] but literally means "the **Saudi Arab Kingdom** ",[^51] or "the **Saudi Kingdom of Arabia** " (compare [the Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Hashemite_Kingdom_of_Jordan "The Hashemite Kingdom of Jordan")).

The word "Saudi" is derived from the element *as-Suʿūdīyya* in the Arabic name of the country, which is a type of adjective known as a *[nisba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nisba_\(onomastics\) "Nisba (onomastics)"),* formed from the dynastic name of the Saudi royal family, the [Al Saud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Saud "House of Saud") ([Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_language "Arabic language"): آل سعود). Its inclusion expresses the view that the country is the personal possession of the royal family.[^52] [^53] *Al Saud* is an [Arabic name](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic_name "Arabic name") formed by adding the word *Al*, meaning "family of" or "House of",[^54] to the personal name of an ancestor. In the case of Al Saud, this is [Saud ibn Muhammad ibn Muqrin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saud_ibn_Muhammad_ibn_Muqrin "Saud ibn Muhammad ibn Muqrin"), the father of the dynasty's 18th-century founder, [Muhammad bin Saud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_bin_Saud "Muhammad bin Saud").[^55]

## History

### Prehistory

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/48/Pergamon-Museum_-_Anthropomorphe_Stele_2.jpg/250px-Pergamon-Museum_-_Anthropomorphe_Stele_2.jpg)

Anthropomorphic stela (4th millennium BC), sandstone, 57x27 cm, from El-Maakir-Qaryat al-Kaafa ( National Museum of Saudi Arabia, Riyadh )

There is evidence that human habitation in the [Arabian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula "Arabian Peninsula") dates back to about 125,000 years ago.[^56] A 2011 study found that the first modern humans to spread east across Asia left [Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "Africa") about 75,000 years ago across the [Bab-el-Mandeb](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bab-el-Mandeb "Bab-el-Mandeb") connecting the [Horn of Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horn_of_Africa "Horn of Africa") and Arabia.[^57] The Arabian Peninsula is regarded as central to the understanding of evolution and dispersals of humanity. Arabia underwent an extreme environmental fluctuation in the [Quaternary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quaternary "Quaternary") that led to profound evolutionary and demographic changes. Arabia's rich [Lower Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lower_Paleolithic "Lower Paleolithic") record, marked by numerous [Oldowan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oldowan "Oldowan") -like sites, indicates a significant role in the early hominin colonization of [Eurasia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasia "Eurasia").[^58]

In the [Neolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic "Neolithic") period, prominent cultures such as [Al-Magar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Magar "Al-Magar"), whose centre lay in modern-day southwestern Najd, flourished. Al-Magar could be considered a "Neolithic Revolution" in human knowledge and handicraft skills.[^59] The culture is characterized as being one of the world's first to involve the widespread domestication of animals, particularly the horse.[^60] [^61] Al-Magar statues were made from local stone, and it seems that the statues were fixed in a central building that might have had a significant role in the social and religious life of the inhabitants.[^62]

In 2017, hunting scenes showing images of most likely domesticated dogs (resembling the [Canaan Dog](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canaan_Dog "Canaan Dog")) were discovered in northwestern Saudi Arabia. These rock engravings date back more than 8,000 years, making them the earliest depictions of dogs in the world.[^63]

At the end of the 4th millennium BC, Arabia entered the [Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age "Bronze Age"); metals were widely used, and the period was characterized by its 2 m high burials which were simultaneously followed by the existence of numerous temples that included many free-standing sculptures originally painted with red colours.[^64]

In May 2021, archaeologists announced that a 350,000-year-old [Acheulean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acheulean "Acheulean") site named An Nasim in the [Hail region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E1%B8%A4a%CA%BCil_Province "Ḥaʼil Province") could be the oldest human habitation site in northern Saudi Arabia. [Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleolithic "Paleolithic") artefacts are similar to material remains uncovered at the Acheulean sites in the [Nefud Desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/An_Nafud "An Nafud").[^65] [^66] [^67] [^68]

### Pre-Islamic

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6f/Statue_at_National_Museum_of_Korea.jpg/250px-Statue_at_National_Museum_of_Korea.jpg)

The "Worshipping Servant" statue (2500 BC), above one metre (3 ft 3 in) in height, is much taller than any possible Mesopotamian or Harappan models. Photo courtesy of the National Museum of Korea. 69

The earliest sedentary culture in Saudi Arabia dates back to the [Ubaid period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ubaid_period "Ubaid period") at [Dosariyah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dosariyah "Dosariyah"). Climatic change and the onset of aridity may have brought about the end of this phase of settlement, as little archaeological evidence exists from the succeeding millennium.[^70] The settlement of the region picks up again in the period of [Dilmun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dilmun "Dilmun") in the early 3rd millennium. Known records from [Uruk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uruk "Uruk") refer to a place called Dilmun, associated on several occasions with copper, and in later periods it was a source of imported woods in southern Mesopotamia. Scholars have suggested that Dilmun originally designated the [Eastern Province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Province,_Saudi_Arabia "Eastern Province, Saudi Arabia"), notably linked with the major Dilmunite settlements of Umm an-Nussi and Umm ar-Ramadh in the interior and Tarout on the coast. It is likely that [Tarout Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarout_Island "Tarout Island") was the main port and the capital of Dilmun.[^69] Mesopotamian inscribed clay tablets suggest that, in the early period of Dilmun, a hierarchical organized political structure existed. In 1966, an earthwork in Tarout exposed an ancient burial field that yielded a large statue dating to the Dilmunite period (mid 3rd millennium BC). The statue was locally made under the strong Mesopotamian influence on the artistic principle of Dilmun.[^69]

By 2200 BC, the centre of Dilmun shifted for unknown reasons from Tarout and the Saudi Arabian mainland to the island of Bahrain, and a highly developed settlement emerged there, where a laborious temple complex and thousands of burial mounds dating to this period were discovered.[^69]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Qasr_al_Farid.JPG/250px-Qasr_al_Farid.JPG)

Qaṣr Al-Farīd, the largest of the 131 rock-cut monumental tombs built from the 1st century BC to the 1st century AD, with their elaborately ornamented façades, at the extensive ancient Nabatean archaeological site of Hegra located in the area of Al-'Ula within Al Madinah Region in the Hejaz. A UNESCO World Heritage Site since 2008.

By the late [Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age "Bronze Age"), a historically recorded people and land ([Midian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midian "Midian") and the Midianites) in the north-western portion of Saudi Arabia are well-documented in the Bible. Centred in [Tabouk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tabuk,_Saudi_Arabia "Tabuk, Saudi Arabia"), it stretched from [Wadi Arabah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wadi_Arabah "Wadi Arabah") in the north to the area of [al-Wajh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Wajh "Al-Wajh") in the south.[^71] The capital of Midian was Qurayyah,[^72] it consists of a large, fortified citadel encompassing 35 hectares and below it lies a walled settlement of 15 hectares. The city hosted as many as 12,000 inhabitants.[^73] The Bible recounts [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Israel_\(united_monarchy\) "Kingdom of Israel (united monarchy)") 's [two wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midian_war "Midian war") with Midian, in the early 11th century BC. Politically, the Midianites were described as having a decentralized structure headed by five kings (Evi, Rekem, Tsur, Hur, and Reba); the names appear to be toponyms of important Midianite settlements.[^74] It is common to view that Midian designated a confederation of tribes, the sedentary element settled in the Hijaz while its nomadic affiliates pastured and sometimes pillaged as far away as [Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine_\(region\) "Palestine (region)").[^75] The nomadic [Midianites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midianites "Midianites") were one of the earliest exploiters of the domestication of camels that enabled them to navigate through the harsh terrains of the region.[^75]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/07/Statue_of_a_man_at_National_Museum_of_Korea_01.jpg/250px-Statue_of_a_man_at_National_Museum_of_Korea_01.jpg)

Colossal statue from Al-'Ula in the Hejaz (6th–4th century BC), it followed the standardized artistic sculpting of the Lihyanite kingdom. The original statue was painted with white. ( Louvre Museum, Paris ) 76

At the end of the 7th century BC, an emerging kingdom appeared in north-western Arabia. It started as a sheikdom of Dedan, which developed into the kingdom of [Lihyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lihyan "Lihyan").[^77] [^78] During this period, Dedan transformed into a kingdom that encompassed a much wider domain.[^77] In the early 3rd century BC, with bustling economic activity between the south and north, Lihyan acquired large influence suitable to its strategic position on the caravan road.[^79] The Lihyanites ruled over a large domain from [Yathrib](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medina "Medina") in the south and parts of the Levant in the north.[^80] In antiquity, Gulf of Aqaba used to be called Gulf of Lihyan, a testimony to the extensive influence that Lihyan acquired.[^81]

The Lihyanites fell into the hands of the [Nabataeans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nabataeans "Nabataeans") around 65 BC upon their seizure of Hegra then marching to [Tayma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tayma "Tayma"), and to their capital Dedan in 9 BC. The Nabataeans ruled large portions of north Arabia until their domain was annexed by the [Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire"), which renamed it [Arabia Petraea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabia_Petraea "Arabia Petraea"), and remained under the rule of the Romans until 630.[^82]

### Middle Ages and rise of Islam

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/Umayyad750ADloc.png/250px-Umayyad750ADloc.png)

At its greatest extent, the Umayyad Caliphate (661–750) covered 11 100 000 km 2 ( 4 300 sq mi) 83 and 62 million people (29 per cent of the world's population), 84 making it one of the largest empires in history in both area and proportion of the world's population. It was also larger than any previous empire in history.

Shortly before the advent of Islam, apart from urban trading settlements (such as Mecca and Medina), much of what was to become Saudi Arabia was populated by nomadic pastoral tribal societies.[^85] Muhammad was born in Mecca in about 570 [CE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Common_Era "Common Era"). In the early 7th century, [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad "Muhammad") united the [various tribes of the peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tribes_of_Arabia "Tribes of Arabia") and created a single Islamic religious polity.[^86] Following his death in 632, his followers expanded the territory under Muslim rule beyond Arabia, conquering territory in the Iberian Peninsula in the west, to parts of Central and South Asia in the east, in a matter of decades.[^27] [^28] [^29] Arabia became a more politically peripheral region of the Muslim world as the focus shifted to the newly conquered lands.[^86]

[Arabs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab "Arab") originating from modern-day Saudi Arabia, the [Hejaz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hejaz "Hejaz") in particular, founded the [Rashidun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashidun_Caliphate "Rashidun Caliphate") (632–661), [Umayyad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate "Umayyad Caliphate") (661–750), [Abbasid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbasid_Caliphate "Abbasid Caliphate") (750–1517), and the [Fatimid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fatimid_Caliphate "Fatimid Caliphate") (909–1171) caliphates. From the 10th century to the early 20th century, Mecca and Medina were under the control of a local Arab ruler known as the [Sharif of Mecca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharif_of_Mecca "Sharif of Mecca"), but at most times the Sharif owed allegiance to the ruler of one of the major Islamic empires based in [Baghdad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baghdad "Baghdad"), [Cairo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cairo "Cairo") or [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul"). Most of the remainder of what became Saudi Arabia reverted to traditional tribal rule.[^87] [^88]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4d/Siyer-i_Nebi_-_Imam_Ali_und_Hamza_bei_dem_vorgezogenen_Einzelkampf_in_Badr_gegen_die_G%C3%B6tzendiener.jpg/250px-Siyer-i_Nebi_-_Imam_Ali_und_Hamza_bei_dem_vorgezogenen_Einzelkampf_in_Badr_gegen_die_G%C3%B6tzendiener.jpg)

The Battle of Badr, 13 March 624 CE

For much of the 10th century, the [Isma'ili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isma%27ilism "Isma'ilism") -Shi'ite [Qarmatians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qarmatians "Qarmatians") were the most powerful force in the Persian Gulf. In 930, the Qarmatians pillaged Mecca, outraging the Muslim world, particularly with their theft of the [Black Stone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Stone "Black Stone").[^89] In 1077–1078, an Arab sheikh named [Abdullah bin Ali Al Uyuni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdullah_bin_Ali_Al_Uyuni "Abdullah bin Ali Al Uyuni") defeated the Qarmatians in [Bahrain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Arabia "Eastern Arabia") and [al-Hasa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Ahsa_Oasis "Al-Ahsa Oasis") with the help of the [Seljuq Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seljuk_Empire "Seljuk Empire") and founded the [Uyunid dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uyunid_dynasty "Uyunid dynasty").[^90] [^91] The [Uyunid Emirate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uyunid_Emirate "Uyunid Emirate") later underwent expansion, with its territory stretching from Najd to the [Syrian Desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_Desert "Syrian Desert").[^92] They were overthrown by the [Usfurids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Usfurids "Usfurids") in 1253.[^93] Usfurid rule was weakened after Persian rulers of [Hormuz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ormus "Ormus") captured Bahrain and [Qatif](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qatif "Qatif") in 1320.[^94] The vassals of Ormuz, the Shia [Jarwanid dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jarwanid_dynasty "Jarwanid dynasty") came to rule eastern Arabia in the 14th century.[^95] [^96] The [Jabrids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jabrids "Jabrids") took control of the region after overthrowing the Jarwanids in the 15th century and clashed with Hormuz for more than two decades over the region for its economic revenues, until finally agreeing to pay [tribute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tribute "Tribute") in 1507.[^95] [Al-Muntafiq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Muntafiq "Al-Muntafiq") tribe later took over the region and came under [Ottoman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") [suzerainty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suzerainty "Suzerainty"). The [Bani Khalid tribe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bani_Khalid_\(tribe\) "Bani Khalid (tribe)") later revolted against them in the 17th century and took control.[^97] Their rule extended from Iraq to Oman at its height, and they too came under Ottoman suzerainty.[^98] [^99]

### Ottoman Hejaz and Portuguese era

In the 16th century, the Ottomans added the Red Sea and Persian Gulf coast (the Hejaz, [Aseer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aseer "Aseer") and [Al-Ahsa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Arabia "Eastern Arabia")) to the empire and claimed suzerainty over the interior. One reason was to [thwart Portuguese attempts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Portuguese_conflicts_\(1538%E2%80%931560\) "Ottoman–Portuguese conflicts (1538–1560)") to attack the Red Sea (hence the Hejaz) and the Indian Ocean.[^100] The Ottoman degree of control over these lands varied over the next four centuries with the fluctuating strength or weakness of the empire's central authority.[^101] [^102] These changes contributed to later uncertainties, such as the dispute with Transjordan over the [inclusion of the sanjak of Ma'an](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupation_of_Ma%27an "Occupation of Ma'an"), including the cities of [Ma'an](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ma%27an "Ma'an") and [Aqaba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aqaba "Aqaba").

### Saud dynasty and unification

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/da/The_First_Saudi_State_Greatest_Extent.png/250px-The_First_Saudi_State_Greatest_Extent.png)

Expansion of the first Saudi State in 1810

The emergence of what was to become the Saudi royal family, known as the Al Saud, began at the town of [Diriyah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diriyah "Diriyah") in central Arabia with the accession as [emir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emir "Emir") of [Muhammad bin Saud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_bin_Saud "Muhammad bin Saud") on 22 February 1727.[^103] [^104] In 1744 he joined forces with the religious leader [Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_ibn_Abd_al-Wahhab "Muhammad ibn Abd al-Wahhab"),[^105] founder of the Wahhabi movement, a strict puritanical form of Sunni Islam.[^106] This alliance provided the ideological impetus to Saudi expansion and remains the basis of Saudi Arabian dynastic rule today.[^107]

The [Emirate of Diriyah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Diriyah "Emirate of Diriyah") established in the area around Riyadh rapidly expanded and briefly controlled most of the present-day territory of Saudi Arabia, [sacking Karbala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wahhabi_sack_of_Karbala "Wahhabi sack of Karbala") in 1802, and [capturing Mecca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Destruction_of_early_Islamic_heritage_sites_in_Saudi_Arabia "Destruction of early Islamic heritage sites in Saudi Arabia") in 1803. In 1818, it was destroyed by the Ottoman viceroy of Egypt, [Mohammed Ali Pasha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Ali_of_Egypt "Muhammad Ali of Egypt").[^108] The much smaller [Emirate of Nejd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Nejd "Emirate of Nejd") was established in 1824. Throughout the rest of the 19th century, the Al Saud contested control of the interior of what was to become Saudi Arabia with another Arabian ruling family, the [Al Rashid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashidi_dynasty "Rashidi dynasty"), who ruled the [Emirate of Jabal Shammar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Jabal_Shammar "Emirate of Jabal Shammar"). By 1891, the Al Rashid were victorious and the Al Saud were driven into exile in Kuwait.[^87]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4f/Churchill_and_King_Abd_al-Aziz_of_Saudi_Arabia.jpg/250px-Churchill_and_King_Abd_al-Aziz_of_Saudi_Arabia.jpg)

British Prime Minister Winston Churchill and Ibn Saud, the founding father and first king of Saudi Arabia, in Egypt in 1945

At the beginning of the 20th century, the Ottoman Empire continued to control or have a suzerainty over most of the peninsula. Subject to this suzerainty, Arabia was ruled by a patchwork of tribal rulers,[^109] [^110] with the [Sharif of Mecca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharif_of_Mecca "Sharif of Mecca") having pre-eminence and ruling the Hejaz.[^111] In 1902, [Abdul Rahman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdul_Rahman_bin_Faisal "Abdul Rahman bin Faisal") 's son, Abdulaziz—later known as [Ibn Saud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibn_Saud "Ibn Saud") —recaptured control of Riyadh bringing the Al Saud back to Nejd, creating the [third "Saudi state"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Nejd_and_Hasa "Emirate of Nejd and Hasa").[^87] Ibn Saud gained the support of the [Ikhwan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ikhwan "Ikhwan"), a tribal army inspired by Wahhabism and led by [Faisal Al-Dawish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faisal_Al-Dawish "Faisal Al-Dawish"), and which had grown quickly after its foundation in 1912.[^112] With the aid of the Ikhwan, Ibn Saud captured Al-Ahsa from the Ottomans in 1913.

In 1916, with the encouragement and support of [Britain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom_of_Great_Britain_and_Ireland "United Kingdom of Great Britain and Ireland") (which was fighting the Ottomans in [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I")), the Sharif of Mecca, [Hussein bin Ali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hussein_bin_Ali,_Sharif_of_Mecca "Hussein bin Ali, Sharif of Mecca"), led a pan- [Arab Revolt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Revolt "Arab Revolt") against the Ottoman Empire to create a united Arab state.[^113] Although the revolt failed in its objective, [the Allied](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I "Allies of World War I") victory in World War I resulted in the end of Ottoman suzerainty and control in Arabia, and Hussein bin Ali became [King of Hejaz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Hejaz "Kingdom of Hejaz").[^114]

Ibn Saud avoided involvement in the Arab Revolt and instead continued his struggle with the Al Rashid. Following the latter's final defeat, he took the title [Sultan of Nejd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate_of_Nejd "Sultanate of Nejd") in 1921. With the help of the Ikhwan, the Kingdom of Hejaz was conquered in 1924–25, and on 10 January 1926, Ibn Saud declared himself king of Hejaz.[^115] For the next five years, he administered the two parts of his dual kingdom as separate units.[^87]

After the conquest of the Hejaz, the Ikhwan leadership's objective switched to expansion of the Wahhabist realm into the British protectorates of Transjordan, Iraq and Kuwait, and began raiding those territories. This met with Ibn Saud's opposition, as he recognized the danger of a direct conflict with the British. At the same time, the Ikhwan became disenchanted with Ibn Saud's domestic policies which appeared to favour modernization and the increase in the number of non-Muslim foreigners in the country. As a result, they turned against Ibn Saud and, after a two-year struggle, were defeated in 1929 at the [Battle of Sabilla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Sabilla "Battle of Sabilla"), where their leaders were massacred.[^116] On Ibn Saud's behalf, [Prince Faisal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faisal_bin_Abdulaziz_al_Saud "Faisal bin Abdulaziz al Saud") declared the unification on 23 September 1932, and the two kingdoms of Hejaz and Nejd were unified as the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia.[^87] That date is now a national holiday called [Saudi National Day](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_National_Day "Saudi National Day").[^117]

### 20th century

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c4/Dammam_No._7_on_March_4%2C_1938.jpg/250px-Dammam_No._7_on_March_4%2C_1938.jpg)

The oil well Dammam No. 7 on March 4, 1938, the day it struck oil in commercial quantities, becoming the first in Saudi Arabia to do so

The new kingdom was reliant on limited agriculture and pilgrimage revenues.[^118] [In 1938, vast reserves of oil were discovered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dammam_No._7 "Dammam No. 7") in the Al-Ahsa region along the coast of the Persian Gulf, and full-scale development of the oil fields began in 1941 under the US-controlled [Aramco (Arabian American Oil Company)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Aramco "Saudi Aramco"). Oil provided Saudi Arabia with economic prosperity and substantial political leverage internationally.[^87] Cultural life rapidly developed, primarily in the Hejaz, which was the centre for newspapers and radio. However, the large influx of [foreign workers in Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_workers_in_Saudi_Arabia "Foreign workers in Saudi Arabia") in the oil industry increased the pre-existing propensity for [xenophobia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xenophobia "Xenophobia"). At the same time, the government became increasingly wasteful and extravagant. By the 1950s this had led to large governmental deficits and excessive foreign borrowing.[^87] In 1953, [Saud of Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saud_of_Saudi_Arabia "Saud of Saudi Arabia") succeeded as the king of Saudi Arabia. In 1964 he was deposed in favour of his half brother [Faisal of Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faisal_of_Saudi_Arabia "Faisal of Saudi Arabia"), after an intense rivalry, fuelled by doubts in the royal family over Saud's competence. In 1972, Saudi Arabia gained a 20% control in Aramco, thereby decreasing US control over Saudi oil.[^119] In 1973, Saudi Arabia led an [oil boycott](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1973_oil_crisis "1973 oil crisis") against the Western countries that supported Israel in the [Yom Kippur War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yom_Kippur_War "Yom Kippur War") against Egypt and Syria, leading to the quadrupling of oil prices.[^87] In 1975, Faisal was assassinated by his nephew, Prince [Faisal bin Musaid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faisal_bin_Musaid "Faisal bin Musaid") and was succeeded by his half-brother [King Khalid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khalid_of_Saudi_Arabia "Khalid of Saudi Arabia").[^120]

![Nasser and Faisal](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Nasser_and_Faisal.jpg/250px-Nasser_and_Faisal.jpg)

Faisal (left) with Egypt's President Gamal Abdel Nasser in Cairo, 1969

By 1976, Saudi Arabia had become the largest oil producer in the world.[^121] Khalid's reign saw economic and social development progress at an extremely rapid rate, transforming the infrastructure and educational system of the country;[^87] in foreign policy, close ties with the US were developed.[^120] In 1979, two events occurred which greatly concerned the government [^122] and had a long-term influence on Saudi foreign and domestic policy. The first was the [Iranian Islamic Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_Revolution "Iranian Revolution"). It was feared that the country's [Shi'ite minority](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shi%27a_Islam_in_Saudi_Arabia "Shi'a Islam in Saudi Arabia") in the Eastern Province (which is also the location of the oil fields) might rebel under the influence of their Iranian co-religionists. There were several anti-government uprisings in the region such as the [1979 Qatif Uprising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1979_Qatif_Uprising "1979 Qatif Uprising").[^123] The second event was the [Grand Mosque Seizure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Grand_Mosque_Seizure "Grand Mosque Seizure") in Mecca by Islamist extremists. The militants involved were in part angered by what they considered to be the corruption and un-Islamic nature of the Saudi government.[^123] The government regained control of the mosque after 10 days, and those captured were executed. Part of the response of the royal family was to enforce the much stricter observance of traditional religious and social norms in the country (for example, the closure of cinemas) and to give the [ulema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ulema "Ulema") a greater role in government.[^124] Neither entirely succeeded as Islamism continued to grow in strength.[^125]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Saudi_Arabia_2003_CIA_map.jpg/250px-Saudi_Arabia_2003_CIA_map.jpg)

Map of Saudi Arabian administrative regions and roadways

In 1980, Saudi Arabia bought out the American interests in Aramco.[^126] King Khalid died in 1982 and was succeeded by his brother, [King Fahd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fahd_of_Saudi_Arabia "Fahd of Saudi Arabia"). Fahd continued to develop close relations with the United States and increased the purchase of American and British military equipment.[^87] The vast wealth generated by oil revenues was beginning to have an even greater impact on Saudi society. It led to rapid technological (but not cultural) modernization, urbanization, mass public education, and the creation of new media. This and the presence of increasingly large numbers of foreign workers greatly affected traditional Saudi norms and values. Although there was a dramatic change in the social and economic life of the country, political power continued to be monopolized by the royal family [^87] leading to discontent among many Saudis who began to look for wider participation in government.[^127]

In the 1980s, Saudi Arabia and [Kuwait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuwait "Kuwait") spent $25 billion in support of [Saddam Hussein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saddam_Hussein "Saddam Hussein") in the [Iran–Iraq War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran%E2%80%93Iraq_War "Iran–Iraq War") (1980–1988);[^128] however, Saudi Arabia condemned the [invasion of Kuwait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invasion_of_Kuwait "Invasion of Kuwait") in 1990 and asked the United States to intervene.[^87] King Fahd allowed American and coalition troops to be stationed in Saudi Arabia. He invited the Kuwaiti government and many of its citizens to stay in Saudi Arabia, but expelled citizens of [Yemen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yemen "Yemen") and [Jordan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan "Jordan") because of their governments' support of [Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ba%27athist_Iraq "Ba'athist Iraq"). In 1991, Saudi Arabian forces were involved both in bombing raids on Iraq and in the land invasion that helped to liberate Kuwait, which became known as the [Gulf War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_War "Gulf War") (1990–1991).[^119]

Saudi Arabia's relations with the West was one of the issues that led to [an increase in Islamist terrorism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_militant_incidents_in_Saudi_Arabia "List of militant incidents in Saudi Arabia") in Saudi Arabia, as well as Islamist terrorist attacks in Western countries by Saudi nationals. [Osama bin Laden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden "Osama bin Laden") was a Saudi citizen (until stripped of his citizenship in 1994) and was responsible for the [1998 U.S. embassy bombings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1998_United_States_embassy_bombings "1998 United States embassy bombings") in East Africa and the 2000 [USS *Cole* bombing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Cole_bombing "USS Cole bombing") near the port of [Aden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aden "Aden"), Yemen. 15 of [the hijackers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hijackers_in_the_September_11_attacks "Hijackers in the September 11 attacks") involved in the [September 11 attacks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/September_11_attacks "September 11 attacks") were Saudi nationals.[^129] Many Saudis who did not support the Islamist terrorists were nevertheless deeply unhappy with the government's policies.[^130]

Islamism was not the only source of hostility to the government. Although extremely wealthy by the 21st century, Saudi Arabia's economy was near stagnant. High taxes and a growth in unemployment have contributed to discontent and have been reflected in a rise in civil unrest, and discontent with the royal family. In response, a number of limited reforms were initiated by King Fahd. In March 1992, he introduced the " [Basic Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_Law_of_Saudi_Arabia "Basic Law of Saudi Arabia") ", which emphasized the duties and responsibilities of a ruler. In December 1993, the Consultative Council was inaugurated. It is composed of a chairman and 60 members—all chosen by the King. Fahd made it clear that he did not have democracy in mind, saying: "A system based on elections is not consistent with our Islamic creed, which \[approves of\] government by consultation \[shūrā\]." [^87]

In 1995, Fahd suffered a stroke, and the Crown Prince, [Abdullah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdullah_of_Saudi_Arabia "Abdullah of Saudi Arabia"), became *de facto* regent; however, his authority was hindered by conflict with Fahd's full brothers (known, with Fahd, as the " [Sudairi Seven](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sudairi_Seven "Sudairi Seven") ").[^131]

### 21st century

Signs of discontent included, in 2003 and 2004, a series of bombings and armed violence in Riyadh, Jeddah, Yanbu and Khobar.[^132] In February–April 2005, [the first-ever nationwide municipal elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2005_Saudi_Arabian_municipal_elections "2005 Saudi Arabian municipal elections") were held in Saudi Arabia. Women were not allowed to take part.[^87]

In 2005, King Fahd died and was succeeded by Abdullah, who continued the policy of minimum reform and clamping down on protests. The king introduced economic reforms aimed at reducing the country's reliance on oil revenue: limited [deregulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deregulation "Deregulation"), encouragement of foreign investment, and [privatization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Privatization "Privatization"). In February 2009, Abdullah announced a series of governmental changes to the judiciary, armed forces, and various ministries to modernize these institutions including the replacement of senior appointees in the judiciary and the [Mutaween](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_religious_police "Islamic religious police") (religious police) with more moderate individuals and the appointment of the country's first female deputy minister.[^87] On 29 January 2011, hundreds of protesters gathered in [Jeddah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeddah "Jeddah") in a rare display of criticism against the city's poor infrastructure after flooding killed 11 people.[^133]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/President_Trump%27s_Trip_Abroad_globe_cropped.jpg/250px-President_Trump%27s_Trip_Abroad_globe_cropped.jpg)

Egyptian President Abdel Fattah el-Sisi with King Salman, U.S. President Donald Trump and First Lady Melania participating in the inaugural opening of the Global Center to Combat Extremism, Sunday, May 21, 2017

Since 2011, Saudi Arabia has been affected by its own [Arab Spring protests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011%E2%80%9313_Saudi_Arabian_protests "2011–13 Saudi Arabian protests").[^134] In response, King Abdullah announced on 22 February 2011 a series of benefits for citizens amounting to $36 billion, of which $10.7 billion was earmarked for housing.[^135] [^136] [^137] No political reforms were included, though some prisoners indicted for financial crimes were pardoned.[^138] Abdullah also announced a package of $93 billion, which included 500,000 new homes to a cost of $67 billion, in addition to creating 60000 new security jobs.[^139] [^140] Although male-only [municipal elections were held in 2011](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabian_municipal_elections,_2011 "Saudi Arabian municipal elections, 2011"),[^141] [^142] Abdullah allowed women to vote and be elected in the [2015 municipal elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabian_municipal_elections,_2015 "Saudi Arabian municipal elections, 2015"), and also to be nominated to the [Shura Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Consultative_Assembly_of_Saudi_Arabia "Consultative Assembly of Saudi Arabia").[^143]

## Geography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Saudi_Arabia_Topography.png/330px-Saudi_Arabia_Topography.png)

Saudi Arabia topography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7f/Harrat_Khaybar_Space.jpg/250px-Harrat_Khaybar_Space.jpg)

Harrat Khaybar seen from the International Space Station. Saudi Arabia is home to more than 2000 dormant volcanoes. 144 Lava fields in Hejaz, known locally by their Arabic name of harrat (the singular is harrah), form one of Earth's largest alkali basalt regions, covering some 180,000 square kilometres (69,000 sq mi). 145

Saudi Arabia occupies about 80% of the [Arabian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Peninsula "Arabian Peninsula") (the world's largest peninsula),[^146] lying between latitudes [16°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/16th_parallel_north "16th parallel north") and [33° N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/33rd_parallel_north "33rd parallel north"), and longitudes [34°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/34th_meridian_east "34th meridian east") and [56° E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/56th_meridian_east "56th meridian east"). Because the country's southeastern and southern borders with the [United Arab Emirates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates "United Arab Emirates") and [Oman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oman "Oman") are not precisely marked, the exact size of the country is undefined.[^146] The [United Nations Statistics Division](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Statistics_Division "United Nations Statistics Division") estimates 2149690 km <sup>2</sup> (830000 sq mi) and lists Saudi Arabia as the world's [12th](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_area "List of countries and dependencies by area") largest state. It is geographically the largest country in the Middle East and on the [Arabian Plate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Plate "Arabian Plate").[^147]

Saudi Arabia's geography is dominated by the [Arabian Desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Desert "Arabian Desert"), associated semi-desert, shrubland, steppes, several mountain ranges, volcanic lava fields and highlands. The 647500 km <sup>2</sup> (250001 sq mi) [Rub' al Khali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rub%27_al_Khali "Rub' al Khali") ("Empty Quarter") in the southeastern part of the country is the world's largest contiguous sand desert.[^148] [^149] Though there are lakes in the country, Saudi Arabia is the largest country in the world by area with no permanent rivers. [Wadis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wadis "Wadis"), non-permanent rivers, however, [are very numerous throughout the kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_wadis_of_Saudi_Arabia "List of wadis of Saudi Arabia"). The fertile areas are to be found in the alluvial deposits in wadis, basins, and oases.[^148] There are [approximately 1,300 islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_islands_of_Saudi_Arabia "List of islands of Saudi Arabia") in the Red Sea and Persian Gulf.[^150]

The main topographical feature is the central plateau which rises abruptly from the Red Sea and gradually descends into the [Nejd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nejd "Nejd") and toward the Persian Gulf. On the Red Sea coast, there is a narrow coastal plain, known as the [Tihamah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tihamah "Tihamah"), parallel to which runs along an imposing escarpment. The southwest province of [Aseer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aseer "Aseer") is mountainous and contains the 3015 m (9892 ft) [Jabal Soudah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jabal_Soudah "Jabal Soudah"), which is the highest point in the country.[^148] Saudi Arabia is home to more than 2,000 dormant volcanoes.[^144] Lava fields in Hejaz, known locally by their Arabic name of harrat (the singular is harrah), form one of Earth's largest [alkali basalt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alkali_basalt "Alkali basalt") regions, covering some 180,000 square kilometres (69,000 sq mi).[^145]

Except for the southwestern regions such as Aseer, Saudi Arabia has a [desert climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desert_climate "Desert climate") with very high day-time temperatures during the summer and a sharp temperature drop at night. Average summer temperatures are around 45 °C (113 °F) but can be as high as 54 °C (129 °F). In the winter the temperature rarely drops below 0 °C (32 °F) with the exception of mostly the northern regions of the country where annual snowfall, in particular in the mountainous regions of [Tabuk Province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tabuk_Province "Tabuk Province"), is not uncommon.[^151] The lowest recorded temperature, −12.0 °C (10.4 °F), was measured in [Turaif](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turaif "Turaif").[^152] Of the Gulf states, Saudi Arabia is likely to experience snowfalls most frequently.[^153]

In the spring and autumn the heat is temperate, temperatures average around 29 °C (84 °F). Annual rainfall is very low. The southern regions differ in that they are influenced by the Indian Ocean [monsoons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsoons "Monsoons"), usually occurring between October and March. An average of 300 mm (12 in) of rainfall occurs during this period, which is about 60% of the annual precipitation.[^154]
