**Spain**,[^7] officially the **Kingdom of Spain**,[^8] is a country in [Southern](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Europe "Southern Europe") and [Western Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Europe "Western Europe") with territories in [North Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Africa "North Africa").[^9] Featuring the southernmost point of [continental Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_Europe "Continental Europe"), it is the largest country in Southern Europe and the fourth-most populous [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union") (EU) member state. Spanning the majority of the [Iberian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula "Iberian Peninsula"), its territory also includes the [Canary Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canary_Islands "Canary Islands"), in the Eastern [Atlantic Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean "Atlantic Ocean"); the [Balearic Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balearic_Islands "Balearic Islands"), in the Western [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea"); and the autonomous cities of [Ceuta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceuta "Ceuta") and [Melilla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melilla "Melilla"), in mainland Africa. Peninsular Spain is bordered to the north by [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "France"), [Andorra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andorra "Andorra"), and the [Bay of Biscay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Biscay "Bay of Biscay"); to the east and south by the Mediterranean Sea and [Gibraltar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gibraltar "Gibraltar") and [Morocco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco "Morocco"), through its exclaves in North Africa; and to the west by [Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal "Portugal") and the Atlantic Ocean. Spain's capital and [largest city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_cities_in_Spain "List of largest cities in Spain") is [Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madrid "Madrid"); other major [urban areas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_metropolitan_areas_in_Spain "List of metropolitan areas in Spain") include [Barcelona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barcelona "Barcelona"), [Valencia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valencia "Valencia"), [Seville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seville "Seville"), [Zaragoza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zaragoza "Zaragoza"), [Málaga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%A1laga "Málaga"), [Murcia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murcia "Murcia"), and [Palma de Mallorca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palma_de_Mallorca "Palma de Mallorca").

In early antiquity, the Iberian Peninsula was inhabited by [Iberians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberians "Iberians"), [Celts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celts "Celts"), and other [pre-Roman peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_the_Pre-Roman_peoples_of_the_Iberian_Peninsula "List of the Pre-Roman peoples of the Iberian Peninsula"). The [Roman conquest of the Iberian peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_conquest_of_the_Iberian_peninsula "Roman conquest of the Iberian peninsula") created the province of [Hispania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hispania "Hispania"), which became deeply [Romanised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_\(cultural\) "Romanization (cultural)") and later [Christianised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianization "Christianization"). After the [fall of the Western Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_the_Western_Roman_Empire "Fall of the Western Roman Empire"), the peninsula was conquered by tribes from Central Europe, among them the [Visigoths](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visigoths "Visigoths"), who established the [Visigothic Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visigothic_Kingdom "Visigothic Kingdom") centred on [Toledo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toledo,_Spain "Toledo, Spain"). In the early 8th century, most of the peninsula was [conquered by the Umayyad Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_conquest_of_Hispania "Umayyad conquest of Hispania"), with [Al-Andalus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Andalus "Al-Andalus") centred on [Córdoba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%B3rdoba,_Spain "Córdoba, Spain"). The northern Christian kingdoms of Iberia launched the so-called *[Reconquista](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reconquista "Reconquista")*, gradually repelling and ultimately expelling Islamic rule from the peninsula, culminating with the fall of the [Nasrid Kingdom of Granada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Granada "Emirate of Granada"). The dynastic union of the [Crown of Castile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_of_Castile "Crown of Castile") and the [Crown of Aragon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crown_of_Aragon "Crown of Aragon") in 1479 under the [Catholic Monarchs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs "Catholic Monarchs") is often seen as the *de facto* unification of Spain as a [nation state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation_state "Nation state").

During the [Age of Discovery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Discovery "Age of Discovery"), Spain led the [exploration and conquest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_colonization_of_the_Americas "European colonization of the Americas") of the [New World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_World "New World"), completed the [first circumnavigation of the globe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_circumnavigation_of_the_globe "First circumnavigation of the globe"), and established [one of the largest empires in history](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Empire "Spanish Empire"), which fostered a global trade system driven by [precious metals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precious_metal "Precious metal"). In the 18th century, the [Nueva Planta decrees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nueva_Planta_decrees "Nueva Planta decrees") centralised Spain under the Bourbons, strengthening royal authority. The 19th century witnessed the victorious [Peninsular War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsular_War "Peninsular War") (1808–1814) against Napoleonic forces and the [loss of most American colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_American_wars_of_independence "Spanish American wars of independence") amid liberal–absolutist conflicts. These struggles culminated in the [Spanish Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War "Spanish Civil War") (1936–1939) and the [Francoist dictatorship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francoist_Spain "Francoist Spain") (1939–1975). With the restoration of democracy and entry into the EU, Spain experienced [a major economic boom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_miracle "Spanish miracle") and social transformation.

Spain is a secular [parliamentary democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_democracy "Parliamentary democracy") and a [constitutional monarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_monarchy "Constitutional monarchy"), with King [Felipe VI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Felipe_VI "Felipe VI") as [head of state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_state "Head of state"). A [developed country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country "Developed country"), Spain has a [high nominal GDP per capita globally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\)_per_capita "List of countries by GDP (nominal) per capita"), and [its advanced economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Spain "Economy of Spain") ranks among the [largest in the world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\) "List of countries by GDP (nominal)") and is the fourth-largest in the EU. [Spanish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language "Spanish language") is the world's [second-most spoken native language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_languages_by_number_of_native_speakers "List of languages by number of native speakers").

## Etymology

The name of Spain (*España*) comes from *[Hispania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hispania "Hispania")*, the name used by the [Ancient Romans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Romans "Ancient Romans") for the [Iberian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberian_Peninsula "Iberian Peninsula") during the [Roman Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic "Roman Republic") and the [Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire"). The etymology of the term *Hispania* remains uncertain.[^32]

The [Phoenicians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicians "Phoenicians") referred to the region as *i-shphan-im*, possibly meaning 'land of [rabbits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_rabbit "European rabbit") or [hyraxes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyraxes "Hyraxes") ',[^33] 'land of metals',[^32] [^34] or 'northern island'.[^35] [Roman coins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_coins "Roman coins") struck in the region from the reign of [Hadrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadrian "Hadrian") show a female figure with a rabbit at her feet,[^36] and [Strabo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strabo "Strabo") called it the 'land of the rabbits'.[^37]

## History

### Prehistory and Iberian peoples

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ae/2014_Castro_de_Santa_Trega._Galiza-2.jpg/250px-2014_Castro_de_Santa_Trega._Galiza-2.jpg)

Celtic castro in Galicia

Archaeological research at [Atapuerca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaeological_site_of_Atapuerca "Archaeological site of Atapuerca") indicates the Iberian Peninsula was populated by [hominids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hominid "Hominid") 1.3 million years ago.[^38]

[Modern humans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human "Human") first arrived in Iberia from the north about 35,000 years ago.[^39] The best-known artefacts of these prehistoric human settlements are the paintings in the [Altamira cave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altamira_\(cave\) "Altamira (cave)") of Cantabria in northern Iberia, which were created from 35,600 to 13,500 [BCE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Before_Common_Era "Before Common Era") by [Cro-Magnon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cro-Magnon "Cro-Magnon").[^40] [^41] Archaeological and genetic evidence suggests that the Iberian Peninsula acted as one of several major refugia from which northern Europe was repopulated following the end of the [last ice age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quaternary_glaciation "Quaternary glaciation").

Before the [Roman conquest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_conquest_of_the_Iberian_Peninsula "Roman conquest of the Iberian Peninsula") the major cultures along the Mediterranean coast were the [Iberians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iberians "Iberians"), the [Celts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celtiberians "Celtiberians") in the interior and north-west, the [Lusitanians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lusitanians "Lusitanians") in the west, and the [Tartessians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tartessos "Tartessos") in the southwest. The seafaring [Phoenicians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicians "Phoenicians"), [Carthaginians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carthaginians "Carthaginians"), and [Greeks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greeks "Greeks") successively established trading settlements along the eastern and southern coast. The [development of writing in the peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleohispanic_script "Paleohispanic script") took place after the arrival of early Phoenician settlers and traders (tentatively dated 9th century BC or later).[^42] [^43]

The south of the peninsula was rich in archaic Phoenician colonies, unmatched by any other region in the central-western Mediterranean. They were small and densely packed settlements. The colony of [Gadir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gadir "Gadir") —which sustained strong links with its metropolis of [Tyre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyre,_Lebanon "Tyre, Lebanon") —stood out from the rest of the network of colonies, also featuring a more complex sociopolitical organization.[^44] [Archaic Greeks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archaic_Greece "Archaic Greece") arrived on the Peninsula by the late 7th century BC. They founded [Greek colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_colonies "Greek colonies") such as [Emporion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emp%C3%BAries "Empúries") (570 BC).[^45]

After the defeat of [Carthage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carthage "Carthage") in the [First Punic War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Punic_War "First Punic War"), the Carthaginian general [Hamilcar Barca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamilcar_Barca "Hamilcar Barca") led an expedition to Iberia, where he hoped to gain a new empire for Carthage to compensate for territorial losses. In eight years, by force of arms and diplomacy, Hamilcar secured an extensive territory, covering around half of the Iberian Peninsula. Hamilcar's premature death in battle (228 BC) prevented him from completing the conquest of the Iberian Peninsula.[^46]

### Roman Hispania and the Visigothic Kingdom

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/bb/Teatro_de_M%C3%A9rida%2C_Espa%C3%B1a%2C_2017_18.jpg/250px-Teatro_de_M%C3%A9rida%2C_Espa%C3%B1a%2C_2017_18.jpg)

The Roman Theatre in Mérida

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/89/Leon_%28San_Isidoro%2C_pante%C3%B3n%29.jpg/250px-Leon_%28San_Isidoro%2C_pante%C3%B3n%29.jpg)

Royal Pantheon of San Isidoro, Kingdom of León

During the [Second Punic War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Punic_War "Second Punic War"), roughly between 210 and 205 BCE, the expanding [Roman Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic "Roman Republic") captured Carthaginian trading colonies along the Mediterranean coast. It took the Romans nearly two centuries to complete the [conquest of the Iberian Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_conquest_of_the_Iberian_peninsula "Roman conquest of the Iberian peninsula"). They retained control of it for over six centuries. Roman rule was bound together by law, language, and the [Roman road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_road "Roman road").[^47] The cultures of the pre-Roman populations were gradually [Romanised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Romanization_of_Hispania "Romanization of Hispania") (Latinised) at different rates depending on what part of the peninsula they lived in, with local leaders being admitted into the Roman aristocratic class.[^10] [^48]

Hispania, the Roman name for the Iberian Peninsula, served as a granary for the Roman market, and its harbours exported gold, [wool](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wool "Wool"), [olive oil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olive_oil "Olive oil"), and wine. Agricultural production increased with the introduction of irrigation projects, some of which remain in use. Emperors [Hadrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hadrian "Hadrian"), [Trajan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trajan "Trajan"), [Theodosius I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodosius_I "Theodosius I"), and the philosopher [Seneca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seneca_the_Younger "Seneca the Younger") were born in Hispania.[^11] Christianity was introduced into Hispania in the 1st century CE, and it became popular in the cities in the 2nd century.[^48] Most of Spain's present languages and religions, as well as the basis of its laws, originate from this period.[^47] Starting in 170 CE, incursions of North-African [Mauri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauri "Mauri") in the province of [Baetica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hispania_Baetica "Hispania Baetica") took place.[^49]

The [Germanic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Germanic_peoples "Germanic peoples") [Suebi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suebi "Suebi") and [Vandals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vandals "Vandals"), together with the [Sarmatian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarmatian "Sarmatian") [Alans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alans "Alans"), entered the peninsula after 409, weakening the Western Roman Empire's jurisdiction over Hispania. The Suebi established a kingdom in north-western Iberia. The Vandals established themselves in the south of the peninsula by 420, before crossing over to North Africa in 429. As the western Roman empire disintegrated, the social and economic base became greatly simplified. The successor regimes maintained many of the institutions and laws of the late empire, including Christianity and assimilation into the evolving Roman culture.

The [Byzantines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine "Byzantine") established an occidental province, [Spania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spania "Spania"), in the south, with the intention of reviving Roman rule throughout Iberia. Eventually, however, Hispania was reunited under [Visigothic rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visigothic_Kingdom "Visigothic Kingdom").

### Muslim era and Reconquista

From 711 to 718, as part of the expansion of the [Umayyad Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate "Umayyad Caliphate") which had [conquered North Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_conquest_of_the_Maghreb "Muslim conquest of the Maghreb") from the [Byzantine Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire "Byzantine Empire"), nearly all of the Iberian Peninsula was conquered by Muslims from across the Strait of Gibraltar, resulting in the collapse of the Visigothic Kingdom. Only a small area in the mountainous north of the peninsula stood out of the territory seized during the initial invasion. The [Kingdom of Asturias-León](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Asturias "Kingdom of Asturias") consolidated upon this territory. Other Christian kingdoms, such as [Navarre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Navarre "Kingdom of Navarre") and [Aragon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon "Kingdom of Aragon") in the mountainous north, eventually surged upon the consolidation of counties of the Carolingian *[Marca Hispanica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marca_Hispanica "Marca Hispanica")*.[^50] For several centuries, the fluctuating frontier between the [Muslim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim "Muslim") and Christian-controlled areas of the peninsula was along the [Ebro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebro "Ebro") and [Douro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douro "Douro") valleys.

Conversion to [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam") proceeded at an increasing pace. The *[muladíes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muwallad "Muwallad")* (Muslims of ethnic Iberian origin) are believed to have formed the majority of the population of [Al-Andalus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Andalus "Al-Andalus") by the end of the 10th century.[^51] [^52] A series of [Viking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viking "Viking") incursions raided the coasts of the Iberian Peninsula in the 9th and 10th centuries.[^53] In the 11th century, the Caliphate of Córdoba collapsed, fracturing into a series of petty kingdoms (*[Taifas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taifa "Taifa")*),[^54] often subject to the payment of a form of [protection money](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protection_racket "Protection racket") (*[Parias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parias "Parias")*) to the Northern Christian kingdoms, which otherwise undertook a southward territorial expansion. The capture of the strategic city of [Toledo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toledo,_Spain "Toledo, Spain") in 1085 marked a significant shift in the balance of power in favour of the Christian kingdoms.[^55] The arrival from North Africa of the Islamic ruling sects of the [Almoravids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almoravids "Almoravids") and the [Almohads](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almohads "Almohads") achieved temporary unity upon the Muslim-ruled territory, with a stricter, less tolerant application of Islam, and partially reversed some Christian territorial gains.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f7/Ferdinand_of_Aragon%2C_Isabella_of_Castile_%28cropped%29.jpg/250px-Ferdinand_of_Aragon%2C_Isabella_of_Castile_%28cropped%29.jpg)

Catholic Monarchs of Spain

The [Kingdom of León](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Le%C3%B3n "Kingdom of León") was the strongest Christian kingdom for centuries. In 1188, the first form (restricted to the bishops, the magnates, and 'the elected citizens of each city') of modern parliamentary session in Europe was held in [León](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Le%C3%B3n_\(Spain\) "León (Spain)") ([Cortes of León](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cortes_of_Le%C3%B3n "Cortes of León")).[^56] The [Kingdom of Castile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Castile "Kingdom of Castile"), formed from Leonese territory, was its successor as the strongest kingdom. The kings and the nobility fought for power and influence in this period. The example of the Roman emperors influenced the political objective of the Crown, while the nobles benefited from [feudalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feudalism "Feudalism").

Muslim strongholds in the [Guadalquivir Valley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guadalquivir_Valley "Guadalquivir Valley") such as Córdoba (1236) and [Seville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seville "Seville") (1248) fell to Castile in the 13th century. The [County of Barcelona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/County_of_Barcelona "County of Barcelona") and the [Kingdom of Aragon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Aragon "Kingdom of Aragon") entered into a dynastic union and gained territory and power in the Mediterranean. In 1229, [Mallorca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mallorca "Mallorca") was conquered, as was [Valencia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valencia "Valencia") in 1238. In the 13th and 14th centuries, the North-African [Marinids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marinid "Marinid") established some enclaves around the Strait of Gibraltar. Upon the conclusion of the [Granada War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Granada_War "Granada War"), the [Nasrid Sultanate of Granada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Granada "Kingdom of Granada") (the remaining Muslim-ruled polity in the Iberian Peninsula after 1246) capitulated in 1492 to the military strength of the [Catholic Monarchs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Monarchs "Catholic Monarchs"), and it was integrated from then on in the Crown of Castile.[^57]

### Spanish Empire

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/La_sevilla_del_sigloXVI.jpg/330px-La_sevilla_del_sigloXVI.jpg)

Late 16th-century Seville, the harbour enjoying the exclusive right to trade with the New World

In 1469, the crowns of the Christian kingdoms of Castile and Aragon were united by the marriage of their monarchs, Isabella I and Ferdinand II, respectively. In 1492, as part of the [Spanish Inquisition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Inquisition "Spanish Inquisition"), Jews were forced to choose between conversion to Catholicism or expulsion;[^58] as many as 200,000 Jews were [expelled from Castile and Aragon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expulsion_of_Jews_from_Spain "Expulsion of Jews from Spain"). The year 1492 also marked the arrival of [Christopher Columbus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_Columbus "Christopher Columbus") in the [New World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_World "New World"), during a voyage funded by Isabella. Columbus's first voyage crossed the Atlantic and reached the Caribbean Islands, beginning the European exploration and conquest of the Americas.

The [Treaty of Granada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Granada "Treaty of Granada") guaranteed religious tolerance towards Muslims,[^59] for a few years before Islam was outlawed in 1502 in Castile and 1527 in Aragon, leading the remaining Muslim population to become nominally Christian *[Moriscos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morisco "Morisco")*. About four decades after the [War of the Alpujarras](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_the_Alpujarras "War of the Alpujarras") (1568–1571), over 300,000 [*moriscos* were expelled](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expulsion_of_the_Moriscos "Expulsion of the Moriscos"), settling primarily in North Africa.[^60]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/63/Spanish_Empire_%28diachronic%29.svg/330px-Spanish_Empire_%28diachronic%29.svg.png)

A diachronic map of the Spanish Empire

The unification of the crowns of Aragon and Castile by the marriage of their sovereigns laid the basis for modern Spain and the Spanish Empire, although each kingdom of Spain remained a separate country socially, politically, legally, and in currency and language.[^61] [^62]

[Habsburg Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habsburg_Spain "Habsburg Spain") was one of the leading world powers throughout the 16th century and most of the 17th century, a position reinforced by trade and wealth from colonial possessions and became the world's leading [maritime power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Navy "Spanish Navy"). It reached its apogee during the reigns of the first two Spanish Habsburgs— [Charles V/I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor "Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor") (1516–1556) and [Philip II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philip_II_of_Spain "Philip II of Spain") (1556–1598). This period saw the [Spanish conquest of the Aztec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_the_Aztec_Empire "Spanish conquest of the Aztec Empire") and [Inca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_the_Inca_Empire "Spanish conquest of the Inca Empire") empires, the [Italian Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Wars "Italian Wars"), the [Schmalkaldic War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Schmalkaldic_War "Schmalkaldic War"), the [Dutch Revolt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_Revolt "Dutch Revolt"), the [War of the Portuguese Succession](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_the_Portuguese_Succession "War of the Portuguese Succession"), clashes with the [Ottomans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Habsburg_wars "Ottoman–Habsburg wars"), intervention in the [French Wars of Religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Wars_of_Religion "French Wars of Religion"), and the [Anglo-Spanish War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Spanish_War_\(1585%E2%80%931604\) "Anglo-Spanish War (1585–1604)").[^63]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/57/Principales_Rutas_Comerciales_del_Imperio_Espa%C3%B1ol.jpg/330px-Principales_Rutas_Comerciales_del_Imperio_Espa%C3%B1ol.jpg)

Main trade routes of the Spanish Empire

Through exploration and conquest or royal marriage alliances and inheritance, the [Spanish Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Empire "Spanish Empire") expanded across vast areas in the Americas, the Indo-Pacific, Africa as well as the European continent, including holdings in the Italian Peninsula, the [Low Countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low_Countries "Low Countries") and the [Franche-Comté](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franche-Comt%C3%A9 "Franche-Comté"). The so-called [Age of Discovery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Discovery "Age of Discovery") featured explorations by sea and by land, the opening-up of new [trade routes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trade_route "Trade route") across oceans, conquests and the beginnings of European [colonialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colonialism "Colonialism"). [Precious metals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precious_metal "Precious metal"), spices, luxuries, and previously unknown plants brought to the metropole played a leading part in transforming the European understanding of the globe.[^64]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/44/Plaza_Mayor_de_Madrid_06.jpg/330px-Plaza_Mayor_de_Madrid_06.jpg)

Plaza Mayor of Madrid built in 1619 during the reign of Philip III.

The cultural efflorescence witnessed during this period is now referred to as the [Spanish Golden Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Golden_Age "Spanish Golden Age"). The expansion of the empire caused immense upheaval in the Americas as the collapse of societies and empires and new diseases from Europe devastated American indigenous populations. The rise of [humanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanism "Humanism"), the [Counter-Reformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Counter-Reformation "Counter-Reformation") and new geographical discoveries and conquests raised issues that were addressed by the intellectual movement now known as the [School of Salamanca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/School_of_Salamanca "School of Salamanca"), which developed the first modern theories of what are now known as [international law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_law "International law") and human rights. Spain's 16th-century maritime supremacy was demonstrated by the victory over the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") at the [Battle of Lepanto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Lepanto "Battle of Lepanto") in 1571 and over Portugal at the [Battle of Ponta Delgada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Vila_Franca_do_Campo "Battle of Vila Franca do Campo") in 1582, and then after the setback of the [Spanish Armada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Armada "Spanish Armada") in 1588, in a series of victories against [England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England "England") in the [Anglo-Spanish War of 1585–1604](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Spanish_War_\(1585%E2%80%931604\) "Anglo-Spanish War (1585–1604)"). In the middle decades of the 17th century, Spain's maritime power went into a long decline with mounting defeats against the [Dutch Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_Republic "Dutch Republic") ([Battle of the Downs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_the_Downs "Battle of the Downs")) and then England in the [Anglo-Spanish War of 1654–1660](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Spanish_War_\(1654%E2%80%931660\) "Anglo-Spanish War (1654–1660)"). By the 1660s, Spain was struggling to defend its overseas possessions from pirates and privateers.

The [Protestant Reformation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestant_Reformation "Protestant Reformation") increased Spain's involvement in religiously charged wars, forcing ever-expanding military efforts across Europe and in the Mediterranean.[^65] By the middle decades of a war- and [plague](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Plague_of_Seville "Great Plague of Seville") -ridden 17th-century Europe, the Spanish Habsburgs had enmeshed Spain in continent-wide religious-political conflicts. These conflicts drained Spain of resources and undermined the economy generally. Spain managed to hold on to most of the scattered Habsburg empire, and help the imperial forces of the [Holy Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire "Holy Roman Empire") reverse a large part of the advances made by Protestant forces.[^66]

Spain was finally forced to recognise the [separation of Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Restoration_War "Portuguese Restoration War") and the United Provinces (Dutch Republic), and eventually suffered some serious military reverses to France in the latter stages of the immensely destructive, Europe-wide [Thirty Years' War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirty_Years%27_War "Thirty Years' War").[^66] In the latter half of the 17th century, Spain went into a gradual decline, during which it surrendered several small territories to France and England; however, it maintained and enlarged its vast overseas empire, which remained intact until the beginning of the 19th century.

#### 18th century

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c1/La_familia_de_Felipe_V_%28Van_Loo%29.jpg/250px-La_familia_de_Felipe_V_%28Van_Loo%29.jpg)

The family of Philip V. During the Enlightenment in Spain a new royal family reigned, the House of Bourbon.

The decline culminated in a controversy over succession to the throne, which consumed the first years of the 18th century. The [War of the Spanish Succession](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_the_Spanish_Succession "War of the Spanish Succession") was a wide-ranging international conflict combined with a civil war, and cost the kingdom its European possessions and its position as a leading European power.[^67]

During this war, a new dynasty originating in France, the [Bourbons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Bourbon "House of Bourbon"), was installed. The Crowns of Castile and Aragon had been long united only by the Monarchy and the common institution of the Inquisition's [Holy Office](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Inquisition "Spanish Inquisition").[^68] A number of reform policies, the so-called [Bourbon Reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bourbon_Reforms "Bourbon Reforms"), were pursued by the Monarchy with the overarching goal of centralised authority and administrative uniformity.[^69] They included the abolishment of many of the old regional privileges and laws,[^70] as well as the customs barrier between the Crowns of Aragon and Castile in 1717, followed by the introduction of new property taxes in the Aragonese kingdoms.[^71]

The 18th century saw a gradual recovery and an increase in prosperity through much of the Spanish empire. The predominant economic policy was interventionist, with the State driving infrastructure development, abolishing internal customs, and reducing export tariffs.[^72] Projects of agricultural colonisation with new settlements took place in the south of mainland Spain.[^73] [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enlightenment_in_Spain "Enlightenment in Spain") ideas began to gain ground among some of the kingdom's elite and monarchy.

### Liberalism and nation state

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Monumento_a_la_Constituci%C3%B3n_de_1812%2C_C%C3%A1diz%2C_Espa%C3%B1a%2C_2015-12-08%2C_DD_80.JPG/250px-Monumento_a_la_Constituci%C3%B3n_de_1812%2C_C%C3%A1diz%2C_Espa%C3%B1a%2C_2015-12-08%2C_DD_80.JPG)

Monument to the Constitution of 1812 in Cádiz

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7b/Fusilamiento_de_Torrijos_%28Gisbert%29.jpg/250px-Fusilamiento_de_Torrijos_%28Gisbert%29.jpg)

Execution of Torrijos and his men in 1831, Málaga. Ferdinand VII took repressive measures against the liberal forces in his country.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/01_eibar.jpg/250px-01_eibar.jpg)

Women voting in Éibar in 1933, after women's suffrage was approved. Deputy Clara Campoamor was a key figure for the right to be granted.

In 1793, Spain went to war against the revolutionary [French Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_French_Republic "First French Republic") as a member of [the first Coalition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_the_First_Coalition "War of the First Coalition"). The subsequent [War of the Pyrenees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_the_Pyrenees "War of the Pyrenees") polarised the country in a reaction against the [gallicised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francization "Francization") elites and following defeat in the field, peace was made with France in 1795 at the [Peace of Basel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_of_Basel "Peace of Basel") in which Spain lost control over two-thirds of the island of [Hispaniola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hispaniola "Hispaniola"). In 1807, a secret treaty between [Napoleon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleon_I_of_France "Napoleon I of France") and the unpopular prime minister led to a new declaration of war against Britain and Portugal. French troops entered the country to invade Portugal but instead occupied Spain's major fortresses. The Spanish king abdicated and a puppet kingdom satellite to the French Empire was installed with [Joseph Bonaparte](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Bonaparte "Joseph Bonaparte") as king.

The [2 May 1808 revolt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dos_de_Mayo_Uprising "Dos de Mayo Uprising") was one of many uprisings across the country against the French occupation.[^74] These revolts marked the beginning of a devastating [war of independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsular_War "Peninsular War") against the Napoleonic regime,[^75] which resulted in an estimated 300,000 to 500,000 Spanish dead. Further military action by Spanish armies, [guerrilla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guerrilla "Guerrilla") warfare and an Anglo-Portuguese allied army, combined with [Napoleon's failure on the Russian front](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleon%27s_Invasion_of_Russia "Napoleon's Invasion of Russia"), led to the retreat of French imperial armies from the Iberian Peninsula in 1814, and the return of [King Ferdinand VII](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ferdinand_VII_of_Spain "Ferdinand VII of Spain").[^76]

During the war, in 1810, a revolutionary body, the [Cortes of Cádiz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cortes_of_C%C3%A1diz "Cortes of Cádiz"), was assembled to coordinate the effort against the Bonapartist regime and to prepare a constitution.[^77] It met as one body, and its members represented the entire Spanish empire.[^78] In 1812, a [constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1812 "Spanish Constitution of 1812") for universal representation under a constitutional monarchy was declared, but after the fall of the Bonapartist regime, the Spanish king dismissed the Cortes Generales, set on ruling as an [absolute monarch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Absolute_monarchy "Absolute monarchy").

The French occupation of mainland Spain created an opportunity for overseas [*criollo*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Criollo_people "Criollo people") elites who resented the privilege towards [Peninsular elites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsulars "Peninsulars") and demanded [retroversion of the sovereignty to the people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Retroversion_of_the_sovereignty_to_the_people "Retroversion of the sovereignty to the people"). Starting in 1809 the American colonies began a series of revolutions and declared independence, leading to the [Spanish American wars of independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_American_wars_of_independence "Spanish American wars of independence") that put an end to the metropole's grip over the [Spanish Main](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Main "Spanish Main"). [Attempts to re-assert control](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reconquista_\(Spanish_America\) "Reconquista (Spanish America)") proved futile with opposition not only in the colonies but also in the Iberian peninsula and army revolts followed. By the end of 1826, the only American colonies Spain held were [Cuba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_Cuba "Captaincy General of Cuba") and [Puerto Rico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_Puerto_Rico "Captaincy General of Puerto Rico"). The Napoleonic War left Spain economically ruined, deeply divided and politically unstable. In the 1830s and 1840s, [Carlism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlism "Carlism") (a reactionary legitimist movement supportive of an alternative Bourbon branch), fought against the government forces supportive of Queen [Isabella II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isabella_II_of_Spain "Isabella II of Spain") 's dynastic rights in the [Carlist Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlist_Wars "Carlist Wars"). Government forces prevailed, but the conflict between [*progressives*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Progressive_Party_\(Spain\) "Progressive Party (Spain)") and *[moderates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moderate_Party_\(Spain\) "Moderate Party (Spain)")* ended in a weak early constitutional period. The 1868 [Glorious Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glorious_Revolution_\(Spain\) "Glorious Revolution (Spain)") was followed by the 1868–1874 progressive *[Sexenio Democrático](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sexenio_Democr%C3%A1tico "Sexenio Democrático")* (including the short-lived [First Spanish Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Spanish_Republic "First Spanish Republic")), which yielded to a stable monarchic period, the [Restoration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Restoration_\(Spain\) "Restoration (Spain)") (1875–1931).[^79]

In the late 19th century nationalist movements arose in the Philippines and Cuba. In 1895 and 1896 the [Cuban War of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuban_War_of_Independence "Cuban War of Independence") and the [Philippine Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippine_Revolution "Philippine Revolution") broke out and eventually the United States became involved. The [Spanish–American War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish%E2%80%93American_War "Spanish–American War") was fought from April to August 1898 and resulted in Spain losing the last of its once vast colonial empire outside of North Africa. *El Desastre* (the Disaster), as the war became known in Spain, gave added impetus to the [Generation of '98](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generation_of_%2798 "Generation of '98"). Although the period around the turn of the century was one of increasing prosperity, the 20th century brought little social peace. Spain played a minor part in the [scramble for Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scramble_for_Africa "Scramble for Africa"). It remained neutral [during World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain_during_World_War_I "Spain during World War I"). The heavy losses suffered by the colonial troops in conflicts in northern Morocco against [Riffian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riffians "Riffians") tribes brought discredit to the government and undermined the monarchy.

Industrialisation, the development of railways and incipient capitalism developed in several areas of the country, particularly in [Barcelona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barcelona "Barcelona"), as well as [labour movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labour_movement "Labour movement") and socialist and anarchist ideas. The [1870 Barcelona Workers' Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1870_Barcelona_Workers%27_Congress "1870 Barcelona Workers' Congress") and the [1888 Barcelona Universal Exposition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1888_Barcelona_Universal_Exposition "1888 Barcelona Universal Exposition") are good examples of this. In 1879, the [Spanish Socialist Workers' Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party "Spanish Socialist Workers' Party") was founded. A trade union linked to this party, [Unión General de Trabajadores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uni%C3%B3n_General_de_Trabajadores "Unión General de Trabajadores"), was founded in 1888. In the anarcho-syndicalist trend of the labour movement in Spain, [Confederación Nacional del Trabajo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confederaci%C3%B3n_Nacional_del_Trabajo "Confederación Nacional del Trabajo") was founded in 1910 and [Federación Anarquista Ibérica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federaci%C3%B3n_Anarquista_Ib%C3%A9rica "Federación Anarquista Ibérica") in 1927. Catalanism and Vasquism, alongside other nationalisms and regionalisms in Spain, arose in that period: the [Basque Nationalist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basque_Nationalist_Party "Basque Nationalist Party") formed in 1895 and [Regionalist League of Catalonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regionalist_League_of_Catalonia "Regionalist League of Catalonia") in 1901. Political corruption and repression weakened the democratic system of the constitutional monarchy of a two-parties system.[^80] The July 1909 [Tragic Week](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tragic_Week_\(Spain\) "Tragic Week (Spain)") events and repression exemplified the social instability of the time. The [La Canadiense strike](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Canadiense_strike "La Canadiense strike") in 1919 led to the first law limiting the working day to eight hours.[^81]

After a period of Crown-supported dictatorship from 1923 to 1931, the first elections since 1923, largely understood as a plebiscite on Monarchy, took place: the [12 April 1931 municipal elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1931_Spanish_local_elections "1931 Spanish local elections"). These gave a resounding victory to the Republican-Socialist candidacies in large cities and provincial capitals, with a majority of monarchist councilors in rural areas. The king left the country and the proclamation of the Republic on 14 April ensued, with the formation of a provisional government. A [constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1931 "Spanish Constitution of 1931") for the country was passed in October 1931 following the [June 1931 Constituent general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1931_Spanish_general_election "1931 Spanish general election"), and a series of cabinets presided by [Manuel Azaña](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuel_Aza%C3%B1a "Manuel Azaña") supported by republican parties and the [PSOE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party "Spanish Socialist Workers' Party") followed. In the election held in 1933 the right triumphed and in 1936, the left. During the [Second Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Spanish_Republic "Second Spanish Republic") there was a great political and social upheaval, marked by a sharp radicalisation of the left and the right. Instances of political violence during this period included the burning of churches, the [1932 failed coup d'état led by José Sanjurjo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanjurjada "Sanjurjada"), the [Revolution of 1934](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revolution_of_1934 "Revolution of 1934") and numerous attacks against rival political leaders. On the other hand, it is also during the Second Republic when important reforms to modernise the country were initiated: a democratic constitution, agrarian reform, restructuring of the army, political decentralisation and [women's right to vote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Women%27s_suffrage "Women's suffrage").

### Civil War and Francoist dictatorship

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4a/Reemplazo_republicano.jpg/250px-Reemplazo_republicano.jpg)

Republican volunteers at Teruel, 1936

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/9/96/Meeting_at_Hendaye_%28en.wiki%29.jpg/250px-Meeting_at_Hendaye_%28en.wiki%29.jpg)

Spanish leader Francisco Franco and Adolf Hitler at the Meeting at Hendaye, 1940

The Spanish Civil War broke out in 1936: on 17 and 18 July, part of the military [carried out a coup d'état](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_coup_of_July_1936 "Spanish coup of July 1936") that triumphed in only part of the country. The situation led to a civil war, in which the territory was divided into two zones: one [under the authority of the Republican government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republican_faction_\(Spanish_Civil_War\) "Republican faction (Spanish Civil War)"), that counted on outside support from the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union") and [Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico "Mexico") (and from [International Brigades](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_response_to_the_Spanish_Civil_War#International_Brigades "International response to the Spanish Civil War")), and the other controlled by the putschists (the [Nationalist or rebel faction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nationalist_faction_\(Spanish_Civil_War\) "Nationalist faction (Spanish Civil War)")), most critically supported by [Nazi Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nazi_Germany "Nazi Germany") and [Fascist Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fascist_Italy_\(1922%E2%80%931943\) "Fascist Italy (1922–1943)"). The Republic was not supported by the Western powers due to the British-led policy of [non-intervention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-intervention "Non-intervention"). General [Francisco Franco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francisco_Franco "Francisco Franco") was sworn in as the supreme leader of the rebels on 1 October 1936. An uneasy relationship between the Republican government and the grassroots anarchists who had initiated a partial [social revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Revolution_of_1936 "Spanish Revolution of 1936") also ensued.

The civil war was viciously fought and there were [many atrocities committed by all sides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War#Atrocities "Spanish Civil War"). The [war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Civil_War "Spanish Civil War") claimed the lives of over 500,000 people and caused the flight of up to a half-million citizens from the country.[^82] [^83] On 1 April 1939, five months before the beginning of [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), the rebel side led by Franco emerged victorious, imposing a dictatorship over the whole country. Thousands were imprisoned after the civil war in [Francoist concentration camps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francoist_concentration_camps "Francoist concentration camps"). Between 1939 and 1943, approximately 200,000 people in Spain died from [famine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Famine "Famine").[^84]

The regime remained nominally " [neutral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neutrality_\(international_relations\) "Neutrality (international relations)") " for much of the Second World War, although it was [sympathetic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spain_in_World_War_II "Spain in World War II") to [the Axis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axis_Powers "Axis Powers") and provided the Nazi [Wehrmacht](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wehrmacht "Wehrmacht") with [Spanish volunteers in the Eastern Front](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blue_Division "Blue Division"). The only legal party under Franco's dictatorship was the [Falange Española Tradicionalista y de las JONS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falange_Espa%C3%B1ola_Tradicionalista_y_de_las_JONS "Falange Española Tradicionalista y de las JONS") (FET y de las JONS), formed in 1937 upon the merging of the Fascist [Falange Española de las JONS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Falange_Espa%C3%B1ola_de_las_JONS "Falange Española de las JONS") and the Carlist traditionalists and to which the rest of right-wing groups supporting the rebels also added. The name of " [Movimiento Nacional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Movimiento_Nacional "Movimiento Nacional") ", sometimes understood as a wider structure than the FET y de las JONS proper, largely imposed over the latter's name in official documents along the 1950s.

After the war Spain was politically and economically isolated, and was kept out of the United Nations. This changed in 1955, during the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War") period, when it became strategically important for the US to establish a military presence on the Iberian Peninsula as a counter to any possible move by the Soviet Union into the Mediterranean basin. US Cold War strategic priorities included the dissemination of American educational ideas to foster modernisation and expansion.[^85] In the 1960s, Spain registered an [unprecedented rate of economic growth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_miracle "Spanish miracle") which was propelled by [industrialisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_miracle#Industrialization "Spanish miracle"), a mass internal migration from rural areas to [Madrid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madrid "Madrid"), [Barcelona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barcelona "Barcelona") and the [Basque Country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basque_Country_\(autonomous_community\) "Basque Country (autonomous community)") and the creation of a mass tourism industry. Franco's rule was also characterised by [authoritarianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francoist_Spain#Fascism_and_authoritarianism "Francoist Spain"), [promotion of a unitary national identity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francoist_Spain#Spanish_nationalism "Francoist Spain"), [National Catholicism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Catholicism "National Catholicism"), and [discriminatory language policies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_policies_of_Francoist_Spain "Language policies of Francoist Spain").

### Restoration of democracy

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/84/De_proclamatie_en_be%C3%ABdiging_van_Prins_Juan_Carlos_tot_Koning_van_Spanje_tijdens_%2C_Bestanddeelnr_254-9763.jpg/250px-De_proclamatie_en_be%C3%ABdiging_van_Prins_Juan_Carlos_tot_Koning_van_Spanje_tijdens_%2C_Bestanddeelnr_254-9763.jpg)

Juan Carlos I before the Cortes Españolas, during his proclamation as King on 22 November 1975

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Felipe_Gonz%C3%A1lez_firma_el_Tratado_de_Adhesi%C3%B3n_de_Espa%C3%B1a_a_la_Comunidad_Econ%C3%B3mica_Europea_en_el_Palacio_Real_de_Madrid._Pool_Moncloa._12_de_junio_de_1985.jpeg/250px-Felipe_Gonz%C3%A1lez_firma_el_Tratado_de_Adhesi%C3%B3n_de_Espa%C3%B1a_a_la_Comunidad_Econ%C3%B3mica_Europea_en_el_Palacio_Real_de_Madrid._Pool_Moncloa._12_de_junio_de_1985.jpeg)

Felipe González signing the treaty of accession to the European Economic Community on 12 June 1985

In 1962, a group of politicians involved in the opposition to Franco's regime inside the country and in exile met in the congress of the [European Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Movement "European Movement") in Munich, where they made a resolution in favour of democracy.[^86] [^87] [^88] With Franco's death in November 1975, [Juan Carlos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_Carlos_I_of_Spain "Juan Carlos I of Spain") succeeded to the position of [King of Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_of_Spain "King of Spain") and [head of state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_state "Head of state") in accordance with the Francoist law. With the approval of the new [Spanish Constitution of 1978](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Constitution_of_1978 "Spanish Constitution of 1978") and the [restoration of democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_transition_to_democracy "Spanish transition to democracy"), the State [devolved](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devolution "Devolution") much authority to the regions and created an internal organisation based on [autonomous communities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomous_communities_of_Spain "Autonomous communities of Spain"). The [Spanish 1977 Amnesty Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_1977_Amnesty_Law "Spanish 1977 Amnesty Law") let people of Franco's regime continue inside institutions without consequences, even perpetrators of some crimes during transition to democracy like the [Massacre of 3 March 1976 in Vitoria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massacre_of_3_March_1976_in_Vitoria "Massacre of 3 March 1976 in Vitoria") or [1977 Massacre of Atocha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1977_Massacre_of_Atocha "1977 Massacre of Atocha").

In the Basque Country, moderate [Basque nationalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basque_nationalism "Basque nationalism") coexisted with a [radical nationalist movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basque_Conflict "Basque Conflict") led by the armed organisation [ETA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ETA_\(separatist_group\) "ETA (separatist group)") until the latter's dissolution in May 2018.[^89] The group was formed in 1959 during Franco's rule but had continued to wage its violent campaign even after the restoration of democracy and the return of a large measure of regional autonomy.

On 23 February 1981, rebel elements among the security forces seized the Cortes in an attempt to impose [a military-backed government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/23-F "23-F"). King Juan Carlos took personal command of the military and successfully ordered the coup plotters, via national television, to surrender.[^90]

During the 1980s the democratic restoration made possible a growing open society. New cultural movements based on freedom appeared, like [La Movida Madrileña](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Movida_Madrile%C3%B1a "La Movida Madrileña"). In May 1982 Spain joined [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO"), followed by [a referendum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1986_Spanish_NATO_membership_referendum "1986 Spanish NATO membership referendum") after a strong social opposition. That year the [Spanish Socialist Workers Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers_Party "Spanish Socialist Workers Party") (PSOE) came to power, the first left-wing government in 43 years. In 1986 Spain joined the [European Economic Community](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Economic_Community "European Economic Community"), which later became the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union"). The PSOE was replaced in government by the [Partido Popular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Party_\(Spain\) "People's Party (Spain)") (PP) in 1996 after scandals around participation of the government of [Felipe González](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Felipe_Gonz%C3%A1lez "Felipe González") in the [Dirty war against ETA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GAL_\(paramilitary_group\) "GAL (paramilitary group)").

### Economic prosperity and crash

On 1 January 2002, Spain fully adopted the [euro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Euro "Euro"), and Spain experienced strong economic growth, well above the EU average during the early 2000s. However, well-publicised concerns issued by many economic commentators at the height of the boom warned that extraordinary property prices and a high foreign trade deficit were likely to lead to a painful economic collapse.[^91]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d7/Madrid_October15.jpg/250px-Madrid_October15.jpg)

Demonstration against the crisis and high youth unemployment in Madrid, 15 October 2011

In 2002, the [Prestige oil spill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prestige_oil_spill "Prestige oil spill") occurred with big ecological consequences along Spain's Atlantic coastline. In 2003 [José María Aznar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Mar%C3%ADa_Aznar "José María Aznar") supported US president [George W. Bush](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_W._Bush "George W. Bush") in the [Iraq War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq_War "Iraq War"), and a strong movement against war rose in Spanish society. In March 2004 a local [Islamist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamist "Islamist") terrorist group inspired by [Al-Qaeda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Qaeda "Al-Qaeda") carried out the largest terrorist attack in Western European history when they killed 191 people and wounded more than 1,800 others by [bombing commuter trains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2004_Madrid_train_bombings "2004 Madrid train bombings") in Madrid.[^92] Though initial suspicions focused on the Basque terrorist group [ETA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ETA_\(separatist_group\) "ETA (separatist group)"), evidence of Islamist involvement soon emerged. Because of the proximity of the [2004 Spanish general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2004_Spanish_general_election "2004 Spanish general election"), the issue of responsibility quickly became a political controversy, with the main competing parties, the [PP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Party_\(Spain\) "People's Party (Spain)") (People's Party) and [PSOE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Socialist_Workers%27_Party "Spanish Socialist Workers' Party") (Spanish Socialist Workers' party) exchanging accusations over the handling of the incident.[^93] The PSOE won the election, led by [José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jos%C3%A9_Luis_Rodr%C3%ADguez_Zapatero "José Luis Rodríguez Zapatero").[^94]

In the early 2000s, the proportion of [Spain's foreign born population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Immigration_to_Spain#Currently "Immigration to Spain") increased rapidly during its economic boom but then declined due to the [2008 financial crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_financial_crisis "2008 financial crisis").[^95] In 2005, the Spanish government legalised [same sex marriage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Same-sex_marriage_in_Spain "Same-sex marriage in Spain"), becoming the third country worldwide to do so.[^96] Decentralisation was supported with much resistance of Constitutional Court and conservative opposition, so did gender politics like quotas or the law against gender violence. Government talks with ETA happened, and the group announced its permanent cease of violence in 2010.[^97]

The bursting of the [Spanish property bubble](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_property_bubble "Spanish property bubble") in 2008 led to the [2008–2014 Spanish financial crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008%E2%80%932014_Spanish_financial_crisis "2008–2014 Spanish financial crisis"). High levels of unemployment, cuts in government spending and corruption within the [Royal family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_family "Royal family") and the People's Party served as a backdrop to the [2011–12 Spanish protests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011%E2%80%9312_Spanish_protests "2011–12 Spanish protests").[^98] The [Catalan independence movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalan_independence_movement "Catalan independence movement") also gained traction. In 2011, [Mariano Rajoy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mariano_Rajoy "Mariano Rajoy") 's conservative People's Party won the election with 44.6% of votes.[^99] As prime minister, he implemented austerity measures for the EU bailout, the EU Stability and Growth Pact.[^100] On 19 June 2014, the monarch, Juan Carlos, abdicated in favour of his son, who became [Felipe VI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Felipe_VI "Felipe VI").[^101]

### Constitutional issues

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/86/01.10.2017_Referendum_1-OCT_%284%29.jpg/250px-01.10.2017_Referendum_1-OCT_%284%29.jpg)

Catalans casting their votes in Barcelona during the 2017 Catalan independence referendum

In October 2017 a [Catalan independence referendum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Catalan_independence_referendum "2017 Catalan independence referendum") was held and the [Parliament of Catalonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliament_of_Catalonia "Parliament of Catalonia") voted to unilaterally declare [independence from Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2017_Catalonia_declaration_of_independence "2017 Catalonia declaration of independence") to form a Catalan Republic [^102] [^103] on the day the [Spanish Senate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senate_of_Spain "Senate of Spain") was discussing approving direct rule over Catalonia as called for by the Spanish Prime Minister.[^104] [^105] On the same day the Senate granted the power to impose direct rule and Mariano Rajoy dissolved the Catalan parliament and called a new election.[^106] No country recognised Catalonia as a separate state.[^107]

In June 2018, the [Congress of Deputies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_Deputies "Congress of Deputies") passed a [motion of no-confidence against Rajoy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_vote_of_no_confidence_in_the_government_of_Mariano_Rajoy "2018 vote of no confidence in the government of Mariano Rajoy") and replaced him with the PSOE leader [Pedro Sánchez](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pedro_S%C3%A1nchez_\(politician\) "Pedro Sánchez (politician)").[^108] In 2019, the first ever coalition government in Spain was formed, between PSOE and [Unidas Podemos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unidas_Podemos "Unidas Podemos") (now Sumar). Between 2018 and 2024, Spain faced an [institutional crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018%E2%80%932023_Spanish_institutional_crisis "2018–2023 Spanish institutional crisis") surrounding the mandate of the [General Council of the Judiciary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Council_of_the_Judiciary "General Council of the Judiciary") (CGPJ), until finally the mandate got renovated.[^109] In January 2020, the [COVID-19](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19 "COVID-19") virus was confirmed to have [spread to Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Spain "COVID-19 pandemic in Spain"), causing life expectancy to drop by more than a year.[^110] The European Commission economic recovery package [Next Generation EU](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Next_Generation_EU "Next Generation EU") was created to support the EU member states to recover from the COVID-19 pandemic, and will be in use in the period 2021–2026. In March 2021, Spain became the sixth nation in the world to make [active euthanasia legal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legality_of_euthanasia "Legality of euthanasia").[^111]

Following the [general election on 23 July 2023](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Spanish_general_election "2023 Spanish general election"), Prime Minister Pedro Sánchez once again formed a coalition government, this time with [Sumar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumar_\(electoral_platform\) "Sumar (electoral platform)") (the successor of [Unidas Podemos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unidas_Podemos "Unidas Podemos")).[^112] In 2024, the first non-independentist Catalan regional president in over a decade, [Salvador Illa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salvador_Illa "Salvador Illa"), was elected, normalising the constitutional and institutional relations between the national and the regional administrations. According to latest polls,[^113] only 17.3% of Catalans feel themselves as "only Catalan". 46% of Catalans would answer "as Spanish as Catalan", while 21.8% "more Catalan than Spanish".[^113] According to a 2024 poll of University of Barcelona, over 50% of Catalans would vote against independence, while less than 40% would vote in favour.[^114]

## Geography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/01/Reliefkarte_Spanien_mit_Kanaren.png/250px-Reliefkarte_Spanien_mit_Kanaren.png)

Topographic map of Spain

At 505,992 km <sup>2</sup> (195,365 sq mi), Spain is the world's [fiftieth largest country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_outlying_territories_by_area "List of countries and outlying territories by area") and [Europe's fourth largest country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Area_and_population_of_European_countries "Area and population of European countries"). At 3,715 m (12,188 ft), Mount [Teide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teide "Teide") ([Tenerife](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tenerife "Tenerife")) is the highest mountain peak in Spain and is the third largest volcano in the world from its base. Spain is a [transcontinental country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_transcontinental_countries "List of transcontinental countries"), having territory in both [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "Europe") and [Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "Africa").

Spain lies between latitudes [27°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/27th_parallel_north "27th parallel north") and [44° N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/44th_parallel_north "44th parallel north"), and longitudes [19° W](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/19th_meridian_west "19th meridian west") and [5° E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/5th_meridian_east "5th meridian east").

On the west, Spain is bordered by [Portugal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal "Portugal"); on the south, it is bordered by [Gibraltar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gibraltar "Gibraltar") and [Morocco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Morocco "Morocco"), through its [exclaves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exclave "Exclave") in North Africa ([Ceuta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceuta "Ceuta") and [Melilla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melilla "Melilla"), and the peninsula of [de Vélez de la Gomera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pe%C3%B1%C3%B3n_de_V%C3%A9lez_de_la_Gomera "Peñón de Vélez de la Gomera")). On the northeast, along the [Pyrenees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrenees "Pyrenees") mountain range, it is bordered by [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "France") and [Andorra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andorra "Andorra"). Along the Pyrenees in [Girona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Province_of_Girona "Province of Girona"), a small exclave town called [Llívia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ll%C3%ADvia "Llívia") is surrounded by France.

Extending to 1,214 km (754 mi), the [Portugal–Spain border](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portugal%E2%80%93Spain_border "Portugal–Spain border") is the [longest uninterrupted border](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_territories_by_land_borders "List of countries and territories by land borders") within the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union").[^115]

### Islands

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/View_of_the_bay_at_Calo_des_Moro%2C_Mallorca_%28Spain%29_%2823899506181%29.jpg/250px-View_of_the_bay_at_Calo_des_Moro%2C_Mallorca_%28Spain%29_%2823899506181%29.jpg)

Caló d'es Moro, Mallorca island

Spain also includes the [Balearic Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balearic_Islands "Balearic Islands") in the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea"), the [Canary Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canary_Islands "Canary Islands") in the Atlantic Ocean and a number of uninhabited islands on the Mediterranean side of the [Strait of Gibraltar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Gibraltar "Strait of Gibraltar"), known as *[plazas de soberanía](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plazas_de_soberan%C3%ADa "Plazas de soberanía")* ("places of sovereignty", or territories under Spanish sovereignty), such as the [Chafarinas Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chafarinas_Islands "Chafarinas Islands") and [Alhucemas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pe%C3%B1%C3%B3n_de_Alhucemas "Peñón de Alhucemas"). The peninsula of [de Vélez de la Gomera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pe%C3%B1%C3%B3n_de_V%C3%A9lez_de_la_Gomera "Peñón de Vélez de la Gomera") is also regarded as a *plaza de soberanía*. The isle of [Alborán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alboran_Island "Alboran Island"), located in the Mediterranean between Spain and North Africa, is also administered by Spain, specifically by the municipality of [Almería](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almer%C3%ADa "Almería"), Andalusia. The little [Pheasant Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pheasant_Island "Pheasant Island") in the River [Bidasoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bidasoa "Bidasoa") is a Spanish-French [condominium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Condominium_\(international_law\) "Condominium (international law)").

There are 11 major islands in Spain, all of them having their own governing bodies ([Cabildos insulares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabildo_insular "Cabildo insular") in the Canaries, [Consells insulars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balearic_Islands#Administration "Balearic Islands") in Baleares). These islands are specifically mentioned by the Spanish Constitution, when fixing its Senatorial representation (Ibiza and Formentera are grouped, as they together form the [Pityusic islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pityusic_Islands "Pityusic Islands"), part of the Balearic archipelago). These islands include [Tenerife](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tenerife "Tenerife"), [Gran Canaria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gran_Canaria "Gran Canaria"), [Lanzarote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lanzarote "Lanzarote"), [Fuerteventura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fuerteventura "Fuerteventura"), [La Palma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Palma "La Palma"), [La Gomera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Gomera "La Gomera") and [El Hierro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/El_Hierro "El Hierro") in the Canarian archipelago and [Mallorca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mallorca "Mallorca"), [Ibiza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ibiza "Ibiza"), [Menorca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Menorca "Menorca") and [Formentera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Formentera "Formentera") in the Balearic archipelago.

### Mountains and rivers

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a5/Roque_Cinchado_und_Teide.jpg/250px-Roque_Cinchado_und_Teide.jpg)

Teide, still an active volcano in Tenerife, Canary Islands, is the tallest peak in Spain.

Mainland Spain is a rather [mountainous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountainous "Mountainous") landmass, dominated by high [plateaus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plateau "Plateau") and mountain chains. After the Pyrenees, the main mountain ranges are the [Cordillera Cantábrica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantabrian_Mountains "Cantabrian Mountains") (Cantabrian Range), [Sistema Ibérico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sistema_Ib%C3%A9rico "Sistema Ibérico") (Iberian System), [Sistema Central](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sistema_Central "Sistema Central") (Central System), [Montes de Toledo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montes_de_Toledo "Montes de Toledo"), [Sierra Morena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Morena "Sierra Morena") and the [Sistema Bético](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sistema_B%C3%A9tico "Sistema Bético") (Baetic System) whose highest peak, the 3,478-metre-high (11,411-foot) [Mulhacén](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mulhac%C3%A9n "Mulhacén"), located in [Sierra Nevada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Nevada_\(Spain\) "Sierra Nevada (Spain)"), is the highest elevation in the Iberian Peninsula. The highest point in Spain is the [Teide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teide "Teide"), a 3,718-metre (12,198 ft) active [volcano](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcano "Volcano") in the Canary Islands. The [Meseta Central](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meseta_Central "Meseta Central") (often translated as 'Inner Plateau') is a vast plateau in the heart of peninsular Spain split in two by the Sistema Central.

There are several major [rivers in Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rivers_in_Spain "Rivers in Spain") such as the [Tagus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tagus "Tagus") (*Tajo*), [Ebro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebro "Ebro"), [Guadiana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guadiana_River "Guadiana River"), [Douro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douro "Douro") (*Duero*), [Guadalquivir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guadalquivir "Guadalquivir"), [Júcar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%C3%BAcar_River "Júcar River"), [Segura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Segura_\(river\) "Segura (river)"), [Turia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turia_River "Turia River") and [Minho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minho_River "Minho River") (*Miño*). [Alluvial plains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alluvial_plain "Alluvial plain") are found along the coast, the largest of which is that of the Guadalquivir in [Andalusia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andalusia "Andalusia").

### Climate

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cf/Spain_K%C3%B6ppen.svg/250px-Spain_K%C3%B6ppen.svg.png)

Köppen climate classification map of Spain

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/06/Urriellu_desde_el_Pozo_de_La_Oracion.jpg/250px-Urriellu_desde_el_Pozo_de_La_Oracion.jpg)

Urriellu peak ( Naranjo de Bulnes ) from Pozo de La Oracion, Picos de Europa

Three main climatic zones can be separated, according to geographical situation and [orographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orography "Orography") conditions:[^116]

- The [Mediterranean climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_climate "Mediterranean climate") is characterised by warm or hot and dry summers and is the predominant climate in the country. It has two varieties: *Csa* and *Csb,* according to the [Köppen climate classification](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%B6ppen_climate_classification "Köppen climate classification").
	- The *Csa* zone is associated with areas with hot summers. It is predominant in the Southern Mediterranean (except southeastern) and Southern Atlantic coast and inland throughout [Andalusia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andalusia "Andalusia"), Extremadura and much of the centre of the country. Some areas of Csa, mainly those inland, such as some areas of [Castilla-La-Mancha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Castilla%E2%80%93La_Mancha "Castilla–La Mancha"), [Extremadura](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extremadura "Extremadura"), Madrid and some parts of Andalusia, have cool winters with some continental influences, while the regions with a Mediterranean climate close to the sea have mild winters.
		- The *Csb* zone has warm rather than hot summers, and extends to additional cool-winter areas not typically associated with a Mediterranean climate, such as much of central and northern-central of Spain (e.g. western [Castile–León](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Castile_and_Le%C3%B3n "Castile and León"), northeastern Castilla-La Mancha and northern Madrid) and into much rainier areas (notably [Galicia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Galicia_\(Spain\) "Galicia (Spain)")).
- The [semi-arid climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semi-arid_climate "Semi-arid climate") (*BSk*, *BSh*) is predominant in the southeastern quarter of the country, but is also widespread in other areas of Spain. It covers most of the [Region of Murcia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Region_of_Murcia "Region of Murcia"), southern and central-eastern [Valencia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valencian_Community "Valencian Community"), eastern Andalusia, various areas of Castilla-La-Mancha, Madrid and some areas of Extremadura. Further to the north, it is predominant in the upper and mid reaches of the [Ebro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebro "Ebro") valley, which crosses southern [Navarre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Navarre "Navarre"), central [Aragon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aragon "Aragon") and western [Catalonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catalonia "Catalonia"). It is also found in a small area in northern Andalusia and in a small area in central Castilla-León. Precipitation is limited with the dry season extending beyond the summer and average temperature depending on altitude and latitude.
- The [oceanic climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oceanic_climate "Oceanic climate") (*Cfb*) is located in the northern quarter of the country, especially in the Atlantic region ([Basque Country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basque_Country_\(autonomous_community\) "Basque Country (autonomous community)"), [Cantabria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantabria "Cantabria"), [Asturias](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asturias "Asturias"), and partly Galicia and Castile–León). It is also found in northern Navarre, in most highlands areas along the [Iberian System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sistema_Ib%C3%A9rico "Sistema Ibérico") and in the [Pyrenean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrenees "Pyrenees") valleys, where a humid subtropical variant (*Cfa*) also occurs. Winter and summer temperatures are influenced by the ocean, and have no seasonal drought.

Apart from these main types, other sub-types can be found, like the [alpine climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alpine_climate "Alpine climate") in areas with very high altitude, the [humid subtropical climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humid_subtropical_climate "Humid subtropical climate") in areas of northeastern Spain and the [continental climates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_climate "Continental climate") (*Dfc*, *Dfb* / *Dsc*, *Dsb*) in the [Pyrenees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyrenees "Pyrenees") as well as parts of the [Cantabrian Range](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cordillera_Cant%C3%A1brica "Cordillera Cantábrica"), the [Central System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sistema_Central "Sistema Central"), [Sierra Nevada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Nevada_\(Spain\) "Sierra Nevada (Spain)") and the [Iberian System](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sistema_Ib%C3%A9rico "Sistema Ibérico"), and a typical [desert climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Desert_climate "Desert climate") (*BWk*, *BWh*) in the zone of [Almería](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Almer%C3%ADa "Almería"), [Murcia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murcia "Murcia") and eastern [Canary Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canary_Islands "Canary Islands"). Low-lying areas of the Canary Islands average above 18.0 °C (64.4 °F) during their coolest month, thus having influences of [tropical climate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_climate "Tropical climate"), although they cannot properly be classified as tropical climates, as according to AEMET, their aridity is high, thus belonging to an arid or semi-arid climate.[^117]

Spain is one of the countries that is most affected by [climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_in_Spain "Climate change in Spain") in Europe. In Spain, which already has a hot and dry climate, extreme events such as [heatwaves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heat_wave "Heat wave") are becoming increasingly frequent.[^118] [^119] The country is also experiencing more episodes of drought and increased severity of these episodes.[^120] [Water resources](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Water_resources "Water resources") will be severely affected in various [climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change "Climate change") scenarios.[^121] To mitigate the [effects of climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change "Effects of climate change"), Spain is promoting an [energy transition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_transition "Energy transition") to [renewable energies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renewable_energy "Renewable energy"), such as [solar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solar_power "Solar power") and [wind energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wind_energy "Wind energy").[^122]

### Fauna and flora

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Iberian_Wolf_AdF_001.jpg/250px-Iberian_Wolf_AdF_001.jpg)

The Iberian wolf in Castile and León. The region has 25% of the land covered by Natura 2000 protected natural spaces.

The [fauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna "Fauna") presents a wide diversity that is due in large part to the geographical position of the Iberian peninsula between the Atlantic and the Mediterranean and between Africa and [Eurasia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasia "Eurasia"), and the great diversity of habitats and [biotopes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biotopes "Biotopes"), the result of a considerable variety of climates and well differentiated regions.

The vegetation of Spain is varied due to several factors including the diversity of the terrain, the climate and [latitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latitude "Latitude"). Spain includes different [phytogeographic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phytogeographic "Phytogeographic") regions, each with its own floral characteristics resulting largely from the interaction of climate, topography, soil type and fire, and [biotic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biotic_component "Biotic component") factors. The country had a 2019 [Forest Landscape Integrity Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest_Landscape_Integrity_Index "Forest Landscape Integrity Index") mean score of 4.23/10, ranking it 130th globally out of 172 countries.[^123]

Within the European territory, Spain has the largest number of plant species (7,600 vascular plants) of all European countries.[^124]

In Spain there are 17.804 billion trees and an average of 284 million more grow each year.[^125]
