**North Korea**,[^4] officially the **Democratic People's Republic of Korea** (**DPRK**),[^5] is a country in [East Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Asia "East Asia"). It constitutes the northern half of the [Korean Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korea "Korea") and borders [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") and [Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russia "Russia") to the north at the [Yalu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yalu_River "Yalu River") (Amnok) and [Tumen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tumen_River "Tumen River") rivers, and [South Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Korea "South Korea") to the south at the [Korean Demilitarized Zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Demilitarized_Zone "Korean Demilitarized Zone") (DMZ).[^6] The country's western border is formed by the [Yellow Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yellow_Sea "Yellow Sea"), while its eastern border is defined by the [Sea of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_of_Japan "Sea of Japan"). [Pyongyang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pyongyang "Pyongyang") is the capital and largest city.

The [Korean Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Peninsula "Korean Peninsula") was first inhabited as early as the [Lower Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lower_Paleolithic "Lower Paleolithic") period. Its [first kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Chos%C5%8Fn "Old Chosŏn") was noted in Chinese records in the early 7th century BCE. Following the unification of the [Three Kingdoms of Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Kingdoms_of_Korea "Three Kingdoms of Korea") into [Silla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unified_Silla "Unified Silla") and [Balhae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balhae "Balhae") in the late 7th century, Korea was ruled by the [Goryeo dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Goryeo_dynasty "Goryeo dynasty") (918–1392) and the [Joseon dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseon_dynasty "Joseon dynasty") (1392–1897). The succeeding [Korean Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Empire "Korean Empire") (1897–1910) was [annexed in 1910](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japan%E2%80%93Korea_Treaty_of_1910 "Japan–Korea Treaty of 1910") into [Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Japan "Empire of Japan"). In 1945, after the [Japanese surrender](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surrender_of_Japan "Surrender of Japan") at the [end of World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/End_of_World_War_II_in_Asia "End of World War II in Asia"), Korea was [divided into two zones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Division_of_Korea "Division of Korea") along the [38th parallel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/38th_parallel_north "38th parallel north"), with the [north occupied by the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Civil_Administration "Soviet Civil Administration") and the [south occupied by the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Army_Military_Government_in_Korea "United States Army Military Government in Korea").

In 1948, separate governments were formed in Korea: the [Soviet-aligned](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Bloc "Eastern Bloc") Democratic People's Republic of Korea in the north, and the [Western-aligned](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Bloc "Western Bloc") Republic of Korea in the south. The North Korean invasion of South Korea in 1950 started the [Korean War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_War "Korean War"). In 1953, the [Korean Armistice Agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Armistice_Agreement "Korean Armistice Agreement") brought about a [ceasefire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceasefire "Ceasefire") and established a [demilitarized zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demilitarized_zone "Demilitarized zone") (DMZ). [Kim Il Sung](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kim_Il_Sung "Kim Il Sung"), North Korea's first leader, consolidated power and promoted his personal philosophy of *[Juche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juche "Juche")* as the [state ideology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ideocracy "Ideocracy"). Pyongyang's international isolation sharply accelerated from the 1980s onwards as the [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War") came to an end. The [dissolution of the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union "Dissolution of the Soviet Union") in 1991 then brought about a sharp decline to the North Korean economy. From 1994 to 1998, [North Korea suffered a famine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990s_North_Korean_famine "1990s North Korean famine") with the population continuing to suffer from malnutrition. In 2024, the DPRK formally abandoned efforts to [reunify Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_reunification "Korean reunification").

North Korea is a [totalitarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Totalitarianism "Totalitarianism") hereditary [dictatorship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dictatorship "Dictatorship") with a [comprehensive cult of personality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Korean_cult_of_personality "North Korean cult of personality") around the [Kim family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kim_family_\(North_Korea\) "Kim family (North Korea)"). [Amnesty International](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amnesty_International "Amnesty International") considers the country to have the worst [human rights record](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_North_Korea "Human rights in North Korea") in the world. Officially, North Korea is a [communist state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_state "Communist state"), and describes itself as a [socialist state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist_state_\(communism\) "Socialist state (communism)") which [holds democratic elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elections_in_North_Korea "Elections in North Korea"). However, outside observers have described the elections as unfair, uncompetitive, and pre-determined. The [Workers' Party of Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Workers%27_Party_of_Korea "Workers' Party of Korea") (WPK) is the [sole ruling party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One-party_state "One-party state") of North Korea, while [Kimilsungism–Kimjongilism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ideology_of_the_Workers%27_Party_of_Korea "Ideology of the Workers' Party of Korea") is the official ideology. The [means of production](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Means_of_production "Means of production") are owned by the state through [state-run enterprises](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State-owned_enterprise "State-owned enterprise") and [collectivized farms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collective_farming "Collective farming"). Most services—such as [healthcare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_in_North_Korea "Health in North Korea"), [education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_school "State school"), [housing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_housing "Public housing"), and [food production](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agricultural_subsidy "Agricultural subsidy") —are subsidized or state-funded.

North Korea is highly militarized, prioritizing the [Korean People's Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_People%27s_Army "Korean People's Army") in the allocation of resources. It [possesses nuclear weapons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Korea_and_weapons_of_mass_destruction "North Korea and weapons of mass destruction"). Its active-duty army of 1.28 million soldiers is the fourth-largest in the world. In addition to being a member of the United Nations since 1991, North Korea is also a member of the [Non-Aligned Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement "Non-Aligned Movement"), the [G77](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_of_77 "Group of 77"), and the [ASEAN Regional Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASEAN "ASEAN").

## Etymology

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/History_of_Korea-476.PNG/250px-History_of_Korea-476.PNG)

The name Korea is derived from Goguryeo, also known as Koryŏ, one of the Three Kingdoms of Korea.

The modern spelling of Korea first appeared in 1671 in the travel writings of [Hendrick Hamel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hendrick_Hamel "Hendrick Hamel"), a sailor of the [Dutch East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company "Dutch East India Company").[^19]

After the division of the country into North and South Korea, the two sides used different terms to refer to Korea: *Chosŏn* (조선) in North Korea, and *Hanguk* (한국) in South Korea. In 1948, North Korea adopted *Democratic People's Republic of Korea* (조선민주주의인민공화국; 朝鮮民主主義人民共和國; *Chosŏnminjujuŭiinmin'gonghwaguk*; [listen](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/transcoded/d/d0/%EC%A1%B0%EC%84%A0%EB%AF%BC%EC%A3%BC%EC%A3%BC%EC%9D%98%EC%9D%B8%EB%AF%BC%EA%B3%B5%ED%99%94%EA%B5%AD_%EB%B0%9C%EC%9D%8C.ogg/%EC%A1%B0%EC%84%A0%EB%AF%BC%EC%A3%BC%EC%A3%BC%EC%9D%98%EC%9D%B8%EB%AF%BC%EA%B3%B5%ED%99%94%EA%B5%AD_%EB%B0%9C%EC%9D%8C.ogg.mp3 "Play audio")) as its official name. Within North Korea, the country's official name is frequently shortened to just "the Republic" (공화국; 共和國; *Konghwaguk*), which is a common moniker within the country.[^20]

In the wider world, because its government controls the northern part of the Korean Peninsula, it is commonly called *North Korea* to distinguish it from South Korea, which is officially called the *Republic of Korea* in English. Both governments historically considered themselves to be the legitimate government of the whole of Korea;[^21] [^22] for this reason, the people of North Korea consider themselves Koreans, not North Koreans, with foreign visitors being discouraged from using the latter term.[^23]

## History

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/92/Hangunhyeon.PNG/250px-Hangunhyeon.PNG)

The Four Commanderies of Han, established in the former territory of Gojoseon after the fall of Wiman Joseon. 24 The location of the commanderies has become a controversial topic in Korea in recent years. 25 However, the location of the commanderies is not controversial outside of Korea. 7

According to [Korean mythology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_mythology "Korean mythology") in 2333 BC, the [Gojoseon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Chos%C5%8Fn "Old Chosŏn") Kingdom was established by the god-king [Dangun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dangun "Dangun"). Following the end of the Three Kingdoms of Korea into Silla and Balhae in the late 7th century, Korea was subsequently ruled by the Goryeo dynasty (918–1392) and the [Joseon dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseon_dynasty "Joseon dynasty") (1392–1897). In 1897, [King Gojong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gojong_of_Korea "Gojong of Korea") proclaimed the [Korean Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_Empire "Korean Empire"), which was [annexed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korea_under_Japanese_rule "Korea under Japanese rule") by the [Empire of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Japan "Empire of Japan") in 1910.[^26]
