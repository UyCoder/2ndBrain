**Vietnam**,[^7] [^8] officially the **Socialist Republic of Viet Nam** (**SRV**),[^9] [^10] is a country at the eastern edge of [Mainland Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_Southeast_Asia "Mainland Southeast Asia"). With an area of about 331,000 square kilometres and a population of over 102 million, it is the world's [16th-most populous country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_population "List of countries and dependencies by population"). One of two [communist states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_state "Communist state") in [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia"),[^11] Vietnam is bordered by [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") to the north, and [Laos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Laos "Laos") and [Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambodia "Cambodia") to the west; it lies along the [Gulf of Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Thailand "Gulf of Thailand") to the southwest and the [South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea "South China Sea") to the east, where it has shared and [disputed maritime borders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Territorial_disputes_in_the_South_China_Sea "Territorial disputes in the South China Sea") with other countries. Its capital is [Hanoi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanoi "Hanoi"), while its [largest city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Vietnam#Largest_cities_by_population "List of cities in Vietnam") is [Ho Chi Minh City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City "Ho Chi Minh City").

Vietnam was inhabited by the [Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleolithic "Paleolithic") age, with states established in the first millennium BC on the [Red River Delta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_River_Delta "Red River Delta") in modern-day [northern Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Vietnam "Northern Vietnam").[^25] The [Han dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty") annexed northern and central Vietnam, which were subsequently under [Chinese rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam_under_Chinese_rule "Vietnam under Chinese rule") from 111 BC until the [first dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ng%C3%B4_dynasty "Ngô dynasty") emerged in 939. Successive [monarchical dynasties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Vietnamese_dynasties "List of Vietnamese dynasties") absorbed Chinese influences through [Confucianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucianism "Confucianism") and [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism"), and [expanded southward](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nam_ti%E1%BA%BFn "Nam tiến") to the [Mekong Delta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mekong_Delta "Mekong Delta"), conquering [Champa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Champa "Champa"). During most of the 17th and 18th centuries, Vietnam was effectively divided into two domains of [Đàng Trong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%C3%A0ng_Trong "Đàng Trong") and [Đàng Ngoài](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%C3%A0ng_Ngo%C3%A0i "Đàng Ngoài"). The [Nguyễn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_dynasty "Nguyễn dynasty") —the last imperial dynasty—surrendered to [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Third_Republic "French Third Republic") in 1883. In 1887, its territory was integrated into [French Indochina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Indochina "French Indochina") as three separate regions. In the immediate [aftermath of World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aftermath_of_World_War_II "Aftermath of World War II"), the communist-led [Viet Minh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viet_Minh "Viet Minh") coalition front launched the [August Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/August_Revolution "August Revolution") and [declared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Declaration_of_independence_of_the_Democratic_Republic_of_Vietnam "Declaration of independence of the Democratic Republic of Vietnam") Vietnam's independence from the [Empire of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Japan "Empire of Japan") in 1945.

Vietnam went through [prolonged warfare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indochina_wars "Indochina wars") in the 20th century. After [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Indochina_in_World_War_II "French Indochina in World War II"), France returned to reassert colonial control in the [First Indochina War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Indochina_War "First Indochina War"), but was defeated in 1954 amid the global [Cold War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cold_War "Cold War"). As a result of the [accords signed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1954_Geneva_Conference "1954 Geneva Conference") between the Viet Minh and France, Vietnam was also separated into two parts. The [Vietnam War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam_War "Vietnam War") later broke out between the communist [North Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Vietnam "North Vietnam"), supported by the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union") and [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China_in_the_Vietnam_War "China in the Vietnam War"), and the anti-communist [South Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Vietnam "South Vietnam"), supported by the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_in_the_Vietnam_War "United States in the Vietnam War"). Upon the [North Vietnamese victory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_Saigon "Fall of Saigon") in 1975, Vietnam reunified as a [communist state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_state "Communist state") that self-designated as a [socialist state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist_state_\(communism\) "Socialist state (communism)") under the [Communist Party of Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Vietnam "Communist Party of Vietnam") (CPV) in 1976. An ineffective [planned economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planned_economy "Planned economy"), a trade embargo by the [West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_world "Western world"), and wars with [Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambodian%E2%80%93Vietnamese_War "Cambodian–Vietnamese War") and [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Vietnamese_War "Sino-Vietnamese War") crippled the country further. In 1986, the CPV launched [economic and political reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BB%95i_M%E1%BB%9Bi "Đổi Mới") similar to the [Chinese economic reform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reform_and_opening_up "Reform and opening up"), transforming the country to a [socialist-oriented market economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist-oriented_market_economy "Socialist-oriented market economy"). The reforms facilitated Vietnamese reintegration into the [global economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_economy "Global economy") and [politics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_politics "Global politics").

Vietnam is a [developing country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country "Developing country") with a lower-middle–income economy.[^26] It has [high levels of corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Vietnam "Corruption in Vietnam"), [censorship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Censorship_in_Vietnam "Censorship in Vietnam"), [environmental issues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_issues_in_Vietnam "Environmental issues in Vietnam"), and a poor [human rights record](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_Vietnam "Human rights in Vietnam"). It is part of international and intergovernmental institutions, including the [ASEAN](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ASEAN "ASEAN"), the [APEC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/APEC "APEC"), the [Non-Aligned Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-Aligned_Movement "Non-Aligned Movement"), the [OIF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_internationale_de_la_Francophonie "Organisation internationale de la Francophonie"), and the [WTO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Trade_Organization "World Trade Organization"). It has assumed a seat on the [United Nations Security Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council "United Nations Security Council") twice.

## Etymology

The name *Việt Nam* (pronounced [\[viə̂tˀ nāːm\]](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/Vietnamese "Help:IPA/Vietnamese"), [chữ Hán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ch%E1%BB%AF_H%C3%A1n "Chữ Hán"): [越南](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%B6%8A%E5%8D%97 "wikt:越南")), literally "Viet South", means per Vietnamese word order "Viet of the South";[^27] meanwhile, per [Classical Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Chinese "Classical Chinese") word order, "Việt Nam" ambiguously means either "South of the Viet" [^27] [^28] or "Viet people's Southern country".[^28]

A variation of the name, [Nanyue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanyue "Nanyue") (or Nam Việt, [南越](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E5%8D%97%E8%B6%8A "wikt:南越")), was first documented in the 2nd century BC.[^29] The term " [Việt](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Vi%E1%BB%87t "wikt:Việt") " (Yue) ([Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): [越](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%B6%8A "wikt:越"); [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Yuè*; [Cantonese Yale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantonese_Yale "Cantonese Yale"): *Yuht*; [Wade–Giles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wade%E2%80%93Giles "Wade–Giles"): *Yüeh <sup>4</sup>*; [Vietnamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_language "Vietnamese language"): *[Việt](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/Vi%E1%BB%87t "wikt:Việt")*) in [Early Middle Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_Middle_Chinese "Early Middle Chinese") was first written using the [logograph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Logogram "Logogram") "戉" for an axe (a homophone), in [oracle bone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oracle_bone "Oracle bone") and bronze inscriptions of the late [Shang dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shang_dynasty "Shang dynasty") (c. 1200 BC), and later as "越".[^30] At that time, it referred to a people or chieftain to the northwest of the Shang.[^31] In the early 8th century BC, a tribe on the middle [Yangtze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yangtze "Yangtze") were called the [Yangyue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yangyue "Yangyue"), a term later used for peoples further south.[^31] [^12]

Between the 7th and 4th centuries BC, 'Yue'/'Việt' referred to the [State of Yue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yue_\(state\) "Yue (state)") in the lower Yangtze basin and its people.[^30] [^31] From the 3rd century BC, the term was used for the non-Chinese populations of southern China and northern Vietnam, with particular ethnic groups called [Minyue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minyue "Minyue"), [Ouyue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ouyue "Ouyue"), Luoyue (Vietnamese: [Lạc Việt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%E1%BA%A1c_Vi%E1%BB%87t "Lạc Việt")), etc., collectively called the [Baiyue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baiyue "Baiyue") (Bách Việt, [Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_language "Chinese language"): [百越](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E7%99%BE%E8%B6%8A "wikt:百越"); [pinyin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pinyin "Pinyin"): *Bǎiyuè*; [Cantonese Yale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantonese_Yale "Cantonese Yale"): *Baak Yuet*; [Vietnamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_language "Vietnamese language"): *Bách Việt*; [lit.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literal_translation "Literal translation") 'Hundred Yue/Viet').[^30] [^31] [^33]

The term 'Baiyue'/'Bách Việt' first appeared in the book *[Lüshi Chunqiu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%BCshi_Chunqiu "Lüshi Chunqiu")* compiled around 239 BC.[^34] By the 17th and 18th centuries AD, educated Vietnamese apparently referred to themselves as *người Việt* (Viet people) or *người Nam* (southern people).[^35]

Người Việt 𠊛越 written in chữ Nôm

The form *Việt Nam* ([越南](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E8%B6%8A%E5%8D%97 "wikt:越南")) is first recorded in the 16th-century oracular poem *[Sấm Trạng Trình](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%E1%BA%A5m_Tr%E1%BA%A1ng_Tr%C3%ACnh "Sấm Trạng Trình")*. The name has also been found on 12 [steles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stele "Stele") carved in the 16th and 17th centuries, including one at Bao Lam Pagoda in [Hải Phòng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haiphong "Haiphong") that dates to 1558.[^36] In 1802, [Nguyễn Phúc Ánh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_Ph%C3%BAc_%C3%81nh "Nguyễn Phúc Ánh") (who later became Emperor Gia Long) established the [Nguyễn dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_dynasty "Nguyễn dynasty"). In the second year of his rule, Gia Long asked the [Jiaqing Emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jiaqing_Emperor "Jiaqing Emperor") of the [Qing dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty") to confer on him the title 'King of Nam Việt / Nanyue' (南越 in Chinese character) after seizing power in Annam. Emperor Jiaqing refused because the name was related to [Zhao Tuo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhao_Tuo "Zhao Tuo") 's Nanyue, which included the regions of [Guangxi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangxi "Guangxi") and [Guangdong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guangdong "Guangdong") in southern China, so he decided to call the area "Việt Nam" instead,[^29] [^37] [^38] meaning per [Classical Chinese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Chinese "Classical Chinese") word order "land to the south of the Viet land" ([Vietnamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_language "Vietnamese language"): *vùng đất phía Nam đất Việt*) but the Vietnamese just understood "Việt Nam" as either, also per Classical Chinese word-order, "Viet people's Southern country" (Vietnamese: *nước Nam của người Việt*) [^28] or even "Viet of the South", per Vietnamese word order.[^27] Between 1804 and 1813, the name Vietnam was used officially by Emperor Gia Long. It was revived in the early 20th century in [Phan Bội Châu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phan_B%E1%BB%99i_Ch%C3%A2u "Phan Bội Châu") 's *[History of the Loss of Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_Loss_of_Vietnam "History of the Loss of Vietnam")*, and later by the [Vietnamese Nationalist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vi%E1%BB%87t_Nam_Qu%E1%BB%91c_D%C3%A2n_%C4%90%E1%BA%A3ng "Việt Nam Quốc Dân Đảng") (VNQDĐ).[^39] The country was usually called Annam until 1945, when the [imperial government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Vietnam "Empire of Vietnam") in [Huế](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hu%E1%BA%BF "Huế") adopted *Việt Nam*.[^40]

## History

### Prehistory and early history

Archaeological excavations have revealed the existence of humans in what is now Vietnam as early as the [Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleolithic "Paleolithic") age. Stone artefacts excavated in [Gia Lai province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gia_Lai_province "Gia Lai province") have been claimed to date to 780,000 years ago,[^41] based on associated find of [tektites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tektite "Tektite"), however this claim has been challenged because tektites are often found in archaeological sites of various ages in Vietnam.[^42] *[Homo erectus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_erectus "Homo erectus")* fossils dating to around 500,000 BC have been found in caves in [Lạng Sơn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%E1%BA%A1ng_S%C6%A1n_province "Lạng Sơn province") and [Nghệ An](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ngh%E1%BB%87_An_province "Nghệ An province") provinces in northern Vietnam.[^43] The oldest *[Homo sapiens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Homo_sapiens "Homo sapiens")* fossils from [Mainland Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_Southeast_Asia "Mainland Southeast Asia") are of [Middle Pleistocene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Pleistocene "Middle Pleistocene") provenance, and include isolated tooth fragments from Tham Om and Hang Hum.[^44] [^45] [^46] Teeth attributed to *Homo sapiens* from the [Late Pleistocene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_Pleistocene "Late Pleistocene") have been found at Dong Can,[^47] and from the Early [Holocene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holocene "Holocene") at Mai Da Dieu,[^48] [^49] Lang Gao [^50] [^51] and Lang Cuom.[^52] Areas comprising what is now Vietnam participated in the Maritime Jade Road, as ascertained by archaeological research.[^53] [^54] [^55] [^56]

By about 1,000 BC, the development of wet- [rice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rice "Rice") cultivation in the [Ma River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ma_River "Ma River") and [Red River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_River_\(Asia\) "Red River (Asia)") floodplains led to the flourishing of [Đông Sơn culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%C3%B4ng_S%C6%A1n_culture "Đông Sơn culture"),[^57] [^58] notable for its [bronze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze "Bronze") casting used to make elaborate bronze [Đông Sơn drums](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%C3%B4ng_S%C6%A1n_drums "Đông Sơn drums").[^59] [^60] [^61] This culture spread to the rest of [Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia "Southeast Asia"), including [Maritime Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_Southeast_Asia "Maritime Southeast Asia"), throughout the first millennium BC.[^60] [^62]

### Dynastic Vietnam

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4e/Southeast_Asian_history_-_13th_century.png/250px-Southeast_Asian_history_-_13th_century.png)

Đại Việt, Champa Angkor Empire and their neighbours, late 13th century

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9c/Nguyen_Dynasty%2C_administrative_divisions_map_%281838%29.svg/250px-Nguyen_Dynasty%2C_administrative_divisions_map_%281838%29.svg.png)

Vietnam's territories around 1838, during the Vietnamese occupation of Cambodia

According to Vietnamese legends, the [Hồng Bàng dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%E1%BB%93ng_B%C3%A0ng_dynasty "Hồng Bàng dynasty") of the [Hùng kings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%C3%B9ng_kings "Hùng kings"), first established in 2879 BC, is considered the first state established in Vietnam (then known as Xích Quỷ and later [Văn Lang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/V%C4%83n_Lang "Văn Lang")).[^63] Văn Lang was established by [Lạc Việt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%E1%BA%A1c_Vi%E1%BB%87t "Lạc Việt") tribes,[^64] who were likely a confederacy of multilingual [Austroasiatic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austroasiatic_languages "Austroasiatic languages") and [Kra-Dai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kra-Dai_languages "Kra-Dai languages") speakers who occupied the [Red River Delta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_River_Delta "Red River Delta") in northern Vietnam.[^65] [^66]

In 257 BC, the last Hùng king was defeated by Thục Phán. He consolidated the [Lạc Việt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%E1%BA%A1c_Vi%E1%BB%87t "Lạc Việt") and [Âu Việt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%82u_Vi%E1%BB%87t "Âu Việt") tribes, who came from southern China,[^67] to form the [Âu Lạc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%82u_L%E1%BA%A1c "Âu Lạc"), proclaiming himself [An Dương Vương](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/An_D%C6%B0%C6%A1ng_V%C6%B0%C6%A1ng "An Dương Vương").[^68]

In 179 BC, a Chinese general named [Zhao Tuo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhao_Tuo "Zhao Tuo") (Triệu Đà) defeated An Dương Vương and consolidated Âu Lạc into [Nanyue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanyue "Nanyue").[^58] However, Nanyue was itself [incorporated into the empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southward_expansion_of_the_Han_dynasty "Southward expansion of the Han dynasty") of the Chinese [Han dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_dynasty "Han dynasty") in 111 BC after the [Han–Nanyue War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han%E2%80%93Nanyue_War "Han–Nanyue War").[^37] [^69] Through Han rule, [Confucianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confucianism "Confucianism") was introduced into the region and came to influence Vietnamese societal norms and governance.[^70] For the next thousand years, what is now northern Vietnam remained mostly under [Chinese rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam_under_Chinese_rule "Vietnam under Chinese rule").[^71] [^72] [^73] Early independence movements, such as those of the [Trưng Sisters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tr%C6%B0ng_Sisters "Trưng Sisters") and [Lady Triệu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lady_Tri%E1%BB%87u "Lady Triệu"),[^74] were temporarily successful,[^75] though the region gained a longer period of independence as Vạn Xuân under the [Anterior Lý dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anterior_L%C3%BD_dynasty "Anterior Lý dynasty") between AD 544 and 602.[^76] [^77] [^78] By the early 10th century, northern Vietnam had gained autonomy, but not sovereignty, under the [Khúc family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kh%C3%BAc_family "Khúc family").[^79]

In AD 938, the Vietnamese lord [Ngô Quyền](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ng%C3%B4_Quy%E1%BB%81n "Ngô Quyền") defeated the forces of the Chinese [Southern Han](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Han "Southern Han") state at [Bạch Đằng River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_B%E1%BA%A1ch_%C4%90%E1%BA%B1ng_\(938\) "Battle of Bạch Đằng (938)") and achieved full independence for Vietnam in 939 after a millennium of Chinese domination.[^80] [^81] [^82] By the 960s, the dynastic [Đại Việt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i_Vi%E1%BB%87t "Đại Việt") (*Great Viet*) kingdom was established, and Vietnamese society enjoyed a golden era under the Lý and [Trần](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tr%E1%BA%A7n_dynasty "Trần dynasty") dynasties. During the rule of the Trần dynasty, Đại Việt repelled three [Mongol invasions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_invasions_of_Vietnam "Mongol invasions of Vietnam").[^83] [^84] Meanwhile, the [Mahāyāna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mah%C4%81y%C4%81na "Mahāyāna") branch of [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism_in_Vietnam "Buddhism in Vietnam") flourished and became the state religion.[^82] [^85] Following the 1406–7 [Ming–Hồ War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming%E2%80%93H%E1%BB%93_War "Ming–Hồ War"), which overthrew the [Hồ dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%E1%BB%93_dynasty "Hồ dynasty"), Vietnamese independence was [interrupted briefly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fourth_Chinese_domination_of_Vietnam "Fourth Chinese domination of Vietnam") by the Chinese [Ming dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ming_dynasty "Ming dynasty"), but was restored by [Lê Lợi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%AA_L%E1%BB%A3i "Lê Lợi"), the founder of the [Lê dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%AA_dynasty "Lê dynasty").[^86] The Vietnamese polity reached their zenith in the Lê dynasty of the 15th century, especially during the reign of emperor [Lê Thánh Tông](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%AA_Th%C3%A1nh_T%C3%B4ng "Lê Thánh Tông") (1460–1497).[^87] [^88] Between the 11th and 18th centuries, the Vietnamese polity expanded southward in a gradual process known as *[Nam tiến](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nam_ti%E1%BA%BFn "Nam tiến")* ("Southward expansion"),[^89] eventually conquering the kingdom of [Champa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Champa "Champa") and part of the [Khmer Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Post-Angkor_Period "Post-Angkor Period").[^90] [^91] [^92]

From the 16th century onward, civil strife and frequent political infighting engulfed much of Đại Việt. First, the Chinese-supported [Mạc dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%E1%BA%A1c_dynasty "Mạc dynasty") challenged the Lê dynasty's power.[^93] After the Mạc dynasty was defeated, the Lê dynasty was nominally reinstalled. Actual power, however, was divided between the northern [Trịnh lords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tr%E1%BB%8Bnh_lords "Trịnh lords") and the southern [Nguyễn lords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_lords "Nguyễn lords"), who engaged in a [civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tr%E1%BB%8Bnh%E2%80%93Nguy%E1%BB%85n_War "Trịnh–Nguyễn War") for more than four decades before a truce was called in the 1670s.[^94] Vietnam was divided into North (Trịnh) and South (Nguyễn) from 1600 to 1777. During this period, the Nguyễn expanded into the [Mekong Delta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mekong_Delta "Mekong Delta"), annexing the [Central Highlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Highlands_\(Vietnam\) "Central Highlands (Vietnam)") and the Khmer lands in the Mekong Delta.[^90] [^92] [^95] The division of the country ended a century later when the [Tây Sơn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T%C3%A2y_S%C6%A1n "Tây Sơn") brothers helped Trịnh to end Nguyễn; they also established a new dynasty and ended Trịnh. However, their rule did not last long, and they were defeated by the remnants of the Nguyễn lords, led by [Nguyễn Ánh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gia_Long "Gia Long"). Nguyễn Ánh unified Vietnam, and established the [Nguyễn dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_dynasty "Nguyễn dynasty"), ruling under the name Gia Long.[^95]

### French Indochina

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/ce/Prise_de_Saigon_18_Fevrier_1859_Antoine_Morel-Fatio.jpg/330px-Prise_de_Saigon_18_Fevrier_1859_Antoine_Morel-Fatio.jpg)

Capture of Saigon by Charles Rigault de Genouilly on 18 February 1859

![Photograph of the Grand Palais building in Hanoi](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/05/Exposition_Hano%C3%AF_1902_-_Grand_Palais.jpg/330px-Exposition_Hano%C3%AF_1902_-_Grand_Palais.jpg)

The Grand Palais built for the 1902–1903 world's fair, when Hanoi became French Indochina's capital

In the 1500s, the [Portuguese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Empire "Portuguese Empire") explored the Vietnamese coast and reportedly erected a [stele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stele "Stele") on the [Chàm Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ch%C3%A0m_Islands "Chàm Islands") to mark their presence.[^96] By 1533, they began landing in the Vietnamese delta but were forced to leave because of local turmoil and fighting. They also had less interest in the territory than they did in China and Japan.[^96] After they had settled in [Macau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Portuguese_Macau "Portuguese Macau") and [Nagasaki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecclesiastical_Nagasaki "Ecclesiastical Nagasaki") to begin the profitable Macau–Japan trade route, the Portuguese began to involve themselves in trade with [Hội An](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%E1%BB%99i_An_\(city\) "Hội An (city)").[^96] Portuguese traders and [Jesuit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesuits "Jesuits") missionaries under the *[Padroado](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Padroado "Padroado")* system were active in both Vietnamese realms of *[Đàng Trong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%C3%A0ng_Trong "Đàng Trong")* ([Cochinchina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cochinchina "Cochinchina")) and *[Đàng Ngoài](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%C3%A0ng_Ngo%C3%A0i "Đàng Ngoài")* ([Tonkin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonkin "Tonkin")) in the 17th century.[^97] The [Dutch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_colonial_empire "Dutch colonial empire") also tried to establish contact with Cochinchina in 1601 but failed to sustain a presence there after several violent encounters with the locals. The [Dutch East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_East_India_Company "Dutch East India Company") only managed to establish official relations with Tonkin in the spring of 1637 after leaving [Dejima](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dejima "Dejima") in Japan to establish trade for silk.[^98] Meanwhile, in 1613, the first [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_England "Kingdom of England") attempt to establish contact with Hội An failed following a violent incident involving the [East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_India_Company "East India Company"). By 1672, the English did establish relations with Tonkin and were allowed to reside in [Phố Hiến](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%C6%B0ng_Y%C3%AAn "Hưng Yên").[^99]

Between 1615 and 1753, French traders also engaged in trade in Vietnam.[^100] [^101] The first French missionaries arrived in 1658, under the Portuguese *Padroado*. From its foundation, the [Paris Foreign Missions Society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_Foreign_Missions_Society "Paris Foreign Missions Society") under [*Propaganda Fide*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congregation_for_the_Evangelization_of_Peoples "Congregation for the Evangelization of Peoples") actively sent missionaries to Vietnam, entering Cochinchina in 1664 and Tonkin in 1666.[^102] Spanish [Dominicans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominican_Order "Dominican Order") joined the Tonkin mission in 1676, and [Franciscans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franciscans "Franciscans") were in Cochinchina from 1719 to 1834. Vietnamese authorities became concerned by increasing [Christianisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianization "Christianization") activities,[^103] and after several Catholic missionaries were detained, the French Navy intervened in 1843 to free them.[^104] In a series of conquests from 1859 to 1885, France eroded Vietnam's sovereignty.[^105] At the [siege of Tourane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siege_of_Tourane "Siege of Tourane") in 1858, France was aided by Spain (with Spanish and Filipino troops from the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Captaincy_General_of_the_Philippines "Captaincy General of the Philippines")) [^106] and perhaps some Tonkinese Catholics.[^107] After the [1862 Treaty of Saigon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Saigon_\(1862\) "Treaty of Saigon (1862)"), and especially after France completely conquered [Lower Cochinchina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Vietnam "Southern Vietnam") in 1867, the [Văn Thân movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/V%C4%83n_Th%C3%A2n_movement "Văn Thân movement") of scholar-gentry class arose and committed violence against Catholics across central and northern Vietnam.[^108]

Between 1862 and 1867, the southern third of the country became the [French colony of Cochinchina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Cochinchina "French Cochinchina").[^109] By 1884, the entire country was under French rule, with the central and northern parts of Vietnam separated into the two protectorates of [Annam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annam_\(French_protectorate\) "Annam (French protectorate)") and [Tonkin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonkin_\(French_protectorate\) "Tonkin (French protectorate)"). The three entities were formally integrated into the union of [French Indochina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Indochina "French Indochina") in 1887.[^110] [^111] During this period, the French administration imposed significant political and cultural changes on Vietnamese society.[^112] A Western-style system of modern education introduced new [humanist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanism "Humanism") values.[^113] Most French settlers in Indochina were concentrated in Cochinchina, particularly in [Saigon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_City "Ho Chi Minh City"), and in [Hanoi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanoi "Hanoi"), the colony's capital.[^114]

During the early colonial period, guerrillas of the royalist [Cần Vương movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%E1%BA%A7n_V%C6%B0%C6%A1ng_movement "Cần Vương movement") rebelled against French rule and massacred around a third of [Vietnam's Christian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity_in_Vietnam "Christianity in Vietnam") population.[^115] Anti-Catholic violence persisted in [Nam Định](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nam_%C4%90%E1%BB%8Bnh "Nam Định"), [Quảng Trị](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_Tr%E1%BB%8B "Quảng Trị"), and [Bình Định](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B%C3%ACnh_%C4%90%E1%BB%8Bnh "Bình Định") during the 1880s.[^116] The French strategy for pacification in Vietnam relied more on alliances with local notables than on Christian missions to establish control and manage resistance.[^117] The French developed a [plantation economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plantation_economy "Plantation economy") to promote export of tobacco, [indigo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indigo_dye "Indigo dye"), tea and coffee.[^118] However, they largely ignored the increasing demands for civil rights and [self-governance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-governance "Self-governance"). An increasing dissatisfaction even led to half-hearted, badly coordinated, and still worse executed plots to oust the French, such as the [Hanoi Poison Plot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanoi_Poison_Plot "Hanoi Poison Plot") of 1908 and the [Thái Nguyên uprising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Th%C3%A1i_Nguy%C3%AAn_uprising "Thái Nguyên uprising") of 1917.[^119]

A nationalist political movement soon emerged, with leaders like [Phan Bội Châu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phan_B%E1%BB%99i_Ch%C3%A2u "Phan Bội Châu"), [Phan Châu Trinh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phan_Ch%C3%A2u_Trinh "Phan Châu Trinh"), [Phan Đình Phùng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phan_%C4%90%C3%ACnh_Ph%C3%B9ng "Phan Đình Phùng"), Emperor [Hàm Nghi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%C3%A0m_Nghi "Hàm Nghi"), and [Ho Chi Minh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh "Ho Chi Minh") fighting or calling for independence.[^120] This resulted in the 1930 [Yên Bái mutiny](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Y%C3%AAn_B%C3%A1i_mutiny "Yên Bái mutiny") by the [Vietnamese Nationalist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vi%E1%BB%87t_Nam_Qu%E1%BB%91c_D%C3%A2n_%C4%90%E1%BA%A3ng "Việt Nam Quốc Dân Đảng") (VNQDĐ), which the French quashed. The mutiny split the independence movement, as many leading members converted to [communism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communism "Communism").[^121] [^122] [^123]

The French maintained full control of their colonies until World War II, when the [war in the Pacific](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_War "Pacific War") led to the [Japanese invasion of French Indochina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_invasion_of_French_Indochina "Japanese invasion of French Indochina") in 1940. Afterwards, the [Japanese Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Japan "Empire of Japan") was allowed to station its troops in Vietnam while the pro- [Vichy French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vichy_France "Vichy France") colonial administration continued.[^124] [^125] Japan exploited Vietnam's natural resources to support its military campaigns, culminating in a [full-scale takeover of the country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Japanese_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_in_French_Indochina "Japanese coup d'état in French Indochina") in March 1945. This led to the [Vietnamese famine of 1944–1945](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_famine_of_1944%E2%80%931945 "Vietnamese famine of 1944–1945"), which killed up to two million people.[^126] [^127]

### First Indochina War

In 1941, the [Việt Minh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viet_Minh "Viet Minh"), a communist-led national liberation movement, emerged under [Ho Chi Minh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh "Ho Chi Minh") 's leadership.[^128] [^129] Mass political mobilization intensified during the [Japanese military occupation of Indochina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Indochina_in_World_War_II "French Indochina in World War II"),[^130] and nationalist parties fostered a stronger sense of national identity.[^131] After Japan's defeat in World War II, the Việt Minh seized Hanoi and Huế in August 1945, dissolved the [Empire of Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Vietnam "Empire of Vietnam"), and established a provisional government that declared national independence on 2 September.[^129] In the south, Saigon's administrative services collapsed, and chaos, riots, and murder were widespread.[^132]

In August 1945, the [Allies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II "Allies of World War II") had decided to divide Indochina at the [16th parallel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/16th_parallel_north "16th parallel north") to allow [Chiang Kai-shek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiang_Kai-shek "Chiang Kai-shek") of the [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") to [receive the Japanese surrender in the north](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_occupation_of_northern_Vietnam,_1945%E2%80%931946 "Chinese occupation of northern Vietnam, 1945–1946") while Britain's [Lord Louis Mountbatten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Mountbatten,_1st_Earl_Mountbatten_of_Burma "Louis Mountbatten, 1st Earl Mountbatten of Burma") received their surrender in the south. The Allies agreed that Indochina still belonged to France.[^133] [^134]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/60/French_Indochina_post_partition.png/250px-French_Indochina_post_partition.png)

Former French Indochina after the 1954 Geneva Conference, with Vietnam partitioned along the 17th parallel.

But as the French were weakened by the [German occupation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_military_administration_in_occupied_France_during_World_War_II "German military administration in occupied France during World War II"), [British-Indian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Raj "British Raj") forces and the remaining Japanese [Southern Expeditionary Army Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Expeditionary_Army_Group "Southern Expeditionary Army Group") were used to maintain order and help France reestablish control through the [1945–1946 War in Vietnam, south of the 16th parallel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_in_southern_Vietnam_\(1945%E2%80%931946\) "War in southern Vietnam (1945–1946)").[^135] The Viet Minh sought to consolidate power by terrorizing and purging rival Vietnamese [nationalist groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_nationalism "Vietnamese nationalism") and [Trotskyist leaders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trotskyism_in_Vietnam "Trotskyism in Vietnam").[^136] [^137] [^138] [^139] In 1946, the Franco-Chinese and [Ho–Sainteny Agreements](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ho%E2%80%93Sainteny_Agreement "Ho–Sainteny Agreement") enabled French forces to replace the Chinese north of the 16th parallel and facilitated a coexistence between the DRV and the French that strengthened the Viet Minh while undermining the nationalists.[^140] [^141] That summer, the Viet Minh, in collaboration with French forces, eliminated rival nationalist parties.[^142] [^143] [^144] [^145] Hồ chose to take a compromising stance to avoid military conflict with France, asking the French to withdraw their colonial administrators and for French professors and engineers to help build a modern independent Vietnam.[^129] But [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provisional_Government_of_the_French_Republic "Provisional Government of the French Republic") did not act on these requests, including the idea of independence, and dispatched the [French Far East Expeditionary Corps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Far_East_Expeditionary_Corps "French Far East Expeditionary Corps") to restore colonial rule. With negotiations having broken down,[^146] tensions between the Viet Minh and French authorities erupted into the full-scale [First Indochina War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Indochina_War "First Indochina War") in December 1946.[^128] [^129] [^147] Surviving nationalist partisans and politico-religious groups rallied behind the exiled [Bảo Đại](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B%E1%BA%A3o_%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i "Bảo Đại") to reopen negotiations with [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Fourth_Republic "French Fourth Republic"), ultimately signing the [Élysée Accords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%89lys%C3%A9e_Accords "Élysée Accords") and establishing the [State of Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_Vietnam "State of Vietnam") in opposition to communist domination.[^148] The defeat of [French Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Union "French Union") forces in the 1954 [Battle of Điện Biên Phủ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_%C4%90i%E1%BB%87n_Bi%C3%AAn_Ph%E1%BB%A7 "Battle of Điện Biên Phủ") allowed Hồ to negotiate a ceasefire from a favourable position at the subsequent [Geneva Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1954_Geneva_Conference "1954 Geneva Conference").[^129] [^149]

The Geneva Accords of 21 July 1954 ended the colonial fighting and affirmed the independence of [Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Cambodia_\(1953%E2%80%931970\) "Kingdom of Cambodia (1953–1970)"), [Laos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Laos "Kingdom of Laos"), and Vietnam, while placing Vietnam under a temporary North–South division along the [Demilitarized Zone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_Demilitarized_Zone "Vietnamese Demilitarized Zone"), roughly following the [17th parallel north](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/17th_parallel_north "17th parallel north") (pending elections scheduled for July 1956).[^13] 300 days of free movement were permitted, during which almost a million northerners, including at least 500,000 Catholics and approximately 200,000 Buddhists, moved south,[^154] fearing persecution by the communists. This migration was in large part aided by the United States military through [Operation Passage to Freedom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Passage_to_Freedom "Operation Passage to Freedom").[^155] [^156] The [partition of Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_Vietnam "Partition of Vietnam") by the Geneva Accords was not intended to be permanent, and stipulated that Vietnam would be reunited after the elections.[^157] In July 1955, the State of Vietnam's prime minister [Ngô Đình Diệm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ngo_Dinh_Diem "Ngo Dinh Diem") announced in a broadcast that South Vietnam would not participate in the elections, as they had not signed the accords and therefore were not bound by them.[^158] [^159] In October 1955, Ngô Đình Diệm toppled [Bảo Đại](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/B%E1%BA%A3o_%C4%90%E1%BA%A1i "Bảo Đại") in a fraudulent [referendum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1955_State_of_Vietnam_referendum "1955 State of Vietnam referendum") organised by his brother [Ngô Đình Nhu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ng%C3%B4_%C4%90%C3%ACnh_Nhu "Ngô Đình Nhu"), and proclaimed himself president of the [Republic of Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Vietnam "Republic of Vietnam").[^157]

### Vietnam War

From 1953 to 1956, the [North Vietnamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Vietnam "North Vietnam") government instituted [agrarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agrarianism "Agrarianism") reforms including " [rent reduction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rent_regulation "Rent regulation") " and " [land reform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_reform_in_North_Vietnam "Land reform in North Vietnam") ", which resulted in significant [political repression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_repression "Political repression").[^160] This included 13,500 to as many as 100,000 executions.[^161] [^162] In the South, Diệm countered North Vietnamese subversion (including the assassination of over 450 South Vietnamese officials in 1956) by detaining tens of thousands of suspected communists in "political reeducation centres".[^163] [^164] This programme incarcerated many non-communists, but was successful at curtailing [communist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communism "Communism") activity in the country, if only for a time.[^165] The North Vietnamese government claimed that 2,148 people were killed in the process by November 1957.[^166] The pro-Hanoi [Viet Cong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Viet_Cong "Viet Cong") began a guerrilla campaign in [South Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Vietnam "South Vietnam") in the late 1950s to overthrow Diệm's government.[^167] From 1960, the Soviet Union and North Vietnam signed treaties providing for further Soviet military support.[^168] [^169] [^170] The Republic of Vietnam in the south was supported by the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States"), [Australia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia "Australia"), [South Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Korea "South Korea"), and [Thailand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thailand "Thailand"), while the Democratic Republic of Vietnam in the north was supported by the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union"), [People's Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China"),[^157] [Khmer Rouge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khmer_Rouge "Khmer Rouge"), and later, [Sweden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweden "Sweden").[^171]

![Three US Fairchild UC-123B aircraft pictured spraying Agent Orange](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fb/Agent_Orange_Cropdusting.jpg/250px-Agent_Orange_Cropdusting.jpg)

Three US Fairchild UC-123B aircraft spraying Agent Orange during the Operation Ranch Hand as part of a herbicidal warfare operation depriving the food and vegetation cover of the Việt Cộng, c. 1962–1971

In 1963, Buddhist discontent with Diệm's perceived pro-Catholic bias erupted into [mass demonstrations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhist_crisis "Buddhist crisis"), leading to a violent government crackdown.[^172] This led to the [collapse of Diệm's relationship with the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cable_243 "Cable 243"), and ultimately to a [1963 coup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1963_South_Vietnamese_coup "1963 South Vietnamese coup") in which [he and Nhu were assassinated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arrest_and_assassination_of_Ng%C3%B4_%C4%90%C3%ACnh_Di%E1%BB%87m "Arrest and assassination of Ngô Đình Diệm").[^173] The Diệm era was followed by more than a dozen successive military governments, before the pairing of Air Marshal [Nguyễn Cao Kỳ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_Cao_K%E1%BB%B3 "Nguyễn Cao Kỳ") and General [Nguyễn Văn Thiệu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_V%C4%83n_Thi%E1%BB%87u "Nguyễn Văn Thiệu") took control in mid-1965.[^174] Thiệu gradually outmaneuvered Kỳ and cemented his grip on power in fraudulent elections in 1967 and 1971.[^175] During this political instability, the communists began to gain ground. To support South Vietnam's struggle against the communist insurgency, the United States used the 1964 [Gulf of Tonkin incident](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Tonkin_incident "Gulf of Tonkin incident") as a pretext for increasing its contribution of military advisers.[^176] US forces became involved in ground combat operations by 1965, and at their peak several years later, numbered more than 500,000.[^177] [^178] The US also engaged in [sustained aerial bombing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Rolling_Thunder "Operation Rolling Thunder"). Meanwhile, China and the Soviet Union provided North Vietnam with significant material aid and 15,000 combat advisers.[^168] [^169] [^179] Communist forces supplying the Việt Cộng carried supplies along the [Ho Chi Minh trail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ho_Chi_Minh_trail "Ho Chi Minh trail"), which passed through [Laos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Laos "Kingdom of Laos").[^180]

The communists attacked South Vietnamese targets during the 1968 [Tết Offensive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T%E1%BA%BFt_Offensive "Tết Offensive"). The campaign failed militarily, but shocked the American establishment and turned US public opinion against the war.[^181] During the offensive, communist troops [massacred over 3,000 civilians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massacre_at_Hu%E1%BA%BF "Massacre at Huế") at [Huế](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hu%E1%BA%BF "Huế").[^182] [^183] Facing an increasing casualty count, [rising domestic opposition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opposition_to_United_States_involvement_in_the_Vietnam_War "Opposition to United States involvement in the Vietnam War") to the war, and growing international condemnation, the US began [withdrawing from ground combat roles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nixon_Doctrine "Nixon Doctrine") in the early 1970s. This also entailed an unsuccessful effort to [strengthen and stabilise South Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamisation "Vietnamisation").[^184] Following the [Paris Peace Accords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paris_Peace_Accords "Paris Peace Accords") of 27 January 1973, all American combat troops were withdrawn by 29 March 1973.[^185] In December 1974, North Vietnam [captured](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Ph%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Bc_Long "Battle of Phước Long") the province of [Phước Long](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ph%C6%B0%E1%BB%9Bc_Long_province "Phước Long province") and started a [full-scale offensive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1975_Spring_Offensive "1975 Spring Offensive"), culminating in the [fall of Saigon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_Saigon "Fall of Saigon") on 30 April 1975.[^186] South Vietnam was ruled by a [provisional government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provisional_Revolutionary_Government_of_the_Republic_of_South_Vietnam "Provisional Revolutionary Government of the Republic of South Vietnam") for 14 months under North Vietnamese control.[^187]

### Reunification and reforms

On 2 July 1976, North and South Vietnam were merged to form the Socialist Republic of Vietnam.[^188] The war had devastated Vietnam and killed 966,000 to 3.8 million people.[^189] [^190] [^191] A 1974 US Senate subcommittee estimated nearly 1.4 million [Vietnamese civilians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnam_War_casualties "Vietnam War casualties") were killed or wounded between 1965 and 1974—including 415,000 killed.[^192] [^193] In its aftermath, under [Lê Duẩn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%AA_Du%E1%BA%A9n "Lê Duẩn") 's administration, there were no mass executions of South Vietnamese who had served the defunct South Vietnamese government, confounding Western fears,[^194] but up to 300,000 South Vietnamese were sent to [reeducation camps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Re-education_camp_\(Vietnam\) "Re-education camp (Vietnam)"), where many endured torture, starvation, and disease while being forced to perform hard labour.[^195] The government embarked on a mass campaign of [collectivisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collectivisation "Collectivisation") of farms and factories.[^196] [Millions fled](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indochina_refugee_crisis "Indochina refugee crisis") the country in the aftermath of the war and during the [subsidy period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subsidy_period "Subsidy period").[^197] In 1978, in response to the [Khmer Rouge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khmer_Rouge "Khmer Rouge") government of Cambodia ordering massacres of Vietnamese residents in the border villages in the districts of [An Giang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/An_Giang_province "An Giang province") and [Kiên Giang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ki%C3%AAn_Giang_province "Kiên Giang province"),[^198] the Vietnamese military [invaded Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cambodian%E2%80%93Vietnamese_War "Cambodian–Vietnamese War") and removed them from power after occupying [Phnom Penh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phnom_Penh "Phnom Penh").[^199] The intervention was a success, resulting in the establishment of a new, pro-Vietnam socialist government, the [People's Republic of Kampuchea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People%27s_Republic_of_Kampuchea "People's Republic of Kampuchea"), which ruled until 1989.[^200] However, this worsened relations with China, which had supported the Khmer Rouge. China later launched a [brief incursion into northern Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sino-Vietnamese_War "Sino-Vietnamese War") in 1979, causing Vietnam to rely even more heavily on Soviet economic and military aid, while mistrust of the [Chinese government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_of_China "Government of China") escalated.[^201]

At the [Sixth National Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/6th_National_Congress_of_the_Communist_Party_of_Vietnam "6th National Congress of the Communist Party of Vietnam") of the [Communist Party of Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Vietnam "Communist Party of Vietnam") (CPV) in December 1986, reformist politicians replaced the "old guard" government with new leadership.[^202] [^203] The reformers were led by 71-year-old [Nguyễn Văn Linh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_V%C4%83n_Linh "Nguyễn Văn Linh"), who became the party's new general secretary.[^202] He and the reformers implemented a series of market reforms known as *[Đổi Mới](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%E1%BB%95i_M%E1%BB%9Bi "Đổi Mới")* ("Renovation") that carefully managed the transition from a [planned economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Planned_economy "Planned economy") to a " [socialist-oriented market economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist-oriented_market_economy "Socialist-oriented market economy") ".[^204] [^205] Although the authority of the state remained unchallenged under *Đổi Mới*, the government encouraged [private ownership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Private_ownership "Private ownership") of farms and factories, economic deregulation, and foreign investment, while maintaining control over strategic industries.[^205] [^206] While Vietnam's reforms catalysed rapid industrial and export growth, they also widened income and gender disparities.[^207] [^208] [^209]

In 2021, [General Secretary of the Communist Party of Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/General_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party_of_Vietnam "General Secretary of the Communist Party of Vietnam"), [Nguyễn Phú Trọng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nguy%E1%BB%85n_Ph%C3%BA_Tr%E1%BB%8Dng "Nguyễn Phú Trọng"), was re-elected for his third term in office, meaning he was Vietnam's most powerful leader in decades.[^210] He died 19 July 2024, and was followed by [Tô Lâm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T%C3%B4_L%C3%A2m "Tô Lâm") as General Secretary of the Communist Party.[^211]

## Geography

![Images showing Hạ Long Bay, the Yên River and the Bản-Giốc Waterfalls](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Geography_of_Vietnam.jpg/250px-Geography_of_Vietnam.jpg)

Natural attractions in Vietnam, clockwise from top: Hạ Long Bay, Yên River, and Bản-Giốc Waterfalls

![Image of the Hoàng Liên Sơn mountain range](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3b/I%27m_coming%2C_PXP.jpg/250px-I%27m_coming%2C_PXP.jpg)

Hoàng Liên Sơn mountain range, the range that includes Fansipan, which is the highest summit on the Indochinese Peninsula

Vietnam is located on the eastern [Indochinese Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_Southeast_Asia "Mainland Southeast Asia") between the latitudes [8°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/8th_parallel_north "8th parallel north") and [24°N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/24th_parallel_north "24th parallel north"), and the longitudes [102°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/102nd_meridian_east "102nd meridian east") and [110°E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/110th_meridian_east "110th meridian east"). It covers a total area of 331,210 km <sup>2</sup> (127,881 sq mi) [^14] or 331,699 km <sup>2</sup> (128,070 sq mi).[^20] The combined length of the country's land boundaries is 4,639 km (2,883 mi), and its coastline is 3,444 km (2,140 mi) long.[^212] At its narrowest point in the central [Quảng Bình province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qu%E1%BA%A3ng_B%C3%ACnh_province "Quảng Bình province"), the country is as little as 50 kilometres (31 mi) across, though it widens to around 600 kilometres (370 mi) in the north.[^213] Vietnam's land is mostly hilly and densely forested, with level land covering no more than 20%. Mountains account for 40% of the country's land area,[^214] and [tropical forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_forest "Tropical forest") cover around 42%.[^215] The [Red River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_River_\(Asia\) "Red River (Asia)") flows past Hanoi in Northern Vietnam before emptying into the [Gulf of Tonkin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Tonkin "Gulf of Tonkin"). The [Red River Delta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_River_Delta "Red River Delta") is a flat, roughly triangular low-level plain covering 15,000 km <sup>2</sup> (5,792 sq mi).[^216] It has the smallest area but the highest population and population density of all [regions in Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regions_of_Vietnam "Regions of Vietnam"). It is criss-crossed by a maze of rivers and canals, which carry so much [sediment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alluvium "Alluvium") that the delta advances 60 to 80 metres (196.9 to 262.5 ft) into the South China Sea every year.[^217] [^218] The [exclusive economic zone of Vietnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exclusive_economic_zone_of_Vietnam "Exclusive economic zone of Vietnam") covers 417,663 km <sup>2</sup> (161,261 sq mi) in the [South China Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_China_Sea "South China Sea").[^219]

Southern Vietnam is divided into coastal lowlands, the mountains of the [Annamite Range](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annamite_Range "Annamite Range"), and extensive forests. Comprising five relatively flat plateaus of [basalt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basalt "Basalt") soil, the highlands account for 16% of the country's [arable land](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arable_land "Arable land") and 22% of its total forested land.[^220] The soil in much of the southern part of Vietnam is relatively low in nutrients as a result of intense cultivation.[^221] Several minor earthquakes have been recorded.[^222] [^223] The northern part of the country consists mostly of highlands and the Red River Delta. [Fansipan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fansipan "Fansipan") ([Vietnamese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_language "Vietnamese language"): *Phan Xi Păng*), the summit of which is located on the border between [Lào Cai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A0o_Cai_province "Lào Cai province") and [Lai Châu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lai_Ch%C3%A2u_province "Lai Châu province") provinces, is the highest mountain on the Indochinese Peninsula, standing 3,143 m (10,312 ft) high.[^224] From north to south Vietnam, the country also has [numerous islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_islands_of_Vietnam "List of islands of Vietnam"); [Phú Quốc](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ph%C3%BA_Qu%E1%BB%91c "Phú Quốc") is the largest.[^225] [Ba Bể Lake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ba_B%E1%BB%83_Lake "Ba Bể Lake") is the largest natural lake in Vietnam, and the [Mekong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mekong "Mekong") River is the longest river in the country.[^226] [^227] [^228] [Hang Sơn Đoòng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hang_S%C6%A1n_%C4%90o%C3%B2ng "Hang Sơn Đoòng"), first surveyed in 2009 by an expedition from the [British Cave Research Association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Cave_Research_Association "British Cave Research Association"), is among the largest caves in the world.[^229] The cave is over 8.8 km (5.5 mi) in length, and some of its caverns are large enough to hold a 40-story skyscraper. It has an enormous [sinkhole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinkhole "Sinkhole"), more than 183 m (600 ft) in depth, beneath which grows a tropical rainforest containing trees nearly 30 m (98 ft) tall. This forest is home to monkeys and other animals not normally found in a subterranean environment. A [subterranean river](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subterranean_river "Subterranean river") also flows within the cave.[^230]

### Climate

![An image of the Köppen climate classification map of Vietnam](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d8/Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_VNM_1991%E2%80%932020.svg/250px-Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_VNM_1991%E2%80%932020.svg.png)

Köppen climate classification map of Vietnam

![Photograph of Nha Trang beach with many high rise buildings behind it](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/61/Nha_Trang_%2843635463040%29.jpg/250px-Nha_Trang_%2843635463040%29.jpg)

Nha Trang, a popular beach destination, has a tropical savanna climate

Due to differences in latitude and the marked variety in [topographical relief](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topographical_relief "Topographical relief"), Vietnam's climate tends to vary considerably for each region.[^231] During the winter or dry season, extending roughly from November to April, the [monsoon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsoon "Monsoon") winds usually blow from the northeast along the Chinese coast and across the Gulf of Tonkin, picking up considerable moisture.[^232] The average annual temperature is generally higher in the plains than in the mountains, especially in southern Vietnam compared to the north. The northern region experiences a monsoonal and humid subtropical climate with four distinct seasons (spring, summer, autumn, and winter), with winters typically dry and summers ranging from hot to mild.[^233] In southern and central areas, the climate is tropical monsoon with only two seasons (rainy and dry).[^234] [^235] [^236] Temperatures vary less in the southern plains around Ho Chi Minh City and the Mekong Delta, ranging from between 21 and 35 °C (70 and 95 °F) over the year.[^237] In Hanoi and the surrounding areas of the Red River Delta, the temperatures are much lower between 15 and 33 °C (59 and 91 °F).[^237] Seasonal variations in the mountains, plateaus, and the northernmost areas are much more dramatic, with temperatures varying from 3 °C (37 °F) in December and January to 37 °C (99 °F) in July and August.[^238] During winter, snow occasionally falls over the highest peaks of the far northern mountains near the Chinese border.[^239] Vietnam receives high rates of [precipitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precipitation "Precipitation") in the form of rainfall with an average amount from 1,500 to 2,000 mm (60 to 80 in) during the monsoon seasons; this often causes flooding, especially in the cities with poor drainage systems.[^240] The country is also affected by [tropical depressions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_depression "Tropical depression"), [tropical storms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_storm "Tropical storm") and [typhoons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Typhoon "Typhoon").[^240] Vietnam is one of the most vulnerable countries to climate change, with 55% of its population living in low-elevation coastal areas.[^241] [^242]

### Biodiversity

![Photographs of Native species in Vietnam, the crested argus; the red-shanked douc, a monkey; the Indochinese leopard and the saola, a bovine](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e9/Wildlife_of_Vietnam.jpg/250px-Wildlife_of_Vietnam.jpg)

Native species in Vietnam, clockwise from top-right: crested argus, Tonkin snub-nosed monkey Indochinese leopard, and saola

![Photograph of the Sa Pa mountain hills with agricultural activity shiwn in the foreground](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3a/Sa_Pa_mountain_hills_with_agricultural_activities.jpg/250px-Sa_Pa_mountain_hills_with_agricultural_activities.jpg)

Sa Pa mountain hills with agricultural activities

As the country is located within the [Indomalayan realm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indomalayan_realm "Indomalayan realm"), Vietnam is one of twenty-five countries considered to possess a uniquely high level of [biodiversity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity "Biodiversity"). This was noted in the country's National Environmental Condition Report in 2005.[^243] It is ranked 16th worldwide in biological diversity, being home to approximately 16% of the world's species. 15,986 species of [flora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flora "Flora") have been identified in the country, of which 10% are [endemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Endemism "Endemism"). Vietnam's [fauna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fauna "Fauna") includes 307 [nematode](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nematode "Nematode") species, 200 [oligochaeta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oligochaeta "Oligochaeta"), 145 [acarina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acarina "Acarina"), 113 [springtails](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Springtail "Springtail"), 7,750 insects, 260 reptiles, and 120 amphibians. There are 840 birds and 310 mammals found in Vietnam, of which 100 birds and 78 mammals are endemic.[^243] Vietnam has two [World Natural Heritage Sites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Natural_Heritage_Site "World Natural Heritage Site") —the [Hạ Long Bay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/H%E1%BA%A1_Long_Bay "Hạ Long Bay") and [Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phong_Nha-K%E1%BA%BB_B%C3%A0ng_National_Park "Phong Nha-Kẻ Bàng National Park") —together with nine [biosphere reserves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Network_of_Biosphere_Reserves_in_Asia_and_the_Pacific#Vietnam "World Network of Biosphere Reserves in Asia and the Pacific"), including [Cần Giờ Mangrove Forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%E1%BA%A7n_Gi%E1%BB%9D_Mangrove_Forest "Cần Giờ Mangrove Forest"), [Cát Tiên](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%A1t_Ti%C3%AAn_National_Park "Cát Tiên National Park"), [Cát Bà](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%C3%A1t_B%C3%A0_National_Park "Cát Bà National Park"), [Kiên Giang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/U_Minh_Th%C6%B0%E1%BB%A3ng_National_Park "U Minh Thượng National Park"), the Red River Delta, Mekong Delta, [Western Nghệ An](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Ngh%E1%BB%87_An "Western Nghệ An"), [Cà Mau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C5%A9i_C%C3%A0_Mau_National_Park "Mũi Cà Mau National Park"), and [Cu Lao Cham Marine Park](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cu_Lao_Cham_Marine_Park "Cu Lao Cham Marine Park").[^244] [^245]

Vietnam is also home to 1,438 species of freshwater [microalgae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microalgae "Microalgae"), constituting 9.6% of all microalgae species, as well as 794 aquatic [invertebrates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Invertebrate "Invertebrate") and 2,458 species of sea fish.[^243] In recent years, 13 [genera](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genera "Genera"), 222 species, and 30 [taxa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taxa "Taxa") of flora have been newly described in Vietnam.[^243] Six new mammal species, including the [saola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saola "Saola"), [giant muntjac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Giant_muntjac "Giant muntjac"), and [Tonkin snub-nosed monkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tonkin_snub-nosed_monkey "Tonkin snub-nosed monkey") have also been discovered, along with one new bird species, the endangered [Edwards's pheasant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edwards%27s_pheasant "Edwards's pheasant").[^246] In the late 1980s, a small population of [Javan rhinoceros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Javan_rhinoceros "Javan rhinoceros") was found in Cát Tiên National Park. However, the last individual of the species in Vietnam was reportedly shot in 2010.[^247] In agricultural [genetic diversity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetic_diversity "Genetic diversity"), Vietnam is one of the world's twelve original [cultivar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultivar "Cultivar") centres. The Vietnam National Cultivar Gene Bank preserves 12,300 cultivars of 115 species.[^243] The Vietnamese government spent US$49.07 million on the preservation of biodiversity in 2004 alone and has established 126 conservation areas, including 30 [national parks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_national_parks_of_Vietnam "List of national parks of Vietnam").[^243]

In Vietnam, wildlife [poaching](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poaching "Poaching") has become a major concern. In 2000, a [non-governmental organisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-governmental_organisation "Non-governmental organisation") (NGO), Education for Nature – Vietnam, was founded to instill in the population the importance of wildlife conservation in the country.[^248] In the years that followed, another NGO called GreenViet was formed by Vietnamese youngsters for the enforcement of wildlife protection. Through collaboration between the NGOs and local authorities, many local poaching syndicates were crippled by their leaders' arrests.[^248] A study released in 2018 revealed Vietnam is a destination for the illegal export of [rhinoceros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhinoceros "Rhinoceros") horns from [South Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Africa "South Africa") due to the demand for them as a medicine and a status symbol.[^249] [^250]

The main environmental concern that persists in Vietnam today is the legacy of the use of the chemical [herbicide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Herbicide "Herbicide") [Agent Orange](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agent_Orange "Agent Orange"), which continues to cause [birth defects](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Birth_defect "Birth defect") and many health problems in the Vietnamese population. In the southern and central areas affected most by the chemical's use during the Vietnam War, nearly 4.8 million Vietnamese people have been exposed to it and suffered from its effects.[^251] [^252] [^253] In 2012, approximately 50 years after the war,[^254] the US began a US$43 million joint clean-up project in the former chemical storage areas in Vietnam to take place in stages.[^252] [^255] Following the completion of the first phase in [Đà Nẵng](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%90%C3%A0_N%E1%BA%B5ng "Đà Nẵng") in late 2017,[^256] the US announced its commitment to clean other sites, especially in the heavily impacted site of [Biên Hòa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bi%C3%AAn_H%C3%B2a "Biên Hòa").[^257]

The Vietnamese government spends over [VNĐ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_%C4%91%E1%BB%93ng "Vietnamese đồng") 10 trillion each year ($431.1 million) for monthly allowances and the physical rehabilitation of victims of the chemicals.[^258] In 2018, the Japanese engineering group [Shimizu Corporation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shimizu_Corporation "Shimizu Corporation"), working with the Vietnamese military, built a plant for the treatment of soil polluted by Agent Orange. Plant construction costs were funded by the company itself.[^259] [^260] One of the long-term plans to restore southern Vietnam's damaged [ecosystems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecosystem "Ecosystem") is through the use of [reforestation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reforestation "Reforestation") efforts. The Vietnamese government began doing this at the end of the war. It started by replanting [mangrove forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mangrove_forest "Mangrove forest") in the Mekong Delta regions and in [Cần Giờ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/C%E1%BA%A7n_Gi%E1%BB%9D_district "Cần Giờ district") outside Ho Chi Minh City, where mangroves are important to ease (though not eliminate) flood conditions during monsoon seasons.[^261] The country had a 2019 [Forest Landscape Integrity Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest_Landscape_Integrity_Index "Forest Landscape Integrity Index") mean score of 5.35/10, ranking it 104th globally out of 172 countries.[^262]

Apart from herbicide problems, [arsenic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arsenic_poisoning "Arsenic poisoning") in the [ground water](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ground_water "Ground water") in the Mekong and Red River Deltas has also become a major concern.[^263] [^264] And most notoriously, [unexploded ordnances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unexploded_ordnance "Unexploded ordnance") (UXO) pose dangers to humans and wildlife—another bitter legacy from the long wars.[^265] As part of the continuous campaign to [demine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demining "Demining") /remove UXOs, several international [bomb removal agencies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mine_clearance_agency "Mine clearance agency") from the United Kingdom,[^266] Denmark,[^267] [South Korea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Korea "South Korea") [^268] and the US [^269] have been providing assistance. The Vietnamese government spends over VNĐ1 trillion ($44 million) annually on demining operations and additional hundreds of billions of đồng for treatment, assistance, rehabilitation, vocational training, and resettlement of the victims of UXOs.[^270]
