**Tajikistan**,[^3] officially the **Republic of Tajikistan**,[^4] is a [landlocked country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked_country "Landlocked country") in [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia"). [Dushanbe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dushanbe "Dushanbe") is its [capital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capital_city "Capital city") and most populous city with a population of 1.24 million people.[^23] Tajikistan borders [Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan "Afghanistan") to the [south](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan%E2%80%93Tajikistan_border "Afghanistan–Tajikistan border"), [Uzbekistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uzbekistan "Uzbekistan") to the [west](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajikistan%E2%80%93Uzbekistan_border "Tajikistan–Uzbekistan border"), [Kyrgyzstan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyrgyzstan "Kyrgyzstan") to the [north](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kyrgyzstan%E2%80%93Tajikistan_border "Kyrgyzstan–Tajikistan border"), and [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") to the [east](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China%E2%80%93Tajikistan_border "China–Tajikistan border") and is narrowly separated from [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan "Pakistan") by Afghanistan's [Wakhan Corridor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wakhan_Corridor "Wakhan Corridor"). It has a population of more than 10.9 million people.[^24] [^25]

The territory was previously home to cultures of the [Neolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic "Neolithic") and the [Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age "Bronze Age"), including the [Oxus civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bactria%E2%80%93Margiana_Archaeological_Complex "Bactria–Margiana Archaeological Complex") in west. Parts of country were part of the [Sogdian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sogdia "Sogdia") and [Bactrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bactria "Bactria") civilisations, and was ruled by those including the [Achaemenids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire"), [Alexander the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_the_Great "Alexander the Great"), the [Greco-Bactrians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greco-Bactrian_Kingdom "Greco-Bactrian Kingdom"), the [Kushans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kushan_Empire "Kushan Empire"), the [Kidarites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kidarites "Kidarites") and [Hephthalites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hephthalites "Hephthalites"), the [First Turkic Khaganate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Turkic_Khaganate "First Turkic Khaganate"), the [Umayyad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate "Umayyad Caliphate") and [Abbasid Caliphates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbasid_Caliphate "Abbasid Caliphate"), the [Samanid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samanid_Empire "Samanid Empire"), the [Kara-Khanids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara-Khanid_Khanate "Kara-Khanid Khanate"), [Seljuks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seljuk_Empire "Seljuk Empire"), [Khwarazmians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khwarazmian_Empire "Khwarazmian Empire"), the [Mongols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_Empire "Mongol Empire"), [Timurids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timurid_Empire "Timurid Empire") and [Khanate of Bukhara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanate_of_Bukhara "Khanate of Bukhara"). The region was [later conquered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_conquest_of_Central_Asia "Russian conquest of Central Asia") by the [Russian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire"), before becoming part of the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union"). Within the Soviet Union, the [country's borders were drawn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_delimitation_in_the_Soviet_Union#National_delimitation_in_Central_Asia "National delimitation in the Soviet Union") when it was part of [Uzbekistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uzbek_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Uzbek Soviet Socialist Republic") as an [autonomous republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajik_Autonomous_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Tajik Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic") before becoming a [constituent republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajik_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Tajik Soviet Socialist Republic") of the Soviet Union on 5 December 1929.[^26]

On 9 September 1991, Tajikistan declared itself an independent sovereign state as [the Soviet Union was disintegrating](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union "Dissolution of the Soviet Union"). A [civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajikistani_Civil_War "Tajikistani Civil War") was fought after independence, lasting from May 1992 to June 1997. Since the end of the war, newly established political stability and foreign aid have allowed the country's economy to grow. The country has been led since 1994 by [Emomali Rahmon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emomali_Rahmon "Emomali Rahmon"), who heads an [authoritarian regime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authoritarianism "Authoritarianism") and whose [human rights record](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_Tajikistan "Human rights in Tajikistan") has been criticised.[^27] [^28]

Tajikistan is a [presidential republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidential_system "Presidential system") consisting of [four provinces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regions_of_Tajikistan "Regions of Tajikistan"). [Tajiks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajiks "Tajiks") form the ethnic majority in the country,[^29] and their national language is [Tajik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajik_language "Tajik language").[^30] [Russian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language") is used as the official inter-ethnic language.[^31] While the state is [constitutionally secular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secular_state "Secular state"), [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_in_Tajikistan "Islam in Tajikistan") is nominally adhered to by 97.5% of the population. In the [Gorno-Badakhshan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gorno-Badakhshan "Gorno-Badakhshan") [oblast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oblast "Oblast"), there is a linguistic diversity where [Rushani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rushani_dialect "Rushani dialect"), [Shughni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shughni_language "Shughni language"), [Ishkashimi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ishkashimi_language "Ishkashimi language"), [Wakhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wakhi_language "Wakhi language"), and Tajik are some of the languages spoken. Mountains cover more than 90% of the country. It is a [developing country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country "Developing country") with a [transitional economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transition_economy "Transition economy") that is dependent on [remittances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Remittance "Remittance") and on the production of [aluminium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aluminium "Aluminium") and [cotton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cotton "Cotton"). Tajikistan is a member of the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations"), [CIS](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Independent_States "Commonwealth of Independent States"), [OSCE](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organization_for_Security_and_Co-operation_in_Europe "Organization for Security and Co-operation in Europe"), [OIC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation "Organisation of Islamic Cooperation"), [ECO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_Cooperation_Organization "Economic Cooperation Organization"), [SCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai_Cooperation_Organisation "Shanghai Cooperation Organisation"), and [CSTO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collective_Security_Treaty_Organization "Collective Security Treaty Organization"), and a [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO") [PfP partner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partnership_for_Peace "Partnership for Peace").

## Etymology

The term " [Tajik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajik_\(word\) "Tajik (word)") " itself ultimately derives from the [Middle Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Persian "Middle Persian") *Tāzīk*, the [Turkic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkic_languages "Turkic languages") rendition of the [Arabic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabic "Arabic") ethnonym *[Ṭayyi’](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tayy "Tayy")*, denoting a [Qahtanite](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qahtanite "Qahtanite") [Arab tribe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_tribe "Arab tribe") who emigrated to the [Transoxiana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transoxiana "Transoxiana") region of [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia") in the 7th century AD.[^32] Tajikistan appeared as *Tadjikistan* or *Tadzhikistan* in English prior to 1991. This is due to a transliteration from the [Russian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_language "Russian language"): "Таджикистан". In Russian, there is no single letter "j" to represent the phoneme /d͡ʒ/, and therefore дж, or dzh, is used. Tadzhikistan is the alternate spelling and is used in English literature derived from Russian sources.[^33]

## History

Cultures in the region have been dated back to at least the fourth millennium BC, including the [Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age "Bronze Age") [Bactria–Margiana Archaeological Complex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bactria%E2%80%93Margiana_Archaeological_Complex "Bactria–Margiana Archaeological Complex"), the [Andronovo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andronovo_culture "Andronovo culture") cultures and the [pro-urban site of Sarazm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarazm "Sarazm"), a UNESCO [World Heritage Site](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Heritage_Site "World Heritage Site").[^36]

The earliest recorded history of the region dates back to about 500 BC when most, if not all, of Tajikistan was part of the [Achaemenid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire").[^37] Some authors have suggested that in the seventh and sixth centuries BC parts of Tajikistan, including territories in the Zeravshan valley, formed part of the [Hindu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism "Hinduism") [Kambojas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kambojas "Kambojas") tribe [^38] [^39] before it became part of the Achaemenid Empire.[^40] After the region's conquest by [Alexander the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_the_Great "Alexander the Great") it became part of the [Greco-Bactrian Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greco-Bactrian_Kingdom "Greco-Bactrian Kingdom"), a successor state of Alexander's empire. Northern Tajikistan (the cities of [Khujand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khujand "Khujand") and [Panjakent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Panjakent "Panjakent")) was part of [Sogdia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sogdia "Sogdia"), a collection of city-states which was overrun by [Scytho-Siberians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scytho-Siberians "Scytho-Siberians") and [Yuezhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuezhi "Yuezhi") nomadic tribes around 150 BC. The [Silk Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silk_Road "Silk Road") passed through the region and following the expedition of Chinese explorer [Zhang Qian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhang_Qian "Zhang Qian") during the reign of [Wudi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emperor_Wu_of_Han "Emperor Wu of Han") (141 BC–87 BC) commercial relations between [Han Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Han_Empire "Han Empire") and Sogdiana flourished.[^41] [^42] Sogdians played a role in facilitating trade and worked in other capacities, as farmers, carpetweavers, glassmakers, and woodcarvers.[^43]

The [Kushan Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kushan_Empire "Kushan Empire"), a collection of [Yuezhi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yuezhi "Yuezhi") tribes, took control of the region in the first century AD and ruled until the fourth century AD during which time [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism"), [Nestorian Christianity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nestorian_Christianity "Nestorian Christianity"), [Zoroastrianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoroastrianism "Zoroastrianism"), and [Manichaeism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manichaeism "Manichaeism") were practised in the region.[^44] Later the [Hephthalite Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hephthalite_Empire "Hephthalite Empire"), a collection of nomadic tribes, moved into the region, and the [Arabs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabs "Arabs") [disseminated Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spread_of_Islam "Spread of Islam") in the eighth century.[^44]

### Samanid Empire

The [Samanid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samanids "Samanids"), 819 to 999, restored [Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_people "Persian people") control of the region and enlarged the cities of [Samarkand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samarkand "Samarkand") and [Bukhara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bukhara "Bukhara"), which became the cultural centres of [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran"); the region was known as Khorasan. The empire was centred in Khorasan and Transoxiana, at its greatest extent encompassing Afghanistan, parts of Iran, Tajikistan, Turkmenistan, Uzbekistan, Kyrgyzstan, parts of Kazakhstan, and Pakistan.

The Samanids revived [Persian culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_culture "Persian culture") by patronizing [Rudaki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rudaki "Rudaki"),[^45] [Bal'ami](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bal%27ami "Bal'ami") and [Daqiqi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daqiqi "Daqiqi").[^46] The Samanids determinedly propagated Sunni Islam, and repressed [Ismaili](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ismaili "Ismaili") Shiism [^47] but were more tolerant of [Twelver](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twelver "Twelver") Shiism. Islamic architecture and Islamo-Persian culture was spread deep into the heartlands of [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia") by the Samanids. Following the first complete translation of the Qur'an into Persian in the 9th century, populations under the Samanid empire began accepting Islam in significant numbers.

Four brothers Nuh, Ahmad, Yahya, and Ilyas founded the Samanid state. Each of them ruled territory under [Abbasid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbasid "Abbasid") suzerainty. In 892, [Ismail Samani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ismail_Samani "Ismail Samani") (892–907) united the Samanid state under one ruler, thus putting an end to the feudal system used by the Samanids. It was under him that the Samanids became independent of Abbasid authority.

The [Kara-Khanid Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara-Khanid_Khanate "Kara-Khanid Khanate") conquered [Transoxania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transoxania "Transoxania") (which corresponded approximately with what later would be Uzbekistan, Tajikistan, southern Kyrgyzstan, and southwest Kazakhstan) and ruled between 999 and 1211.[^48] [^49] Their arrival in Transoxania signalled a definitive shift from Iranian to Turkic predominance in Central Asia,[^50] and gradually the Kara-khanids became assimilated into the Perso-Arab Muslim culture of the region.[^51]

The region was later ruled by the [Ghurid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghurid_dynasty "Ghurid dynasty") and [Kart dynasties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kart_dynasty "Kart dynasty"),[^52] of [Tajik origin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajiks "Tajiks").[^53] [^54] [^55] [^56] While the Qart dynasty ruled in present-day Afghanistan, [Muhammad Gurid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_of_Ghor "Muhammad of Ghor"), the ruler of the [Ghurid dynasty, conquered much of India.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghurid_campaigns_in_India "Ghurid campaigns in India")

In the 13th century, the [Mongol Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_Empire "Mongol Empire") swept through Central Asia, invaded the [Khwarezmian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khwarezmian_Empire "Khwarezmian Empire") and sacked its cities, looting and massacring people. [Turco-Mongol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turco-Mongol_tradition "Turco-Mongol tradition") conqueror [Tamerlane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timur "Timur") founded the [Timurid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timurid_Empire "Timurid Empire"), becoming the first ruler of the [Timurid dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timurid_dynasty "Timurid dynasty") in and around what later became Tajikistan and Central Asia.[^57]

### Bukharan rule

What later became Tajikistan fell under the rule of the [Khanate of Bukhara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanate_of_Bukhara "Khanate of Bukhara") during the 16th century and, with the empire's collapse in the 18th century, it came under the rule of the [Emirate of Bukhara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Bukhara "Emirate of Bukhara") and [Khanate of Kokand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanate_of_Kokand "Khanate of Kokand"). The Emirate of Bukhara remained intact until the 20th century.

### Imperial Russia

During the 19th century, the Russian Empire began to conquer parts of the region.[^58] Russian [Imperialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperialism "Imperialism") led to the [Russian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire") 's conquest of [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia") during the 19th century's [Imperial Era](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Game "Great Game"). Between 1864 and 1885, Russia gradually took control of the entire territory of [Russian Turkestan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Turkestan "Russian Turkestan"), the Tajikistan portion of which had been controlled by the [Emirate of Bukhara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Bukhara "Emirate of Bukhara") and [Khanate of Kokand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanate_of_Kokand "Khanate of Kokand"). Russia was interested in gaining access to a supply of [cotton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cotton "Cotton") and in the 1870s attempted to switch cultivation in the region from grain to cotton (a strategy later copied and expanded by the Soviets).[^59]

During the 19th century, the [Jadidists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jadid "Jadid") established themselves as an Islamic social movement throughout the region. While the Jadidists were pro-modernisation and not necessarily anti-Russian, the Russians viewed the movement as a threat because the Russian Empire was predominantly Christian.[^60] Russian troops were required to restore order during uprisings against the Khanate of Kokand between 1910 and 1913. Further violence occurred in July 1916 when demonstrators attacked Russian soldiers in [Khujand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khujand "Khujand") over the threat of forced [conscription](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conscription "Conscription") during [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I"). While Russian troops brought Khujand back under control, clashes continued throughout the year in various locations in Tajikistan. [^61]

### Soviet period

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/33/Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana%2C_1921.jpg/250px-Negotiations_with_basmachs_Fergana%2C_1921.jpg)

Soviet negotiations with basmachi, 1921

After the [Russian Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Revolution "Russian Revolution") of 1917 guerrillas throughout Central Asia, known as *[basmachi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basmachi "Basmachi")*, waged [a war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kokand_Autonomy "Kokand Autonomy") against [Bolshevik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bolshevik "Bolshevik") armies in an attempt to maintain independence.[^57] The Bolsheviks prevailed after a four-year war, in which [mosques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mosque "Mosque") and villages were burned down and the population suppressed.

Between 1928 and 1941, Soviet authorities started an [anti-religious campaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USSR_anti-religious_campaign_\(1928%E2%80%931941\) "USSR anti-religious campaign (1928–1941)") of [secularisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secularization "Secularization"). Practising [Christianity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity "Christianity"), [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam"), or [Judaism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judaism "Judaism") was discouraged and repressed; due to the [Soviet anti-religious legislation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_anti-religious_legislation "Soviet anti-religious legislation"), several [churches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_\(building\) "Church (building)"), [mosques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mosque "Mosque"), and [synagogues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Synagogue "Synagogue") were closed.[^62] As a consequence of the conflict and Soviet agriculture policies, [Central Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia"), Tajikistan included, underwent a famine that claimed lives.[^63]

Tajikistan was first created in 1924 as the [Tajik Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajik_Autonomous_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Tajik Autonomous Soviet Socialist Republic") (abbrievated Tajik ASSR), an autonomous republic within the [Uzbek Soviet Socialist Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uzbek_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Uzbek Soviet Socialist Republic").[^64] Despite their historical status as Tajik cities and mostly being populated by Tajiks, none of the regional urban centres—such as [Bukhara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bukhara "Bukhara"), [Samarkand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samarkand "Samarkand"), [Tirmiz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tirmiz "Tirmiz"), [Qarshi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qarshi "Qarshi"), and [Khojand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khojand "Khojand") —were included in Tajikistan. Instead, the Tajikistani capital was established in [Dushanbe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dushanbe "Dushanbe"), a mountain town with approximately 1,000 residents.[^65]

A specific Persian dialect, with vocabulary and pronunciation different from formal written Persian, was deliberately made the national language of Tajikistan by the Soviets and renamed "Tajiki" to forcibly separate it from the common variant of Persian spoken from [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul") to [Calcutta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calcutta "Calcutta"). The Tajiks were also excluded from the enormous global world of Persian literature, both past and current, by the creation of a new phonetic alphabet, first Latin-based in 1928 and later Cyrillic in 1940.[^66] With Persian confined to Tajikistan and replaced by Russian as Central Asia's common language, the unity of the [Persianate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persianate "Persianate") world was broken, steering Soviet and post-Soviet Central Asia onto a developmental path distinct from Iran and Afghanistan.[^67] In 1929, the Tajik ASSR was elevated to its own full [union republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republics_of_the_Soviet_Union "Republics of the Soviet Union"), the [Tajik Soviet Socialist Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajik_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Tajik Soviet Socialist Republic"). The [Sughd Region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sughd_Region "Sughd Region") was added to Tajikistan in the same year.[^68]

Between 1927 and 1934, [collectivisation of agriculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collectivization_of_agriculture "Collectivization of agriculture") and an expansion of cotton production took place, especially in the southern region.[^69] Soviet collectivization policy brought violence against farmers and peasants, classified as [anti-Soviet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-Soviet "Anti-Soviet") categories of " [enemies of the people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enemies_of_the_people "Enemies of the people") ", and [forced resettlement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Population_transfer_in_the_Soviet_Union "Population transfer in the Soviet Union") occurred throughout Tajikistan. Consequently, some peasants fought collectivisation and revived the [Basmachi movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basmachi_movement "Basmachi movement"). Some industrial development occurred during this time along with the expansion of irrigation infrastructure.[^69]

Soviet Tajikistan in 1964

Two rounds of [Stalin's purges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Purge "Great Purge") (1927–1934 and 1937–1938) resulted in the expulsion of nearly 10,000 people from all levels of the [Communist Party of Tajikistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist_Party_of_Tajikistan "Communist Party of Tajikistan").[^70] Ethnic [Russians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russians "Russians") were sent in to replace those expelled and subsequently Russians dominated party positions at all levels, including the top position of first secretary.[^70] Between 1926 and 1959 the proportion of Russians among Tajikistan's population grew from less than 1% to 13%.[^71] [Bobojon Ghafurov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bobojon_Ghafurov "Bobojon Ghafurov"), [First Secretary of the Communist Party of Tajikistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Secretary_of_the_Communist_Party_of_Tajikistan "First Secretary of the Communist Party of Tajikistan") from 1946 to 1956, was the only Tajik politician of significance outside of the republic during the Soviet Era.[^72]

Tajiks began to be conscripted into the [Red Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Army "Red Army") in 1939 and during [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II") around 260,000 Tajik citizens fought against [Nazi Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nazi_Germany "Nazi Germany"), [Finland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Finland_in_World_War_II "Finland in World War II"), and the [Empire of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire_of_Japan "Empire of Japan"). Between 60,000 (4%) [^73] and 120,000 (8%) [^74] of Tajikistan's 1,530,000 citizens were killed during World War II.[^75]

Following the war and the end of Stalin's rule, attempts were made to further expand the agriculture and industry of Tajikistan.[^72] During 1957–58 [Nikita Khrushchev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikita_Khrushchev "Nikita Khrushchev") 's [Virgin Lands Campaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgin_Lands_Campaign "Virgin Lands Campaign") focused attention on Tajikistan, where living conditions, education and industry lagged behind the other [Soviet Republics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republics_of_the_Soviet_Union "Republics of the Soviet Union").[^72] In the 1980s, Tajikistan had the lowest household saving rate in the USSR,[^76] the lowest percentage of households in the two top per capita income groups,[^77] and the lowest rate of university graduates per 1000 people.[^78]

By the 1980s Tajik nationalists were calling for increased rights. Real disturbances did not occur within the republic until 1990. The following year, the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union") collapsed, and Tajikistan declared its independence on 9 September 1991, a day which is celebrated as the country's [Independence Day](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Independence_Day_\(Tajikistan\) "Independence Day (Tajikistan)").[^79]
