**Syria**,[^5] officially the **Syrian Arab Republic** (**SAR**),[^6] [^27] is a country in [West Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Asia "West Asia") located in the [Eastern Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Mediterranean "Eastern Mediterranean") and the [Levant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levant "Levant"). It borders the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea") to the west, [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") to [the north and northwest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syria%E2%80%93Turkey_border "Syria–Turkey border"), [Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq "Iraq") to [the east and southeast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq%E2%80%93Syria_border "Iraq–Syria border"), [Jordan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan "Jordan") to [the south](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jordan%E2%80%93Syria_border "Jordan–Syria border"), and [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel "Israel") and [Lebanon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanon "Lebanon") to [the southwest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lebanon%E2%80%93Syria_border "Lebanon–Syria border"). It is a [republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic "Republic") under [a provisional government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_transitional_government "Syrian transitional government") and comprises [14 governorates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governorates_of_Syria "Governorates of Syria"). [Damascus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damascus "Damascus") is the capital and largest city. With a population of 26 million across an area of 185,180 square kilometres (71,500 sq mi), it is the [56th-most](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_population "List of countries and dependencies by population") populous and [87th-largest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_area "List of countries and dependencies by area") country.

The name "Syria" historically referred to the [region of Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syria_\(region\) "Syria (region)"), or known as Greater Syria and Syria-Palestine. The modern state encompasses the sites of several ancient kingdoms and empires, including the [Eblan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ebla "Ebla") civilization. Damascus was the seat of the [Umayyad Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate "Umayyad Caliphate") and a provincial capital under the [Mamluk Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mamluk_Sultanate "Mamluk Sultanate"). The modern Syrian state was established in the mid-20th century [after centuries of Ottoman rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Syria "Ottoman Syria"), as a [French Mandate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandate_for_Syria_and_the_Lebanon "Mandate for Syria and the Lebanon"). The state represented the largest Arab state to emerge from the formerly [Ottoman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") -ruled [Syrian provinces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Syria "Ottoman Syria"). It gained *de jure* independence as a [parliamentary republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_republic "Parliamentary republic") in 1945 when the [First Syrian Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Syrian_Republic "First Syrian Republic") became a founding member of the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations"), an act which legally ended the French Mandate. French troops [withdrew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levant_Crisis "Levant Crisis") in April 1946, granting the nation *de facto* independence. The post-independence period was tumultuous, with [multiple coups and coup attempts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Syrian_coups_d%27%C3%A9tat "List of Syrian coups d'état") between 1949 and 1971. In 1958, Syria entered a [brief pan-Arab union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Republic "United Arab Republic") with [Egypt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egypt "Egypt"), which was terminated following a [1961 coup d'état](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1961_Syrian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "1961 Syrian coup d'état"). The [1963 coup d'état](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1963_Syrian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "1963 Syrian coup d'état") carried out by the [military committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Socialist_Ba%27ath_Party_%E2%80%93_Syria_Region#Military_Bureau "Arab Socialist Ba'ath Party – Syria Region") of the [Ba'ath Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Socialist_Ba%27ath_Party_%E2%80%93_Syria_Region "Arab Socialist Ba'ath Party – Syria Region") established a [one-party state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One-party_state "One-party state"), which [ran Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ba%27athist_Syria "Ba'athist Syria") under martial law from 1963 to 2011. Internal power-struggles within [Ba'athist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ba%27athism "Ba'athism") factions caused further coups in [1966](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1966_Syrian_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "1966 Syrian coup d'état") and [1970](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corrective_Movement_\(Syria\) "Corrective Movement (Syria)"), the latter of which saw [Hafez al-Assad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hafez_al-Assad "Hafez al-Assad") come to power. Under Assad, Syria became a [hereditary dictatorship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dynasty "Dynasty"). [Assad died](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_and_state_funeral_of_Hafez_al-Assad "Death and state funeral of Hafez al-Assad") in 2000, and he was succeeded by his son, [Bashar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bashar_al-Assad "Bashar al-Assad").

Following the [Arab Spring](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arab_Spring "Arab Spring") in 2011, Syria became embroiled in a [multi-sided civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_civil_war "Syrian civil war") with the [involvement of several countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_involvement_in_the_Syrian_civil_war "Foreign involvement in the Syrian civil war"), leading to a [refugee crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Refugees_of_the_Syrian_civil_war "Refugees of the Syrian civil war") in which more than 6 million refugees were displaced from the country. In response to rapid territorial gains made by the [Islamic State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_State "Islamic State") during the civil war in 2014 and 2015, [several countries intervened](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_against_the_Islamic_State "War against the Islamic State") on behalf of various factions opposing it, leading to its territorial defeat in 2017 in both [central](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Syria_campaign "Central Syria campaign") and [eastern](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raqqa_campaign_\(2016%E2%80%932017\) "Raqqa campaign (2016–2017)") Syria. Thereafter, three political entities—the [Syrian Interim Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_Interim_Government "Syrian Interim Government"), [Syrian Salvation Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_Salvation_Government "Syrian Salvation Government"), and the [Democratic Autonomous Administration of North and East Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_Autonomous_Administration_of_North_and_East_Syria "Democratic Autonomous Administration of North and East Syria") —emerged in Syrian territory to challenge Assad's rule. In late 2024, [a series of offensives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Syrian_opposition_offensives "2024 Syrian opposition offensives") from a coalition of [opposition forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_opposition_to_Bashar_al-Assad "Syrian opposition to Bashar al-Assad") led to the [capture of Damascus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_Damascus_\(2024\) "Fall of Damascus (2024)") and the [fall of Assad's regime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_the_Assad_regime "Fall of the Assad regime"). By 2025, the war had left [Syria's economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Syria "Economy of Syria") in a poor state, following years of [international sanctions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_sanctions_against_Syria "International sanctions against Syria") that were later eased. In March 2025, the [Syrian transitional government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_transitional_government "Syrian transitional government") replaced the [Syrian caretaker government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_caretaker_government "Syrian caretaker government") under new Syrian president [Ahmed al-Sharaa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmed_al-Sharaa "Ahmed al-Sharaa").

A country of fertile plains, high mountains, plateaus, and deserts, Syria is home to [diverse ethnic and religious groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Syria "Ethnic groups in Syria"). [Arabs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabs "Arabs") are the largest ethnic group, and [Sunni Muslims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunni_Islam "Sunni Islam") are the largest religious group.

## Etymology

Several sources indicate that the name *Syria* is derived from the [ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "Ancient Greek") name: Σύριοι, **Sýrioi**, or Σύροι, **Sýroi**, which is derived from 8th century BC [Luwian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luwian "Luwian") term "Sura/i", and both of which originally derived from Aššūr ([Assyria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assyria "Assyria")) in northern [Mesopotamia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesopotamia "Mesopotamia") (present-day Iraq and north-eastern Syria).[^28] [^29] However, from the [Seleucid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seleucid_Empire "Seleucid Empire") (323–150 BC), this term was also applied to the [Levant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levant "Levant"),[^30] and from this point the Greeks applied the term without distinction between the [Assyrians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assyrian_people "Assyrian people") of Mesopotamia and [Arameans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arameans "Arameans") of the Levant.[^31] [^32] Mainstream modern academic opinion strongly favors the argument that the Greek word is related to the cognate Ἀσσυρία, **[Assyria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assyria "Assyria")**, ultimately derived from the [Akkadian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Akkadian_language "Akkadian language") **[Aššur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assur "Assur")**.[^33] The Greek name appears to correspond to [Phoenician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenician_language "Phoenician language") **ʾšr** "Assur", **ʾšrym** "Assyrians", recorded in the 8th-century BC [Çineköy inscription](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%87inek%C3%B6y_inscription "Çineköy inscription").[^34]

The area designated by the word has changed over time. Classically, Syria lies at the eastern end of the [Mediterranean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean "Mediterranean"), between [Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabia "Arabia") to the south and [Asia Minor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia_Minor "Asia Minor") to the north, stretching inland to include parts of Iraq, and having an uncertain border to the northeast that [Pliny the Elder](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pliny_the_Elder "Pliny the Elder") describes as including, from west to east, [Commagene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commagene "Commagene"), [Sophene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sophene "Sophene"), and [Adiabene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adiabene "Adiabene").[^35]

By Pliny's time, however, this larger Syria had been divided into a number of provinces under the [Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire") (but politically independent from each other): [Judaea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judaea_\(Roman_province\) "Judaea (Roman province)"), later renamed [Palaestina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine_\(region\) "Palestine (region)") in AD 135 (the region corresponding to modern-day Israel, the Palestinian territories, and Jordan) in the extreme southwest; [Phoenice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenice_\(Roman_province\) "Phoenice (Roman province)") (established in 194) corresponding to modern Lebanon, Damascus and Homs regions; [Coele-Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coele-Syria "Coele-Syria") (or "Hollow Syria") and south of the [Eleutheris river](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nahr_al-Kabir "Nahr al-Kabir").[^26]
