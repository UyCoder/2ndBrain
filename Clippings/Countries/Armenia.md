**Armenia**,[^3] officially the **Republic of Armenia**,[^4] is a [landlocked country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked_country "Landlocked country") in the [Armenian highlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_highlands "Armenian highlands") of [West Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Asia "West Asia").[^12] [^18] [^5] It is a part of the [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus "Caucasus") region and is bordered by [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") to the west, [Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)") to the north, [Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan "Azerbaijan") to the east, and [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran") and the Azerbaijani exclave of [Nakhchivan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nakhchivan_Autonomous_Republic "Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic") to the south.[^20] [Yerevan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yerevan "Yerevan") is the capital, largest city and financial center.

The Armenian highlands have been home to the [Hayasa-Azzi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hayasa-Azzi "Hayasa-Azzi"), [Shupria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shupria "Shupria"), and [Nairi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nairi "Nairi") peoples. By at least 600 BC, an archaic form of [Proto-Armenian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Armenian_language "Proto-Armenian language"), an [Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-European_languages "Indo-European languages") language, had diffused into the Armenian highlands.[^21] [^22] The first Armenian state of [Urartu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urartu "Urartu") was established in 860 BC, and by the 6th century BC it was replaced by the [Satrapy of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satrapy_of_Armenia "Satrapy of Armenia"). The [Kingdom of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Armenia_\(antiquity\) "Kingdom of Armenia (antiquity)") reached its height under [Tigranes the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tigranes_the_Great "Tigranes the Great") in the 1st century BC and in AD 301 became the first state in the world to adopt [Christianity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity "Christianity") as its [official religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_religion "State religion").[^23] [^24] [^25] [^6] Armenia still recognises the [Armenian Apostolic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Apostolic_Church "Armenian Apostolic Church"), the world's oldest [national church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_church "National church"), as the country's primary religious establishment.[^26] [^7] The ancient Armenian kingdom was split between the [Byzantine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire "Byzantine Empire") and [Sasanian Empires](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sasanian_Empire "Sasanian Empire") around the early 5th century. Under the [Bagratuni dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bagratuni_dynasty "Bagratuni dynasty"), the [Bagratid Kingdom of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bagratid_Armenia "Bagratid Armenia") was restored in the 9th century before falling in 1045. [Cilician Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Kingdom_of_Cilicia "Armenian Kingdom of Cilicia"), an Armenian principality and later a kingdom, was located on the coast of the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea") between the 11th and 14th centuries.

Between the 16th and 19th centuries, the traditional Armenian homeland composed of [Eastern Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Armenia "Eastern Armenia") and [Western Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Armenia "Western Armenia") came under the rule of the [Ottoman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") and [Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_Armenia_\(1502%E2%80%931828\) "Iranian Armenia (1502–1828)") empires, repeatedly ruled by either of the two over the centuries. By the 19th century, Eastern Armenia had been conquered by the [Russian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire") while most of Western Armenia remained under Ottoman rule. During [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I") up to 1.5 million Armenians were systematically exterminated in the [Armenian genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_genocide "Armenian genocide"). Following the [Russian Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Revolution "Russian Revolution"), the [First Republic of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Republic_of_Armenia "First Republic of Armenia") declared independence in 1918. By 1920, the state was incorporated into the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union") as the [Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic"). Today's Republic of Armenia became independent in 1991 during the [dissolution of the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union "Dissolution of the Soviet Union").

Modern Armenia is a [unitary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unitary_state "Unitary state"), [multi-party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multi-party_system "Multi-party system"), democratic [nation-state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation_state "Nation state"). It is a [developing country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country "Developing country") and ranks 69th on the [Human Development Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_Development_Index "Human Development Index") as of 2023.[^16] [Its economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Armenia "Economy of Armenia") is primarily based on industrial output and mineral extraction. While Armenia is geographically located in the [South Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Caucasus "South Caucasus"), it views itself as part of Europe [^12] and is generally considered [geopolitically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geopolitics "Geopolitics") European. The country is a member of numerous European organisations including the [Organization for Security and Co-Operation in Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organization_for_Security_and_Co-Operation_in_Europe "Organization for Security and Co-Operation in Europe"), the [Council of Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Europe "Council of Europe"), the [Eastern Partnership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Partnership "Eastern Partnership"), [Eurocontrol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurocontrol "Eurocontrol"), the [Assembly of European Regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assembly_of_European_Regions "Assembly of European Regions"), and the [European Bank for Reconstruction and Development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Bank_for_Reconstruction_and_Development "European Bank for Reconstruction and Development"). Armenia is a member of certain regional groups throughout Eurasia, including the [Asian Development Bank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_Development_Bank "Asian Development Bank"), the [Collective Security Treaty Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collective_Security_Treaty_Organization "Collective Security Treaty Organization"),[^8] the [Eurasian Economic Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasian_Economic_Union "Eurasian Economic Union"), and the [Eurasian Development Bank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasian_Development_Bank "Eurasian Development Bank"). Armenia supported the once *de facto* independent [Republic of Artsakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Artsakh "Republic of Artsakh") (Nagorno-Karabakh), which had [seceded from Azerbaijan in 1991](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1991_Nagorno-Karabakh_independence_referendum "1991 Nagorno-Karabakh independence referendum"), until Azerbaijan reincorporated the region through a [siege](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockade_of_Nagorno-Karabakh "Blockade of Nagorno-Karabakh") and [military offensive in 2023](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Azerbaijani_offensive_in_Nagorno-Karabakh "2023 Azerbaijani offensive in Nagorno-Karabakh").

## Etymology

The original native [Armenian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_language "Armenian language") name for the country was Հայք ([*Hayk'*](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D5%80%D5%A1%D5%B5%D6%84 "wikt:Հայք")); however, it is currently rarely used. The contemporary name Հայաստան (*[Hayastan](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D5%80%D5%A1%D5%B5%D5%A1%D5%BD%D5%BF%D5%A1%D5%B6 "wikt:Հայաստան")*) became popular in the [Middle Ages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Ages "Middle Ages") by addition of the [Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_language "Persian language") suffix *[\-stan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/-stan "-stan")* (place).[^27] However the origins of the name Hayastan trace back to much earlier dates and were first attested in c. 5th century in the works of [Agathangelos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agathangelos "Agathangelos"),[^28] [^29] [Faustus of Byzantium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faustus_of_Byzantium "Faustus of Byzantium"),[^30] [^31] [Ghazar Parpetsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghazar_Parpetsi "Ghazar Parpetsi"),[^32] [Koryun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koryun "Koryun"),[^33] and [Sebeos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sebeos "Sebeos").[^34]

The name has traditionally been derived from [Hayk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hayk "Hayk") (Հայկ), the legendary patriarch of the [Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenians "Armenians") and a great-great-grandson of [Noah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noah "Noah"), who, according to the 5th-century AD author [Movsis Khorenatsi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Movses_Khorenatsi "Movses Khorenatsi"), defeated the [Babylonian king](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Kings_of_Babylon "List of Kings of Babylon") Bel in 2492 BC and established his nation in the [Ararat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ararat,_Armenia "Ararat, Armenia") region.[^35] Further origin of the name is uncertain. It is postulated [^36] [^37] that the name *Hay* comes from one of the two confederated, Hittite vassal states – the [Ḫayaša](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hayasa "Hayasa") -Azzi (1600–1200 BC).

The [exonym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exonym_and_endonym "Exonym and endonym") *Armenia* is attested in the [Old Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Persian "Old Persian") [Behistun Inscription](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Behistun_Inscription "Behistun Inscription") (515 BC) as *[Armina](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%F0%90%8E%A0%F0%90%8E%BC%F0%90%8E%B7%F0%90%8E%A1%F0%90%8E%B4#Old_Persian "wikt:𐎠𐎼𐎷𐎡𐎴")* (𐎠𐎼𐎷𐎡𐎴). The [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "Ancient Greek") terms [Ἀρμενία](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E1%BC%88%CF%81%CE%BC%CE%B5%CE%BD%CE%AF%CE%B1#Ancient_Greek "wikt:Ἀρμενία") (*Armenía*) and [Ἀρμένιοι](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%E1%BC%88%CF%81%CE%BC%CE%AD%CE%BD%CE%B9%CE%BF%CF%82#Ancient_Greek "wikt:Ἀρμένιος") (*Arménioi*, "Armenians") are first mentioned by [Hecataeus of Miletus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hecataeus_of_Miletus "Hecataeus of Miletus") (c. 550 BC – c. 476 BC).[^38] [Xenophon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xenophon "Xenophon"), a Greek general serving in some of the Persian expeditions, describes many aspects of Armenian village life and hospitality in around 401 BC.[^39]

Some scholars have linked the name *Armenia* with the Early Bronze Age state of *[Armani (Armanum, Armi)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armani_\(kingdom\) "Armani (kingdom)")* or the Late Bronze Age state of *[Arme (Shupria)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shupria "Shupria")*.[^40] These connections are inconclusive as it is not known what languages were spoken in these kingdoms. Additionally, while it is agreed that Arme was located to the immediate west of [Lake Van](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Van "Lake Van") (probably in the vicinity of [Sason](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sason "Sason"), and therefore in the greater Armenia region), the location of the older site of Armani is a matter of debate. Some modern researchers have placed it near modern [Samsat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samsat "Samsat"),[^41] and have suggested it was populated, at least partially, by an early Indo-European-speaking people.[^42] It is possible that the name *Armenia* originates in *Armini*, Urartian for "inhabitant of Arme" or "Armean country".[^43] The Arme tribe of Urartian texts may have been the Urumu, who in the 12th century BC attempted to invade Assyria from the north with their allies the [Mushki](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mushki "Mushki") and the [Kaskians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kaskians "Kaskians"). The Urumu apparently settled in the vicinity of Sason, lending their name to the regions of Arme and the nearby lands of Urme and Inner Urumu.[^44]

According to the histories of both Movsis Khorenatsi and Michael Chamchian, *Armenia* derives from the name of [Aram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aram_\(Nahapet\) "Aram (Nahapet)"), a lineal descendant of Hayk.[^45] [^46] In the [Hebrew Bible](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_Bible "Hebrew Bible"), the [Table of Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Generations_of_Noah "Generations of Noah") lists [Aram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aram,_son_of_Shem "Aram, son of Shem") as the son of [Shem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shem "Shem"), to whom the [Book of Jubilees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Book_of_Jubilees "Book of Jubilees") attests,

> And for Aram there came forth the fourth portion, all the land of Mesopotamia between the Tigris and the Euphrates to the north of the Chaldees to the border of the mountains of Asshur and the land of 'Arara'.[^47] [^48]

Jubilees 8:21 also apportions the Mountains of Ararat to Shem, which Jubilees 9:5 expounds to be apportioned to Aram.[^47] [^48]

Jewish historian Flavius [Josephus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josephus "Josephus") states in his *[Antiquities of the Jews](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antiquities_of_the_Jews "Antiquities of the Jews")*,

> Aram had the Aramites, which the Greeks called [Syrians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrians "Syrians");... Of the four sons of Aram, Uz founded [Trachonitis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trachonitis "Trachonitis") and [Damascus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Damascus "Damascus"): this country lies between [Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine_\(region\) "Palestine (region)") and [Celesyria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Celesyria "Celesyria"). Ul founded Armenia; and Gather the [Bactrians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bactrians "Bactrians"); and Mesa the Mesaneans; it is now called [Charax Spasini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charax_Spasini "Charax Spasini").[^49]

## History

### Prehistoric

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/0d/Zorats_Karer_2008%2C_part_of_the_stone_circle.jpg/250px-Zorats_Karer_2008%2C_part_of_the_stone_circle.jpg)

Bronze Age burial site Zorats Karer (also known as Karahunj )

The first human traces are supported by the presence of [Acheulean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acheulean "Acheulean") tools, generally close to the [obsidian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obsidian "Obsidian") outcrops more than 1 million years ago.[^50] The most recent and important excavation is at the [Nor Geghi 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nor_Geghi "Nor Geghi") [Stone Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stone_Age "Stone Age") site in the [Hrazdan river](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hrazdan_\(river\) "Hrazdan (river)") valley.[^51] Thousands of 325,000 year-old artifacts may indicate that this stage of human technological innovation occurred intermittently throughout the Old World, rather than spreading from a single point of origin (usually hypothesized to be Africa), as was previously thought.[^52]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Bull_Petroglyph_Ukhtasar_Volcano.jpg/250px-Bull_Petroglyph_Ukhtasar_Volcano.jpg)

Petroglyphs with images of various animals on Mount Ughtasar

Many early [Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age "Bronze Age") settlements were built in Armenia (Valley of Ararat, Shengavit, Harich, Karaz, Amiranisgora, Margahovit, Garni, etc.). One of the important sites of the Early Bronze Age is [Shengavit settlement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shengavit_\(site\) "Shengavit (site)").[^53] It was located on the site of today's capital of Armenia, [Yerevan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yerevan "Yerevan").

### Antiquity

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/Arshakuni_Armenia_150-en.svg/330px-Arshakuni_Armenia_150-en.svg.png)

Historical Armenia, 150 BC

Armenia lies in the highlands surrounding the mountains of [Ararat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Ararat "Mount Ararat"). There is evidence of an early civilisation in Armenia in the Bronze Age and earlier, dating to about 4000 BC. Archaeological surveys in 2010 and 2011 at the [Areni-1 cave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Areni-1_cave "Areni-1 cave") complex have resulted in the discovery of [the world's earliest known leather shoe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Areni-1_shoe "Areni-1 shoe"),[^54] [^55] skirt,[^56] and [wine-producing facility](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Areni-1_winery "Areni-1 winery").[^57]

Several Bronze Age cultures and states flourished in the area of Greater Armenia, including the [Trialeti-Vanadzor culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trialeti_culture "Trialeti culture"), [Hayasa-Azzi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hayasa-Azzi "Hayasa-Azzi"), and [Mitanni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mitanni "Mitanni") (located in southwestern historical Armenia), all of which are believed to have had Indo-European populations.[^58] [^59] [^60] [^61] [^62] [^63] The [Nairi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nairi "Nairi") confederation and its successor, [Urartu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urartu "Urartu"), successively established their sovereignty over the [Armenian Highlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Highlands "Armenian Highlands"). Each of the aforementioned nations and confederacies participated in the ethnogenesis of the [Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenians "Armenians").[^64] [^65] [^66] [^67] A large cuneiform lapidary inscription found in [Yerevan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yerevan "Yerevan") established that the modern capital of Armenia was founded in the summer of 782 BC by King [Argishti I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Argishti_I_of_Urartu "Argishti I of Urartu"). Yerevan is one of the world's [oldest continuously inhabited cities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_oldest_continuously_inhabited_cities "List of oldest continuously inhabited cities").[^68]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/04/Darius_I_the_Great%27s_inscription.jpg/250px-Darius_I_the_Great%27s_inscription.jpg)

Behistun Inscription of Darius I mentioning Armenia. 6th century BC.

After the fall of the state of [Urartu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urartu "Urartu") at the beginning of the 6th century BC, the [Armenian highlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_highlands "Armenian highlands") were for some time under the hegemony of the [Medes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medes "Medes"), and after that they were part of the [Achaemenid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire"). Armenia was part of the Achaemenid state from the second half of the 6th century BC until the second half of the 4th century BC divided into two satrapies: XIII (western part, with the capital in [Melitene](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melitene "Melitene")) and XVIII (northeastern part).[^69] During the late 6th century BC, the [first geographical entity that was called Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Satrapy_of_Armenia "Satrapy of Armenia") by neighbouring populations was established under the [Orontid dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orontid_dynasty "Orontid dynasty").[^70]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/8a/Coin_of_Tigranes_II_the_Great%2C_Antioch_mint.jpg/250px-Coin_of_Tigranes_II_the_Great%2C_Antioch_mint.jpg)

Coin of Tigranes the Great

The kingdom became fully sovereign from the sphere of influence of the [Seleucid Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seleucid_Empire "Seleucid Empire") in 190 BC under King [Artaxias I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artaxias_I "Artaxias I") and begun the rule of the [Artaxiad dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artaxiad_dynasty "Artaxiad dynasty").[^71] Armenia reached its height between 95 and 66 BC under [Tigranes the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tigranes_the_Great "Tigranes the Great"), becoming the most powerful kingdom of its time east of the [Roman Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic "Roman Republic").[^72] In the next centuries, Armenia was in the [Persian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_Empire "Persian Empire") 's sphere of influence during the reign of [Tiridates I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiridates_I_of_Armenia "Tiridates I of Armenia"), the founder of the [Arsacid dynasty of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arsacid_dynasty_of_Armenia "Arsacid dynasty of Armenia"), which was a branch of the [Parthian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parthian_Empire "Parthian Empire"). Throughout its history, the kingdom of Armenia enjoyed both periods of independence and periods of autonomy subject to contemporary empires. Its strategic location between two continents has subjected it to invasions by many peoples, including [Assyria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assyria "Assyria") (under [Ashurbanipal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashurbanipal "Ashurbanipal"), at around 669–627 BC, the boundaries of Assyria reached as far as Armenia and the [Caucasus Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus_Mountains "Caucasus Mountains")),[^73] Medes, Achaemenid Empire, [Greeks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greeks "Greeks"), Parthians, [Romans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Rome "Ancient Rome"), [Sasanian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sasanian_Empire "Sasanian Empire"), [Byzantine Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire "Byzantine Empire"), [Arabs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabs "Arabs"), [Seljuk Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seljuk_Empire "Seljuk Empire"), [Mongols](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongols "Mongols"), [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire"), the successive [Safavid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Safavid_dynasty "Safavid dynasty"), [Afsharid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afsharid_dynasty "Afsharid dynasty"), and [Qajar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qajar_dynasty "Qajar dynasty") dynasties of Iran, and the Russians.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c5/Garni_Temple_02.JPG/250px-Garni_Temple_02.JPG)

The pagan Garni Temple, probably built in the first century, is the only "Greco-Roman colonnaded building" in the post-Soviet space. 74

Religion in ancient Armenia was historically related to a set of beliefs that, in Persia, led to the emergence of [Zoroastrianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zoroastrianism "Zoroastrianism"). It particularly focused on the worship of [Mithra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mithra "Mithra") and also included a pantheon of gods such as [Aramazd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aramazd "Aramazd"), [Vahagn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vahagn "Vahagn"), [Anahit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anahit "Anahit"), and [Astghik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Astghik "Astghik"). The country used the solar [Armenian calendar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_calendar "Armenian calendar"), which consisted of 12 months.[^75]

Christianity first appeared in Armenia in the 1st century AD, with the arrival of two of [Jesus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesus "Jesus") 's twelve [apostles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apostles_in_the_New_Testament "Apostles in the New Testament") – [Thaddaeus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jude_the_Apostle "Jude the Apostle") and [Bartholomew](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bartholomew_the_Apostle "Bartholomew the Apostle").[^76] [^77] Under the influence of [Gregory the Illuminator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gregory_the_Illuminator "Gregory the Illuminator"), King [Tiridates III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiridates_III_of_Armenia "Tiridates III of Armenia") proclaimed Christianity as the [state religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_religion "State religion") in 301,[^78] partly in defiance of the [Sasanian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sasanian_Empire "Sasanian Empire").[^79] Armenia thus became the first officially Christian state.[^24] Prior to this, during the latter part of the Parthian era, Armenia was a predominantly Zoroastrian country.[^79]

Another major cornerstone of Armenian identity – the [Armenian alphabet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_alphabet "Armenian alphabet") – was invented a century later by [Mesrop Mashtots](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesrop_Mashtots "Mesrop Mashtots") c. 405.[^80] After the fall of the Armenian Arsacids in 428, most of Armenia was incorporated as a [*marzpanate* within Sasanian Persia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sasanian_Armenia "Sasanian Armenia").[^81] Following the [Battle of Avarayr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Avarayr "Battle of Avarayr") in 451, Christian Armenians maintained their religion, and Armenia gained autonomy in the [Treaty of Nvarsak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Nvarsak "Treaty of Nvarsak") in 484.[^82]

### Middle Ages

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/99/Etchmiadzin_cathedral.jpg/250px-Etchmiadzin_cathedral.jpg)

The Etchmiadzin Cathedral, Armenia's Mother Church traditionally dated 303 AD, is considered the oldest cathedral in the world. 83 84 85

The Sassanid Empire was conquered by the [Rashidun Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashidun_Caliphate "Rashidun Caliphate") in the mid 7th century, reuniting Armenian lands previously taken by the Byzantine Empire, and Armenia subsequently emerged as [Arminiya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arminiya "Arminiya"), an autonomous principality under the [Umayyad Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate "Umayyad Caliphate"). The principality was ruled by the Prince of Armenia, and recognised by the [Caliph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caliphate "Caliphate") and the [Byzantine Emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Byzantine_emperors "List of Byzantine emperors"). It was part of the administrative division/emirate *Arminiya* created by the Arabs, which also included parts of Georgia and [Caucasian Albania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasian_Albania "Caucasian Albania"), and had its centre in the Armenian city [Dvin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dvin_\(ancient_city\) "Dvin (ancient city)"). Arminiya lasted until 884, when it regained its independence from the weakened [Abbasid Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbasid_Caliphate "Abbasid Caliphate") under [Ashot I of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ashot_I_of_Armenia "Ashot I of Armenia").[^86]

The reemergent Armenian kingdom was ruled by the [Bagratuni dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bagratuni_dynasty "Bagratuni dynasty") and lasted until 1045. In time, several areas of the [Bagratid Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bagratid_Armenia "Bagratid Armenia") separated as independent kingdoms and principalities such as the Kingdom of [Vaspurakan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vaspurakan "Vaspurakan") ruled by the House of [Artsruni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artsruni "Artsruni") in the south, Kingdom of [Syunik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syunik_Province "Syunik Province") in the east, or [Kingdom of Artsakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Artsakh "Kingdom of Artsakh") on the territory of modern [Nagorno-Karabakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh "Nagorno-Karabakh"), while still recognising the supremacy of the Bagratid kings.[^87]

In 1045, the Byzantine Empire conquered Bagratid Armenia. Soon, the other Armenian states fell under Byzantine control as well. The Byzantine rule was short-lived, as in 1071 the [Seljuk Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seljuk_Empire "Seljuk Empire") defeated the Byzantines and conquered Armenia at the [Battle of Manzikert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Manzikert "Battle of Manzikert").[^88] [Ruben I, Prince of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ruben_I,_Prince_of_Armenia "Ruben I, Prince of Armenia"), went with some of his countrymen into the gorges of the [Taurus Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taurus_Mountains "Taurus Mountains") and then into [Tarsus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarsus,_Mersin "Tarsus, Mersin") of [Cilicia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cilicia "Cilicia"); he was escaping death or servitude at the hands of those who had killed [Gagik II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gagik_II_of_Armenia "Gagik II of Armenia"), King of [Ani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ani "Ani"), his relative. The Byzantine governor of the palace gave them shelter where the [Armenian Kingdom of Cilicia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Kingdom_of_Cilicia "Armenian Kingdom of Cilicia") was eventually established on 6 January 1198 under [Leo I, King of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leo_I,_King_of_Armenia "Leo I, King of Armenia"), a descendant of Prince Ruben.[^89]

Cilicia was a strong ally of the [European Crusaders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crusades "Crusades") and saw itself as a bastion of [Christendom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christendom "Christendom") in the East. Cilicia's significance in Armenian history and statehood is also attested by the transfer of the seat of the [Catholicos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholicos "Catholicos") of the [Armenian Apostolic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Apostolic_Church "Armenian Apostolic Church"), the spiritual leader of the Armenian people, to the region.[^90]

In the early 12th century, Armenian princes of the [Zakarid family](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zakarid_dynasty "Zakarid dynasty") drove out the Seljuk Turks and established a semi-independent principality in northern and eastern Armenia known as [Zakarid Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zakarid_Armenia "Zakarid Armenia"), which lasted under the patronage of the [Georgian Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Georgia_\(country\) "History of Georgia (country)"). The [Orbelian dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orbelian_dynasty "Orbelian dynasty") shared control with the Zakarids in various parts of the country, especially in [Syunik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syunik_Province "Syunik Province") and [Vayots Dzor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vayots_Dzor_Province "Vayots Dzor Province"), while the [House of Hasan-Jalalyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Hasan-Jalalyan "House of Hasan-Jalalyan") controlled provinces of [Artsakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artsakh_\(historical_province\) "Artsakh (historical province)") and [Utik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utik "Utik") as the [Kingdom of Artsakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Artsakh "Kingdom of Artsakh").[^91]

### Early modern era

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/82/Map_Safavid_persia.png/250px-Map_Safavid_persia.png)

In 1501–02, most of the Eastern Armenian territories, including Yerevan, were conquered by the emerging Safavid dynasty of Iran led by Shah Ismail I.

During the 1230s, the [Mongol Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_Empire "Mongol Empire") conquered Zakarid Armenia and then the remainder of Armenia. The Mongolian invasions were soon followed by those of other Central Asian tribes, such as the [Kara Koyunlu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kara_Koyunlu "Kara Koyunlu"), [Timurid dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timurid_dynasty "Timurid dynasty") and [Ağ Qoyunlu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A%C4%9F_Qoyunlu "Ağ Qoyunlu"), which continued from the 13th century until the 15th century. After incessant invasions, each bringing destruction to the country, with time Armenia became weakened.[^92]

In the 16th century, the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") and the [Safavid dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Safavid_dynasty "Safavid dynasty") of Iran divided Armenia. From the early 16th century, both [Western Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Armenia "Western Armenia") and [Eastern Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Armenia "Eastern Armenia") fell to the Safavids.[^93] [^94] Owing to the century long [Turco-Iranian geopolitical rivalry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Safavid_relations "Ottoman–Safavid relations") that would last in West Asia, significant parts of the region were frequently fought over between the two rivalling empires during the [Ottoman–Persian Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Persian_Wars "Ottoman–Persian Wars"). From the mid 16th century with the [Peace of Amasya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_of_Amasya "Peace of Amasya"), and decisively from the first half of the 17th century with the [Treaty of Zuhab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Zuhab "Treaty of Zuhab") until the first half of the 19th century,[^95] Eastern Armenia was ruled by the successive Safavid, [Afsharid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afsharid_dynasty "Afsharid dynasty") and [Qajar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qajar_dynasty "Qajar dynasty") dynasties, while Western Armenia remained under Ottoman rule. From 1604, [Abbas I of Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abbas_I_of_Persia "Abbas I of Persia") implemented a " [scorched earth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scorched_earth "Scorched earth") " policy in the region to protect his north-western frontier against any [invading Ottoman forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman%E2%80%93Safavid_War_\(1603%E2%80%931618\) "Ottoman–Safavid War (1603–1618)"), a policy that involved a [forced resettlement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Armenia#Persian_Armenia "History of Armenia") of masses of Armenians outside of their homelands.[^96]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3c/Siege_of_Erivan_Fortress_on_1_October_1827.jpg/250px-Siege_of_Erivan_Fortress_on_1_October_1827.jpg)

Capture of Erivan fortress by Russian troops in 1827 during the Russo-Persian War (1826–28) by Franz Roubaud

The 18th century saw the first foray of the [Russian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire") into the region, with [Peter the Great](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peter_the_Great "Peter the Great") and his alliance with the Armenian [meliks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melik "Melik") of [Syunik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syunik_\(historical_province\) "Syunik (historical province)") and [Artsakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melikdoms_of_Karabakh "Melikdoms of Karabakh") during the [Russo-Persian War (1722–1723)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Persian_War_\(1722%E2%80%931723\) "Russo-Persian War (1722–1723)").[^97] [^98] Despite the setbacks of Peter's campaign, this initial move encouraged the meliks in their [resistance struggle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_liberation_struggle_\(1722%E2%80%931730\) "Armenian liberation struggle (1722–1730)") against the Safavids and the Ottomans.[^97] However, it was not until the early 19th century that Eastern Armenia formally passed to Russian control, following the [Russo-Persian War (1804–1813)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Persian_War_\(1804%E2%80%931813\) "Russo-Persian War (1804–1813)") and the [Russo-Persian War (1826–1828)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Persian_War_\(1826%E2%80%931828\) "Russo-Persian War (1826–1828)").[^99] In the 1813 [Treaty of Gulistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Gulistan "Treaty of Gulistan") and the 1828 [Treaty of Turkmenchay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Turkmenchay "Treaty of Turkmenchay"), Qajar Iran ceded to Russia the [Karabakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karabakh_Khanate "Karabakh Khanate"), [Erivan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erivan_Khanate "Erivan Khanate"), and [Nakhichevan Khanates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nakhichevan_Khanate "Nakhichevan Khanate").[^100] [^101] The [Russo-Turkish War (1877–1878)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russo-Turkish_War_\(1877%E2%80%931878\) "Russo-Turkish War (1877–1878)") resulted in further gains for [Russian Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Armenia "Russian Armenia"), notably the regions of [Kars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kars "Kars") and [Ardahan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ardahan "Ardahan").[^102] [^103]

Most of Western Armenia, however, remained under Ottoman rule. Within the Ottoman *[millet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Millet_\(Ottoman_Empire\) "Millet (Ottoman Empire)")* system, Armenians lived in relative harmony with other groups, including the ruling Turks. However, as Christians under a strict Muslim [social structure](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_structure "Social structure"), Armenians faced pervasive discrimination. In response to the [1894 Sasun rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1894_Sasun_rebellion "1894 Sasun rebellion"), Sultan [Abdul Hamid II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdul_Hamid_II "Abdul Hamid II") organised state-sponsored massacres against the Armenians between 1894 and 1896, resulting in close to 200,000 deaths.[^104] The [Hamidian massacres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamidian_massacres "Hamidian massacres"), as they came to be known, gave Abdul Hamid international infamy as the "Bloody Sultan" [^105] or the "Red Sultan".[^106]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/ErevanKhanate.gif/250px-ErevanKhanate.gif)

The Armenian Oblast (1828–1840) included the territory of the former Erivan (marked in yellow) and Nakhichevan (marked in light green) khanates.

During the 1890s, the [Armenian Revolutionary Federation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Revolutionary_Federation "Armenian Revolutionary Federation"), commonly known as the *Dashnaktsutyun*, became active within the Ottoman Empire with the aim of unifying the various small groups in the empire that were advocating for reform and defending Armenian villages from massacres that were widespread in some of the Armenian-populated areas of the empire. Dashnaktsutyun members also formed [Armenian fedayi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_fedayi "Armenian fedayi") groups that defended Armenian civilians through armed resistance.[^107] The Dashnaks also worked for the wider goal of creating a "free, independent and unified" Armenia, although they sometimes set aside this goal in favour of a more realistic approach, such as advocating autonomy.[^108] [^109]

The Ottoman Empire began to collapse, and in 1908, the [Young Turk Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Young_Turk_Revolution "Young Turk Revolution") overthrew the government of Sultan Hamid. In April 1909, the [Adana massacre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adana_massacre "Adana massacre") occurred in the [Adana Vilayet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adana_Vilayet "Adana Vilayet") resulting in the deaths of as many as 20,000–30,000 Armenians. The Armenians living in the empire hoped that the [Committee of Union and Progress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Committee_of_Union_and_Progress "Committee of Union and Progress") would change their second-class status. The [Armenian reform package](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_reform_package "Armenian reform package") (1914) was presented as a solution by appointing an [inspector general](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inspector_general "Inspector general") over Armenian issues.[^110]

### World War I and the Armenian genocide

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Ambassador_Morgenthau%27s_Story_p314.jpg/250px-Ambassador_Morgenthau%27s_Story_p314.jpg)

Armenian genocide victims in 1915

The outbreak of World War I led to confrontation between the Ottoman Empire and the Russian Empire in the [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus_campaign "Caucasus campaign") and [Persian campaigns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_campaign_\(World_War_I\) "Persian campaign (World War I)"). The new government in [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul") began to look on the Armenians with distrust and suspicion because the [Imperial Russian Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_Russian_Army "Imperial Russian Army") contained a contingent of [Armenian volunteers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_volunteer_units "Armenian volunteer units"). On 24 April 1915, [Armenian intellectuals were arrested by Ottoman authorities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deportation_of_Armenian_intellectuals_on_24_April_1915 "Deportation of Armenian intellectuals on 24 April 1915") and, with the [Tehcir Law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temporary_Law_of_Deportation "Temporary Law of Deportation") (29 May 1915), eventually a large proportion of Armenians living in [Anatolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anatolia "Anatolia") perished in what has become known as the [Armenian genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_genocide "Armenian genocide").[^111] [^112]

The genocide was implemented in two phases: the wholesale killing of the able-bodied male population through massacre and subjection of army conscripts to forced labour, followed by the deportation of women, children, the elderly and infirm on [death marches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_march "Death march") leading to the [Syrian desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syrian_desert "Syrian desert"). Driven forward by military escorts, the deportees were deprived of food and water and subjected to periodic robbery, rape, and massacre.[^113] [^114] There was local [Armenian resistance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_resistance_during_the_Armenian_genocide "Armenian resistance during the Armenian genocide") in the region, developed against the activities of the Ottoman Empire. The events of 1915 to 1917 are regarded by Armenians and the vast majority of Western historians to have been state-sponsored mass killings, or genocide.[^115]

Turkish authorities deny the genocide took place to this day. The Armenian genocide is acknowledged to have been one of the first modern genocides.[^116] [^117] According to the research conducted by [Arnold J. Toynbee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arnold_J._Toynbee "Arnold J. Toynbee"), an estimated 600,000 Armenians died during deportation from 1915 to 1916. This figure, however, accounts for solely the first year of the genocide and does not take into account those who died or were killed after the report was compiled on 24 May 1916.[^118] The [International Association of Genocide Scholars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Association_of_Genocide_Scholars "International Association of Genocide Scholars") places the death toll at "more than a million".[^119] The total number of people killed has been most widely estimated at between 1 and 1.5 million.[^120] Armenia and the Armenian diaspora have been campaigning for [official recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_genocide_recognition "Armenian genocide recognition") of the events as genocide for over 30 years. These events are traditionally commemorated yearly on 24 April, the Armenian Martyr Day, or the Day of the Armenian genocide.[^121]

### First Republic of Armenia

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/31/First_Republic_of_Armenia.png/250px-First_Republic_of_Armenia.png)

Territory held by Armenia and the Karabakh Council at some point Area given to Armenia by the Treaty of Sèvres, which was never enforced 122

The [Imperial Russian Caucasus Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus_Army_\(Russian_Empire,_1914%E2%80%931917\) "Caucasus Army (Russian Empire, 1914–1917)"), commanded by [Nikolai Yudenich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikolai_Yudenich "Nikolai Yudenich"), and the Armenian volunteer units, led by [Andranik Ozanian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andranik_Ozanian "Andranik Ozanian") and [Tovmas Nazarbekian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tovmas_Nazarbekian "Tovmas Nazarbekian"), succeeded in gaining much of Western Armenia during World War I. However, their gains were lost with the [October Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/October_Revolution "October Revolution") of 1917.[^123] At the time, Russian-controlled Eastern Armenia, Georgia, and Azerbaijan attempted to bond together in the [Transcaucasian Democratic Federative Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transcaucasian_Democratic_Federative_Republic "Transcaucasian Democratic Federative Republic"). This federation, however, lasted from only February to May 1918, when all three parties decided to dissolve it. As a result, the Dashnak-dominated government of Eastern Armenia declared its independence on 28 May as the [First Republic of Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Republic_of_Armenia "First Republic of Armenia") under the leadership of [Aram Manukian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aram_Manukian "Aram Manukian").[^124]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ed/Government-House-of-Republic-of-Armenia-1918-1920.jpg/250px-Government-House-of-Republic-of-Armenia-1918-1920.jpg)

The Government house of the First Republic of Armenia (1918–1920)

The First Republic's short-lived independence was fraught with war, territorial disputes, [ethnic unrest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_uprisings_in_Kars_and_Sharur%E2%80%93Nakhichevan "Muslim uprisings in Kars and Sharur–Nakhichevan"), [Bolshevik rebellions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/May_Uprising "May Uprising"), and a mass influx of refugees from Western Armenia, bringing with them disease and starvation. The [Entente Powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I "Allies of World War I") sought to help the newly founded Armenian state through relief funds and other forms of support.[^125]

At the end of the war, the victorious powers sought to divide up the Ottoman Empire. Signed between the Allied Powers and the Ottoman Empire at [Sèvres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/S%C3%A8vres "Sèvres") on 10 August 1920, the [Treaty of Sèvres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_S%C3%A8vres "Treaty of Sèvres") promised to maintain the existence of the Armenian republic and to attach the former territories of Western Armenia to it.[^126] Because the new borders of Armenia were to be drawn by United States President [Woodrow Wilson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson "Woodrow Wilson"), Western Armenia was also referred to as " [Wilsonian Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wilsonian_Armenia "Wilsonian Armenia") ". In addition, just days prior on 5 August, [Mihran Damadian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mihran_Damadian "Mihran Damadian") of the Armenian National Union, the de facto Armenian administration in Cilicia, declared the independence of Cilicia as an Armenian autonomous republic under French protectorate.[^127] There was even consideration of making Armenia a [mandate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/League_of_Nations_mandate "League of Nations mandate") under the protection of the United States. The treaty, however, was rejected by the [Turkish National Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_National_Movement "Turkish National Movement") and never came into effect.[^126]

In 1920, Turkish nationalist forces [invaded](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_invasion_of_Armenia "Turkish invasion of Armenia") the fledgling Armenian republic from the east. Under the command of [Kâzım Karabekir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/K%C3%A2z%C4%B1m_Karabekir "Kâzım Karabekir"), they captured the Armenian territories that Imperial Russia had annexed in 1878 and occupied Alexandropol (today [Gyumri](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gyumri "Gyumri")). Hostilities finally concluded with the [Treaty of Alexandropol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Alexandropol "Treaty of Alexandropol") on 2 December 1920. The treaty forced Armenia to disarm most of its military forces and to cede all former Ottoman territory granted to it by the Treaty of Sèvres. Simultaneously, the [11th Red Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/11th_Army_\(RSFSR\) "11th Army (RSFSR)"), led by [Sergo Ordzhonikidze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sergo_Ordzhonikidze "Sergo Ordzhonikidze"), invaded Armenia at Karavansarai (today [Ijevan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ijevan "Ijevan")) on 29 November. By early December, Ordzhonikidze's forces entered Yerevan and the short-lived Armenian republic collapsed.[^128]

After the fall of the First Republic, the [war communism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_communism "War communism") policies of the new [Soviet Armenian government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Armenian Soviet Socialist Republic") led to the [February Uprising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/February_Uprising "February Uprising") of 1921.[^129] Following the defeat of that revolt, Armenian forces under the command of [Garegin Nzhdeh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garegin_Nzhdeh "Garegin Nzhdeh") established the [Republic of Mountainous Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Mountainous_Armenia "Republic of Mountainous Armenia") and continued to fight Soviet forces in the southern region of [Zangezur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zangezur "Zangezur").[^130] To end the rebellion, Soviet authorities pledged to include the [Syunik Province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syunik_Province "Syunik Province") within Armenia's borders.[^131] The Red Army soon took control of the region, driving the leaders of the revolt out of Armenia, across the [Araks River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aras_\(river\) "Aras (river)") into Iran on 15 July 1921.[^130]

### Soviet Armenia

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cc/Emblem_of_the_Armenian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic_%281937%E2%80%931990%29%2C_Emblem_of_Armenia_%281990%E2%80%931992%29.svg/250px-Emblem_of_the_Armenian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic_%281937%E2%80%931990%29%2C_Emblem_of_Armenia_%281990%E2%80%931992%29.svg.png)

The state emblem of Soviet Armenia depicting Mount Ararat

#### Sovietization to Stalinism

Following Armenia's [Sovietization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_invasion_of_Armenia "Soviet invasion of Armenia"), the Treaty of Alexandropol was superseded by the [Treaty of Moscow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Moscow_\(1921\) "Treaty of Moscow (1921)") and the [Treaty of Kars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Kars "Treaty of Kars"). In these agreements, Turkey renounced its claims on [Batumi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Batumi "Batumi") to [Soviet Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Georgian Soviet Socialist Republic") in exchange for the provinces of [Kars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kars_oblast "Kars oblast"), [Ardahan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ardahan_okrug "Ardahan okrug"), and [Surmalu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surmalu_uezd "Surmalu uezd"), all of which had been under Turkish military control since the Turkish invasion of Armenia in 1920. The territory ceded to Turkey included the medieval Armenian capital [Ani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ani "Ani") and [Mount Ararat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Ararat "Mount Ararat"), the national symbol of the Armenian people.[^132] Together with Soviet Georgia and [Soviet Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Azerbaijan Soviet Socialist Republic"), Soviet Armenia officially entered the [USSR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union") as part of the [Transcaucasian SFSR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transcaucasian_SFSR "Transcaucasian SFSR") (TSFSR) in March 1922.[^133] The TSFSR later split into three separate republics in December 1936 – the Armenian SSR, the Azerbaijan SSR, and the Georgian SSR.[^134]

[Vladimir Lenin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vladimir_Lenin "Vladimir Lenin"), who "favored a moderate policy in Armenia," [^135] appointed [Alexander Miasnikian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexander_Miasnikian "Alexander Miasnikian"), an experienced administrator, to oversee affairs in the republic.[^136] During the era of the [New Economic Policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Economic_Policy "New Economic Policy") (NEP), Armenia enjoyed a period of stability and cultural revival within the USSR, in contrast to the turbulent years of the First Republic.[^137] However, after the [death of Lenin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_and_state_funeral_of_Vladimir_Lenin "Death and state funeral of Vladimir Lenin") and the [rise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Stalin%27s_rise_to_power "Joseph Stalin's rise to power") of [Joseph Stalin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Stalin "Joseph Stalin") to the Soviet leadership, the situation changed significantly. Armenians suffered greatly during [Stalin's Great Purge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_victims_of_the_Great_Purge "Armenian victims of the Great Purge"), with [Lavrentiy Beria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lavrentiy_Beria "Lavrentiy Beria") playing an especially conspicuous role, beginning with the death of the popular Soviet Armenian leader [Aghasi Khanjian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aghasi_Khanjian "Aghasi Khanjian") in 1936.[^138] [^139] The repressions claimed the lives of major Armenian intellectual figures like [Yeghishe Charents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yeghishe_Charents "Yeghishe Charents"), [Axel Bakunts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axel_Bakunts "Axel Bakunts"), and [Vahan Totovents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vahan_Totovents "Vahan Totovents").[^140] The Armenian Church also suffered in this period, culminating in the murder of the Armenian Catholicos [Khoren I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khoren_I_of_Armenia "Khoren I of Armenia"), an act for which Beria is usually held responsible.[^141]

#### Great Patriotic War and post-war period

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/8/87/ArmenianStamps-066-069.jpg/500px-ArmenianStamps-066-069.jpg)

Armenian Marshals and Admiral of World War II on stamps: Bagramyan, Isakov Babadzhanian Khudyakov

Armenia was not the scene of any battles in the [Great Patriotic War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Front_\(World_War_II\) "Eastern Front (World War II)") of [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"). Nevertheless, an estimated 500,000 Armenians (nearly a third of the population) served in the [Red Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Red_Army "Red Army") during the war, and 175,000 died.[^142] A total of 117 citizens of Soviet Armenia, including 10 non-Armenians, were awarded [Hero of the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hero_of_the_Soviet_Union "Hero of the Soviet Union"). Six special military divisions were formed in Armenia in 1941–42. Five of them, the [89th](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/89th_Rifle_Division "89th Rifle Division"), [409th](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/409th_Rifle_Division "409th Rifle Division"), [408th](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/408th_Rifle_Division_\(Soviet_Union\) "408th Rifle Division (Soviet Union)"), [390th](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/390th_Rifle_Division "390th Rifle Division"), and [76th](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/76th_Rifle_Division_\(Soviet_Union\) "76th Rifle Division (Soviet Union)") Divisions, would have a distinguished war record, while the sixth was ordered to stay in Armenia to guard the republic's western borders against a possible incursion by neighboring Turkey. The 89th Tamanyan Division, composed of ethnic Armenians, fought in the [Battle of Berlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Berlin "Battle of Berlin"). Over 60 Armenians were promoted to the rank of general, while one attained the rank of [Admiral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Admiral_of_the_Fleet_of_the_Soviet_Union "Admiral of the Fleet of the Soviet Union") ([Ivan Isakov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Isakov "Ivan Isakov")) and three achieved the rank of [Marshal of the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marshal_of_the_Soviet_Union "Marshal of the Soviet Union") ([Ivan Bagramyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ivan_Bagramyan "Ivan Bagramyan"), [Hamazasp Babadzhanian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamazasp_Babadzhanian "Hamazasp Babadzhanian"), and [Sergei Khudyakov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sergei_Khudyakov "Sergei Khudyakov")).[^143]

After the [death of Stalin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_funeral_of_Joseph_Stalin "State funeral of Joseph Stalin") in 1953, Armenia experienced a new period of liberalization under [Nikita Khrushchev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikita_Khrushchev "Nikita Khrushchev") 's [Thaw](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khrushchev_Thaw "Khrushchev Thaw"). In March 1954, two years before Khrushchev [denounced Stalin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/On_the_Cult_of_Personality_and_Its_Consequences "On the Cult of Personality and Its Consequences"), his close ally, the Armenian statesman [Anastas Mikoyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anastas_Mikoyan "Anastas Mikoyan"), flew to Yerevan and called for the [rehabilitation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rehabilitation_\(Soviet\) "Rehabilitation (Soviet)") of Charents and the republication of the writers [Raphael Patkanian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raphael_Patkanian "Raphael Patkanian") and [Raffi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raffi_\(novelist\) "Raffi (novelist)").[^144] During the Thaw, life in Soviet Armenia soon began to see rapid improvement with greater investment in housing and the consumer economy. The Armenian Church, which was limited under Stalin, was revived when [Catholicos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholicos_of_All_Armenians "Catholicos of All Armenians") [Vazgen I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vazgen_I "Vazgen I") assumed the duties of his office in 1955.[^145] In 1962, the massive statue of Stalin that towered over Yerevan was pulled down from its pedestal by troops and replaced with that of [Mother Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mother_Armenia "Mother Armenia") in 1967.[^146] That same year, a memorial to the victims of the Armenian genocide was built at the [Tsitsernakaberd](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsitsernakaberd "Tsitsernakaberd") hill above the Hrazdan gorge in [Yerevan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yerevan "Yerevan").[^146] This occurred after [mass demonstrations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1965_Yerevan_demonstrations "1965 Yerevan demonstrations") took place on the tragic event's fiftieth anniversary in 1965.[^147]

#### Glasnost and perestroika

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b5/Karabakh_movement_demonstration_at_Yerevan_Opera_square_%284%29.jpg/250px-Karabakh_movement_demonstration_at_Yerevan_Opera_square_%284%29.jpg)

Armenians gather at Theater Square in central Yerevan to claim unification of Nagorno-Karabakh Autonomous Oblast with the Armenian SSR, 1988.

Following the [Brezhnev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leonid_Brezhnev "Leonid Brezhnev") era, [Mikhail Gorbachev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mikhail_Gorbachev "Mikhail Gorbachev") 's reforms of *[glasnost](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glasnost "Glasnost")* and *[perestroika](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Perestroika "Perestroika")* witnessed a major national revival in Armenia with the rise of the [Karabakh movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karabakh_movement "Karabakh movement"). Taking advantage of the new political climate, the Armenians of the [Nagorno-Karabakh Autonomous Oblast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_Autonomous_Oblast "Nagorno-Karabakh Autonomous Oblast") (NKAO) of Soviet Azerbaijan began to demand unification with Soviet Armenia, citing [discrimination by Baku](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forced_assimilation_in_Azerbaijan "Forced assimilation in Azerbaijan").[^148] Peaceful protests in Armenia supporting the Karabakh Armenians became common. By the beginning of 1988, nearly one million Armenians from several regions of the republic engaged in these demonstrations, centered on Yerevan's Theater Square (today [Freedom Square](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_Square,_Yerevan "Freedom Square, Yerevan")).[^149] However, in neighboring Azerbaijan, [violence against Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumgait_pogrom "Sumgait pogrom") erupted in the city of [Sumgait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumgait "Sumgait").[^150] Ethnic rioting soon broke out between Armenians and Azeris, preventing any peaceful resolution from taking place.[^151] Compounding Armenia's problems was a [devastating earthquake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1988_Armenian_earthquake "1988 Armenian earthquake") in 1988 with a [surface-wave magnitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surface-wave_magnitude "Surface-wave magnitude") of 6.8.[^152] Another major [pogrom against Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baku_pogrom "Baku pogrom") in [Baku](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baku "Baku") in January 1990 forced almost all of the 200,000 Armenians in the Azerbaijani capital to flee to Armenia.[^153]

Gorbachev's inability to alleviate any of Armenia's problems created disillusionment among the Armenians and fed a growing hunger for independence. On 23 August 1990, Armenia declared its sovereignty on its territory. On 17 March 1991, Armenia, along with the [Baltic states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltic_states "Baltic states"), Georgia and [Moldova](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moldavian_Soviet_Socialist_Republic "Moldavian Soviet Socialist Republic"), boycotted a nationwide [referendum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1991_Soviet_Union_referendum "1991 Soviet Union referendum") in which 78% of all voters voted for the retention of the Soviet Union in a reformed form.[^154]

### Post-Soviet independence

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d3/NKR_war.JPG/250px-NKR_war.JPG)

Armenian soldiers in 2008, during Nagorno-Karabakh conflict

On 21 September 1991, Armenia officially declared its [statehood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Statehood "Statehood") after the [failed August coup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1991_Soviet_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt "1991 Soviet coup d'état attempt") in [Moscow](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moscow "Moscow") in the [Russian SFSR](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Soviet_Federative_Socialist_Republic "Russian Soviet Federative Socialist Republic"). [Levon Ter-Petrosyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Levon_Ter-Petrosyan "Levon Ter-Petrosyan"), who rose to prominence during the Karabakh movement, was [popularly elected](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1991_Armenian_presidential_election "1991 Armenian presidential election") the first president of the newly independent Republic of Armenia in October 1991.[^155] The Soviet Union formally [dissolved](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union "Dissolution of the Soviet Union") following the [Belovezha Accords](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belovezha_Accords "Belovezha Accords") in December 1991 and Armenia's independence was recognised.[^156]

Ter-Petrosyan led Armenia alongside defence minister [Vazgen Sargsyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vazgen_Sargsyan "Vazgen Sargsyan") through the [First Nagorno-Karabakh War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Nagorno-Karabakh_War "First Nagorno-Karabakh War") with neighbouring Azerbaijan. The initial post-Soviet years were marred by economic difficulties and a [severe energy crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990s_Armenian_energy_crisis "1990s Armenian energy crisis"). The latter was rooted in the early years of the Karabakh conflict when the [Azerbaijani Popular Front](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijani_Popular_Front_Party "Azerbaijani Popular Front Party") managed to pressure the Azerbaijan SSR to instigate a railway and air [blockade against Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish%E2%80%93Azeri_blockade_of_Armenia "Turkish–Azeri blockade of Armenia"). This move effectively debilitated Armenia's economy as 85% of its cargo and goods arrived through rail traffic.[^157] In 1993, Turkey joined the blockade against Armenia in support of Azerbaijan.[^158]

The first Karabakh war ended after a Russian-brokered [ceasefire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ceasefire "Ceasefire") was concluded in 1994. The war was a success for the Karabakh Armenian forces who managed to capture practically the entire territory of the former NKAO as well as most of [seven adjacent districts of Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian-occupied_territories_surrounding_Nagorno-Karabakh "Armenian-occupied territories surrounding Nagorno-Karabakh").[^159] The Armenian backed forces remained in control of almost all of that territory until 2020. By the time both Azerbaijan and Armenia had finally agreed to a ceasefire in 1994, an estimated 30,000 people had been killed and over a million had been displaced.[^160]

### 21st century

In the 21st century, Armenia continued to face many hardships. The [2018 Armenian Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Armenian_revolution "2018 Armenian revolution") was a series of anti-government protests in Armenia from April to May 2018 staged by various political and civil groups led by a member of the Armenian parliament — [Nikol Pashinyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikol_Pashinyan "Nikol Pashinyan") (head of the [Civil Contract](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_Contract_\(Armenia\) "Civil Contract (Armenia)") party). Protests and marches took place initially in response to [Serzh Sargsyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serzh_Sargsyan "Serzh Sargsyan") 's third consecutive term as President of Armenia and later against the [Republican Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republican_Party_of_Armenia "Republican Party of Armenia") controlled government in general. Pashinyan declared the movement, which led to Sargsyan's resignation, a " [velvet revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Velvet_revolution "Velvet revolution") ".[^161]

In March 2018, the Armenian parliament elected [Armen Sarkissian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armen_Sarkissian "Armen Sarkissian") as the new President of Armenia. The controversial constitutional reform to reduce presidential power was implemented, while the authority of the prime minister was strengthened.[^162] In May 2018, parliament elected opposition leader Nikol Pashinyan as the new prime minister. His predecessor Serzh Sargsyan resigned two weeks earlier following [widespread anti-government demonstrations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Armenian_revolution "2018 Armenian revolution").[^163]

On 27 September 2020, a full-scale war erupted due to the unresolved [Nagorno-Karabakh conflict](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh_conflict "Nagorno-Karabakh conflict").[^164] Both the armed forces of Armenia and Azerbaijan reported military and civilian casualties.[^165] The [Nagorno-Karabakh ceasefire agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Nagorno-Karabakh_ceasefire_agreement "2020 Nagorno-Karabakh ceasefire agreement") to end the six-week [war between Armenia and Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Nagorno-Karabakh_war "2020 Nagorno-Karabakh war") was seen by many as Armenia's defeat and capitulation.[^166] The year-long [March of Dignity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020%E2%80%932021_Armenian_protests "2020–2021 Armenian protests") protests forced early elections.

On 20 June 2021, Pashinyan's Civil Contract party won an early parliamentary [election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2021_Armenian_parliamentary_election "2021 Armenian parliamentary election"). Acting Prime Minister Nikol Pashinyan was officially appointed to the post of prime minister by Armenia's President Armen Sarkissian.[^167] In January 2022, Armenian President Armen Sarkissian resigned from office, stating that the constitution no longer gives the president sufficient powers or influence.[^168] On 3 March 2022, [Vahagn Khachaturyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vahagn_Khachaturyan "Vahagn Khachaturyan") was elected as the fifth president of Armenia in the second round of parliamentary vote.[^169] The next month [yet more protests broke out](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_Armenian_protests "2022 Armenian protests").[^170]

#### 2023 Azerbaijani offensive in Nagorno-Karabakh

Between 19 and 20 September 2023, [Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan "Azerbaijan") launched a large-scale military offensive against the [self-declared](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_status_of_Nagorno-Karabakh "Political status of Nagorno-Karabakh") breakaway state of [Artsakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Artsakh "Republic of Artsakh"), a move seen by the European Parliament as a violation of the [2020 ceasefire agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2020_Nagorno-Karabakh_ceasefire_agreement "2020 Nagorno-Karabakh ceasefire agreement").[^171] [^172] The offensive took place in the disputed region of [Nagorno-Karabakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nagorno-Karabakh "Nagorno-Karabakh"), which is internationally recognized as part of Azerbaijan, but populated by [Armenians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenians "Armenians").[^173] [^174] The attacks occurred in the midst of an escalating crisis caused by Azerbaijan [blockading Artsakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blockade_of_the_Republic_of_Artsakh_\(2022%E2%80%93present\) "Blockade of the Republic of Artsakh (2022–present)"), which resulted in significant scarcities of essential supplies such as food, medicine, and other goods in the affected region.[^175]

One day after the offensive started, on 20 September, a [ceasefire agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2023_Nagorno-Karabakh_ceasefire_agreement "2023 Nagorno-Karabakh ceasefire agreement") was reached at the mediation of the [Russian peacekeeping command](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peacekeeping_operations_in_Nagorno-Karabakh "Peacekeeping operations in Nagorno-Karabakh") in Nagorno-Karabakh.[^176] Azerbaijan held a meeting with representatives of the Nagorno-Karabakh Armenians on 21 September in [Yevlakh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yevlakh "Yevlakh"), to be followed by another meeting in October.[^177] [^178] Ceasefire violations by Azerbaijan were nonetheless reported by both Artsakhi residents and officials.[^179] [^180]

Human rights organisations and experts in genocide prevention issued multiple alerts, stating that the region's Armenian population was at risk or actively [being subjected to ethnic cleansing and genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flight_of_Nagorno-Karabakh_Armenians "Flight of Nagorno-Karabakh Armenians"). [Luis Moreno Ocampo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luis_Moreno_Ocampo "Luis Moreno Ocampo"), a former [prosecutor of the International Criminal Court](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prosecutor_of_the_International_Criminal_Court "Prosecutor of the International Criminal Court"), warned that another [Armenian genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_genocide "Armenian genocide") could take place, and attributed the inaction of the international community to encouraging Azerbaijan that it would face no serious consequences.[^181]

On 8 August 2025, Armenian Prime Minister [Nikol Pashinyan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nikol_Pashinyan "Nikol Pashinyan") and Azerbaijani President [Ilham Aliyev](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilham_Aliyev "Ilham Aliyev") signed a joint declaration at the [White House](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_House "White House"),[^182] committing to a [peace deal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenia%E2%80%93Azerbaijan_peace_agreement "Armenia–Azerbaijan peace agreement") facilitated by the [Trump administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_presidency_of_Donald_Trump "Second presidency of Donald Trump") that would end nearly four decades of conflict between the two countries.[^183]

## Geography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c8/Satellite_image_of_Armenia_in_May_2003.jpg/250px-Satellite_image_of_Armenia_in_May_2003.jpg)

Satellite image of the territory of Armenia (2003)

Armenia is a [landlocked country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landlocked_country "Landlocked country") in the [geopolitical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geopolitical "Geopolitical") [Transcaucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transcaucasus "Transcaucasus") (South [Caucasus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus "Caucasus")) region, that is located in the Southern [Caucasus Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus_Mountains "Caucasus Mountains") and their lowlands between the [Black Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Sea "Black Sea") and [Caspian Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caspian_Sea "Caspian Sea"), and northeast of the [Armenian Highlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Highland "Armenian Highland"). Armenia is widely accepted as part of [West Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Asia "West Asia") geographically, although other definitions place it either fully in Europe or across Europe and Asia.[^19] [^184] Located on the [Armenian Highlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenian_Highlands "Armenian Highlands"), it is bordered by Turkey to the west, [Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)") to the north, the [Lachin corridor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lachin_corridor "Lachin corridor") which is a part of [Lachin District](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lachin_District "Lachin District") that is under the control of a Russian peacekeeping force and [Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan "Azerbaijan") proper to the east, and [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran") and Azerbaijan's [exclave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exclave "Exclave") of [Nakhchivan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nakhchivan_Autonomous_Republic "Nakhchivan Autonomous Republic") to the south.[^20] Armenia lies between latitudes [38°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/38th_parallel_north "38th parallel north") and [42° N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/42nd_parallel_north "42nd parallel north"), and meridians [43°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/43rd_meridian_east "43rd meridian east") and [47° E](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/47th_meridian_east "47th meridian east"). It contains two terrestrial ecoregions: [Caucasus mixed forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caucasus_mixed_forests "Caucasus mixed forests") and [Eastern Anatolian montane steppe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Anatolian_montane_steppe "Eastern Anatolian montane steppe").[^185]

### Topography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/4b/Armenien_topo.jpg/250px-Armenien_topo.jpg)

Armenia's mountainous and volcanic topography

Armenia has a territorial area of 29,743 square kilometres (11,484 sq mi). The terrain is mostly mountainous, with fast flowing rivers, and few forests. The land rises to 4,090 metres (13,419 feet) [above sea level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metres_above_sea_level "Metres above sea level") at [Mount Aragats](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Aragats "Mount Aragats"), and no point is below 390 metres (1,280 ft) above sea level.[^186] [Average elevation of the country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_average_elevation "List of countries by average elevation") area is tenth highest in the world and it has 85.9% mountain area, more than Switzerland or [Nepal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nepal "Nepal").[^187]

[Mount Ararat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mount_Ararat "Mount Ararat"), which was historically part of Armenia, is the highest mountain in the region at 5,137 meters (16,854 feet). Now located in Turkey, but clearly visible from Armenia, it is regarded by the Armenians as a symbol of their land. Because of this, the mountain is present on the [Armenian national emblem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coat_of_arms_of_Armenia "Coat of arms of Armenia") today.[^188] [^189] [^190]

### Climate

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/09/Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_ARM_1991%E2%80%932020.svg/250px-Koppen-Geiger_Map_v2_ARM_1991%E2%80%932020.svg.png)

Köppen-Geiger climate classification map for Armenia 191

The climate in Armenia is markedly highland [continental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Continental_climate "Continental climate"). Summers are hot, dry and sunny, lasting from June to mid-September. The temperature fluctuates between 22 and 36 °C (72 and 97 °F). However, the low humidity level mitigates the effect of high temperatures. Evening breezes blowing down the mountains provide a welcome refreshing and cooling effect. Springs are short, while autumns are long. Autumns are known for their vibrant and colourful foliage.

Winters are quite cold with plenty of snow, with temperatures ranging between −10 and −5 °C (14 and 23 °F). [Winter sports](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Winter_sport "Winter sport") enthusiasts enjoy skiing down the hills of [Tsaghkadzor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tsaghkadzor "Tsaghkadzor"), located thirty minutes outside Yerevan. [Lake Sevan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lake_Sevan "Lake Sevan"), nestled up in the Armenian highlands, is the second largest lake in the world relative to its altitude, at 1,900 metres (6,234 ft) [above sea level](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metres_above_sea_level "Metres above sea level").

### Environment

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f2/CO2_emissions_per_capita_in_Armenia%2C_Azerbaijan%2C_Georgia%2C_Russia%2C_Germany%2C_Italy%2C_USA_in_2000-2012.jpg/250px-CO2_emissions_per_capita_in_Armenia%2C_Azerbaijan%2C_Georgia%2C_Russia%2C_Germany%2C_Italy%2C_USA_in_2000-2012.jpg)

Armenia ranked 63rd out of 180 countries on [Environmental Performance Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Environmental_Performance_Index "Environmental Performance Index") (EPI) in 2018. Its rank on subindex Environmental Health (which is weighted at 40% in EPI) is 109, while Armenia's rank on subindex of Ecosystem Vitality (weighted at 60% in EPI) is 27th best in the world.[^192] This suggests that main environmental issues in Armenia are with [population health](#Health_care), while environment vitality is of lesser concern. Out of sub-subindices contributing to Environmental Health subindex ranking on Air Quality to which population is exposed is particularly unsatisfying.

In Armenia [forest cover](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest_cover "Forest cover") is around 12% of the total land area, equivalent to 328,470 hectares (ha) of forest in 2020, down from 334,730 hectares (ha) in 1990. In 2020, naturally regenerating forest covered 310,000 hectares (ha) and planted forest covered 18,470 hectares (ha). Of the naturally regenerating forest 5% was reported to be [primary forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_forest "Primary forest") (consisting of native tree species with no clearly visible indications of human activity) and around 0% of the forest area was found within protected areas. For the year 2015, 100% of the forest area was reported to be under [public ownership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_ownership "State ownership").[^193] [^194]

[Waste management in Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Waste_management_in_Armenia "Waste management in Armenia") is underdeveloped, as no waste sorting or recycling takes place at Armenia's 60 landfills. A waste processing plant is scheduled for construction near [Hrazdan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hrazdan "Hrazdan") city, which will allow for closure of 10 waste dumps.[^195]

Despite the availability of abundant renewable energy sources in Armenia (especially [hydroelectric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hydroelectricity "Hydroelectricity") and [wind power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wind_power "Wind power")) and calls from EU officials to shut down the [nuclear power plant at Metsamor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metsamor_Nuclear_Power_Plant "Metsamor Nuclear Power Plant"),[^196] the Armenian Government is exploring the possibilities of installing new small modular nuclear reactors. In 2018 existing nuclear plant is scheduled for modernisation to enhance its safety and increase power production by about 10%.[^197] [^198]
