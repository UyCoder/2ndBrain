**Bangladesh**,[^1] officially the **People's Republic of Bangladesh**,[^2] is a country in [South Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia "South Asia"). It is the [eighth-most populous country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_population "List of countries and dependencies by population") in the world and among the [most densely populated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_population_density "List of countries and dependencies by population density") with a population of almost 176 million within an area of 148,460 square kilometres (57,320 sq mi).[^11] Bangladesh shares land borders with [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India") to the north, west, and east, and [Myanmar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar "Myanmar") to the southeast. It has a coastline along the [Bay of Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal "Bay of Bengal") to its south and is separated from [Bhutan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhutan "Bhutan") and [Nepal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nepal "Nepal") by the [Siliguri Corridor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siliguri_Corridor "Siliguri Corridor"), and from [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") by the [Indian state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Indian_states "List of Indian states") of [Sikkim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sikkim "Sikkim") to its north. [Dhaka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhaka "Dhaka"), the capital and [largest city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_and_towns_in_Bangladesh "List of cities and towns in Bangladesh"), is the nation's political, financial, and cultural centre.[^17] [Chittagong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chittagong "Chittagong") is the second-largest city and [the busiest port](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Port_of_Chittagong "Port of Chittagong") of the country.

The territory of modern Bangladesh was a stronghold of many [Hindu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Hindu_empires_and_dynasties "List of Hindu empires and dynasties") and [Buddhist dynasties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Buddhist_kingdoms_and_empires "List of Buddhist kingdoms and empires") in ancient history. Following the [Muslim conquest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghurid_conquest_of_Bengal "Ghurid conquest of Bengal") in 1204, the region saw [Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal_Sultanate "Bengal Sultanate") and [Mughal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_Empire "Mughal Empire") rule. As the [largest subdivision of the Mughal Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal_Subah "Bengal Subah"), the region of [Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal "Bengal") emerged one of the most prosperous and commercially active areas of the world, known for its [thriving textile industry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslin_trade_in_Bengal "Muslin trade in Bengal") and agricultural productivity. The [Battle of Plassey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Plassey "Battle of Plassey") in 1757 marked the beginning of [British colonial rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal_Presidency "Bengal Presidency") for the following two centuries. The period experienced immense flight of capital which was used to finance the industrial revolution in Great Britain while Bengal was devastated by periods of deadly famines. In the aftermath of the [Partition of India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_British_India "Partition of British India") in 1947, [East Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Bengal "East Bengal") became the eastern and most populous wing of the newly formed [Dominion of Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion_of_Pakistan "Dominion of Pakistan") and was later renamed to [East Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Pakistan "East Pakistan").

Following over two decades of political repression and [systemic racism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institutional_racism "Institutional racism") from the [West Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Pakistan "West Pakistan") –based government, [East Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Pakistan "East Pakistan") experienced civil unrest with the [1971 non-cooperation movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-cooperation_movement_\(1971\) "Non-cooperation movement (1971)"), which led to the [Bangladesh Liberation War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Liberation_War "Bangladesh Liberation War") following a violent government [military operation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Searchlight "Operation Searchlight"). The [Mukti Bahini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mukti_Bahini "Mukti Bahini"), with [aid and assistance from Indian forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Pakistani_war_of_1971 "Indo-Pakistani war of 1971"), waged a successful [armed revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revolution "Revolution"); and, despite the [Bangladesh genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_genocide "Bangladesh genocide") carried out by Pakistan, Bangladesh became a [sovereign nation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_nation "Sovereign nation") on 16 December 1971. Post-Independence, [Sheikh Mujibur Rahman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheikh_Mujibur_Rahman "Sheikh Mujibur Rahman") led the country until his [assassination in 1975](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassination_of_Sheikh_Mujibur_Rahman "Assassination of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman"). Presidency was later transferred to [Ziaur Rahman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ziaur_Rahman "Ziaur Rahman"), who himself was [assassinated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassination_of_Ziaur_Rahman "Assassination of Ziaur Rahman") in 1981. The 1980s were dominated by the dictatorship of [Hussain Muhammad Ershad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hussain_Muhammad_Ershad "Hussain Muhammad Ershad"), who was overthrown in a [mass uprising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990_Bangladesh_mass_uprising "1990 Bangladesh mass uprising") in 1990. Following the democratisation in 1991, the [Battle of the Begums](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Begums "Battle of Begums") between [Khaleda Zia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khaleda_Zia "Khaleda Zia") and [Sheikh Hasina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheikh_Hasina "Sheikh Hasina") defined the country's politics for the next 34 years. Hasina was overthrown in a [mass uprising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/July_Uprising "July Uprising") on 5 August 2024.[^18] [^19]

Bangladesh is a unitary [parliamentary republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_republic "Parliamentary republic") based on the [Westminster system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westminster_system "Westminster system"). It is a [middle power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_power "Middle power") with the [second-largest economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Bangladesh "Economy of Bangladesh") in South Asia. Bangladesh is home to the [fourth-largest Muslim population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_by_country "Islam by country") in the world. It maintains the [third-largest military](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Armed_Forces "Bangladesh Armed Forces") in South Asia and is the largest contributor to the [peacekeeping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peacekeeping "Peacekeeping") operations of the United Nations. Bangladesh consists of [eight divisions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divisions_of_Bangladesh "Divisions of Bangladesh"), [64 districts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Districts_of_Bangladesh "Districts of Bangladesh"), [500 sub-districts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upazila "Upazila"), and [4,599 union councils](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Union_councils_of_Bangladesh "Union councils of Bangladesh"), and is home to the [largest mangrove forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundarbans "Sundarbans") in the world. It has one of the largest [refugee populations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rohingya_refugees_in_Bangladesh "Rohingya refugees in Bangladesh") in the world and continues to face challenges such as endemic [corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Bangladesh "Corruption in Bangladesh"), [human rights abuses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_Bangladesh "Human rights in Bangladesh"), [religious violence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religious_violence "Religious violence"), [political instability](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_decay "Political decay"), and adverse [effects of climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Effects_of_climate_change "Effects of climate change"). Bangladesh is a member state of [SAARC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asian_Association_for_Regional_Cooperation "South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation") and [several other international organisations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_relations_of_Bangladesh "Foreign relations of Bangladesh").

## Etymology

The etymology of *Bangladesh* ("Bengali country") can be traced to the early 20th century, when Bengali patriotic songs, such as *Aaji Bangladesher Hridoy* by [Rabindranath Tagore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rabindranath_Tagore "Rabindranath Tagore") and *Namo Namo Namo Bangladesh Momo* by [Kazi Nazrul Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kazi_Nazrul_Islam "Kazi Nazrul Islam"), used the term in 1905 and 1932 respectively.[^20] Starting in the 1950s, Bengali nationalists used the term in political rallies in [East Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Pakistan "East Pakistan").

The term *Bangla* can refer to both the [Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal "Bengal") region and the [Bengali language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengali_language "Bengali language"). The origins of the term *Bangla* are unclear, with theories pointing to a Bronze Age [proto-Dravidian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Dravidian_language "Proto-Dravidian language") tribe,[^21] and the Iron Age [Vanga Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanga_Kingdom "Vanga Kingdom").[^22] The earliest known usage of the term is the [Nesari plate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nesari "Nesari") in 805 AD. The term *Vangala Desa* is found in 11th-century South Indian records.[^23] [^24] The term gained official status during the [Sultanate of Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultanate_of_Bengal "Sultanate of Bengal") in the 14th century.[^25] [^26] [Shamsuddin Ilyas Shah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shamsuddin_Ilyas_Shah "Shamsuddin Ilyas Shah") proclaimed himself as the first " [Shah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shah "Shah") of Bangala" in 1342.[^25] The word *Bangāl* became the most common name for the region during the Islamic period.[^27] Sixteenth century historian [Abu'l-Fazl ibn Mubarak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abu%27l-Fazl_ibn_Mubarak "Abu'l-Fazl ibn Mubarak") mentions in his *[Ain-i-Akbari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ain-i-Akbari "Ain-i-Akbari")* that the addition of the suffix *"al"* came from the fact that the ancient rajahs of the land raised mounds of earth in lowlands at the foot of the hills which were called "al".[^28] This is also mentioned in [Ghulam Husain Salim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghulam_Husain_Salim "Ghulam Husain Salim") 's [Riyaz-us-Salatin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riyaz-us-Salatin "Riyaz-us-Salatin").[^29]

The Indo-Aryan suffix *[Desh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/-desh "-desh")* is derived from the Sanskrit word *deśha*, which means "land" or "country". Hence, the name *Bangladesh* means "Land of Bengal" or "Country of Bengal".[^24]

## History

### Early history

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/53/Mahasthan_Brahmi_Inscription.png/250px-Mahasthan_Brahmi_Inscription.png)

The Mahasthan Brahmi Inscription from the 3rd century BCE, discovered at Mahasthangarh, Bogra District in 1931

The first great indigenous empire to cover the territory was the [Maurya Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurya_Empire "Maurya Empire") (c. 320–185 BC). Following its decline, the kingdom of [Samatata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samatata "Samatata") arose, which was a tributary state of the [Gupta Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gupta_Empire "Gupta Empire") (ca. 319–ca. 540 AD). [Harsha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harsha "Harsha") (606–47 AD) drew Samatata into its loosely administered political structure. The Buddhist [Pala Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pala_Empire "Pala Empire") ruled the region from 750 to 1150 AD. It was overthrown by the Hindu [Sena dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sena_dynasty "Sena dynasty"), which ruled the territory until the [Muslim conquests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslim_conquests_in_the_Indian_subcontinent "Muslim conquests in the Indian subcontinent") led by [Muhammad Bakhtiyar Khalji](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Bakhtiyar_Khalji "Muhammad Bakhtiyar Khalji") of the [Ghurid dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghurid_dynasty "Ghurid dynasty") in 1204.[^30]

### Medieval period

Bengal was then incorporated into the [Delhi Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delhi_Sultanate "Delhi Sultanate") (1206–1526 AD).[^31] In 1341, the independent [Bengal Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal_Sultanate "Bengal Sultanate") was established by [Fakhruddin Mubarak Shah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fakhruddin_Mubarak_Shah "Fakhruddin Mubarak Shah").[^31] Two dynasties ruled the independent Sultanate of Bengal consecutively, the [Hussain Shahi dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hussain_Shahi_dynasty "Hussain Shahi dynasty") and the [Ilyas Shahi dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ilyas_Shahi_dynasty "Ilyas Shahi dynasty"), with a brief reign of the [House of Ganesha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Ganesha "House of Ganesha"). Amidst geographic expansion and economic prosperity, it was regarded by European and Chinese visitors as the "richest country to trade with".[^32]<sup><span title="Page / location: 10">: 10</span> </sup> The [Mughal Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_Empire "Mughal Empire") conquered Bengal in 1576.[^31] [^33] By the 18th century, the [Bengal Subah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal_Subah "Bengal Subah") emerged as the wealthiest province of the empire and was described as the "Paradise of Countries" and the "breadbasket of India".[^31] Its citizens maintained some of the best [standards of living](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Standard_of_living "Standard of living") in the world, as the region was a major global exporter and producer of cotton textiles ([muslin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muslin_trade_in_Bengal "Muslin trade in Bengal") in particular), silk and [shipbuilding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shipbuilding_in_Bangladesh "Shipbuilding in Bangladesh").[^34]<sup><span title="Page / location: 174">: 174</span> </sup> Following the decline of the Mughal Empire in the early 1700s, the region became a semi-independent state under the [Nawabs of Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nawabs_of_Bengal "Nawabs of Bengal"), founded by [Murshid Quli Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Murshid_Quli_Khan "Murshid Quli Khan") in 1717.

### British colonial rule

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/02/Bengal_Presidency_in_British_India_with_dependencies_1852.png/250px-Bengal_Presidency_in_British_India_with_dependencies_1852.png)

Bengal Presidency (green) in British India with dependencies (gray)

On 23 June 1757, the state led by [Siraj-ud-Daulah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siraj-ud-Daulah "Siraj-ud-Daulah") was defeated by the British [East India Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_India_Company "East India Company") in the [Battle of Plassey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Plassey "Battle of Plassey") —which was key in establishing colonial [British rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Raj "British Raj") over Bengal and the wider Indian subcontinent. Bengal played a crucial role in the [Industrial Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrial_Revolution "Industrial Revolution") at the expense of an extraordinary [capital flight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capital_flight "Capital flight") and deindustrialisation following British colonial loot and the collapse of the Bengali textile industry.[^35] [^34]<sup><span title="Page / location: 7–10">: 7–10</span> </sup> The catastrophic [Great Bengal famine of 1770](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Bengal_famine_of_1770 "Great Bengal famine of 1770") caused between one ten million deaths,[^36] [^37] [^38] killing up to one-third of the total population of the Bengal Presidency.[^39]<sup><span title="Page / location: 47">: 47</span></sup>

### As a part of Pakistan

In the aftermath of [direct British rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal_Presidency "Bengal Presidency") for nearly two centuries, the borders of modern Bangladesh were established with the [partition of Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_Bengal_\(1947\) "Partition of Bengal (1947)") between India and Pakistan by the [Radcliffe Line](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radcliffe_Line "Radcliffe Line") [^40] during the [partition of India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_India "Partition of India") on 15 August 1947, when the region became [East Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Bengal "East Bengal") as the eastern and most populous wing of the newly formed [Dominion of Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion_of_Pakistan "Dominion of Pakistan") —alongside [West Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Pakistan "West Pakistan").[^41] The western and eastern wings of the newly formed Pakistan were geographically separated by a distance of over 1,000 miles, which became the root cause of deep [economic inequality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_inequality "Economic inequality").[^42] Khawaja Nazimuddin was East Bengal's first [chief minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_Minister_of_East_Bengal "Chief Minister of East Bengal") with [Frederick Chalmers Bourne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frederick_Chalmers_Bourne "Frederick Chalmers Bourne") its governor. The [All Pakistan Awami Muslim League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All_Pakistan_Awami_Muslim_League "All Pakistan Awami Muslim League") was formed in 1949. In 1950, the [East Bengal Legislative Assembly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Bengal_Legislative_Assembly "East Bengal Legislative Assembly") enacted [land reform](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Land_reform "Land reform"), abolishing the Permanent Settlement and the zamindari system.[^43] The Awami Muslim League was renamed as a more "secular" [Awami League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Awami_League "Awami League") in 1953.[^44] The first constituent assembly was dissolved in 1954. The [United Front](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Front_\(East_Pakistan\) "United Front (East Pakistan)") coalition led by [A. K. Fazlul Huq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A._K._Fazlul_Huq "A. K. Fazlul Huq") swept aside the Muslim League in a landslide victory in the [1954 East Bengali legislative election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1954_East_Bengali_legislative_election "1954 East Bengali legislative election"). East Bengal was renamed [East Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Pakistan "East Pakistan") in 1956 as part of the [One Unit Scheme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/One_Unit_Scheme "One Unit Scheme") under the [new constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1956_Constitution_of_Pakistan "1956 Constitution of Pakistan"), and the province became a vital part of the [Southeast Asia Treaty Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeast_Asia_Treaty_Organization "Southeast Asia Treaty Organization").

Amidst rising cultural and societal differences, the brutal government crackdown on the 1952 [Bengali language movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengali_language_movement "Bengali language movement") to establish [Bengali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengali_language "Bengali language") as the official language of Pakistan spurred [Bengali nationalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengali_nationalism "Bengali nationalism") and [pro-democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pro-democracy "Pro-democracy") movements. Pakistan adopted a new constitution in 1956.[^45] The [Pakistan Armed Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan_Armed_Forces "Pakistan Armed Forces") imposed [martial law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Martial_law "Martial law") in 1958, following a [coup d'état](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1958_Pakistani_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat "1958 Pakistani coup d'état"), with [Ayub Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ayub_Khan_\(general\) "Ayub Khan (general)") establishing a dictatorship for over a decade. A new constitution was introduced in 1962, replacing the [parliamentary system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_system "Parliamentary system") with a presidential and gubernatorial system (based on [electoral college](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electoral_college "Electoral college") selection) known as "Basic Democracy".[^46] In 1962, Dhaka became the seat of the [National Assembly of Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Assembly_of_Pakistan "National Assembly of Pakistan"), a move seen as appeasing increased Bengali nationalism.[^47] In 1966, Awami League leader [Sheikh Mujibur Rahman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheikh_Mujibur_Rahman "Sheikh Mujibur Rahman") announced a [six-point movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six_point_movement "Six point movement") for a federal parliamentary democracy.

Ethnic, linguistic, and cultural discrimination was common in Pakistan's civil and military services, in which Bengalis were under-represented;[^48] leading to East Pakistan forging a distinct political identity.[^49] Authorities banned Bengali literature and music in the state media.[^50] The Pakistani government practised extensive [economic discrimination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_discrimination "Economic discrimination") against East Pakistan, including the refusal for foreign aid allocation.[^51] Despite generating 70% of Pakistan's export revenue with jute and tea, East Pakistan received much less government spending. Notable economists from East Pakistan, including [Rehman Sobhan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rehman_Sobhan "Rehman Sobhan") and [Nurul Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nurul_Islam_\(economist\) "Nurul Islam (economist)") demanded a separate foreign exchange account for the eastern wing, also pointing to the existence of two different economies within Pakistan itself, dubbed the Two-Economies Theory.[^52] [^53] The populist leader Sheikh Mujibur Rahman was arrested for treason in the [Agartala Conspiracy Case](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agartala_Conspiracy_Case "Agartala Conspiracy Case") and was released during the [1969 uprising in East Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1969_uprising_in_East_Pakistan "1969 uprising in East Pakistan") which resulted in Ayub Khan's resignation. General [Yahya Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yahya_Khan "Yahya Khan") assumed power, reintroducing martial law.

After the Pakistan Government's poor response to the [1970 Bhola Cyclone](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1970_Bhola_Cyclone "1970 Bhola Cyclone") which left over 500,000 people dead,[^54] [Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdul_Hamid_Khan_Bhashani "Abdul Hamid Khan Bhashani") a prominent political figure who had previously led multiple rebellion against the [British Raj](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Raj "British Raj") was the first ever Bengali to declare independence of East Pakistan in a massive public rally on 23 November 1970 but it was not officially recognized.[^55] [^56] After the December 1970 elections, the Bengali-nationalist Awami League won 167 of 169 East Pakistani seats in the National Assembly. The League claimed the right to form a government and develop a new constitution but was strongly opposed by the Pakistani military and the [Pakistan Peoples Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan_Peoples_Party "Pakistan Peoples Party") led by [Zulfikar Ali Bhutto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zulfikar_Ali_Bhutto "Zulfikar Ali Bhutto").

The [7 March Speech](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/7_March_Speech_of_Sheikh_Mujibur_Rahman "7 March Speech of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman") of Mujib led to a [non-cooperation movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-cooperation_movement_\(1971\) "Non-cooperation movement (1971)"). The autocratic Pakistani government then initiated [Operation Searchlight](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Searchlight "Operation Searchlight") on 25 March 1971 in response.[^57] While Mujib was arrested by the Pakistani Army on 26 March 1971, freedom fighter Major [Ziaur Rahman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ziaur_Rahman "Ziaur Rahman") publicly broadcast the independence of Bangladesh on behalf of Mujib.[^58] A nine-month-long bloody [liberation war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Liberation_War "Bangladesh Liberation War") ensued, during which [a genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_genocide "Bangladesh genocide") of ethnic Bengalis occurred.[^59] The culmination of the war established Bangladesh as a sovereign nation following [Pakistani surrender](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistani_Instrument_of_Surrender "Pakistani Instrument of Surrender") on 16 December 1971.

### Independent Bangladesh

The [Constitution of Bangladesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Bangladesh "Constitution of Bangladesh") was enacted on 4 November 1972.[^60] Following independence, the Mujib-led government engaged in large-scale corruption and mismanagement, leading to nationwide lawlessness and economic devastation. Efforts to establish [One-party socialism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BAKSAL "BAKSAL") and a [large famine in 1974](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_famine_of_1974 "Bangladesh famine of 1974") led to [Mujib's assassination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassination_of_Sheikh_Mujibur_Rahman "Assassination of Sheikh Mujibur Rahman") in 1975 following a significant decline in his popularity.[^61] [^62]<sup><span title="Page / location: 131">: 131</span> </sup> The presidency was then transferred to [Ziaur Rahman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ziaur_Rahman "Ziaur Rahman"), who re-established public order, industrialised agriculture, founded the [Bangladesh Nationalist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Nationalist_Party "Bangladesh Nationalist Party") and initiated the creation of the [South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asian_Association_for_Regional_Cooperation "South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation").[^63] Ziaur Rahman founded the [Bangladesh Nationalist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Nationalist_Party "Bangladesh Nationalist Party") on September 1, 1978.[^64] Following [Rahman's assassination](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assassination_of_Ziaur_Rahman "Assassination of Ziaur Rahman") in 1981, the ensuing decade was a [military dictatorship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_dictatorship "Military dictatorship") under [Hussain Muhammad Ershad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hussain_Muhammad_Ershad "Hussain Muhammad Ershad") that saw infrastructural development, [devolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Devolution "Devolution") reforms, privatisation of nationalised industries and the declaration of Islam as the [state religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_religion "State religion") in 1988.[^65] [^66] [^67] [^68]

After the [restoration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1990_Bangladesh_mass_uprising "1990 Bangladesh mass uprising") of [parliamentary democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parliamentary_democracy "Parliamentary democracy") in [1991](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1991_Bangladeshi_general_election "1991 Bangladeshi general election"), power alternated between [Khaleda Zia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khaleda_Zia "Khaleda Zia") of the BNP and [Sheikh Hasina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheikh_Hasina "Sheikh Hasina") of the Awami League, an era dubbed the [Battle of Begums](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Begums "Battle of Begums") —which defined Bangladesh's politics and history for 34 years until 2024.[^69] [^62]<sup><span title="Page / location: 130">: 130</span> </sup> Following the return of the Awami League to power after the [2008 general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2008_Bangladeshi_general_election "2008 Bangladeshi general election").[^70] The country under [Sheikh Hasina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sheikh_Hasina "Sheikh Hasina") 's leadership saw economic progress but also witnessed [democratic backsliding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratic_backsliding "Democratic backsliding"), increasing [authoritarianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authoritarianism "Authoritarianism"), endemic [corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Bangladesh "Corruption in Bangladesh"), and widespread [human rights abuses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights_in_Bangladesh "Human rights in Bangladesh").[^71] According to various reports Hasina and the Awami League Government, aided by cooperation with India, organized the [2009 Bangladesh Rifles revolt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2009_Bangladesh_Rifles_revolt "2009 Bangladesh Rifles revolt") to kill capable army officers whom Hasina deemed a threat to her power destabilizing the [Bangladesh Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Army "Bangladesh Army") and solidifying de facto one party rule.[^72] [^73] Hasina won her second, third and fourth consecutive terms in the [2014](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2014_Bangladeshi_general_election "2014 Bangladeshi general election"), [2018](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2018_Bangladeshi_general_election "2018 Bangladeshi general election") and the [2024 general elections](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Bangladeshi_general_election "2024 Bangladeshi general election") —all of which were shams and neither [free nor fair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_and_fair_election "Free and fair election").[^74] [^75] [^76] Following a [July Uprising](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/July_Uprising "July Uprising") against the authoritarian government, Hasina was [forced to resign and flee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Non-cooperation_movement_\(2024\) "Non-cooperation movement (2024)") to India on [5 August 2024](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/July_36 "July 36").[^77] An interim government was formed in August 2024, with Nobel laureate [Muhammad Yunus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad_Yunus "Muhammad Yunus") as the [Chief Adviser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_Adviser_of_Bangladesh "Chief Adviser of Bangladesh").[^78] After the [2026 general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2026_Bangladeshi_general_election "2026 Bangladeshi general election") the [Bangladesh Nationalist Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Nationalist_Party "Bangladesh Nationalist Party") (BNP) returned to power with Chairman [Tarique Rahman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarique_Rahman "Tarique Rahman") being inaugurated as the Prime Minister of Bangladesh on 17 February 2026. [Bangladesh Jamaat-e-Islami](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Jamaat-e-Islami "Bangladesh Jamaat-e-Islami") became the main opposition party.[^79]

Since the early 1990s, driven by [free market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_market "Free market") policies and [economic liberalisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_liberalisation "Economic liberalisation") measures, Bangladesh has achieved significant economic growth—emerging as one of the [fastest-growing economies in the world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_real_GDP_growth_rate "List of countries by real GDP growth rate"), driven by its large [textile industry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Textile_industry_in_Bangladesh "Textile industry in Bangladesh"), which is the second-largest in the world.[^80] It has emerged as the [second-largest economy in South Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\) "List of countries by GDP (nominal)"), surpassing the nominal GDP per capita of neighboring India since 2018.[^81] [^82] Bangladesh has achieved remarkable feats in reducing its [poverty rate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty_in_Bangladesh "Poverty in Bangladesh"), which has gone down from 80% in 1971,[^83] to 44.2% in 1991,[^84] and all the way down to 18.7% in 2022.[^85] Its [Human Development Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_Human_Development_Index "List of countries by Human Development Index") growth during the 21st century was surpassed only by China.[^86] As part of the [green transition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_transition "Green transition"), Bangladesh's industrial sector emerged as a leader in building [green factories](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_factory "Green factory"), with the country having the largest number of certified green factories in the world.[^87] It has also given shelter to [over a million Rohingya refugees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rohingya_refugees_in_Bangladesh "Rohingya refugees in Bangladesh") fleeing the [Rohingya genocide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rohingya_genocide "Rohingya genocide") since 2017, which has strained its resources and highlighted its humanitarian commitments.[^88]

## Geography

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Map_of_Bangladesh-en.svg/250px-Map_of_Bangladesh-en.svg.png)

Physical map of Bangladesh

Bangladesh is in South Asia on the [Bay of Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Bengal "Bay of Bengal"). It is surrounded almost entirely by neighbouring India, and shares a small border with [Myanmar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Myanmar "Myanmar") to its southeast, though it lies very close to [Nepal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nepal "Nepal"), [Bhutan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bhutan "Bhutan"), and China. The country is divided into three regions. Most of the country is dominated by the fertile [Ganges Delta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganges_Delta "Ganges Delta"), the largest river delta in the world.[^89] The northwest and central parts of the country are formed by the [Madhupur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madhupur_tract "Madhupur tract") and the [Barind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barind_Tract "Barind Tract") plateaus. The northeast and southeast are home to [evergreen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evergreen "Evergreen") hill ranges.

The Ganges delta is formed by the confluence of the Ganges (local name [Padma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Padma_River "Padma River") or *Pôdda*), [Brahmaputra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brahmaputra_River "Brahmaputra River") ([Jamuna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamuna_River_\(Bangladesh\) "Jamuna River (Bangladesh)") or *Jomuna*), and [Meghna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Meghna_River "Meghna River") rivers and their tributaries. The Ganges unites with the Jamuna (main channel of the Brahmaputra) and later joins the Meghna, finally flowing into the Bay of Bengal. Bangladesh is called the "Land of Rivers",[^90] as it is home to over 57 [trans-boundary rivers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trans-boundary_river "Trans-boundary river"), the most of any nation-state. Water issues are politically complicated since Bangladesh is downstream of India.[^91]

Bangladesh is predominantly rich fertile flat land. Most of it is less than 12 m (39 ft) above sea level, and it is estimated that about 10% of its land would be flooded if the sea level were to rise by 1 m (3.3 ft).[^92] 12% of the country is covered by hill systems. The country's [haor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Haor "Haor") wetlands are of significance to global environmental science. The [highest point in Bangladesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_mountains_of_Bangladesh "List of mountains of Bangladesh") is the [Saka Haphong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saka_Haphong "Saka Haphong"), located near the border with Myanmar, with an elevation of 1,064 m (3,491 ft).[^93] Previously, either [Keokradong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keokradong "Keokradong") or [Tazing Dong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tazing_Dong "Tazing Dong") were considered the highest.

In Bangladesh [forest cover](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest_cover "Forest cover") is around 14% of the total land area, equivalent to 1,883,400 hectares (ha) of forest in 2020, down from 1,920,330 hectares (ha) in 1990. In 2020, naturally regenerating forest covered 1,725,330 hectares (ha) and planted forest covered 158,070 hectares (ha). Of the naturally regenerating forest 0% was reported to be [primary forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primary_forest "Primary forest") (consisting of native tree species with no clearly visible indications of human activity) and around 33% of the forest area was found within protected areas. For the year 2015, 100% of the forest area was reported to be under [public ownership](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_ownership "State ownership").[^94] [^95]

### Climate

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/Aerosol_pollution_over_Northern_India%2C_Bangladesh%2C_and_Bay_of_Bengal.jpg/250px-Aerosol_pollution_over_Northern_India%2C_Bangladesh%2C_and_Bay_of_Bengal.jpg)

A photo from space showing the pollution over Bangladesh

Straddling the [Tropic of Cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer "Tropic of Cancer"), Bangladesh's climate is tropical, with a mild winter from October to March and a hot, humid summer from March to June. The country has never recorded an air temperature below 0 °C (32 °F), with a record low of 1.1 °C (34.0 °F) in the northwest city of [Dinajpur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dinajpur "Dinajpur") on 3 February 1905.[^96] A warm and humid [monsoon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monsoon "Monsoon") season lasts from June to October and supplies most of the country's rainfall. Natural calamities, such as [floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Floods_in_Bangladesh "Floods in Bangladesh"), [tropical cyclones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_cyclone "Tropical cyclone"), [tornadoes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tornado "Tornado"), and [tidal bores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tidal_bore "Tidal bore") occur almost every year,[^97] combined with the effects of [deforestation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deforestation "Deforestation"), [soil degradation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soils_retrogression_and_degradation "Soils retrogression and degradation") and [erosion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Erosion "Erosion"). The [cyclones](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Bangladesh_tropical_cyclones "List of Bangladesh tropical cyclones") of 1970 and 1991 were particularly devastating, the [latter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1991_Bangladesh_cyclone "1991 Bangladesh cyclone") killing approximately 140,000 people.[^98]

In September 1998, Bangladesh saw the [most severe flooding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1998_Bangladesh_floods "1998 Bangladesh floods") in modern history, after which two-thirds of the country went underwater, along with a death toll of 1,000.[^99] As a result of various international and national level initiatives in disaster risk reduction, the human toll and economic damage from floods and cyclones have come down over the years.[^100] The [2007 South Asian floods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2007_South_Asian_floods "2007 South Asian floods") ravaged areas across the country, leaving five million people displaced, with a death toll around 500.[^101]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/90/Flooding_after_1991_cyclone.jpg/250px-Flooding_after_1991_cyclone.jpg)

Flooding after the 1991 Bangladesh cyclone, which killed around 140,000 people

#### Climate change

Bangladesh is recognised to be one of the countries most [vulnerable to climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_vulnerability "Climate change vulnerability").[^102] [^103] Over the course of a century, 508 cyclones have affected the Bay of Bengal region, 17% of which are believed to have made landfall in Bangladesh.[^104] [Natural hazards](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_hazards "Natural hazards") that come from increased rainfall, rising sea levels, and tropical cyclones are expected to increase as the climate changes, each seriously affecting agriculture, water and food security, human health, and shelter.[^105] It is estimated that by 2050, a three-foot rise in sea levels will inundate some 20% of the land and displace more than 30 million people.[^106] To address the [sea level rise](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sea_level_rise "Sea level rise") threat in Bangladesh, the [Bangladesh Delta Plan 2100](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Delta_Plan_2100 "Bangladesh Delta Plan 2100") has been launched.[^107] [^108]

### Biodiversity

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d4/Bengal_Tiger_gets_down_in_a_shallow_canal_in_Sundarban.jpg/250px-Bengal_Tiger_gets_down_in_a_shallow_canal_in_Sundarban.jpg)

A Bengal tiger, the national animal, in the Sundarbans

Bangladesh is located in the [Indomalayan realm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indomalayan_realm "Indomalayan realm"), and lies within four terrestrial ecoregions: [Lower Gangetic Plains moist deciduous forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lower_Gangetic_Plains_moist_deciduous_forests "Lower Gangetic Plains moist deciduous forests"), [Mizoram–Manipur–Kachin rain forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mizoram%E2%80%93Manipur%E2%80%93Kachin_rain_forests "Mizoram–Manipur–Kachin rain forests"), [Sundarbans freshwater swamp forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundarbans_freshwater_swamp_forests "Sundarbans freshwater swamp forests"), and [Sundarbans mangroves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundarbans_mangroves "Sundarbans mangroves").[^109] Its ecology includes a long sea coastline, numerous [rivers and tributaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_rivers_in_Bangladesh "List of rivers in Bangladesh"), lakes, [wetlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wetland "Wetland"), evergreen forests, semi evergreen forests, hill forests, moist [deciduous forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deciduous_forest "Deciduous forest"), freshwater swamp forests and flat land with tall grass. The Bangladesh Plain is famous for its fertile [alluvial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alluvial "Alluvial") soil which supports extensive cultivation. The country is dominated by lush vegetation, with villages often buried in groves of [mango](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mango "Mango"), [jackfruit](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jackfruit "Jackfruit"), [bamboo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bamboo "Bamboo"), [betel nut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Betel_nut "Betel nut"), [coconut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coconut "Coconut"), and [date palm](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Date_palm "Date palm").[^110] The country has up to 6000 species of plant life, including 5000 flowering plants.[^111] Water bodies and wetland systems provide a habitat for many aquatic plants. [Water lilies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nymphaeaceae "Nymphaeaceae") and [lotuses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nelumbo_nucifera "Nelumbo nucifera") grow vividly during the monsoon season. The country has [60 wildlife sanctuaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_protected_areas_of_Bangladesh "List of protected areas of Bangladesh").[^112]

Bangladesh is home to most of the [Sundarbans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundarbans "Sundarbans"), the world's largest [mangrove forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mangrove_forest "Mangrove forest"), covering an area of 6,000 square kilometres (2,300 sq mi) in the southwest littoral region. It is divided into three protected sanctuaries: the [South](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundarbans_South_Wildlife_Sanctuary "Sundarbans South Wildlife Sanctuary"), [East](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundarbans_East_Wildlife_Sanctuary "Sundarbans East Wildlife Sanctuary"), and [West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundarbans_West_Wildlife_Sanctuary "Sundarbans West Wildlife Sanctuary") zones. The forest is a UNESCO World Heritage Site.[^113] The northeastern Sylhet region is home to haor wetlands, a unique ecosystem. It also includes [tropical and subtropical coniferous forests](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_and_subtropical_coniferous_forests "Tropical and subtropical coniferous forests"), a [freshwater swamp forest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freshwater_swamp_forest "Freshwater swamp forest"), and mixed deciduous forests. The southeastern Chittagong region covers evergreen and semi-evergreen hilly jungles. Central Bangladesh includes the plainland Sal forest running along with the districts of Gazipur, [Tangail](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangail "Tangail"), and [Mymensingh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mymensingh "Mymensingh").[^114] [St. Martin's Island](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Martin%27s_Island "St. Martin's Island") is the only [coral reef](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coral_reef "Coral reef") in the country.[^115]

Bangladesh has an abundance of [wildlife](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildlife_in_Bangladesh "Wildlife in Bangladesh") in its forests, marshes, woodlands, and hills.[^110] The vast majority of animals dwell within a habitat of 150,000 square kilometres (58,000 sq mi).[^116] The [Bengal tiger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal_tiger "Bengal tiger"), [clouded leopard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clouded_leopard "Clouded leopard"), [saltwater crocodile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saltwater_crocodile "Saltwater crocodile"), [black panther](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_panther "Black panther") and [fishing cat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fishing_cat "Fishing cat") are among the chief predators in the Sundarbans.[^117] Northern and eastern Bangladesh is home to the [Asian elephant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_elephant "Asian elephant"), [hoolock gibbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hoolock_gibbon "Hoolock gibbon"), [Asian black bear](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_black_bear "Asian black bear") and [oriental pied hornbill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oriental_pied_hornbill "Oriental pied hornbill").[^118] The [chital](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chital "Chital") deer are widely seen in southwestern woodlands. Other animals include the [black giant squirrel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_giant_squirrel "Black giant squirrel"), [capped langur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capped_langur "Capped langur"), [Bengal fox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal_fox "Bengal fox"), [sambar deer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sambar_deer "Sambar deer"), [jungle cat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jungle_cat "Jungle cat"), [king cobra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/King_cobra "King cobra"), [wild boar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wild_boar "Wild boar"), [mongooses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongoose "Mongoose"), [pangolins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pangolin "Pangolin"), [pythons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Python_\(genus\) "Python (genus)") and [water monitors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_water_monitor "Asian water monitor"). Bangladesh has one of the largest populations of [Irrawaddy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irrawaddy_dolphins "Irrawaddy dolphins") and [Ganges dolphins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asian_river_dolphin "South Asian river dolphin").[^119] The country has numerous species of [amphibians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amphibian "Amphibian") (53), reptiles (139), [marine reptiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_reptile "Marine reptile") (19) and [marine mammals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_mammal "Marine mammal") (5). It also has [628 species of birds](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_birds_of_Bangladesh "List of birds of Bangladesh").[^120]

Several animals became extinct in Bangladesh during the last century, including the one-horned and two-horned [rhinoceros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhinoceros "Rhinoceros") and common [peafowl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peafowl "Peafowl"). The human population is concentrated in urban areas, limiting deforestation to a certain extent. Rapid urban growth has threatened natural habitats. The country has widespread environmental issues; pollution of the [Dhaleshwari River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dhaleshwari_River "Dhaleshwari River") by the textile industry and shrimp cultivation in Chakaria Sundarbans have both been described by academics as [ecocides](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecocide "Ecocide").[^121] [^122] Although many areas are protected under law, some Bangladeshi wildlife is threatened by this growth. The [Bangladesh Environment Conservation Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Environment_Conservation_Act "Bangladesh Environment Conservation Act") was enacted in 1995. The government has designated several regions as [Ecologically Critical Areas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecologically_Critical_Area "Ecologically Critical Area"), including wetlands, forests, and rivers. The [Sundarbans tiger project](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sundarbans_tiger_project "Sundarbans tiger project") and the Bangladesh Bear Project are among the key initiatives to strengthen conservation.[^118] It ratified the Rio [Convention on Biological Diversity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Convention_on_Biological_Diversity "Convention on Biological Diversity") on 3 May 1994.[^123] As of 2014, the country was set to revise its [National Biodiversity Strategy and Action Plan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity_action_plan "Biodiversity action plan").[^123]
