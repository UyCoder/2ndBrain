**Mexico**,[^1] [^2] officially the **United Mexican States**,[^3] is a country in [North America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_America "North America").[^15] It is the northernmost country in [Latin America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_America "Latin America") and borders the [United States of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States") to the north, and [Guatemala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guatemala "Guatemala") and [Belize](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Belize "Belize") to the southeast; while having [maritime boundaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_boundary "Maritime boundary") with the [Pacific Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean "Pacific Ocean") to the west, the [Caribbean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caribbean_Sea "Caribbean Sea") to the southeast, and the [Gulf of Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico "Gulf of Mexico") to the east. Mexico covers 1,972,550 km <sup>2</sup> (761,610 sq mi),[^16] and is the [thirteenth-largest country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_area "List of countries by area") in the world by land area. With a population exceeding 134 million as of 2026,[^7] Mexico is the [tenth-most populous country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_population "List of countries by population") in the world and is home to the [largest number of native Spanish speakers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hispanophone#Countries "Hispanophone").[^4] [Mexico City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico_City "Mexico City") is the capital and [largest city in Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Mexico "List of cities in Mexico"), which ranks among the [most populous metropolitan areas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_by_population "List of cities by population") in the world.

Human presence in Mexico dates back to around 28,000 BC.[^17] [Mesoamerica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoamerica "Mesoamerica"), considered a [cradle of civilization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cradle_of_civilization "Cradle of civilization"), was home to numerous advanced societies, including the [Olmecs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olmecs "Olmecs"), [Maya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_civilization "Maya civilization"), [Zapotecs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapotec_civilization "Zapotec civilization"), [Teotihuacan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teotihuacan "Teotihuacan") civilization, and [Purépecha](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pur%C3%A9pecha_Empire "Purépecha Empire"). [Spanish colonization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_colonization_of_the_Americas "Spanish colonization of the Americas") began in 1521 with an alliance that [defeated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_the_Aztec_Empire "Spanish conquest of the Aztec Empire") the [Aztec Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aztec_Empire "Aztec Empire"), establishing the colony of [New Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Spain "New Spain") with its capital at [Tenochtitlan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tenochtitlan "Tenochtitlan"), now Mexico City. New Spain became a major center of the transoceanic economy during the [Age of Discovery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Discovery "Age of Discovery"), fueled by [silver mining](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camino_Real_de_Tierra_Adentro "Camino Real de Tierra Adentro") and its position as a [hub between Europe and Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manila_galleon "Manila galleon"). This gave rise to one of the largest multiracial populations in the world. The [Peninsular War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peninsular_War "Peninsular War") led to the 1810–1821 [Mexican War of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_War_of_Independence "Mexican War of Independence"), which ended Peninsular rule and led to the creation of the [First Mexican Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Mexican_Empire "First Mexican Empire"), which quickly collapsed into the short-lived [First Mexican Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Mexican_Republic "First Mexican Republic"). In 1848, Mexico [lost nearly half its territory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Territorial_evolution_of_Mexico "Territorial evolution of Mexico") in the [Mexican-American War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican%E2%80%93American_War "Mexican–American War"). [Liberal reforms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Reforma "La Reforma") set in the [Constitution of 1857](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_1857 "Constitution of 1857") led to [civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reform_War "Reform War") and [French intervention](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_French_intervention_in_Mexico "Second French intervention in Mexico"), culminating in the establishment of the [Second Mexican Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Mexican_Empire "Second Mexican Empire") under [Emperor Maximilian I of Habsburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximilian_I_of_Mexico "Maximilian I of Mexico"), who was overthrown by Republican forces led by [Benito Juárez](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benito_Ju%C3%A1rez "Benito Juárez"). The late 19th century saw [the long dictatorship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porfiriato "Porfiriato") of [Porfirio Díaz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porfirio_D%C3%ADaz "Porfirio Díaz"), whose modernization policies came at the cost of severe social unrest. The 1910–1920 [Mexican Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_Revolution "Mexican Revolution") led to the overthrow of Díaz and the adoption of the [1917 Constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Mexico "Constitution of Mexico"). Mexico experienced [rapid industrialization and economic growth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_Miracle "Mexican Miracle") in the 1940s–1970s, amidst [electoral fraud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electoral_fraud "Electoral fraud"), the [Tlatelolco massacre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tlatelolco_massacre "Tlatelolco massacre"), and economic crises. The late 20th century saw a shift towards [neoliberalism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neoliberalism "Neoliberalism"), marked by the signing of the [North American Free Trade Agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_American_Free_Trade_Agreement "North American Free Trade Agreement") (NAFTA) in 1994, amidst [unrest in Chiapas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiapas_conflict "Chiapas conflict").

Mexico is a [federal republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_republic "Federal republic") with a [presidential system of government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Mexico "President of Mexico"), characterized by a democratic framework and the separation of powers into three branches: [executive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_\(government\) "Executive (government)"), legislative, and judicial. The federal legislature consists of the [bicameral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bicameralism "Bicameralism") [Congress of the Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_the_Union "Congress of the Union"), comprising the [Chamber of Deputies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chamber_of_Deputies_\(Mexico\) "Chamber of Deputies (Mexico)"), which represents the population, and the [Senate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senate_of_the_Republic_\(Mexico\) "Senate of the Republic (Mexico)"), which provides equal representation for each state. The Constitution establishes three levels of government: the federal Union, the state governments, and the municipal governments. Mexico's federal structure grants autonomy to its 32 states, and its political system is deeply influenced by indigenous traditions and [European Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Enlightenment "European Enlightenment") ideals.

Mexico is a [newly industrialized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newly_industrialized_country "Newly industrialized country") and [developing country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developing_country "Developing country"),[^18] with the world's [15th-largest economy by nominal GDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\) "List of countries by GDP (nominal)") and the [13th-largest by purchasing power parity (PPP)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(PPP\) "List of countries by GDP (PPP)"). It ranks [first in the Americas and seventh in the world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_World_Heritage_Sites_in_Mexico "List of World Heritage Sites in Mexico") by the number of [UNESCO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UNESCO "UNESCO") [World Heritage Sites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Heritage_Site "World Heritage Site").[^19] It is one of the world's 17 [megadiverse countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megadiverse_countries "Megadiverse countries"), ranking fifth in natural [biodiversity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity "Biodiversity").[^20] It is a major tourist destination: as of 2022, it is the [sixth most-visited country in the world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Tourism_rankings "World Tourism rankings"), with 42.2 million international arrivals.[^21] Mexico's large economy and population, global cultural influence, and steady [democratization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democratization "Democratization") make it a [regional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_power "Regional power") and [middle power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_power "Middle power"),[^22] [^23] [^24] increasingly identifying as an [emerging power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emerging_power "Emerging power").[^25] [^26] [^27] [^28] As with much of Latin America, [poverty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty_in_Mexico "Poverty in Mexico"), [systemic corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Mexico "Corruption in Mexico"), and [crime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crime_in_Mexico "Crime in Mexico") remain widespread.[^29] Since 2006, approximately 127,000 deaths have been caused by [ongoing conflict](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_drug_war "Mexican drug war") between [drug trafficking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drug_trafficking "Drug trafficking") syndicates.[^30] [^31] [^32]

Mexico is a member of [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations"), the [G20](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G20 "G20"), the [Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_for_Economic_Co-operation_and_Development "Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development") (OECD), the [Coffee Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coffee_Club "Coffee Club"), the [World Trade Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Trade_Organization "World Trade Organization") (WTO), the [Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia-Pacific_Economic_Cooperation "Asia-Pacific Economic Cooperation") (APEC) forum, the [Organization of American States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organization_of_American_States "Organization of American States") (OAS), the [Community of Latin American and Caribbean States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_of_Latin_American_and_Caribbean_States "Community of Latin American and Caribbean States") (CELAC), the [Organization of Ibero-American States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organization_of_Ibero-American_States "Organization of Ibero-American States") (OEI), and has an observer status at the [Council of Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Europe "Council of Europe").[^33]

## Etymology

*[Mēxihko](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Name_of_Mexico "Name of Mexico")* is the [Nahuatl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nahuatl "Nahuatl") term for the heartland of the [Aztec Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aztec_Empire "Aztec Empire"), namely the [Valley of Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Valley_of_Mexico "Valley of Mexico") and surrounding territories, with its people being known as the [Mexica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexica "Mexica"). It is generally believed that the [toponym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toponym "Toponym") for the valley was the origin of the primary [ethnonym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnonym "Ethnonym") for the [Aztec Triple Alliance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aztec_Triple_Alliance "Aztec Triple Alliance"), but it may have been the other way around.[^34] In the colonial era (1521–1821) when Mexico was known as [New Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Spain "New Spain"), this central region became the Intendency of Mexico. After New Spain achieved independence from the [Spanish Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Empire "Spanish Empire") in 1821 and became a sovereign state, the Intendency came to be known as the [State of Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_Mexico "State of Mexico"), with the new country being named after its capital, [Mexico City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico_City "Mexico City").

The country's official name has changed as the [form of government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Form_of_government "Form of government") has changed. The declaration of independence signed on 6 November 1813 by the deputies of the [Congress of Anáhuac](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_An%C3%A1huac "Congress of Anáhuac") called the territory *[América Septentrional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Solemn_Act_of_the_Declaration_of_Independence_of_Northern_America "Solemn Act of the Declaration of Independence of Northern America")* (Northern America). The 1821 [Plan of Iguala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan_of_Iguala "Plan of Iguala") also used *América Septentrional*. On two occasions (1821–1823 and 1863–1867), the country was known as *Imperio Mexicano* ([Mexican Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_Empire_\(disambiguation\) "Mexican Empire (disambiguation)")). All three federal constitutions (1824, 1857, and 1917, the current constitution) used the name *Estados Unidos Mexicanos* [^35] —or the variant *Estados-Unidos Mexicanos*,[^36] all of which have been translated as "United Mexican States". The phrase *República Mexicana*, "Mexican Republic", was used in the 1836 [Constitutional Laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siete_Leyes "Siete Leyes").[^37] In the Nahuatl version of the current constitution,[^38] the official name is *Mexika Sentik Wexteyowalko*,[^39] and in its [Yucatec Maya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yucatec_Maya_language "Yucatec Maya language") version, *U Múuchꞌ Péetluꞌumiloꞌob México*.[^40] In fact, by virtue of the legal equality of Spanish with the 68 indigenous languages of the country, all have their own translation of the official name.

## History

### Indigenous civilizations before European contact (pre-1519)

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Off-center_view_of_Pyramid_of_the_Sun_from_Pyramid_of_the_Moon%2C_Teotihuacan.jpg/250px-Off-center_view_of_Pyramid_of_the_Sun_from_Pyramid_of_the_Moon%2C_Teotihuacan.jpg)

Teotihuacan was the 6th largest city in the world at its peak, 1 AD to 500 AD

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/51/Chichen_Itza_3.jpg/250px-Chichen_Itza_3.jpg)

Temple of Kukulcán (El Castillo) in the Maya city of Chichen Itza

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a1/Painting_of_Tenochtitlan-Tlatelolco_on_Lake_Texcoco_%289755215791%29.jpg/250px-Painting_of_Tenochtitlan-Tlatelolco_on_Lake_Texcoco_%289755215791%29.jpg)

An artistic depiction of Mexico-Tenochtitlan, the Aztec capital and largest city in the Americas at the time. The city was completely destroyed in the 1521 siege of Tenochtitlan and rebuilt as Mexico City

The earliest human artifacts in Mexico are chips of [stone tools](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stone_tool "Stone tool") found in the Valley of Mexico, dated to circa 10,000 years ago.[^41] Mexico is the site of the domestication of maize, tomato, and [beans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phaseolus "Phaseolus"), which produced an agricultural surplus. This enabled the transition from [paleo-Indian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paleo-Indians "Paleo-Indians") hunter-gatherers to sedentary agricultural villages beginning around 5000 BC.[^42] The formative period of Mesoamerica is one of the six independent [cradles of civilization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cradles_of_civilization "Cradles of civilization"), this era saw the origin of distinct cultural traits such as religious and symbolic traditions, maize cultivation, artistic and architectural complexes as well as a [vigesimal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vigesimal "Vigesimal") (base 20) numeric system [^43] that spread from Mexican cultures to the rest of [Mesoamerica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoamerica "Mesoamerica"). Villages became more densely populated, socially stratified with an artisan class, and developed into [chiefdoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiefdom "Chiefdom"). The most powerful rulers had religious and political power, organizing the construction of ceremonial centers.[^44]

The earliest complex civilization was the [Olmec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Olmec "Olmec") culture, which flourished on the Gulf Coast from around 1500 BC. Olmec cultural traits diffused through Mexico into other formative-era cultures in Chiapas, Oaxaca, and the Valley of Mexico.[^45] In the [pre-classical period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoamerican_chronology "Mesoamerican chronology"), the [Maya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_civilization "Maya civilization") and [Zapotec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapotec_civilization "Zapotec civilization") civilizations developed complex centers. The first true [Mesoamerican writing systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoamerican_writing_systems "Mesoamerican writing systems") were developed in the [Epi-Olmec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epi-Olmec_culture "Epi-Olmec culture") and Zapotec cultures. The Mesoamerican writing tradition reached its height in the Classic [Maya Hieroglyphic script](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_Script "Maya Script"), the earliest written histories date from then. The tradition of writing was important after Spanish conquest in 1521, with indigenous scribes learning to write their languages in alphabetic letters, while continuing to create pictorial texts.[^46] [^47]

In Central Mexico, the height of the classic period saw the ascendancy of [Teotihuacán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Teotihuacan "Teotihuacan"), which formed a military and commercial empire. Teotihuacan, with a population of more than 150,000, had some of the largest [pyramidal structures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoamerican_pyramid "Mesoamerican pyramid") in the pre-Columbian Americas.[^48] After the collapse of Teotihuacán around 600 AD, competition ensued between political centers in such as [Xochicalco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xochicalco "Xochicalco") and [Cholula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cholula_\(Mesoamerican_site\) "Cholula (Mesoamerican site)"). During the Epi-Classic, [Nahua peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nahua_people "Nahua people") began moving south into Mesoamerica, and became politically and culturally dominant in central Mexico. During 1000–1519 AD, central Mexico was dominated by the [Toltec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Toltec "Toltec") culture, [Oaxaca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oaxaca "Oaxaca") by the [Mixtec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mixtec_civilization "Mixtec civilization"), and the lowland Maya area had centers at [Chichén Itzá](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chich%C3%A9n_Itz%C3%A1 "Chichén Itzá") and [Mayapán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mayap%C3%A1n "Mayapán"). Toward the end of the post-Classic period, the [Aztecs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aztecs "Aztecs") established dominance, establishing a [political and economic empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aztec_Empire "Aztec Empire") based in [Tenochtitlan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tenochtitlan "Tenochtitlan") (modern [Mexico City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico_City "Mexico City")), extending from central Mexico to Guatemala.[^49]

### Spanish conquest and colonial era (1519–1821)

Although the [Spanish Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Empire "Spanish Empire") established colonies in the [Caribbean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caribbean "Caribbean") in 1493, it first learned of Mexico during the [Juan de Grijalva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Juan_de_Grijalva "Juan de Grijalva") expedition of 1518. The [Spanish conquest of the Aztec Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_conquest_of_the_Aztec_Empire "Spanish conquest of the Aztec Empire") began in 1519 when [Hernán Cortés](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hern%C3%A1n_Cort%C3%A9s "Hernán Cortés") founded [Veracruz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veracruz,_Veracruz "Veracruz, Veracruz"). The 1521 [capture of Tenochtitlan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capture_of_Tenochtitlan "Capture of Tenochtitlan") and posterior founding of [Mexico City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexico_City "Mexico City") on its ruins, was the beginning of a 300-year colonial era, during which Mexico was known as *Nueva España* ([New Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Spain "New Spain")). Two factors made Mexico a jewel in the Empire: the existence of large, hierarchically organized Mesoamerican populations that rendered tribute and performed obligatory labor, and discovery of silver deposits in north Mexico.[^50]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Guanajuato_Skyline.jpg/250px-Guanajuato_Skyline.jpg)

Guanajuato was one of the richest and most opulent cities in New Spain

The [Kingdom of New Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_New_Spain "Kingdom of New Spain") was created from the remnants of the Aztec empire. The two pillars of Spanish rule were the State and Church, both under the authority of the Spanish crown. In 1493 the pope granted [sweeping powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patronato_real "Patronato real") to the Spanish monarchy for its overseas empire, with the proviso it spread Christianity. In 1524, [King Charles I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_V,_Holy_Roman_Emperor "Charles V, Holy Roman Emperor") created the [Council of the Indies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_the_Indies "Council of the Indies") based in Spain to oversee State power in its colonies. The crown established a high court in Mexico City, the *[Real Audiencia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Real_Audiencia "Real Audiencia")* ('royal audience'). In 1535, the crown appointed the first [Viceroy of New Spain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_viceroys_of_New_Spain "List of viceroys of New Spain"), the highest official of the State. The Diocese of Mexico, created in 1530, was elevated to the [Archdiocese of Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Archdiocese_of_Mexico "Archdiocese of Mexico") in 1546, with an archbishop as its head. Castilian Spanish was the language of the rulers. Catholic faith was the only one permitted, with non-Catholics and Catholics, excluding Indians, holding unorthodox views subject to the [Mexican Inquisition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_Inquisition "Mexican Inquisition").[^51]

Spanish military forces, sometimes accompanied by native allies, led expeditions to conquer territory or quell rebellions. Notable Amerindian revolts include the [Chichimeca War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chichimeca_War "Chichimeca War") (1576–1606),[^52] the [Tepehuán Revolt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tepehu%C3%A1n_Revolt "Tepehuán Revolt") (1616–20),[^53] and [Pueblo Revolt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pueblo_Revolt "Pueblo Revolt") (1680). Most rebellions were small-scale, posing no major threat to the elites.[^54] To protect Mexico from the attacks of English, French, and Dutch [pirates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pirate "Pirate") and the Crown's monopoly of revenue, only two ports were open to foreign trade—Veracruz on the Atlantic, connecting to Spain, and Acapulco on the Pacific, connecting to the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines"). Pirate attacks included the 1663 [Sack of Campeche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sack_of_Campeche_\(1663\) "Sack of Campeche (1663)") [^55] and 1683 [Attack on Veracruz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attack_on_Veracruz "Attack on Veracruz").[^56]

Of greater concern to the crown was the issue of invasion, especially after Britain seized [Havana](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Havana "Havana") and [Manila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manila "Manila") in 1762, during the [Seven Years' War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Seven_Years%27_War "Seven Years' War"). It created a standing military, increased coastal fortifications, and expanded the northern [presidios](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidio "Presidio") and [missions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_missions_in_California "Spanish missions in California") into [Alta California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alta_California "Alta California"). The volatility of the urban poor in Mexico City was evident in the 1692 riot over the price of maize. It escalated to a full-scale attack on the seats of power, with the viceregal palace and archbishop's residence attacked.[^57]

### Independence era (1808–1855)

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/17/Plaza_de_Dolores.JPG/250px-Plaza_de_Dolores.JPG)

Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla 's Cry of Dolores on 16 September 1810, by J.J. del Moral. The call to arms marks the beginning of Mexico's War of Independence against Spanish colonial rule

In 1810, [secular priest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secular_clergy "Secular clergy") [Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguel_Hidalgo_y_Costilla "Miguel Hidalgo y Costilla") declared against "bad government" in [Dolores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dolores_Hidalgo "Dolores Hidalgo"), Guanajuato. Known as the [Cry of Dolores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cry_of_Dolores "Cry of Dolores") ([Spanish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language "Spanish language"): *Grito de Dolores*) it is commemorated each year, on 16 September, as Mexico's independence day.[^58] The upheaval in the Empire, that resulted in the independence of most of its New World territories, was due to [Napoleon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleon "Napoleon") 's invasion of Spain in 1808. Hidalgo and some of his soldiers were [executed by firing squad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Execution_by_firing_squad "Execution by firing squad") in 1811. The first 35 years after Mexico's independence were marked by instability and changing of the Mexican state from a [transient monarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Mexican_Empire "First Mexican Empire") to a fragile federated republic.[^59] There were military coups, foreign invasions, ideological conflict between [Conservatives](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conservative_Party_\(Mexico\) "Conservative Party (Mexico)") and [Liberals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberal_Party_\(Mexico\) "Liberal Party (Mexico)"), and [economic stagnation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economic_history_of_Mexico "Economic history of Mexico").

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Batalla_de_la_Alh%C3%B3ndiga_de_Granaditas.jpg/250px-Batalla_de_la_Alh%C3%B3ndiga_de_Granaditas.jpg)

The capture of Alhóndiga de Granaditas in Guanajuato by Hidalgo 's army on 28 September 1810, by José Díaz del Castillo

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a9/Generales_del_Trigarante.jpg/250px-Generales_del_Trigarante.jpg)

The entry of the Army of the Three Guarantees into Mexico City on 27 September 1821

Former Royal Army General [Agustín de Iturbide](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agust%C3%ADn_de_Iturbide "Agustín de Iturbide") became regent, as newly independent Mexico sought a [constitutional monarch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Mexican_Empire "First Mexican Empire") from Europe. When no member of a European royal house desired the position, Iturbide himself was declared Emperor Agustín I. The US was the first country to recognize Mexico's independence, sending a message to Europe via the [Monroe Doctrine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monroe_Doctrine "Monroe Doctrine") not to intervene in Mexico. The emperor's rule was short (1822–23), he was overthrown by army officers in the [Plan of Casa Mata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan_of_Casa_Mata "Plan of Casa Mata").[^60] Central America and [Chiapas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiapas "Chiapas") left the union to form the [Federal Republic of Central America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Republic_of_Central_America "Federal Republic of Central America").

In 1824, the [First Mexican Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Mexican_Republic "First Mexican Republic") was established. Former insurgent General [Guadalupe Victoria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guadalupe_Victoria "Guadalupe Victoria") became the first president — the first of many generals to hold the presidency. In 1829, former insurgent general and fierce Liberal [Vicente Guerrero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vicente_Guerrero "Vicente Guerrero"), a signatory of the [Plan of Iguala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan_of_Iguala "Plan of Iguala") that achieved independence, became president in a disputed election. During his term, from April to December 1829, he abolished slavery.[^61] His Conservative vice president, former Royalist General [Anastasio Bustamante](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anastasio_Bustamante "Anastasio Bustamante"), led a coup against him and Guerrero was [judicially murdered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judicial_murder "Judicial murder").[^62]

Mexico's ability to maintain its independence and establish a viable government was in question. Spain [attempted to reconquer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_attempts_to_reconquer_Mexico "Spanish attempts to reconquer Mexico") it during the 1820s, but eventually recognized its independence. France attempted to recoup losses it claimed for its citizens during Mexico's unrest and blockaded the Gulf Coast during the [Pastry War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pastry_War "Pastry War") of 1838–39.[^63] General [Antonio López de Santa Anna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Antonio_L%C3%B3pez_de_Santa_Anna "Antonio López de Santa Anna") emerged as a national hero because of his role in these conflicts; Santa Anna came to dominate politics for the next 25 years, often known as the "Age of Santa Anna", until his overthrow in 1855.[^64]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/de/FalloftheAlamo.jpg/250px-FalloftheAlamo.jpg)

The 1836 Battle of El Álamo between the Mexican army led by President Antonio López de Santa Anna and American troops

Mexico contended with indigenous groups that controlled the territory that Mexico claimed in the north. For example, the [Comanche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comanche "Comanche") controlled a [huge territory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Comancheria "Comancheria") in sparsely populated central and northern Texas.[^65] Wanting to stabilize and develop that area — and as few from central Mexico had chosen to resettle to this remote and hostile territory — the Mexican government encouraged [Anglo-American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Americans "Anglo-Americans") immigration into present-day Texas, a region that bordered the US. Mexico by law was a Catholic country; the Anglo-Americans were primarily Protestant English speakers from the southern US. Some brought their black slaves, which after 1829 was contrary to Mexican law.[^65]

In 1835, Santa Anna sought to centralize government rule in Mexico, suspending the 1824 constitution and promulgating the [Seven Laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Siete_Leyes "Siete Leyes"), which placed power in his hands. As a result, civil war spread. Three new governments declared independence: the [Republic of Texas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Texas "Republic of Texas"), the [Republic of the Rio Grande](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_the_Rio_Grande "Republic of the Rio Grande") and the [Republic of Yucatán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Yucat%C3%A1n "Republic of Yucatán").[^66]<sup><span title="Page / location: 129–137">: 129–137</span> </sup> The largest blow to Mexico was the US invasion in 1846 in the [Mexican–American War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican%E2%80%93American_War "Mexican–American War"). Mexico lost much of its [northern territory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_Cession "Mexican Cession"), sealed in the 1848 [Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Guadalupe_Hidalgo "Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo"). Despite this, Santa Anna returned to the presidency, but was ousted and exiled during the Liberal [Revolution of Ayutla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Revolution_of_Ayutla "Revolution of Ayutla").

### Liberal era (1855–1911)

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/10/Photograph_of_Benito_Juarez.jpg/250px-Photograph_of_Benito_Juarez.jpg)

Liberal President Benito Juárez. Known for his efforts to modernize Mexico, defend its sovereignty, and promote liberal reforms, especially during the mid-19th century

The overthrow of Santa Anna, and establishment of a civilian government by liberals, allowed them to enact laws they considered vital for development. [La Reforma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/La_Reforma "La Reforma") attempted to modernize the economy and institutions along liberal principles. They promulgated a new [Constitution of 1857](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution "Constitution"), separating Church and State, stripping the Church and military of privileges (*[fueros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fueros "Fueros")*); mandating the sale of Church-owned property and indigenous community lands, and secularizing education.[^67] Conservatives revolted, touching off the [Reform War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reform_War "Reform War") between Liberal and Conservative governments (1858–61).

The Liberals defeated the Conservative army on the battlefield, but Conservatives sought to gain power via intervention by the French, asking Emperor [Napoleon III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleon_III "Napoleon III") to place a European monarch as head of state in Mexico. The French Army defeated the Mexicans and placed [Maximilian Habsburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximilian_I_of_Mexico "Maximilian I of Mexico") on the [newly established throne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Mexican_Empire "Second Mexican Empire"), supported by Mexican Conservatives.[^68] The Liberal Republic under [Benito Juárez](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benito_Ju%C3%A1rez "Benito Juárez") was a government in internal exile, but with the end of the [American Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Civil_War "American Civil War") in 1865, the US government began aiding the Mexican Republic. The French Army withdrew its support, but Maximilian remained in Mexico; Republican forces executed him. The "Restored Republic" saw the return of Juárez, "the personification of the embattled republic," [^68] as president.

The Conservatives had been defeated militarily and discredited politically, for their collaboration with the French, and liberalism became synonymous with patriotism.[^69] The Mexican Army, which originated in the colonial royalist forces and transitioned into the early Republic military, was effectively destroyed following its defeat by Liberal forces in the Reform War and subsequently by the French intervention, enabling the emergence of a new generation of military leaders during the restoration of the Republic. Notable was [Porfirio Díaz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porfirio_D%C3%ADaz "Porfirio Díaz"), a hero of the *[Cinco de Mayo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Puebla "Battle of Puebla")*, who sought civilian power and challenged Juárez on his re-election in 1867.[^70] Díaz then rebelled but was crushed by Juárez. Having won re-election, Juárez died in office in 1872. Liberal [Sebastián Lerdo de Tejada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sebasti%C3%A1n_Lerdo_de_Tejada "Sebastián Lerdo de Tejada") became president, declaring a "religion of the state" for the rule of law, peace, and order. When Lerdo ran for re-election, Díaz rebelled against the civilian president, issuing the [Plan of Tuxtepec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan_of_Tuxtepec "Plan of Tuxtepec"). Díaz had more support and waged guerrilla war against Lerdo. On the verge of Díaz's victory on the battlefield, Lerdo fled from office into exile.[^70]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/40/Edouard_Manet_022.jpg/250px-Edouard_Manet_022.jpg)

The Execution of Emperor Maximilian, June 1867. Gen. Tomás Mejía, left; Maximiian, center; Gen. Miguel Miramón, right. Painting by Édouard Manet 1868

After the turmoil of 1810 to 1876, the 35-year rule of Liberal General [Porfirio Díaz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porfirio_D%C3%ADaz "Porfirio Díaz") (1876–1911) allowed Mexico to modernize in a period described one of "order and progress". The [Porfiriato](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Porfiriato "Porfiriato") was characterized by economic stability and growth, foreign investment and influence, an expansion of the [rail network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rail_transport_in_Mexico "Rail transport in Mexico") and telecommunications, and investments in the arts and sciences.[^71] Díaz ruled with a group of advisors that became known as the *[científicos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cient%C3%ADfico "Científico")* ('scientists').[^72] The regime was influenced by [positivism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Positivism "Positivism").[^73] They rejected theology and [idealism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idealism "Idealism") in favor of scientific methods applied towards development. An integral aspect was secular education. The Díaz government led a protracted [conflict against the Yaqui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yaqui_Wars "Yaqui Wars") that culminated with the forced relocation of [Yaqui](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yaqui "Yaqui") to Yucatán and Oaxaca. Díaz gave an [interview](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/James_Creelman "James Creelman") where he said he was not going to run in the 1910 elections, when he would be 80. Opposition had been suppressed and there were few avenues for new leaders. His announcement set off a frenzy of activity, including the unlikely candidacy of the son of a rich landowning family, [Francisco I. Madero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francisco_I._Madero "Francisco I. Madero"). Madero won a surprising amount of support when Díaz changed his mind and ran in the election, jailing Madero.

### Mexican Revolution (1910–20)

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f4/Francisco_I_Madero.jpg/250px-Francisco_I_Madero.jpg)

Francisco I. Madero, who challenged Díaz in the fraudulent 1910 election and was elected president when Díaz was forced to resign in May 1911

The Mexican Revolution was a decade-long transformational conflict,[^74] the "wind that swept Mexico." [^75] It began with uprisings against Díaz after the fraudulent 1910 election, his resignation in May 1911, demobilization of rebel forces, an interim presidency and the democratic election of Madero in fall 1911. In [February 1913](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ten_Tragic_Days "Ten Tragic Days"), a military coup overthrew Madero's government, with the support of the US, resulting in Madero's murder by agents of [Federal Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Army "Federal Army") General [Victoriano Huerta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victoriano_Huerta "Victoriano Huerta"). The US administration of [Taft](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Howard_Taft "William Howard Taft") supported the Huerta coup, but when Democrat [Woodrow Wilson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woodrow_Wilson "Woodrow Wilson") was inaugurated as president in March 1913, Wilson refused to recognize Huerta's regime and allowed arms sales to the Constitutionalists. Wilson ordered troops to [occupy the strategic port of Veracruz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_occupation_of_Veracruz "United States occupation of Veracruz") in 1914.[^76] A coalition of anti-Huerta forces in the North, the [Constitutional Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitutional_Army "Constitutional Army") led by [Governor of Coahuila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Governor_of_Coahuila "Governor of Coahuila") [Venustiano Carranza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venustiano_Carranza "Venustiano Carranza"), and a peasant army in the South under [Emiliano Zapata](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emiliano_Zapata "Emiliano Zapata") defeated the Federal Army in 1914, leaving only revolutionary forces.[^77]

Following the revolutionaries' victory against Huerta, they sought to broker a political solution, but the coalition splintered, plunging Mexico again into civil war. Constitutionalist general [Pancho Villa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pancho_Villa "Pancho Villa") broke with Carranza and allied with Zapata. Carranza's best general [Alvaro Obregón](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alvaro_Obreg%C3%B3n "Alvaro Obregón") defeated Villa, his former comrade, in the [Battle of Celaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Celaya "Battle of Celaya") in 1915, and Villa's forces melted away. Carranza became president, and the US recognized his government [^77] while Zapata's forces in the south reverted to guerrilla war. After Villa was defeated by revolutionary forces in 1915, he led an incursion raid into [Columbus, New Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Columbus,_New_Mexico "Columbus, New Mexico"), prompting the US to send [10,000 troops](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pancho_Villa_Expedition "Pancho Villa Expedition") in an unsuccessful attempt to capture Villa. Carranza pushed back against US troops being in northern Mexico. The expeditionary forces withdrew as the US entered World War I.[^78] Although often viewed as internal, the revolution had international elements:[^79] Germany attempted to get Mexico to side with it, sending a coded [telegram](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zimmermann_Telegram "Zimmermann Telegram") in 1917 to incite war between the US and Mexico, with Mexico to regain the territory it lost in the Mexican-American War [^80] but Mexico remained neutral in the conflict.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/1/1a/Urbina%2C_Villa_y_Zapata_en_Palacio%2C_Museo_de_la_Ciudad_de_M%C3%A9xico%2C_M%C3%A9xico_D.F.%2C_M%C3%A9xico%2C_2013-10-16%2C_DD_138.JPG/250px-Urbina%2C_Villa_y_Zapata_en_Palacio%2C_Museo_de_la_Ciudad_de_M%C3%A9xico%2C_M%C3%A9xico_D.F.%2C_M%C3%A9xico%2C_2013-10-16%2C_DD_138.JPG)

Tomás Urbina, Pancho Villa and Emiliano Zapata in the National Palace during the Mexican Revolution, 1914

In 1916, the winners of the Mexican revolution met at a constitutional convention to draft the [Constitution of 1917](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_1917 "Constitution of 1917"), ratified in February 1917. The Constitution empowered the government to expropriate resources including land, gave rights to labor, and strengthened anticlerical provisions.[^77] It remains the governing document of Mexico. The revolutionary war killed 900,000 out of Mexico's 15 million population.[^81] [^82] Consolidating power, Carranza had Zapata assassinated in 1919. Carranza had gained the support of the peasantry, but once in power, he did little to institute land reform, which had motivated many to fight. Carranza returned some confiscated land to their original owners. Carranza's best general, Obregón, served in his administration, but returned to his home state Sonora to position himself to run in the 1920 election. Since Carranza could not run for re-election, he chose a civilian to succeed him, intending to remain in power behind the presidency. Obregón and two other Sonoran revolutionary generals drew up the [Plan of Agua Prieta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plan_of_Agua_Prieta "Plan of Agua Prieta"), overthrowing Carranza. General [Adolfo de la Huerta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adolfo_de_la_Huerta "Adolfo de la Huerta") became interim president, followed by the election of General [Álvaro Obregón](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Obreg%C3%B3n "Álvaro Obregón").

### Political consolidation and one-party rule (1920–2000)

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/cb/General_PE_Calles_%28cropped%29.jpg/250px-General_PE_Calles_%28cropped%29.jpg)

Plutarco Elías Calles, the ruler of the Maximato and the founder of the Institutional Revolutionary Party, that held uninterrupted power in Mexico from 1929 to 2000

The post-revolutionary period (1920–46) was characterized by revolutionary generals serving as presidents, including [Álvaro Obregón](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%81lvaro_Obreg%C3%B3n "Álvaro Obregón") (1920–24), [Plutarco Elías Calles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plutarco_El%C3%ADas_Calles "Plutarco Elías Calles") (1924–28), [Lázaro Cárdenas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A1zaro_C%C3%A1rdenas "Lázaro Cárdenas") (1934–40), and [Manuel Avila Camacho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuel_Avila_Camacho "Manuel Avila Camacho") (1940–46). The government sought to bring order, end military intervention in politics, and create organizations of interest groups. Workers, peasants, office workers, and even the army for a short period, were incorporated as sectors of the single party that dominated politics from its founding in 1929. Obregón instigated land reform and strengthened organized labor. He gained recognition from the US and [settled claims](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American-Mexican_Claims_Commission "American-Mexican Claims Commission") with companies and individuals that lost property during the Revolution. He imposed his fellow revolutionary general, Calles, as his successor. Calles provoked a [major conflict](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cristero_War "Cristero War") with the [Catholic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church_in_Mexico "Catholic Church in Mexico") and Catholic guerrilla armies when he enforced anticlerical articles of the constitution, which ended with an agreement. Although the constitution prohibited the reelection of the president, Obregón wished to run again and the constitution was amended to allow non-consecutive re-election. He won the 1928 elections but was assassinated by a Catholic activist, causing a succession crisis. Calles could not become president again, so he sought to set up a structure to manage succession, founding the [Institutional Revolutionary Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institutional_Revolutionary_Party "Institutional Revolutionary Party"), which dominated Mexico for the rest of the 20th century.[^83] Despite not holding the presidency, Calles remained the key politician during the period known as the [Maximato](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maximato "Maximato") (1929–34), that ended during the presidency of Lázaro Cárdenas, who expelled Calles and implemented economic and social reforms. This included the [Mexican oil expropriation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_oil_expropriation "Mexican oil expropriation") in 1938, which nationalized the U.S. and Anglo-Dutch oil company known as the [Mexican Eagle Petroleum Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_Eagle_Petroleum_Company "Mexican Eagle Petroleum Company"), which would result in the creation of the state-owned [Pemex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pemex "Pemex").[^84]

Cárdenas's successor, [Manuel Ávila Camacho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manuel_%C3%81vila_Camacho "Manuel Ávila Camacho") (1940–46) was moderate, and relations between the US and Mexico improved during World War II, when Mexico was a significant ally. From 1946 the election of [Miguel Alemán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miguel_Alem%C3%A1n_Vald%C3%A9s "Miguel Alemán Valdés"), the first civilian president in the post-revolutionary period, Mexico embarked on a program of development, known as the [Mexican miracle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_miracle "Mexican miracle"), characterized by industrialization, urbanization, and increased inequality between urban and rural areas.[^84] The [Green Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Green_Revolution "Green Revolution"), a technological movement that led to worldwide increases in crop production, began in the [Yaqui Valley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yaqui_Valley "Yaqui Valley") in the middle of the 20th century.[^85]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/Ex%C3%A8rcit_al_Z%C3%B3calo-28_d%27agost.jpg/250px-Ex%C3%A8rcit_al_Z%C3%B3calo-28_d%27agost.jpg)

Armored cars in the Zócalo during the protests of 1968

With robust growth, Mexico sought to showcase itself by hosting the [1968 Summer Olympics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1968_Summer_Olympics "1968 Summer Olympics"). The government poured resources into new facilities, prompting unrest among students and others. Demonstrations in Mexico City went on for weeks before the opening of the games, with the government of [Gustavo Díaz Ordaz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gustavo_D%C3%ADaz_Ordaz "Gustavo Díaz Ordaz") cracking down. The culmination was the [Tlatelolco Massacre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tlatelolco_Massacre "Tlatelolco Massacre"),[^86] which killed 300-800 protesters.[^87] Although the economy continued to flourish for some, [inequality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Distribution_of_wealth "Distribution of wealth") remained a factor of discontent. PRI rule became authoritarian and oppressive in the [Mexican Dirty War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_Dirty_War "Mexican Dirty War").[^88]

In the 1980s the first cracks emerged in the PRI's political dominance. In [Baja California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baja_California "Baja California"), the [PAN candidate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernesto_Ruffo_Appel "Ernesto Ruffo Appel") was elected governor. When De la Madrid chose [Carlos Salinas de Gortari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carlos_Salinas_de_Gortari "Carlos Salinas de Gortari") as the candidate for the PRI, and therefore a foregone presidential victor, [Cuauhtémoc Cárdenas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuauht%C3%A9moc_C%C3%A1rdenas "Cuauhtémoc Cárdenas"), son of former President [Lázaro Cárdenas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/L%C3%A1zaro_C%C3%A1rdenas "Lázaro Cárdenas"), broke with the PRI and challenged Salinas in the 1988 elections. In 1988 there was [electoral fraud](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electoral_fraud "Electoral fraud"), with results showing that Salinas had won the election by the narrowest percentage ever. There were protests in Mexico City over the stolen election. Salinas took the oath of office in December 1988.[^89] In 1990 the PRI was described by [Mario Vargas Llosa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mario_Vargas_Llosa "Mario Vargas Llosa") as the "perfect dictatorship", but there had been major challenges to the PRI's hegemony.[^90] [^91] [^92]

Salinas embarked on a program of [neoliberal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neoliberalism "Neoliberalism") reforms that fixed the exchange rate, controlled inflation, opened Mexico to foreign investment, and began talks with the US and Canada to join their [free-trade agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free-trade_agreement "Free-trade agreement"), which culminated in the [North American Free Trade Agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_American_Free_Trade_Agreement "North American Free Trade Agreement") (NAFTA) on 1 January 1994; the same day, the [Zapatista Army of National Liberation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zapatista_Army_of_National_Liberation "Zapatista Army of National Liberation") (EZLN) in Chiapas began armed rebellion against the federal government, which captured towns and brought world attention. The armed conflict was short-lived and continues as a non-violent opposition movement against neoliberalism and [globalization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Globalization "Globalization"). In 1994, following the assassination of the PRI's presidential candidate [Luis Donaldo Colosio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luis_Donaldo_Colosio "Luis Donaldo Colosio"), Salinas was succeeded by victorious PRI candidate [Ernesto Zedillo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ernesto_Zedillo "Ernesto Zedillo"). Salinas left Zedillo's government to deal with the [Mexican peso crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_peso_crisis "Mexican peso crisis"), requiring a $50 billion [IMF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_Monetary_Fund "International Monetary Fund") bailout. Macroeconomic reforms were started by Zedillo, and the economy recovered and growth peaked at 7% by 1999.[^93]

### Contemporary Mexico

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c0/Vicente_Fox_flag_%28cropped%29.jpg/250px-Vicente_Fox_flag_%28cropped%29.jpg)

Vicente Fox won the 2000 general election and became the first president not from the PRI since 1929, and the first elected from an opposition party since Francisco I. Madero in 1911

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/Fuerza_del_Estado_Michoac%C3%A1n.jpg/250px-Fuerza_del_Estado_Michoac%C3%A1n.jpg)

A confrontation in Apatzingán during Operation Michoacán in August 2007

After 71 years of rule, the incumbent PRI lost the [2000 presidential election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2000_Mexican_general_election "2000 Mexican general election") to [Vicente Fox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vicente_Fox "Vicente Fox") of the opposing conservative [National Action Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Action_Party_\(Mexico\) "National Action Party (Mexico)") (PAN). In the [2006 presidential election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2006_Mexican_general_election "2006 Mexican general election"), [Felipe Calderón](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Felipe_Calder%C3%B3n "Felipe Calderón") from the PAN was declared the winner, with a narrow margin (0.6%) over leftist politician [Andrés Manuel López Obrador](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andr%C3%A9s_Manuel_L%C3%B3pez_Obrador "Andrés Manuel López Obrador") of the [Party of the Democratic Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Party_of_the_Democratic_Revolution "Party of the Democratic Revolution") (PRD).[^94] López Obrador, however, [contested the election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Controversies_of_the_2006_Mexican_general_election "Controversies of the 2006 Mexican general election") and pledged to create an "alternative government".[^95] In the [2012 presidential election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2012_Mexican_general_election "2012 Mexican general election"), the PRI again won the presidency with the election of [Enrique Peña Nieto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enrique_Pe%C3%B1a_Nieto "Enrique Peña Nieto"). However, he won with a plurality of around 38% and did not have a legislative majority.[^96]

During the 21st century, Mexico has contended with [high crime rates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crime_in_Mexico "Crime in Mexico"), [bureaucratic corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Mexico "Corruption in Mexico"), [narcotrafficking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_drug_war "Mexican drug war"), and a stagnant economy. Many state-owned enterprises were privatized starting in the 1990s with neoliberal reforms. [Pemex](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pemex "Pemex"), the state-owned petroleum company is being privatized, with exploration licenses being issued.[^97] In a push against corruption, the ex-CEO of Pemex, [Emilio Lozoya Austin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emilio_Lozoya_Austin "Emilio Lozoya Austin"), was arrested in 2020.[^98]

After founding the new political party [MORENA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Regeneration_Movement "National Regeneration Movement"), Andrés Manuel López Obrador (known as AMLO) won the 2018 election with over 50%. His coalition, led by his left-wing party founded after the 2012 elections, included parties from across the spectrum. The coalition won a majority in the upper and lower Congress chambers. His success is attributed to opposing political forces exhausting their chances, as well as AMLO's adoption of moderate discourse focused on reconciliation.[^99] The first confirmed case of [COVID-19 in Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic_in_Mexico "COVID-19 pandemic in Mexico") occurred in February 2020, and [COVID-19 vaccination in Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/COVID-19_vaccination_in_Mexico "COVID-19 vaccination in Mexico") began in December. [Claudia Sheinbaum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Claudia_Sheinbaum "Claudia Sheinbaum"), AMLO's successor, won the [2024 presidential election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2024_Mexican_general_election "2024 Mexican general election") in a landslide and became the first woman to lead Mexico.[^100] She was sworn in on 1 October 2024.[^101]

## Geography

A topographic map of Mexico

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d2/Mexico_map.svg/250px-Mexico_map.svg.png)

Mexico map of states and capitals

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/34/Pico_de_Orizaba_desde_Hidalgo%2C_Puebla.jpg/250px-Pico_de_Orizaba_desde_Hidalgo%2C_Puebla.jpg)

Pico de Orizaba, the highest mountain in Mexico

Mexico is located between latitudes [14°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/14th_parallel_north "14th parallel north") and [33°N](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/33rd_parallel_north "33rd parallel north"), and longitudes [86°](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/86th_meridian_west "86th meridian west") and [119°W](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/119th_meridian_west "119th meridian west") in the southern portion of North America, with a total area of 1,972,550 km <sup>2</sup> (761,606 sq mi), is the [13th largest country by total area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_outlying_territories_by_total_area "List of countries and outlying territories by total area"). It has coastlines on the [Pacific Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Ocean "Pacific Ocean") and [Gulf of California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_California "Gulf of California"), as well as the [Gulf of Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Mexico "Gulf of Mexico") and [Caribbean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caribbean_Sea "Caribbean Sea"), the latter two forming part of the [Atlantic Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean "Atlantic Ocean").[^102] Within these seas are about 6,000 km <sup>2</sup> (2,317 sq mi) of islands. Almost all of Mexico lies in the [North American Plate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_American_Plate "North American Plate"), with small parts of the [Baja California peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baja_California_peninsula "Baja California peninsula") on the [Pacific](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Plate "Pacific Plate") and [Cocos Plates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cocos_Plate "Cocos Plate"). [Geophysically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geophysics "Geophysics"), some geographers include the territory east of the [Isthmus of Tehuantepec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Isthmus_of_Tehuantepec "Isthmus of Tehuantepec") (around 12% of the total) within Central America.[^103] [Geopolitically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geopolitics "Geopolitics"), Mexico is entirely considered part of North America.[^104]

The majority of Mexican central and northern territories are located at high altitudes, and as such the highest elevations are found at the [Trans-Mexican Volcanic Belt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trans-Mexican_Volcanic_Belt "Trans-Mexican Volcanic Belt") which crosses Mexico east to west: [Pico de Orizaba](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pico_de_Orizaba "Pico de Orizaba") (5,700 m or 18,701 ft), [Popocatépetl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popocat%C3%A9petl "Popocatépetl") (5,462 m or 17,920 ft) and [Iztaccihuatl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iztaccihuatl "Iztaccihuatl") (5,286 m or 17,343 ft) and the [Nevado de Toluca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nevado_de_Toluca "Nevado de Toluca") (4,577 m or 15,016 ft). Two mountain ranges known as [Sierra Madre Oriental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Oriental "Sierra Madre Oriental") and [Sierra Madre Occidental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental "Sierra Madre Occidental"), which are the extension of the [Rocky Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rocky_Mountains "Rocky Mountains") from northern North America crossed the country from north to south and a fourth mountain range, the [Sierra Madre del Sur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Madre_del_Sur "Sierra Madre del Sur"), runs from [Michoacán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michoac%C3%A1n "Michoacán") to [Oaxaca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oaxaca "Oaxaca"). The Mexican territory is prone to [volcanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Volcanism "Volcanism").[^105]

Mexico has nine distinct regions: [Baja California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baja_California "Baja California"), the Pacific Coastal Lowlands, the [Mexican Plateau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexican_Plateau "Mexican Plateau"), the [Sierra Madre Oriental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Oriental "Sierra Madre Oriental"), the [Sierra Madre Occidental](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sierra_Madre_Occidental "Sierra Madre Occidental"), the [Cordillera Neovolcánica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cordillera_Neovolc%C3%A1nica "Cordillera Neovolcánica"), the [Gulf Coastal Plain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_Coastal_Plain "Gulf Coastal Plain"), the Southern Highlands, and the [Yucatán Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula "Yucatán Peninsula").[^106] An important geologic feature of the Yucatán peninsula is the [Chicxulub crater](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicxulub_crater "Chicxulub crater"), the scientific consensus is that the [Chicxulub impactor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicxulub_impactor "Chicxulub impactor") was responsible for the [Cretaceous–Paleogene extinction event](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cretaceous%E2%80%93Paleogene_extinction_event "Cretaceous–Paleogene extinction event"). Although Mexico is large (a little over 2,000 mi (3,219 km) in length from its farthest land points), much of its land mass is incompatible with agriculture due to aridity, soil, or terrain.[^105]

In 2018, an estimated 54.9% of land was agricultural; 11.8% was arable; 1.4% was in permanent crops; 41.7% was permanent pasture; and 33.3% was forest.[^105] Mexico is irrigated by several rivers, with the longest being the [Rio Grande](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rio_Grande "Rio Grande"), which serves as a natural eastern border with the United States.[^107] The [Usumacinta River](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Usumacinta_River "Usumacinta River") serves as a natural southern border between Mexico and Guatemala.[^108]

### Climate

The climate of Mexico is varied due to the country's size and topography. The [Tropic of Cancer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropic_of_Cancer "Tropic of Cancer") effectively divides the country into temperate and tropical zones. Land north of the Tropic of Cancer experiences cooler temperatures during the winter months. South of the Tropic of Cancer, temperatures are fairly constant year-round and vary solely as a function of elevation. This gives Mexico one of the world's most diverse weather systems.[^109]

Maritime air masses bring seasonal precipitation from May until August. Many parts of Mexico, particularly the north, have a dry climate with only sporadic rainfall, while parts of the tropical lowlands in the south average more than 2,000 mm (78.7 in) of annual precipitation. For example, many cities in the north like [Monterrey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monterrey "Monterrey"), [Hermosillo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hermosillo "Hermosillo"), and [Mexicali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mexicali "Mexicali") experience temperatures of 40 °C (104 °F) or more in summer. In the [Sonoran Desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonoran_Desert "Sonoran Desert") temperatures reach 50 °C (122 °F) or more.[^109]

There are seven major climate types in Mexico [^110] with warm sub-humid climate being coastal up to 900 meters found mostly in the southern region of Mexico; dry and desertic climates being found in the northern half of the country; temperate humid and sub-humid being found mostly on pastures at an elevation of 1,800 meters and higher in central Mexico and cold climate usually found at an elevation of 3,500 meters and beyond. Most of the country's territory has a temperate to dry climate.[^110]

Areas south of the Tropic of Cancer with elevations up to 1,000 m (3,281 ft), the southern parts of both coastal plains as well as the [Yucatán Peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yucat%C3%A1n_Peninsula "Yucatán Peninsula"), have a yearly median temperature between 24 and 28 °C (75.2 and 82.4 °F). Temperatures here remain high throughout the year, with only a 5 °C (9 °F) difference between winter and summer median temperatures. The Pacific coast is subject to natural hazards such as tsunamis and both Mexican coasts with the exception of the south coast of the [Bay of Campeche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay_of_Campeche "Bay of Campeche") and northern Baja California are vulnerable to serious [hurricanes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tropical_cyclone "Tropical cyclone") during the summer and fall. Although low-lying areas north of the Tropic of Cancer are hot and humid during the summer, they generally have lower yearly temperature averages (from 20 to 24 °C or 68.0 to 75.2 °F) because of more moderate conditions during the winter.[^109]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f8/Flooding_in_Mexico_%28837%29.jpg/250px-Flooding_in_Mexico_%28837%29.jpg)

A flash flood in Mexico

[Climate change in Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change_in_Mexico "Climate change in Mexico") is causing widespread impacts including rising temperatures, altered precipitation patterns, intensified hurricanes, and sea-level rise in coastal regions.[^111] These changes pose threats to water resources and agriculture (particularly for rural and smallholder farmers),[^112] [^113] and affect crops including maize and coffee, contributing to economic insecurity.[^114] [^113] [Climate change](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Climate_change "Climate change") is impacting Mexicans' health,[^115] exacerbating human migration,[^116] [^117] [^118] and increasing [extinction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extinction "Extinction") risk for Mexico's [biodiversity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Biodiversity "Biodiversity"), as protected areas are expected to face warming temperatures and decreased precipitation.[^119]

### Biodiversity

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/54/Mexican_wolf_lounging.jpg/250px-Mexican_wolf_lounging.jpg)

A Mexican wolf

Mexico ranks fourth [^120] in the world in biodiversity and is one of the 17 [megadiverse countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megadiverse_countries "Megadiverse countries"). With over 200,000 species, Mexico is home of 10–12% of the world's biodiversity.[^121] Mexico ranks first in biodiversity in [reptiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reptile "Reptile") with 707 known species, [second in mammals with 438 species](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_mammals_of_Mexico "List of mammals of Mexico"), fourth in [amphibians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amphibian "Amphibian") with 290 species, and fourth in flora, with 26,000 species.[^122] Mexico is also considered the second country in the world in [ecosystems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecosystem "Ecosystem") and fourth in overall species.[^123] About 2,500 species are protected by Mexican legislation.[^123]

In 2002, Mexico had the second fastest rate of deforestation in the world, second only to Brazil.[^124] It had a 2019 [Forest Landscape Integrity Index](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Forest_Landscape_Integrity_Index "Forest Landscape Integrity Index") mean score of 6.82/10, ranking it 63rd globally out of 172 countries.[^125] According to SGI there is [Deforestation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deforestation "Deforestation") and [soil erosion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soil_erosion "Soil erosion") especially in rural areas of Mexico. In the 2022 report it was noted environmental protection laws have improved in major cities but remain unenforced or unregulated in rural regions.[^126]

In Mexico, 170,000 square kilometers (65,637 sq mi) are considered "Protected Natural Areas". These include 34 biosphere reserves, 67 [national parks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_national_parks_of_Mexico "List of national parks of Mexico"), 4 natural monuments, 26 areas of protected flora and fauna, 4 areas for natural resource protection and 17 sanctuaries (zones rich in diverse species).[^121] Some of Mexico's native [culinary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culinary_art "Culinary art") ingredients include maize, tomato, beans, squash, chocolate, [vanilla](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vanilla "Vanilla"), [avocado](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avocado "Avocado"), [guava](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guava "Guava"), [chayote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chayote "Chayote"), [epazote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dysphania_ambrosioides "Dysphania ambrosioides"), [camote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sweet_potato "Sweet potato"), [jícama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J%C3%ADcama "Jícama"), [nopal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nopal "Nopal"), [zucchini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zucchini "Zucchini"), [tejocote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Crataegus_mexicana "Crataegus mexicana"), [huitlacoche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corn_smut "Corn smut"), [sapote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sapote "Sapote"), [mamey sapote](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mamey_sapote "Mamey sapote"), and a great variety of [chiles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chili_pepper "Chili pepper"), such as the [habanero](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habanero "Habanero") and the [jalapeño](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jalape%C3%B1o "Jalapeño"). [Tequila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tequila "Tequila"), the distilled alcoholic drink made from cultivated [agave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agave "Agave") cacti is a major industry. Because of its high biodiversity Mexico has also been a frequent site of [bioprospecting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bioprospecting "Bioprospecting") by international research bodies.[^127]
