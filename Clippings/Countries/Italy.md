**Italy**,[^1] officially the **Italian Republic**,[^2] is a country in [Southern](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Europe "Southern Europe") and [Western Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Europe "Western Europe").[^3] It consists of [a peninsula](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Peninsula "Italian Peninsula") that extends into the [Mediterranean Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mediterranean_Sea "Mediterranean Sea"), with the [Alps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alps "Alps") on its northern land border, as well as [nearly 800 islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_islands_of_Italy "List of islands of Italy"), notably [Sicily](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sicily "Sicily") and [Sardinia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sardinia "Sardinia"). Italy shares land borders with [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "France") to the west; [Switzerland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Switzerland "Switzerland") and [Austria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Austria "Austria") to the north; [Slovenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slovenia "Slovenia") to the east; and the two [enclaves](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclave "Enclave") of [Vatican City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vatican_City "Vatican City") and [San Marino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Marino "San Marino"). It is the [tenth-largest country in Europe by area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_European_countries_by_area "List of European countries by area"), covering 301,340 km <sup>2</sup> (116,350 sq mi), and the third-most populous [member state of the European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Member_state_of_the_European_Union "Member state of the European Union"), with nearly 59 million inhabitants. Italy's capital and [largest city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Italy "List of cities in Italy") is [Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome "Rome"); other major cities include [Milan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milan "Milan") (the largest [metropolitan area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolitan_area "Metropolitan area") in the country), [Naples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Naples "Naples"), [Turin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turin "Turin"), [Palermo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palermo "Palermo"), [Bologna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bologna "Bologna"), [Florence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florence "Florence"), [Genoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genoa "Genoa"), [Bari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bari "Bari"), and [Venice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Venice "Venice").

The [history of Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Italy "History of Italy") goes back to numerous [Italic peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_ancient_peoples_of_Italy "List of ancient peoples of Italy"), notably including the [ancient Romans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Romans "Ancient Romans"), who conquered the Mediterranean world during the [Roman Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic "Roman Republic") and ruled it for centuries during the [Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Empire "Roman Empire"). With the spread of Christianity, Rome became the seat of the [Catholic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church "Catholic Church") and the [Papacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papacy "Papacy"). [Barbarian invasions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barbarian_invasions "Barbarian invasions") and other factors led to the decline and [fall of the Western Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_the_Western_Roman_Empire "Fall of the Western Roman Empire") between [late antiquity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Late_antiquity "Late antiquity") and the [Early Middle Ages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_Middle_Ages "Early Middle Ages"). By the 11th century, [Italian city-states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_city-states "Italian city-states") and [maritime republics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_republics "Maritime republics") expanded, bringing renewed prosperity through commerce and laying the groundwork for modern capitalism. The [Italian Renaissance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Renaissance "Italian Renaissance") flourished during the 15th and 16th centuries and [spread to the rest of Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance#Spread "Renaissance"). Italian explorers discovered new routes to the Far East and the [New World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_World "New World"), contributing significantly to the [Age of Discovery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Discovery "Age of Discovery").

After centuries of political and territorial divisions, the [Kingdom of Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy "Kingdom of Italy") was established in 1861, following [wars of independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_War_of_Independence "Italian War of Independence") and the [Expedition of the Thousand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Expedition_of_the_Thousand "Expedition of the Thousand"). From the late 19th to the early 20th century, [Italy industrialised](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Industrialization_in_Italy "Industrialization in Italy") and acquired [a colonial empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Empire "Italian Empire"), while [the south](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_Italy "Southern Italy") remained largely impoverished, fuelling [a large immigrant diaspora](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_diaspora "Italian diaspora") to the Americas. From 1915 to 1918, [Italy took part](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_history_of_Italy_during_World_War_I "Military history of Italy during World War I") in [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I") with the [Entente](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I "Allies of World War I") against the [Central Powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Powers "Central Powers"). In 1922, the [Italian fascist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_fascist "Italian fascist") dictatorship was established. [During World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_history_of_Italy_during_World_War_II "Military history of Italy during World War II"), Italy was first part of the [Axis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Axis_powers "Axis powers") until [an armistice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armistice_of_Cassibile "Armistice of Cassibile") with the [Allied powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II "Allies of World War II") (1940–1943), then a co-belligerent of the Allies during the [Italian resistance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_resistance "Italian resistance") and [liberation of Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberation_of_Italy "Liberation of Italy") from German occupation and the collaborationist [RSI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Social_Republic "Italian Social Republic") (1943–1945). In 1946, the monarchy [was replaced by a republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1946_Italian_institutional_referendum "1946 Italian institutional referendum") and the country made [a strong recovery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_economic_miracle "Italian economic miracle").

A [developed country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Developed_country "Developed country") with [an advanced economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Italy "Economy of Italy"), Italy has the [eighth-largest nominal GDP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_GDP_\(nominal\) "List of countries by GDP (nominal)") in the world, the [second-largest manufacturing sector](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_manufacturing_output "List of countries by manufacturing output") in Europe, and [plays a significant role in regional](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regional_power "Regional power") and – [to a lesser extent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Least_of_the_great_powers "Least of the great powers") – global economic, military, cultural, and political affairs. It is [a founding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inner_Six "Inner Six") and [leading member](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EU_three "EU three") of the European Union, and [is part of numerous other international organisations and forums](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_relations_of_Italy#International_institutions "Foreign relations of Italy"). As a [cultural superpower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultural_superpower "Cultural superpower"), Italy has long been a renowned global centre [of art](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_art "Italian art"), [music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_of_Italy "Music of Italy"), [literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_literature "Italian literature"), [cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_cuisine "Italian cuisine"), [fashion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_fashion "Italian fashion"), [science and technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_and_technology_in_Italy "Science and technology in Italy"), and the source of [multiple inventions and discoveries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Italian_inventions_and_discoveries "List of Italian inventions and discoveries"). It has the [highest number](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Heritage_Sites_by_country "World Heritage Sites by country") of [World Heritage Sites](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Heritage_Site "World Heritage Site") ([61](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_World_Heritage_Sites_in_Italy "List of World Heritage Sites in Italy")) and is the [second-most visited country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Tourism_rankings "World Tourism rankings") in the world with 74 million tourists in 2024.

## Name

Hypotheses for the etymology of *Italia* are numerous.[^19] One theory suggests it originated from an [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek "Ancient Greek") term for the land of the *Italói*, a tribe that resided in the region now known as [Calabria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calabria "Calabria"). Originally thought to be named *Vituli*, some scholars suggest their [totemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Totem "Totem") animal to be the calf ([Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin "Latin"): *vitulus*; [Umbrian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umbrian "Umbrian"): *vitlo*; [Oscan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oscan "Oscan"): *Víteliú*).[^20] Several ancient authors said it was named after a local ruler [Italus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italus "Italus").[^21]

The ancient Greek term for Italy initially referred only to the south of the [Bruttium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Calabria "Calabria") peninsula and parts of [Catanzaro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catanzaro "Catanzaro") and [Vibo Valentia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vibo_Valentia "Vibo Valentia"). The larger concept of [Oenotria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oenotria "Oenotria") and "Italy" became synonymous, and the name applied to most of [Lucania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lucania "Lucania") as well. Before the Roman Republic's expansion, the name was used by Greeks for the land between the [strait of Messina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Strait_of_Messina "Strait of Messina") and the line connecting the [gulfs of Salerno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Salerno "Gulf of Salerno") and [Taranto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Taranto "Gulf of Taranto"), corresponding to Calabria. The Greeks came to apply "Italia" to a larger region.[^22] In addition to the " [Greek Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_Italy "Greek Italy") " in the south, historians have suggested the existence of an "Etruscan Italy", which consisted of areas of central Italy.[^23]

The borders of [Roman Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Italy "Roman Italy"), *Italia*, are better established. Cato's *[Origines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Origines "Origines")* describes Italy as the entire peninsula south of the Alps.[^24] In 264 BC, Roman Italy extended from the [Arno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arno "Arno") and [Rubicon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rubicon "Rubicon") rivers of the centre-north to the entire south. The northern area, [Cisalpine Gaul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cisalpine_Gaul "Cisalpine Gaul"), considered geographically part of Italy, was occupied by Rome in the 220s BC,[^25] but remained politically separated. It was legally merged into the administrative unit of Italy in 42 BC.[^26] Sardinia, [Corsica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corsica "Corsica"), Sicily, and [Malta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malta "Malta") were added to Italy by [Diocletian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diocletian "Diocletian") in 292 AD,[^27] which made late-ancient Italy coterminous with the modern [Italian geographical region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_geographical_region "Italian geographical region").[^28]

The Latin *Italicus* was used to describe "a man of Italy" as opposed to a *provincial*, or one from the [Roman province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_province "Roman province").[^29] The adjective *italianus*, from which *Italian* was derived, is from [medieval Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Medieval_Latin "Medieval Latin") and was used alternatively with *Italicus* during the [early modern period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_modern_period "Early modern period").[^30] After the [fall of the Western Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_the_Western_Roman_Empire "Fall of the Western Roman Empire"), the [Ostrogothic Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ostrogothic_Kingdom "Ostrogothic Kingdom") of Italy was created. After the [Lombard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lombards "Lombards") invasions, *Italia* was retained as the name for their kingdom, and its [successor kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy_\(Holy_Roman_Empire\) "Kingdom of Italy (Holy Roman Empire)") within the [Holy Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire "Holy Roman Empire").[^31]

## History

### Prehistory and antiquity

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/75/Etruscan_Painting_1.jpg/250px-Etruscan_Painting_1.jpg)

Etruscan fresco in the Monterozzi necropolis, 5th century BC

[Lower Paleolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lower_Paleolithic "Lower Paleolithic") artefacts, dating back 850,000 years, have been recovered from [Monte Poggiolo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monte_Poggiolo "Monte Poggiolo").[^32] Excavations throughout Italy revealed a [Neanderthal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neanderthal "Neanderthal") presence in the Middle Palaeolithic period 200,000 years ago,[^33] while [modern humans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Early_modern_human "Early modern human") appeared about 40,000 years ago at [Riparo Mochi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Riparo_Mochi "Riparo Mochi").[^34]

The [ancient peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_peoples_of_Italy "Ancient peoples of Italy") of pre-Roman Italy were [Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Indo-Europeans "Proto-Indo-Europeans"), specifically the [Italic peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italic_peoples "Italic peoples"). The main historic peoples of possible non-Indo-European or [pre-Indo-European](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pre-Indo-European_languages "Pre-Indo-European languages") heritage include the [Etruscans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Etruscans "Etruscans"), the [Elymians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elymians "Elymians") and [Sicani](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sicani "Sicani") of Sicily, and the prehistoric [Sardinians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sardinians "Sardinians"), who gave birth to the [Nuragic civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nuragic_civilisation "Nuragic civilisation"). Other ancient populations include the [Rhaetian people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhaetian_people "Rhaetian people") and [Camunni](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camunni "Camunni"), known for their [rock drawings in Valcamonica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_drawings_in_Valcamonica "Rock drawings in Valcamonica").[^35] A natural mummy, [Ötzi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C3%96tzi "Ötzi"), dated 3400–3100 BC, was discovered in the [Similaun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Similaun "Similaun") glacier in 1991.[^36]

The first colonisers were the [Phoenicians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicia "Phoenicia"), who established [emporiums](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emporium_\(antiquity\) "Emporium (antiquity)") on the coasts of Sicily and Sardinia. Some became small urban centres and developed parallel to [Greek colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_colonies "Greek colonies").[^37] During the 8th and 7th centuries, Greek colonies were established at [Pithecusae](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pithecusae "Pithecusae"), eventually extending along the south of the Italian Peninsula and the coast of Sicily, an area later known as [Magna Graecia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Magna_Graecia "Magna Graecia").[^38] [Ionians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ionians "Ionians"), [Doric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doric_Greek "Doric Greek") colonists, [Syracusans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syracusan "Syracusan"), and the [Achaeans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaeans_\(tribe\) "Achaeans (tribe)") founded various cities. [Greek colonisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_colonization_of_Italy "Greek colonization of Italy") placed the [Italic peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italic_peoples "Italic peoples") in contact with democratic forms of government and high artistic and cultural expressions.[^39]
