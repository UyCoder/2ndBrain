**Pakistan**,[^6] officially the **Islamic Republic of Pakistan**,[^7] is a country in [South Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asia "South Asia"). It is the [fifth-most populous country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_population "List of countries and dependencies by population"), with a population of over 241.5 million,[^4] having the [second-largest Muslim population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_by_country#Countries "Islam by country") as of 2023. [Islamabad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamabad "Islamabad") is the nation's capital, while [Karachi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Karachi "Karachi") is [its largest city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_cities_in_Pakistan_by_population "List of cities in Pakistan by population") and [financial centre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Financial_centre "Financial centre"). Pakistan is the [33rd-largest country by area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_and_dependencies_by_area "List of countries and dependencies by area"). Bounded by the [Arabian Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arabian_Sea "Arabian Sea") on the south, the [Gulf of Oman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_of_Oman "Gulf of Oman") on the southwest, and the [Sir Creek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sir_Creek "Sir Creek") on the southeast, it shares land borders with [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/India "India") to the east; [Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan "Afghanistan") to the west; [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran") to the southwest; and [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China") to the northeast. It shares a maritime border with [Oman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oman "Oman") in the Gulf of Oman, and is separated from [Tajikistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajikistan "Tajikistan") in the northwest by Afghanistan's narrow [Wakhan Corridor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wakhan_Corridor "Wakhan Corridor").

Pakistan is the site of [several ancient cultures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Pakistan "History of Pakistan"), including the 8,500-year-old [Neolithic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Neolithic "Neolithic") site of [Mehrgarh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mehrgarh "Mehrgarh") in [Balochistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balochistan,_Pakistan "Balochistan, Pakistan"), the [Indus Valley Civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indus_Valley_Civilisation "Indus Valley Civilisation") of the [Bronze Age](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bronze_Age "Bronze Age"),[^18] and the ancient [Gandhara civilisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gandhara_civilisation "Gandhara civilisation").[^19] The regions that compose the modern state of Pakistan were the realm of multiple empires and dynasties, including the [Achaemenid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Achaemenid_Empire "Achaemenid Empire"), the [Maurya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurya_Empire "Maurya Empire"), the [Kushan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kushan_Empire "Kushan Empire"), the [Gupta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gupta_Empire "Gupta Empire");[^20] the [Umayyad Caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Umayyad_Caliphate "Umayyad Caliphate") in its southern regions, the [Hindu Shahis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hindu_Shahis "Hindu Shahis"), the [Ghaznavids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghaznavids "Ghaznavids"), the [Delhi Sultanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delhi_Sultanate "Delhi Sultanate"), the [Samma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samma_dynasty "Samma dynasty"), the [Shah Miris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shah_Miris "Shah Miris"), the [Mughals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_Empire "Mughal Empire"),[^21] and finally, the [British Raj](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Raj "British Raj") from 1858 to 1947.

Spurred by the [Pakistan Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan_Movement "Pakistan Movement"), which sought a homeland for the Muslims of [British India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidencies_and_provinces_of_British_India "Presidencies and provinces of British India"), and election victories in 1946 by the [All-India Muslim League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All-India_Muslim_League "All-India Muslim League"), Pakistan gained independence in 1947 after the [partition of British India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_British_India "Partition of British India"), which awarded separate statehood to its Muslim-majority regions and was accompanied by an unparalleled mass migration and loss of life.[^22] [^23] Initially [a dominion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominion_of_Pakistan "Dominion of Pakistan") of the [British Commonwealth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations "Commonwealth of Nations"), Pakistan [adopted a republican constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Pakistan_\(1956%E2%80%931958\) "Constitution of Pakistan (1956–1958)") in 1956 and became an [Islamic republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_republic "Islamic republic") with two geographically separate provinces, [East Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Pakistan "East Pakistan") and [West Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Pakistan "West Pakistan"). East Pakistan seceded as the new country of [Bangladesh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh "Bangladesh") in 1971 after a [nine-month-long civil war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangladesh_Liberation_War "Bangladesh Liberation War"). In the following four decades, Pakistan has been ruled by governments that alternated between civilian and military, democratic and authoritarian, relatively [secular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secularism_in_Pakistan "Secularism in Pakistan") and Islamist.[^24]

Pakistan is considered a [middle power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_power "Middle power") and is a declared [nuclear-weapon state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_states_with_nuclear_weapons "List of states with nuclear weapons"), with the world's [seventh-largest standing armed forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_countries_by_number_of_military_and_paramilitary_personnel "List of countries by number of military and paramilitary personnel"). The [Pakistani economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Pakistan "Economy of Pakistan") is ranked amongst the world's emerging and growth-leading economies,[^25] with a large and rapidly growing middle class.[^26] [^27] Pakistan's political history since independence has been characterised by periods of significant economic and military growth as well as those of political and economic instability. It is an [ethnically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_groups_in_Pakistan "Ethnic groups in Pakistan") and [linguistically](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Languages_of_Pakistan "Languages of Pakistan") diverse country, with similarly diverse [geography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_of_Pakistan "Geography of Pakistan") and [wildlife](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wildlife_of_Pakistan "Wildlife of Pakistan"). The country continues to face [poverty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty_in_Pakistan "Poverty in Pakistan"), [illiteracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Literate_Pakistan_Foundation "Literate Pakistan Foundation"), [corruption](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corruption_in_Pakistan "Corruption in Pakistan"), and [terrorism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Terrorism_in_Pakistan "Terrorism in Pakistan").[^28] [^29] [^30] Pakistan is a member of the United Nations, the [Shanghai Cooperation Organisation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shanghai_Cooperation_Organisation "Shanghai Cooperation Organisation"), the [Organisation of Islamic Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Organisation_of_Islamic_Cooperation "Organisation of Islamic Cooperation"), the [Commonwealth of Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Nations "Commonwealth of Nations"), the [South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Asian_Association_for_Regional_Cooperation "South Asian Association for Regional Cooperation"), and the [Islamic Military Counter-Terrorism Coalition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Military_Counter_Terrorism_Coalition "Islamic Military Counter Terrorism Coalition"), and is designated as a [major non-NATO ally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_non-NATO_ally "Major non-NATO ally") by the United States.

## Etymology

The name *Pakistan* was coined by [Choudhry Rahmat Ali](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Choudhry_Rahmat_Ali "Choudhry Rahmat Ali"), a [Pakistan Movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan_Movement "Pakistan Movement") activist, who in January 1933 first published it (originally as "Pakstan") in a pamphlet *[Now or Never](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan_Declaration "Pakistan Declaration")*, using it as an [acronym](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Acronym "Acronym").[^31] [^32] [^33] Rahmat Ali explained: "It is composed of letters taken from the names of all our homelands, Indian and Asian, [Panjab](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Punjab_Province_\(British_India\) "Punjab Province (British India)"), [Afghania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North-West_Frontier_Province_\(1901%E2%80%932010\) "North-West Frontier Province (1901–2010)"), [Kashmir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jammu_and_Kashmir_\(princely_state\) "Jammu and Kashmir (princely state)"), [Sindh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sind_Division "Sind Division"), and [Baluchistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baluchistan_Agency "Baluchistan Agency")." He added, "Pakistan is both a [Persian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Persian_language "Persian language") and [Urdu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Urdu "Urdu") word... It means the land of the Paks, the spiritually pure and clean." [^34] Etymologists note that [پاک](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D9%BE%D8%A7%DA%A9#Persian "wikt:پاک") *pāk*, is 'pure' in Persian and [Pashto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pashto "Pashto") and the Persian suffix [ـستان](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%D9%80%D8%B3%D8%AA%D8%A7%D9%86#Persian "wikt:ـستان") *[\-stan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/-stan "-stan")* means 'land' or 'place of'.[^32]

Rahmat Ali's concept of Pakistan only related to the northwestern area of the [Indian subcontinent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_subcontinent "Indian subcontinent"). He also proposed the name "Banglastan" for the Muslim areas of [Bengal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bengal "Bengal") and "Osmanistan" for [Hyderabad State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hyderabad_State "Hyderabad State"), as well as a political federation between the three.[^35]
