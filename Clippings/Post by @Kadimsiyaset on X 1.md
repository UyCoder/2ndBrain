“The difference between a ruler and a tyrant is entirely or essentially this: that the ruler obeys the law and governs his subjects with a will that dedicates them to his service.”

John of Salisbury draws a more foundational line—parallel to the classical Islamic mirror-for-princes’ “just ruler-tyrannical sultan” distinction, but in the Latin tradition:

• The ruler is the servant of the law.

• The tyrant is the master of the law.

The boundary between them is not intention, but subjection to the law.

— (Policraticus, John of Salisbury, Timaş Akademi, p.59)
