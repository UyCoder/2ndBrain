### The Qing dynasty empire

**0:20** · China.

**0:22** · A cradle of world civilisation.

**0:25** · The land that invented printing, paper and gunpowder.

**0:31** · And under successive dynasties, the centre of a vast empire that stretched from the Pacific Ocean to the Himalayas... and from the eastern steppe to the tropical South China Sea.

**1:33** · Around 450 million people live within these borders – one third of the world’s population.

**1:41** · It is also estimated that China accounts for one third of the world’ wealth.

**1:48** · The imperial capital, Peking, and major hubs such as Nanking, and Canton, are among the greatest cities in the world.

**1:58** · It is a multi-ethnic empire, whose self-proclaimed aim is not economic or political expansion, but the maintenance of ‘harmony’... a worldview that derives from Confucius, the 6th century BC sage.

**2:14** · His teachings have governed society, in both public and private realms, for two thousand years.

**2:22** · Fundamental values - such as reverence for parents and ancestors, loyalty, and benevolence - seek to create an orderly and harmonious society.

**2:33** · It is also one that is rigidly conservative.

**2:38** · Young men from well-to-do families spend years studying the Confucian classics, in preparation for the imperial civil service exam. This is the only route to the higher levels of the Qing administration.

**2:54** · But – governed by scholarly bureaucrats, and an emperor who is both revered and feared - China is failing to keep up with a rapidly changing world.

### Century of humiliation

**5:31** · Westerners mock China as ‘the sick man of Asia’: a giant fallen prey to the imperialism of foreign powers, better adapted to life in the modern world.

**5:47** · But, by the turn of the twentieth century, there is a growing sense of outrage among the Chinese people... and a determination to fight back.

**15:36** · More than 2,000 years of imperial rule in China are at an end.
