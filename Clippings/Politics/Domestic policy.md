**Domestic policy**, also known as internal policy, is a type of [public policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_policy "Public policy") overseeing administrative decisions that are directly related to all issues and activity within a [state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_\(polity\) "State (polity)") 's borders. Sometimes also called [social policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_policy "Social policy"), it differs from [foreign policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_policy "Foreign policy"), which refers to the ways a government advances its interests in external politics. Domestic policy covers a wide range of areas, including [business](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Business "Business"), [education](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education_policy "Education policy"), [energy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_policy "Energy policy"), [healthcare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_policy "Health policy"), [law enforcement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law_enforcement "Law enforcement"), money and [taxes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taxes "Taxes"), [natural resources](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_resource_management "Natural resource management"), [social](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_policy "Social policy") [welfare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welfare_spending "Welfare spending"), and personal [rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rights "Rights") and freedoms.

## Implementation

The form of government of any particular state largely determines how its domestic policy is formed and implemented. Under [authoritarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Authoritarianism "Authoritarianism") governments, a [ruling group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Totalitarianism#Politics "Totalitarianism") may pursue its domestic policy goals without the input or consent of the people being governed. But in parliamentary democratic societies, the will of citizens has a much greater influence.

In a [democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy "Democracy"), the formal design of domestic policy is chiefly the responsibility of elected leaders, lawmaking bodies, and specialized government agencies. But a number of other factors also play a role in the process. Voters, for instance, determine which individuals and political parties have the power to determine policy. The [mass media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mass_media "Mass media") distribute, and opine, information about domestic issues and influence the beliefs and opinions of the people. [Lobbyists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lobbyists "Lobbyists"), [activist groups](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Activist_groups "Activist groups"), and other organizations also work to influence policy through a variety of methods. Such methods may include monetary donations, promises of support, advertising campaigns, or demonstrations and protests.

The effectiveness of domestic policy depends on the government bureaucracy (system of agencies) that puts laws and programs into action. In some cases, bureaucracies act slowly or inefficiently, or fail to apply policies as they were originally intended. Domestic policy may also face challenges in the courts. In many countries, courts have the power of [judicial review](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judicial_review "Judicial review"), which allows its judges to strike down any legislative or executive action that they find in violation of the policy's [constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution "Constitution").

## Areas

### Cultural policy

Cultural policy pertains to [the arts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_arts "The arts") and creative endeavors of a government or its citizens. A state's cultural policy is used to "channel both aesthetic creativity and collective ways of life" through a bureaucratic process.[^1] Cultural policy defines many of the fundamental aspects of a nation's existence, such as the [national language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_language "National language").[^2] These policies may be influential in forming a national identity, fostering [civic responsibilities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civic_responsibilities "Civic responsibilities"), and defining ethical behavior.[^3] Many countries have a [ministry of culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_minister "Culture minister") that oversees the government's cultural policy. [Arts policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arts_policy "Arts policy"), [language policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Language_policy "Language policy"), sports policy, and [museum planning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Museum_planning "Museum planning") are all policy areas governed by cultural policy.

### Economic policy

Economic policy pertains to a country's [economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy "Economy") and [treasury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treasury "Treasury"). [Monetary policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monetary_policy "Monetary policy") governs the supply of money and [interest rates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interest_rate "Interest rate") in a state, while [fiscal policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiscal_policy "Fiscal policy") governs how the state raises funds and decides how they are [spent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government_spending "Government spending").[^4] Developed nations typically have a [central bank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_bank "Central bank") that regulates monetary policy semi-independently of political actors.[^5] [Tax policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tax_policy "Tax policy"), [regulation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regulation "Regulation"), [monetary systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monetary_system "Monetary system"), [corporate law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corporate_law "Corporate law"), [public works](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_works "Public works"), [competition law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Competition_law "Competition law"), [incomes policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incomes_policy "Incomes policy"), [food policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Food_policy "Food policy"), [energy policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Energy_policy "Energy policy"), and [natural resource management](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Natural_resource_management "Natural resource management") are all policy areas governed by economic policy.

### Social policy

Social policy pertains to the well-being of society and the response to societal challenges.[^6] [Civil and political rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_and_political_rights "Civil and political rights"), [education policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Education_policy "Education policy"), [drug policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drug_policy "Drug policy"), [health policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Health_policy "Health policy"), [housing policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_housing "Public housing"), and [public security](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_security "Public security") are all policy areas governed by social policy.
