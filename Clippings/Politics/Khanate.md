A **khanate** ([/ ˈ x ɑː n eɪ t, - ə t /](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:IPA/English "Help:IPA/English") [*KHAHN-ayt, -⁠ət*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key "Help:Pronunciation respelling key")) or **khaganate** was a [polity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polity "Polity") ruled by a [khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khan_\(title\) "Khan (title)"), [khagan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khagan "Khagan"), [khatun](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khatun "Khatun"), or [khanum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanum "Khanum").[^1] [^2] Khanates were typically nomadic [Mongolic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol "Mongol") and [Turkic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkic_peoples "Turkic peoples") societies located on the [Eurasian Steppe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurasian_Steppe "Eurasian Steppe"),[^3] [^4] [^5] and politically equivalent in status to [kinship-based chiefdoms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiefdom "Chiefdom") and [feudal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Feudalism "Feudalism") [monarchies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchy "Monarchy"). Khanates and khaganates were organised [tribally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tribe "Tribe"), where leaders gained power on the support and loyalty of their warrior subjects,[^3] gaining tribute from subordinates as realm funding.[^6] In comparison to a khanate, a khaganate, the realm of a [khagan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khagan "Khagan"), was a [large nomadic state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nomadic_empire "Nomadic empire") maintaining subjugation over numerous smaller khanates.[^7] The title of khagan, translating as "Khan of the Khans", roughly corresponds in status to that of an emperor.[^4]

## Mongol khanates

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Asia_in_1335.svg/250px-Asia_in_1335.svg.png)

The successor states of the Mongol Empire in 1335: the Ilkhanate, Golden Horde Yuan dynasty and Chagatai Khanate

- [Mongol Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongol_Empire "Mongol Empire") (1206–1368) was the largest steppe nomadic Khaganate as well as [second largest empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_largest_empires "List of largest empires") and the largest contiguous empire [^8] in history. After [Genghis Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genghis_Khan "Genghis Khan") established [appanages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Appanage "Appanage") for his family in the Mongol Empire during his rule (1206–1227), his sons, daughters, and grandsons inherited separate sections of the empire. The Mongolian khanates that emerged from those appanages are listed below.
	- [Chagatai Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chagatai_Khanate "Chagatai Khanate") (1226–1347), established by [Chagatai Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chagatai_Khan "Chagatai Khan"), the second son of Genghis Khan. At its height in the late 13th century, the khanate extended from the [Amu Darya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amu_Darya "Amu Darya") south of the [Aral Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aral_Sea "Aral Sea") to the [Altai Mountains](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Altai_Mountains "Altai Mountains") in the border of modern-day Mongolia and China, roughly corresponding to the defunct [Qara Khitai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qara_Khitai "Qara Khitai") Empire. Initially the rulers of the Chagatai Khanate recognized the supremacy of the Great Khan, but by the reign of [Kublai Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kublai_Khan "Kublai Khan"), [Ghiyas-ud-din Baraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghiyas-ud-din_Baraq "Ghiyas-ud-din Baraq") no longer obeyed the emperor's orders. By 1347 the khanate had split into the [Moghulistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moghulistan "Moghulistan") and West Chagatai Khanate.[^9]

		- [Il-Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Il-Khanate "Il-Khanate") (1256–1335), established by the grandson of Genghis Khan, [Hulagu Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hulagu_Khan "Hulagu Khan"). Its core territory lies in what is now part of the countries of [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran"), [Azerbaijan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Azerbaijan "Azerbaijan"), and [Turkey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey"). At its greatest extent, the Ilkhanate also included parts of modern [Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iraq "Iraq"), [Syria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Syria "Syria"), [Armenia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armenia "Armenia"), [Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)"), [Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan "Afghanistan"), [Turkmenistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkmenistan "Turkmenistan"), [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan "Pakistan"), part of modern [Dagestan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dagestan "Dagestan"), and part of modern [Tajikistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajikistan "Tajikistan"). Later Ilkhanate rulers, beginning with [Ghazan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ghazan "Ghazan") in 1295, converted to [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam"). In the 1330s, the Ilkhanate was ravaged by the [Black Death](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Black_Death "Black Death"). Its last khan [Abu Sa'id](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abu_Sa%27id_\(Ilkhanid_dynasty\) "Abu Sa'id (Ilkhanid dynasty)") died in 1335, after which the khanate disintegrated. ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/32/450_roman-hunnic-empire_1764x1116.jpg/330px-450_roman-hunnic-empire_1764x1116.jpg)

Hunnic Empire of Attila in c. 450 CE

- [Avar Khaganate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Avar_Khaganate "Avar Khaganate")
- [Khanate of Kalat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanate_of_Kalat "Khanate of Kalat") – Brahui/Baloch Khanate
- [Later Jin dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Later_Jin_\(1616%E2%80%931636\) "Later Jin (1616–1636)") – Later evolved into the [Qing dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty")
- [Nishapur Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nishapur_Khanate "Nishapur Khanate")
- [Rouran Khaganate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rouran_Khaganate "Rouran Khaganate")
- [Xiongnu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xiongnu "Xiongnu"), mentioned in Chinese sources, were [possibly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xiongnu#Turkic_theories "Xiongnu") of [Proto-Turkic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Proto-Turkic_language "Proto-Turkic language"), [Turkic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkic_peoples "Turkic peoples"), [Mongolic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolic_peoples "Mongolic peoples") or else [Iranic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_peoples "Iranian peoples") origin ([disputed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xiongnu#Ethnolinguistic_origins "Xiongnu")) and are proposed as the possible ancestors of the [Huns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huns "Huns") ([whose language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hunnic_language "Hunnic language") remains unclassified).
	- [Xueyantuo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xueyantuo "Xueyantuo"), mentioned in Chinese sources, were a [Tiele](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tiele_people "Tiele people") tribe, related to the earlier [Dingling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dingling "Dingling") people, who emerged after the disintegration of the Xiongnu confederacy (they were at one point vassals of the [Göktürks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%B6kt%C3%BCrks "Göktürks"), later aligning with the [Tang dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tang_dynasty "Tang dynasty") against the [Eastern Göktürks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Turkic_Khaganate "Eastern Turkic Khaganate")).
