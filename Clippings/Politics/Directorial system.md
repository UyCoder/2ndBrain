The **directorial** or **executive council system** is a [political system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_system "Political system") in which [executive power](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_\(government\) "Executive (government)") is held directly by a [collegial body](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Collegial_body "Collegial body"), typically called a council, directory, or cabinet, with no single [head of government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_government "Head of government"). The system was pioneered by the [Supreme Executive Council of Pennsylvania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_Executive_Council_of_Pennsylvania "Supreme Executive Council of Pennsylvania") in the [American Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Revolution "American Revolution") and the [French Directory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Directory "French Directory"), which ended the [Reign of Terror](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reign_of_Terror "Reign of Terror"). It has since been used by the [Swiss Confederation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swiss_Confederation "Swiss Confederation") as part of the [1848 Swiss Constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1848_Swiss_Constitution "1848 Swiss Constitution"), adopted after the [Sonderbund War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sonderbund_War "Sonderbund War").[^1]

## Current directorial systems

Countries with directorial [heads of state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heads_of_state "Heads of state") sharing ceremonial functions:

- [Andorra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Andorra "Andorra"): [co-princes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Co-princes_of_Andorra "Co-princes of Andorra")
- [Bosnia and Herzegovina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bosnia_and_Herzegovina "Bosnia and Herzegovina"): [presidency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidency_of_Bosnia_and_Herzegovina "Presidency of Bosnia and Herzegovina")
- [Eswatini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eswatini "Eswatini"): [Ngwenyama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ngwenyama "Ngwenyama") and [Ndlovukati](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ndlovukati "Ndlovukati")
- [San Marino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Marino "San Marino"): [Captains Regent](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Captains_Regent "Captains Regent")

Countries governed by an executive directorial head of state:

- [Nicaragua](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nicaragua "Nicaragua"): [co-presidents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Co-presidents_of_Nicaragua "Co-presidents of Nicaragua")
- [San Marino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/San_Marino "San Marino"): [Congress of State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congress_of_State "Congress of State")
- [Switzerland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Switzerland "Switzerland"): [Federal Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Council_\(Switzerland\) "Federal Council (Switzerland)")

Supranational and subnational entities governed by a directorial system:

- [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union"): [European Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Council "European Council") and [European Commission](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Commission "European Commission")
- [Northern Ireland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Ireland "Northern Ireland"): [First Minister and deputy First Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Minister_of_Northern_Ireland "First Minister of Northern Ireland")
- [Switzerland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Switzerland "Switzerland"): [cantons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantons_of_Switzerland "Cantons of Switzerland"), [municipalities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Municipalities_of_Switzerland "Municipalities of Switzerland")

### Directorial republic in Switzerland

One country now using this form of government is [Switzerland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Switzerland "Switzerland").[^2] In Switzerland, directories operate at all levels of administration: federal, [cantonal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantons_of_Switzerland "Cantons of Switzerland") and municipal. On the face of it, the [Swiss Federal Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swiss_Federal_Council "Swiss Federal Council") might appear to be a typical parliamentary government; technically, however, it is not a meeting of ministers, but a college of heads of state and simultaneously the federal [cabinet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabinet_\(government\) "Cabinet (government)"). The current president of the confederation is in fact only a *[primus inter pares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primus_inter_pares "Primus inter pares")* (first among equals) with representative functions in particular for diplomacy with other States, and without any power either of direction or of coordination of the activity of colleagues.[^3] The [Swiss Federal Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swiss_Federal_Council "Swiss Federal Council") is elected by the [Federal Assembly](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Assembly_\(Switzerland\) "Federal Assembly (Switzerland)") for four years, and comprises seven members, among whom one serves as president and one as vice-president on a rotating basis, although these positions are symbolic in normal circumstances. Unlike in a parliamentary system, the Federal Council is not answerable before the Federal Assembly, but is elected for a roughly concurrent term of office. The link between the Swiss managerial system and the presidential system is even more evident for the cantonal governments, where currently all directors are all individually directly elected by the voters.[^4]

## History

In political history, the term directory, in French **directoire**, is applied to high collegial institutions of state composed of members styled director. Early directorial systems were the [Ambrosian Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Golden_Ambrosian_Republic "Golden Ambrosian Republic") (1447-1450), the [Bohemian Revolt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bohemian_Revolt "Bohemian Revolt") (1618–1620), [New England Confederation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England_Confederation "New England Confederation") (1643–1686), partially in [First Stadtholderless Period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Stadtholderless_Period "First Stadtholderless Period") of [Dutch Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_Republic "Dutch Republic") (1650–1672), [Pennsylvania Constitution of 1776](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pennsylvania_Constitution_of_1776 "Pennsylvania Constitution of 1776") and the [Directory of 1795–1799](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Directory "French Directory") in [France](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/France "France").[^5]

The French Directory was inspired by the [Pennsylvania Constitution of 1776](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pennsylvania_Constitution_of_1776 "Pennsylvania Constitution of 1776"), which prominently featured a collegial 12-member Supreme Executive Council with the president in fact only *[primus inter pares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primus_inter_pares "Primus inter pares")* (first among equals). Variants of this form of government, based on the French model, were also established in the European regions conquered by France during the [French Revolutionary Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Revolutionary_Wars "French Revolutionary Wars"). Directorial systems have a lower [presidentialism metric](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidentialism_metrics "Presidentialism metrics") value compared to presidential systems due to lower concentration of political power in the hands of one individual.[^6] [Military juntas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_junta "Military junta") differ from the directorial system by not being elected. Utilizing [sortition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sortition "Sortition") to select multiple executives can lead to a directorial system.

## Former directorial systems

In the past, countries with elected directories included:

- [Bohemian Revolt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bohemian_Revolt "Bohemian Revolt") from 1618 to 1620
- [New England Confederation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England_Confederation "New England Confederation") from 1643 to 1686
- [Commonwealth of England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_England "Commonwealth of England") from 1649 to 1653
- [Dutch Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dutch_Republic "Dutch Republic") partially in [First Stadtholderless Period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Stadtholderless_Period "First Stadtholderless Period") from 1650 to 1672
- [Pennsylvania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pennsylvania "Pennsylvania") - [Supreme Executive Council of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_Executive_Council_of_the_Commonwealth_of_Pennsylvania "Supreme Executive Council of the Commonwealth of Pennsylvania") from 1777 to 1790
- [First French Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_First_Republic "French First Republic") - [French Directory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_Directory "French Directory") from 1795 to 1799
- [Cisalpine Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cisalpine_Republic "Cisalpine Republic") from 1795 to 1799
- [Cispadane Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cispadane_Republic "Cispadane Republic") from 1796 to 1797
- [Roman Republic (1798–1799)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic_\(1798%E2%80%931799\) "Roman Republic (1798–1799)") from 1798 to 1799
- [Parthenopaean Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Parthenopaean_Republic "Parthenopaean Republic") in 1799
- [Costa Rica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Costa_Rica "Costa Rica") - [The Town's Legates Junta and High Government Junta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pact_of_Concord "Pact of Concord") from 1821 to 1823
- [Provisional Government of Oregon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Provisional_Government_of_Oregon "Provisional Government of Oregon") - [Executive Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_Committee_\(Oregon_Country\) "Executive Committee (Oregon Country)") from 1843 to 1845
- [Roman Republic (1849)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic_\(1849\) "Roman Republic (1849)") from 1849 to 1850 ([Triumvirate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Triumvirate "Triumvirate"))
- [Ukrainian People's Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ukrainian_People%27s_Republic "Ukrainian People's Republic") - [Directorate of Ukraine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Directorate_of_Ukraine "Directorate of Ukraine") from 1918 to 1920 [^7]
- [Republic of China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_China_\(1912%E2%80%931949\) "Republic of China (1912–1949)") - [National Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nationalist_government "Nationalist government") - from 1928 to 1947
- [Uruguay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uruguay "Uruguay") - [National Council of Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Council_of_Administration "National Council of Administration") from 1919 to 1933 and [National Council of Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Council_of_Government_\(Uruguay\) "National Council of Government (Uruguay)") from 1952 to 1967
- [East Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Germany "East Germany") - [State Council of East Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Council_of_East_Germany "State Council of East Germany") from 1960 to 1990
- [Union of Soviet Socialist Republics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union") - [All-Union Central Executive Committee](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/All-Union_Central_Executive_Committee "All-Union Central Executive Committee") from 1922 to 1938; [Presidium of the Supreme Soviet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidium_of_the_Supreme_Soviet "Presidium of the Supreme Soviet") from 1938 to 1989
- [Polish People's Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polish_People%27s_Republic "Polish People's Republic") - [State Council of the Polish People's Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_Council_of_the_Polish_People%27s_Republic "State Council of the Polish People's Republic") from 1952 to 1989
- [Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socialist_Federal_Republic_of_Yugoslavia "Socialist Federal Republic of Yugoslavia") - [Presidency of Yugoslavia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presidency_of_Yugoslavia "Presidency of Yugoslavia"), *de jure* from 1971, *de facto* from 1980 to 1991.
