Politics is the process by which groups of people make decisions on behalf of governing a specific society.[^1] The term usually refers to actions pertaining to civil [governments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government "Government"), but the word has also been applied to other areas of human group interactions, including [corporate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corporation "Corporation"), [academic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Academic_institution "Academic institution"), and [religious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion "Religion") institutions. Politics consists of social relations involving authority or power. Topics related to this area include [political philosophy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_philosophy "Political philosophy"), which seeks a rationale for politics and answers regarding the best form of society, and [public administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_administration "Public administration"), which examines the practices of governance.[^2]
