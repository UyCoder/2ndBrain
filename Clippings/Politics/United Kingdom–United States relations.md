Since 1776, relations between the [United Kingdom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom "United Kingdom") and the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States") have ranged from military opposition to close allyship. The [Thirteen Colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies "Thirteen Colonies") seceded from the [Kingdom of Great Britain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Great_Britain "Kingdom of Great Britain") and [declared independence in 1776](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence "United States Declaration of Independence"), fighting a successful [revolutionary war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War "American Revolutionary War"). While Britain was fighting [Napoleon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Napoleon "Napoleon"), the two nations fought the stalemated [War of 1812](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_1812 "War of 1812"). Relations were generally positive thereafter, save for [a short crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Trent_Affair "Trent Affair") in 1861 during the [American Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Civil_War "American Civil War"). In the 1920s, [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "New York City") surpassed [London](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London "London") as the world's leading financial center. The two nations fought Germany together during the [two World Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War "World War"); since 1940, the two countries have been close military allies, enjoying the [Special Relationship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_Relationship "Special Relationship") built as wartime allies and [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO") and [G7](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G7 "G7") partners. America and Britain are bound together by a shared history, a common language, an overlap in religious beliefs and legal principles, and kinship ties that reach back hundreds of years. In the 21st century, the United Kingdom and United States have closely coordinated on foreign policy, launching joint military invasions of [Afghanistan in 2001](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_invasion_of_Afghanistan "United States invasion of Afghanistan") and [Iraq in 2003](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq "2003 invasion of Iraq") and leading the [2011 NATO-led intervention in Libya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2011_military_intervention_in_Libya "2011 military intervention in Libya").

In the early 21st century, Britain affirmed its relationship with the United States as its "most important bilateral partnership" in current [British foreign policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_foreign_policy "British foreign policy"),[^3] and the [American foreign policy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Foreign_policy_of_the_United_States "Foreign policy of the United States") affirms its relationship with Britain as its second most important relationship, behind only [Canada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada "Canada").[^4] [^5] as evidenced in aligned political affairs, cooperation in the areas of trade, commerce, finance, technology, academics, as well as the arts and sciences; the sharing of government and military intelligence, and joint combat operations and [peacekeeping](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peacekeeping "Peacekeeping") missions carried out between the [United States Armed Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Armed_Forces "United States Armed Forces") and the [British Armed Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Armed_Forces "British Armed Forces"). As of January 2015, the United Kingdom was the fifth largest US trading partner in terms of exports and seventh in terms of imports of goods.[^6] In long-term perspective, the historian [Paul Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Johnson_\(writer\) "Paul Johnson (writer)") has called the United Kingdom–United States relations "the cornerstone of the modern, [liberal democratic world order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberal_international_order "Liberal international order") ".[^7] The two countries have also had a significant impact on the cultures of many other countries, as well as each other. They are the two main nodes of the [Anglosphere](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglosphere "Anglosphere"), with a combined population of just under 400 million in 2019. Together, they have given the English language a dominant [lingua franca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lingua_franca "Lingua franca") role in many aspects of the modern world.

## Special Relationship

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ab/President_Roosevelt_and_Winston_Churchill_seated_on_the_quarterdeck_of_HMS_PRINCE_OF_WALES_for_a_Sunday_service_during_the_Atlantic_Conference%2C_10_August_1941._A4816.jpg/250px-thumbnail.jpg)

UK Prime Minister Winston Churchill and US President Franklin Roosevelt at Atlantic Conference, August 1941

The [Special Relationship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_Relationship "Special Relationship") characterises the exceptionally close political, diplomatic, cultural, economic, military, and historical relations between the two countries. It has been specially used for relations since 1940.[^8]

## History

### Origins

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/7a/Mayflower_in_Plymouth_Harbor%2C_by_William_Halsall.jpg/250px-Mayflower_in_Plymouth_Harbor%2C_by_William_Halsall.jpg)

The Mayflower transported the Pilgrims to the New World in 1620, as depicted in William Halsall 's The Mayflower in Plymouth Sound, 1882.

After several failed attempts, the first permanent English settlement in mainland North America was established in 1607 at [Jamestown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamestown,_Virginia "Jamestown, Virginia") in the [Virginia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colony_and_Dominion_of_Virginia "Colony and Dominion of Virginia"). In 1630, the [Puritans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Puritans "Puritans") established the [Massachusetts Bay Colony](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Massachusetts_Bay_Colony "Massachusetts Bay Colony"); they emphasised not only pure religiosity, but also education and entrepreneurship.[^9]

Smaller colonies followed throughout the 17th and early 18th centuries. Each colony reported separately to London. During British colonization, liberal administrative, juridical, and market institutions were introduced.[^10] The colonial period also saw the introduction of [indentured servitude](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indentured_servitude "Indentured servitude") and [slavery](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Slavery_in_the_colonial_United_States "Slavery in the colonial United States").[^10] All of the [Thirteen Colonies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies "Thirteen Colonies") were involved in the [slave trade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_slave_trade "Atlantic slave trade").[^10]

### Early history

Over time, the Thirteen Colonies gradually obtained more self-government.[^11] British [mercantilist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mercantilism "Mercantilism") policies became more stringent, benefiting the mother country, which resulted in trade restrictions. Tensions escalated from 1765 to 1775 over issues of taxation without any American representation in Parliament. Congress unanimously [declared independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence "United States Declaration of Independence") in July 1776, formally initiating the [Revolutionary War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Revolutionary_War "American Revolutionary War"). The British managed to control New York City and parts of the South, but 90% of the American population was controlled by the Patriots. The entry of the French and Spanish decisively hurt British efforts. The [Treaty of Paris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Paris_\(1783\) "Treaty of Paris (1783)") ended the war in 1783,[^12] and the United States of America became the first colony in the world to successfully achieve independence in the modern era.[^13]

### War of 1812

The two nations fought again in the [War of 1812](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_1812 "War of 1812"). Neither side prevailed. The end of the war marked the end of a long period of conflict (1775–1815) and ushered in a new era of peace between the two nations. It was celebrated in the US as a victorious "second war of independence", while the British, having finally defeated Napoleon at the [Battle of Waterloo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Battle_of_Waterloo "Battle of Waterloo"), celebrated that triumph and largely forgot their second war with the US.[^14]

### American Civil War

Relations verged on open conflict during the war, and remained chilly after it as Americans resented British and Canadian roles in their [Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Civil_War "American Civil War"). Both sides worked to make sure tensions did not escalate toward war.[^15] The arbitration of the Alabama Claims in 1872 provided a satisfactory reconciliation; The British paid the United States $15.5 million for the economic damage caused by [Confederate Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confederate_States_Navy "Confederate States Navy") warships purchased from it.[^16] The British also withdrew from their military and political responsibilities in North America, bringing home their troops from Canada (keeping [Halifax](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Halifax,_Nova_Scotia "Halifax, Nova Scotia") as an Atlantic naval base), and turning responsibility over to the locals; in 1867, the separate Canadian colonies unified into a self-governing confederation named the " [Dominion of Canada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada "Canada") ".[^17]

### World Wars

[The Great Rapprochement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Great_Rapprochement "The Great Rapprochement") saw the convergence of social and political objectives between London and Washington from 1895 until World War I began in 1914.[^18] During [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I"), the Americans planned to send money, food and munitions, but it soon became clear that millions of soldiers would be needed to decide the war on the [Western Front](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Front_\(World_War_I\) "Western Front (World War I)").[^19] Two million soldiers were sent to Europe, with more on the way as the war ended.[^20]

Throughout the 1920s and 1930s, the level of mutual hostility was moderately high. The British diplomatic establishment largely distrusted the United States for a series of reasons. They included British suspicion of America's newfound global power, intentions, and reliability. Despite the frictions, London realized the United States was now the strongest power, and made it a cardinal principle of British foreign policy to "cultivate the closest relations with the United States".[^21]

Although many of the American people were sympathetic to Britain during its [World War II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II") conflict with [Nazi Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nazi_Germany "Nazi Germany"), there was widespread opposition to American intervention in European affairs. But beginning in March 1941, the United States enacted [Lend-Lease](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lend-Lease "Lend-Lease") in the form of tanks, fighter airplanes, munitions, bullets, food, and medical supplies. Britain received $31.4 billion out of a total of $50.1 billion sent to the Allies. Lend-lease aid was freely given, with no payments. There were also cash loans that were repaid at low rates over half a century.[^22] [^23] In August 1941, Churchill and Roosevelt announced the [Atlantic Charter](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Charter "Atlantic Charter") at a meeting in Newfoundland. It became a fundamental document—All the Allies had to sign it—and it led to the formation of the [United Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations "United Nations"). Shortly after the [Pearl Harbor attack](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Attack_on_Pearl_Harbor "Attack on Pearl Harbor"), Churchill spent several weeks in Washington with the senior staff hammering out wartime strategy with the American counterparts at the [Arcadia Conference](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arcadia_Conference "Arcadia Conference"). They set up the [Combined Chiefs of Staff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Combined_Chiefs_of_Staff "Combined Chiefs of Staff") to plot and coordinate strategy and operations. Military cooperation was close and successful.[^24] Technical collaboration was even closer, as the two nations shared secrets and weapons.[^25] [^26] [^27] Millions of American servicemen were based in Britain during the war. Americans were paid five times more than comparable British servicemen, which led to a certain amount of friction with British men.[^28]

### Cold War

In the aftermath of the war, Britain faced a deep financial crisis, whereas the United States enjoyed an economic boom. The United States continued to finance the British treasury after the war. Much of this aid was designed to restore infrastructure and help refugees. Britain received an [emergency loan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-American_loan "Anglo-American loan") of $3.75 billion in 1946; it was a 50-year loan with a low 2% interest rate.[^29] A more permanent solution was the [Marshall Plan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marshall_Plan "Marshall Plan") of 1948–51, which poured $13 billion into western Europe, of which $3.3 billion went to Britain to help modernise its infrastructure and business practices. The aid was a gift and carried requirements that Britain balance its budget, control tariffs, and maintain adequate currency reserves.[^30] In [British Malaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Malaya "British Malaya"), the British colonialists got American aid in the [war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Malayan_Emergency "Malayan Emergency") against the communist independence movement (1948–1960).[^31] The need to form a united front against the Soviet threat compelled the US and Britain to cooperate in helping to form the [North Atlantic Treaty Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Atlantic_Treaty_Organization "North Atlantic Treaty Organization") with their European allies. NATO is a mutual defence alliance whereby an attack on one member country is deemed an attack on all members.

The [Suez Crisis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suez_Crisis "Suez Crisis") erupted in October 1956 after Britain, France, and Israel invaded Egypt to regain control of the Suez Canal. Eisenhower had repeatedly warned London against any such action and feared a collapse of Western influence and the risk of a wider war in the region. Washington responded with heavy financial and diplomatic pressure to force the invaders to withdraw. When it became clear that the international sanctions were serious, the invaders withdrew in consideration of the very sizeable British post-war debt. The world noted Britain's fall from status in the Middle East and worldwide, and Anglo-American cooperation fell to the lowest point since the 1890s.[^32] [^33] [^34] However, the new prime minister [Harold Macmillan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harold_Macmillan "Harold Macmillan") (1957–1963) restored good terms with Eisenhower and President [John F. Kennedy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_F._Kennedy "John F. Kennedy") (1961–1963).[^35]

Throughout the 1980s, Thatcher was strongly supportive of Reagan's unwavering stance towards the Soviet Union. Often described as "political soulmates" and a high point in the " [Special Relationship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_Relationship "Special Relationship") ", Reagan and Thatcher met many times throughout their political careers.

### Post-Cold War

When the United States became the world's lone superpower after the [dissolution of the Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dissolution_of_the_Soviet_Union "Dissolution of the Soviet Union"), new threats emerged that confronted the United States and its [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO") allies. With military build-up beginning in August 1990 and the use of force beginning in January 1991, the United States, followed at a distance by Britain, provided the two largest forces respectively for the coalition army which liberated [Kuwait](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kuwait "Kuwait") from [Saddam Hussein](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saddam_Hussein "Saddam Hussein") 's regime during the [Persian Gulf War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gulf_War "Gulf War").

In the [1997 general election](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1997_United_Kingdom_general_election "1997 United Kingdom general election"), the [British Labour Party](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labour_Party_\(UK\) "Labour Party (UK)") was elected to office for the first time in eighteen years. The new prime minister, [Tony Blair](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Blair "Tony Blair"), and [Bill Clinton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bill_Clinton "Bill Clinton") both used the expression " [Third Way](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Third_Way "Third Way") " to describe their centre-left ideologies. In August 1997, the American people expressed solidarity with the British people, sharing in their grief and sense of shock on the [death of Diana, Princess of Wales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_of_Diana,_Princess_of_Wales "Death of Diana, Princess of Wales"), who perished in a car crash in Paris. Throughout 1998 and 1999, the United States and Britain sent troops to impose peace during the [Kosovo War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kosovo_War "Kosovo War"). Tony Blair made it a point to develop very close relationships with the White House.[^36]

### War on terror and Iraq War

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/70/Blair_Bush_Whitehouse_%282004-11-12%29.jpg/250px-Blair_Bush_Whitehouse_%282004-11-12%29.jpg)

Tony Blair and George W. Bush at a press conference in the White House on November 12, 2004

Sixty-seven Britons were among the [2,977 victims killed](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_fatal_victims_of_the_September_11_attacks "List of fatal victims of the September 11 attacks") during the terrorist attacks on the [World Trade Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Trade_Center_\(1973%E2%80%932001\) "World Trade Center (1973–2001)") and elsewhere on [September 11, 2001](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/September_11_attacks "September 11 attacks"). [Al-Qaeda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Qaeda "Al-Qaeda") was the attacker. Following the attacks, there was an enormous outpouring of sympathy from the United Kingdom for the American people, and Blair was one of Bush's strongest international supporters for military action against [Al-Qaeda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Qaeda "Al-Qaeda") and the Taliban. Indeed, Blair became the most articulate spokesman. President Bush told Congress that "America has no truer friend than Great Britain".[^37]

The United States declared a [war on terror](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_on_terror "War on terror") following the attacks. British forces participated in NATO's [war in Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_in_Afghanistan_\(2001%E2%80%93present\) "War in Afghanistan (2001–present)"). Blair took the lead (against the opposition of France, Canada, Germany, China, and Russia) in advocating the [invasion of Iraq](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2003_invasion_of_Iraq "2003 invasion of Iraq") in 2003. Again, Britain was second only to the US in sending forces to Iraq. Both sides wound down after 2009 and withdrew their last troops in 2011. President Bush and Prime Minister Blair provided sustained mutual political and diplomatic support and won votes in Congress and parliament against their critics at home.[^38] During this period, Secretary of Defense [Donald Rumsfeld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Rumsfeld "Donald Rumsfeld") said that "America has no finer ally than the United Kingdom." [^39] The [7 July 2005 London bombings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/7_July_2005_London_bombings "7 July 2005 London bombings") emphasised the difference in the terrorist threat to both nations. Terrorism against the United States and its military assets was primarily committed by foreign groups, like the [al-Qaeda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Al-Qaeda "Al-Qaeda") network and other Islamic extremists from the Middle East. The London bombings were carried out by homegrown extremist Muslims, and they emphasised the United Kingdom's threat from the radicalisation of its own people.

After claims by [Liberty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberty_\(pressure_group\) "Liberty (pressure group)") those British airports had been used by the [CIA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency "Central Intelligence Agency") for [extraordinary rendition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Extraordinary_rendition "Extraordinary rendition") flights, the [Association of Chief Police Officers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_of_Chief_Police_Officers "Association of Chief Police Officers") launched an investigation in November 2005. The report was published in June 2007 and found no evidence to support the claim. This was on the same day the Council of Europe released its report with evidence that the UK had colluded in extraordinary rendition, thus directly contradicting ACPO's findings.[^40] A 2018 report by the [Intelligence and Security Committee of Parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intelligence_and_Security_Committee_of_Parliament "Intelligence and Security Committee of Parliament") found the United Kingdom, specifically the [MI5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MI5 "MI5") and [MI6](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MI6 "MI6"), to be complicit in many of the renditions done by the US, having helped fund them, supplying them with intelligence and knowingly allowing them to happen.[^41]

By 2007, support amongst the British public for the Iraq war had plummeted.[^42] Despite Tony Blair's historically low approval ratings with the British people, mainly due to allegations of faulty government intelligence of Iraq possessing [weapons of mass destruction](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weapons_of_mass_destruction "Weapons of mass destruction"), his unapologetic and unwavering stance for the British alliance with the United States can be summed up in his own words. He said, "We should remain the closest ally of the US... not because they are powerful, but because we share their values." [^43] The alliance between [George W. Bush](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_W._Bush "George W. Bush") and Tony Blair seriously damaged the prime minister's standing in the eyes of many [British citizens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_people "British people").[^44] Tony Blair argued it was in the United Kingdom's interest to "protect and strengthen the bond" with the United States regardless of who is in the White House.[^45] A perception that the relationship was unequal led to use of the term "Poodle-ism" in the British media, that Britain and its leaders were lapdogs to the Americans.[^46] [^47]

On June 11, 2009, the [British Overseas Territory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Overseas_Territory "British Overseas Territory") of [Bermuda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bermuda "Bermuda") accepted four [Chinese Uighurs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uyghurs "Uyghurs") from the American detainment facility at the [Guantanamo Bay Naval Base](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guantanamo_Bay_Naval_Base "Guantanamo Bay Naval Base") in Cuba.[^48] All had been captured during the [United States-led invasion of Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_invasion_of_Afghanistan "United States invasion of Afghanistan") in October 2001. This decision angered London, as British officials felt they should have been consulted.[^49]

### Tension with Scotland

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/65/First_Minister_of_Scotland_and_Mayor_of_Chicago_%28cropped%29.jpg/250px-First_Minister_of_Scotland_and_Mayor_of_Chicago_%28cropped%29.jpg)

First Minister of Scotland, Alex Salmond, (left) was heavily criticised by Washington regarding the Scottish Government's decision to release Abdelbaset al-Megrahi.

On August 20, 2009, the [Scottish Government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scottish_Government "Scottish Government"), headed by [First Minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Minister_of_Scotland "First Minister of Scotland") [Alex Salmond](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alex_Salmond "Alex Salmond"), announced that it would release [Abdelbaset al-Megrahi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Abdelbaset_al-Megrahi "Abdelbaset al-Megrahi") on medical grounds. He was the only person convicted in the terrorist plot which killed 190 Americans and 43 Britons on [Pan American Worldways' Flight 103](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pan_Am_Flight_103 "Pan Am Flight 103") over the town of [Lockerbie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lockerbie "Lockerbie"), Scotland on December 21, 1988. He was sentenced to life in prison in 2001, but was now released after being diagnosed with terminal cancer, with around three months to live. Americans said the decision was uncompassionate and insensitive to the memory of the victims of the 1988 Lockerbie bombing.

President [Barack Obama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barack_Obama "Barack Obama") said that the decision was "highly objectionable".[^50] US Ambassador [Louis Susman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Susman "Louis Susman") said that although the decision made by Scotland was extremely regrettable, relations with the United Kingdom would remain fully intact and strong.[^51] The British government, led by Prime Minister [Gordon Brown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gordon_Brown "Gordon Brown"), was not involved in the release and Prime Minister Brown stated at a press conference his government had played 'no role' in the Scottish decision, as responsibility for [Scots law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scots_law "Scots law") and justice in Scotland is the full responsibility of the Scottish Government.[^52] Abdelbaset al-Megrahi died May 20, 2012, at the age of 60.

The decision from the Scottish Government to release Abdelbaset al-Megrahi was defended by First Minister Alex Salmond, who said that he was released "in good faith" after the Scottish Government received "expert advice" which advised that al-Megrahi had only three months to live.[^53] Salmond claimed that the advice had been presented to the Scottish Government by Dr Andrew Fraser, the head of medical services for the [Scottish Prison Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scottish_Prison_Service "Scottish Prison Service"), who Salmond described as "a physician of great experience and a man with personal integrity".[^53] In 2010, Salmond rejected a request from the [United States Senate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Senate "United States Senate") to testify about [Libya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Libya "Libya") and the decision to release Abdelbaset al-Megrahi.[^54]

### Present status

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/fd/Barack_Obama_Michelle_Obama_Queen_Elizabeth_II_Buckingham_Palace_London.jpg/250px-Barack_Obama_Michelle_Obama_Queen_Elizabeth_II_Buckingham_Palace_London.jpg)

Queen Elizabeth II welcomed President Barack Obama and First Lady Michelle Obama to Buckingham Palace on April 1, 2009.

British policy is that the relationship with the United States represents the United Kingdom's "most important bilateral relationship" in the world.[^3] United States Secretary of State [Hillary Clinton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hillary_Clinton "Hillary Clinton") paid tribute to the relationship in February 2009 by saying, "it stands the test of time".[^55]

John Dumbrell wrote in 2006:

> Any confidence in the absence of British anti-Americanism is misplaced. British attitudes towards the US often exhibit cultural snobbery, envy, crude stereotyping, and resentment at America's power in the world. Such attitudes do not, as we will see demonstrated in public opinion surveys, amount to a rabid hostility. In many ways, they are understandable expressions of group feeling towards an ever-present and powerful 'other'. Many of these attitudes – that, for example, the US is the land both of rampant, destructive individualism and of homogenized sameness – are inherently contradictory. It is absurd, however, to pretend that they do not exist.[^56]

#### Obama administration 2009–2017

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ad/President_Barack_Obama_meets_Prime_Minister_Gordon_Brown.jpg/250px-President_Barack_Obama_meets_Prime_Minister_Gordon_Brown.jpg)

Prime Minister Gordon Brown, and President Barack Obama meet at the White House in March 2009

On March 3, 2009, [Gordon Brown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gordon_Brown "Gordon Brown") made his first visit to the [White House](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_House "White House"). During his visit, he presented the president a gift in the form of a pen holder carved from [HMS *Gannet*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HMS_Gannet_\(1878\) "HMS Gannet (1878)"), which served anti-slavery missions off the coast of Africa. [Barack Obama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barack_Obama "Barack Obama") 's gift to the prime minister was a box of 25 DVDs with movies including *[Star Wars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Star_Wars_\(film\) "Star Wars (film)")* and *[E.T.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/E.T._the_Extra-Terrestrial "E.T. the Extra-Terrestrial")* The wife of the prime minister, [Sarah Brown](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sarah_Jane_Brown "Sarah Jane Brown"), gave the Obama daughters, Sasha and Malia, two dresses from British clothing retailer [Topshop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Topshop "Topshop"), and a few unpublished books that have not reached the United States. [Michelle Obama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michelle_Obama "Michelle Obama") gave the prime minister's sons two [Marine One](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marine_One "Marine One") helicopter toys.[^57] During this visit to the United States, Gordon Brown made an address to a joint session of the [United States Congress](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Congress "United States Congress"), a privilege rarely accorded to foreign heads of government.

In March 2009, a [Gallup poll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gallup_poll "Gallup poll") of Americans showed 36% identified Britain as their country's "most valuable ally", followed by Canada, Japan, Israel, and Germany rounding out the top five.[^58] The poll also indicated that 89% of Americans view the United Kingdom favourably, second only to Canada with 90%.[^58] According to the [Pew Research Center](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pew_Research_Center "Pew Research Center"), a global survey conducted in July 2009 revealed that 70% of Britons who responded had a favourable view of the United States.[^59]

In 2010, Obama stated, "The United States has no closer friend and ally than the United Kingdom, and I reiterated my deep and personal commitment to the special relationship between our two countries." [^60] In February 2011, *[The Daily Telegraph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Daily_Telegraph "The Daily Telegraph")*, based on evidence from [WikiLeaks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WikiLeaks "WikiLeaks"), reported that the United States had tendered sensitive information about the [British Trident nuclear arsenal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UK_Trident_programme "UK Trident programme") (whose missile delivery systems are manufactured and maintained in the United States) to the [Russian Federation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Federation "Russian Federation") as part of a deal to encourage Russia to ratify the [New START Treaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_START_Treaty "New START Treaty"). Professor Malcolm Chalmers of the [Royal United Services Institute for Defence and Security Studies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Royal_United_Services_Institute_for_Defence_and_Security_Studies "Royal United Services Institute for Defence and Security Studies") speculated that serial numbers could undermine Britain's non-verification policy by providing Russia "with another data point to gauge the size of the British arsenal".[^61]

On May 25, 2011, during his official visit to the UK, Obama reaffirmed the relationship between the United Kingdom and the United States of America in an address to Parliament at [Westminster Hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westminster_Hall "Westminster Hall"). Amongst other points, Obama stated: "I've come here today to reaffirm one of the oldest, one of the strongest alliances the World has ever known. It's long been said that the United States and the United Kingdom share a special relationship." [^62] In the final days before the [Scottish independence referendum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scottish_independence_referendum "Scottish independence referendum") in September 2014, Obama announced in public the vested interest of the United States of America in enjoying the continued partnership with a 'strong and united' UK which he described as "one of the closest allies we will ever have".[^63]

In 2015, [GCHQ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GCHQ "GCHQ") notified the US of [links between Trump associates and Russian officials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Links_between_Trump_associates_and_Russian_officials "Links between Trump associates and Russian officials").[^64] During a joint press conference with Prime Minister [Theresa May](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theresa_May "Theresa May"), Obama stated, "The bottom line is, is that we don't have a stronger partner anywhere in the world than the United Kingdom." ##### Controversy over American foods

In 2017, US President [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump "Donald Trump") appointed pharmaceutical heir [Woody Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Woody_Johnson "Woody Johnson"), a financial supporter of his campaign, as ambassador 2017–2021. Johnson advocated for more agricultural trade and the deregulation of US food exports to Britain.[^92] In March 2019, Johnson wrote an article in the *Daily Telegraph* promoting American chlorinated chicken as safe, and stating that health fears over hormone-fed beef were "myths".[^93] This came after he urged the UK to open up to the US agriculture market after the British exit from the European Union and ignore the "smear campaign" of those with "their own protectionist agenda".[^94]

Johnson was criticised by several British agriculture standard boards, such as the Red Tractor Assurance whose CEO, Jim Moseley stated the UK's food standards were "now under threat from... the United States food lobby".[^95] [^96] Minette Batters, president of the UK National Farmers Union, agreed with Johnson's claims that chlorine-rinsed chicken was safe for consumption, but stated that factors such as animal welfare and environmental protection also had to be considered.[^97] George Eustace, former British agriculture minister, told the press:

> Agriculture in the US remains quite backward in many respects....Whereas we have a 'farm to fork' approach to managing disease and contamination risk throughout the supply chain through good husbandry, the US is more inclined to simply treat contamination of its meat at the end with a chlorine or similar wash.[^98]

##### Blocking Chinese technology

In 2020, while the UK was planning to invest in new [5G](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/5G "5G") mobile telecommunications equipment, Washington was openly lobbying and pressuring the British government, to prevent allowing the Chinese telecommunications giant [Huawei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huawei "Huawei") from installing its equipment in the UK.[^99] This was over allegations it will allow the Chinese to espionage in the country, and this might be a break in the [Five Eyes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five_Eyes "Five Eyes") intelligence programme. Since 2003, the UK has allowed its telecoms operators, such as the incumbent [BT](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BT_Group "BT Group"), to install Huawei equipment in its infrastructure backbone. To prevent any concerns about possible hacking after reports of unusual activity in the Huawei equipment, in 2010 Huawei jointly created with the British intelligence agency [GCHQ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/GCHQ "GCHQ") an equipment investigate centre in the outskirts of [Banbury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banbury "Banbury") called the *Huawei Cyber Security Evaluation Centre* which is also known by its nickname *"the Cell"*.[^100] [^101] In July 2020 after American pressure, the British government announced that it has banned adding any new Huawei telecoms equipment into the British [landline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landline "Landline") and [mobile networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cellular_network "Cellular network"), and request that all companies replace the existing equipment by 2027.[^102] [^103]

## Trade, investment, and the economy

The United States accounts for the United Kingdom's largest single export market, buying $57 billion worth of British goods in 2007.[^125] Total trade of imports and exports between the United Kingdom and the United States amounted to the sum of $107.2 billion in 2007.[^126]

The United States and the United Kingdom share the world's largest foreign direct investment partnership. In 2005, American direct investment in the United Kingdom totaled $324 billion, while British direct investment in the United States totaled $282 billion.[^127] In a press conference that made several references to the special relationship, US Secretary of State [John Kerry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Kerry "John Kerry"), in London with UK Foreign Secretary [William Hague](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Hague "William Hague") on September 9, 2013, said:

> We are not only each other's largest investors in each of our countries, one to the other, but the fact is that every day almost one million people go to work in America for British companies that are in the United States, just as more than one million people go to work here in Great Britain for American companies that are here. So we are enormously tied together, obviously. And we are committed to making both the U.S.-UK and the U.S.-EU relationships even stronger drivers of our prosperity.[^128]

### Trade agreements

In 2020, the two countries opened negotiations for a [free trade agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Kingdom%E2%80%93United_States_free_trade_agreement "United Kingdom–United States free trade agreement"); however, talks have been postponed until 2025 at the earliest.[^129]

In 2022, with the administration of President [Joe Biden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Biden "Joe Biden") uninterested in further negotiations, the United Kingdom began negotiating economic agreements with individual states.[^130] Regulation of international trade is a federal responsibility under the [Commerce Clause](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commerce_Clause "Commerce Clause") of the US Constitution, preventing state agreements from changing customs rules; therefore, the UK has aimed at signing [Memorandum of Understanding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memorandum_of_Understanding "Memorandum of Understanding") (MoU) agreements with the [US states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/US_state "US state"). MoUs aim to remove market access barriers and increase trade and investment opportunities for UK and US companies. Former [British trade minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minister_of_State_for_Trade_Policy "Minister of State for Trade Policy") [Penny Mordaunt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penny_Mordaunt "Penny Mordaunt") claimed that US state-level deals would pave the way for a full UK-US FTA.[^131]

In June 2023, Biden and Sunak announced the 'Atlantic Declaration' to strengthen economic ties between the UK and the US.[^147] The agreement included a limited trade pact covering critical minerals needed for [EV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_vehicle "Electric vehicle") [batteries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Electric_vehicle_battery "Electric vehicle battery") and a new data protection deal, in addition to easing trade barriers.[^148] [^149] The declaration commits both nations to increase research collaboration in future technologies, such as [AI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/AI "AI"), future [5G](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/5G "5G") and [6G](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/6G_\(network\) "6G (network)") telecoms, [quantum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quantum "Quantum"), [semiconductors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Semiconductors "Semiconductors") and [engineering biology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Engineering_biology "Engineering biology").[^150] In addition to a commitment in principle to a new UK-US Data Bridge; that facilitates the transfer of data by UK businesses to certified US organisations.[^151]

During the signing of the [accession of the United Kingdom to CPTPP](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Accession_of_the_United_Kingdom_to_CPTPP "Accession of the United Kingdom to CPTPP") on July 16, 2023, [Kemi Badenoch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kemi_Badenoch "Kemi Badenoch") blamed the lack of progress on the UK-US FTA on the change of administration from Donald Trump to Joe Biden after the 2020 election.[^152] [^153]

On October 3, 2023, Biden and Sunak were reported to be preparing a "foundational" trade agreement between the two countries which will be modelled on the [Indo-Pacific Economic Framework](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indo-Pacific_Economic_Framework "Indo-Pacific Economic Framework"), however it will not constitute a [free trade agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_trade_agreement "Free trade agreement") under [World Trade Organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Trade_Organization "World Trade Organization") rules as the proposals do not contain [market access](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Market_access "Market access") commitments.[^154] The proposed partnership aims to cover subjects such as digital trade, labour protections and [agriculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agriculture "Agriculture").[^155] On the same day, Badenoch reiterated that there was "zero" chance of a free trade agreement under President Biden's administration, citing his attitude to such deals.[^156]

### 2026 Greenland dispute and trade war

Relations between the United Kingdom and the United States underwent a significant diplomatic rupture in early 2026. On January 17, 2026, President [Donald Trump](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Donald_Trump "Donald Trump") announced via [Truth Social](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Truth_Social "Truth Social") the imposition of a 10% [tariff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tariff "Tariff") on all British goods, effective February 1, 2026.[^157] The administration stated the rate would increase to 25% on June 1, 2026, unless a deal was reached for the "Complete and Total purchase of [Greenland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greenland "Greenland") " by the United States.[^158]

The tariffs were characterized as retaliation for the UK's participation in [Operation Arctic Endurance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Operation_Arctic_Endurance "Operation Arctic Endurance"), a multilateral mission supporting Danish sovereignty on the island. Prime Minister [Keir Starmer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Keir_Starmer "Keir Starmer") condemned the measures as "completely wrong," asserting that "applying tariffs on allies for pursuing the collective security of [NATO](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NATO "NATO") allies" was unacceptable and that Greenland's future remained a matter for Denmark and Greenlanders.[^159] The dispute has been described by analysts as the most significant threat to the [Special Relationship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Special_Relationship "Special Relationship") since its inception.[^160]

At the [World Economic Forum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_Economic_Forum "World Economic Forum") in [Davos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Davos "Davos") on 21 January 2026, Trump said the United States would not proceed with the tariffs on British goods that had been scheduled to take effect on 1 February.[^161] [^162]

## Tourism

More than 4.5 million Britons visit the United States every year, spending $14 billion. Around 3 million people from the United States visit the United Kingdom every year, spending $10 billion.[^163] With the worldwide pandemic of COVID-19, international tourism in both countries collapsed in 2020.

Following the end of the COVID-19 pandemic, tourism has recovered globally, with some regions of the world experiencing a higher degree of tourism than before the pandemic.[^164] In 2023, the UK received 5.1 million visitors from the US, the highest number ever recorded; spending £6.3 billion.[^165] As of January 2025, British visitors to the US totalled almost 3 million, using a YTD format.[^166]

## Transportation

All three major American airlines, [American Airlines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Airlines "American Airlines"), [United Airlines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Airlines "United Airlines"), and [Delta Air Lines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Delta_Air_Lines "Delta Air Lines") fly directly between the US and the UK, principally between London and New York, although all three fly to [Heathrow Airport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heathrow_Airport "Heathrow Airport") from several hubs, as well as to other major UK airports such as [Manchester Airport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_Airport "Manchester Airport"), [Edinburgh Airport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edinburgh_Airport "Edinburgh Airport") and [Glasgow Airport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glasgow_Airport "Glasgow Airport"). Additionally, Delta [codeshares](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Codeshare_agreement "Codeshare agreement") with the UK's [Virgin Atlantic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Virgin_Atlantic "Virgin Atlantic") which it owns a 49% stake. Low-cost carriers [JetBlue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/JetBlue "JetBlue") and [Southwest Airlines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southwest_Airlines "Southwest Airlines") fly between the eastern US and the British overseas territories of [Bermuda](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bermuda "Bermuda"), [British Virgin Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Virgin_Islands "British Virgin Islands"), [Cayman Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cayman_Islands "Cayman Islands"), and the [Turks and Caicos Islands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turks_and_Caicos_Islands "Turks and Caicos Islands"), with JetBlue also flying between London and New York. The British flag carrier [British Airways](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Airways "British Airways") flies to over twenty destinations in the US. Also, the main British [charter airline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Air_charter "Air charter"), [TUI Airways](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TUI_Airways "TUI Airways"), flies to the US, although principally to the holiday destinations of Florida and California. Both American Airlines and British Airways are founders of the [airline alliance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Airline_alliance "Airline alliance") [Oneworld](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oneworld "Oneworld"). BA, TUI Airways, and Virgin Atlantic are major purchasers of American-made [Boeing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boeing "Boeing") aircraft. Flying between the US and UK is at the moment in 2019 supported by the [US-EU Open Skies Agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/EU-US_Open_Skies_Agreement "EU-US Open Skies Agreement"), which came about in 2008, which allows any airline from either country to fly between each other.

[John F. Kennedy International Airport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_F._Kennedy_International_Airport "John F. Kennedy International Airport") in [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "New York City") is the most popular international destination for people flying out of [Heathrow Airport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heathrow_Airport "Heathrow Airport"). Over 2.8 million people on multiple daily non-stop flights flew from Heathrow to JFK in 2008.[^167] [Concorde](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Concorde "Concorde"), British Airways' flagship supersonic airliner, began trans-Atlantic service to [Washington Dulles International Airport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Washington_Dulles_International_Airport "Washington Dulles International Airport") in the United States on May 24, 1976. The trans-Atlantic route between London's Heathrow and New York's JFK, in under 3½ hours, had its first operational flight between the two hubs on October 19, 1977, and the last on October 23, 2003.[^168]

The two main American [intercity bus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intercity_bus_service "Intercity bus service") carriers; [Greyhound Lines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greyhound_Lines "Greyhound Lines") and during the period from 1999 to 2019 [Coach USA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coach_USA "Coach USA"), plus their subsidiaries are each owned by a major British transportation company [FirstGroup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FirstGroup "FirstGroup") with Greyhound [^169] and [Stagecoach](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stagecoach_Group "Stagecoach Group") with Coach USA. Coach USA's budget brand [Megabus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megabus_\(North_America\) "Megabus (North America)"), which started in 2006, is a copycat of [the British version](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Megabus_\(Europe\) "Megabus (Europe)") of the discount coach company that started in 2003.[^170]

In the 20th century, there were 78 formal and informal summits bringing together the president and the prime minister to deal with an agreed-upon agenda. The first was in 1918, the second in 1929. The rest began in 1941, which marked the decline of ambassadors as the key transmitters of policy discussions. In three out of four of the summits, the British delegation traveled to America. Summits have become much less important in the 21st century, with its new communication modes.[^171]

State visits involving the head of state have been made over the years by four presidents and two monarchs. [Queen Elizabeth II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Queen_Elizabeth_II "Queen Elizabeth II") met all the presidents from [Harry Truman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Truman "Harry Truman") to [Joe Biden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joe_Biden "Joe Biden") (The Queen did not meet [Lyndon Johnson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lyndon_Johnson "Lyndon Johnson") during his presidency,[^1] but Johnson attended the state dinner hosted by [Dwight Eisenhower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dwight_Eisenhower "Dwight Eisenhower") at the [White House](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/White_House "White House") for the Queen on her state visit to the United States on October 17, 1957, as [Senate Majority Leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Senate_Majority_Leader "Senate Majority Leader").[^173] [^174]).[^175] [^176] In addition, the Queen made three private visits in 1984, 1985, and 1991 to see stallion stations and stud farms.[^177]

### Strategic Alliance Cyber Crime Working Group

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f6/Saccwg_500_031708.jpg/250px-Saccwg_500_031708.jpg)

Map showing member countries of the Strategic Alliance Cyber Crime Working Group and their respective lead agencies. This map refers to Serious Organised Crime Agency; this body is now known as the National Crime Agency.

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5f/UKUSA_Map.svg/250px-UKUSA_Map.svg.png)

Map of the UKUSA Agreement countries: Australia, Canada New Zealand, the United Kingdom, and the United States

The *Strategic Alliance Cyber Crime Working Group* is an initiative by [Australia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Australia "Australia"), [Canada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canada "Canada"), [New Zealand](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Zealand "New Zealand"), the United Kingdom, and headed by the United States as a "formal partnership between these nations dedicated to tackling larger global crime issues, particularly organised crime". The cooperation consists of "five countries from three continents banding together to fight cyber crime synergistically by sharing intelligence, swapping tools and best practices, and strengthening and even synchronising their respective laws".[^196]

Within this initiative, there is increased information sharing between the United Kingdom's [National Crime Agency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Crime_Agency "National Crime Agency") and the United States' [Federal Bureau of Investigation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Bureau_of_Investigation "Federal Bureau of Investigation") on matters relating to serious fraud or cyber crime.

### UK–USA Security Agreement

The [UK–USA Security Agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UKUSA_Agreement "UKUSA Agreement") is an alliance of five English-speaking countries: Australia, Canada, New Zealand, the United Kingdom, and the United States, for the sole purpose of sharing intelligence. The precursor to this agreement is essentially an extension of the historic [BRUSA Agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1943_BRUSA_Agreement "1943 BRUSA Agreement"), which was signed in 1943. In association with the [ECHELON](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ECHELON "ECHELON") system, all five nations are assigned to intelligence collection and analysis from different parts of the world. For example, the United Kingdom hunts for communications in [Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Europe "Europe"), [Africa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Africa "Africa"), and [European Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Russia "European Russia") whereas the United States has responsibility for gathering intelligence in [Latin America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_America "Latin America"), [Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asia "Asia"), [Asiatic Russia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/North_Asia "North Asia"), and [northern mainland China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_China "Northern China").[^197]

## Heritage

![A man sitting behind a desk](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/94/Barack_Obama_sitting_at_the_Resolute_desk_2009.jpg/250px-Barack_Obama_sitting_at_the_Resolute_desk_2009.jpg)

Barack Obama sitting at the Resolute desk, which was handcrafted from barque timbers of the decommissioned HMS Resolute, and then presented by Queen Victoria as a gift to the United States on November 23, 1880

The United States and Britain share many threads of cultural heritage.

Since English is the main language of both the British and the Americans, both nations belong to the [English-speaking world](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English-speaking_world "English-speaking world"). Their common language comes with (relatively minor) [differences in spelling, pronunciation, and the meaning of words](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_and_British_English_differences "American and British English differences").[^199]

The [American legal system](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law_of_the_United_States "Law of the United States") is largely based on [English common law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_law "English law"). The [American system of local government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_government_in_the_United_States "Local government in the United States") is rooted in [English precedents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_government_in_England "Local government in England"), such as the offices of county courts and sheriffs. Although the US remains more highly religious than Britain,[^200] the largest Protestant denominations emerged from British churches brought across the Atlantic, such as the [Baptists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baptists "Baptists"), [Methodists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methodism "Methodism"), [Congregationalists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congregational_church "Congregational church") and Episcopalians. Britain and the United States practice what is commonly referred to as an [Anglo-Saxon economy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglo-Saxon_economy "Anglo-Saxon economy") in which levels of regulation and taxes are relatively low, and the government provides a low to medium level of social services in return.[^201] [Independence Day](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Independence_Day_\(United_States\) "Independence Day (United States)"), July 4, is a national celebration that commemorates the July 4, 1776, adoption of the [Declaration of Independence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Declaration_of_Independence "United States Declaration of Independence") from the British Empire. American defiance of Britain is expressed in the American national anthem, " [The Star-Spangled Banner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Star-Spangled_Banner "The Star-Spangled Banner") ", written during the [War of 1812](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/War_of_1812 "War of 1812") to the tune of a British celebratory song as the Americans beat off a British attack on Baltimore.

It is estimated that between 40.2 million and 72.1 million Americans today have British ancestry, i.e., between 13% and 23.3% of the US population.[^202] [^203] [^204] In the [1980 US census](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1980_US_census "1980 US census"), 61,311,449 Americans reported British ancestry reaching 32.56% of the US population at the time which, even today, would make them the largest ancestry group in the United States.[^205]

Particular symbols of the close relationship between the two countries are the JFK Memorial and the [American Bar Association](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Bar_Association "American Bar Association") 's Magna Carta Memorial, both at [Runnymede](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Runnymede "Runnymede") in England.

## Religion

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e0/Whitfield_Memorial_Church%2C_Tottenham_Court_Road%2C_London-8Feb2008.jpg/250px-Whitfield_Memorial_Church%2C_Tottenham_Court_Road%2C_London-8Feb2008.jpg)

Whitfield Memorial Church in the Camden section of London, which is home to the American International Church

Both the United States and the United Kingdom share the similarity that a majority of their populations state that their belief is [Christian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity "Christianity"), at [70.4% in the US](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christianity_in_the_United_States "Christianity in the United States") [^206] and [59.5% in the UK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion_in_the_United_Kingdom "Religion in the United Kingdom"). Also, in both countries, the majority of Christian followers are members of the [mainline Protestant group of churches](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainline_Protestant "Mainline Protestant"), rather than the [Roman Catholic Church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church "Catholic Church"), although the Catholic church is relatively sizeable in both countries. Many of these mainline Protestant churches in the United States have their origins in the United Kingdom, or their founders were British. This includes [Episcopal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Episcopal_Church_\(United_States\) "Episcopal Church (United States)") ([Anglican](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anglicanism "Anglicanism")), [Baptist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baptists "Baptists"), [Methodist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Methodism "Methodism"), [Presbyterian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Presbyterianism "Presbyterianism"), [Congregational](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Congregational_church "Congregational church"), and [Quaker.](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quakers "Quakers")

Nevertheless, there are three big disparities between the two nations in the role of religion and faith. Firstly, the United Kingdom has [an established church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_state "Christian state") in two of the four nations of the country; the Anglican [Church of England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_of_England "Church of England"), where [the head of state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchy_of_the_United_Kingdom "Monarchy of the United Kingdom") is [the head of the church](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_Governor_of_the_Church_of_England "Supreme Governor of the Church of England") in one, and the Presbyterian [Church of Scotland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_of_Scotland "Church of Scotland") which plays a notable role of the other. The United States, on the other hand, requires a strict [separation of church and state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Separation_of_church_and_state "Separation of church and state"), as stated in [the First Amendment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution "First Amendment to the United States Constitution"). Another sizable difference between the US and the UK is the [piety](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Piety "Piety") of followers, as the UK is much more [secular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secularism "Secularism") than the US. A Gallup poll in 2015 reported that 41% of Americans said they [regularly attend religious services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_attendance "Church attendance"),[^207] compared to just 10% of Britons.[^208] Thirdly, a preeminent distinction between the two countries is the declaration of faith. In the United Kingdom, religion, especially those who follow the mainstream Protestant churches, is rarely discussed, and the country is a secular society. However, in the US, religion and faith are seen as a major part of the personal being, and declarations are much stronger.

The United Kingdom also has a large number of those possessing [no faith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atheism "Atheism") or are [agnostic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Agnosticism "Agnosticism") with 25.7% saying they are [irreligious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irreligion "Irreligion"), compared with just [10% in the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atheism_in_the_United_States "Atheism in the United States") who say that they don't believe in a God. Many notable British atheists, including [Richard Dawkins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Richard_Dawkins "Richard Dawkins") and [Christopher Hitchens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christopher_Hitchens "Christopher Hitchens"), are known in the US. The [Atheist Bus campaign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atheist_Bus_campaign "Atheist Bus campaign"), which started in London in 2008 by [Ariane Sherine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ariane_Sherine "Ariane Sherine"), was copied by local atheists in America and put on buses in Washington, DC,[^209] and [Bloomington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bloomington,_Indiana "Bloomington, Indiana"), Indiana.

The differing attitudes towards religion between the US and the UK cause a large schism between the two nations, and much of the general attitude of the society as a whole on fundamental social issues, including abortion, minority rights, blasphemy, the role of church and the state in society, etc. Both the United States and the United Kingdom share several followers of other minority faiths, although the numbers and type of faith practice in both countries differ wildly due to the ethnic and cultural makeup of both countries. The other minority faiths that are practiced in both countries include [Judaism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Judaism "Judaism"), [Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam"), [Hinduism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism "Hinduism"), [Sikhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sikhism "Sikhism"), [Paganism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paganism "Paganism") and [Buddhism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhism "Buddhism").

## Food and drink

Many classic dishes or foods from [American cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_cuisine "American cuisine") such as [hamburgers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamburger "Hamburger"), [hot dogs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hot_dog "Hot dog"), [barbecue chicken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barbecue_chicken "Barbecue chicken"), [New York Pizza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York%E2%80%93style_pizza "New York–style pizza"), [deep-pan pizza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago-style_pizza "Chicago-style pizza"), [chewing gum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chewing_gum "Chewing gum"), [tomato soup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tomato_soup "Tomato soup"), [chilli-con-carne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chili_con_carne "Chili con carne"), [chocolate chip cookies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chocolate_chip_cookie "Chocolate chip cookie"), [chocolate brownies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chocolate_brownie "Chocolate brownie"), [soft-scoop ice cream](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soft_serve "Soft serve") and [donuts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doughnut "Doughnut") are popular in the United Kingdom. Drinks like [cola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cola "Cola"), [milkshakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milkshake "Milkshake") and [bourbon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bourbon_whiskey "Bourbon whiskey") are also popular. Several major American food trends and fads have also been popular and influential in the British palate, for example, [weight management diets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Weight_management "Weight management") and [craft beer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Microbrewery "Microbrewery").

Some American foods, like [cornflakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corn_flakes "Corn flakes"), [baked beans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baked_beans "Baked beans"), and [crisps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potato_chip "Potato chip") (known as potato chips in the United States), have become so entrenched in the UK's food culture that they have completely lost their American roots and are considered part of British cuisine. [Breakfast cereals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakfast_cereal "Breakfast cereal") like corn flakes, [bran flakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bran_flakes "Bran flakes") and [puffed rice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rice_Krispies "Rice Krispies") came from the US to the UK in the beginning of twentieth century, and virtually changed the perception of [breakfasts locally](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Breakfast#United_Kingdom_and_Ireland "Breakfast").[^210]

Some British foods have been just as popular in the US, such as [apple pie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_pie "Apple pie"), [macaroni and cheese](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macaroni_and_cheese "Macaroni and cheese"), and [sandwich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sandwich "Sandwich").[^211] British cuisine was a major influence on the [cuisine of the Southern United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cuisine_of_the_Southern_United_States "Cuisine of the Southern United States"), including [fried chicken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fried_chicken "Fried chicken").[^212] British foods like [fish and chips](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fish_and_chips "Fish and chips"), [shepherd's pie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shepherd%27s_pie "Shepherd's pie"), [Sunday Roast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunday_Roast "Sunday Roast"), [Beef](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beefsteak "Beefsteak") [Steak](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steak "Steak"), [Steak Diane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steak_Diane "Steak Diane"), [Afternoon Tea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afternoon_Tea "Afternoon Tea") and [gingerbread](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gingerbread "Gingerbread") are also entrenched in American food culture. Drinking culture in the US has been heavily influenced by Britain, especially the introduction of [whisky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whisky "Whisky") and certain styles of beer in the colonial period.[^213] [^214] By the late 20th Century, British cuisine was sometimes stereotyped as being unappealing in the United States, although British cuisine is commonly eaten there.[^215] This reputation has been attributed to the impact that WWII rationing had on British cuisine in the mid-20th Century.[^211]

Many major American food and [fast moving consumer goods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast-moving_consumer_goods "Fast-moving consumer goods") companies have British operations including [Molson Coors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Molson_Coors_Brewing_Company "Molson Coors Brewing Company"),[^216] [McCormick & Company](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McCormick_%26_Company "McCormick & Company"),[^217] [Kellogg's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kellogg%27s "Kellogg's"),[^218] [Campbell's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Campbell_Soup_Company "Campbell Soup Company"),[^219] [Kraft-Heinz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kraft_Heinz "Kraft Heinz"),[^220] [PepsiCo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/PepsiCo "PepsiCo"),[^221] [Coca-Cola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coca-Cola "Coca-Cola") [^222] & [Mondelez](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mondelez_International "Mondelez International") [^223] The major British food manufacturers that operate in the United States are [Unilever](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unilever "Unilever"),[^224] [Associated British Foods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Associated_British_Foods "Associated British Foods") [^225] and [Diageo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diageo "Diageo").[^226] The purchase of the British food company [Cadbury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cadbury "Cadbury") by the American company [Kraft Foods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kraft_Foods "Kraft Foods") in 2010, caused a storm on whether the company would change the recipe for its [signature chocolate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cadbury_Dairy_Milk "Cadbury Dairy Milk") [^227] and the conditions at Cadbury's food factories.[^228]

Additionally, there are several American restaurant and café chains like [McDonald's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonald%27s "McDonald's"), [Burger King](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burger_King "Burger King"), [KFC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/KFC "KFC"), [Domino's Pizza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domino%27s_Pizza "Domino's Pizza"), [Pizza Hut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pizza_Hut "Pizza Hut"), [Krispy Kreme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Krispy_Kreme "Krispy Kreme"),[^229] and [Starbucks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Starbucks "Starbucks") that have enterprises on the other side of the Atlantic. A small number of British chains like [Pret a Manger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pret_a_Manger "Pret a Manger"),[^230] [YO! Sushi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/YO!_Sushi "YO! Sushi") and [Itsu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Itsu "Itsu") [^231] have operations in the US, principally around New York City. The British catering company [Compass Group](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compass_Group "Compass Group") has several catering contracts in the States, including for the federal government and the US military.[^232] During the start of 2020, YouTube channel [Insider](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Insider_Inc. "Insider Inc.") asked their resident American and British journalists Joe Avella and Harry Kersh to compare various chain restaurant menus of both the US and UK, under the title of Food Wars.[^233]

Since the [2016 EU referendum](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_United_Kingdom_European_Union_membership_referendum "2016 United Kingdom European Union membership referendum"), there has been growing concern about whether a possible UK–US [free trade agreement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_trade_agreement "Free trade agreement") would lead to changes in food practices and laws in the UK.[^234] The concern is that American food standards laws are much looser than the UK's, such as rules governing cleanliness, the use of antibiotics and pesticides, [animal welfare conditions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Animal_husbandry "Animal husbandry") and the use of [genetically modified food](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genetically_modified_food "Genetically modified food"). Many of these concerns have been symbolised by the production process of American poultry, often known as " [chlorinated chicken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poultry_farming_in_the_United_States "Poultry farming in the United States") ".[^235] [^236]

## Culture and media

Both the US and UK are considered cultural [superpowers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Superpower "Superpower"); both countries have a large-scale influence around the world in film, music, literature, and television.[^237]

### Literature

Literature is transferred across the [Atlantic Ocean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlantic_Ocean "Atlantic Ocean"), as evidenced by the appeal of British authors such as [William Shakespeare](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/William_Shakespeare "William Shakespeare"), [Charles Dickens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charles_Dickens "Charles Dickens"), [J. R. R. Tolkien](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._R._R._Tolkien "J. R. R. Tolkien"), [Jackie Collins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jackie_Collins "Jackie Collins"), and [J. K. Rowling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/J._K._Rowling "J. K. [Henry James](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Henry_James "Henry James") and [T. S. Eliot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T._S._Eliot "T. S. Eliot") both moved to Britain and were well known in both countries. Eliot moved to England in 1914 and became a British subject in 1927. He was a dominant figure in literary criticism and greatly influenced the [Modern period](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Modernist_poetry_in_English "Modernist poetry in English") of [British literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_literature "British literature").[^238]

In the UK, many American novels including *[The Catcher in the Rye](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Catcher_in_the_Rye "The Catcher in the Rye")*, *[Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roll_of_Thunder,_Hear_My_Cry "Roll of Thunder, Hear My Cry")*,[^239] *[Of Mice and Men](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Of_Mice_and_Men "Of Mice and Men")*,[^240] and *[The Color Purple](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Color_Purple "The Color Purple")* [^241] are frequently used texts for British secondary-level education English and English Literature exams as set by the main examination boards.

### Radio

Compared to music and television broadcasting, radio broadcasting is limited to both sides of the pond. There are several reasons for this. The major one is that the majority of radio broadcasting in the United States is [commercial](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commercial_broadcasting "Commercial broadcasting") and funded by advertising and the small [network of public radio stations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_broadcasting "Public broadcasting") are supported by donations, compared to the United Kingdom where the national public broadcaster, the [BBC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC "BBC") is the major player which funded by the obligatory [television licence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Television_licence "Television licence"). This leads to a completely different structure of the number and type of radio stations and their broadcasting schedules.

Other factors include differing technical standards of radio broadcasting. This is influenced by their countries' broadcasting authorities, which shape over-the-air radio. In the UK, it is influenced by the authorities of [Ofcom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ofcom "Ofcom") and the [EBU](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Broadcasting_Union "European Broadcasting Union"), which are working towards [DAB](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Audio_Broadcasting "Digital Audio Broadcasting") and [DRM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Digital_Radio_Mondiale "Digital Radio Mondiale"). While in the United States, it is influenced by the [FCC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Federal_Communications_Commission "Federal Communications Commission"), which is working towards [HD Radio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/HD_Radio "HD Radio"). The British [international broadcasting](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_broadcasting "International broadcasting") station, the [BBC World Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_World_Service "BBC World Service") is syndicated on various major city public radio stations in the United States such as [WNYC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WNYC "WNYC"), and on [SiriusXM](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sirius_XM_Satellite_Radio "Sirius XM Satellite Radio") satellite radio,[^255] through the broadcaster [American Public Media](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Public_Media "American Public Media").[^256] The American international broadcaster, [Voice of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Voice_of_America "Voice of America") has no remit in be needed to be heard in the UK, so it doesn't broadcast there and none of its programmes is relayed on domestic stations. In a resource-saving exercise between the two international broadcasters, Voice of America shares its [transmission towers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Transmission_tower "Transmission tower") with the BBC World Service to help with [shortwave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shortwave_radio "Shortwave radio") transmissions in remote areas.

[Internet radio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Internet_radio "Internet radio") and [streaming services](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Streaming_media "Streaming media") are growing in popularity in both countries; however, listening to each other's feeds is hampered by the countries' [broadcasting rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadcasting_rights "Broadcasting rights"). This causes the internet radio feeds of American and British radio stations to be sometimes [blocked](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geo-blocking "Geo-blocking") or on restricted bandwidth. For example, [BBC Radio 2](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Radio_2 "BBC Radio 2") is on a 128 kbit/s [AAC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Advanced_Audio_Coding "Advanced Audio Coding") domestic stream, while internationally it's on a 48 kbit/s [AAC+](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High-Efficiency_Advanced_Audio_Coding "High-Efficiency Advanced Audio Coding") stream. However, both the American and the British international broadcasters, Voice of America and the BBC World Service, are fully accessible online in each other's countries. Streaming services that are popular in both countries include the American [TuneIn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TuneIn "TuneIn"), [Apple Music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Music "Apple Music"), and Swedish-owned [Spotify](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spotify "Spotify"). The other major services in the US, like [Pandora Radio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pandora_Radio "Pandora Radio") and [Radio.com](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio.com "Radio.com"), don't operate in the UK, and are inaccessible.

In the past, before the [Second World War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_II "World War II"), connections between the United States and the United Kingdom in the radio industry were virtually unheard of. Radio in the UK was not influenced by the US, due to the vast distance, and the only regular services that were heard were the BBC and the ["pirate" station](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pirate_radio "Pirate radio") [Radio Luxembourg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Radio_Luxembourg "Radio Luxembourg"). When the Americans joined the war as part of the [Allies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_II "Allies of World War II"), some soldiers were billeted in the UK, in the BBC provided programming for these people. So the [Forces Programme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Forces_Programme "BBC Forces Programme"), broadcast many popular American variety shows such as [*Charlie McCarthy*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edgar_Bergen "Edgar Bergen"), [*The Bob Hope Show*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Pepsodent_Show "The Pepsodent Show"), and *[The Jack Benny Program](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Jack_Benny_Program "The Jack Benny Program").* As the Forces Programme, and the subsequent [General Forces Programme](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_General_Forces_Programme "BBC General Forces Programme"), was easily available for civilians, they were also heard by domestic audiences. After the War in 1946 on the [Home Service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Home_Service "BBC Home Service"), the BBC started to broadcast the factual programme *[Letter from America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Letter_from_America "Letter from America")*, which was presented by [Alistair Cooke](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alistair_Cooke "Alistair Cooke"), bringing information about the States to British audiences until Cooke died in 2004. It was one of the BBC's longest-running radio programmes, broadcasting on the Home Service, and continuing [BBC Radio 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Radio_4 "BBC Radio 4"). It was also relayed on the BBC World Service. The programme itself was based on a similar programme by Alistair Cooke in the 1930s for American listeners about life in the UK on the [NBC Red Network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NBC_Radio_Network "NBC Radio Network"). After Letter from America, the BBC continued with a factual programme about the States in [*Americana*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americana_\(radio_series\) "Americana (radio series)") from 2009 to 2011, presented by the resident American correspondent.

As of 2019, the BBC co-produced with [Public Radio International](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Public_Radio_International "Public Radio International") and [WGBH Boston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/WGBH_\(FM\) "WGBH (FM)"), a weekly factual programme called *[The World](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_World_\(radio_program\) "The World (radio program)")*, which is broadcast on various American public radio stations. Parts of the show are put together for a shorter programme called *Boston Calling*, which is available on Radio 4 and the domestic feed of the World Service.[^257] There have been attempts in the past to bring British formats to American audiences, such as [the News Quiz USA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_News_Quiz "The News Quiz").[^258] From 2005 to 2011, a time-shifted version of [BBC Radio 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Radio_1 "BBC Radio 1") was available on Sirius satellite radio.[^259] While in the UK, *[A Prairie Home Companion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/A_Prairie_Home_Companion "A Prairie Home Companion")* (called Garrison Keillor's Radio Show) was available weekly from 2002 on [BBC7](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC_Radio_4_Extra "BBC Radio 4 Extra") to 2016, on BBC Radio 4 Extra.[^260] There have been several American personalities that have been on British airwaves, including music journalist [Paul Gambaccini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paul_Gambaccini "Paul Gambaccini"), disc jockey [Suzi Quattro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Suzi_Quatro "Suzi Quatro"), and comedians [Rich Hall](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rich_Hall "Rich Hall") and [Greg Proops](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greg_Proops "Greg Proops"). While New Zealand-born disc jockey [Zane Lowe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zane_Lowe "Zane Lowe"), who spent much of his career in the UK, was recruited to [Apple's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_Inc. "Apple Inc.") [Beats 1](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Beats_1 "Beats 1") station in the United States.

## Sports

Despite sports being a major cultural interest in both the United States and the United Kingdom, there is little overlap in their most popular sports. The most popular team sports in the UK are [football (soccer)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_football "Association football"), [rugby union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rugby_union "Rugby union"), [rugby league](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rugby_league "Rugby league") and [cricket](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket "Cricket"), while the most popular sports in the US are [\[American\] football](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_football "American football"), [baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baseball "Baseball"), [ice hockey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice_hockey "Ice hockey") and [basketball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basketball "Basketball"). The most popular sports in each country are considered minor sports in the other, with growing interest. Both nations are among the strongest in the world in all-time sporting success, with the United States being the most successful sports nation in the world.

### Association football

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a0/FIFA_World_Cup_2010_England_USA.jpg/250px-FIFA_World_Cup_2010_England_USA.jpg)

England and USA play each other at 2010 FIFA World Cup

The [United States men's team](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team "United States men's national soccer team") has played multiple [friendlies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Exhibition_game "Exhibition game") against their [Home Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Home_Nations "Home Nations") counterparts. Being members of separate [confederations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_football_federations "List of football federations") with the Home Nations being part of [UEFA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/UEFA "UEFA") and the US being part of [CONCACAF](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/CONCACAF "CONCACAF"), their only chance to play competitive games is during [FIFA tournaments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIFA#FIFA_competitions "FIFA"). The United States and [England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/England_national_football_team "England national football team") have played thrice at the [FIFA World Cup](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/FIFA_World_Cup "FIFA World Cup") — in [1950](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1950_FIFA_World_Cup "1950 FIFA World Cup"), [2010](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2010_FIFA_World_Cup "2010 FIFA World Cup") and [2022](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_FIFA_World_Cup "2022 FIFA World Cup") — and remain unbeaten. The United States' [1–0 victory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_v_England_\(1950_FIFA_World_Cup\) "United States v England (1950 FIFA World Cup)") over England in 1950 is considered to be one of the biggest [upsets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upset_\(competition\) "Upset (competition)") in World Cup history.[^264] [^265] [^266] The other two games have both ended in draws; 1–1 in 2010 [^267] and 0–0 in 2022.[^268] Additionally, the two have played in nine friendlies, with England winning eight and the United States one.[^269]

The United States and [Wales](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wales_national_football_team "Wales national football team") have played once at the World Cup — in [2022](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2022_FIFA_World_Cup "2022 FIFA World Cup") — in addition to two friendlies. The game ended in a 1–1 draw.[^270] The United States have yet to play competitive matches against [Scotland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scotland_national_football_team "Scotland national football team") and [Northern Ireland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Ireland_national_football_team "Northern Ireland national football team").[^271] [^272]

The [Premier League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Premier_League "Premier League") has seen many American players since its inception in 1992. Some of the prominent ones include [Tim Howard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tim_Howard "Tim Howard"), [Christian Pulisic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Christian_Pulisic "Christian Pulisic"), [Clint Dempsey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Clint_Dempsey "Clint Dempsey") and [Landon Donovan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Landon_Donovan "Landon Donovan").[^273] Similarly, the [Major League Soccer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Soccer "Major League Soccer") has seen English players, including [David Beckham](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Beckham "David Beckham"), [Frank Lampard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Lampard "Frank Lampard") and [Steven Gerrard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_Gerrard "Steven Gerrard").[^274]

### American football

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/NFL_International_Series_2010.jpg/250px-NFL_International_Series_2010.jpg)

The opening ceremony at Wembley before the Denver Broncos play the San Francisco 49ers in a 2010 National Football League game

[Gridiron football](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gridiron_football "Gridiron football"), which is known in the United Kingdom as American football, originated from two British sports, association football and rugby union football. It came about in the later part of the 19th century due to the development into a separate code, and led to becoming a separate sport from the other codes of football. Gridiron was in the past only known and played in UK by visiting American servicemen; firstly in 1910, by navy crews from *[USS Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Georgia_\(BB-15\) "USS Georgia (BB-15)"), [USS Idaho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_battleship_Lemnos "Greek battleship Lemnos")* and *[USS Vermont](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/USS_Vermont_\(BB-20\) "USS Vermont (BB-20)")*, and then in the Second World War by UK-based service personnel. (The other gridiron code, [Canadian football](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canadian_football "Canadian football"), is hardly known in the UK.)

After [Channel 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Channel_4 "Channel 4") started showing the highlights of the American [NFL](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Football_League "National Football League") in 1982, the sport became acknowledged by the British sporting world. Due to the Britons' unfamiliarity with American football, television guides and newspapers had printed articles explaining it.[^275] A year later, the first match between two British teams the London Ravens and the [Northwich Spartans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manchester_Spartans "Manchester Spartans") was played; the Ravens won. Later in the 1980s, the sport grew and rival teams started to play, which was helped by support from various American players, coaches, and sponsors like [Coca-Cola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coca-Cola "Coca-Cola") and [Budweiser](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Budweiser "Budweiser").[^276] In 1986, the [American Bowl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Bowl "American Bowl") was the first preseason NFL game to be played at the original [Wembley Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Wembley_Stadium_\(1923\) "Wembley Stadium (1923)"), between the [Chicago Bears](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago_Bears "Chicago Bears") and the [Dallas Cowboys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dallas_Cowboys "Dallas Cowboys").[^277] By the early 1990s, due in part to the recession, Channel 4 ceased regular broadcasts of the NFL, but the [Super Bowl](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Super_Bowl "Super Bowl") has continued to be broadcast regularly on British television. The NFL has been broadcast by other British networks, including [ITV](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/ITV_\(TV_network\) "ITV (TV network)"), [Channel 5](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Channel_5_\(UK\) "Channel 5 (UK)"), [ESPN UK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BT_Sport_ESPN "BT Sport ESPN"), [British Eurosport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eurosport_1 "Eurosport 1"), and [Sky Sports](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sky_Sports "Sky Sports").

In 2007, the NFL returned to Wembley with a regular season game between the [Miami Dolphins](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miami_Dolphins "Miami Dolphins") and the [New York Giants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Giants "New York Giants").[^278] [^279] Since then, the NFL has held [additional games](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/NFL_International_Series#London_Games "NFL International Series") at Wembley and at other British stadiums. The NFL is considering siting [a team in London permanently](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Potential_London_NFL_franchise "Potential London NFL franchise"),[^280] with the [Jacksonville Jaguars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jacksonville_Jaguars "Jacksonville Jaguars") being the most likely team to relocate to the city.[^281] Noted British NFL players active during the [2019 season](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_NFL_season "2019 NFL season") are [Carolina Panthers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carolina_Panthers "Carolina Panthers") [defensive end](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Defensive_end "Defensive end"), Nigerian-born [Efe Obada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Efe_Obada "Efe Obada") and [Atlanta Falcons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Atlanta_Falcons "Atlanta Falcons") [tight end](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tight_end "Tight end"), [Alex Grey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alex_Gray_\(rugby_union\) "Alex Gray (rugby union)").

### Baseball

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5b/190630-F-QP712-0092_U.S._Airmen_present_colors_at_MLB_London_Series.jpg/250px-190630-F-QP712-0092_U.S._Airmen_present_colors_at_MLB_London_Series.jpg)

A pre-game photo of second game of the 2019 season of 2019 MLB London Series between the New York Yankees and the Boston Red Sox

The first recorded writings about [baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baseball "Baseball") came in the mid-18th century when a version of the sport played indoors in 1748 in London, where it was played by then Prince of Wales, [George III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_III_of_the_United_Kingdom "George III of the United Kingdom"),[^282] and played outside in 1755 in the southern English town of [Guildford](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guildford "Guildford").[^283] It was later brought over to the United States by British immigrants, where it developed in the modern version of the sport in the early 19th century in the creation and fountain of the modern baseball rule book, the [Knickerbocker Rules](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Knickerbocker_Rules "Knickerbocker Rules") in 1845. Eventually, it suppressed the popularity of the other notable [ball-and-bat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bat-and-ball_games "Bat-and-ball games") sport, which was played in the US at the time, which was [cricket](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cricket_in_the_United_States "Cricket in the United States"), by the end of the 19th century.

Sheffield-born [Harry Wright](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harry_Wright "Harry Wright") was instrumental in the development of professional baseball in the United States, and he brought his touring team to Britain to promote the sport. Later, at the end of the 19th century [Francis Ley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francis_Ley "Francis Ley"), a [Derby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Derby "Derby") man claimed erroneously to have had 'discovered' the game on a trip to the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States"), and [Albert Goodwill Spalding](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Albert_Goodwill_Spalding "Albert Goodwill Spalding"), an American former star player and sporting goods businessman who saw opportunities to expand his business across the Atlantic, funded a second tour to the United Kingdom (Spalding had earlier toured under Wright's leadership). This continued with the establishment of the [1890 National League of Baseball of Great Britain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1890_National_League_of_Baseball_of_Great_Britain "1890 National League of Baseball of Great Britain"), the first professional league in Britain. Baseball clubs were formed from well-known [association football](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_football "Association football") clubs [Aston Villa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aston_Villa_F.C. "Aston Villa F.C."), [Stoke City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stoke_City_F.C. "Stoke City F.C."), and [Preston North End](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Preston_North_End_F.C. "Preston North End F.C."), who were joined by Ley's own [Derby Baseball Club](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Derby_Baseball_Club "Derby Baseball Club"). During [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I"), visiting U.S. service personnel from the [U.S. Army](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Army "United States Army") and [Navy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Navy "United States Navy") played a [demonstration game](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Demonstration_sport "Demonstration sport") at [Chelsea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chelsea_F.C. "Chelsea F.C.") 's [Stamford Bridge](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stamford_Bridge_\(stadium\) "Stamford Bridge (stadium)") in 1918. A crowd of 38,000 people, including King [George V](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/George_V "George V"), attended. This led to a growing interest in the game across the Atlantic, and baseball teams were created during the inter-war period. This led to a peak in 1938 when there was a victory by [Great Britain](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Britain_national_baseball_team "Great Britain national baseball team") over the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_national_baseball_team "United States national baseball team") in the [1938 Amateur World Series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1938_Amateur_World_Series "1938 Amateur World Series") which was held in England, which is considered the first [World Cup of Baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baseball_World_Cup "Baseball World Cup").

The popularity of baseball in the United Kingdom diminished during and after the Second World War; today, baseball is not widely played among Britons. Notwithstanding, [Major League Baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Baseball "Major League Baseball") coverage is available to watch in the United Kingdom on the [TNT Sports 4](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/TNT_Sports_4 "TNT Sports 4") channel, formerly [BT Sport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BT_Sport "BT Sport").[^284] In 2018, [Major League Baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Baseball "Major League Baseball") announced a two-year deal to start the [MLB London Series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/MLB_London_Series "MLB London Series"), a series of regular-season games at [London Stadium](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/London_Stadium "London Stadium").[^285] The [2019 series](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2019_MLB_London_Series "2019 MLB London Series") was contested between two [rival teams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yankees%E2%80%93Red_Sox_rivalry "Yankees–Red Sox rivalry"), the [New York Yankees](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_Yankees "New York Yankees") and the [Boston Red Sox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Boston_Red_Sox "Boston Red Sox"). These games were broadcast on both BT Sport and the [BBC](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/BBC "BBC").[^286] The 2020 series between the [St. Louis Cardinals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/St._Louis_Cardinals "St. Louis Cardinals") and the [Chicago Cubs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chicago_Cubs "Chicago Cubs") were cancelled due to the [COVID-19 pandemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic_on_baseball "Impact of the COVID-19 pandemic on baseball").[^287] The [Cardinals–Cubs rivalry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cardinals%E2%80%93Cubs_rivalry "Cardinals–Cubs rivalry") series was played in London in 2023, as part of an extended agreement to resume the London Series.[^288]

[John Spinks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Spinks_\(musician\) "John Spinks (musician)"), leader of the English rock band [The Outfield](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Outfield "The Outfield"), originally named the band "The Baseball Boys", in a reference to a gang in the film *[The Warriors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Warriors_\(film\) "The Warriors (film)")*. The band members said in 1986 that none of them were knowledgeable about baseball, but they were curious about the sport.[^289]

### Cricket

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/43/Major_League_Cricket_logo.svg/120px-Major_League_Cricket_logo.svg.png)

The logo for the American Major League Cricket

Cricket was one of the major sports in the United States during its time as a British colony and for about a century afterward. Its major decline began with the 1860s Civil War, as it could not compete with the far shorter playing duration of baseball, among other factors. In the 21st century, [immigration from cricket-playing countries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_diaspora "Commonwealth diaspora") and the spread of the shortened [T20 format](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/T20_format "T20 format") have contributed to a minor revival of the game.[^290]
