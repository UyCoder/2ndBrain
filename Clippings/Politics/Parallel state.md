The **"parallel state"** is a term coined by American historian [Robert Paxton](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Robert_Paxton "Robert Paxton") [^1] to describe a collection of organizations or institutions that are state-like in their organization, management and structure, but are not officially part of the legitimate state or government.[^2] They serve primarily to promote the prevailing political and social ideology of the state.[^3]

The parallel state differs from the more commonly used " [state within a state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_within_a_state "State within a state") " in that they are usually endorsed by the prevailing political elite of a country, while the "state within a state" is a [pejorative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pejorative "Pejorative") term to describe state-like institutions that operate without the consent of and even to the detriment to the authority of an established state (such as churches and religious institutions or [secret societies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secret_society "Secret society") with their own laws and court systems).

Parallel states are common in [totalitarian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Totalitarian "Totalitarian") societies, such as [Nazi Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nazi_Germany "Nazi Germany"), [Fascist Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy_\(1861%E2%80%931946\) "Kingdom of Italy (1861–1946)"), and the [Soviet Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soviet_Union "Soviet Union"). Organizations usually associated with the idea of a Parallel state include [political parties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_party "Political party"), [unions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Labor_union "Labor union"), [intelligence agencies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Intelligence_agency "Intelligence agency"), and [militaries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military "Military").

## 'Parallel States' academic studies

"Parallel States" is also a study into the possibility of uniting one country while giving them two states parallel to each other in power and representation; both those states would however be compliant to one central-authority. This study was also suggested as a corner-stone for possible peace scenarios in war torn countries. One such example is the "Parallel States Project", hosted at [Lund University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lund_University "Lund University"), which seeks to explore the potential for a "Parallel States" approach to proposing a solution to the Israeli-Palestinian issue.[^4] [^5]

## Examples

### Turkey

[Turkish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkey "Turkey") President [Recep Tayyip Erdoğan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recep_Tayyip_Erdo%C4%9Fan "Recep Tayyip Erdoğan") has used the term "parallel state" (or "parallel structure") to describe followers of [Fethullah Gülen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fethullah_G%C3%BClen "Fethullah Gülen") who occupy senior bureaucratic and judicial positions, which have been accused of attempting to bring down Erdoğan's government. This is in contrast to the meaning of the term initially coined by Paxton, and instead resembles the term "state within a state." Gülen's [Cemaat movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/G%C3%BClen_movement "Gülen movement"), which has a large presence within Turkey, has allegedly been involved in limiting the power of the [Turkish Armed Forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_Armed_Forces "Turkish Armed Forces") through the [Ergenekon trials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ergenekon_trials "Ergenekon trials") and the [Sledgehammer case](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sledgehammer_\(coup_plan\) "Sledgehammer (coup plan)") while allied with Erdoğan. Following the [2013–14 protests against Erdoğan's government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013%E2%80%9314_protests_in_Turkey "2013–14 protests in Turkey"), the Cemaat Movement turned against Erdoğan, who thereupon labelled them as a "parallel state." [^6] Erdoğan has blamed Gülen's followers on orchestrating the [2013 government corruption scandal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2013_corruption_scandal_in_Turkey "2013 corruption scandal in Turkey"), as well as the [2016 coup attempt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/2016_Turkish_coup_d%27%C3%A9tat_attempt "2016 Turkish coup d'état attempt").

### Manipur

The Meitei ethno-nationalist armed militia [Arambai Tenggol](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arambai_Tenggol "Arambai Tenggol") have been "virtually ran a parallel government" in [Manipur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Manipur "Manipur"), India.[^7]
