The [culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture "Culture") of the [United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States "United States") encompasses various [social behaviors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_behavior "Social behavior"), institutions, and [norms](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_norm "Social norm"), including forms of [speech](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Languages_of_the_United_States "Languages of the United States"), [literature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_literature "American literature"), [music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_of_the_United_States "Music of the United States"), [visual arts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Visual_art_of_the_United_States "Visual art of the United States"), [performing arts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theater_in_the_United_States "Theater in the United States"), [cinema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinema_of_the_United_States "Cinema of the United States"), [food](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_cuisine "American cuisine"), [sports](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sports_in_the_United_States "Sports in the United States"), [religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion_in_the_United_States "Religion in the United States"), [law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law_of_the_United_States "Law of the United States"), [technology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Science_and_technology_in_the_United_States "Science and technology in the United States"), as well as other customs, beliefs, and forms of knowledge. American culture has been shaped by the [history of the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_the_United_States "History of the United States"), [its geography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_of_the_United_States "Geography of the United States"), and various internal and external forces and [immigration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_immigration_to_the_United_States "History of immigration to the United States").[^1]

America's foundations were initially [Western](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_culture "Western culture") -based, and primarily [English-derived](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_England "Culture of England"), but also with prominent [French](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_France "Culture of France"), [German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Germany "Culture of Germany"), [Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Greece "Culture of Greece"), [Irish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Ireland "Culture of Ireland"), [Italian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Italy "Culture of Italy"), [Scottish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scottish_culture "Scottish culture"), [Welsh](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Welsh_culture "Welsh culture"), [Jewish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jewish_culture "Jewish culture"), [Polish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Poland "Culture of Poland"), [Scandinavian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scandinavian_culture "Scandinavian culture"), and [Spanish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Spain "Culture of Spain") regional influences.[^2] However, non-Western influences, including [African](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Africa "Culture of Africa") and [Indigenous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_American_cultures_in_the_United_States "Native American cultures in the United States") cultures, and more recently, [Asian cultures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Asia "Culture of Asia") and [Hispanic culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Latin_America "Culture of Latin America") [^3], have firmly established themselves in the fabric of American culture as well. Since the United States was [established](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Revolution "American Revolution") in 1776, its culture has been influenced by successive waves of [immigrants](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_immigration_to_the_United_States "History of immigration to the United States"), and the resulting " [melting pot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melting_pot "Melting pot") " of cultures has been a distinguishing feature of [its society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Society_of_the_United_States "Society of the United States"). [Americans pioneered](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_of_the_United_States "Music of the United States") or made great strides in musical [genres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Genre "Genre") such as [heavy metal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heavy_metal_music "Heavy metal music"), [rhythm and blues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues "Rhythm and blues"), [jazz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jazz "Jazz"), [gospel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gospel_music "Gospel music"), [country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Country_music "Country music"), [hip hop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip_hop_music "Hip hop music"), and [rock 'n' roll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_and_roll "Rock and roll"). The " [big four sports](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_professional_sports_leagues_in_the_United_States_and_Canada "Major professional sports leagues in the United States and Canada") " are [American football](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_football "American football"), [baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baseball "Baseball"), [basketball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basketball "Basketball"), and [ice hockey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice_hockey "Ice hockey"). In [terms of religion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion_in_the_United_States "Religion in the United States"), the majority of Americans are [Protestant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestantism "Protestantism") or [Catholic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church "Catholic Church"), with a growing [irreligious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irreligion_in_the_United_States "Irreligion in the United States") population. [American cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_cuisine "American cuisine") includes popular tastes such as [hot dogs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hot_dog "Hot dog"), [milkshakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Milkshake "Milkshake"), and [barbecue](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barbecue_in_the_United_States "Barbecue in the United States"), as well [as many other class and regional preferences](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_cuisine#Influential_figures "American cuisine"). The most commonly used language is English; while no law designates it official, a [2025 executive order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_Order_14224 "Executive Order 14224") declares English the official language.[^4] Distinct [cultural regions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regions_of_the_United_States "Regions of the United States") include [New England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England "New England"), [Mid-Atlantic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mid-Atlantic_States "Mid-Atlantic States"), the [South](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_United_States "Southern United States"), [Midwest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midwestern_United_States "Midwestern United States"), [Southwest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southwest_United_States "Southwest United States"), [Mountain West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mountain_states "Mountain states"), and [Pacific Northwest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pacific_Northwest "Pacific Northwest").[^5]

Politically, the country takes its values from the [American Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Revolution "American Revolution") and [American Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Enlightenment "American Enlightenment"), with an emphasis on [liberty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Liberty "Liberty"), [individualism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individualism "Individualism"), and [limited government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Limited_government "Limited government"), as well as the [Bill of Rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_Bill_of_Rights "United States Bill of Rights") and [Reconstruction Amendments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reconstruction_Amendments "Reconstruction Amendments"). Under the [First Amendment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution "First Amendment to the United States Constitution"), the United States has the [strongest protections of free speech of any country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_of_speech_in_the_United_States "Freedom of speech in the United States").[^6] [^7] [^8] [^9] American popular opinion is also the most supportive of [free expression](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom_of_speech "Freedom of speech") and the [right to use the Internet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Right_to_Internet_access "Right to Internet access").[^10] [^11] The large majority of the United States has a legal system that is based upon [English common law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_common_law "English common law").[^12] According to the [Inglehart–Welzel cultural map](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Inglehart%E2%80%93Welzel_cultural_map_of_the_world "Inglehart–Welzel cultural map of the world"), it leans greatly towards " [self-expression values](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-expression_values "Self-expression values") ", while also uniquely blending aspects of "secular-rational" (with a strong emphasis on [human rights](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_rights "Human rights"), [the individual](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individualism "Individualism"), and [anti-authoritarianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-authoritarianism "Anti-authoritarianism")) and "traditional" (with high [fertility rates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baby_boom "Baby boom"), [religiosity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion_in_the_United_States "Religion in the United States"), and [patriotism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patriotism "Patriotism")) values together.[^13] [^14] [^15] Its culture can vary by factors such as [region](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_regions_of_the_United_States "List of regions of the United States"), [race and ethnicity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States "Race and ethnicity in the United States"), age, religion, [socio-economic status](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Socioeconomic_status "Socioeconomic status"), or [population density](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_states_and_territories_of_the_United_States_by_population_density "List of states and territories of the United States by population density"), among others. Different aspects of American culture can be thought of as [low culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Low_culture "Low culture") or [high culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/High_culture "High culture"), or belonging to any of a variety of [subcultures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Subculture "Subculture"). The United States exerts major cultural influence [on a global scale](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americanization "Americanization") and is considered a [cultural superpower](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultural_superpower "Cultural superpower").[^16] [^17]

## History

### Origins, development, and spread

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/4/45/Albert_Bierstadt_-_The_Rocky_Mountains%2C_Lander%27s_Peak.jpg/330px-Albert_Bierstadt_-_The_Rocky_Mountains%2C_Lander%27s_Peak.jpg)

The Rocky Mountains, Lander's Peak (1863) by Albert Bierstadt, at the Metropolitan Museum of Art in Manhattan

The European roots of the United States originate with the [English](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/English_Americans "English Americans") and [Spanish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_Americans "Spanish Americans") settlers of [colonial North America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colonial_history_of_the_United_States "Colonial history of the United States") during [British](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thirteen_Colonies "Thirteen Colonies") and [Spanish rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Spain "New Spain"). The varieties of English people, as opposed to the other peoples on the British Isles, were the overwhelming majority ethnic group in the 17th century (the population of the colonies in 1700 was 250,000) and were 47.9% of percent of the total population of 3.9 million. These early settlers mostly came from what is referred to as [Southern England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_England "Southern England").[^18] They constituted 60% of the whites at the first census in 1790 (%: 3.5 Welsh, 8.5 [Scotch Irish](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Scotch-Irish_Americans "Scotch-Irish Americans"), 4.3 Scots, 4.7 Irish, 7.2 German, 2.7 Dutch, 1.7 French, and 2 Swedish).[^19] The English ethnic group contributed to the major cultural and social mindset and attitudes that evolved into the American character. Of the total population in each colony, they numbered from 30% in Pennsylvania to 85% in Massachusetts.[^20] Large non-English immigrant populations from the 1720s to 1775, such as the Germans (100,000 or more), Scotch Irish (250,000), added enriched and modified the English cultural substrate.[^21]

[Jeffersonian democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jeffersonian_democracy "Jeffersonian democracy") was a foundational American cultural innovation, which is still a core part of the country's identity.[^22] Thomas Jefferson's *[Notes on the State of Virginia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Notes_on_the_State_of_Virginia "Notes on the State of Virginia")* was perhaps the first influential domestic cultural critique by an American and was written in reaction to the views of some influential Europeans that America's native flora and fauna (including humans) were [degenerate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_degeneration "Social degeneration").[^22]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b6/Betsy_Ross_sewing.jpg/250px-Betsy_Ross_sewing.jpg)

Betsy Ross was an American upholsterer who was credited by her relatives in 1870 with making the first American flag.

Non-indigenous cultural influences have been brought by historical immigration, especially from [Germany](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_Germany "Culture of Germany") in much of the country,[^23] Ireland and Italy in the [Northeast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northeastern_United_States "Northeastern United States"), and Japan in [Hawaii](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaii "Hawaii"). [Latin American culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_American_culture "Latin American culture") is especially pronounced in former Spanish areas but has also been introduced by immigration, as have [Asian American cultures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Asian_American_culture "Asian American culture") (especially in the Northeast and West Coast regions). [Caribbean](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caribbean "Caribbean") culture has been increasingly introduced by immigration and is pronounced in many urban areas. Since the abolition of slavery, the Caribbean has been the source of the earliest and largest Black immigrant group, a significant source of growth of the Black population in the U.S. and has made major cultural impacts in education, music, sports and entertainment.[^24]

Indigenous cultures remains strong in both reservation and urban communities, including traditional government and communal organization of property now legally managed by [Indian reservations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_reservations "Indian reservations") (large reservations are mostly in the West, especially [Oklahoma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oklahoma "Oklahoma"), [Arizona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Arizona "Arizona") and [South Dakota](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/South_Dakota "South Dakota")). The fate of indigenous cultures after contact with Europeans is quite varied. For example, [Taíno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno "Taíno") culture in U.S. Caribbean territories is undergoing [cultural revitalization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cultural_revitalization "Cultural revitalization") and, like many Native American languages, the [Taíno language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ta%C3%ADno_language "Taíno language") is no longer spoken. By contrast, the [Hawaiian language](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaiian_language "Hawaiian language") and [culture of the Native Hawaiians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_the_Native_Hawaiians "Culture of the Native Hawaiians") has survived in Hawaii alongside that of immigrants from the mainland U.S. (starting before the [1898 annexation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Newlands_Resolution "Newlands Resolution")) and to some degree Asian immigrants. Indigenous Hawaiian influences on mainstream American culture include [surfing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Surfing "Surfing") and [Hawaiian shirts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hawaiian_shirt "Hawaiian shirt"). Most languages native to what is now U.S. territory are endangered.[^25]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/39/Welcome_to_the_land_of_freedom_51996u.jpg/330px-Welcome_to_the_land_of_freedom_51996u.jpg)

European immigrants arriving in New York City by ship in the late 19th century and early 20th century

American culture includes both conservative and liberal elements, scientific and religious competitiveness, political structures, risk taking and free expression, materialist and moral elements. Despite certain consistent ideological principles (e.g. [individualism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Individualism "Individualism"), [egalitarianism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egalitarianism "Egalitarianism"), and faith in [freedom](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Freedom "Freedom") and [republicanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republicanism "Republicanism")), American culture has a variety of expressions due to its geographical scale and demographics.[^26]

As a [melting pot](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melting_pot "Melting pot") of cultures and [ethnicities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Race_and_ethnicity_in_the_United_States "Race and ethnicity in the United States"), the U.S. has been shaped by the world's [largest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_sovereign_states_and_dependent_territories_by_immigrant_population "List of sovereign states and dependent territories by immigrant population") [immigrant population](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_immigration_to_the_United_States "History of immigration to the United States"). The country is home to a [wide variety](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multiculturalism "Multiculturalism") of ethnic groups, traditions, and values,[^27] [^28] and exerts major cultural influence on a global scale, with the phenomenon being termed *[Americanization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americanization "Americanization")*.[^29] [^30] [^16] [^17]

## Regional variations

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f1/Census_Regions_and_Division_of_the_United_States.svg/250px-Census_Regions_and_Division_of_the_United_States.svg.png)

Regions of the United States as defined by the United States Census Bureau

According to cultural geographer [Colin Woodard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colin_Woodard "Colin Woodard") there are as many as [eleven cultural areas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Nations "American Nations") of the United States, which spring from their settlement history. In the east, from north to south: there are Puritan areas ("Yankeedom") of New England which spread across the northern Great Lakes to the northern reaches of the [Mississippi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippi_River "Mississippi River") and [Missouri Rivers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Missouri_River "Missouri River"); the [New Netherlands](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Netherlands "New Netherlands") area in the densely populated [New York metropolitan area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_metropolitan_area "New York metropolitan area"); the Midland area which spread from [Pennsylvania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pennsylvania "Pennsylvania") to the lower [Great Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Lakes "Great Lakes") and the trans-Mississippi upper midwest; Greater [Appalachia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Appalachia "Appalachia") which angles from West Virginia through the lower midwest and upper-south to trans-Mississippi Arkansas, and southern Oklahoma; the Deep South from the Carolinas to Florida and west to Texas. In the west, there is the southwestern "El Norte" areas originally colonized by Spain, the "Left Coast" colonized quickly on the 19th century by a mix of Yankees and upper Appalachians, and the large but sparsely populated interior West.[^31] [^32]

The [South](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Culture_of_the_Southern_United_States "Culture of the Southern United States") is sometimes informally called the " [Bible Belt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bible_Belt "Bible Belt") " due to [socially conservative](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_conservatism "Social conservatism") [evangelical](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Evangelicalism "Evangelicalism") [Protestantism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestantism "Protestantism"), which is a significant part of the region's culture. Christian [church attendance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Church_attendance "Church attendance") across all denominations is generally higher there than the national average. This region is usually contrasted with the [mainline Protestantism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainline_Protestant "Mainline Protestant") and [Catholicism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Catholic_Church "Catholic Church") of the [Northeast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northeastern_United_States "Northeastern United States"), the religiously diverse [Midwest](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Midwestern_United_States "Midwestern United States") and [Great Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Lakes_region "Great Lakes region"), the [Mormon Corridor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mormon_Corridor "Mormon Corridor") in [Utah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utah "Utah") and southern [Idaho](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idaho "Idaho"), and the [relatively secular](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unchurched_Belt "Unchurched Belt") [West](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_United_States "Western United States"). The percentage of [non-religious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Irreligion "Irreligion") people is the highest in the [northeastern](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northeastern_United_States "Northeastern United States") and New England state of [Vermont](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vermont "Vermont") at 34%, compared to 6% in the Bible Belt state of [Alabama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alabama "Alabama").[^33]

Strong cultural differences have a long history in the U.S., with the southern slave society in the antebellum period serving as a prime example. Social and economic tensions between the Northern and Southern states were so severe that they eventually caused the South to declare itself an independent nation, the [Confederate States of America](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confederate_States_of_America "Confederate States of America"); thus initiating the [American Civil War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Civil_War "American Civil War").[^34]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/US_flag_reflexion_on_Vietnam_Veterans_Memorial_12_2011_000124.JPG/330px-US_flag_reflexion_on_Vietnam_Veterans_Memorial_12_2011_000124.JPG)

More than 300 languages nationwide, and up to 800 languages in [New York City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_York_City "New York City"), besides English, have native speakers in the United States—some are spoken by [indigenous peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Native_Americans_in_the_United_States "Native Americans in the United States") (about 150 [living languages](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Living_language "Living language")) and others imported by immigrants. English is not the first language of most immigrants in the US, though many do arrive knowing how to speak it, especially from countries where English is broadly used.[^35] This not only includes immigrants from countries such as [Canada](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canadian_Americans "Canadian Americans"), [Jamaica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jamaican_Americans "Jamaican Americans"), and the [UK](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/British_Americans "British Americans"), where English is the primary language, but also countries where English is an official language, such as [India](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Indian_Americans "Indian Americans"), [Nigeria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nigerian_Americans "Nigerian Americans"), and the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Filipino_Americans "Filipino Americans").[^35]

According to the 2000 census, there were nearly 30 million native speakers of [Spanish in the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Spanish_language_in_the_United_States "Spanish language in the United States"). Spanish has official status in the [Commonwealth of Puerto Rico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Commonwealth_of_Puerto_Rico "Commonwealth of Puerto Rico"), where it is the primary language spoken, and the state of [New Mexico](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Mexico "New Mexico"); numerous Spanish enclaves exist around the country as well.[^36]

## Customs and traditions

### Cuisine

Iconic American dishes such as [apple pie](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apple_pie "Apple pie"), [donuts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Doughnut "Doughnut"), [Southern fried chicken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fried_chicken "Fried chicken"), [American pizza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pizza_in_the_United_States "Pizza in the United States"), [hamburgers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamburger "Hamburger"), and [hot dogs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hot_dog "Hot dog") derive from the recipes of various immigrants and domestic innovations.[^38] [^39] [French fries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/French_fries "French fries"), Mexican dishes such as [burritos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burrito "Burrito") and [tacos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taco "Taco"), and pasta dishes freely adapted from Italian sources are consumed.[^40]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/6e/The_First_Thanksgiving_cph.3g04961.jpg/250px-The_First_Thanksgiving_cph.3g04961.jpg)

The First Thanksgiving 1621, oil-on-canvas by Jean Leon Gerome Ferris (1899)

The types of food served at home vary greatly and depend upon the region of the country and the family's own cultural heritage. Recent immigrants tend to eat food similar to that of their country of origin, and [Americanized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Americanization "Americanization") versions of these cultural foods, such as [Chinese American cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Chinese_cuisine "American Chinese cuisine") or [Italian American cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian-American_cuisine "Italian-American cuisine") often eventually appear. [Vietnamese cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vietnamese_cuisine "Vietnamese cuisine"), [Korean cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Korean_cuisine "Korean cuisine"), and [Thai cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thai_cuisine "Thai cuisine") in authentic forms are often readily available in large cities. [German cuisine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/German_cuisine "German cuisine") has a profound impact on American cuisine, especially Midwestern cuisine; potatoes, noodles, roasts, stews, cakes, and other pastries are the most iconic ingredients in both cuisines.[^41] Dishes such as the hamburger, pot roast, baked ham, and hot dogs are examples of American dishes derived from German cuisine.[^42] [^43]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/6/69/Motherhood_and_apple_pie.jpg/250px-Motherhood_and_apple_pie.jpg)

Apple pie is one of a number of American cultural icons.

Americans generally prefer coffee over tea, and more than half the adult population drinks at least one cup of coffee per day.[^44] Marketing by U.S. industries is largely responsible for making [orange juice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orange_juice "Orange juice") and [milk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dairy_industry_in_the_United_States "Dairy industry in the United States") (now often fat-reduced) ubiquitous breakfast beverages.[^45] During the 1980s and 1990s, the [caloric intake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caloric_intake "Caloric intake") of Americans rose by 24%;[^40] and frequent dining at fast food outlets is associated with what health officials call the [American](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Obesity_in_the_United_States "Obesity in the United States") " [obesity epidemic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Epidemiology_of_obesity "Epidemiology of obesity") ". Highly sweetened [soft drinks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soft_drink "Soft drink") are popular; sugared beverages account for 9% of the average American's daily caloric intake.[^46]

The American [fast food](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fast_food "Fast food") industry, the world's first and largest, is also often viewed as being a symbol of U.S. marketing dominance. Companies such as [McDonald's](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/McDonald%27s "McDonald's"),[^47] [Burger King](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Burger_King "Burger King"), [Pizza Hut](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pizza_Hut "Pizza Hut"), [Kentucky Fried Chicken](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kentucky_Fried_Chicken "Kentucky Fried Chicken"), and [Domino's Pizza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Domino%27s_Pizza "Domino's Pizza") among others, have numerous outlets around the world,[^48] and pioneered the [drive-through](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Drive-through "Drive-through") format in the 1940s.[^49]

### Sports

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/ba/2007_Texas_Longhorns_football_team_entry.jpg/250px-2007_Texas_Longhorns_football_team_entry.jpg)

The opening of College football season is a major part of American pastime. Massive marching bands, cheerleaders, and colorguard are common at American football games.

In the 1800s, colleges were encouraged to focus on intramural sports, particularly [track and field](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Track_and_field "Track and field"), and, in the late 1800s, American football. Physical education was incorporated into primary school curriculums in the 20th century.[^50]

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/71/Typical_baseball_game.JPG/250px-Typical_baseball_game.JPG)

A typical baseball diamond as seen from the stadium

[Baseball](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baseball "Baseball") is the oldest of the major American team sports. Professional baseball dates from 1869 and had no close rivals in popularity until the 1960s. Though baseball is no longer the most popular sport,[^51] it is still referred to as "the [national pastime](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_pastime "National pastime") ".

[Ice hockey](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ice_hockey "Ice hockey") is the fourth-leading professional team sport. Always a mainstay of [Great Lakes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Lakes "Great Lakes") and [New England](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_England "New England") -area culture, the sport gained tenuous footholds in regions like the [American South](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_South "American South") since the early 1990s, as the [National Hockey League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/National_Hockey_League "National Hockey League") pursued a policy of expansion.[^52]

[Soccer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soccer "Soccer") is very popular as a participation sport, particularly among youth, and the [US national teams](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_States_men%27s_national_soccer_team "United States men's national soccer team") are competitive internationally. A thirty-team professional league, [Major League Soccer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_League_Soccer "Major League Soccer"), plays from March to October, but its television audience and overall popularity lag behind other American professional sports.[^53]

Relative to other parts of the world, the United States is unusually competitive in women's sports, a fact usually attributed to the [Title IX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Title_IX "Title IX") anti-discrimination law, which requires most American colleges to give equal funding to men's and women's sports.[^54]

### Housing

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e5/831Richland.jpg/250px-831Richland.jpg)

The American Foursquare was a popular house style from the late 19th century until the 1930s.

American cities with housing prices near the national median have also been losing the [middle income](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Household_income_in_the_United_States "Household income in the United States") neighborhoods, those with median income between 80% and 120% of the metropolitan area's median household income. Here, the more affluent members of the middle-class, who are also often referred to as being professional or [upper-middle-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper-middle-class "Upper-middle-class"), have left in search of larger homes in more exclusive suburbs. This trend is largely attributed to the [middle-class squeeze](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_middle-class#Middle-class_squeeze "American middle-class"), which has caused a starker distinction between the [statistical middle class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_middle-class#statistical_middle_class "American middle-class") and the more privileged members of the [middle class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_middle_class "American middle class").[^73] In more expensive areas such as California, however, another trend has been taking place where an influx of more affluent middle-class households has displaced those in the actual middle of society and converted former American [middle-middle-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_middle-class#statistical_middle_class "American middle-class") neighborhoods into [upper-middle-class](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_upper_middle_class "American upper middle class") neighborhoods.[^74]

### Volunteerism

[Alexis de Tocqueville](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexis_de_Tocqueville "Alexis de Tocqueville") first noted, in 1835, the American attitude towards helping others in need. A 2011 [Charities Aid Foundation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Charities_Aid_Foundation "Charities Aid Foundation") study found that Americans were the first most willing to help a stranger and donate time and money in the world at 60%. Many low-level crimes are punished by assigning hours of " [community service](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Community_service "Community service") ", a requirement that the offender perform volunteer work.[^75]

### Drugs and alcohol

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ec/5_Prohibition_Disposal%289%29_%28cropped%29.jpg/250px-5_Prohibition_Disposal%289%29_%28cropped%29.jpg)

New York City Police officers pour liquor into sewer during Prohibition in 1921

American attitudes towards drugs and alcoholic beverages have evolved considerably throughout the country's history. In the 19th century, alcohol was readily available and consumed, and no laws restricted the use of other drugs. Attitudes on drug addiction started to change, resulting in the [Harrison Act](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harrison_Narcotics_Tax_Act "Harrison Narcotics Tax Act"), which eventually became proscriptive.[^76]

A movement to ban alcoholic beverages called the [Temperance movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Temperance_movement "Temperance movement"), emerged in the late 19th century. Several American Protestant religious groups and women's groups, such as the [Women's Christian Temperance Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Women%27s_Christian_Temperance_Union "Women's Christian Temperance Union"), supported the movement. In 1919, Prohibitionists succeeded in [amending the Constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eighteenth_Amendment_to_the_United_States_Constitution "Eighteenth Amendment to the United States Constitution") to prohibit the sale of alcohol. Although the Prohibition period did result in a 50% decrease in alcohol consumption,[^77] banning alcohol outright proved to be unworkable, as the previously legitimate distillery industry was replaced by criminal gangs that trafficked in alcohol.

A " [Just Say No](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Just_Say_No "Just Say No") to Drugs" movement replaced the more liberal ethos of the 1960s. This led to stricter drug laws and greater police latitude in drug cases. Drugs are, however, widely available, and 16% of Americans 12 and older used an illicit drug in 2012.[^78]

## Arts

### Architecture

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/0/08/EmpireStateNewYokCity.jpg/500px-EmpireStateNewYokCity.jpg)

The current One World Trade Center in Lower Manhattan (background, seen under construction) surpassed the height of the Empire State Building Midtown Manhattan (foreground) on April 30, 2012.

Architecture in the United States is regionally diverse and has been shaped by many external forces. [U.S. architecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Architecture_of_the_United_States "Architecture of the United States") can therefore be said to be eclectic.[^79] Traditionally American architecture has influences from [English architecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Architecture_of_England "Architecture of England") [^80] to [Greco Roman architecture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Greek_and_Roman_architectural_records "List of Greek and Roman architectural records").[^81]

### Theater and performing arts

Theater of the United States is based in the Western tradition. The United States originated [stand-up comedy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stand-up_comedy "Stand-up comedy") and [modern improvisational theatre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Improvisational_theatre#Modern "Improvisational theatre"), which involves taking suggestions from the audience.

#### Minstrel show

The [minstrel show](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Minstrel_show "Minstrel show"), though now widely recognized as racist and offensive, is also recognized as the first uniquely American theatrical art form. Minstrel shows were developed in the 19th century and they were typically performed by white actors wearing [blackface](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blackface "Blackface") makeup for the purpose of imitating and caricaturing the speech and music of [African Americans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African_Americans "African Americans"). [Stephen Foster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stephen_Foster "Stephen Foster") was a famous composer for minstrel shows. Many of his songs such as " [Camptown Races](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Camptown_Races "Camptown Races") ", " [Oh Susanna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oh_Susanna "Oh Susanna") ", and " [My Old Kentucky Home](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/My_Old_Kentucky_Home "My Old Kentucky Home") " became popular American folk songs. [Tap dancing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tap_dancing "Tap dancing") and [stand-up comedy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Stand-up_comedy "Stand-up comedy") have origins in minstrel shows.[^82]

[Banjos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Banjo "Banjo"), originally hand-made by slaves for entertainment on [plantations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plantation_complexes_in_the_Southern_United_States "Plantation complexes in the Southern United States"), began to be mass-produced in the United States in the 1840s as a result of their extensive use on the minstrel stage.[^83]

#### Drama

[Social commentary](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_commentary "Social commentary") has also been a preoccupation of American theater, often addressing issues not discussed in the mainstream. Writers such as [Lorraine Hansbury](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lorraine_Hansbury "Lorraine Hansbury"), [August Wilson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/August_Wilson "August Wilson"), [David Mamet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/David_Mamet "David Mamet") and [Tony Kushner](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tony_Kushner "Tony Kushner") have all won [Pulitzer Prizes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pulitzer_Prizes "Pulitzer Prizes") for their polemical plays on American society.[^84]

### Music

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9e/Elvis_Presley_Publicity_Photo%2C_1955.jpg/120px-Elvis_Presley_Publicity_Photo%2C_1955.jpg) ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/d/d9/Michael_Jackson_1984_%28enhanced%29.jpg/120px-Michael_Jackson_1984_%28enhanced%29.jpg) ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/e4/Whitney_Houston_by_Richard_Avedon_%28cropped%29.jpg/120px-Whitney_Houston_by_Richard_Avedon_%28cropped%29.jpg) ![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/c/c9/Madonna_%C3%A0_Nice_26.jpg/120px-Madonna_%C3%A0_Nice_26.jpg)

American music artists such as [Elvis Presley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elvis_Presley "Elvis Presley"), [Michael Jackson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jackson "Michael Jackson"), [Whitney Houston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whitney_Houston "Whitney Houston"), and [Madonna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madonna "Madonna") have been considered global celebrities.

American music styles and influences (such as [country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Country_music "Country music"), [jazz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jazz "Jazz"), [blues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blues "Blues"), [rock](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_music "Rock music"), [pop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pop_music "Pop music"), [techno](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Techno "Techno"), [soul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Soul_music "Soul music"), and [hip hop](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hip_hop_music "Hip hop music")) and music based on them can be heard all over the world. Music in the U.S. is very diverse, and the country has the world's [largest music market](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Global_music_industry_market_share_data "Global music industry market share data") with a total retail value of $4.9 billion in 2014.[^85]

The rhythmic and lyrical styles of [African-American music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/African-American_music "African-American music") have significantly influenced American music at large, distinguishing it from European and African traditions. The [Smithsonian Institution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Smithsonian_Institution "Smithsonian Institution") states, "African-American influences are so fundamental to American music that there would be no American music without them." [^86] [Country music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Country_music "Country music") developed in the 1920s, and [rhythm and blues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rhythm_and_blues "Rhythm and blues") in the 1940s. Elements from folk idioms such as the [blues](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Blues "Blues") and what is known as [old-time music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old-time_music "Old-time music") were adopted and transformed into [popular genres](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular_music "Popular music") with global audiences. [Jazz](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jazz "Jazz") was developed by innovators such as [Louis Armstrong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Louis_Armstrong "Louis Armstrong") and [Duke Ellington](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duke_Ellington "Duke Ellington") early in the 20th century.[^87] Known for singing in a wide variety of genres, [Aretha Franklin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aretha_Franklin "Aretha Franklin") is considered one of the all-time greatest American singers.[^88]

[Chuck Berry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chuck_Berry "Chuck Berry"), [Elvis Presley](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Elvis_Presley "Elvis Presley"), and [Little Richard](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Little_Richard "Little Richard") were among the pioneers of [rock and roll](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rock_and_roll "Rock and roll") in the mid-1950s. Rock bands such as [Metallica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metallica "Metallica"), the [Eagles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eagles_\(band\) "Eagles (band)"), and [Aerosmith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aerosmith "Aerosmith") are among the [highest grossing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_best-selling_music_artists "List of best-selling music artists") in worldwide sales.[^89] [^90] [^91] In the 1960s, [Bob Dylan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bob_Dylan "Bob Dylan") emerged from the [folk revival](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_folk_music_revival "American folk music revival") to become one of America's most celebrated songwriters.[^92]

[American popular music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_popular_music "American popular music"), as part of the wider U.S. pop culture, has a worldwide influence and following.[^93] Mid-20th-century American [pop stars](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Popular_music "Popular music") such as [Bing Crosby](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bing_Crosby "Bing Crosby"), [Frank Sinatra](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Frank_Sinatra "Frank Sinatra"),[^94] and Elvis Presley became global celebrities,[^87] as have artists of the late 20th century such as [Michael Jackson](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Michael_Jackson "Michael Jackson"), [Prince](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince_\(musician\) "Prince (musician)"), [Madonna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madonna "Madonna"), and [Whitney Houston](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Whitney_Houston "Whitney Houston").[^95] [^96]

As of 2022, [Taylor Swift](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taylor_Swift "Taylor Swift"), [Miley Cyrus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Miley_Cyrus "Miley Cyrus"), [Ariana Grande](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ariana_Grande "Ariana Grande"), [Eminem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eminem "Eminem"), [Lady Gaga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lady_Gaga "Lady Gaga"), [Katy Perry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Katy_Perry "Katy Perry"), and many others contemporary artists dominate [global streaming rankings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_most-streamed_artists_on_Spotify "List of most-streamed artists on Spotify").[^97]

The annual [Coachella](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coachella_\(festival\) "Coachella (festival)") music festival in [California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/California "California") is one of the largest, most famous, and most profitable music festivals in the United States and the world.[^98] [^99]

### Cinema

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/5/5a/Hollywood_Sign_%28Zuschnitt%29.jpg/250px-Hollywood_Sign_%28Zuschnitt%29.jpg)

The Hollywood Sign

The United States movie industry has a worldwide influence and following. [Hollywood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hollywood,_Los_Angeles "Hollywood, Los Angeles"), a northern district of Los Angeles, California, is the leader in motion picture production and the most recognizable movie industry in the world.[^100] [^101] [^102] The [major film studios](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Major_film_studios "Major film studios") of the United States are the primary source of the [most commercially successful](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_highest_grossing_films "List of highest grossing films") and most ticket selling movies in the world.[^103] [^104]

The dominant style of American cinema is [classical Hollywood cinema](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Hollywood_cinema "Classical Hollywood cinema"), which developed from 1913 to 1969 and is still typical of most films made there to this day. While Frenchmen [Auguste and Louis Lumière](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auguste_and_Louis_Lumi%C3%A8re "Auguste and Louis Lumière") are generally credited with the birth of modern cinema,[^105] American cinema soon came to be a dominant force in the emerging industry. The world's first [sync-sound](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sound_film "Sound film") [musical film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Musical_film "Musical film"), *[The Jazz Singer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Jazz_Singer "The Jazz Singer")*, was released in 1927,[^106] and was at the forefront of sound-film development in the following decades. [Orson Welles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Orson_Welles "Orson Welles") 's *[Citizen Kane](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citizen_Kane "Citizen Kane")* (1941) is frequently cited in critics' polls as the [greatest film of all time](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_films_considered_the_best "List of films considered the best").[^107]

### Broadcasting

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b1/Comcastcenter_vertical.jpg/250px-Comcastcenter_vertical.jpg)

Comcast Center in Philadelphia, headquarters of Comcast, the world's largest telecommunications and media conglomerate

Television constitutes a significant part of the traditional [media of the United States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Media_of_the_United_States "Media of the United States"). Household ownership of television sets in the country is 96.7%,[^108] and the majority of households have more than one set. The peak ownership percentage of households with at least one television set occurred during the 1996–97 season, with 98.4% ownership.[^109] As a whole, the [television networks](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Television_network "Television network") of the United States is the largest and most [syndicated](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Broadcast_syndication "Broadcast syndication") in the world.[^110]

As of August 2013, approximately 114,200,000 American households own at least one television set.[^111]

In 2014, due to a recent surge in the number and popularity of critically acclaimed television series, many critics have said that American television is currently enjoying a golden age.[^112] [^113]

### Philosophy

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9d/Scene_at_the_Signing_of_the_Constitution_of_the_United_States.jpg/250px-Scene_at_the_Signing_of_the_Constitution_of_the_United_States.jpg)

Painting by Howard Chandler Christy of the scene at the Philadelphia Convention which led to the signing of the United States Constitution, an important document in American political and legal philosophy

Early American philosophy was heavily shaped by the European [Age of Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Age_of_Enlightenment "Age of Enlightenment"), which promoted ideals such as reason and individual liberty.[^114]

[Artificial intelligence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Artificial_intelligence "Artificial intelligence") and the [philosophy of mind](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philosophy_of_mind "Philosophy of mind") have been heavily influenced by American philosophers such as [Daniel Dennett](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Daniel_Dennett "Daniel Dennett"),[^115] [Noam Chomsky](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Noam_Chomsky "Noam Chomsky"),[^116] [Hilary Putnam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hilary_Putnam "Hilary Putnam"),[^117] [Jerry Fodor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jerry_Fodor "Jerry Fodor"), and [John Searle](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Searle "John Searle"), who contributed to [cognitivism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cognitivism_\(psychology\) "Cognitivism (psychology)"), the [hard problem of consciousness](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hard_problem_of_consciousness "Hard problem of consciousness"), and the [mind-body problem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mind-body_problem "Mind-body problem").

## Society

### Education

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/2/2e/Fest_of_Books_2009.jpg/250px-Fest_of_Books_2009.jpg)

Los Angeles Times Festival of Books is the largest book festival in the United States, annually drawing approximately 150,000 attendees.

In the year 2000, there were 76.6 million students enrolled in schools from [kindergarten](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kindergarten "Kindergarten") through [graduate schools](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Graduate_school "Graduate school"). Of these, 72 percent aged 12 to 17 were judged academically "on track" for their age (enrolled in school at or above grade level). Of those enrolled in compulsory education, 5.2 million (10.4 percent) were attending private schools. Among the country's adult population, over 85 percent have completed high school and 27 percent have received a [bachelor's degree](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bachelor%27s_degree "Bachelor's degree") or higher.[^118]

The large majority of the world's top universities, as listed by various ranking organizations, are in the United States, including 19 of the top 25, and the most prestigious [Harvard University](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Harvard_University "Harvard University").[^119] [^120] [^121] [^122] The country also has by far the most [Nobel Prize winners](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Nobel_laureates_by_country "List of Nobel laureates by country") in history, with 403 (having won 406 awards).[^123]
