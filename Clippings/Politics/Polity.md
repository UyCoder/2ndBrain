A **polity** is a group of [people](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/People "People") with a collective identity, who are organized by some form of [political](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics "Politics"), [institutionalized](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Institutionalisation "Institutionalisation"), [social relations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Social_relation "Social relation"), and have a capacity to mobilize resources.[^1] [^2] It is the unit or entity of a political community or [body politic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Body_politic "Body politic").[^3]

## Overview

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a3/Leviathan_frontispiece_cropped_British_Library.jpg/250px-Leviathan_frontispiece_cropped_British_Library.jpg)

Frontispiece of Leviathan, 1690

In [geopolitics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geopolitics "Geopolitics"), a polity can manifest in different forms such as a province, a nation, a [state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_\(polity\) "State (polity)"), an [empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire "Empire"), an [international organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_organization "International organization"), a [political organization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_organization "Political organization") or another identifiable, resource-manipulating organizational structure. A polity like a state does not need to be a [sovereign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereignty "Sovereignty") unit. The preeminent polities today are [Westphalian states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Westphalian_sovereignty "Westphalian sovereignty") and [nation-states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation-state "Nation-state"), commonly referred to as countries. The term *country* may refer to a variety of types of polity: usually to a [sovereign state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state "Sovereign state"), but also to a [state with limited recognition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_states_with_limited_recognition "List of states with limited recognition"), a [constituent country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Country_\(disambiguation\)#Administrative_divisions "Country (disambiguation)") of a sovereign state, or a [dependent territory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dependent_territory "Dependent territory").[^4] [^5] [^6]

A polity may encapsulate a multitude of organizations. Many of these form (or are involved in) the administrative apparatus of contemporary nation states: such as their subordinate [civil](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_authorities "Civil authorities"), regional, and [local government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Local_government "Local government") authorities.[^7] [^8]
