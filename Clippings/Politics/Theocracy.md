**Theocracy** or **ethiocracy** is a form of [autocracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autocracy "Autocracy") [^1] or [oligarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oligarchy "Oligarchy") in which one or more [deities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deity "Deity") are recognized as supreme ruling authorities giving divine guidance to human intermediaries with executive, legislative or judicial power who manage the government's daily affairs.[^2] [^3]

## Etymology

The word theocracy originates from the [Ancient Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greek_language "Ancient Greek language"): θεοκρατία (*theocratia*) meaning "the rule of God". This, in turn, derives from [θεός](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%B8%CE%B5%CF%8C%CF%82 "wikt:θεός") (theos), meaning "god", and [κρατέω](https://en.wiktionary.org/wiki/%CE%BA%CF%81%CE%B1%CF%84%CE%AD%CF%89 "wikt:κρατέω") (*krateo*), meaning "to rule". Thus the meaning of the word in Greek was "rule by god(s)" or human [incarnation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Incarnation "Incarnation") (s) of god(s).

The term was initially coined by [Flavius Josephus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flavius_Josephus "Flavius Josephus") in the first century AD to describe the characteristic government of the [Jews](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jew "Jew"). Josephus argued that while mankind had developed many forms of rule, most could be subsumed under the following three types: [monarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarchy "Monarchy"), [oligarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Oligarchy "Oligarchy"), and [democracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Democracy "Democracy"). However, according to Josephus, the government of the Jews was unique. Josephus offered the term *theocracy* to describe this polity in which a god was sovereign and the god's word was law.[^4]

Josephus' definition was widely accepted until the [Enlightenment](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Age_of_Enlightenment "The Age of Enlightenment") era, when the term took on negative [connotations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Connotation "Connotation") and was barely salvaged by [Hegel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hegel "Hegel") 's commentary.[^5] The first recorded English use was in 1622, with the meaning " [sacerdotal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sacerdotal "Sacerdotal") government under divine inspiration" (as in [ancient Israel and Judah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_ancient_Israel_and_Judah "History of ancient Israel and Judah")); the meaning "priestly or religious body wielding political and civil power" was first recorded in 1825.

## Definition

The term *theocracy* derives from the [Koine Greek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Koine_Greek "Koine Greek") θεοκρατία, "rule of God", a term used by [Josephus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Josephus "Josephus") for the [kingdoms of Israel and Judah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdoms_of_Israel_and_Judah "Kingdoms of Israel and Judah"),[^6] reflecting the view that "God himself is recognized as the head" of the state.[^7] The common, generic use of the term, as defined above in terms of rule by a church or analogous religious leadership, may be more accurately described as an ecclesiocracy.[^8]

In an *hierocracy*, a specific type of theocracy, the governing body is composed of a hierarchical structure of religious officials or clergy.

In an *ecclesiocracy*, the religious leaders assume a leading role in the state, but do not claim that they are instruments of divine revelation.

In a *church-state*, a state where religious and political power are intertwined, often with the church having significant influence over the government

A related phenomenon is a secular government co-existing with a state religion or delegating some aspects of civil law to religious communities. For example, in [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel "Israel") (which is sometimes referred to as a theocracy [^9] [^10] [^11] [^12] [^13] [^14] [^15]), [marriage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marriage_in_Israel "Marriage in Israel") is governed by officially recognized religious bodies who each provide marriage services for their respected adherents, yet no form of civil marriage (free of religion) exists, nor marriage by non-recognized minority religions.

According to Merriam-Webster's Dictionary, there are two meanings for the word *theocracy*: (1) government of a state by immediate divine guidance or by officials who are regarded as divinely guided; and (2) a state governed by a theocracy.

## Current theocracies

### Christian theocracies

#### Holy See

Following the [Capture of Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capture_of_Rome "Capture of Rome") on 20 September 1870, the [Papal States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papal_States "Papal States") including Rome with the [Vatican](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_See "Holy See") were annexed by the [Kingdom of Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy "Kingdom of Italy"). In 1929, through the [Lateran Treaty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateran_Treaty "Lateran Treaty") signed with the Italian government, the new state of [Vatican City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vatican_City "Vatican City") was formally created and recognised as an independent state from [Fascist Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fascist_Italy "Fascist Italy").[^16] The head of state of the Vatican is the [pope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope "Pope"), [elected](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Conclave "Conclave") by the [College of Cardinals](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/College_of_Cardinals "College of Cardinals"), an assembly of high-ranking clergy.[^16] The pope is elected for life, and either dies in office, or in extremely rare situations may [resign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papal_renunciation "Papal renunciation"). Cardinals themselves are appointed by popes, who thereby choose the electors of their successors.

Voting since 1970 has been limited to cardinals under 80 years of age.[^16] A [Secretary for Relations with States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Secretary_for_Relations_with_States "Secretary for Relations with States"), directly responsible for international relations, is appointed by the pope. The Vatican legal system is rooted in [canon law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canon_law_\(Catholic_Church\) "Canon law (Catholic Church)"). The Bishop of Rome, as [Supreme Pontiff](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_Pontiff "Supreme Pontiff"), "has the fullness of legislative, executive and judicial powers." [^17] Although the laws of Vatican City come from the secular laws of Italy, under article 3 of the Law of the Sources of the Law, a provision is made for the application of the "laws promulgated by the [Kingdom of Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Italy "Kingdom of Italy") ".[^18]

#### Mount Athos

Mount Athos is a mountain peninsula in [Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greece "Greece") which is an [Eastern Orthodox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eastern_Orthodox "Eastern Orthodox") [autonomous area](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomous_area "Autonomous area") consisting of 20 [monasteries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monasteries "Monasteries") under the direct jurisdiction of the [Primate of Constantinople](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Primate_of_Constantinople "Primate of Constantinople"). There have been almost 1,800 years of a continuous Christian presence on Mount Athos, and it has a long history of [monasteries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monasteries "Monasteries"), which dates back to at least 800 AD. The origin of [self-rule](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Self-rule "Self-rule") at Mount Athos can be traced back to a royal [edict](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edict "Edict") issued by the [Byzantine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire "Byzantine Empire") Emperor [John Tzimisces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Tzimisces "John Tzimisces") in 972, and reaffirmed by Emperor [Alexios I Komnenos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Alexios_I_Komnenos "Alexios I Komnenos") in 1095. Greece wrestled control of the area from the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") during the [First Balkan War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/First_Balkan_War "First Balkan War") in 1912. However, it was formally recognized as part of Greece only after a diplomatic dispute with the [Russian Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Russian_Empire "Russian Empire") was no longer an obstacle, after the latter's collapse during [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I").[^19]

Mount Athos is specifically exempt from the [free movement of people and goods](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Single_Market "European Single Market") required by Greece's membership of the [European Union](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/European_Union "European Union"),[^20] and entrance is allowed only with express permission from the monks. The number of daily visitors to Mount Athos is restricted, with all visitors required to obtain an entrance permit. Only men are permitted to visit, and Eastern Orthodox Christians take precedence in the issuing of permits. Residents of Mount Athos must be men aged 18 and over who are members of the Eastern Orthodox Church and also either monks or workers.[^21]

Athos is governed jointly by [a community consisting of members of the 20 monasteries](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monastic_community_of_Mount_Athos "Monastic community of Mount Athos") and a [Civil Administrator](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_Administrator_of_Mount_Athos "Civil Administrator of Mount Athos"), appointed by the [Greek Ministry of Foreign Affairs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_Ministry_of_Foreign_Affairs "Greek Ministry of Foreign Affairs"). The monastic community is led by the *[Protos](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protos_\(monastic_office\) "Protos (monastic office)")*.

### Islamic theocracies

An [Islamic republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_republic "Islamic republic") is the name given to several states that are officially ruled by [Islamic laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_laws "Islamic laws"), including the Islamic Republics of [Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran"), [Pakistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pakistan "Pakistan"), and [Mauritania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mauritania "Mauritania"). Pakistan first adopted the title under the constitution of 1956. Mauritania adopted it on 28 November 1958. Iran adopted it after the 1979 [Iranian Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iranian_Revolution "Iranian Revolution") that overthrew the [Pahlavi dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pahlavi_dynasty "Pahlavi dynasty").

The term "Islamic republic" has come to mean several different things, at times contradictory. To some Muslim religious leaders in the Middle East and Africa who advocate it, an Islamic republic is a [state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereign_state "Sovereign state") under a particular [Islamic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam "Islam") [form of government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Form_of_government "Form of government"). They see it as a compromise between a purely Islamic [caliphate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caliphate "Caliphate") and secular nationalism and [republicanism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republicanism "Republicanism"). In their conception of the Islamic republic, the [penal code](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Penal_code "Penal code") of the state is required to be compatible with some or all laws of [Sharia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharia "Sharia"), and the state does not necessarily have to be a monarchy, as many Middle Eastern states presently are.[^22]

#### Afghanistan

Afghanistan was an Islamic theocracy when the [Taliban](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taliban "Taliban") first ruled it from 1996 to 2001, and again since their reinstatement of the [Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Emirate_of_Afghanistan "Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan") in 2021.

Spreading from [Kandahar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kandahar "Kandahar"), the Taliban eventually captured [Kabul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kabul "Kabul") in 1996. By the end of 2000, the Taliban controlled 90% of the country, aside from the opposition ([Northern Alliance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Alliance "Northern Alliance")) strongholds which were primarily found in the northeast corner of [Badakhshan Province](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Badakhshan_Province "Badakhshan Province"). Areas under the Taliban's direct control were mainly Afghanistan's major cities and highways. Tribal khans and warlords had *de facto* direct control over various small towns, villages, and rural areas.[^23] The Taliban sought to establish [law and order](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law_and_order_\(politics\) "Law and order (politics)") and to impose a strict interpretation of Islamic *[Sharia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharia "Sharia")*, along with the religious edicts of Mullah [Mohammed Omar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mullah_Omar "Mullah Omar"), upon the entire country of Afghanistan.[^24]

During the five-year history of the [Islamic Emirate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate "Emirate"), the Taliban regime interpreted the *Sharia* in accordance with the [Hanafi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanafi "Hanafi") [school of Islamic jurisprudence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiqh "Fiqh") and the religious edicts of [Mullah Omar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mullah_Omar "Mullah Omar").[^24] The Taliban forbade pork and alcohol, many types of consumer technology such as [music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Music_of_Afghanistan "Music of Afghanistan"),[^24] [television](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Television_in_Afghanistan "Television in Afghanistan"),[^24] and [film](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cinema_of_Afghanistan "Cinema of Afghanistan"),[^24] as well as most forms of art such as [paintings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Paintings "Paintings") or [photography](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Photography "Photography"),[^24] male and female participation in [sport](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sport "Sport"),[^24] including [football](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Association_football "Association football") and [chess](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chess "Chess");[^24] [recreational activities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Recreation "Recreation") such as [kite-flying](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kite "Kite") and keeping [pigeons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pigeons "Pigeons") or other [pets](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pets "Pets") were also forbidden, and the birds were killed according to the Taliban's ruling.[^24] [Movie theaters](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Movie_theater "Movie theater") were closed and repurposed as [mosques](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mosque "Mosque").[^24] Celebration of the [Western](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/New_Year%27s_Day "New Year's Day") and [Iranian New Year](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nauruz_in_Afghanistan "Nauruz in Afghanistan") was forbidden.[^24] Taking photographs and displaying pictures or portraits was forbidden, as it was considered by the Taliban as a form of [idolatry](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Idolatry#Islam "Idolatry").[^24] Women were [banned from working](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taliban_treatment_of_women#Employment "Taliban treatment of women"),[^24] girls were [forbidden to attend schools or universities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taliban_treatment_of_women#Education "Taliban treatment of women"),[^24] were requested to observe [purdah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Purdah "Purdah") and to be accompanied outside their households by male relatives; those who violated these restrictions were punished.[^24] Men were forbidden to shave their beards and required to let them grow and keep them long according to the Taliban's liking, and to wear turbans outside their households.[^24] [^25] [Communists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Communist "Communist") were systematically executed. [Prayer](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Salah "Salah") was made compulsory and those who did not respect the religious obligation after the *[azaan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adhan "Adhan")* were arrested.[^24] [Gambling](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gambling_in_Islam "Gambling in Islam") was banned.[^24] Thieves were punished by [amputating their hands or feet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islam_and_violence#Islam_and_crime "Islam and violence").[^24] In 2000, the Taliban leader Mullah Omar officially banned [opium cultivation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Opium_production_in_Afghanistan "Opium production in Afghanistan") and [drug trafficking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Illegal_drug_trade "Illegal drug trade") in Afghanistan;[^24] [^26] [^27] the Taliban succeeded in nearly eradicating opium production (99%) by 2001.[^26] [^27] [^28] Under the Taliban governance of Afghanistan, both drug users and dealers were severely prosecuted.[^24]

[Cabinet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabinet_\(government\) "Cabinet (government)") ministers and deputies were mullahs with a " [madrasah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Madrasah "Madrasah") education." Several of them, such as the Minister of Health and Governor of the State bank, were primarily military commanders who were ready to leave their administrative posts to fight when needed. Military reverses that trapped them behind lines or led to their deaths increased the chaos in the national administration.[^29] At the national level, "all senior [Tajik](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tajiks "Tajiks"), [Uzbek](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uzbeks "Uzbeks") and [Hazara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hazaras "Hazaras") bureaucrats" were replaced "with Pashtuns, whether qualified or not." Consequently, the ministries "by and large ceased to function." [^30]

Journalist [Ahmed Rashid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahmed_Rashid "Ahmed Rashid") described the Taliban government as "a secret society run by [Kandaharis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kandahar "Kandahar")... mysterious, secretive, and dictatorial." [^31] They did not hold elections, as their spokesman explained:

> The *[Sharia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharia "Sharia")* does not allow politics or political parties. That is why we give no salaries to officials or soldiers, just food, clothes, shoes, and weapons. We want to live a life like the Prophet lived 1400 years ago, and jihad is our right. We want to recreate the time of the Prophet, and we are only carrying out what the Afghan people have wanted for the past 14 years.[^32]

They modeled their decision-making process on the Pashtun tribal council (*[jirga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jirga "Jirga")*), together with what they believed to be the early Islamic model. Discussion was followed by a building of a consensus by the "believers".[^33] Before capturing Kabul, there was talk of stepping aside once a government of "good Muslims" took power, and law and order were restored.

As the Taliban's power grew, decisions were made by Mullah Omar without consulting the *jirga* and without consulting other parts of the country. One such instance is the rejection of Loya Jirga decision about expulsion of [Osama bin Laden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Osama_bin_Laden "Osama bin Laden"). Mullah Omar visited the capital, Kabul, only twice while in power. Instead of an election, their leader's legitimacy came from an oath of allegiance (" [Bay'ah](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bay%27ah "Bay'ah") "), in imitation of [the Prophet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad "Muhammad") and the [first four](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rashidun "Rashidun") [Caliphs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caliph "Caliph"). On 4 April 1996, Mullah Omar had "the [Cloak of Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cloak_of_Muhammad "Cloak of Muhammad") " taken from its shrine, [Kirka Sharif](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kirka_Sharif "Kirka Sharif"), for the first time in 60 years. Wrapping himself in the relic, he appeared on the roof of a building in the center of Kandahar while hundreds of Pashtun [mullahs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mullah "Mullah") below shouted " [Amir al-Mu'minin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amir_al-Mu%27minin "Amir al-Mu'minin")!" (Commander of the Faithful), in a pledge of support. Taliban spokesman Mullah Wakil explained:

> Decisions are based on the advice of the Amir-ul Momineen. For us, consultation is not necessary. We believe that this is in line with the *Sharia*. We abide by the Amir's view even if he alone takes this view. There will not be a head of state. Instead, there will be an Amir al-Mu'minin. Mullah Omar will be the highest authority, and the government will not be able to implement any decision to which he does not agree. General elections are incompatible with *Sharia* and therefore we reject them.[^34]

The Taliban were reluctant to share power, and since their ranks were overwhelmingly Pashtun they ruled as overlords over the 60% of Afghans from other ethnic groups. In local government, such as the Kabul city council [^31] or Herat,[^35] Taliban loyalists, not locals, dominated, even when the [Pashto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pashto "Pashto") -speaking Taliban could not communicate with roughly half of the population who spoke [Dari](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dari "Dari") or other non-Pashtun tongues.[^35] Critics complained that this "lack of local representation in urban administration made the Taliban appear as an occupying force." After the collapse of the government following the [fall of Kabul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fall_of_Kabul_\(2021\) "Fall of Kabul (2021)") in August 2021, the Taliban returned to power and reestablished an authoritarian theocracy. They declared the restoration of the [Second Islamic Emirate of Afghanistan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Afghanistan "Afghanistan"), with their governance rooted in their strict interpretation of Sharia law.[^30]

#### Iran

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/f/f5/%D8%AC%D9%88%D8%A7%D9%86_%D9%BE%DB%8C%D8%B4%D8%B1%D9%88%D8%8C_%D8%AF%D9%84%DB%8C%D9%84_%D8%A7%D9%85%DB%8C%D8%AF.jpg/500px-%D8%AC%D9%88%D8%A7%D9%86_%D9%BE%DB%8C%D8%B4%D8%B1%D9%88%D8%8C_%D8%AF%D9%84%DB%8C%D9%84_%D8%A7%D9%85%DB%8C%D8%AF.jpg)

Supreme Leader Ali Khamenei propaganda poster

[Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Iran "Iran") has been described as a "theocratic republic" by various sources,[^36] [^37] [^38] including the [CIA](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Intelligence_Agency "Central Intelligence Agency") [World Factbook](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_World_Factbook "The World Factbook").[^39] Its [constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution_of_Iran "Constitution of Iran") has been described as a "hybrid" of "theocratic and democratic elements" by [Francis Fukuyama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Francis_Fukuyama "Francis Fukuyama").[^40] Like other Islamic states, it maintains religious laws and has religious courts to interpret all aspects of law. According to Iran's constitution, "all civil, penal, financial, economic, administrative, cultural, military, political, and other laws and regulations must be based on Islamic criteria." [^41]

In addition, Iran has a religious ruler and many religious officials in powerful governmental positions. The head of state, or " [Supreme Leader](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_Leader_of_Iran "Supreme Leader of Iran") ", is a *[faqih](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faqih "Faqih")* (scholar of Islamic law) [^42] and has more power than [the president of Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_of_Iran "President of Iran"). Iran's current Supreme Leader is [Mojtaba Khamenei](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mojtaba_Khamenei "Mojtaba Khamenei"), a role he has held since 2026. The Leader appoints the heads of many powerful governmental positions: the commanders of [the armed forces](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Military_of_Iran "Military of Iran"), the director of the [national radio and television network](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Republic_of_Iran_Broadcasting "Islamic Republic of Iran Broadcasting"), the heads of powerful [major religious and economic foundations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bonyad "Bonyad"), the [chief justice of Iran](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_Justice_of_Iran "Chief Justice of Iran"), the attorney general (indirectly through the chief justice), special tribunals, and members of [the supreme national security council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Supreme_National_Security_Council "Supreme National Security Council") who are dealing with defense and foreign affairs. He also co-appoints the 12 jurists of the [Guardian Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guardian_Council "Guardian Council").[^43]

The Leader is elected by the [Assembly of Experts](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Assembly_of_Experts "Assembly of Experts") [^39] [^44] which is made up of [mujtahids](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ijtihad "Ijtihad"),[^45] who are Islamic scholars competent in interpreting *[Sharia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sharia "Sharia")*. The [Guardian Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guardian_Council "Guardian Council"), has the power to reject bills passed by [the Parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Consultative_Assembly "Islamic Consultative Assembly"). It can also approve or reject candidates who want to run for the Presidency, Parliament, and the Assembly of Experts. The council supervises elections, and can allow or ban investigations into elections.[^39] Six of the twelve council members are [faqih](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Faq%C4%ABh "Faqīh") and have the power to approve or reject all bills passed by [the Parliament](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_Consultative_Assembly "Islamic Consultative Assembly"), whether the faqih believes that the bill is in accordance with Islamic laws and customs (*Sharia*) or not. The other six members are lawyers appointed by the chief justice, who is a cleric and appointed by the Leader.[^46]

#### Saudi Arabia

In the [Basic Law of Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_Law_of_Saudi_Arabia "Basic Law of Saudi Arabia"), [Saudi Arabia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saudi_Arabia "Saudi Arabia") defines itself as a sovereign Arab [Islamic state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Islamic_state "Islamic state") with Islam as its official religion. However, some critiques describe Saudi Arabia as an Islamic theocracy. Religious minorities do not have the right to practice their religion openly. Conversion from Islam to another religion is punishable by death as [apostasy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Apostasy "Apostasy").[^47] Muhammad Al-Atawneh describes the current Saudi regime as a 'theo-monarchy, that draws power from long-standing religio-cultural norms.' [^48]

#### Yemen (Houthi-controlled territories)

Yemen has operated as a divided state since the Houthi movement (officially Ansar Allah) seized the capital Sanaa in 2014, expelling the internationally recognized government. The Houthis draw on the Zaydi Imamate political theory, which holds that governance must be theocratic and led by a descendant of the Prophet Muhammad. Though rooted in Zaydi Shia Islam, a sect distinct from Iran's Twelver tradition the movement has adopted increasingly Iranian-style revolutionary politics, including compulsory Shia holiday observances and systematically rewritten school curricula. The Houthi-controlled territory is a theocratic police state characterized by repression, militarization, and religious indoctrination.[^49]

#### Mauritania

Mauritania's constitution defines the country as an Islamic republic, designates Islam as the sole religion of its citizenry and the state, and bases its legal system on a combination of French civil law and Sharia. It classifies apostasy as a capital offense: convicted apostates are given three days to repent, after which they face execution, dissolution of family rights, and property confiscation. Blasphemy carries the same penalty. Non-Muslims are barred from citizenship, and conversion from Islam results in automatic loss of citizenship. The death penalty for religious crimes has not been carried out in practice, though prosecutions have occurred. Mauritania holds elections and maintains a nominally civilian government, distinguishing it from clerical theocracies such as Iran; its theocratic character derives from constitutional and legal structure rather than direct clerical rule.

#### Notes

Countries like Pakistan, Libya, and parts of Somalia have Sharia as a constitutional source of law but retain secular governing structures alongside it. They are generally classified as Islamic republics rather than theocracies.

### Central Tibetan Administration

The [Central Tibetan Administration](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Tibetan_Administration "Central Tibetan Administration"), colloquially known as the Tibetan government in exile, is a [Tibetan exile](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tibetan_exile "Tibetan exile") organization with a state-like internal structure. According to its charter, the position of head of state of the Central Tibetan Administration belongs *ex officio* to the [Dalai Lama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dalai_Lama "Dalai Lama"), a religious hierarch. In this respect, it continues the traditions of the [former government of Tibet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ganden_Podrang "Ganden Podrang"), which was ruled by the Dalai Lamas and their ministers, with a specific role reserved for a class of monk officials.

On 14 March 2011, at the [14th Dalai Lama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/14th_Dalai_Lama "14th Dalai Lama") 's suggestion, the parliament of the Central Tibetan Administration began considering a proposal to remove the Dalai Lama's role as head of state in favor of an elected leader.

The first directly elected [Kalön Tripa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalon_Tripa "Kalon Tripa") was [Samdhong Rinpoche](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Samdhong_Rinpoche "Samdhong Rinpoche"), who was elected on 20 August 2001.[^50]

Before 2011, the [Kalön Tripa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kalon_Tripa "Kalon Tripa") position was subordinate to the [14th Dalai Lama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/14th_Dalai_Lama "14th Dalai Lama") [^51] who presided over the government in exile from its founding.[^52] In August of that year, Lobsang Sangay received 55 percent of 49,189 votes, defeating his nearest rival Tethong Tenzin Namgyal by 8,646 votes,[^53] becoming the second popularly elected Kalön Tripa. The Dalai Lama announced that his political authority would be transferred to Sangay.[^54]

#### Change to Sikyong

On 20 September 2012, the 15th Tibetan Parliament-in-Exile unanimously voted to change the title of Kalön Tripa to *Sikyong* in Article 19 of the Charter of the Tibetans in exile and relevant articles.[^55] The Dalai Lama had previously referred to the Kalön Tripa as Sikyong, and this usage was cited as the primary justification for the name change. According to *Tibetan Review*, "Sikyong" translates to "political leader", as distinct from "spiritual leader".[^56] Foreign affairs Kalön [Dicki Chhoyang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dicki_Chhoyang "Dicki Chhoyang") stated that the term "Sikyong" has had a precedent dating back to the [7th Dalai Lama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/7th_Dalai_Lama "7th Dalai Lama"), and that the name change "ensures historical continuity and legitimacy of the traditional leadership from the Fifth Dalai Lama".[^57] The online Dharma Dictionary translates sikyong (*srid skyong*) as "secular ruler; regime, regent".[^58] The title *sikyong* had previously been used by [regents](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Regent "Regent") who ruled Tibet during the Dalai Lama's minority.

### States with official state religions

Having a state religion is not sufficient to mean that a state is a theocracy in the narrow sense of the term. Many countries have a state religion without the government directly deriving its powers from a divine authority or a religious authority which is directly exercising governmental powers. Since few theocracies exist in the modern world, the word "theocracy" is now used as a descriptive term for a government which enforces a state religion.

## Historical Theocracies

### Byzantine Empire

The [Byzantine Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Byzantine_Empire "Byzantine Empire") (a.d. 324–1453) operated under [Symphonia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Symphonia_\(theology\) "Symphonia (theology)"), meaning that the [emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_Byzantine_emperors "List of Byzantine emperors") was both the head of [civil society](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Civil_society "Civil society") and the ultimate authority over the ecclesiastical authorities, the [patriarchates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Patriarchates "Patriarchates"). The emperor, regarded as God's representative on earth, ruled as an absolute [autocrat](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autocracy "Autocracy").[^59]

Jennifer Fretland VanVoorst argues that "the Byzantine Empire became a theocracy in the sense that Christian values and ideals were the foundation of the empire's political ideals and heavily entwined with its political goals".[^60] [Steven Runciman](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Steven_Runciman "Steven Runciman") writes in his book *The Byzantine Theocracy* (2004):

> The constitution of the Byzantine Empire was based on the conviction that it was the earthly copy of the Kingdom of Heaven. Just as God ruled in Heaven, so the Emperor, made in His image, should rule on earth and carry out his commandments....It saw itself as a universal empire. Ideally, it should embrace all the peoples of the Earth who, ideally, should all be members of the one true Christian Church, its own Orthodox Church. Just as man was made in God's image, so man's kingdom on Earth was made in the image of the Kingdom of Heaven.[^61]

### Caliphates

The [Sunni Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sunni_Islam "Sunni Islam") stipulates that, as a head of state, a [Caliph](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Caliph "Caliph") should be selected or elected by Muslims or by their representatives. Followers of [Shia Islam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shia_Islam "Shia Islam"), however, believe a Caliph should be an [Imam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imam "Imam") chosen by [God](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/God_in_Islam "God in Islam") from the [Ahl al-Bayt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ahl_al-Bayt "Ahl al-Bayt") (the "Family of the House", [Muhammad](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Muhammad "Muhammad") 's direct descendants). In both cases, caliphs theoretically combine religious and secular powers.

### China

Similar to the Roman Emperor, the [Chinese sovereign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_sovereign "Chinese sovereign") was historically held to be the [Son of Heaven](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Son_of_Heaven "Son of Heaven"). However, from the first historical Emperor on, this was largely ceremonial and tradition quickly established it as a posthumous dignity, like the Roman institution. The situation before [Qin Shi Huang Di](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_Shi_Huang "Qin Shi Huang") (r. 221–210 BCE) is less clear.

The [Shang dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shang_dynasty "Shang dynasty") (c. 1600 BCE to c. 1045 BCE) essentially functioned as a theocracy, declaring the ruling family the [sons of heaven](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Son_of_heaven "Son of heaven") and calling the chief sky god [Shangdi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shangdi "Shangdi") after a word for their deceased ancestors.[^62] After their overthrow by the [Zhou](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Zhou_dynasty "Zhou dynasty"), the royal clan of Shang were not eliminated but instead moved to a ceremonial capital where they were charged to continue the performance of their rituals.

The titles combined by [Shi Huangdi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shi_Huangdi "Shi Huangdi") to form his new title of [emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_emperor "Chinese emperor") were originally applied to [god-like beings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Sovereigns "Three Sovereigns") who ordered the heavens and earth and to [culture heroes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Three_Sovereigns_and_Five_Emperors "Three Sovereigns and Five Emperors") credited with the invention of [agriculture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Five_Grains "Five Grains"), [clothing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_clothing "Traditional Chinese clothing"), [music](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_music "Traditional Chinese music"), [astrology](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chinese_astrology "Chinese astrology"), etc. Even after the fall of [Qin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qin_dynasty "Qin dynasty") in 206 BCE, an emperor's words were considered sacred edicts (聖旨) and his written proclamations "directives from above" (上諭).

As a result, some [Sinologists](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sinologist "Sinologist") translate the title *huangdi* (usually rendered ["emperor"](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emperor_of_China "Emperor of China")) as *thearch*. The term properly refers to the head of a thearchy (a kingdom of gods), but the more specific "theocrat" carries associations of a strong priesthood that would be generally inaccurate in describing imperial China. Others reserve the use of the word "thearch" to describe the legendary figures of Chinese prehistory while continuing to use "emperor" to describe historical rulers.[^62]

The [Heavenly Kingdom of Great Peace](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Heavenly_Kingdom_of_Great_Peace "Heavenly Kingdom of Great Peace") (1851 to 1864) in [Qing China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_China "Qing China") was a heterodox Christian theocracy led by [Hong Xiuquan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Xiuquan "Hong Xiuquan"), who portrayed himself as the younger brother of [Jesus Christ](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jesus_Christ "Jesus Christ"). His theocratic state fought one of the most destructive wars in history, the [Taiping Rebellion](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taiping_Rebellion "Taiping Rebellion"), against the [Qing dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qing_dynasty "Qing dynasty") for fifteen years before being crushed following the 1864 fall of the rebel capital [Nanjing](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nanjing "Nanjing").

### Deseret

The question of theocracy has been debated extensively by historians regarding the [Latter-Day Saint](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Church_of_Jesus_Christ_of_Latter-day_Saints "The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints") communities in Illinois and (especially) in Utah.[^63] [^64] [^65]

[Joseph Smith](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Joseph_Smith "Joseph Smith"), mayor of [Nauvoo, Illinois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nauvoo,_Illinois "Nauvoo, Illinois") and founder of the [Latter Day Saint movement](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latter_Day_Saint_movement "Latter Day Saint movement"), ran as an [independent for U.S. president in 1844](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1844_United_States_presidential_election "1844 United States presidential election"). He proposed the redemption of slaves by selling public lands, reducing the size and salary of Congress, the closure of prisons, the [annexation of Texas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Annexation_of_Texas "Annexation of Texas"), Oregon, and parts of Canada, the securing of international rights on the high seas, [free trade](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_trade "Free trade"), and the re-establishment of a [national bank](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_Bank_of_the_United_States "Second Bank of the United States").[^66] Smith's top aide, [Brigham Young](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brigham_Young "Brigham Young"), campaigned for Smith, saying, "He it is that God of Heaven designs to save this nation from destruction and preserve the Constitution." [^67] The campaign ended when Smith was [killed by a mob](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Death_of_Joseph_Smith "Death of Joseph Smith") while in the [Carthage, Illinois](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carthage,_Illinois "Carthage, Illinois"), jail on June 27, 1844.[^68]

After severe persecution, the Mormons left the United States and resettled in a remote part of what is now Utah, (then part of Mexico). However the United States [took control in 1848](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Guadalupe_Hidalgo "Treaty of Guadalupe Hidalgo") and would not accept polygamy. The Mormon [State of Deseret](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/State_of_Deseret "State of Deseret") was short-lived.[^69] Its original borders stretched from western [Colorado](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colorado "Colorado") to the [southern California](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southern_California "Southern California") coast. When the Mormons arrived in the valley of the [Great Salt Lake](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Salt_Lake "Great Salt Lake") in 1847, the [Great Basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Great_Basin "Great Basin") was still a part of Mexico and had no secular government. As a result, [Brigham Young](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Brigham_Young "Brigham Young") administered the region both spiritually and temporally through the highly organized and centralized [Melchizedek Priesthood](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Melchizedek_priesthood_\(Latter_Day_Saints\) "Melchizedek priesthood (Latter Day Saints)"). This original organization, based upon Joseph Smith's concept of [theodemocracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodemocracy "Theodemocracy"), instituted a governmental system combining biblical theocracy with mid-19th-century American political ideals.[^70] [^71]

In 1849 the Saints organized a secular government in Utah, although many ecclesiastical leaders maintained their positions of secular power. The Mormons also petitioned Congress to have Deseret admitted into the Union as a state. However, the [Compromise of 1850](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Compromise_of_1850 "Compromise of 1850") established [Utah Territory](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utah_Territory "Utah Territory"), and U.S. President [Millard Fillmore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Millard_Fillmore "Millard Fillmore") appointed Brigham Young as governor (in office 1851 to 1858)). In this situation, Young still stood as head of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints (LDS Church) as well as of Utah's secular government.

After the abortive [Utah War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Utah_War "Utah War") of 1857–1858, the replacement of Young by an outside Federal Territorial Governor, intense federal [prosecution of LDS Church leaders](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anti-Mormonism "Anti-Mormonism"), the eventual resolution of controversies regarding [plural marriage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Plural_marriage "Plural marriage"), and accession by Utah to statehood, the apparent temporal aspects of LDS [theodemocracy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Theodemocracy "Theodemocracy") receded markedly.[^72]

### Egypt

Ancient Egyptian society regarded its [pharaohs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pharaoh "Pharaoh") as divine and associated them with [Horus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Horus "Horus"), and after death, with [Osiris](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Osiris "Osiris").[^73] While not considered equal to other members of the [Egyptian pantheon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Egyptian_pantheon "Egyptian pantheon"), the pharaoh had the responsibility of mediating between the gods and the people.[^74]

High Priests of Amun held control over the treasury and estates of Amun-Re of Karnak, the chief god of the Egyptian empire. These Supreme Pontiffs were chief prophets or interpreters of the god’s will, and they wielded great political power. The prestige of this priesthood rivaled that of the Pharaoh. [^75]

### Florence

The short rule (1494–1498) of [Girolamo Savonarola](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Girolamo_Savonarola "Girolamo Savonarola"), a [Dominican](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dominican_Order "Dominican Order") priest, over the city of [Florence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Florence "Florence") had features of a theocracy. During his rule, "unchristian" books, statues, poetry, and other items were burned (in the [Bonfire of the Vanities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bonfire_of_the_Vanities "Bonfire of the Vanities")), [sodomy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sodomy "Sodomy") was made a capital offense, and other Christian practices became law.

### Israel

In biblical times, [Early Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_ancient_Israel_and_Judah "History of ancient Israel and Judah") was a [kritarchy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kritarchy "Kritarchy"), ruled by [Judges](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hebrew_Bible_judges "Hebrew Bible judges") before the institution of a monarchy under [Saul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Saul "Saul"). The Israelites regarded the Judges as representatives of God to dispense His judgement, and were often themselves prophets.

### Japan

The Japanese people have historically venerated their [emperor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emperor_of_Japan "Emperor of Japan") as the descendant of the [Shinto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shinto "Shinto") sun-goddess [Amaterasu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amaterasu "Amaterasu"). Through this line of descent, the emperor was seen as a living god who was the supreme leader of the Japanese people. This status only changed with the [Occupation of Japan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupation_of_Japan "Occupation of Japan") following the end of the [Second World War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Second_World_War "Second World War") in 1945: on 1 January 1946 Emperor [Hirohito](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hirohito "Hirohito"), at the request of [Douglas MacArthur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Douglas_MacArthur "Douglas MacArthur"), [declared that he was not a living god](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Humanity_Declaration "Humanity Declaration") in order for Japan to reorganize into a democratic nation.[^76]

### Mississippians

Many of the [polities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polity "Polity") that existed within the [Mississippian archaeological culture](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mississippian_culture "Mississippian culture") were ruled by theocrats who often held [titles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial,_royal_and_noble_ranks "Imperial, royal and noble ranks") claiming to be 'children of the sun.' Some of them had power over life and death in their communities, reportedly sometimes [killing people on a whim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Human_sacrifice_in_pre-Columbian_cultures "Human sacrifice in pre-Columbian cultures"). Such polities includes [Cahokia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cahokia_polity "Cahokia polity"), [Pafalaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Moundville_phase "Moundville phase"), [Ocute](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ocute "Ocute"), [Cofitachequi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cofitachequi "Cofitachequi"), and [Coosa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Coosa_chiefdom "Coosa chiefdom").[^77]

### Mongolia

The [Bogd Khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outer_Mongolia_\(1911%E2%80%931919\) "Outer Mongolia (1911–1919)") period of [Mongolia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mongolia "Mongolia") (1911–19) featured a Buddhist theocratic [khanate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Khanate "Khanate").[^78]

### Prince-Bishopric of Montenegro

The [Prince-Bishopric of Montenegro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prince-Bishopric_of_Montenegro "Prince-Bishopric of Montenegro") existed as a [Serbian Orthodox](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serbian_Orthodox "Serbian Orthodox") [ecclesiastical principality](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ecclesiastical_principality "Ecclesiastical principality") from 1516 until 1852. The principality was located around modern-day [Montenegro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montenegro "Montenegro"). It emerged from the [Eparchy of Cetinje](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Eparchy_of_Cetinje "Eparchy of Cetinje"), later known as the [Metropolitanate of Montenegro and the Littoral](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolitanate_of_Montenegro_and_the_Littoral "Metropolitanate of Montenegro and the Littoral"), whose bishops defied the [Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ottoman_Empire "Ottoman Empire") overlordship and transformed the parish of Cetinje into a *de facto* theocracy, ruling it as Metropolitans (*Vladike*, also known as *prince-bishops*). The first prince-bishop was [Vavila](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vavila,_Metropolitan_of_Zeta "Vavila, Metropolitan of Zeta"). The system was transformed into a hereditary one by [Danilo Šćepčević](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Danilo_%C5%A0%C4%87ep%C4%8Devi%C4%87 "Danilo Šćepčević"), bishop of [Cetinje](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cetinje "Cetinje") from 1697 to 1735. He united the several [tribes of Montenegro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tribes_of_Montenegro "Tribes of Montenegro") into fighting the Ottoman Empire that had occupied all of Montenegro (as the [Sanjak of Montenegro](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sanjak_of_Montenegro "Sanjak of Montenegro") and [Montenegro Vilayet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Montenegro_Vilayet "Montenegro Vilayet")) and most of [southeastern Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Southeastern_Europe "Southeastern Europe") at the time.

### Polynesia

[Pitirim Sorokin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pitirim_Sorokin "Pitirim Sorokin") identified theocratic régimes in traditional [Polynesia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polynesia "Polynesia").[^79]

### Protestant Theocracies

Many protestant groups attempted to form theocratic governments throughout their early history.

Between 1533 and 1535 the [Protestant](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Protestant "Protestant") leaders [Jan Mattys](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jan_Mattys "Jan Mattys") and [John of Leiden](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_of_Leiden "John of Leiden") erected a short-lived [theocratic kingdom in the city of Münster](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/M%C3%BCnster_rebellion "Münster rebellion") in Westphalia. They established an [Anabaptist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Anabaptist "Anabaptist") régime of "New Jerusalem" with [chiliastic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chiliastic "Chiliastic") and millenarian expectations. [Money](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Money "Money") was abolished and any violations of the [Ten Commandments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ten_Commandments "Ten Commandments") were punished by death. Despite the pietistic ideology, [polygamy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polygamy "Polygamy") was allowed, and von Leiden had 17 wives. In 1535, the Prince-Bishop of Münster, [Franz von Waldeck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Franz_von_Waldeck "Franz von Waldeck"), recaptured the city, ending the existence of the kingdom.

Historians debate the extent to which [Geneva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geneva "Geneva"), Switzerland, in the days of [John Calvin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Calvin "John Calvin") (1509–64) was a theocracy. On the one hand, Calvin's theology clearly called for separation between church and state. Other historians have stressed the enormous political power wielded on a daily basis by the clerics.[^80] [^81]

In nearby [Zürich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Z%C3%BCrich "Zürich"), Switzerland, Protestant reformer [Huldrych Zwingli](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Huldrych_Zwingli "Huldrych Zwingli") (1484–1531) built a political system that many scholars have called a theocracy, while others have denied it.[^82]

From 1620 until the [American Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/American_Revolution "American Revolution") (but with many lingering traces extending into the modern era), Massachusetts colonies operated as strict theocracies, in which every white resident was required to belong to the Congregational (Puritan) Church. The leaders of the communities generally believed that they were carrying out the will of God, and they were often ministers. No religious deviation was tolerated. Any members of other sects were (initially) dispatched to Rhode Island. Later other forms of religious discrimination were uniformly practiced with governmental approval. (Today's Massachusetts and Maine formed one single colony/state until 1820.). Puritans and other groups such as the [Diggers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diggers "Diggers") sought to establish theocratic governance in England as well.

### Quebec

[Quebec](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quebec "Quebec") under the premiership of [Maurice Duplessis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maurice_Duplessis "Maurice Duplessis") (1936–1939 and 1944–1959) had attributes of a [Roman Catholic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Catholic "Roman Catholic") theocracy. The church controlled education and healthcare, books and films were censored, drive-in movie theatres were illegal, religion had strong influence over civil law (for example, only churches could conduct marriages, and divorce was banned), and [Jehovah's Witnesses](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Jehovah%27s_Witnesses "Jehovah's Witnesses") were persecuted. This theocracy ended in 1960 with the beginning of the [Quiet Revolution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Quiet_Revolution "Quiet Revolution") (1960–1966).[^83] [^84]

### Rome

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/9/9f/Augusto_come_giove%2C_00-50_dc_circa.JPG/250px-Augusto_come_giove%2C_00-50_dc_circa.JPG)

Roman emperor Augustus as Jupiter, holding scepter and orb (first half of 1st century AD)

From c. 27 BCE the [Imperial cult of ancient Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Imperial_cult_\(ancient_Rome\) "Imperial cult (ancient Rome)") identified [Roman emperors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_emperor "Roman emperor") and some members of their families with the [divinely sanctioned](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Divine_right_of_kings "Divine right of kings") authority (*[auctoritas](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Auctoritas "Auctoritas")*) of the [Roman State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_State "Roman State"). The official offer of [*cultus*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Glossary_of_ancient_Roman_religion#cultus "Glossary of ancient Roman religion") to a living emperor acknowledged his office and rule as divinely approved and constitutional: his Principate should therefore demonstrate [pious respect](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pietas "Pietas") for traditional [republican](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republicanism "Republicanism") [deities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deity "Deity") and [mores](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mos_maiorum "Mos maiorum").

### Sumer

[Sumerian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumer "Sumer") cities were probably theocratic and were most likely headed by a priest-king (*ensi*), assisted by a council of elders including both men and women.[^85]

### Tibet

Unified religious rule in [Buddhist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Buddhist "Buddhist") Tibet began in 1642, when the [Fifth Dalai Lama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fifth_Dalai_Lama "Fifth Dalai Lama") allied with the military power of the Mongol [Gushri Khan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gushri_Khan "Gushri Khan") to consolidate political power and to center control around his office as head of the [Gelug](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gelug "Gelug") school.[^86] This form of government is known as the [dual system of government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dual_system_of_government "Dual system of government"). Prior to 1642, particular monasteries and monks had held considerable power throughout Tibet but had not achieved anything approaching complete control, and their power continued in a diffuse, feudal system after the ascension of the Fifth [Dalai Lama](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dalai_Lama "Dalai Lama"). Power in Tibet was held by a number of traditional élites, including members of the nobility, the heads of the major Buddhist sects (including their various [tulkus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tulku "Tulku")), and various large and influential monastic communities.[^87]

### Timurid and Mughal Empires

The Emperors of the [Timurid](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Timurid_Empire "Timurid Empire") and [Mughal dynasty](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mughal_dynasty "Mughal dynasty") were regarded as intermediaries of their subordinates and God by virtue of the blessings of the Hazrat Ishaans, who were the spiritual guides of the Timurid and Mughal Emperors. The Emperors believed the Hazrat Ishaans to be the rightful spiritual successors of Muhammad and by this virtue to be the intermediaries between God and man.[^88] [^89] [^90]

### Travancore

The state of [Travancore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Travancore "Travancore") adopted a form of theocracy in the 18th century under the King [Marthanda Varma](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marthanda_Varma "Marthanda Varma") by donating the country to the [Hindu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hinduism "Hinduism") deity [Vishnu](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vishnu "Vishnu"), and thereafter rule as the vice-regent of the deity. It is known as [Thrippadidanam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Marthanda_Varma#Thrippadidanam "Marthanda Varma"). The holding of the ritual might be a tactics move by the king on the model of the strategies of countries in Europe.[^91]
