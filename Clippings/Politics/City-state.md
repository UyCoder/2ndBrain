Monaco is a city state located on the Mediterranean and the second smallest country in the world (after the Vatican, another city state)

A **city-state** is an independent [sovereign](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sovereignty "Sovereignty") city that serves as the primary hub of political, economic, and cultural life within its contiguous territory.[^1] This concept stands in contrast to that of a regular state or [country](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Country "Country"), which typically encompasses a [capital city](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Capital_city "Capital city") and additional urban centers, in addition to the countryside. Throughout history, numerous city-states have emerged in various regions of the world, including prominent examples such as [Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome "Rome"), [Carthage](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Carthage "Carthage"), [Athens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Athens "Athens"), and [Sparta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sparta "Sparta"), as well as the [Italian city-states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_city-states "Italian city-states") that flourished during the [Medieval](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Middle_Ages "Middle Ages") and [Renaissance](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Renaissance "Renaissance") periods, such as [Florence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Florence "Republic of Florence"), [Venice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Venice "Republic of Venice"), [Genoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa "Republic of Genoa"), and [Milan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Milan "Duchy of Milan").

With the rise of [nation states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation_states "Nation states") worldwide, there remains some disagreement on the number of modern city-states that still exist; [Singapore](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore "Singapore"), [Monaco](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monaco "Monaco") and [Vatican City](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vatican_City "Vatican City") are the candidates most commonly discussed. Out of these, Singapore is the largest and most populous city-state in the world, with full sovereignty, international borders, its own [currency](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore_dollar "Singapore dollar"), a robust [military](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Singapore_Armed_Forces "Singapore Armed Forces"), and substantial international influence in its own right.[^2] *[The Economist](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Economist "The Economist")* refers to it as the "world's only fully functioning city-state".[^3]

Several non-sovereign cities enjoy a high degree of autonomy and are often considered to be city-states, such as [Hong Kong](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hong_Kong "Hong Kong") and [Macau](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Macau "Macau").[^4] Cities of the [United Arab Emirates](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Arab_Emirates "United Arab Emirates"), most notably [Dubai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dubai "Dubai"), have been cited in similar terms.[^5] [^6] [^7] Additionally, certain non-sovereign overseas territories, such as [Gibraltar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gibraltar "Gibraltar"), are also sometimes called city-states.[^8]

## Historical background

### Ancient and medieval world

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/3/3d/Dubrovnik_crop.jpg/250px-Dubrovnik_crop.jpg)

The Republic of Ragusa, a maritime city-state, was based in the walled city of Dubrovnik.

Historical city-states included [Sumerian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sumer "Sumer") cities such as [Uruk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Uruk "Uruk"), [Ur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ur "Ur") and [Nippur](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nippur "Nippur"); [Ancient Egyptian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egypt "Ancient Egypt") city-states, such as [Thebes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thebes,_Egypt "Thebes, Egypt") and [Memphis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Memphis,_Egypt "Memphis, Egypt"); the [Phoenician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicia "Phoenicia") cities (such as [Tyre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tyre,_Lebanon "Tyre, Lebanon") and [Sidon](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sidon "Sidon")); the five [Philistine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philistines "Philistines") city-states; the [Berber](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Berber_people "Berber people") city-states of the [Garamantes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garamantes "Garamantes"); the city-states of [ancient Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Greece "Ancient Greece") (the [poleis](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poleis "Poleis") such as [Athens](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Classical_Athens "Classical Athens"), [Sparta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sparta "Sparta"), [Thebes](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thebes,_Greece "Thebes, Greece"), and [Corinth](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Corinth "Ancient Corinth")); the [Roman Republic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Republic "Roman Republic") (which grew from a city-state into a vast empire); the [Italian city-states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_city-states "Italian city-states") from the Middle Ages to the early modern period, such as [Florence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Florence "Republic of Florence"), [Siena](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Siena "Republic of Siena"), [Ferrara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Ferrara "Duchy of Ferrara"), [Milan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Milan "Duchy of Milan") (which as they grew in power began to dominate neighboring cities) and [Genoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa "Republic of Genoa") and [Venice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Venice "Republic of Venice"), which became powerful [thalassocracies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Thalassocracies "Thalassocracies"); the [Mayan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maya_civilization "Maya civilization") and other cultures of pre-Columbian [Mesoamerica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mesoamerica "Mesoamerica") (including cities such as [Chichen Itza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chichen_Itza "Chichen Itza"), [Tikal](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tikal "Tikal"), [Copán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cop%C3%A1n "Copán") and [Monte Albán](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monte_Alb%C3%A1n "Monte Albán")); the [central Asian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Asia "Central Asia") cities along the [Silk Road](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Silk_Road "Silk Road"); the city-states of the [Swahili coast](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swahili_coast "Swahili coast"); [Ragusa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Ragusa "Republic of Ragusa") and [Poljica](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Poljica "Republic of Poljica") [^9] in [Croatia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croatia "Croatia"); [Tbilisi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Emirate_of_Tbilisi "Emirate of Tbilisi") in [Georgia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Georgia_\(country\) "Georgia (country)"); the medieval Russian city-states of [Novgorod](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Novgorod_Republic "Novgorod Republic") and [Pskov](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pskov_Republic "Pskov Republic");[^10] the [free imperial cities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_imperial_city "Free imperial city") of [German-speaking Europe](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geographical_distribution_of_German_speakers#Europe "Geographical distribution of German speakers"); [mueang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mueang "Mueang") of [Indochina](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_Southeast_Asia "Mainland Southeast Asia"); [barangay states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barangay "Barangay") of the [Philippines](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Philippines "Philippines"); and many others. Danish historian Poul Holm has classed the [Viking](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vikings "Vikings") colonial cities in medieval [Ireland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ireland "Ireland"), most importantly the [Kingdom of Dublin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kingdom_of_Dublin "Kingdom of Dublin"), as city-states.[^11]

In [Cyprus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cyprus "Cyprus"), the [Phoenician](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Phoenicia "Phoenicia") settlement of [Kition](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Kition "Kition") (in present-day Larnaca) was a city-state that existed from around 800 BC until the end of the 4th century BC.[^12]

Some of the best-known examples of city-state culture in human history are the ancient [Greek city-states](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greek_city-states "Greek city-states") and the merchant city-states of [Renaissance Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_Renaissance "Italian Renaissance"), which organised themselves as independent centers. The success of regional units coexisting as [autonomous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomy "Autonomy") actors in loose geographical and cultural unity, as in [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy "Italy") and [Greece](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greece "Greece"), often prevented their [amalgamation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Amalgamation_\(politics\) "Amalgamation (politics)") into larger national units. However, such small political entities often survived only for short periods because they lacked the resources to defend themselves against incursions by larger states (such as Roman conquest of Greece). Thus they inevitably gave way to larger organisations of society, including the [empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Empire "Empire") and the [nation-state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation-state "Nation-state").[^13]

### Central Europe

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/7/77/Free_Imperial_Cities_1792.png/330px-Free_Imperial_Cities_1792.png)

The Free imperial cities as of 1792

In the [Holy Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire "Holy Roman Empire") (962–1806) more than 80 [Free Imperial Cities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_imperial_city "Free imperial city") came to enjoy considerable autonomy in the Middle Ages and in early modern times, buttressed legally by [international law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/International_law "International law") following the [Peace of Westphalia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Peace_of_Westphalia "Peace of Westphalia") of 1648. Some, like three of the earlier [Hanseatic cities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanseatic_cities "Hanseatic cities") – [Bremen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bremen "Bremen"), [Hamburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_Hamburg "History of Hamburg") and [Lübeck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_City_of_L%C3%BCbeck "Free City of Lübeck") – pooled their economic relations with foreign powers and were able to wield considerable diplomatic clout. Individual cities often made protective alliances with other cities or with neighbouring regions, including the [Hanseatic League](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hanseatic_League "Hanseatic League") (1358 – 17th century), the [Swabian League of Cities](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Swabian_League_of_Cities "Swabian League of Cities") (1331–1389), the [Décapole](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/D%C3%A9capole "Décapole") (1354–1679) in the Alsace, or the [Old Swiss Confederacy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Swiss_Confederacy "Old Swiss Confederacy") (c. 1300 – 1798). The [Swiss cantons](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cantons_of_Switzerland "Cantons of Switzerland") of [Zürich](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canton_of_Z%C3%BCrich "Canton of Zürich"), [Bern](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canton_of_Bern "Canton of Bern"), [Lucerne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canton_of_Lucerne "Canton of Lucerne"), [Fribourg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canton_of_Fribourg "Canton of Fribourg"), [Solothurn](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canton_of_Solothurn "Canton of Solothurn"), [Basel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canton_of_Basel "Canton of Basel"), [Schaffhausen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canton_of_Schaffhausen "Canton of Schaffhausen"), and [Geneva](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Canton_of_Geneva "Canton of Geneva") originated as city-states.

After the dissolution of the Holy Roman Empire in 1806, some cities – then members of different [confederacies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Confederation "Confederation") – officially became sovereign city-states, such as the [Free Hanseatic City of Bremen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bremen_\(state\) "Bremen (state)") (1806–11 and again 1813–71), the [Free City of Frankfurt upon Main](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_City_of_Frankfurt "Free City of Frankfurt") (1815–66), the [Free and Hanseatic City of Hamburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Hamburg "Hamburg") (1806–11 and again 1814–71), the [Free and Hanseatic City of Lübeck](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_City_of_L%C3%BCbeck "Free City of Lübeck") (1806–11 and again 1813–71), and the [Free City of Kraków](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Free_City_of_Krak%C3%B3w "Free City of Kraków") (1815–1846). Under [Habsburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habsburg "Habsburg") rule the city of [Fiume](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiume "Fiume") had the status of a [*corpus separatum*](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corpus_separatum_\(Fiume\) "Corpus separatum (Fiume)") (1779–1919), which – while falling short of an independent sovereignty – had many attributes of a city-state.

### Italy

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Italy_1494.svg/250px-Italy_1494.svg.png)

Italy in 1494, after the Peace of Lodi

In [Northern](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Northern_Italy "Northern Italy") and [Central Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Central_Italy "Central Italy") during the medieval and Renaissance periods, city-states – with various amounts of associated land – became the standard form of polity. Some of them, despite being [de facto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/De_facto "De facto") independent states, were formally part of the [Holy Roman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Holy_Roman_Empire "Holy Roman Empire"). The era of the Italian states, in particular from the 11th to the 15th centuries, featured remarkable economic development, trade, manufacture, and mercantile capitalism, together with increasing urbanization, with remarkable influence throughout much of the Mediterranean world and Europe as a whole. During this time, most of the Italian city-states were ruled by one person, such as the [Signoria](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Signoria "Signoria") or by a dynasty, such as the [House of Gonzaga](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Gonzaga "House of Gonzaga") and the [House of Sforza](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/House_of_Sforza "House of Sforza").[^14]

#### Examples of Italian city-states during the Middle Ages and the Renaissance

- [Republic of Florence](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Florence "Republic of Florence"), [Duchy of Milan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Milan "Duchy of Milan"), [Duchy of Ferrara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Ferrara "Duchy of Ferrara"),[^15] [San Marino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/History_of_San_Marino "History of San Marino"), [Duchy of Modena and Reggio](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Modena_and_Reggio "Duchy of Modena and Reggio"), [Duchy of Urbino](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Urbino "Duchy of Urbino"), [Duchy of Mantua](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Mantua "Duchy of Mantua") and the [Republic of Lucca](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Lucca "Republic of Lucca").
- The powerful [maritime republics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Maritime_republics "Maritime republics"): [Republic of Venice](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Venice "Republic of Venice"), [Republic of Genoa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Genoa "Republic of Genoa"), [Republic of Amalfi](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Amalfi "Republic of Amalfi"), [Republic of Pisa](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Pisa "Republic of Pisa"), [Republic of Ancona](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_of_Ancona "Republic of Ancona") and [Duchy of Gaeta](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Duchy_of_Gaeta "Duchy of Gaeta").

### Southeast Asia

In the history of [Mainland Southeast Asia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mainland_Southeast_Asia "Mainland Southeast Asia"), aristocratic groups, Buddhist leaders, and others organized settlements into autonomous or semi-autonomous city-states. These were referred to as *[mueang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mueang "Mueang")*, and were usually related in a tributary relationship now described as [mandala](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandala_\(political_model\) "Mandala (political model)") or as *over-lapping sovereignty*, in which smaller city-states paid tribute to larger ones that paid tribute to still larger ones – until reaching the apex in cities like [Ayutthaya](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ayutthaya_\(city\) "Ayutthaya (city)"), [Bagan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bagan "Bagan"), [Bangkok](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bangkok "Bangkok") and others that served as centers of Southeast Asian royalty. The system existed until the 19th century, when [colonization](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Colonization "Colonization") by European powers occurred. [Siam](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rattanakosin_Kingdom "Rattanakosin Kingdom"), a regional power at the time, needed to define their territories for negotiation with the European powers so the Siamese government established a [nation-state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Nation-state "Nation-state") system, incorporated their tributary cities ([Lan Xang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lan_Xang "Lan Xang"), [Cambodia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dark_Ages_of_Cambodia "Dark Ages of Cambodia") and some Malay cities) into their territory and abolished the mueang and the tributary system.[^16] [^17] [^18]

In early Philippine history, the [barangay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Precolonial_barangay "Precolonial barangay") was a complex sociopolitical unit which scholars have historically [^19] considered the dominant organizational pattern among the various [peoples](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Filipinos "Filipinos") of the [Philippine archipelago](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Geography_of_the_Philippines "Geography of the Philippines").[^20] These sociopolitical units were sometimes also referred to as barangay states, but are more properly referred to using the technical term *[polity](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Polity "Polity")*.[^20] [^21] Evidence suggests a considerable degree of independence as city states ruled by [Datus](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Datu "Datu"), [Rajahs](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Raja "Raja") and [Sultans](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Sultan "Sultan").[^22] Early chroniclers [^23] record that the name evolved from the term *[balangay](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Balangay "Balangay")*, which refers to a plank boat widely used by various cultures of the Philippine archipelago prior to the arrival of European colonizers.[^20]

### 20th-century cities under international supervision

#### Danzig

The Free City of Danzig was a semi- [autonomous](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Autonomous "Autonomous") city-state that existed between 1920 and 1939, consisting of the [Baltic Sea](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Baltic_Sea "Baltic Sea") port of Danzig (now [Gdańsk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gda%C5%84sk "Gdańsk"), [Poland](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poland "Poland")) and nearly 200 towns in the surrounding areas. It was created on 15 November 1920 [^24] [^25] under the terms of Article 100 (Section XI of Part III) of the 1919 [Treaty of Versailles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Versailles "Treaty of Versailles") after the end of [World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/World_War_I "World War I").

#### Fiume

After a prolonged period where the city of [Fiume](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Fiume "Fiume") enjoyed considerable autonomy under [Habsburg](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Habsburg "Habsburg") rule (see [Corpus separatum (Fiume)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corpus_separatum_\(Fiume\) "Corpus separatum (Fiume)")), the Free State of Fiume was proclaimed as a fully independent free state which existed between 1920 and 1924. Its territory of 28 km <sup>2</sup> (11 sq mi) comprised the city of Fiume (now in [Croatia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Croatia "Croatia") and, since the end of World War II, known as [Rijeka](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rijeka "Rijeka"), both names meaning "river" in the respective languages) and rural areas to its north, with a corridor to its west connecting it to [Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italy "Italy").

#### Jerusalem

Under the [United Nations Partition Plan for Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Partition_Plan_for_Palestine "United Nations Partition Plan for Palestine") of 1947, [Mandatory Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mandatory_Palestine "Mandatory Palestine") was to be partitioned into three states: a Jewish state of [Israel](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israel "Israel"), an Arab state of [Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Palestine "Palestine"), and a *corpus separatum* ([Latin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Latin_language "Latin language") for " [separated body](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corpus_separatum_\(disambiguation\) "Corpus separatum (disambiguation)") ") consisting of a Jerusalem city-state under the control of [United Nations Trusteeship Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Trusteeship_Council "United Nations Trusteeship Council"). Although the plan had some international support and the UN accepted this proposal (and still officially holds the stance that Jerusalem should be held under this regime), implementation of the plan failed as the [1948 Palestine war](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1948_Palestine_war "1948 Palestine war") broke out with the [1947–48 Civil War in Mandatory Palestine](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/1947%E2%80%9348_Civil_War_in_Mandatory_Palestine "1947–48 Civil War in Mandatory Palestine"), ultimately resulting in Jerusalem being split into [West Jerusalem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Jerusalem "West Jerusalem") and [East Jerusalem](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/East_Jerusalem "East Jerusalem"). Israel would eventually gain control of East Jerusalem in the [Six-Day War](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Six-Day_War "Six-Day War") in 1967.

#### Memel

The Klaipėda Region or Memel Territory was defined by the [Treaty of Versailles](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Versailles "Treaty of Versailles") in 1920 when it was put under the administration of the [Council of Ambassadors](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Ambassadors "Council of Ambassadors"). The Memel Territory was to remain under the control of the [League of Nations](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/League_of_Nations "League of Nations") until a future day when the people of the region would be allowed to vote on whether the land would return to Germany or not. The then predominantly [ethnic German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ethnic_German "Ethnic German") Memel Territory ([Prussian Lithuanians](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prussian_Lithuanians "Prussian Lithuanians") and Memellanders constituted the other ethnic groups), situated between the river and the town of that name, was occupied by [Lithuania](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lithuania "Lithuania") in the [Klaipėda Revolt](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Klaip%C4%97da_Revolt "Klaipėda Revolt") of 1923.

#### Ottoman

Some proposals for the [partition of the Ottoman Empire](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Partition_of_the_Ottoman_Empire "Partition of the Ottoman Empire") envisaged international zones at [Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Istanbul "Istanbul") /Constantinople or the wider [Turkish straits](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Turkish_straits "Turkish straits"),[^26] and possibly also at [İzmir](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/%C4%B0zmir "İzmir") /Smyrna.[^27] Although the [allies of World War I](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Allies_of_World_War_I "Allies of World War I") occupied both after the 1918 [Armistice of Mudros](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Armistice_of_Mudros "Armistice of Mudros"), the British-led [occupation of Istanbul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupation_of_Istanbul "Occupation of Istanbul") recognised Turkey as de jure sovereign, while the Greek [occupation of Smyrna](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Occupation_of_Smyrna "Occupation of Smyrna") was [an attempted annexation](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Greco-Turkish_War_\(1919%E2%80%931922\) "Greco-Turkish War (1919–1922)"). The 1923 [Treaty of Lausanne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Treaty_of_Lausanne "Treaty of Lausanne") re-established Turkish control of both areas.

#### Shanghai

The Shanghai International Settlement (1845–1943) was an international zone with its own legal system, postal service, and currency.

#### Tangier

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/aa/Tangier_12.JPG/250px-Tangier_12.JPG)

Tangier

The international zone within the city of [Tangier](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tangier "Tangier"), in North Africa was approximately 373 km <sup>2</sup> (144 sq mi). It was at first under the joint administration of France, Spain, and the United Kingdom, plus later Portugal, Italy, Belgium, the Netherlands, Sweden, and the United States. The international zone was initially attached to Morocco. It then became a French-Spanish protectorate from 1923 until 29 October 1956, when it was reintegrated into the state of Morocco.

#### Trieste

The Free Territory of Trieste was an independent territory situated in Central Europe between northern Italy and Yugoslavia, facing the north part of the Adriatic Sea, under direct responsibility of the [United Nations Security Council](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/United_Nations_Security_Council "United Nations Security Council") in the aftermath of World War II, from 1947 to 1954.

#### West Berlin

In the 20th century [West Berlin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Berlin "West Berlin"), though lacking sovereignty, functioned from 1948 until 1990 as a state legally not belonging to any other state, but ruled by the [Western Allies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_Bloc "Western Bloc"). They allowed – notwithstanding their overlordship as occupant powers – its internal organisation as one state simultaneously being a city, officially called Berlin (West). Though West Berlin maintained close ties to the [West German](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/West_Germany "West Germany") Federal Republic, it never legally formed a part of it.

## Contemporary sovereign city-states

### Vatican City

![](https://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b8/Vatican_City_map_EN.svg/250px-Vatican_City_map_EN.svg.png)

Vatican City, a city-state well known for being the smallest country in the world

Until September 1870, the city of [Rome](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rome "Rome") had been controlled by the [pope](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope "Pope") as part of his [Papal States](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Papal_States "Papal States"). When King [Victor Emmanuel II](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victor_Emmanuel_II "Victor Emmanuel II") seized the city in 1870, [Pope Pius IX](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope_Pius_IX "Pope Pius IX") refused to recognize the newly formed [Kingdom of Italy](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Italian_unification "Italian unification").

Because he could not travel without effectively acknowledging the authority of the king, Pius IX and his successors each claimed to be a " [Prisoner in the Vatican](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Prisoner_in_the_Vatican "Prisoner in the Vatican") ", unable to leave the 0.49 km <sup>2</sup> (0.19 sq mi) papal [enclave](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Enclave "Enclave") once they had ascended the [papal throne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Throne "Throne").

The [impasse](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Roman_Question "Roman Question") was resolved in 1929 by the [Lateran Treaties](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lateran_Treaties "Lateran Treaties") negotiated by the Italian dictator [Benito Mussolini](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Benito_Mussolini "Benito Mussolini") between King [Victor Emmanuel III](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Victor_Emmanuel_III "Victor Emmanuel III") and [Pope Pius XI](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Pope_Pius_XI "Pope Pius XI"). Under this treaty, Vatican City was recognized as an independent state, with the Pope as its head. The [Vatican City State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Vatican_City "Vatican City") has its own [citizenship](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Citizenship "Citizenship"), [diplomatic corps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Diplomatic_corps "Diplomatic corps"), [flag](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Flag_of_Vatican_City "Flag of Vatican City"), and [postage stamps](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Postage_stamps_and_postal_history_of_Vatican_City "Postage stamps and postal history of Vatican City"). With a population of less than 1,000 (mostly clergymen), it is by far the smallest sovereign country in the world.
