The following [outline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Outline_\(list\) "Outline (list)") is provided as an overview of and topical guide to politics and political science:

**[Politics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics "Politics")** – the exercise of power; process by which groups of people make [collective decisions](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Group_decision_making "Group decision making"). Politics is the art or science of running governmental or state affairs (including behavior within civil [governments](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Government "Government")), institutions, fields, and special interest groups such as the [corporate](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Corporation "Corporation"), [academic](https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Academia%E2%88%92&action=edit&redlink=1 "Academia− (page does not exist)"), and [religious](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Religion "Religion") segments of society.

**[Political science](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_science "Political science")** – the field concerning the theory and practice of [politics](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Politics "Politics") and the description and analysis of [political systems](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Political_system "Political system") and political behavior.

### Branches of government

The [separation of powers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Separation_of_powers "Separation of powers") is typically set in the [constitution](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Constitution "Constitution") or [basic law](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Basic_law "Basic law") in order to achieve [checks and balances](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Checks_and_balances "Checks and balances") within government. The typical model has three branches, and is referred to as the ***trias politica***.

- [Legislature](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Legislature "Legislature"), deliberates and passes [laws](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Law "Law").
	- [Unicameralism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Unicameralism "Unicameralism")
		- [Bicameralism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Bicameralism "Bicameralism")
		- [Upper house](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Upper_house "Upper house")
				- [Lower house](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Lower_house "Lower house")
		- [Tricameralism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tricameralism "Tricameralism")
		- [Tetracameralism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tetracameralism "Tetracameralism")
		- [Multicameralism](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Multicameralism "Multicameralism")
- [Executive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Executive_\(government\) "Executive (government)"), executes laws.
	- [Head of state](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_state "Head of state"), formal, often symbolic, leader of state. Sometimes has [veto](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Veto "Veto") power over proposed legislation.
		- [Monarch](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Monarch "Monarch")
				- [President](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/President_\(government_title\) "President (government title)")
		- [Head of government](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Head_of_government "Head of government"), the person(s) in charge of day-to-day affairs of the state. Usually heads a [cabinet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cabinet_\(government\) "Cabinet (government)"), a [Council of Ministers](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_Ministers "Council of Ministers") or a [Council of State](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Council_of_State "Council of State").
		- [Chancellor](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chancellor "Chancellor")
				- [Chief executive](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_executive "Chief executive")
				- [Chief minister](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Chief_minister "Chief minister")
