The kings of Aceh wondered why the Indian emperors lived such long lives and enjoyed such long reigns.

To learn the secret, they sent an envoy.

When the envoy arrived in the presence of the Indian ruler and presented the letter, the Indian ruler gave him this reply: “There is a large tree in such-and-such a place. Find out how it can be felled without an axe or saw. The answer to your question lies there.”

This promise seemed impossible to the envoy; but he had no choice but to accept. They lodged him in a mansion facing the tree.

Every day, the envoy would stand before the tree and beseech God: “O Lord, they have tied my salvation to this tree. Create a cause, and with Your hidden grace, untie this knot.”

Soon after, by sheer chance, a fierce wind arose; it toppled the tree.

The envoy ran to the Indian ruler’s palace. He told him what happened. The Indian ruler said: “The answer lies in this very event. If the prayer of a single innocent can fell such a mighty tree in so short a time—imagine how much the prayers of thousands of innocents can extend or shorten a ruler’s life.”

— (Kānûn-i Şâhenşâhî, İdrîs-i Bitlîsî, Timaş Yayınları, s.77–78)
