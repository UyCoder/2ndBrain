## The SADAT International Defense Consultancy has grown into a key instrument of Turkish influence in Africa and the Middle East.

[Turkey](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/turkey) ’s use of private military actors has become an increasingly important—if opaque —feature of its foreign policy toolkit. Among these, SADAT International Defense Consultancy stands out as a quasi-official instrument that blurs the line between state policy and deniable coercion.

[SADAT](https://sadat.com.tr/) is a Turkish private military company (PMC) founded in 2012 by former Turkish Brigadier General Adnan Tanriverdi, a close ally of President [Recep Tayyip Erdogan](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/recep-tayyip-erdogan). The firm describes its [mission](https://sadat.com.tr/en/our-mission/) as providing military training, defense consulting, and security assistance primarily to Muslim-majority countries. In reality, it is a paramilitary instrument advancing Ankara’s regional and ideological agenda to spread Islamist extremism, primarily in sub-Saharan [Africa](https://nationalinterest.org/region/africa) and the [Caucasus](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/caucasus).

SADAT’s mission emanates from a grievance narrative against the West. Its corporate manifesto is explicit in its Islamist inclinations. It intends to [prevent](https://sadat.com.tr/en/why-sadat-inc-international-defense-consulting/) Muslim countries “from dependence on Western crusader imperialist countries and to help establishment of a defensive collaboration and defensive industrial cooperation among Islamic countries with the intent of serving Islamic union.” SADAT [intends](https://sadat.com.tr/en/why-sadat-inc-international-defense-consulting/) to “to respond to all of 60 Islamic countries’…needs at defense sector \[sic\].” The [company](https://www.sadat.com.tr/download/SADAT-eng-full-v02.pdf) “will contribute to the emergence of the World of Islam as a Super power and to promote an environment of cooperation in \[the\] field of defense and defense industry among Islamic countries \[sic\].”

Tanriverdi was [dismissed](https://warontherocks.com/2021/10/making-sense-of-sadat-turkeys-private-military-company/) from the Turkish Armed Forces (TAF) in 1997 for his Islamist leanings. Following the purge, Tanriverdi founded Turkey’s first PMC, SADAT. Tanriverdi was a [personal confidant](https://warontherocks.com/2021/10/making-sense-of-sadat-turkeys-private-military-company/) of Erdogan. Since its inception, SADAT has provided military security and training to organizations [aligned with Islamist ideologies](https://www.sadat.com.tr/download/SADAT-eng-full-v02.pdf) in [Libya](https://www.lemonde.fr/en/le-monde-africa/article/2024/06/07/sadat-the-turkish-wagner-whose-shadow-hangs-over-west-africa_6674134_124.html), [Azerbaijan](https://www.rferl.org/a/are-syrian-mercenaries-helping-azerbaijan-fight-for-nagorno-karabakh-/30895331.html), West Africa, [Syria](https://greydynamics.com/sadat-defense-inc-turkeys-pmc/), and [Iraq](https://anfenglish.com/news/turkish-company-sadat-trains-south-kurdistan-special-forces-48102).

Tanriverdi died in 2024, but SADAT—like its Russian counterpart, [Wagner](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/wagner-group) —has survived its founder. Before his death, Tanriverdi [called](https://nordicmonitor.com/2020/12/islamic-union-congress-organized-by-paramilitary-sadats-owner-called-for-joint-islamic-army/) for an Islamic “army for Palestine” and threatened that any Israeli operations in Gaza should lead to attacks on Tel Aviv. In Tanriverdi’s vision, this would be achieved by establishing a joint Islamic army. Tanriverdi’s son and the Turkish PMC’s new chief, [Melih Tanriverdi](https://nordicmonitor.com/2024/10/turkeys-paramilitary-firm-sadat-and-ceo/), seeks to fill any vacuum left by the United States, Europe, or moderate Arab partners in the region.

The precise number of mercenaries SADAT operates is unknown. But the organization has [supported](https://www.longwarjournal.org/archives/2024/09/analysis-erdogans-private-military-company-looks-to-raise-an-islamist-army.php) terror by training and recruiting fighters for al-Nusra Front and the [Islamic State](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/isis). In September 2024, the Armenian Bar Association also [requested](https://asbarez.com/armenian-bar-files-global-magnitsky-sanctions-complaint-against-turkeys-sadat/) Global Magnitsky Sanctions against SADAT over “allegations of serious human rights abuses and complicity in violence and instability across conflict zones, particularly in Artsakh ([Nagorno-Karabakh](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/nagorno-karabakh)), Libya, and Syria.”

SADAT is fundamental to Turkish leaders’ desire for military power and Islamist influence in Africa and [maintains](https://foreignpolicy.com/2025/10/28/turkey-africa-drones-sudan-arms-guns-conflict/) a presence in nine states across the Horn of Africa, the Sahel, and the Maghreb. The PMC [began](https://greydynamics.com/sadat-defense-inc-turkeys-pmc/) its first overseas mission in [Libya](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/libya) in 2013. During Turkey’s intervention there in 2020, SADAT [oversaw](https://media.defense.gov/2020/Sep/02/2002489948/-1/-1/1/LEAD%20IG%20EAST%20AFRICA%20AND%20NORTH%20AND%20WEST%20AFRICA%20COUNTERTERRORISM%20OPERATIONS.PDF) 5,000 Syrians recruited to fight alongside the Tripoli government.

The PMC has more recently expanded its operations into the Sahel, where it guards Turkish economic interests and protects violent dictators. In 2024, French journalists reported that SADAT [began](https://www.lemonde.fr/en/le-monde-africa/article/2024/06/07/sadat-the-turkish-wagner-whose-shadow-hangs-over-west-africa_6674134_124.html) staffing the security detail of Malian junta leader Assimi Goita. The UN has [implicated](https://www.france24.com/en/africa/20220531-un-report-links-malian-army-to-exponential-rise-in-abuses) Goita and his forces in mass murder and indiscriminate violence against civilians.

[Syria](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/syria) is SADAT’s primary recruiting ground: the PMC [enlisted](https://nationalinterest.org/blog/lebanon-watch/inside-bloody-business-turkeys-syrian-mercenaries-204589) recruits from eight militias within the Turkish-backed Syrian National Army (SNA) for combat in Libya. In 2023, the US Treasury [sanctioned](https://home.treasury.gov/news/press-releases/jy1699) one such militia, the Hamza Division, for torture, abduction, sexual violence, and robbery against civilians. SADAT also [forced](https://www.bbc.com/news/stories-55238803) Syrian recruits into front-line combat in Libya and Nagorno-Karabakh, while enriching rights-abusing SNA leaders and often withholding pay. Captured foreign fighters explained that SADAT also [trained](https://www.aei.org/foreign-and-defense-policy/middle-east/has-sadat-become-erdogans-revolutionary-guards/) Russian Muslims who joined ISIS and Jabhat al-Nusra ([Al Qaeda](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/al-qaeda) ’s Syrian branch), entering Syria through Turkey’s “ [jihadi highway](https://jstribune.com/ciddi-turkeys-push-for-regime-change-in-syria-the-jihadi-highway/).”

In August 2020, SADAT began recruiting and sending between [1,500 and 2,000 Syrians](https://www.bbc.com/news/stories-55238803) to fight for Azerbaijan in Nagorno-Karabakh. French President [Emmanuel Macron](https://nationalinterest.org/tags/emmanuel-macron) cited intelligence [revealing 300 of these Syrians](https://www.theguardian.com/world/2020/oct/02/syrian-recruit-describes-role-of-foreign-fighters-in-nagorno-karabakh) to be members of jihadist militias.

The cumulative picture is one of a state leveraging privatized force to pursue strategic and ideological objectives under a veneer of deniability. While SADAT enables Turkey to act with agility in contested regions, it also complicates international efforts to enforce norms around conflict conduct and accountability. As Ankara continues to expand its influence across multiple theaters, SADAT is likely to remain a central—if controversial—pillar of its foreign policy architecture.
