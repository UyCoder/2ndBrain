## Middle East

#### Political Optics Get in the Way of a Bad Deal on Iran

What Happened

The week was characterized by mounting pressure on the ceasefire:

- Over the weekend, Israel [announced](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/cdep04kzz5wo) that the IDF has occupied Beaufort Castle, a strategic fort in southern Lebanon. This marks the deepest incursion into Lebanese territory for 26 years, when Israel withdrew from Lebanon in 2000 after having [maintained](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Israeli_occupation_of_Southern_Lebanon_\(1982%E2%80%932000\)) a ‘security zone’ along with proxies since 1985. On May 28, the IDF [declared](https://www.france24.com/en/middle-east/20260528-israel-declares-new-combat-zone-in-southern-lebanon-and-orders-evacuations) a new evacuation zone covering anything south of the Zahrani River, suggesting further northward encroachment by the IDF in the weeks to come. Israel [occupies](https://www.aljazeera.com/news/2026/5/31/israeli-forces-push-past-lebanons-litani-river-how-significant-is-it) around one-fifth of Lebanon’s territory at present.
- Just one week after Marco Rubio [signaled](https://www.geopoliticalmonitor.com/geopolitics-weekly-iran-war-putin-xi-summit-drc-ebola-outbreak/) an imminent deal, the diplomatic waters are turbid as ever. The two sides were believed to be [narrowing in](https://www.reuters.com/world/middle-east/trumps-room-maneuver-narrows-us-iran-close-framework-deal-2026-05-29/) on a framework deal earlier in the week, only to have President Trump pull back amid a growing din of [criticism](https://www.yahoo.com/news/politics/article/ted-cruz-thom-tillis-and-other-republicans-criticize-trumps-emerging-iran-deal-153625510.html) from GOP allies and Israel. As of June 1, US media outlets are [reporting](https://www.bbc.com/news/articles/c1w2xve315do) that the deal has been sent back to Iran with edits concerning the Strait of Hormuz and enriched uranium.
- The IRGC [announced](https://aje.news/xhk3kr?update=4617338) on June 1 that 15 ships traversed the Strait of Hormuz over the past 24 hours, abiding by its new procedures and potentially paying tolls. In normal times, anywhere from [120-140 vessels](https://www.nytimes.com/2026/04/20/world/middleeast/strait-hormuz-traffic-ships-iran-us.html) move through the Strait daily.
