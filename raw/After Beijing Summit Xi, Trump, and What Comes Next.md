May 21, 2026 — Following the May 14–15 summit in Beijing between Presidents Trump and Xi Jinping, Asia Society convenes a timely public webinar examining the meeting’s outcomes, strategic signals, and global implications amid rising trade tensions, intensifying tech rivalry, and growing competition across the Indo-Pacific and beyond. The discussion spans tariffs, export controls, investment, Taiwan, fentanyl, AI, health, and major global flashpoints from Ukraine to the Middle East.  
  
Featuring  
\- The Honorable Dr. Kevin Rudd AC, global president and CEO of Asia Society;  
\- Wendy Cutler, senior vice president at the Asia Society Policy Institute (ASPI);  
\- Danny Russel, distinguished fellow at ASPI;  
\- Orville Schell, Arthur Ross vice president of the Center on U.S.-China Relations at Asia Society;  
\- Jing Qian, vice president, co-founder, and managing director of ASPI's Center for China Analysis (CCA); and  
\- Lizzi C. Lee (moderator), fellow on the Chinese economy at CCA.  
  
This expert panel explores what was achieved, what remains unresolved, and how the summit may shape the next phase of U.S.–China relations across economics, technology, security, diplomacy, and the broader international order.
