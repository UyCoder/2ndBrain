**Following heightened tensions between Kabul and Islamabad and the closure of trade routes between the two countries, Pakistan’s Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister, Ishaq Dar, says Islamabad is seeking to expand trilateral and regional cooperation with Afghanistan, China, Bangladesh, and other countries.**

Dar also stressed that regional stability and cooperation are in the interest of all countries in the region.

Ishaq Dar, Pakistan’s Deputy Prime Minister and Foreign Minister, said: “We as Pakistan, we are looking to now try and go for trilateral, for example, China, Pakistan, Afghanistan, China, Pakistan, Bangladesh and some other countries because we can't just live in isolation."

Meanwhile, some political analysts insist that for multilateral cooperation to take shape, Pakistan must reconsider its policies toward neighboring countries, including Afghanistan.

Mohammad Aslam Danishmal, a university lecturer, said: “Pakistan must seriously reconsider the policies and games it has pursued. Only then can genuine regional connectivity and cooperation emerge.”

Economic analyst Mohammad Tawous Farzad said: “First, discussions should focus on reopening the trade routes between Afghanistan and Pakistan that have been closed. Other issues can then be addressed afterward.”

This comes as several Pakistani politicians had previously criticized Islamabad’s approach and policies toward Afghanistan, calling for disputes to be resolved through dialogue and for trade routes between the two countries to be reopened.
