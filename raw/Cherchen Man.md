**Cherchen Man** or **Chärchän Man** or **Ur-David** is the modern name given to a mummy found in the town [Cherchen](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cherchen "Cherchen"), located in current [Xinjiang](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Xinjiang "Xinjiang") region of China. The mummy is a member of the group known as [Tarim mummies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarim_mummies "Tarim mummies"). His naturally- [mummified](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mummy "Mummy") remains were discovered in *Tomb 2* at the Zaghunluq cemetery, near the town of [Qiemo](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Qiemo_Town "Qiemo Town") (Chärchän) in the [Taklamakan Desert](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Taklamakan_Desert "Taklamakan Desert") of north-west [China](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/China "China").[^1] [^2] [^3] Other such remains have also been recovered at sites throughout the [Tarim Basin](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarim_Basin "Tarim Basin"), including [Qäwrighul](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Q%C3%A4wrighul "Qäwrighul"), [Yanghai](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Yanghai "Yanghai"), [Shengjindian](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shengjindian "Shengjindian"), Shanpula (Sampul), and Qizilchoqa.

## Description

The mummy is an adult male who is believed to have died around 1000 BC and is likely to have been aged around fifty years at the time of his death.[^1] [^4] [^2]

His height is estimated at 176–178 cm.[^2] His hair was "reddish brown flecked with grey, framing high cheekbones", he had an [aquiline](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Aquiline_nose "Aquiline nose") "long nose, full lips and a ginger beard", and was wearing "a red [twill](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Twill "Twill") [tunic](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tunic "Tunic") " and [leggings](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Leggings "Leggings") with a pattern resembling [tartan](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tartan "Tartan").[^5] [^6] Yellow and purple spiral and sun patterns on the mummy's face have been misidentified as tattoos in some sources; they are actually an [ochre](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ochre "Ochre") paint.[^1]

## Mummification

Like other mummies from the [Tarim](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Tarim_Basin "Tarim Basin"), Cherchen Man was buried in a tomb made of mud bricks topped with reeds and brush. The Cherchen man and the other female mummy were placed on multiple branches, with small mats underneath them that reduced the moisture in the tomb, adding to their preservation.[^1] The Cherchen man also appears to have had a piece of wood holding his legs up in the bent position which would have increased the amount of air circulation, slowing the rate of decomposition.[^1]

The Cherchen man and his companions (there were one man and three women in the same tomb, and one baby in the adjacent tomb) [^8] were natural [mummies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Mummy "Mummy") meaning that unlike the well known [Egyptian mummies](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Ancient_Egyptian_funerary_practices "Ancient Egyptian funerary practices"), they became mummies from exposure to their ambient environment, as opposed to intentional human practices.
