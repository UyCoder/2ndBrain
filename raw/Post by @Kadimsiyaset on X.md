Alexander demoted one of his workers, a great man, and assigned him a vile and lowly task.

After a while, the man came before Alexander. The ruler asked: “How do you find your job?”

The man replied:

“May the king’s reign be long! A man does not become great through his work; but with good character, justice, and equity, a job can be made honorable and noble.”

Alexander was so pleased with this response that he reinstated the man to his position—and even promoted him.

It is not the office that is honorable, but the person who holds the office. There are no lowly jobs—only lowly men.

— (Advice to the Sultan, Imam Ghazali, Timaş Akademi, p.73)
